Gold has lost steam after a five-week rally, slipping 3% as the US Dollar strengthened and crude oil prices surged. According to BNY’s Head of Markets Macro Strategy, Bob Savage, the yellow metal is facing pressure from broad-based USD buying, which has lifted key currency pairs like EUR/USD lower. Traders are watching gold’s technical support around $1,950 as momentum wanes.  

The rally in oil, meanwhile, has shifted investor focus to commodities with tighter supply dynamics, reinforcing the USD’s appeal as a safe haven. The combination of rising energy prices and a firmer Dollar has undermined gold’s appeal as an inflation hedge, prompting traders to reduce long positions and reassess exposure across forex and commodities markets.  

Market participants are also eyeing short-term exchange rate movements, as USD strength against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY may continue to weigh on precious metals. Analysts suggest that unless gold regains traction above $1,975, the downward trend could extend further, influencing both forex trading strategies and commodity portfolios in the coming weeks.
