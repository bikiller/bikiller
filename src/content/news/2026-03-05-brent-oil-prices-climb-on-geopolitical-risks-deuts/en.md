Brent crude oil prices have steadied following their sharpest two‑day rally since 2020, with Deutsche Bank analysts highlighting persistent geopolitical tensions as the main catalyst. Traders noted that markets remain sensitive to developments in key oil-producing regions, keeping volatility elevated. The recent surge has pressured risk-sensitive currency pairs, particularly those tied to commodity-exporting economies, as investors reassess exposure to USD‑denominated assets.  

The rebound in Brent has coincided with mixed movements in global forex markets. The euro slipped slightly against the dollar, while commodity-linked currencies like the Australian and Canadian dollars showed tentative gains on higher crude valuations. Deutsche Bank points out that the exchange rate fluctuations are closely tied to geopolitical risk sentiment, with traders adjusting positions across major pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/CAD, in response to oil price dynamics.  

Looking ahead, analysts warn that rapid shifts in geopolitics could trigger further swings in both oil and currency markets. Technical levels around $85 per barrel are seen as key resistance, while support near $80 could guide short-term trading strategies. Forex traders are keeping a close eye on Brent’s trajectory, given its influence on inflation expectations, central bank policies, and cross‑currency flows in the coming weeks.
