Gold (XAU/USD) extended its recent rally, posting gains for a second consecutive day, climbing more than 0.50% as the US Dollar weakened. The greenback came under pressure following Japan’s unexpected intervention in currency markets, which disrupted USD trading and fueled safe-haven demand for gold. Investors noted heightened volatility across major currency pairs, including USD/JPY and EUR/USD, as central bank actions reshaped short-term exchange rate dynamics.  

The precious metal’s push higher coincided with softer oil prices, which fell after Iran submitted a fresh proposal on production adjustments. This development weighed on crude benchmarks and slightly eased inflation concerns, further supporting gold’s appeal as an alternative store of value amid USD weakness. Analysts highlighted that XAU/USD faces resistance near recent highs, suggesting cautious optimism for traders eyeing technical breakouts.  

Market participants are now closely monitoring the broader impact of Japan’s intervention on global forex flows. The move has prompted increased trading activity in major pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD, while speculative positioning in gold continues to rise. With the USD under persistent pressure, safe-haven assets are likely to retain momentum, shaping strategies for traders navigating both currency and commodity markets.
