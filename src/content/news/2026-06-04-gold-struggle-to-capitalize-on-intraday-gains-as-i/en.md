Gold (XAU/USD) remained in a cautious recovery during Thursday’s Asian session, struggling to extend earlier intraday gains. Despite touching the previous day’s swing high, the commodity has failed to break decisively higher, as market participants weigh geopolitical risks in Iran alongside expectations of continued Federal Reserve policy support for the USD. The exchange rate is closely watched near key technical levels, reflecting mixed trading sentiment.  

The recent Israel-Lebanon truce triggered some USD profit-taking, providing limited support to gold prices. Traders are navigating a delicate balance between safe-haven demand and currency movements, with XAU/USD hovering amid modest volatility. Market dynamics suggest that while the metal benefits from temporary USD weakness, broader trading trends remain influenced by potential central bank actions and regional tensions.  

Looking ahead, gold’s ability to sustain gains may hinge on shifts in the USD and euro (EUR) currency pairs, as investors assess global macro conditions and risk sentiment. Analysts note that without a stronger directional push, XAU/USD could remain range-bound, offering short-term trading opportunities for market participants who track technical levels and intraday exchange rate fluctuations.
