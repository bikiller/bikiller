NZD/USD continues its downward trajectory, slipping below the 0.5800 mark and reaching its lowest levels in two months. As of Thursday’s European session, the currency pair trades around 0.5780, extending a three-day losing streak. The bearish momentum is evident on the daily chart, with the exchange rate consolidating within a descending channel pattern, suggesting further downside potential.

The ongoing weakness in NZD/USD can be attributed to broader market trends, with the US Dollar maintaining its strength amid robust economic data and expectations of continued monetary tightening by the Federal Reserve. Traders remain cautious, awaiting any shift in the market’s risk sentiment or key updates from both the USD and NZD economies, which could influence the pair’s next move.
