GBP/USD edged higher on Monday, trading around 1.3565, marking a 0.23% gain on the day. The currency pair benefited from a softer US Dollar, as traders reacted to signs of improving risk appetite and easing concerns over immediate Fed policy moves. Market participants remain attentive to US economic data, with the dollar’s retreat providing temporary relief for the pound.  

The rise comes amid cooler-than-expected UK inflation data, which has tempered expectations of aggressive Bank of England tightening. Analysts note that this combination of a weaker USD and moderated domestic price pressures is encouraging traders to favor GBP in short-term trading. Technical levels around 1.3550-1.3570 are being closely monitored as potential support and resistance zones.  

Across the broader forex market, the softer USD has also lifted EUR/USD and other major dollar-denominated pairs. Traders are positioning ahead of upcoming US and UK economic releases, weighing the balance between risk sentiment and central bank signals. For GBP/USD, sustaining gains above 1.3560 could open the way toward the next resistance near 1.3600, while any USD rebound may quickly cap further advances.
