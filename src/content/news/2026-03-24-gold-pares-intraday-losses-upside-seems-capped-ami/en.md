Gold (XAU/USD) has managed to recover most of its intraday losses, inching closer to the $4,400 mark as the European session begins on Tuesday. However, a substantial recovery remains out of reach as the precious metal faces pressure from a strengthening USD and hawkish central bank policies. Gold's upside remains limited, with market participants watching for further developments in monetary policy.

The USD's firm performance continues to weigh on gold's recovery, as higher interest rates in major economies strengthen the greenback. The EUR/USD exchange rate remains volatile, with fluctuations driven by central bank rhetoric. Gold's price action suggests that any significant bullish momentum may be capped unless a shift in the broader macroeconomic environment provides support for the metal.
