TD Securities anticipates a slowdown in Canadian inflation, projecting headline CPI to ease to 1.9% year-on-year in February. Softer core measures are also expected, leaving overall inflation below the Bank of Canada’s forecast. Analysts note that while this suggests limited domestic price pressure, the Canadian dollar’s near-term trajectory may be influenced more by external factors, particularly recent geopolitical tensions affecting global oil markets.  

The CAD/USD currency pair is under scrutiny as traders weigh weaker inflation data against rising oil prices driven by the Iran conflict. Exchange rate movements could see heightened volatility, with the loonie’s sensitivity to crude prices potentially offsetting the impact of softer domestic data. USD and EUR traders are monitoring how these dynamics might shape Bank of Canada policy expectations and cross-currency flows.  

Market participants are positioning for trading opportunities around the CAD, balancing the implications of slower domestic price growth with commodity-driven gains. The interplay between inflation signals and oil supply risks suggests a nuanced outlook, where technical levels may respond more to external shocks than to muted CPI readings. Forex traders remain alert to sudden swings in the CAD as energy prices and geopolitical news continue to dominate sentiment.
