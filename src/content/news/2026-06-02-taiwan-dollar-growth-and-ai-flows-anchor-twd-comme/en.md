USD/TWD traded in a relatively stable range around 31.37, with the Taiwan dollar continuing to draw support from resilient economic growth and sustained artificial intelligence-related capital inflows, according to Commerzbank. The bank noted that realised one-month volatility for the currency pair remained close to 4.5%, a comparatively moderate level versus several regional Asian currencies, highlighting calmer trading conditions in Taiwan’s foreign exchange market.

Analysts said steady demand linked to Taiwan’s technology and semiconductor sectors has helped anchor the TWD despite broader fluctuations in the USD and shifting expectations for global interest rates. AI-driven investment flows have also reinforced confidence in the local exchange rate, limiting downside pressure even as traders monitor US Federal Reserve policy signals and regional market sentiment.

The relatively subdued volatility profile suggests investors continue to view the Taiwan dollar as one of the more stable currency plays in Asia. Market participants are now watching whether continued export momentum and technology-sector inflows can keep USD/TWD near current levels, while any sharp move in the USD or weakening global risk appetite could test support for the Taiwanese currency in coming sessions.
