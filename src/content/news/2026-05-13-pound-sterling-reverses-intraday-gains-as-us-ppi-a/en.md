Pound Sterling saw a volatile session on Wednesday, with the GBP/USD currency pair swinging roughly 65 pips intraday before closing near its previous level. Traders initially pushed the pound higher, but gains reversed as the U.S. Producer Price Index (PPI) reinforced expectations of persistent inflationary pressures in the U.S., supporting the USD. The exchange rate ended largely unchanged, reflecting a tug-of-war between bullish and bearish factors.

Market participants also factored in heightened political uncertainty in the U.K., which weighed on sterling sentiment. Analysts noted that domestic risks, coupled with cautious positioning ahead of upcoming economic data, limited GBP’s upside despite earlier strength. In technical terms, key resistance around $1.2850 remained intact, while immediate support near $1.2720 prevented further declines, keeping trading ranges relatively contained.

The session underscored a broader cautious mood in the forex market, with EUR/USD and other major pairs also reacting to U.S. inflation cues. Traders are now eyeing upcoming central bank signals and U.K. political developments, which could dictate the next leg for GBP/USD and related currencies. The market appears poised for continued volatility as investors balance fundamental drivers with technical levels in the short term.
