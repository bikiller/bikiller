The USD/MYR exchange rate has seen a partial recovery for the Malaysian Ringgit, driven by a combination of factors. OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong note that the easing of oil prices, along with a softer US Dollar and a stronger Chinese Yuan (RMB), has supported the Ringgit's movement. 

This shift in market dynamics has allowed the Ringgit to regain some ground, although geopolitical risks continue to loom. The USD/MYR currency pair has fluctuated as a result of these external factors, reflecting the delicate balance between commodity prices and broader macroeconomic trends.
