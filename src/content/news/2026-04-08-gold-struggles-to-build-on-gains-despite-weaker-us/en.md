Gold (XAU/USD) retreated slightly after touching three-week highs on Wednesday, despite initial optimism fueled by a two-week ceasefire between the United States and Iran. The currency pair climbed as much as 1% earlier in the session, reaching an intraday peak of $4,857, before settling near $4,755. Traders cited a softer USD and geopolitical developments as key drivers behind the rally, though profit-taking capped further gains.  

Market participants noted that the broader trading environment for gold remains sensitive to the strength of the US Dollar. A weaker USD tends to boost the appeal of gold as a hedge, influencing exchange rates and positioning across related currency pairs. Analysts suggest that any renewed tension in the Middle East or shifts in US monetary policy could quickly sway trading sentiment, keeping XAU/USD volatile in the near term.  

Technically, the $4,800-$4,850 zone is emerging as a short-term resistance level, while support near $4,740 could attract buyers on dips. Investors and forex traders are watching both geopolitical cues and USD movements closely, as these factors continue to shape gold’s exchange rate dynamics against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Short-term momentum appears positive, but volatility remains elevated, highlighting cautious positioning in the forex and commodity markets.
