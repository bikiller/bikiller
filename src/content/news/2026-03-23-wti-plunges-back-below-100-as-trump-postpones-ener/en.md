WTI Crude Oil plunged nearly 9% on Monday, falling below the $100 mark and testing the $90.00 level. The steep decline was fueled by news that former President Trump had postponed potential energy infrastructure strikes on Iran. The market reacted to the delay in geopolitical tensions, which had previously kept oil prices elevated. This sharp drop marked one of the widest intraday price swings in modern oil trading.

The fall in WTI prices had a ripple effect on the forex market, with the USD showing strength against other currencies. The EUR/USD exchange rate saw notable movement as investors adjusted positions in response to fluctuating commodity prices. As oil continues to dominate global trading, the recent volatility adds to ongoing concerns about inflation and energy security, making for a dynamic outlook on both crude oil and currency markets.
