The British Pound lost ground against the USD on Friday, with the GBP/USD currency pair slipping after fresh UK data showed the economy contracted in April. The weaker-than-expected GDP reading reinforced concerns that growth momentum is fading, prompting traders to trim bullish Sterling positions. As a result, the exchange rate turned lower during European trading, despite recent resilience in the Pound against major peers, including the EUR.

Market sentiment was also shaped by improving diplomatic signals between the United States and Iran. Growing expectations of a potential agreement between the two countries boosted overall risk appetite, encouraging investors to move away from traditional safe-haven assets. The stronger risk mood supported broader USD trading dynamics, while reducing demand for defensive positions across currency markets.

For forex participants, the combination of disappointing UK economic data and shifting geopolitical expectations created fresh pressure on Sterling. Traders will now monitor upcoming economic releases and central bank signals for clues on future policy direction. The near-term outlook for the GBP/USD currency pair remains sensitive to both UK growth trends and broader risk sentiment, with exchange-rate movements likely to reflect changing expectations around interest rates, economic performance, and global market conditions.
