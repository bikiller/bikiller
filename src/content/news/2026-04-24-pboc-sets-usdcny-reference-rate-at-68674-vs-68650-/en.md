On Friday, the People’s Bank of China (PBOC) set the USD/CNY currency pair reference rate at 6.8674, slightly weaker for the yuan compared with the previous fixing of 6.8650. The official exchange rate also came in above the Reuters estimate of 6.8400, signaling a modest deviation from market expectations in the onshore trading session ahead.

Traders noted that the slightly stronger-than-expected fixing suggests continued cautious management of the yuan, as the central bank guides the onshore currency pair within a controlled trading range. By setting the USD/CNY reference rate above consensus forecasts, the PBOC appears to be signaling tolerance for mild depreciation pressure while maintaining overall exchange rate stability amid mixed dollar sentiment.

Market participants will continue to monitor the daily PBOC fixing as a key indicator for yuan direction, especially against the USD and other major currency pairs such as EUR/USD. The persistent gap between official guidance and market estimates may keep traders alert to potential volatility in China’s foreign exchange market in the near term.
