AUD/USD saw a modest increase on Monday, trading around 0.7040, up 0.24% on the day. The Australian Dollar (AUD) benefited from stronger-than-expected inflation data out of China, which supported the currencies tied to Chinese economic performance. The upbeat economic figures from China boosted market sentiment, driving demand for the AUD and pushing the exchange rate higher.

However, the gains were tempered by the ongoing strength of the US Dollar (USD), which continued to provide resistance for the currency pair. Despite the positive Chinese data, the USD remains supported by solid US economic fundamentals, making it difficult for the AUD/USD to sustain larger advances. As of now, the pair holds near 0.7040, with traders eyeing potential moves based on upcoming US data releases and further Chinese economic developments.
