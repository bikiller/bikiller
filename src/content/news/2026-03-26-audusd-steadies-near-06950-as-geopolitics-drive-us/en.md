The AUD/USD currency pair steadied near the 0.6950 exchange rate during Thursday’s trading session, with the Australian Dollar posting modest gains of 0.04% after falling 0.68% on Wednesday. The previous decline was largely driven by broad USD strength, as geopolitical tensions supported safe-haven demand for the US Dollar despite a generally improved risk appetite in global markets.

Currency markets remain sensitive to geopolitical developments, which continue to underpin the USD across major currency pairs including EUR/USD and AUD/USD. Even as equity markets show resilience, traders are maintaining defensive positioning in the forex market, supporting the US Dollar and limiting upside potential for risk-sensitive currencies like the Australian Dollar.

From a trading perspective, the AUD/USD exchange rate holding around 0.6950 suggests near-term consolidation, with traders watching USD momentum, bond yields, and global risk sentiment for direction. If USD demand persists, the currency pair could face renewed pressure, while improving risk sentiment may support a recovery in the Australian Dollar in upcoming trading sessions.
