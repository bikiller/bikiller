Gold (XAU/USD) is experiencing a downward trend as it faces pressure from a stronger US Dollar (USD) and rising US Treasury yields. This negative bias culminates in a trading range where investors are weighing the allure of safe-haven assets against the impact of the firmer dollar. Recent geopolitical tensions, particularly concerning the US-Iran conflict, have kept market participants on edge, influencing their trading decisions.

As the USD strengthens, the exchange rate dynamics shift, making gold less attractive as a non-yielding asset. With the price of gold dipping, traders are keenly monitoring key support levels to gauge potential reversals. The interplay between currency movements and geopolitical risks continues to shape investor sentiment, reflecting the broader complexities within the forex market.
