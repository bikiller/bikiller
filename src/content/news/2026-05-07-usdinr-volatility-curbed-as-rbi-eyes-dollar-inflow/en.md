The USD/INR currency pair saw subdued volatility as market participants awaited guidance from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) on potential measures to attract dollar inflows. Analysts at DBS Group Research highlighted that the central bank is actively exploring policies aimed at strengthening the rupee and narrowing the balance of payments gap. Trading activity remained cautious, with the pair hovering around key technical levels as investors weighed the implications of these interventions.  

The rupee’s exchange rate has shown moderate stability against the dollar in recent sessions, reflecting a mix of steady inflows and restrained speculative activity. Traders noted that any decisive action by the RBI to enhance USD liquidity could provide support to the domestic currency, while also influencing broader forex sentiment across major pairs including EUR/INR and GBP/INR. Market participants are closely monitoring the central bank’s steps for signs of structural easing or targeted interventions.  

Foreign exchange markets are expected to remain sensitive to such policy signals, with USD/INR likely to respond sharply to confirmed inflows or adjustments in liquidity management. Technical analysts suggest that sustained inflows could reinforce support near recent trading lows, while a lack of action may see the pair test resistance zones established over the past month. Overall, the RBI’s strategies will play a key role in shaping short-term trading dynamics and cross-currency flows.
