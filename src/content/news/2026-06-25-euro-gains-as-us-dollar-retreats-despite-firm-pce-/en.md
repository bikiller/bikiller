EUR/USD edged higher near 1.1380 on Thursday, posting modest gains as the US Dollar retreated across major currency pairs. The USD weakened despite fresh United States data showing firm PCE inflation, stronger growth momentum, and resilient labor market conditions, which typically support the greenback. In forex trading, the currency pair reflected mild euro demand amid dollar profit-taking.

Market sentiment was influenced by a modest improvement in German economic sentiment, offering limited but notable support to the euro. The slight uptick helped stabilize EUR/USD trading around the 1.1380 handle, as investors weighed diverging signals between a resilient US economy and a gradually stabilizing eurozone outlook. Broader forex flows remained cautious, limiting volatility.

From a trading perspective, EUR/USD holds steady near 1.1380 as markets digest mixed macroeconomic signals. Persistent US inflation pressures contrast with softening dollar momentum, while euro resilience prevents deeper declines. The pair may continue to consolidate unless a decisive break in data shifts expectations for USD direction in the near term.
