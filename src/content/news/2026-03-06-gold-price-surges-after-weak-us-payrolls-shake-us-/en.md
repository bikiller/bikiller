Gold prices surged on Friday, with XAU/USD climbing more than 1% to trade near $5,140 during the North American session. The rally was driven by a weaker-than-expected US jobs report, which weighed on the USD. The data showed disappointing payroll numbers, triggering a decline in the US Dollar as traders adjusted their positions.

The risk-off sentiment across markets, partly due to escalating tensions in the Middle East, further supported gold’s rise. As investors sought safe-haven assets, gold benefited from the shift in risk appetite, reinforcing its status as a store of value in times of uncertainty. The rally in gold has left the USD vulnerable, pushing XAU/USD higher and sparking further speculation about the future direction of US economic data and Federal Reserve policies.
