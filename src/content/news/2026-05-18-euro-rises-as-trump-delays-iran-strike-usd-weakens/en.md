The Euro gained ground against the US Dollar late in the North American trading session, rising 0.26% as the EUR/USD currency pair climbed to 1.1654. Earlier, the pair tested intraday lows at 1.1608, reflecting volatility driven by sudden geopolitical developments. Broad weakness in the USD supported the rally, with traders reacting to news that US President Donald Trump delayed planned military action against Iran.

Market participants highlighted that the pause in hostilities eased risk sentiment, prompting increased demand for the Euro. The currency pair’s rebound from daily lows underscores the sensitivity of exchange rates to geopolitical headlines and the broader appetite for risk-linked assets. Analysts noted that the EUR/USD could remain volatile in coming sessions, especially if headlines continue to influence USD performance.

Technical trading also played a role, as the pair tested key support near 1.1608 before buyers stepped in, pushing prices higher. With the USD struggling across multiple currency pairs, traders are closely watching how macroeconomic data and political developments intersect. For now, the Euro’s advance reflects both safe-haven rotation and momentum-driven trading in response to changing market conditions.
