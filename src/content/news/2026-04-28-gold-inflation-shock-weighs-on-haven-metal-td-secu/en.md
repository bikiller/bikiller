Gold is facing pressure as rising oil prices fuel inflation, keeping real interest rates high. Bart Melek, Head of Commodity Strategy at TD Securities, notes that the persistent inflationary pressures are increasing the opportunity cost of holding gold, which struggles to attract buyers amid these elevated rates. With the USD strengthening, gold has been trading lower, as higher yields make alternative assets more appealing.

The impact of oil-driven inflation is particularly evident in the exchange rate dynamics, where USD strength continues to suppress demand for the precious metal. As a result, gold is struggling to maintain its safe-haven status. Currently, gold is hovering around $1,980 per ounce, with key technical support levels near $1,950. The ongoing market sentiment suggests that unless inflationary pressures ease, gold may face further downward pressure in the short term.
