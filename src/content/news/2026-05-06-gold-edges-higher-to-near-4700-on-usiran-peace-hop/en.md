Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher toward $4,700 in early Thursday Asian trading, hitting a one-week peak as the market digested developments around a potential US-Iran peace agreement. The rally in the precious metal comes amid a broader risk-on sentiment that has tempered safe-haven demand for the USD, while investors reassess inflation expectations and its potential impact on central bank policies.  

Traders noted that the improved outlook on geopolitical tensions boosted appetite for commodities, with gold benefiting from inflows as a hedge against uncertainty. The currency pairs involving the USD, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, showed modest retracements, reflecting the dollar’s slight pullback in response to easing market anxieties. Analysts suggest this price action may influence trading strategies, particularly in balancing gold positions with USD-denominated assets.  

Technical watchers highlighted that sustaining levels near $4,700 could signal further upside potential, though volatility remains elevated amid ongoing macroeconomic data releases. Market participants are also tracking exchange rates closely, as any shifts in US or Iranian policy could ripple through forex markets. Overall, gold’s upward momentum underscores the interplay between geopolitical developments, currency movements, and commodity trading dynamics.
