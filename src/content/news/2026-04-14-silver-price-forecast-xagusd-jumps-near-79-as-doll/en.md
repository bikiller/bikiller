Silver (XAG/USD) rallied sharply on Tuesday, trading near $78.80 as investors responded to a weaker US dollar. The currency pair climbed 4.16% on the day, reaching an intraday high of $79.32. Traders cited strong buying interest fueled by softer-than-expected US inflation data, which undermined the dollar and boosted demand for safe-haven assets like silver. The exchange rate movement reflected a shift in sentiment toward precious metals amid concerns over the greenback’s near-term strength.  

Market participants noted that the USD’s recent pullback against major currencies, including the EUR and JPY, created favorable conditions for commodities priced in dollars. As silver approached the $79 technical level, trading activity intensified, highlighting momentum-driven buying alongside longer-term hedging strategies. Analysts suggest that the metal’s gains may attract further speculative inflows if the dollar remains under pressure, with XAG/USD potentially testing higher resistance points in the coming sessions.  

The surge in silver comes amid broader forex market volatility, where currency pairs are reacting to macroeconomic signals. A softer USD often translates into increased appeal for dollar-denominated assets, supporting both trading volumes and price momentum. Investors tracking XAG/USD are monitoring key levels and sentiment shifts closely, as movements in the greenback continue to influence trading strategies and market positioning across metals and major forex pairs.
