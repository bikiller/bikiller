EUR/USD continued its downward trend on Thursday, slipping below the 1.1550 mark for the third consecutive session. The currency pair was trading around 1.1540 during the Asian session, reflecting ongoing pressure from the strengthening US Dollar. The move comes amid growing concerns over rising inflationary risks, which are prompting traders to adjust their positions in favor of the USD.

The US Dollar's recent gains are linked to mounting expectations of tighter monetary policy from the Federal Reserve. With inflation showing persistent strength, market participants are increasingly pricing in the likelihood of more aggressive interest rate hikes in the coming months. As a result, EUR/USD remains vulnerable to further downside, with technical support around 1.1500 now under scrutiny.
