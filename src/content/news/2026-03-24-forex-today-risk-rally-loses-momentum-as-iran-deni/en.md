Risk appetite in global markets faltered Tuesday as Iran denied engaging in talks with the United States, dampening optimism for geopolitical easing. In forex trading, the USD edged higher against major peers, with the EUR/USD slipping to 1.0845 amid safe-haven flows. The Japanese yen also gained, with USD/JPY retreating to 133.20, reflecting renewed caution in risk-sensitive assets and a temporary pause in the recent risk rally.  

Commodity-linked currencies saw mixed action, with the AUD/USD holding at 0.6670, pressured by weaker risk sentiment, while the NZD/USD dipped to 0.6275. Technical analysts noted key support levels: EUR/USD at 1.0820 and USD/JPY at 132.80, suggesting further downside if selling persists. Traders cited the lack of diplomatic progress and a cautious tone in US Treasury yields as weighing on speculative positioning.  

The greenback maintained broad strength, with the USD index climbing to 103.65, supported by expectations of continued Fed tightening. Market participants remain focused on economic data and central bank guidance, watching whether risk appetite can revive or if safe-haven flows will dominate. Volatility is likely to remain elevated as geopolitical uncertainty continues to shape currency pair movements.
