West Texas Intermediate (WTI) held above $92.00 early Friday in Asian trading, showing a mild uptick but little momentum. Market participants remain cautious as the Strait of Hormuz stays closed, limiting supply and supporting crude prices. Traders are weighing geopolitical risks against hopes of a potential Iran ceasefire, which could ease tension and pressure oil prices lower. The USD shows mixed performance, with the dollar index trading near recent highs.  

In forex markets, the EUR/USD pair dipped slightly as risk sentiment stayed muted, while USD/JPY found support around 134.50 amid safe-haven flows. Commodity-linked currencies such as AUD and CAD tracked oil movements closely, with the CAD/USD pairing edging higher in line with the crude rebound. Exchange rates reflected a careful balance between energy-driven inflation concerns and broader global growth uncertainty.  

Technically, WTI remains above key support near $91.50, but bulls have yet to push through resistance around $93.00. Traders note that a prolonged closure of the Strait could sustain volatility, influencing energy-linked currencies and USD trading behavior. Analysts suggest monitoring crude futures alongside currency pairs for signals of market shifts, with risk sentiment and geopolitical headlines likely to drive short-term trading decisions.
