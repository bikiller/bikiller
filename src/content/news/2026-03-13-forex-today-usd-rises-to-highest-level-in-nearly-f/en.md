The US dollar climbed to its strongest level in nearly four months on Friday, driven by renewed concerns over inflation. The USD surged against major currencies, with the EUR/USD pair slipping to 1.0850, marking a 0.4% decline, while USD/JPY touched 133.20. Traders cited mounting worries that persistent price pressures could prompt the Federal Reserve to maintain a hawkish stance, keeping interest rates elevated longer than anticipated.  

Market participants also reacted to mixed economic signals from Europe and Asia. The euro faced pressure as softer-than-expected German and French data raised doubts about the region’s recovery, contributing to the EUR/USD slide. Meanwhile, the Japanese yen weakened amid rising US yields, supporting USD/JPY gains. Analysts noted that momentum may continue if inflation expectations persist, influencing currency pair trends and trading strategies in the short term.  

Technically, USD/JPY now tests resistance around 133.50, while EUR/USD looks poised for further downside toward the 1.0800 support. Traders are closely monitoring US consumer price data and Fed commentary for clues on future monetary policy, which could dictate the next leg in USD movements. With volatility high, forex markets are bracing for swift reactions across major currency pairs.
