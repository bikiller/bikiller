Gold price (XAU/USD) climbed to a weekly high during Asian trading hours on Monday, extending gains in the currency pair after the United States and Iran reportedly reached a peace agreement to end their conflict. The development eased concerns over inflation pressures and the outlook for higher interest rates, supporting broader trading sentiment in precious metals.

Market sentiment improved as easing geopolitical risk reduced safe-haven flows, yet gold maintained upward momentum as traders reassessed US dollar strength and interest rate expectations. The XAU/USD exchange rate benefited from softer yield assumptions, with reduced inflation fears potentially limiting aggressive Federal Reserve policy tightening. Meanwhile, USD positioning weakened modestly against major currencies during early trading.

Looking ahead, forex markets will focus on whether the improved risk environment persists and how it influences USD demand across major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Traders are also watching upcoming central bank commentary for signals on interest rates, which could determine whether gold sustains its rebound or faces renewed pressure in the trading session.
