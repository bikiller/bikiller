Nordea expects the USD/JPY currency pair to remain elevated as persistent US–Japan yield differentials continue to dominate trading flows. The exchange rate stays supported by a strong USD and structurally low Japanese yields, keeping the yen under pressure in global forex markets. Despite occasional pullbacks, overall momentum in FX trading continues to favor upside in USD/JPY in the near term.

Policy divergence between the Federal Reserve and the Bank of Japan remains a key driver, with the BoJ still maintaining a highly accommodative stance. While gradual normalization is expected from the central bank, Nordea notes it is too modest to materially strengthen the Japanese yen or reverse USD/JPY gains in the short term.

Looking ahead, sustained yield differentials could keep the USD supported against the yen, reinforcing carry trade strategies and broader USD strength across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD. For forex traders, the environment suggests continued volatility but with bias toward a firmer USD/JPY exchange rate unless policy expectations shift meaningfully in Japan or the US.
