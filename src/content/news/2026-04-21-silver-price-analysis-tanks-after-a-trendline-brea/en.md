Silver price plunged nearly 3.50% on Tuesday, breaking below a key support trendline after forming a doji candlestick in the previous session. The move signaled a sharp shift in trading momentum as sellers dominated the market. The breakdown in the precious metals complex came amid broader volatility in USD-denominated assets.

Market sentiment weakened as uncertainty over US-Iran talks persisted, with a ceasefire nearing expiration adding further pressure on safe-haven demand. Precious metals, including silver, extended losses as risk appetite shifted across global markets. Volatility also spilled into major currency pairs, influencing USD and EUR exchange rate movements and broader trading flows in commodities and forex.

Looking ahead, the break of the trendline leaves silver vulnerable to deeper downside risk, with traders now eyeing the $70 level as the next key reference point. Continued USD strength could amplify pressure on commodities, while forex markets monitor EUR/USD and other currency pairs for spillover effects. The shift underscores tightening conditions in global trading environments.
