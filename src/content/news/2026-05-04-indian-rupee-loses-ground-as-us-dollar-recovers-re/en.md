The Indian rupee slipped against the US dollar on Monday as the greenback recovered from recent losses amid ongoing geopolitical uncertainty. The USD/INR currency pair edged higher for the second consecutive session, trading around the 95.00 mark during Asian hours. Investors appeared cautious ahead of key economic data, while risk-off sentiment supported the dollar’s rebound across emerging market currencies.  

Market participants reacted to the latest HSBC Manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) for India, which influenced trading in the domestic currency. Though the PMI signaled steady manufacturing activity, concerns over global tensions and potential capital outflows kept the rupee under pressure. The USD/INR exchange rate now hovers near resistance levels, suggesting that short-term volatility could persist if the geopolitical backdrop remains unsettled.  

The broader forex market mirrored these dynamics, with the USD gaining against the euro, yen, and other major currencies. Traders are watching whether the rupee can stabilize or if the dollar’s strength continues to dominate, shaping flows in emerging markets. Analysts note that the combination of domestic data releases and external uncertainty will likely dictate near-term trends for the USD/INR pair and other Asian currencies.
