Silver (XAG/USD) climbed toward the $76.00 per troy ounce mark during Asian trading on Friday, extending its recent rally. The currency pair gained momentum as investors reassessed the outlook for U.S. interest rates, with easing bets on rate hikes fueling demand for safe-haven assets like silver. The precious metal’s upward trajectory reflects a broader shift in market sentiment toward risk-sensitive and dollar-linked commodities.  

Traders noted that the softer USD supported the advance in XAG/USD, while cross-currency flows, particularly against the EUR, added further impetus. Technical charts show the pair holding key support near $75.50, with $76.00 acting as an immediate resistance level. Momentum indicators suggest potential for continued trading strength if the USD remains under pressure and global economic data fail to reinforce hawkish Federal Reserve signals.  

Market watchers highlighted that the silver rally could influence broader commodity-linked currency pairs and emerging market flows, as investors rotate between metals and FX positions. With volatility in U.S. Treasury yields and inflation data shaping expectations, traders remain attentive to shifts in USD exchange rates, which could either sustain or temper silver’s gains in the near term. The interplay between interest rate sentiment and currency movements remains central to XAG/USD dynamics.
