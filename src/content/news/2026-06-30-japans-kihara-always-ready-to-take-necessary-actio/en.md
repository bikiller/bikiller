Kihara reiterated during a regular press briefing that Japanese authorities are always ready to take necessary action in the foreign exchange market. He avoided commenting on specific levels, but the remarks reinforced ongoing monitoring of yen currency pairs such as USD/JPY, as officials watch for excessive moves in the exchange rate environment.

Market participants continue to track policy signals as the yen trades across major currency pairs, including USD and EUR crosses, amid concerns over rapid exchange rate fluctuations. Kihara’s comments are seen as a reminder that authorities are prepared to respond if trading becomes disorderly, keeping traders cautious in short-term positioning.

While no specific exchange rate thresholds were mentioned, the stance underscores the risk of intervention if volatility intensifies. Forex traders are likely to stay alert to movements in USD/JPY and broader dollar yen dynamics, as well as EUR-linked currency pair flows, adjusting trading strategies amid uncertainty in global USD strength and policy divergence.
