The Dow Jones Industrial Average reversed an early slide on Thursday, jumping from a session low near 49,700 to finish sharply higher after news of a potential US-Iran agreement surfaced. Investors had been pressured by firmer oil prices and rising Treasury yields, which kept the USD firm against most major currency pairs, with EUR/USD trading cautiously near key technical support levels. Trading sentiment shifted quickly as risk appetite returned.

In equities, the sudden uptick in the Dow supported broader market rallies, with investors reassessing positions amid expectations that geopolitical tensions could ease. The move spurred activity in currency markets, as USD/JPY and GBP/USD reacted to the news-driven optimism, highlighting the sensitivity of exchange rates to macro and geopolitical developments. Traders eyed whether the Fed’s static stance on interest rates might face market pressure if economic conditions stabilize.

Analysts note that while the headlines ignited trading momentum, the market’s response underscores the interplay between equity indices, commodities, and forex flows. A confirmed deal could reinforce risk-on behavior, potentially boosting EUR, AUD, and other commodity-linked currencies, while keeping the USD under pressure. Market participants remain alert to volatility, technical resistance near 50,000 on the Dow, and the next directional cues for major currency pairs.
