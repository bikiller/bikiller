Gold prices (XAU/USD) continue to face pressure, dropping below $4,650 during the early Asian session on Friday. The precious metal's decline is fueled by concerns over rising inflation, as crude oil and energy prices soar. The US-Israeli conflict, particularly its potential impact on Iran, has exacerbated these fears, prompting investors to reassess risk in the market.

The stronger inflation outlook has led to a shift in market sentiment, with traders cautious about holding gold, typically seen as a hedge against inflation. As crude oil prices surge, market participants are revisiting expectations for interest rate hikes, particularly in the US. This shift in focus is driving gold lower, with the metal struggling to recover amid growing concerns over liquidity and broader economic risks.
