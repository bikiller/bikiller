Silver (XAG/USD) edged lower on Tuesday, slipping nearly 2% to around $79, even as the US Dollar softened against major currency pairs. The retreat came despite falling US Treasury yields, which typically support precious metals. Traders noted that higher crude oil prices added pressure on silver, offsetting safe-haven demand amid improving risk appetite in global markets.  

The XAG/USD exchange rate reflects growing sensitivity to broader commodity trends, with oil-driven inflation concerns weighing on precious metals. Over the week, silver is down 1.81%, highlighting persistent volatility in trading despite a weaker USD. Market participants are closely monitoring whether this trend will continue or if a rebound is likely as Treasury yields and currency flows stabilize.  

Analysts suggest that silver’s decline could influence EUR/USD and other commodity-linked currency pairs, as shifts in risk sentiment ripple through forex markets. Investors are balancing positions between safe-haven metals and higher-yielding assets, making the XAG/USD pair a barometer for broader market sentiment. With trading volumes steady, the short-term outlook remains sensitive to crude oil fluctuations and USD movements.
