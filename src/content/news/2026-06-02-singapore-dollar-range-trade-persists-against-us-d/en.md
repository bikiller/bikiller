The USD/SGD currency pair maintained a firm tone on Monday, closing near 1.2788 after fluctuating between 1.2759 and 1.2803. Market participants noted that a combination of a stronger US Dollar and a steady Singapore Dollar Nominal Effective Exchange Rate (NEER) underpinned the trading range. Analysts from UOB highlighted that the pair continued to exhibit limited directional momentum, reflecting ongoing range-bound behavior.

The Singapore Dollar's resilience against the US Dollar suggests that local factors, including stable economic indicators and NEER support, are keeping the exchange rate anchored. Traders observed that while USD strength persists amid global market cues, the SGD’s stability provides a buffer against abrupt swings, resulting in relatively narrow intraday movements between established technical levels.

Looking ahead, market watchers expect USD/SGD to continue its range-trading pattern unless disrupted by significant macroeconomic events or shifts in USD dynamics. The current setup indicates that the currency pair could remain confined between 1.2750 and 1.2805 in the short term, making it a key focus for forex trading strategies. Both retail and institutional participants are likely monitoring these levels closely, as any breach could signal potential directional moves.
