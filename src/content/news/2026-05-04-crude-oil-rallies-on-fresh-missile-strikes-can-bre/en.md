Crude oil surged on Monday, fueled by renewed geopolitical tensions after fresh missile strikes rattled markets. Brent climbed sharply from last week’s dip, pushing toward the $115 mark, while WTI followed suit. Traders reacted quickly, with speculative positions in energy-linked currency pairs like USD/CAD and USD/NOK seeing heightened activity as the dollar strengthened against major peers amid safe-haven flows.  

The rally lifted Brent above key technical resistance at $112.50, prompting analysts to eye $115 as a potential short-term target. The advance also weighed on currencies in oil-importing regions, with EUR/USD slipping toward 1.0850 and GBP/USD testing support near 1.2450. Market participants cited supply concerns and rising risk premiums as drivers, while hedging activity in futures and options added to the volatility.  

Forex traders monitored the exchange rate shifts closely, noting that the USD gained against commodity-linked currencies, reflecting heightened demand for dollar liquidity. Oil exporters’ currencies, including CAD and NOK, strengthened on the back of surging crude, while equity indices and risk-sensitive pairs experienced choppy trading. With Brent momentum building, investors are assessing implications for inflation, central bank policy, and broader market positioning.
