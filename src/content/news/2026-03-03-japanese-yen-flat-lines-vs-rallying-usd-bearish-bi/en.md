The USD/JPY currency pair continues to trade within a narrow range during the early European session on Tuesday. Spot prices hover just below the five-week high reached on Monday, holding steady around the mid-157.00s. With little movement on the day, the pair remains trapped in a consolidative phase as investors weigh upcoming developments.

Market sentiment remains skewed to the downside for the yen, with expectations of delayed rate hikes from the Bank of Japan (BoJ) contributing to bearish pressure. The lack of a clear policy shift from the BoJ amid rising U.S. rates keeps the yen vulnerable. This dynamic, coupled with stronger-than-expected U.S. economic data, continues to weigh on the yen’s outlook, keeping the USD/JPY pair supported.
