AUD/JPY held near the 111.00 level on Thursday, trading around 110.90 during Asian hours after three consecutive days of declines. The Australian Dollar showed resilience following supportive remarks from RBA’s Kent, helping the currency pair stabilize against the Japanese Yen. Market participants noted that the exchange rate’s sideways movement reflects cautious sentiment amid mixed global risk appetite.  

Traders highlighted that AUD’s strength was partly offset by a firm USD, which continues to influence broader forex flows. The pair’s technical levels suggest consolidation, with immediate resistance near 111.20 and support around 110.70. Analysts pointed out that short-term trading may remain range-bound as investors digest RBA signals alongside ongoing global economic data, including US inflation metrics and EUR performance in European sessions.  

Market implications suggest that any further commentary from RBA or shifts in risk sentiment could drive the next leg of AUD/JPY movement. With the Yen remaining sensitive to global risk trends, traders are closely monitoring USD and EUR dynamics, positioning cautiously for potential breakouts. Momentum indicators imply that the currency pair could test 111.50 on a positive risk swing or retreat toward 110.50 if safe-haven flows intensify.
