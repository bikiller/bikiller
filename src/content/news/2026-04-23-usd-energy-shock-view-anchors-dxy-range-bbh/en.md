Brown Brothers Harriman (BBH) strategist Elias Haddad notes that firmer crude oil prices alongside rising global bond yields are providing support for the US dollar. The USD has strengthened against a basket of major currencies, as the dollar index (DXY) remains broadly range-bound but biased higher. Energy-market dynamics continue to shape short-term FX sentiment across major currency pairs.

In spot forex trading, the firmer USD has left major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD under modest pressure, with investors reassessing relative rate differentials. Higher yields are reinforcing the dollar’s yield advantage, while commodity-linked currencies show mixed performance. Overall exchange rate moves remain contained, but directional bias favors USD strength in risk-sensitive sessions.

BBH highlights that the “energy shock” narrative is helping anchor the DXY within its current trading range rather than triggering a breakout. Persistent oil strength and elevated yields are likely to keep volatility elevated across FX markets. Traders are closely watching macro signals, as USD resilience continues to hinge on energy costs and global bond market dynamics.
