The GBP/USD exchange rate was supported by a hawkish tilt from the Bank of England (BoE) as it surprised markets with a unanimous decision to hold rates while signaling readiness to act against inflationary pressures. The BoE’s strong guidance spurred expectations of additional tightening by year-end, prompting the market to price in more aggressive rate hikes. 

Despite the market's optimism, ING analysts, including Francesco Pesole, caution that this tightening outlook may be overly ambitious. While the pound initially surged, the outlook for GBP/USD remains tethered to broader market sentiment, particularly any shifts in USD strength and global risk sentiment. As traders continue to digest the BoE’s stance, the pair’s near-term trading may face resistance at higher levels.
