The Australian dollar remained under scrutiny as inflation risks keep the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) cautious. RBA Governor Michele Bullock emphasized a projected 75 basis point increase in the cash rate this year, aimed at returning inflation to target. Despite prior tightening, inflation re-accelerated in late 2025, fueled by robust economic growth, a tight labor market, and rising oil prices, pressuring the AUD/USD and AUD/EUR exchange rates.  

Market participants are watching how these dynamics influence trading sentiment. The prospect of higher rates has supported the Australian dollar, with the AUD/USD hovering near key technical levels, while currency pairs against the euro and yen reflect cautious positioning. Traders are weighing the balance between interest rate expectations and inflation pressures, with AUD liquidity responding to both domestic and global risk factors.  

Looking ahead, sustained inflation above target could keep the RBA on an aggressive path, potentially driving further volatility in AUD pairs. Investors are closely monitoring oil price trends and labor market data, which could shift short-term trading strategies. The combination of tightening monetary policy and persistent inflation signals a delicate environment for forex traders, with the Australian dollar likely to react sharply to any shifts in RBA guidance or global economic cues.
