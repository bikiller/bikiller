Sterling received a rare boost from positive domestic data on Tuesday, prompting renewed buying interest in the GBP/USD currency pair. The exchange rate climbed from just below 1.3350 to slightly above 1.3400 during the session, as traders reacted to improving sentiment around the UK economy. The move briefly strengthened the Pound against the USD and outperformed several major peers, including the EUR, in early trading.

However, the rally quickly ran into a familiar technical barrier. GBP/USD tested the 200-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA) near the 1.3400 area, a level that has repeatedly capped upside attempts in recent months. After touching the key resistance zone, the currency pair struggled to attract follow-through buying and gradually surrendered gains through the remainder of the session.

The price action highlights the market’s ongoing caution toward Sterling despite occasional signs of domestic resilience. While the positive news flow helped lift the Pound in the short term, traders remain reluctant to push the exchange rate decisively higher without stronger evidence of sustained economic momentum. For now, the 200-day EMA continues to act as a significant technical ceiling, leaving GBP/USD vulnerable to further consolidation as USD demand and broader market sentiment continue to influence trading conditions.
