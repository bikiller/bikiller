WTI Crude Oil prices are struggling to maintain momentum near the $82.00 level after a brief recovery from lows below $76.00. During the Asian session on Wednesday, the commodity faced renewed selling pressure, dropping nearly 4% on the day. The current resistance around $82.00 is significant as traders await further direction, with global geopolitical tensions, including the ongoing Iran conflict, weighing on market sentiment.

Technically, the 200-hour moving average remains a crucial support level for bulls, and a sustained hold above this mark could indicate a potential rebound. If WTI Crude breaks below this support, the price may retest the lower levels seen earlier in the week. The volatility in oil markets continues to impact currency pairs like the USD/JPY and EUR/USD, as fluctuations in energy prices influence broader market sentiment and USD strength.
