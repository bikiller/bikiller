Gold (XAU/USD) remains under pressure on Friday, extending its decline toward a third consecutive weekly loss as traders weigh developments in the US-Iran peace deal alongside the Federal Reserve’s hawkish monetary policy outlook. Strength in the USD and rising rate expectations continue to weigh on the currency pair, reinforcing a broader bearish sentiment across precious metals trading.

At the time of writing, XAU/USD trades around $4,150 after sliding from intraday highs, with sellers briefly pushing the metal to a one-week low of $4,121. The currency pair shows heightened volatility as USD strength and shifting bond yields influence trading conditions, limiting upside momentum despite occasional corrective rebounds during the session as investors reassess Fed rate trajectory and global risk sentiment remains fragile.

Market participants note that the gold exchange rate remains sensitive to Federal Reserve rate bets, with higher-for-longer expectations dampening demand for non-yielding assets. Easing geopolitical tensions from the US-Iran peace deal have also reduced safe-haven flows, leaving XAU/USD vulnerable to further downside pressure if USD strength persists into next week’s trading sessions while traders eye key US data releases for direction.
