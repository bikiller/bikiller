The US dollar remains under close market scrutiny as traders anticipate the Federal Reserve’s next move. Brown Brothers Harriman’s Elias Haddad highlights expectations that the Fed will maintain the federal funds rate at 3.50%-3.75%, with attention turning to the vote split, the dot plot, and Chair Powell’s commentary. Investors are weighing the potential impact on currency pairs such as USD/EUR and USD/JPY, amid lingering inflation pressures.  

Recent trading shows the USD holding steady against major currencies, though volatility has risen ahead of the announcement. Exchange rates have fluctuated as traders factor in a possible hawkish shift if inflation data surprises to the upside. EUR/USD, in particular, has tested support near 1.0800, while USD/JPY is approaching key resistance around 150.50, reflecting caution across the forex market.  

Analysts suggest that Fed signals could drive near-term trends, with market participants monitoring whether Powell’s tone signals further rate hikes or a pause. Technical levels are likely to guide trading, as the USD remains sensitive to macroeconomic data and geopolitical developments. A clear message from the central bank may reshape short-term currency movements, influencing both spot and derivatives markets in the coming sessions.
