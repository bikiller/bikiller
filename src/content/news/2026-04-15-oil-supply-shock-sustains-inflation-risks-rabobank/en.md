Oil prices remain under pressure due to persistent supply constraints, with a notable impact on inflation risks. Despite a slight improvement in overall risk sentiment, the physical oil market presents a different scenario. Crude oil prices continue to stay elevated, contributing to concerns about rising costs across multiple sectors. As of now, WTI crude hovers around $84 per barrel, signaling potential volatility ahead.

This supply shock has broader implications for the forex market, particularly the USD. With inflation risks on the rise, the USD remains supported against major pairs like EUR/USD, which struggles to break above the 1.0950 resistance level. Traders are closely watching these developments, with the outlook for oil potentially influencing the Fed’s future rate decisions, thus adding complexity to USD trading strategies.
