Euro came under renewed selling pressure in forex trading on Thursday, with the EUR/USD currency pair sliding to fresh two-month lows at 1.1478 against the US Dollar. The move reflected a firming USD and fading risk appetite, as the earlier optimism surrounding the US-Iran peace deal proved short-lived in global exchange rate markets.

Weak Eurozone economic prospects continued to weigh on the common currency, while growing expectations of further Federal Reserve rate hikes strengthened the USD outlook. The policy divergence between the ECB and the Fed kept the currency pair under sustained bearish pressure, encouraging traders to favor dollar-denominated positions in the forex market.

Implications for trading remain cautious, with EUR/USD likely to remain volatile around key support levels after breaking 1.1478. Market participants are closely watching US data and central bank signals, as sustained USD strength could extend losses for the euro in near-term exchange rate dynamics. Traders may continue positioning for dollar strength as rate differentials remain supportive, limiting recovery attempts in the euro against the greenback.
