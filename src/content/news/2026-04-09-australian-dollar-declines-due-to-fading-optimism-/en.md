The Australian Dollar (AUD) experienced a decline on Thursday, halting its three-day winning streak against the US Dollar (USD). As trading resumed in the Asian session, the AUD/USD currency pair dropped to around 0.7030, reflecting a shift in market sentiment. The decline was driven by fading optimism surrounding the US-Iran ceasefire, which had initially boosted risk appetite across global markets.

The market’s focus shifted away from the potential easing of geopolitical tensions as investors began to question the sustainability of the ceasefire. This led to a pullback in the Aussie’s gains, with the exchange rate for AUD/USD slipping below the critical 0.7050 mark. As traders assessed the evolving situation, the weakening of risk sentiment provided further support to the USD, contributing to the downtrend in the pair.
