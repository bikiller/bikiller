Gold prices jumped sharply as a temporary lull in Middle East tensions lifted market sentiment. Following a two-week ceasefire between the US and Iran, gold surged more than 2%, climbing past the $4,800 mark. Traders cited reduced immediate escalation risks as a key driver, with demand for safe-haven assets softening amid calmer geopolitical conditions. This spike has sparked active trading in USD-linked commodities, while the USD itself saw modest retracement against major pairs like EUR/USD.  

The ceasefire’s impact extended to forex markets, influencing exchange rates and investor positioning. The rise in gold coincided with slight weakness in the USD, prompting traders to reassess exposure across currencies. EUR/USD and other major pairs experienced modest volatility as market participants balanced geopolitical relief against ongoing macroeconomic concerns. Technical levels around $4,800 now act as a psychological resistance for gold, shaping short-term trading strategies.  

Analysts note that while the ceasefire provides temporary relief, markets remain alert to shifts in the Strait of Hormuz and other flashpoints. Any renewed tensions could quickly reverse recent gains, impacting both commodities and currency pairs. Traders are closely monitoring liquidity flows and hedge positions, particularly in USD, as the interplay between gold and forex markets signals potential shifts in risk appetite and portfolio adjustments over the coming weeks.
