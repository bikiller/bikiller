WTI crude oil slipped below the $68.00 mark on Thursday, extending its recent decline after reports that Qatari mediators had seen “positive progress” in indirect US-Iran peace talks held in Doha on Tuesday. The prospect of improving diplomatic relations raised expectations that global crude supplies could increase, prompting fresh selling during trading and weighing on broader commodity sentiment.

The move in oil prices also drew attention across the forex market, where traders monitored the potential impact on the USD exchange rate and major currency pair performance. Lower energy prices can influence inflation expectations and central bank outlooks, making crude oil an important driver for USD, EUR, and other major currencies. Market participants continued to assess whether the latest diplomatic developments could alter the balance between energy demand and future supply.

While WTI remained under pressure below $68.00, investors stayed focused on incoming geopolitical headlines for confirmation that negotiations are moving toward a lasting agreement. Any further progress could reinforce expectations of additional Iranian oil returning to global markets, keeping pressure on crude prices. At the same time, volatility in commodity trading may continue to shape sentiment across currency pairs, with the USD exchange rate and broader risk appetite likely to remain closely linked to developments in the energy market.
