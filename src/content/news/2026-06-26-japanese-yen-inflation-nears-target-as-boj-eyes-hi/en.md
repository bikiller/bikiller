Japanese inflation dynamics in Greater Tokyo are edging higher, with headline readings now hovering just below the 2% target, according to Commerzbank’s Volkmar Baur. The development provides a firmer backdrop for the Japanese yen, as currency markets reassess Bank of Japan normalization prospects. Expectations of policy tightening are gradually returning to focus in USD/JPY and EUR/JPY trading.

Limited second-round effects from higher oil prices and signs of broader structural normalization are helping reinforce the case for continued Bank of Japan policy adjustment. With inflation stabilizing closer to target, policymakers may find more room to consider additional rate hikes, supporting the yen through interest rate differentials versus USD and other majors.

Overall FX markets are increasingly sensitive to Japanese data surprises, with yen strength likely to persist if inflation remains near target. Traders are closely watching BoJ communication for signals on the timing of the next move, while USD/JPY volatility and broader G10 currency pair flows remain tied to policy divergence expectations.
