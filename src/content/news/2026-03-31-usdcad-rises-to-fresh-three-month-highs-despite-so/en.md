USD/CAD climbed to fresh three-month highs on Tuesday, trading around 1.3960 as the Canadian Dollar extended its decline for a seventh straight session against the US Dollar. Despite a softer Greenback, the pair maintained upward momentum, reflecting persistent pressure on the loonie amid weaker domestic sentiment. Traders noted that the exchange rate is now hovering near levels last seen in December 2025, signaling sustained demand for USD.  

Market participants linked the CAD’s slide to ongoing concerns over commodity-linked currency vulnerabilities and slower-than-expected Canadian economic data. Meanwhile, USD gains were muted, but the currency pair’s technical strength drew attention from short-term traders monitoring resistance near the 1.3960 mark. Analysts suggest that the trading dynamic may continue to favor the US Dollar unless supportive data emerge for the Canadian economy.  

The move in USD/CAD highlights broader trends in forex markets, where shifts in risk sentiment and central bank signals influence trading flows. Investors are also keeping an eye on EUR/USD and other major pairs, assessing whether momentum in the Greenback may spill over across currencies. With the loonie under pressure, market watchers anticipate further volatility and potential tests of key technical levels in the near term.
