Gold (XAU/USD) reclaimed the $4,500 level on Tuesday during early Asian trading, but momentum remains tepid. Traders remain cautious as the USD shows signs of weakness amid a ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah, which has temporarily eased geopolitical tensions. Despite the psychological milestone, buying pressure is moderate, and gold is struggling to maintain a clear bullish trajectory.  

The currency pair’s exchange rate reflects a mixed market sentiment. The USD’s pullback against major currencies like the EUR and JPY has lent support to precious metals, while risk-on sentiment in equities has limited upside. Analysts note that technical levels near $4,500 could act as short-term resistance, with traders watching for a decisive break above this zone to confirm further gains.  

Looking ahead, market participants remain vigilant. Any reversal in Middle East developments or a shift in USD strength could quickly test gold’s gains. Traders continue to monitor key forex indicators and trading volumes, assessing whether XAU/USD can sustain above $4,500 or retreat toward support levels around $4,480–$4,490. The interplay between geopolitical catalysts and currency pair fluctuations will likely dictate near-term market dynamics.
