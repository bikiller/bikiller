EUR/USD pared part of its earlier losses on Wednesday as the currency pair found support after softer-than-expected US economic data and remarks from Kevin Warsh weighed on the US Dollar. The exchange rate recovered intraday, reflecting renewed EUR demand as USD weakness dominated trading sentiment across major FX markets. The move highlights sensitivity to US macro surprises and Fed-related commentary.

Softer-than-expected US economic data reinforced expectations of easing growth momentum, pressuring the US Dollar and lifting the EUR/USD exchange rate. Traders noted that weaker indicators reduced confidence in near-term USD strength, prompting repositioning in forex trading desks. The currency pair benefited as EUR stability improved against a broadly softer dollar backdrop.

Looking ahead, EUR/USD trading remains sensitive to incoming US data and central bank signals, with volatility expected to persist in forex markets. If USD weakness continues, the euro could maintain its recovery momentum, though resistance levels may cap gains. Market participants will closely monitor EUR demand and Fed commentary for further direction.
