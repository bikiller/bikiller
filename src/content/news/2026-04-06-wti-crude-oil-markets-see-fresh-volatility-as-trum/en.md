WTI Crude Oil prices experienced significant volatility on Monday as traders reacted to the looming deadline set by former President Donald Trump. Early in the day, May futures surged to $115 per barrel, driven by heightened uncertainty. However, this spike was short-lived, with prices retracting to around $112, ultimately closing roughly flat compared to Thursday’s settlement.

The price fluctuations highlighted a divergence between the spot and futures markets, reflecting the ongoing market instability. While the USD remains a key driver in oil trading, the potential for further volatility depends on geopolitical developments and any policy changes tied to the deadline. The EUR/USD exchange rate remains sensitive to broader risk sentiment, with oil price moves likely to influence the broader forex market in the coming days.
