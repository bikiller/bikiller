The US Dollar Index (DXY) dropped below the key 100 mark on Monday, reaching 99.10 as market sentiment shifted in favor of risk-on assets. This decline came as investors responded positively to signals from former President Donald Trump, suggesting a potential de-escalation in tensions with Iran. The dollar's retreat was further fueled by steady yields and a tempered outlook for Federal Reserve policy, providing little support for the greenback.

In forex markets, the EUR/USD pair saw a modest rebound, benefiting from the dollar’s weakness. The improved risk appetite, coupled with concerns about the Fed's cautious stance, contributed to the euro’s gains. Meanwhile, oil prices also fell, adding to the broader risk-on trend. Traders are now closely watching any developments in US-Iran relations, as further de-escalation could significantly influence the USD's trajectory.
