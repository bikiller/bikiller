The British Pound faced pressure on Thursday, as GBP/USD struggled to maintain its ground below the 1.33 mark. The pair opened the session near 1.3300 but experienced a steady decline throughout the day. By the close, it was trading around 1.3220, reflecting a 0.65% drop. The ongoing uncertainty surrounding the Bank of England's (BoE) rate decision weighed on market sentiment, fueling the Pound's weakness against the US Dollar.

This decline highlights the impact of the BoE's rate dilemma on the currency pair. Traders remain cautious, with the exchange rate caught below key technical levels, including 1.33. The market is closely monitoring potential shifts in monetary policy, which could trigger further volatility. As the BoE navigates its next steps, GBP/USD's movement will likely remain constrained, and the pair may continue to face downward pressure unless a clearer direction emerges.
