Gold prices retreated on Tuesday as renewed US military strikes in southern Iran boosted demand for the USD and lifted Oil prices, reducing appetite for safe-haven metals despite ongoing diplomatic negotiations. The XAU/USD currency pair slipped toward $4,525 after touching an intraday high of $4,580, with traders closely monitoring geopolitical risks and broader exchange rate movements across major forex markets.

The rebound in the US Dollar added further pressure on bullion, as stronger USD trading conditions typically weigh on Gold by making the metal more expensive for holders of other currencies, including the EUR. Market participants also reacted to rising energy prices, which reinforced expectations that inflation risks could remain elevated if tensions in the Middle East continue to disrupt supply chains and global trade sentiment.

In the broader forex market, investors remain cautious as uncertainty surrounding the US-Iran conflict continues to drive volatility across commodities and currency pairs. Analysts note that sustained USD strength could keep XAU/USD under pressure in the near term, although persistent geopolitical instability may still provide underlying support for Gold if risk sentiment deteriorates further.
