The US Dollar (USD) is maintaining its safe-haven status as tensions in the Middle East intensify, driving heightened investor anxiety. As geopolitical concerns rise, the USD gains momentum against major currencies, with the EUR/USD exchange rate dipping to 1.0520, a near two-month low. The dollar’s strength is bolstered by its perceived stability amid global instability, keeping risk-sensitive assets under pressure.

The escalation in the Middle East has prompted traders to flock to the USD, traditionally seen as a refuge in times of uncertainty. The USD/JPY pair continues to see upward movement, holding steady around the 149.30 mark. Meanwhile, the GBP/USD struggles, with the pair testing support near 1.2100 as investors seek safer alternatives. The ongoing geopolitical risk is expected to keep the USD supported in the short term, with analysts eyeing any further developments for market direction.
