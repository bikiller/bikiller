TD Securities strategists Jayati Bharadwaj and Linda Cheng suggest that the US Dollar (USD) is regaining its status as a safe-haven currency amidst ongoing geopolitical tensions. The current market shock has shifted investor sentiment, leading to fluctuations in various currency pairs. As traders assess risks, the USD’s strength is becoming increasingly prominent, particularly against the euro (EUR) and other major currencies.

In this environment, the exchange rate for USD/EUR has been influenced by rising conflict premiums, prompting traders to reconsider traditional safe-haven dynamics. Although the USD is no longer an automatic choice for risk aversion, its role as a refuge currency is re-emerging. As the market reacts, it will be crucial for investors to monitor the implications of these shifts, watching key technical levels and potential reversals in other major pairs.
