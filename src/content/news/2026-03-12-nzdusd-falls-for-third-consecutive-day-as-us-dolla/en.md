NZD/USD extended its losing streak on Thursday, slipping for a third consecutive session as the US Dollar regained ground amid lingering inflation concerns. The currency pair traded near 0.5860 at the time of writing, down 0.90% on the day. Traders noted that renewed USD strength weighed heavily on the Kiwi, overshadowing any domestic support from New Zealand’s economic data.  

Market participants cited rising geopolitical tensions and persistent inflationary pressures in the US as key drivers behind the dollar’s resilience. This environment pushed investors toward safe-haven assets, with the USD benefiting across major currency pairs, including against the EUR and JPY. The exchange rate’s decline highlights the sensitivity of commodity-linked currencies like the NZD to global risk sentiment.  

Technical analysts suggest that continued USD strength could test support levels around 0.5840, while a recovery above 0.5900 may signal a pause in the Kiwi’s decline. Traders remain attentive to upcoming US inflation data and central bank commentary, as any shift in monetary policy expectations could trigger renewed volatility in the NZD/USD pair and broader forex markets.
