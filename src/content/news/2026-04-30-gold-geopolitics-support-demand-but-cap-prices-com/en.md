Gold has come under pressure despite geopolitical tensions providing some support, according to Commerzbank strategists. The metal briefly slipped below the USD 4,500 level as traders reacted to rising Oil prices and evolving expectations for US interest rates. The combination of stronger commodity costs and shifting monetary policy signals has created a tug-of-war in the gold market, influencing both trading sentiment and short-term positioning.  

The USD has maintained its influence, with movements in key currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY reflecting broader risk sentiment. Higher Oil prices have bolstered the USD against commodity-linked currencies, while gold’s appeal as a safe haven remains capped by the potential for further rate adjustments. Investors are monitoring the exchange rate environment closely, weighing whether gold can reclaim momentum or if pressure will persist.  

Technically, the dip below USD 4,500 has sparked cautious buying, but resistance persists near previous highs, suggesting consolidation may continue. Traders are watching both macroeconomic data and geopolitical developments to gauge the metal’s next moves, balancing the interplay between safe-haven demand and USD strength. For forex participants, these dynamics could shape positioning across commodity-linked currencies and USD crosses in the near term.
