The GBP/USD currency pair climbed more than 0.20% on Wednesday, as renewed risk appetite pushed traders toward Sterling and other higher-yielding assets. The exchange rate benefited from a fragile ceasefire between the US and Iran, which eased geopolitical tensions and prompted a dip in the US Dollar. Investors responded by rotating out of safe-haven USD positions, boosting GBP alongside other major currencies such as the EUR.  

Oil prices retreated in tandem with the improved geopolitical outlook, alleviating concerns over inflationary pressures. US equities rallied, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq seeing modest gains, reflecting increased confidence in risk assets. This broader market sentiment supported GBP/USD trading, with Sterling gaining traction against the greenback after recent consolidation near technical support levels.  

Traders are now watching for follow-through in both FX and equity markets as the ceasefire continues to hold. Analysts note that while the ceasefire is fragile, it has temporarily trimmed USD demand, offering a window for the GBP to test resistance levels around 1.2850. Market participants remain cautious, eyeing upcoming economic data and central bank signals that could influence the next leg of trading in key currency pairs.
