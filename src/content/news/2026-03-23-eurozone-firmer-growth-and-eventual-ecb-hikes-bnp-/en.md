BNP Paribas forecasts the Eurozone’s economy to grow by 1.6% in 2026, driven by Germany’s fiscal policies, increased military and AI investments, and a strong labour market. This steady growth is expected to support the EUR/USD exchange rate, keeping it relatively stable in the short term. As the Eurozone strengthens, the euro may maintain its resilience against the USD, despite global economic uncertainties.

Inflation is anticipated to remain below 2% in 2026, but BNP Paribas warns of a gradual uptick in 2027. The European Central Bank (ECB) may respond by tightening monetary policy, potentially pushing EUR higher in the longer term. Investors will likely monitor ECB rate hike signals, which could have significant effects on the EUR/USD trading range, influencing broader forex market trends.
