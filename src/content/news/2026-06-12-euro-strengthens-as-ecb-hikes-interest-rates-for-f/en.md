The euro gained momentum on Friday, pushing the EUR/USD currency pair to around 1.1575 in early Asian trading. The move comes as the European Central Bank raised interest rates for the first time since 2023, signaling a more hawkish stance. Improved risk sentiment also supported the euro, prompting traders to adjust positions against the US dollar.

Market participants reacted swiftly to the ECB’s policy shift, viewing the rate hike as a sign of confidence in the eurozone economy. The EUR’s rise reflects both the tighter monetary stance and a broader appetite for riskier assets, as investors reassess currency exposure. Technical levels near 1.1575 are being closely monitored for potential continuation or retracement.

Analysts suggest that this move could influence short-term trading strategies, with the euro likely to maintain strength if the ECB continues its tightening path. For the USD, the stronger euro may prompt cautious positioning, as currency markets digest both fundamental drivers and technical momentum. Traders remain alert to upcoming economic data that could sway exchange rates further.
