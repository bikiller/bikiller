The euro weakened against the US dollar for the third day in a row on Thursday, with the EUR/USD currency pair falling 0.22% during North American trading. Traders reacted to resilient US retail sales, signaling ongoing consumer strength that underpins the US economy. This boost in spending expectations drove demand for the USD, pushing the exchange rate lower for the euro.

Market participants also weighed fresh labor market data, which showed a modest uptick in initial jobless claims. While not alarming, the increase highlighted subtle pressures in the US employment landscape, yet it was insufficient to counter the bullish sentiment toward the dollar. EUR/USD trading reflected a cautious market adjusting to these mixed signals.

Technically, the euro faced resistance near recent highs, with momentum favoring the USD in intraday sessions. Analysts suggest that continued strong US economic indicators could extend the dollar’s rally, keeping EUR/USD under pressure in the near term. Traders are watching both consumer spending and labor trends closely for clues on the trajectory of this key currency pair.
