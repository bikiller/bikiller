The USD/CAD currency pair extended its losses on Wednesday, trading around 1.3847 as the US Dollar faced broad-based selling pressure. Investor sentiment shifted following news of a temporary ceasefire between the United States and Iran, which dampened safe-haven demand for the greenback. The pair is now marking its third consecutive day of declines, reflecting a cautious mood in the forex markets amid easing geopolitical tensions.  

Traders noted that the US Dollar’s weakness against major currencies, including the Euro and Yen, contributed to USD/CAD’s downside bias. Technical analysts point to the pair testing key support levels near 1.3840, with any sustained break potentially opening the way toward the 1.3800 region. Meanwhile, Canadian Dollar strength, buoyed by resilient crude oil prices, has further pressured the exchange rate, highlighting the interplay of commodity trends and currency movements in North American trading.  

Market participants are watching for follow-through in global risk sentiment, as a calmer geopolitical landscape could weigh on the USD across multiple currency pairs. Momentum indicators suggest that the immediate trading range may remain between 1.3820 and 1.3880, with traders weighing both macroeconomic data and geopolitical headlines. Investors remain alert to potential volatility spikes, particularly in USD-linked pairs, as the ceasefire news continues to reshape short-term market dynamics.
