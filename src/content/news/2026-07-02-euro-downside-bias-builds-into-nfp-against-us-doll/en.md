MUFG analyst Lee Hardman notes that softer Euro-zone inflation has reinforced a bearish tone for the euro, with EUR/USD sliding below the key 1.1400 support level. The move reflects falling European yields and a reassessment of European Central Bank policy expectations, as markets scale back confidence in further rate hikes.

The weakening inflation backdrop has led traders to price a more “mild” ECB scenario, reducing expectations for additional tightening. As a result, the euro remains under pressure against the US dollar in currency pair trading, particularly ahead of the US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) release, which could reinforce USD strength if labour data surprises to the upside.

Technically, the break below 1.1400 signals a deterioration in euro sentiment, opening the door to further downside risks in EUR/USD if momentum persists. Traders are watching whether the currency pair can reclaim lost support or extend losses, with broader forex markets increasingly driven by US data expectations and interest rate differentials.
