Swedish inflation surprised to the downside for a fourth consecutive month in February, with both CPIF and CPIF ex-energy falling slightly below levels aligned with the 2% target, Nomura analysts note. The drop was driven primarily by goods, particularly clothing, signaling weaker domestic price pressures despite lingering external risks. Market participants are weighing how this may influence the krona’s performance against major currencies.  

Oil price volatility is clouding hopes for an imminent rate cut by the Riksbank, Nomura adds. Traders watching the SEK/USD and EUR/SEK currency pairs are digesting mixed signals: softer inflation suggests easing, but higher energy costs may limit policy flexibility. This dynamic has kept the exchange rate range-bound, with short-term trading focused on reactions to oil and commodity price swings.  

Analysts suggest that while the recent CPI data could support dovish expectations, the krona remains sensitive to global energy movements. FX traders are monitoring USD/SEK trends closely, noting that any sharp moves in oil could trigger corresponding adjustments in the SEK against the euro and dollar. The combination of subdued domestic inflation and external price pressures makes the near-term outlook for Swedish monetary policy—and SEK trading—highly uncertain.
