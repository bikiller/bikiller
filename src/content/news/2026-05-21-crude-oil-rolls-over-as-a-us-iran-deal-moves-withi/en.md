Crude oil reversed its gains on Thursday as markets weighed the possibility of a US-Iran deal. Earlier, prices surged on reports that Iran would retain its enriched uranium domestically, pushing Brent above $85 per barrel. WTI mirrored the move, climbing toward $82, while traders monitored USD strength across major currency pairs. The sudden pivot sparked volatility in EUR/USD and GBP/USD, with exchange rates reacting to shifting risk sentiment.

Investors noted that oil’s retreat coincided with broader trading dynamics, including a firmer USD, which pressured commodities priced in dollars. Short-term technical levels around $84 for Brent and $81 for WTI held as resistance, prompting traders to adjust positions. Currency pairs such as USD/JPY and USD/CAD saw heightened activity, reflecting cross-market flows and hedging against oil-linked exposure.

Market implications remain nuanced: a potential US-Iran accord could ease geopolitical tensions, weighing on oil but supporting risk-sensitive currencies like the AUD and NZD. Traders in forex and commodities are watching closely for confirmation, as both oil prices and major exchange rates could swing sharply if negotiations conclude in the coming hours, shaping momentum for the remainder of the week.
