The Indian Rupee (INR) rebounded strongly against the US Dollar (USD) during Thursday's early trading session, following intervention by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI). The USD/INR pair dropped sharply, falling below the key support level of 83.00. This marked a recovery for the INR, which had been under pressure due to global inflation concerns and rising oil prices.

The RBI's actions appeared to stabilize the currency, providing much-needed relief for the Indian Rupee. Traders are closely monitoring this intervention, as it could signal ongoing efforts by the RBI to manage volatility in the exchange rate. As of the latest market update, the USD/INR pair was trading near 82.85, showing a significant 0.30% drop from the previous session. This shift underscores the growing impact of central bank policies on the currency market.
