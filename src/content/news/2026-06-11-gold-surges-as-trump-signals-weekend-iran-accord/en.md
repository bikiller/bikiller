Gold extended gains on Thursday as markets reacted to US President Donald Trump’s comments suggesting a US-Iran deal could be imminent. The XAU/USD currency pair jumped to $4,212, marking a 3.50% rise and partially reversing Wednesday’s losses. Traders cited a weaker USD, as safe-haven demand for gold intensified amid optimism over the potential weekend accord.

The rally in gold coincided with broader currency market moves, with the US Dollar index retreating against the EUR and other major currencies. Analysts noted that such geopolitical developments can trigger short-term trading shifts, as investors reassess risk positions and hedge against potential volatility. Technical levels around $4,200 provided support, reinforcing bullish momentum for precious metals.

Market participants now watch for follow-through in both gold and USD pairs, as confirmation of the Iran agreement could influence trading across commodities and forex markets. With gold trading near multi-week highs, strategies incorporating both XAU/USD and correlated currency pairs are expected to see increased activity as investors weigh geopolitical and macroeconomic risks.
