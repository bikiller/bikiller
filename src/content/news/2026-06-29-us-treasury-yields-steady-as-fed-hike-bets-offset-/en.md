US Treasury yields traded steadily during Monday’s North American session as easing concerns over further spikes in crude Oil prices were counterbalanced by persistent expectations of additional Federal Reserve tightening. While geopolitical tensions in the Middle East prevented Oil prices from extending their decline, investors continued to assess the outlook for US monetary policy, keeping demand for the USD broadly supported across major currency pair trading.

Money markets are still pricing in at least 34 basis points of Federal Reserve tightening in 2026, reinforcing expectations that US interest rates could remain higher for longer. That outlook helped stabilize Treasury yields despite calmer energy markets, as traders weighed the balance between inflation risks and slowing price pressures. The evolving rate outlook remained a key driver of exchange rate movements, particularly for USD crosses against the EUR and other major currencies.

For the forex market, the combination of steady Treasury yields and resilient Fed tightening expectations may continue to underpin the USD in the near term. Traders are closely monitoring incoming economic data and geopolitical developments for fresh direction, as shifts in interest rate expectations could influence trading sentiment, currency pair performance, and broader exchange rate trends across global financial markets.
