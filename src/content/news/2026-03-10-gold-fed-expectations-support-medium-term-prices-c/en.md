Gold prices have surged as market expectations of the Federal Reserve’s future rate hikes eased following President Trump's signals suggesting a possible end to the Iran conflict. Spot gold traded at $1,315.80 per ounce, reflecting a 0.7% increase on the day. This rebound comes as investors turn cautious about tighter U.S. monetary policy, shifting focus from the recent global political uncertainties.

The rally in gold is also supported by a weakening USD, as traders adjust their positions ahead of the Fed's next moves. The EUR/USD exchange rate showed a slight uptick to 1.1240, with the Euro finding support from the dollar's pullback. As traders remain wary of U.S. rate hikes, medium-term gold prices are expected to maintain upward momentum, barring any major shifts in geopolitical tensions or economic data.
