West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices are holding steady near $75.00 per barrel, currently trading around $74.80 during Thursday's Asian session. This marks the third consecutive day of gains as supply disruptions continue to be a concern in the wake of the ongoing conflict in the Middle East, impacting the flow of oil through the critical Strait of Hormuz.

As traders monitor the geopolitical landscape, the stability of WTI prices could influence currency pairs such as USD/EUR. Heightened oil prices often correlate with fluctuations in the exchange rate, affecting broader market sentiment. Investors remain cautious, weighing the potential implications on inflation and economic growth, particularly in regions heavily reliant on oil imports.
