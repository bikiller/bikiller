The Indian Rupee extended its recent decline against the US Dollar, holding onto a 10-day losing streak in early Tuesday trading. The USD/INR currency pair showed persistent strength, driven by rising crude oil prices, which add pressure to India’s import bill and weigh on the rupee. Traders noted the exchange rate hovered near key technical levels as market participants monitored external headwinds.

Foreign institutional investors (FIIs) turned net buyers in domestic equity markets, offering some support to the rupee, though the impact was limited amid broader commodity-driven concerns. The INR's exchange rate movements against major currencies, including the euro and Japanese yen, reflected a cautious sentiment, as investors balanced oil-related outflows with portfolio inflows. Market analysts highlighted that near-term volatility could remain elevated.

Technically, the INR struggled to breach resistance levels, while support around the previous low provided temporary stability. The combination of stronger USD demand and higher energy costs continues to shape trading patterns, influencing both spot and forward markets. Currency strategists advised monitoring global oil trends and FII flows closely, as these remain key determinants of INR performance in the coming sessions.
