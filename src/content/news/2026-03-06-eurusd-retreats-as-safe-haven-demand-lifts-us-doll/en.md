EUR/USD slipped to around 1.1560 on Friday, down 0.40% as the market weighed the latest US labor market figures. The currency pair had briefly rebounded toward 1.1590 following the release of the data, but safe-haven demand for the US Dollar quickly reasserted itself. Traders are closely watching how the exchange rate reacts amid a mix of strong US payroll numbers and lingering concerns about global growth.  

The US Dollar gained broad strength after the Non-Farm Payroll (NFP) report, prompting EUR/USD to retreat from intraday highs. The exchange rate’s dip reflects investor caution, as the market interprets the labor data as supportive of further USD resilience. Technical levels around 1.1560 now act as a near-term support, while resistance remains near 1.1590, framing the short-term trading range.  

Market participants are navigating a complex backdrop where safe-haven flows and economic data drive currency moves. The EUR/USD retreat underscores the USD’s appeal amid uncertainty, with traders weighing the implications for upcoming monetary policy decisions. The exchange rate’s performance highlights the tug-of-war between euro weakness and dollar strength, keeping the pair under close observation as the week closes.
