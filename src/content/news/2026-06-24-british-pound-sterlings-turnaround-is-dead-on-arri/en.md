British pound sterling extended its decline on Wednesday as GBP/USD drifted lower, confirming what the daily chart has been signalling for the past week: the attempted recovery in the currency pair has effectively run out of momentum. The exchange rate slipped steadily through the session, reflecting renewed USD strength across major forex trading flows.

During Wednesday’s session, cable briefly broke down to a low just under 1.3150 before stabilising into the close, trimming only a small portion of intraday losses. Price action suggests sellers remain in control, with rallies being faded as traders reassess near-term support levels in the GBP/USD currency pair amid broader USD demand and cautious risk sentiment across forex markets.

With the rebound now largely dismissed, traders are focusing on whether GBP/USD can hold above nearby support or risk extending the downside trend. The failure of the turnaround reinforces a stronger USD-driven narrative in global forex trading, leaving the pound vulnerable unless fresh catalysts emerge to restore momentum in the near-term outlook and broader exchange rate positioning.
