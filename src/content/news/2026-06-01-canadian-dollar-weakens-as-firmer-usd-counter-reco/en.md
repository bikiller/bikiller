The Canadian Dollar remained under pressure at the start of the week as the USD/CAD currency pair extended its rebound for a second straight session, climbing back above the 1.3800 level during Asian trading on Monday. The move reflected renewed strength in the US Dollar, which regained momentum against major peers as investors favored the greenback amid a cautious market tone and shifting expectations for global growth and interest rates.

Although crude oil prices attempted to recover, the rebound was not strong enough to provide meaningful support for the commodity-linked Canadian Dollar. Higher oil prices typically bolster the CAD because of Canada's status as a major energy exporter, but the stronger USD offset that positive influence. As a result, the exchange rate continued to edge higher, highlighting the divergence between energy market performance and currency trading dynamics.

Market participants will closely monitor upcoming economic data and central bank signals for further direction in the USD/CAD pair. Sustained trading above the 1.3800 mark could reinforce bullish momentum for the USD, while any stronger recovery in oil prices may help limit further losses in the Canadian Dollar. The latest price action underscores how broader US Dollar demand remains a key driver of forex market sentiment, influencing not only CAD crosses but also major currency pairs such as EUR/USD.
