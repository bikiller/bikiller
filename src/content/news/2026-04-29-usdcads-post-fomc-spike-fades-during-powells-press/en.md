USD/CAD saw its early post-FOMC surge lose momentum on Wednesday, ultimately ending the session near 1.3688, down less than 0.1%. The currency pair experienced heightened volatility following the Federal Reserve’s policy announcement, as traders digested both the central bank’s rate guidance and signals on future monetary policy. Initial buying pressure for the USD eased as Fed Chair Jerome Powell’s remarks offered a nuanced view on inflation and economic growth.  

During Powell’s presser, market participants reassessed the US dollar’s outlook against major currencies, including the Canadian dollar. The exchange rate hovered near key technical levels, reflecting uncertainty over the Fed’s next moves. Analysts noted that while the USD retained broad support from stronger economic data, profit-taking and cautious positioning ahead of upcoming US and Canadian economic releases tempered the earlier gains.  

Traders continue to monitor the USD’s performance against the CAD and other pairs like EUR/USD, interpreting FOMC signals for clues on policy direction. The muted net change in USD/CAD highlights how sentiment-driven swings can dominate short-term trading, even after major central bank events. As the market settles, focus shifts to inflation readings and employment data that could influence both the USD and CAD in coming sessions.
