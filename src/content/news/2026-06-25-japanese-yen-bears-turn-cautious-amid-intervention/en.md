The USD/JPY currency pair edged lower during Thursday’s Asian trading session, extending a modest pullback in the exchange rate. However, downside momentum remained limited, with the pair struggling to build follow-through selling pressure. Despite the soft tone, spot prices held relatively firm, suggesting traders remain hesitant to push the dollar-yen lower aggressively in current trading conditions.

Intervention fears from Japanese authorities continued to influence sentiment, as traders watch for any signs of official action aimed at slowing yen depreciation. At the same time, a modest US dollar pullback added mild pressure on the USD/JPY exchange rate, though broader USD demand remains comparatively resilient across major currency pairs.

On the technical front, the currency pair continues to find support ahead of the 161.50 level, limiting deeper losses in intraday trading. Market participants remain cautious, balancing intervention risks against US-Japan yield differentials, while short-term trading ranges are likely to dominate unless a clearer catalyst emerges. Volatility may remain contained in the near term as liquidity thins and positioning stays defensive.
