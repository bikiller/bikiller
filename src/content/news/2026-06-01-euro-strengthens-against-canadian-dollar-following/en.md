The euro extended its gains against the Canadian dollar on Monday, with the EUR/CAD currency pair trading around 1.6110 during European hours. This marks the second consecutive day of strength for the euro, as investors reacted to fresh German economic data. The euro’s advance suggests increased confidence in the single currency amid softer-than-expected domestic consumption figures from Europe’s largest economy.

German Retail Sales fell 0.3% month-on-month in the latest report, narrowly outperforming expectations of a 0.4% decline. While the contraction points to persistent consumer caution, the smaller-than-anticipated drop appears to have supported the euro, keeping the EUR/USD exchange rate resilient and encouraging further trading in risk-sensitive euro pairs. Traders are weighing these signals against broader market trends, including USD movements and global risk sentiment.

Technically, EUR/CAD remains above key support levels near 1.6080, with the next resistance seen around 1.6150. Market participants are closely monitoring European economic indicators for clues on future euro strength, while Canadian economic data and USD fluctuations continue to influence the cross. The pair’s current trajectory highlights the interplay between regional data releases and currency market positioning, offering insight for short-term trading strategies.
