Gold (XAU/USD) experienced a significant drop, falling below $5,000 to hit $4,967.70 during the early Asian trading session on Monday. The precious metal is facing intense selling pressure, driven largely by market uncertainty ahead of upcoming monetary policy announcements from major central banks. As traders position themselves for potential changes in interest rates, gold has struggled to maintain its upward momentum.

The geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which have typically supported gold prices as a safe-haven asset, are currently being overshadowed by the anticipation of central bank decisions. This shift in focus has led to increased volatility in the currency markets, particularly in the USD, as investors await clues on monetary tightening or easing. As central banks prepare to set their policies, the gold market remains highly sensitive to any potential signals from the Fed, ECB, or other key institutions.
