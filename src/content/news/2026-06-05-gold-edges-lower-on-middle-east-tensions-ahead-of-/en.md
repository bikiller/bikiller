Gold edged lower on Friday, with XAU/USD hovering around $4,463, slipping 0.26% as traders scaled back positions ahead of the highly anticipated US May employment report. The cautious trading reflects investor sensitivity to geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which have historically supported safe-haven assets, while the looming data keeps markets on edge. 

The currency markets are also feeling the impact, with the USD showing mixed strength against major pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD as traders weigh potential Fed policy implications. A stronger-than-expected employment print could reinforce the dollar’s position, while weaker numbers may prompt a softening, affecting gold’s exchange rate and broader forex sentiment. 

Technical analysts note that immediate support for gold remains near $4,450, with resistance around $4,480. Market participants are balancing geopolitical risk against economic signals, positioning for possible volatility spikes in both precious metals and currency pairs. As the US labor data approaches, trading volumes may rise, with forex and commodities traders closely monitoring the interplay between USD strength and safe-haven demand.
