Mary Daly, President of the Federal Reserve Bank of San Francisco, highlighted her current stance on interest rates during a speech at the University of California-Berkeley's Fisher Center. Daly suggested that the Fed could maintain rates at their current levels, while monitoring the impact of rising oil prices. She pointed out the potential influence of higher energy costs on broader price levels, which could shape future policy decisions.

The comments come as market participants weigh the Fed’s next moves in light of economic data and global developments. While the USD remains strong against major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, traders are closely watching for signs of inflationary pressures. Should oil prices continue to climb, the Fed may have to reconsider its rate outlook, potentially influencing market volatility and exchange rate fluctuations in the coming months.
