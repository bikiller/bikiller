The GBP/USD currency pair saw a modest decline of 0.16% on Tuesday, as the US Dollar gained strength. The rally in USD was fueled by higher oil prices and a rise in US Treasury yields, with investors adjusting their expectations for the Federal Reserve. Markets are now pricing in no rate cuts for 2026, supporting the greenback's strength.

At the close, GBP/USD traded at 1.3400, after briefly reaching a daily high of 1.3445. The current market sentiment suggests limited upside for the Pound as the USD continues to benefit from a more hawkish outlook for US interest rates. This shift is likely to keep the currency pair under pressure in the short term.
