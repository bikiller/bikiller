EUR/USD remains under pressure as rising energy prices continue to weigh on the Euro. Societe Generale’s Kenneth Broux points to strong Dutch gas prices as a key factor driving bearish sentiment, signaling that the currency pair is reacting more to growth concerns than interest rate differentials. Traders are closely watching how elevated oil and gas costs could impact broader Eurozone economic activity and the EUR/USD exchange rate.  

The pair’s trading momentum reflects heightened sensitivity to energy shocks, with the USD benefiting from its relative stability. Broux emphasizes that conventional monetary policy signals are taking a backseat, while the market recalibrates expectations around growth risks. This dynamic suggests that the EUR/USD could struggle to gain traction until energy price pressures ease, keeping short-term trading skewed toward the downside.  

Investors and traders are advised to monitor both oil and natural gas developments, as any shifts could rapidly influence the EUR/USD. The combination of Euro weakness and sustained USD strength underscores the importance of positioning around potential volatility. For the near term, the currency pair may continue to reflect these structural pressures, with exchange rate levels adjusting in line with ongoing energy market repricing.
