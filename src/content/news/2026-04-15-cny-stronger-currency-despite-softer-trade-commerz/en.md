China’s yuan showed resilience in recent trading, holding firm against the USD despite softer-than-expected March trade data. Exports missed forecasts while imports surged, compressing the trade surplus and signaling shifting external demand dynamics. The CNY exchange rate remained supported, suggesting underlying confidence in the currency pair, even as headline figures pointed to moderation in China’s external balance.

According to Commerzbank analyst Volkmar Baur, the narrower surplus likely reflects a cooling current account position following Q4’s multi-year peak. Stronger import growth may indicate improving domestic demand, but it also reduces one of the traditional pillars supporting the yuan. In forex markets, this shift has tempered bullish momentum, particularly in USD/CNY trading, where investors are reassessing medium-term direction.

Looking ahead, traders will closely monitor whether the yuan can maintain its strength against both USD and EUR as macroeconomic conditions evolve. A softer current account surplus could limit upside in the currency pair, especially if global demand remains uneven. Still, stable capital flows and policy expectations may continue to anchor the exchange rate in the near term.
