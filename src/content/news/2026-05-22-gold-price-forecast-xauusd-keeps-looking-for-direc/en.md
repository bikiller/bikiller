Gold (XAU/USD) edged lower for a second straight day on Friday, maintaining a tight range above the $4,500 level. Despite the modest decline, trading activity remains cautious as investors weigh USD strength against broader market uncertainty. The exchange rate has struggled to break decisively in either direction, reflecting ongoing hesitancy among traders across major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.  

Market participants are eyeing key technical thresholds, with the $4,500 support line acting as a critical floor for downside attempts. Volatility has remained subdued, suggesting that traders are waiting for fresh macroeconomic cues, including U.S. economic data and central bank signals. This cautious sentiment is influencing trading in gold alongside other safe-haven assets, with the USD playing a central role in shaping short-term price movements.  

Analysts note that while gold’s sideways trading does not indicate a strong directional bias, any significant breach of the $4,500 level could trigger sharper moves. Exchange rates across currency pairs, particularly those sensitive to risk sentiment, may see spillover effects. For now, XAU/USD continues to hover in its established band, leaving traders to monitor both fundamental and technical factors before committing to larger positions.
