Austan Goolsbee, President of the Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago, warned of a potential surge in inflation, citing the rising cost of oil as a significant contributor. In a prepared speech on Tuesday in Detroit, Goolsbee stated that the increase in energy prices is triggering a stagflationary shock to the market. This could have a notable impact on inflationary pressures, which are already a key concern for traders.

The market responded by adjusting exchange rate expectations, particularly with the USD. As oil prices climb, the likelihood of continued inflationary pressures rises, possibly affecting the Federal Reserve's monetary policy. Traders are keeping an eye on currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY for signs of increased volatility. If inflation accelerates as Goolsbee predicts, the USD could see further fluctuations in response to market adjustments to higher energy costs.
