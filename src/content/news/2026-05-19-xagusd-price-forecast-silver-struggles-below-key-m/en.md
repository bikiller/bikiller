Silver (XAG/USD) is facing renewed selling pressure as it struggles below key moving averages on Tuesday. The currency pair is weighed down by a stronger US Dollar, which continues to lift the exchange rate against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, making silver less attractive to traders. Rising US Treasury yields are also keeping the precious metal under pressure.  

Market participants are increasingly pricing in hawkish Federal Reserve policies amid mounting Oil-driven inflation concerns. Expectations that the Fed may raise interest rates to tame price pressures are driving USD demand, prompting traders to reduce exposure to non-yielding assets such as silver. This dynamic is keeping XAG/USD near technical resistance levels, limiting upside momentum in trading sessions.  

Analysts note that silver’s near-term trading range remains capped until clear signals emerge on US monetary policy. A sustained move above its key moving averages could reopen the door for bullish positions, while continued USD strength and rising yields may push the currency pair lower. Investors are closely monitoring economic data and inflation indicators for cues on the Fed’s next moves.
