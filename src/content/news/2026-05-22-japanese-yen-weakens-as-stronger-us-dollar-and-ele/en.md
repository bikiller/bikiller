The Japanese yen softened on Friday, pressured by a stronger US dollar and persistent energy price concerns, leaving the USD/JPY currency pair largely range-bound this week. Traders have been cautious around the 160.00 level, hesitant to place aggressive long positions amid speculation of past intervention by Japanese authorities in late April, which has kept the exchange rate within a confined corridor.  

Market participants noted that elevated energy costs are adding to risk-off sentiment in Asia, supporting the dollar against the yen. Technical levels around 160.00 remain key, as any decisive break could trigger renewed volatility. Meanwhile, the broader forex market has seen mixed moves, with the euro and other major currencies adjusting to ongoing economic data and commodity-driven pressures.  

Analysts suggest the near-term trading of USD/JPY may continue to reflect a tug-of-war between dollar strength and central bank sensitivity in Tokyo. For investors, the interplay between global energy prices, US monetary policy, and Japanese intervention expectations will likely dictate whether the yen can regain footing or extend its recent weakness, influencing hedging and carry trade strategies across major currency pairs.
