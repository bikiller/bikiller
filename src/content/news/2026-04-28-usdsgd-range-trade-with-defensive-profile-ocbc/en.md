The USD/SGD currency pair is showing signs of a defensive, range-bound profile as it slips amid broad USD weakness. Strategists at OCBC, Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, note that recent trading reflects cautious investor sentiment, with the exchange rate struggling to gain traction despite intermittent USD rebounds. Market participants are weighing mixed economic data from the U.S. and Asia, keeping momentum subdued.  

In the near term, USD/SGD is expected to oscillate within a well-defined range. Resistance is identified between 1.2780 and 1.2850, while support lies near 1.2720 to 1.2670. Analysts highlight that these levels may act as key pivot points for traders, influencing short-term trading decisions as the pair navigates global USD fluctuations and regional economic cues.  

Market watchers are closely monitoring how USD sentiment, alongside movements in EUR and other major currencies, impacts the Singapore dollar. Any significant shift in U.S. economic indicators or risk appetite could prompt sharper moves, but for now, the pair is likely to remain contained. Traders are advised to remain vigilant around the identified technical levels to capture potential range-bound opportunities.
