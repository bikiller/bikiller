EUR/USD has pulled back to the middle of its 1.1400–1.2000 range as a result of growing pressures on the Euro. Analysts at MUFG highlight the impact of the Middle East energy shock, which has added volatility to energy prices and weighed on market sentiment. This shift comes alongside disappointing PMI data from the euro-zone, further limiting the EUR’s upside potential.

The exchange rate of EUR/USD has been struggling to maintain upward momentum amid these external and internal factors. The delayed response from the European Central Bank (ECB) to rising inflation has also left the Euro vulnerable. As trading continues within the 1.1400–1.2000 range, market participants are cautious, with the USD benefiting from its safe-haven status amidst global uncertainty.
