The USD remains well-supported as the Federal Reserve signals a more cautious outlook, with only one rate cut projected for 2026. The Fed's decision to keep policy unchanged comes amid growing concerns over the US-Iran conflict and rising energy prices, which are complicating economic projections. These factors continue to weigh on the broader market, affecting currency pair movements, including USD/EUR and USD/JPY.

Higher energy costs, coupled with geopolitical tensions, are contributing to a more uncertain environment for the USD. With inflationary pressures potentially rising, the Fed's decision to delay further rate cuts until 2026 indicates a more measured approach. Traders are keeping an eye on the USD's resilience, particularly as the EUR/USD exchange rate fluctuates around key technical levels, highlighting ongoing volatility in the forex market.
