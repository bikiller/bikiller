EUR/USD is expected to continue its choppy, range-bound trading as market participants weigh inflationary pressures against the Dollar’s safe-haven appeal. Rabobank’s FX Strategy team highlights that rising oil and food prices are fueling concerns over sustained inflation, which in turn supports the USD. The currency pair has struggled to sustain directional momentum, reflecting investor caution amid economic uncertainty.  

Traders are keeping a close eye on the exchange rate, which remains sensitive to shifts in commodity prices and broader risk sentiment. The Euro has shown limited upside, while the Dollar benefits from inflows seeking stability. Volatility is likely to persist, with technical levels acting as key battlegrounds for short-term trading strategies.  

For forex markets, the interplay between inflation expectations and safe-haven flows suggests that EUR/USD could experience sudden swings despite the overall range-bound environment. Traders are advised to monitor both macroeconomic data and commodity developments closely, as these factors are expected to dictate intraday and medium-term movements in the exchange rate. The outlook underscores the importance of flexible positioning and cautious risk management in navigating current market dynamics.
