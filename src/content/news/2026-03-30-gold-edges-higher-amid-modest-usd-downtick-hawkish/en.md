Gold (XAU/USD) recovered from a slight dip during the Asian session, where it tested the $4,420 level, and now seeks to build on Friday's robust rally of over 2.50%. The precious metal appears to be benefiting from a modest pullback in the USD, but its upside is still limited by persistent hawkish expectations surrounding the Federal Reserve's policy stance.

Despite the gains, a bearish technical setup and the ongoing bets on further Fed tightening are capping gold's potential. With the USD showing signs of weakness, gold traders are watching closely for any shifts in sentiment, particularly in the EUR/USD and broader currency markets, as these factors will influence the exchange rate dynamics in the coming sessions.
