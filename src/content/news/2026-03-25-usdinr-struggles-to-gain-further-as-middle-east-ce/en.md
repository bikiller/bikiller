The Indian Rupee (INR) saw modest early gains against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, as the USD/INR pair pulled back to around 94.30. This comes after hitting a lifetime high of 94.75 the day before, as market sentiment improved on rising hopes of a ceasefire in the Middle East. 

The reversal in the currency pair suggests a brief stabilization after recent volatility. Traders are closely watching the evolving geopolitical situation, with any developments potentially impacting the INR's trajectory. The USD/INR exchange rate remains sensitive to external factors, with the Rupee trying to maintain its foothold amidst global uncertainties.
