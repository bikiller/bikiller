The Indian Rupee (INR) gained ground against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, with the USD/INR currency pair retreating toward the 93.00 mark. Traders noted cautious buying in the domestic market as investors positioned themselves ahead of the looming Iran-related deadline set by former President Donald Trump. The exchange rate movement reflected a combination of global risk sentiment and domestic capital flows, keeping the pair within a narrow range.  

Market participants pointed to heightened volatility in the broader forex market, as geopolitical tensions and policy announcements influenced trading strategies. While the USD showed resilience against other major currencies like the EUR, the INR managed modest gains, supported by stable foreign fund inflows and expectations of limited intervention by the Reserve Bank of India. Analysts highlighted that the pair could face further pressure if USD strength intensifies globally.  

Technically, the USD/INR hovered just above the 93.00 support level, with traders closely watching 93.10 to 93.20 as potential resistance. Short-term trading focused on capitalizing on small oscillations, given uncertainty over geopolitical developments. Any significant movement in the USD against major currencies such as EUR and JPY could trigger sharper adjustments in the USD/INR exchange rate in the coming sessions.
