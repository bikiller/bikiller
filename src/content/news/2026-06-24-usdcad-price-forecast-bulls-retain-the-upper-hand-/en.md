USD/CAD extends its rally on Wednesday, climbing to fresh highs not seen since April 2025 as the currency pair holds firmly above the 1.4000 level. The Canadian Dollar weakens under a double pressure from a broadly stronger US Dollar and declining oil prices, both of which erode CAD support in forex trading sessions.

Despite signs of overbought conditions, bulls maintain control as RSI readings remain elevated, suggesting strong upside momentum in USD/CAD trading. The pair’s ability to sustain levels above the psychological 1.4000 mark reinforces bullish sentiment, with traders watching for continued follow-through buying as short-term dips are being absorbed by demand in the currency pair market structure.

Looking ahead, the overbought RSI hints at a possible technical correction, yet the broader trend remains bullish as long as USD strength persists and oil prices stay under pressure. Traders will monitor upcoming US economic data and commodity-driven CAD sentiment for signals that could either extend the rally or trigger a consolidation phase.
