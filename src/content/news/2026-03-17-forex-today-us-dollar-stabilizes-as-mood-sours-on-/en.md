The US Dollar (USD) held steady as market sentiment turned sour on the back of rising oil prices. As WTI crude surged 2.3%, reaching $81.50 per barrel, inflation concerns resurfaced, fueling market jitters. This shift in sentiment pressured riskier assets while pushing safe-haven currencies, including the USD, into focus. EUR/USD traded near 1.0500, staying well below its 1.0600 resistance level, as traders continued to monitor inflation and central bank policies.

The dollar's stability was reinforced by a shift in global economic outlooks, with the EUR/USD slipping to 1.0450 in early trading. Analysts remain cautious, noting that oil's price hike could trigger renewed fears of tighter financial conditions. The USD's resilience was also seen across other major pairs, with the USD/JPY climbing to 134.40. The broader forex market stayed focused on how rising energy costs could impact future central bank decisions and overall economic stability.
