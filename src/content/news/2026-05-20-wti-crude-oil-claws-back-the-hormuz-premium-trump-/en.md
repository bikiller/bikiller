WTI crude oil reclaimed the Hormuz premium Tuesday, shrugging off the market jitters stirred by former President Trump’s earlier attempts to downplay geopolitical risks. Benchmark WTI rose toward $78.50 per barrel, up 1.8% on the session, reflecting renewed supply concerns in the Middle East. Rising oil prices also supported the USD as traders rebalanced currency pairs, with EUR/USD slipping to 1.0870 amid heightened risk sentiment.  

Energy traders noted that technical buying near $77.20 helped cement WTI’s rebound, while Brent crude climbed 1.5% to $84.10. Currency markets mirrored oil volatility: USD/JPY strengthened to 149.50, and commodity-linked currencies like AUD and CAD saw trading gains. Analysts emphasized that the persistence of the Hormuz premium underscores the interplay between oil supply risks and global exchange rates.  

The move sent ripples through broader forex markets, as USD liquidity tightened against major currencies. Euro traders reassessed EUR/USD positions, while USD/CAD activity increased alongside crude gains. Market watchers highlighted that oil-driven fluctuations could influence central bank policy expectations, particularly for the Federal Reserve, potentially affecting USD trading strategies and cross-currency valuations in the weeks ahead.
