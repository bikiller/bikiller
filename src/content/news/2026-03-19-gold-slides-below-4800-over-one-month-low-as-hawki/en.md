Gold (XAU/USD) retreated sharply below the $4,800 level on Thursday, hitting its lowest point since February 6. After briefly holding modest intraday gains, the precious metal succumbed to renewed selling pressure as a hawkish Federal Reserve outlook outweighed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Traders noted that the USD’s firm stance supported broader risk-off sentiment, prompting a rotation out of gold into higher-yielding assets.  

The euro-dollar (EUR/USD) exchange rate showed relative stability, hovering near 1.0860, while gold’s weakness reflected a market increasingly sensitive to U.S. monetary policy signals. Analysts highlighted that persistent concerns about Fed rate hikes are keeping bullion under pressure, even amid escalating geopolitical risks. Technical charts indicate support around $4,780, but momentum favors further declines unless the USD eases.  

Market participants continue to monitor trading flows across key currency pairs, including GBP/USD and USD/JPY, as gold’s trajectory often mirrors broader dollar strength. With gold breaking crucial psychological levels, investors may reassess hedging strategies and safe-haven allocations, weighing Fed-driven USD gains against the potential impact of Middle East volatility on commodity markets.
