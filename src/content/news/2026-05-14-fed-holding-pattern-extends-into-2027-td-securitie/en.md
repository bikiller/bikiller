TD Securities economists, led by Oscar Munoz, are revising their outlook for the Federal Reserve, signaling a prolonged holding pattern for interest rates into 2027. Persistent inflation pressures, fueled by the ongoing Iran conflict, elevated oil prices, and strained global supply chains, are delaying the disinflation trend, prompting the team to now expect no rate cuts in 2026. Market participants are watching the USD closely as these dynamics could sustain its strength against major currencies like the EUR and JPY.  

The lack of imminent Fed easing is influencing forex trading, with the EUR/USD currency pair showing resistance near 1.09 and support around 1.07. Traders are recalibrating positions, anticipating a stronger USD environment as the Fed maintains its restrictive stance. Elevated oil prices are also feeding into the USD’s performance, affecting commodity-linked currencies such as the AUD and CAD, which are trading with increased volatility.  

Investor focus remains on geopolitical developments and inflation data, as these will shape the trajectory of U.S. monetary policy. Exchange rates are expected to reflect these pressures, with technical levels guiding short-term trades. The market appears poised for extended USD strength, while EUR/USD and GBP/USD remain sensitive to global supply chain shifts and oil price fluctuations, keeping traders alert to rapid movements.
