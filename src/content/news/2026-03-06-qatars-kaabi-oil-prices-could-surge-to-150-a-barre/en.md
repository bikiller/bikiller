Qatar’s Energy Minister Saad al-Kaabi warned in a recent Financial Times interview that oil prices could spike to $150 a barrel, driven by the intensifying geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The potential disruption of Gulf oil exports amid the ongoing conflict between the U.S., Israel, and Iran threatens global energy markets. This uncertainty has already caused significant volatility, impacting global trading pairs such as USD/JPY and EUR/USD, with investors adjusting their positions in response to the risk.

The possibility of energy disruptions is heightening concerns over inflationary pressures and global economic stability. The USD has strengthened as a safe-haven currency amid these geopolitical developments, while oil-linked currencies, including the Canadian Dollar (CAD) and the Norwegian Krone (NOK), are under pressure. If the situation escalates, oil prices could see sustained rallies, further influencing forex market movements and leading to broader shifts in exchange rates, particularly affecting energy-dependent economies.
