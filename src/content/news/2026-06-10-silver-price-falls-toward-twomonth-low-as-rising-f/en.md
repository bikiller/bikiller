Silver (XAG/USD) extended its recent decline on Wednesday, trading around $64.70, marking a 1.02% drop for the day. The currency pair is approaching a two-month low as investor sentiment reacts to expectations of further U.S. Federal Reserve rate hikes. Market participants remain cautious, with the stronger USD putting pressure on precious metals and influencing trading activity across commodity-linked currency pairs.  

Analysts point to growing bets on tighter monetary policy as a key driver behind the weakening silver exchange rate. As traders adjust positions in response to potential interest rate moves, XAG/USD has struggled to maintain support levels, reflecting broader risk-off sentiment in the forex market. The metal’s performance is increasingly tied to fluctuations in the USD and investor appetite for yield-sensitive assets.  

Looking ahead, continued hawkish signals from the Fed could further dampen silver trading, while EUR/USD and other major currency pairs may see increased volatility amid shifting expectations. Traders are closely watching both technical levels and macroeconomic indicators to gauge the next direction for XAG/USD, balancing speculative positions with safe-haven considerations as global monetary conditions evolve.
