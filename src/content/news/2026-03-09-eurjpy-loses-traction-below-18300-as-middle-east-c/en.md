EUR/JPY is trading in negative territory around 182.95 during the early European session on Monday, as the Japanese Yen (JPY) strengthens against the Euro (EUR). The Yen's uptick is attributed to growing concerns over the escalating conflict in the Middle East, which has spooked global markets and driven safe-haven demand for the JPY. This movement is shifting the exchange rate below the key 183.00 level.

As traders await the German Industrial Production data for January later in the day, market sentiment remains cautious. A weaker-than-expected report could put additional pressure on the EUR, potentially pushing EUR/JPY lower. With geopolitical tensions intensifying, the outlook for the Euro remains uncertain, while the Yen's safe-haven appeal could continue to support its gains in the short term.
