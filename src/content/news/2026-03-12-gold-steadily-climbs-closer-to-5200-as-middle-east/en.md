Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher during the early European session on Thursday, testing the upper end of its daily trading range as safe-haven demand was buoyed by escalating Middle East tensions. The precious metal remains shy of the $5,200 mark, reflecting cautious investor sentiment amid geopolitical uncertainty. Traders are closely monitoring the USD, which has seen mixed moves against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, adding complexity to gold’s exchange rate dynamics.  

Market participants highlighted that rising geopolitical risks continue to support gold, while technical indicators show the metal consolidating just below key resistance levels. The interplay between USD strength and regional instability is prompting a cautious buying pattern, with gold acting as a hedge against potential currency volatility. The trading range between $5,180 and $5,200 has become a focus for short-term strategies, influencing both speculative and hedging flows.  

Analysts note that further upward momentum in XAU/USD may hinge on shifts in the USD exchange rate and developments in global risk sentiment. Should tensions intensify, investors could increase positions in safe-haven assets, potentially pushing gold closer to the $5,200 threshold. Conversely, any signs of easing could see retracements toward the lower end of the day’s range, reflecting the currency pair’s sensitivity to both macroeconomic and geopolitical drivers.
