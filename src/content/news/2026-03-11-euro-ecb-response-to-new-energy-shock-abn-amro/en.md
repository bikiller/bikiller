ABN AMRO economists warn that the ongoing Iran conflict, which has driven up oil and gas prices, could significantly impact Eurozone growth. The elevated energy costs are expected to hurt real income and consumer confidence more in the Eurozone than in the US. As a result, the EUR/USD exchange rate could face downward pressure, with traders eyeing potential weakening as the euro struggles to absorb the economic fallout.

The energy shock could lead to a divergence in growth between the US and Eurozone, strengthening the USD relative to the EUR. With oil prices pushing higher, EUR/USD is likely to face resistance at key technical levels, particularly around the 1.0700 mark. Investors may adopt a cautious outlook, leading to increased volatility in the currency pair and possibly altering the Euro's near-term trajectory.
