South Korea’s external sector remains firmly upbeat, with DBS Group Research’s Ma Tieying projecting June exports to surge 50–60% year-on-year. The trade surplus is expected to widen beyond USD30bn, reinforcing inflation dynamics and keeping the Bank of Korea’s rate hike path in focus. Strong data may support the Korean won versus the USD currency pair in trading sessions.

AI-related semiconductor demand and rising memory prices are key drivers behind the export strength, helping to offset higher energy import costs. This mix improves South Korea’s trade balance and underpins resilience in external accounts, which is closely watched by FX traders positioning in KRW/USD and broader Asian currency pair movements against the USD.

Markets will likely focus on how sustained export momentum feeds into inflation expectations and the Bank of Korea’s policy stance. For forex trading desks, stronger fundamentals could enhance appetite for KRW-linked exposure, while USD and EUR cross rates may see indirect spillovers as global risk sentiment adjusts to Asia’s improving trade outlook.
