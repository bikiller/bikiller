The Indian Rupee (INR) strengthened against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, advancing after a flat opening despite a renewed escalation of geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The USD/INR currency pair slipped toward 95.15 as investors weighed the impact of the United States’ retaliation against Iran following the reported shooting down of an American helicopter. The move highlighted the Rupee’s resilience even as broader risk sentiment remained fragile across global financial markets.

In forex trading, heightened geopolitical uncertainty typically supports safe-haven demand for the USD, yet the Indian currency managed to gain ground. Market participants continued to monitor developments in energy markets and regional security risks, factors that could influence capital flows and emerging-market exchange rates. The EUR, USD, and other major currencies also remained in focus as traders reassessed risk exposure amid the latest headlines from the Middle East.

Attention now turns to upcoming US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, a key catalyst for near-term currency market direction. The inflation report could shape expectations for Federal Reserve policy and drive volatility across major currency pairs, including USD/INR and EUR/USD. A stronger-than-expected reading may bolster the US Dollar, while softer inflation figures could increase pressure on the greenback and provide further support for emerging-market currencies such as the Rupee.
