The Indian Rupee continues to face pressure from external factors, with analysts from Commerzbank highlighting vulnerabilities to geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, rising energy costs, and potential El Niño-related risks. The USD/INR currency pair has seen increased volatility, reflecting investor concerns over the Rupee’s resilience amid global shocks, while EUR/INR and other major crosses remain sensitive to commodity-driven inflation and policy shifts.

Trading activity shows the Rupee struggling to maintain its exchange rate against the USD, with market participants monitoring crude oil prices and regional conflicts for cues. Higher energy costs are expected to weigh on India’s trade balance, potentially limiting capital inflows and putting downward pressure on the INR. The combination of these external pressures may influence short-term trading ranges and technical support levels.

Commerzbank strategists advise caution for traders and investors, noting that sudden market shifts could amplify exchange rate swings. With seasonal weather events like El Niño adding uncertainty to agricultural output and economic performance, the Rupee’s outlook is subject to swift reassessment. Investors tracking USD/INR, EUR/INR, and other currency pairs are likely to adjust positions in response to evolving external risks and macroeconomic indicators.
