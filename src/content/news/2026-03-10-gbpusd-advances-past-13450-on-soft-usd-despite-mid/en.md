The GBP/USD currency pair climbed above 1.3450 in North American trading as the US dollar showed continued weakness. Sterling touched a daily high of 1.3483 before settling around 1.3467, reflecting modest gains amid elevated geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Investors remained cautious, though US President Trump’s remarks suggesting a nearing end to the conflict provided some relief, keeping market sentiment supportive for the Pound.  

Traders noted that the softer USD has been a primary driver behind the exchange rate movements, allowing GBP to outperform despite global uncertainties. Technical levels around 1.3450 acted as a support zone, with resistance near the intraday high at 1.3483. The Euro and other major currencies also benefited from the Dollar’s retracement, reinforcing broader risk-on trading dynamics.  

Market participants are watching developments closely, as sustained Middle East tensions could trigger sudden volatility in currency markets. Analysts suggest that if the USD remains pressured, the GBP/USD may test higher levels in the coming sessions. For forex traders, this environment underscores the interplay between geopolitical factors, central bank outlooks, and technical trading levels in shaping exchange rate trends.
