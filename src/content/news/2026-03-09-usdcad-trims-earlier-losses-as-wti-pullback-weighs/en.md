The USD/CAD pair recovers from earlier losses on Monday, as a sharp pullback in oil prices dampens the Canadian Dollar's momentum. The Loonie, typically sensitive to fluctuations in crude, is facing downward pressure due to the 3% dip in WTI, impacting the commodity-linked currency. Despite the US Dollar being under pressure from a mixed economic outlook, the CAD struggles to capitalize on this weakness.

As of mid-day trading, USD/CAD is hovering around the 1.3700 level, having trimmed earlier declines. The Canadian Dollar's inability to extend its recent rally is attributed to the pullback in oil prices, which typically supports the CAD. Market participants are now eyeing further fluctuations in crude prices, as well as upcoming US economic data for insights into potential shifts in the exchange rate.
