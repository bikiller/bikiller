Donald Trump said on Monday that the Iran deal had been signed, adding that the Strait of Hormuz had fully reopened, triggering immediate risk-on sentiment across global markets. Oil prices moved lower while equities advanced, as traders reassessed geopolitical risk. In forex trading, the US dollar and major currency pairs reacted to shifting safe-haven demand and improving liquidity expectations in early European trading sessions broad risk appetite.

Following the announcement, EUR/USD saw increased volatility as traders rotated out of safe-haven positions into risk-sensitive assets. The US dollar fluctuated across major currency pairs, while the euro gained modest traction against the USD amid improved global risk sentiment. Commodity-linked currencies also strengthened as oil benchmarks eased, influencing short-term exchange rate movements in forex trading desks across European and US sessions.

Market participants now focus on whether the geopolitical de-escalation will sustain risk appetite ahead of the G7 meeting in Evian, France. Continued reopening of the Strait of Hormuz could weigh on oil prices and reinforce USD softness, while supporting EUR and risk-linked currencies. Forex traders expect heightened volatility across key currency pairs as policy signals evolve in the near term outlook.
