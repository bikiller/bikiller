NZD/USD is trading lower around 0.5840 on Wednesday, reflecting a 0.76% decline as markets remain cautious ahead of the Federal Reserve's monetary policy decision later today. The market's focus on potential rate adjustments by the Fed has put pressure on the New Zealand Dollar, while the US Dollar gains strength on cautious sentiment.

The decline in NZD/USD comes amid rising tensions in the Middle East, particularly with Iran, which has contributed to the strengthening of the USD. Investors are adopting a wait-and-see approach, awaiting the Fed's stance on future rate hikes. The exchange rate of 0.5840 marks a critical level as traders position themselves ahead of the decision, reflecting broader market uncertainty.
