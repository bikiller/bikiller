Gold (XAU/USD) continued its slide on Thursday, sinking to over a one-month low as the metal’s safe-haven appeal faltered against a hawkish Federal Reserve outlook. Traders noted that despite escalating geopolitical tensions from the US-Israel conflict with Iran, gold struggled to gain traction, pressured by a stronger USD and broad risk-on sentiment in global markets. The XAU/USD exchange rate fell toward key support near $1,960 per ounce, prompting cautious trading.  

Analysts highlighted that the US dollar’s strength is a critical headwind for gold, as the currency pair’s movements influence investor flows into commodities. Meanwhile, EUR/USD trading reflected mixed risk sentiment, with the euro slightly weaker against a resilient dollar amid Fed signals of continued interest rate vigilance. Market participants are closely watching upcoming economic data and central bank commentary for guidance on near-term trends.  

The decline in gold underscores the tension between geopolitical risk and macroeconomic fundamentals, particularly the interplay of USD strength and Fed policy expectations. Traders suggest that unless the Fed adopts a more dovish stance or global tensions escalate further, XAU/USD could face continued pressure, influencing broader trading strategies in commodities and currency pairs alike. Technical levels around $1,950–$1,970 are likely to be tested in the coming sessions.
