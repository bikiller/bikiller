Gold (XAU/USD) advanced on Thursday as the US Dollar slipped to a two-week low following a weaker-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report. The currency pair benefited from broad USD softness, with traders reassessing interest rate expectations. In forex trading, the move reinforced demand for safe-haven assets as bullion extended gains across major exchanges in global markets.

Meanwhile, the USD weakness extended after the Japanese yen (JPY) hit a 40-year low earlier this week, fueling speculation of potential intervention from Tokyo authorities. This development influenced currency pair flows, with traders adjusting positions across USD/JPY and broader forex markets amid heightened volatility and risk sensitivity supporting demand for alternative major currencies while volatility remains elevated in trading sessions.

Forex markets remained sensitive to US data surprises, with the USD index hovering near a two-week low as traders recalibrate expectations for Federal Reserve policy. Major currency pairs such as EUR/USD saw improved bid tone amid dollar softness. The outlook for trading remains cautious, with participants closely watching upcoming data releases and central bank commentary across global financial markets overall.
