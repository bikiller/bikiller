The US Dollar eased on Monday, breaking a four-day winning streak as traders digested geopolitical tensions following the US strike on Kharg Island, a key Iranian oil facility in the Persian Gulf. Concerns over potential disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz pressured risk sentiment, with USD/JPY retreating from recent highs and the DXY index slipping below 106.50. Analysts noted that safe-haven flows moderated as markets weighed the likelihood of broader escalation.  

EUR/USD climbed modestly toward 1.1050, rebounding from a two-week low near 1.0980 as the euro gained on a softer dollar. Market participants cited caution ahead of the Federal Reserve and European Central Bank policy meetings, with expectations of a steady Fed stance supporting short-term trading. Technical levels around 1.1020-1.1050 are now in focus, with higher resistance at 1.1100.  

Trading remained sensitive to geopolitical news, with oil prices edging higher on supply concerns, adding further complexity to USD dynamics. Investors are closely watching USD/JPY and other major currency pairs for signs of renewed volatility, while positioning ahead of central bank decisions may drive the next directional moves in exchange rates. Market liquidity is expected to remain thin until the Fed and ECB releases provide clearer guidance.
