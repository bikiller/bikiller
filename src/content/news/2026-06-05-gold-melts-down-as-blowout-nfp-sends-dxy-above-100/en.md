Gold (XAU/USD) plunged sharply during Friday’s North American session as the latest US Nonfarm Payrolls report dramatically exceeded expectations. Job gains for the last three months were revised upward, reinforcing market bets on a Fed rate hike. The US dollar index (DXY) surged past the 100 level, lifting the USD across major currency pairs and weighing on safe-haven assets like gold.

Traders reacted swiftly, with gold breaking key technical support near $1,980 per ounce, while EUR/USD and GBP/USD retraced as USD strength dominated the trading landscape. The sharp uptick in payrolls data pushed Treasury yields higher, signaling tighter monetary policy ahead and prompting a shift from commodities to dollar-denominated assets in forex trading.

Market watchers now anticipate increased volatility in both forex and metals markets. Investors are closely monitoring Fed commentary, as further rate hikes could sustain USD momentum and pressure XAU/USD. With the DXY holding above 100, currency pairs and cross-rates are likely to remain sensitive to US economic releases, shaping trading strategies for the week ahead. The outlook favors USD-linked assets while gold struggles to regain footing amid hawkish expectations.
