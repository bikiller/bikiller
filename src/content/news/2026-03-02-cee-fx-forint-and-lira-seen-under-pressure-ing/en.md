The Hungarian forint (HUF) and Turkish lira (TRY) are facing pressure as rising oil and gas prices, exacerbated by the ongoing Iran-related energy crisis, affect the Central and Eastern Europe (CEE) region. The stronger USD is further complicating matters for import-reliant economies, pushing both currencies down. Analysts expect this combination of factors to hinder any potential rate cuts in the near term.

Frantisek Taborsky from ING highlights that the higher energy prices, along with a firmer USD, are expected to delay monetary easing plans across CEE nations. As a result, both EUR/HUF and EUR/TRY have been trading under strain. The impact on exchange rates could continue as market conditions persist, keeping the pressure on regional currencies for the foreseeable future.
