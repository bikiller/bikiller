The Indian Rupee (INR) continued its downward trajectory against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, marking a third consecutive day of losses. The USD/INR exchange rate rose to 83.15, reflecting a 0.2% increase. Despite positive developments surrounding the US-Iran ceasefire extension, the Rupee struggled to gain ground, indicating broader market concerns over inflation and economic pressures in India.

The US Dollar maintained its strength, bolstered by robust US economic data and a hawkish stance from the Federal Reserve. Meanwhile, the INR remains under pressure, with traders closely monitoring upcoming domestic economic indicators. Analysts suggest the Rupee may face further losses if the global risk sentiment remains volatile, pushing the USD/INR pair towards new highs in the near term.
