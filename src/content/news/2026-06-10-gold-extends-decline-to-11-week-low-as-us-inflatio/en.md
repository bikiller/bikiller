Gold (XAU/USD) slipped to an 11-week low on Wednesday, pressured by US inflation data that largely met market expectations. Traders are increasingly pricing in the likelihood of a Federal Reserve interest rate hike later this year, keeping the USD supported against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. The bullion market’s current trading range has seen gold fall below key support near $1,970 per ounce, reflecting cautious risk sentiment.

The US Consumer Price Index data showed inflation trends aligning with forecasts, failing to significantly shift the Fed’s policy outlook. As a result, investors are positioning around the USD, pushing other currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, into more volatile sessions. Technical charts highlight gold testing lower trendlines, while the dollar index (DXY) remains resilient, reinforcing pressure on precious metals amid rate hike expectations.

Market analysts note that gold’s decline could extend if the Fed maintains a hawkish stance, prompting traders to favor USD-denominated assets over commodities. Short-term trading strategies may target $1,950 support and $2,000 resistance, while broader forex markets watch inflation and employment data closely. The interplay between US economic indicators, Fed policy signals, and currency pair movements continues to drive gold’s exchange rate dynamics.
