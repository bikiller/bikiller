USD/JPY edged slightly lower on Thursday as the Japanese Yen posted modest gains against the US Dollar, following a broadly expected US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) report. The currency pair eased after the data failed to surprise markets, with the exchange rate trading around 161.75 during the session, reflecting mild USD weakness in forex trading.

Market sentiment was shaped by the latest US PCE inflation figures, which broadly matched expectations and provided limited catalyst for further US dollar upside. As a result, USD demand softened slightly across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, while traders reassessed near-term policy expectations and overall forex trading momentum in the US dollar complex.

Despite the modest rebound, the Japanese Yen remains under pressure, hovering near multi-decade lows against the US Dollar. USD/JPY continues to trade close to the 161.75 level, keeping market participants alert to potential intervention risk and volatility in the currency pair. Broader forex trading conditions suggest the yen’s weakness persists even as short-term USD momentum cools.
