West Texas Intermediate (WTI) Crude Oil has pulled back from a one-year high, currently trading around $74.32 after hitting $77.20 earlier this week. Traders are closely monitoring the escalating tensions in the US-Iran conflict, which have influenced market sentiment and contributed to the recent price volatility. The geopolitical landscape remains a critical factor for market participants as they assess potential impacts on supply and demand dynamics.

The fluctuations in WTI prices could also affect the broader currency markets, particularly the USD/EUR exchange rate, as oil prices are a significant driver of inflation and economic outlook. A sustained increase in crude prices may prompt shifts in monetary policy discussions, influencing dollar strength. As traders remain vigilant, further developments in the geopolitical arena will likely dictate the trading landscape for oil and associated currency pairs in the coming days.
