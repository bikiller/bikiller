West Texas Intermediate (WTI) surged roughly 8% in early trading, eyeing the $100 per barrel mark, after reports that the US has implemented blockades in the Strait of Hormuz. The sudden supply disruption triggered a risk-off move across financial markets, lifting oil-linked currencies and pressuring risk-sensitive pairs. Traders are watching USD/JPY and EUR/USD closely as safe-haven flows push the greenback higher against major currencies.  

Oil futures trading has seen heightened volatility, with WTI opening the week on a bullish gap. Commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian dollar (USD/CAD) and Norwegian krone reacted sharply, reflecting the tight supply outlook. The surge comes amid speculation that Brent crude could mirror the spike, feeding into broader energy-driven inflation concerns that influence central bank policy expectations and forex positioning.  

Market participants are recalibrating exchange rates as geopolitical tensions ripple through financial markets. Short-term traders are eyeing technical levels near $100 for WTI, while long-term strategies may weigh the potential impact on USD liquidity and EUR/USD fluctuations. With volatility elevated, currency pairs tied to oil exporters could see amplified moves, underscoring the tight interplay between energy markets and forex trading dynamics.
