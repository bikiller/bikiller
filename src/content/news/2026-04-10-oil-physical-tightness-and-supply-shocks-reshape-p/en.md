Oil markets are showing unprecedented strain as supply disruptions and physical tightness push pricing to extreme levels. North Sea Forties Blend hovers near $147 a barrel, while Dated Brent trades significantly above futures, reflecting acute market dislocations. BNY’s Head of Markets Macro Strategy, Bob Savage, points to Iran’s tightening grip on the Strait of Hormuz, reducing flows to just 8% of normal, driving both energy and currency market volatility.  

Traders are reacting swiftly across forex markets, with the USD strengthening against commodity-linked currencies such as the AUD and CAD, reflecting safe-haven demand. EUR/USD exchange rates are also adjusting as oil-driven inflation expectations feed into broader macro sentiment. Market participants are watching technical levels closely, as sudden price swings in Brent crude could trigger cross-asset volatility, influencing risk appetite and trading strategies across major currency pairs.  

The tight physical market and ongoing geopolitical risk are likely to sustain elevated price pressure, with implications for global liquidity and USD trading. Currency pairs with energy exposure are especially sensitive, as traders weigh higher oil costs against central bank policy expectations. Hedge funds and institutional traders remain alert to potential sharp reversals, while persistent supply shocks may continue to redefine the relationship between crude benchmarks and currency exchange rates.
