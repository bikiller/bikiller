USD/CHF currency pair retreated on Thursday, slipping below the 0.8100 level as the exchange rate declined 0.34% in the North American session, in early trading conditions, amid cautious sentiment. The US Dollar’s six-day rally stalled, with trading momentum fading across major forex pairs as buyers took profits near recent highs.

The pullback came despite a hotter-than-expected US Core PCE inflation reading, as falling US Treasury yields outweighed inflation support for the USD. Lower yields reduced the dollar’s appeal in rate-sensitive trading, pressuring the currency pair and reinforcing broader weakness in greenback positioning against major counterparts even as traders monitored macro signals.

Technically, the move coincides with a developing ‘tweezer top’ pattern, signaling potential exhaustion in the recent uptrend. For forex traders, the break below 0.8100 may invite further downside pressure unless USD demand returns. Market focus now shifts to whether support can stabilize the exchange rate or extend the reversal in the near term.
