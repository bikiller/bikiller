Gold (XAU/USD) steadied after sliding to a fresh seven-month low of $3,941 during the Asian session, as expectations of further Federal Reserve rate hikes continued to pressure the precious metal. The currency pair remained sensitive to USD strength, with higher US yields weighing on non-yielding assets, while traders assessed broader exchange rate movements across major forex markets.

In Tuesday trading, dip buyers stepped in, helping gold rebound from intraday losses. At the time of writing, XAU/USD trades around $4,028, recovering part of its earlier decline. The rebound reflects short-term positioning shifts in the forex market as investors balance USD demand with safe-haven flows and monitor volatility across commodity-linked currency pairs.

Market participants remain focused on Fed policy expectations and upcoming macroeconomic data, which could influence USD direction and gold trading momentum. A sustained break below $3,941 could expose further downside risk, while recovery above $4,050 may stabilize sentiment. Cross-market dynamics between USD and EUR, along with bond yields, continue to shape exchange rate behavior for XAU/USD.
