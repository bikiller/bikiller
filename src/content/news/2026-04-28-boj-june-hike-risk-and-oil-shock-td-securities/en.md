The Bank of Japan held its policy rate steady at 0.75% in June, with a 6–3 vote reflecting a subtle shift toward tightening. Three dissenting members supported a 25-basis-point hike, highlighting rising concerns over inflation and the impact of global oil prices. Analysts at TD Securities, including Alex Loo and Prashant Newnaha, point out that this mixed stance keeps the yen under pressure against the USD, influencing short-term trading dynamics.  

Following the announcement, the USD/JPY exchange rate saw heightened volatility, testing resistance near 145.20, while EUR/JPY edged higher as traders recalibrated positions. Market participants are weighing the BoJ’s cautious approach against persistent global energy shocks, which could stoke further inflationary pressures. Technical traders are watching support around 144.00, noting that momentum could shift rapidly if the BoJ signals a more aggressive path in the coming months.  

The wider forex market reacted to the BoJ’s decision with renewed focus on USD liquidity and safe-haven flows. The yen remains sensitive to oil-driven inflationary trends, while USD trading dominates as investors assess global risk sentiment. TD Securities emphasizes that any hint of a June rate hike could trigger sharper currency moves, underscoring the delicate balance between policy signaling, market expectations, and trading strategy in major pairs like USD/JPY and EUR/JPY.
