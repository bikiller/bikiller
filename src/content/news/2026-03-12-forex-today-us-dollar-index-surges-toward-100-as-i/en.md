The US dollar strengthened sharply on escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) pushing toward the psychologically significant 100 level. Traders cited remarks from Iran’s new Supreme Leader, Mojtaba Khamenei, who vowed that attacks on neighboring military bases would continue and pledged retribution for fallen soldiers. The prospect of heightened conflict has fueled demand for safe-haven assets, boosting the USD against major currencies.  

EUR/USD slid below key support at 1.0900, while USD/JPY extended gains above 145.50, reflecting broad dollar strength across forex markets. Investors are increasingly weighing the potential impact of conflict on global trade and oil supply, prompting heavier USD buying in trading sessions. Analysts note that technical resistance near 100 on the DXY could act as a short-term target, but volatility remains elevated amid geopolitical uncertainties.  

The surge in the USD has pressured commodity-linked currencies, with AUD/USD testing 0.6400 and USD/CAD climbing toward 1.3650. Market participants are monitoring statements from Washington and Tehran closely, as further escalation could sustain the dollar rally. For forex traders, these developments highlight the need for caution, as rapid shifts in sentiment could drive significant moves across currency pairs and exchange rates.
