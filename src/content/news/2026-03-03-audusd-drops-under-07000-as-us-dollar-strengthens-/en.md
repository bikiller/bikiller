The AUD/USD currency pair is experiencing a significant pullback, trading at 0.6990 as of Tuesday, marking a 1.36% drop on the day. The pair breached the 0.7000 support level, reaching multi-week lows as the US Dollar (USD) gains strength. This drop is a result of heightened demand for the USD, driven by a market environment characterized by risk aversion.

The strengthening of the USD is closely linked to growing geopolitical tensions, particularly the ongoing conflict in the Middle East, which has led to a surge in demand for safe-haven assets. As investors retreat from riskier assets like the Australian Dollar (AUD), the exchange rate continues to reflect heightened caution in global markets. With the AUD/USD hovering below 0.7000, further volatility could follow depending on developments in the broader economic and geopolitical landscape.
