Silver prices fell for the third consecutive day, slipping below the $81 mark, as the US Dollar Index surged to a three-and-a-half-month high. The precious metal dropped over 2.90% as traders reacted to strong USD performance, driven by recovery in oil prices and broader market sentiment. Despite US President Trump lifting sanctions on Russian oil for 30 days, the USD remained resilient, supporting bearish pressure on silver.

The stronger greenback weighed heavily on silver, reinforcing the bearish outlook as it approached key technical levels. The EUR/USD exchange rate was also impacted, hovering near recent lows, while USD/JPY saw continued strength, reflecting the market’s preference for the US dollar. The ongoing market dynamics point to further pressure on silver as USD dominance continues to guide investor sentiment.
