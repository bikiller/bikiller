Brent crude surged toward USD 96 per barrel as renewed hostilities between Israel and Iran heightened concerns over Middle East supply disruptions. Danske Bank's research team noted that recent air strikes have dashed hopes for a broader agreement to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, prompting traders to reassess risk premiums in energy markets. This spike is influencing USD-based commodity trading, with the dollar strengthening slightly against the euro and yen.

Currency pairs sensitive to oil, such as USD/NOK and USD/CAD, saw increased volatility as traders adjusted positions amid the geopolitical uncertainty. The uptick in oil prices has bolstered the greenback, affecting exchange rates across major pairs and prompting a wave of short-term trading activity in both spot and futures markets.

Market participants are watching technical levels closely, with Brent approaching key resistance near USD 96 and WTI following suit. Analysts warn that further escalation could push crude higher, impacting global energy-linked currencies and FX liquidity. Investors remain attentive to the evolving situation, balancing risk appetite with defensive moves in major currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY.
