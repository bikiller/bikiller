Silver (XAG/USD) extended gains on Monday, climbing more than 2% as the US Dollar retreated from recent highs. The currency pair surged to $86.35, recovering sharply from intraday lows near $79.65. Traders noted that the partial retracement in the USD provided a supportive backdrop for precious metals, lifting silver amid broader market volatility.  

The rebound in XAG/USD reflected increased demand as investors sought safe-haven assets amid mixed signals from US economic data. The exchange rate movements in the Dollar against major currencies like the EUR and GBP weighed on the USD index, prompting traders to rotate into commodities. Silver’s technical rebound above key support levels highlights renewed bullish sentiment in the metal.  

Market participants remain attentive to ongoing currency fluctuations and global risk sentiment, as the interplay between the USD and XAG/USD could shape near-term trading ranges. Analysts suggest that further USD weakness may push silver toward psychological resistance zones above $87, while any resurgence in Dollar strength could cap upside momentum. Investors are closely watching these levels to guide entry and exit decisions in the current forex environment.
