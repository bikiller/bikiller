White House National Economic Council Director Kevin Hassett said during the late European trading session on Wednesday that it would be a mistake for the Federal Reserve (Fed) to raise interest rates. His remarks come amid ongoing debate over monetary policy direction, with investors closely monitoring USD stability and broader currency pair movements in the forex market.  

Markets reacted cautiously in currency trading, with traders assessing potential implications for USD-denominated assets and major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD. The comments reinforced expectations that higher rates could be politically sensitive, potentially influencing near-term exchange rate volatility and risk sentiment across forex markets during a period of thin liquidity in late European trading.  

Looking ahead, traders will watch for Federal Reserve communications and US economic data to gauge whether policy tightening remains on the table. Any perceived pressure from political figures may heighten volatility in USD crosses, influencing short-term positioning in key currency pairs and shaping broader expectations for global forex trading direction.
