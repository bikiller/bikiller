Gold (XAU/USD) held steady near the $1,268-$1,267 range during Tuesday’s Asian session, maintaining its sideways consolidation after touching the lowest level since March 23. The yellow metal found modest support from a softer USD, with investors weighing expectations for the Federal Reserve’s next rate decision. Trading sentiment appears cautious, as bulls remain hesitant amid lingering Fed hike bets.  

The currency markets show the USD under mild pressure against major pairs like EUR/USD, reflecting some easing in dollar strength. Gold traders are watching key technical levels closely, with the $1,267 support acting as a short-term floor. Meanwhile, broader market activity remains subdued, as traders digest mixed economic signals and anticipate upcoming U.S. data that could influence the exchange rate of major currencies.  

Analysts note that gold’s limited upside highlights the delicate balance between safe-haven demand and interest rate expectations. Should the USD weaken further, XAU/USD could test higher resistance levels, while any hawkish shift from the Fed may cap gains. For now, trading remains range-bound, with market participants navigating both geopolitical developments and central bank guidance in shaping short-term price action.
