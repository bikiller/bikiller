S&P 500 futures fell sharply by nearly 2.5% during the Asian trading session on Monday, reflecting heightened investor anxiety over escalating tensions in the Middle East. Concerns about a prolonged conflict are fueling fears of rising Crude Oil prices, which could significantly impact the global economy and trade dynamics. As this uncertainty looms, traders are closely monitoring related currency pairs, particularly USD/EUR, as shifts in oil prices could influence exchange rates.

The decline in U.S. stock futures has prompted a cautious outlook across global markets, with traders reassessing their positions amid fears of inflationary pressures. The rising cost of oil often leads to increased volatility in forex markets, making it imperative for investors to stay vigilant. As market sentiment continues to sway, the implications for the USD and other major currencies will hinge on developments in the Middle East and their ripple effects on the broader economic landscape.
