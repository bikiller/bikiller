EUR/USD traders may face renewed pressure as rising natural gas prices threaten the euro’s outlook. ING’s Chris Turner highlighted that elevated energy costs could act as a fresh headwind for the EUR, weighing on sentiment and potentially limiting upside in the currency pair. The analyst maintains that current conditions in energy, policy, and equities underpin the view that 1.17 remains a fair value for EUR/USD.  

Market participants are watching the exchange rate closely, with volatility likely to persist amid energy-driven concerns. Any further spike in gas prices could strengthen USD flows as investors seek safety, keeping EUR trading under pressure. ING’s assessment suggests that while the euro is not in freefall, its room to rally is constrained unless energy market pressures ease.  

Technical levels around 1.17 continue to serve as a reference point for traders positioning in EUR/USD. Short-term trading may hinge on developments in European gas markets and broader macroeconomic indicators. For investors, the interplay of energy costs, policy signals, and equity performance will remain critical in shaping the euro’s trajectory against the dollar in the coming sessions.
