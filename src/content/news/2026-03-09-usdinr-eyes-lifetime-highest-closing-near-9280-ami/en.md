The USD/INR currency pair is on track to close near its all-time high of 92.80 on Monday, reflecting strong upward momentum. The Indian Rupee (INR) has come under heavy selling pressure, driven by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The ongoing conflict involving the US, Iran, and Israel has led to a sharp spike in oil prices, further weighing on the INR.

As oil prices surge, India’s import costs rise, exacerbating inflation concerns and putting strain on the rupee. The exchange rate has been volatile, with the INR struggling to maintain its ground against the USD. This has set the stage for a potential lifetime closing high for USD/INR, highlighting the growing impact of global energy supply disruptions on emerging market currencies.
