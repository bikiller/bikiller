AUD/USD extended its recovery during the North American trading session, climbing 0.70% as the US Dollar weakened across major currency markets. The currency pair advanced to 0.7167 after rebounding from intraday lows near 0.7150, with bullish momentum building as traders increasingly target a potential breakout above the key 0.72 resistance zone. Improved risk sentiment also supported demand for the Australian Dollar against the USD.

Market sentiment strengthened after the United States and Iran agreed to extend their ceasefire arrangement by another 60 days. The agreement includes the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, easing concerns over global energy supply disruptions and boosting confidence across financial markets. The softer Greenback also helped commodity-linked currencies outperform, while EUR/USD and other major exchange rates gained traction during active trading hours.

From a technical perspective, AUD/USD remains supported above the 0.7150 area, reinforcing short-term bullish expectations. Currency traders are now closely watching whether the pair can sustain momentum toward the psychologically important 0.72 level. A confirmed move higher could encourage additional buying interest, while failure to break resistance may trigger renewed USD demand and profit-taking in the forex market.
