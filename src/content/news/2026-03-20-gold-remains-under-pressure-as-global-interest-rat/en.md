Gold (XAU/USD) remains under pressure in early Friday trading, struggling to recover after a steep two-day sell-off that drove prices to their lowest level since early February, hovering near the $4,500 mark. Traders are cautious as the exchange rate continues to react to expectations of higher global interest rates, which weigh on safe-haven demand for the precious metal.  

The recent decline has intensified bearish sentiment, with the USD strengthening against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Market participants are closely monitoring the broader currency pair movements, as a firmer dollar typically reduces gold’s appeal for investors holding alternative currencies. Technical analysts note that breaking the $4,500 support could open the door for further downside toward key psychological levels.  

Investors remain attentive to upcoming central bank signals, as shifts in monetary policy could further influence trading in gold and related currency pairs. Short-term traders are weighing the implications for portfolio positioning, balancing gold’s traditional hedge role against the attraction of higher-yielding USD assets. The interplay between exchange rates, interest rate expectations, and global economic sentiment continues to drive volatility in the metals market.
