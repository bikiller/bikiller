The USD/INR currency pair slipped on Friday, with the Indian Rupee showing notable gains against the US Dollar. Traders attributed the uptick to the Reserve Bank of India’s recent move to open special credit lines for state-run oil importers, allowing them easier access to foreign exchange. This measure has eased demand pressures on USD, bolstering INR in offshore and domestic trading sessions.  

Market participants noted that the exchange rate responded quickly, with USD/INR retreating from recent highs. The intervention is seen as a tactical support to stabilize the rupee amid elevated oil import bills and persistent dollar strength. Analysts suggest that this liquidity facility may encourage smoother forex flows for corporates, reducing volatility in the currency pair and supporting broader market confidence.  

The move comes amid wider trends in Asian currency trading, where emerging market currencies have been reacting to global dollar trends and commodity-linked capital flows. With EUR/USD and other major pairs also showing heightened sensitivity to central bank actions, INR’s appreciation could influence cross-currency strategies, hedging decisions, and short-term technical levels for traders navigating both regional and international forex markets.
