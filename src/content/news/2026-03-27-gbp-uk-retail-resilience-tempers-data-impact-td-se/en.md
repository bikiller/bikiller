The UK pound showed resilience as February retail sales exceeded expectations, signaling only a modest pullback following recent strong gains. Analysts at TD Securities highlighted that the three-month trend remains firmly positive, suggesting underlying consumer demand remains robust. While weather-related factors caused some weakness, the data did not raise fundamental concerns, providing support for the GBP against major currencies.  

In forex trading, the GBP/USD pair reacted with moderate gains, reflecting investor confidence in the UK domestic consumption story. The exchange rate held above key technical levels, with traders noting that sustained retail strength could encourage further positioning in sterling against both the USD and EUR. Market participants are watching for whether this momentum continues into the spring months.  

Looking ahead, the combination of resilient retail figures and stable economic indicators may limit downside pressure on the pound, offering opportunities for short-term traders. The GBP/EUR exchange rate also showed mild appreciation, reflecting broader market sentiment favoring UK assets. For currency markets, the data underscores how domestic spending can influence trading decisions, exchange rate volatility, and cross-currency flows.
