EUR/USD exchange rate remains steady above the 1.1600 level during early Asian trading on Wednesday, hovering around 1.1610. The currency pair shows limited movement as traders avoid large positions ahead of the US Federal Reserve interest rate decision. The cautious tone reflects subdued USD and EUR trading activity across major forex markets.

Market participants remain focused on the Federal Reserve policy announcement, which is expected to guide near-term USD direction and broader forex volatility. The upcoming US May Retail Sales data also adds to uncertainty, influencing expectations for consumer strength and interest rate outlook. As a result, trading activity in the EUR/USD currency pair stays muted.

Technically, the pair continues to trade above the 1.1600 support zone, suggesting a short-term consolidation phase. A sustained break above 1.1620 could open room for further upside, while failure to hold current levels may trigger a pullback toward lower support areas. Overall, traders remain cautious, awaiting fresh catalysts from USD-driven news.
