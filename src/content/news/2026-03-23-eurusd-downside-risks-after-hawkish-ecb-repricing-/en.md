The EUR/USD currency pair is showing heightened downside risk following recent hawkish repricing of European Central Bank (ECB) policy. ING strategist Francesco Pesole notes that the market has aggressively priced in three ECB rate hikes, a scenario that may have overshot realistic expectations. Traders are now weighing the sustainability of the current euro strength against potential market corrections.  

The euro’s exchange rate has faced increased volatility as investors react to the sharp repricing. The rapid adjustment in ECB expectations has driven trading flows into USD, reflecting concerns that the euro may be vulnerable if the central bank’s tightening path slows or proves less aggressive than priced in. Market participants are monitoring technical levels closely for signs of a reversal.  

This shift in sentiment underscores the interplay between monetary policy expectations and currency markets. While EUR/USD trading has benefited from the initial hawkish repricing, Pesole suggests that the risk of a pullback is rising. Traders may see short-term opportunities in USD strength, particularly if upcoming ECB communications fail to reinforce the aggressive hike narrative, potentially reshaping euro-dollar dynamics in the near term.
