Crude oil prices surged Wednesday amid geopolitical speculation, with Brent crude climbing over 2% to $89.45 a barrel. Traders reacted to reports that Iran has received a draft 14-point Memorandum of Understanding from the United States, potentially reopening the Strait of Hormuz and lifting the US naval blockade. The move, if finalized, could ease regional tensions and support risk-on sentiment across forex markets, particularly benefiting commodity-linked currencies like the CAD and NOK.  

The USD saw mixed activity, with the DXY index holding near 103.75 as market participants digested conflicting signals from Washington. The EUR/USD pair slipped slightly to 1.0892, pressured by cautious European bond yields and persistent safe-haven flows into the greenback. Technical levels around 1.0900 remain key resistance for euro bulls, while USD/JPY traders eyed support near 145.20 amid yen volatility linked to Middle East developments.  

Market analysts note that crude-driven FX swings may persist until clarity emerges on the MOU's implementation. Oil-exporting economies could see currency gains if sanctions ease, while risk-off currencies like the JPY might retreat. Trading desks are watching intraday correlations between oil futures and exchange rates, highlighting how geopolitical negotiations continue to drive liquidity and volatility in global forex markets.
