Rising oil prices amid heightened Middle East tensions and the potential closure of the Strait of Hormuz have sent oil-based bunker costs sharply higher, creating a favorable backdrop for LNG demand. ING economists Gerben Hieminga and Rico Luman highlight that LNG’s cost competitiveness has improved relative to traditional fuels, prompting energy traders to reassess fuel-switch strategies and shipping operators to reconsider LNG-powered vessels.

In currency markets, this dynamic has fueled broader trading activity as USD and EUR exchange rates react to energy cost pressures. The euro-dollar pair has experienced volatility, with traders factoring in the potential inflationary impact of higher oil prices on the eurozone, while USD strength benefits from safe-haven flows. Commodity-linked currencies such as NOK and CAD are also moving in tandem with oil and LNG fundamentals, influencing forex positioning.

Analysts note that technical levels across key currency pairs may be tested as market participants weigh geopolitical risks against supply constraints. The LNG shift could support sustained trading in energy-sensitive currencies, while oil-driven shocks continue to ripple through global forex markets, shaping hedging strategies and influencing central bank considerations on interest rates.
