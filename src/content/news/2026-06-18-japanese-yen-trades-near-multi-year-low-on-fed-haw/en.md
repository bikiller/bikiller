Japanese Yen remains under pressure in currency trading as USD/JPY holds near 160.85 on Thursday, marking a 0.12% daily gain at the time of writing. The exchange rate briefly touched 160.95, its highest level since July 2024, highlighting continued USD strength against the Japanese currency. Market participants remain cautious as the pair tests levels not seen in months.

The move is largely driven by persistent Federal Reserve hawkish bias, which continues to support the US dollar across major forex pairs, including EUR/USD and other USD crosses. Higher US yields and expectations of prolonged restrictive policy are reinforcing demand for USD in global currency markets, keeping upward pressure on USD/JPY intact.

At the same time, traders are closely watching for potential Japanese intervention risk as the yen weakens toward multi-year lows. Authorities in Japan have signaled discomfort with rapid depreciation, raising the possibility of FX intervention if volatility intensifies. For now, USD/JPY trading remains range-bound but biased higher as fundamentals continue to favor the dollar.
