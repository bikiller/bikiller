Gold (XAU/USD) struggled to find momentum on Friday despite weaker-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data. Traders had anticipated the NFP report to pressure the US Dollar, but the USD maintained its strength, keeping the yellow metal under pressure. The exchange rate hovered around $1,985 per ounce, with short-term technical support near $1,980 and resistance at $1,995, reflecting cautious trading amid persistent volatility.  

Rising US Treasury yields added headwinds for gold, a non-yielding asset, as investors favored higher-yielding instruments. The US 10-year yield climbed to 4.05%, reinforcing USD demand and limiting the upside for XAU/USD. Currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD showed mixed reactions, with the euro slightly weakening against the greenback, further illustrating broad USD strength across the forex market.  

Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East provided some support, but were insufficient to drive a significant rally. Market participants remain focused on potential shifts in Fed policy and macroeconomic indicators, as movements in the USD exchange rate continue to dictate gold’s trading range. Analysts note that sustaining levels above $2,000 would require a meaningful USD pullback or a spike in safe-haven demand.
