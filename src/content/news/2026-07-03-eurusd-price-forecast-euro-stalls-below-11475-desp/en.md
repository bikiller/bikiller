The euro extended gains for a second consecutive session on Friday, as the US dollar remained under pressure following a weaker-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report released on Thursday. In forex trading, the EUR/USD currency pair moved higher, but upside momentum remained limited as the exchange rate struggled to break above the key resistance level at 1.1475.

Persistent US dollar weakness has provided a supportive backdrop for the euro, with traders reacting to signs of cooling US labor market conditions. However, the EUR/USD exchange rate continues to face technical resistance near 1.1475, suggesting that buyers are hesitant to push the currency pair into a stronger bullish breakout despite favorable macroeconomic sentiment.

Looking ahead, traders will closely monitor upcoming US economic data for further clues on the Federal Reserve policy outlook, which could drive renewed volatility in the EUR/USD currency pair. A decisive break above 1.1475 would open the door for additional euro gains, while failure to clear resistance may keep the exchange rate confined to a range-bound trading pattern.
