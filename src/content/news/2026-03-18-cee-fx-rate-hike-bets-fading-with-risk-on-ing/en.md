Central and Eastern European currencies are benefiting from a stronger global risk appetite, with traders increasingly favoring higher-yielding assets over defensive positions. According to ING’s Frantisek Taborsky, this shift is weighing on expectations for further rate hikes, despite persistently high energy prices that continue to pressure inflation. The region’s currency pairs, including EUR/CZK, USD/HUF, and PLN/USD, are showing more resilience amid the risk-on mood.  

The Czech koruna and Polish zloty have been trading firmer against the dollar, reflecting investor confidence in economic stability and foreign inflows. EUR/HUF has remained within the 390–395 range, while USD/PLN has tested 4.10 support levels. Traders are closely watching technical thresholds, as a sustained move through these levels could influence short-term trading strategies and market sentiment.  

With rate hike bets fading, central bank policy divergence is less of a driver for FX traders, who are focusing more on global growth cues and commodity prices. Elevated energy costs still imply inflation risks, but the immediate market reaction favors carry trades and risk-sensitive positions. Currency pairs are thus expected to remain responsive to geopolitical developments, USD movements, and broader European economic data.
