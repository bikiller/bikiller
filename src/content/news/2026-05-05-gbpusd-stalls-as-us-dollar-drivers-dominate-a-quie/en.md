GBP/USD struggled to find direction on Tuesday, closing near 1.3545 after testing resistance around 1.3550. The currency pair has remained confined to a roughly 60-pip range over the past two sessions, with overlapping wicks signaling indecision among traders. Market participants appear hesitant, as momentum fails to build amid a quiet UK economic calendar and steady US Dollar influence.  

The USD continued to exert pressure across major currency pairs, underpinning GBP/USD’s subdued movement. Trading volumes remained modest, reflecting limited catalysts in the UK ahead of upcoming data releases. Technical levels such as the 1.3550 resistance and 1.3485 support are increasingly shaping short-term trading, with the exchange rate oscillating within this narrow channel.  

Analysts note that without fresh UK data or shifts in USD sentiment, GBP/USD may continue to range-bound trading in the near term. Broader market conditions, including EUR/USD strength and US Treasury yields, could influence the next directional move. Traders are watching closely for any breakout above resistance or a slide toward support, as the currency pair remains tethered by external forces rather than domestic drivers.
