AUD/USD extended its mild upward momentum on Tuesday, trading near the 0.7150-0.7170 resistance zone as the US Dollar (USD) softened. Investors responded to renewed optimism over potential US-Iran talks, which have tempered demand for the Greenback and bolstered appetite for risk-sensitive currencies. The Australian Dollar (AUD) benefited, edging higher against its US counterpart as traders weighed geopolitical developments.  

Market participants observed the currency pair navigating key technical levels, with bulls eyeing a sustained break above 0.7170 to open the door for further gains. The exchange rate’s resilience reflects both sentiment-driven flows and broader market positioning, while volatility remains modest amid cautious risk-on sentiment. Analysts highlight that any decisive breach could attract additional short-covering, pushing AUD/USD toward its next resistance levels.  

Traders remain attentive to macroeconomic cues from the US and Australia, with USD movements heavily influenced by geopolitical narratives and risk sentiment. A weaker Greenback could see AUD strengthening further, while any reversal in talks or shifts in US economic data might temper the rally. The market is watching the 0.7150-0.7170 zone closely, as breaking this range could signal a fresh phase of trading for the currency pair.
