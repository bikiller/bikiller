Asian currencies are under pressure as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, particularly the Iran conflict, threaten to disrupt oil supplies through the Strait of Hormuz. This raises concerns over rising energy prices, with implications for inflation and economic stability in the region. The heightened risk is likely to weaken the outlook for Asian FX, with currencies like the Japanese yen, South Korean won, and Chinese yuan vulnerable to further volatility.

Oil prices have surged amid the uncertainty, adding to the pressure on regional currencies. The USD/JPY pair, for example, saw a spike as the dollar strengthened, trading near ¥131.50, while the EUR/USD exchange rate remained subdued, hovering just below 1.0700. As markets brace for potential supply disruptions, the risk of higher energy costs could weigh heavily on Asian economies, prompting further currency fluctuations and challenging the stability of emerging market currencies.
