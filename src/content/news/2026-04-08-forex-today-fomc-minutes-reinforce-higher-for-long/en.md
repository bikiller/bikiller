The US Dollar Index (DXY) steadied around 99.10 as investors sought safe-haven assets, reacting to the latest FOMC minutes that underscored the Federal Reserve’s “higher-for-longer” stance on interest rates. Traders interpreted the minutes as a signal that the Fed is likely to maintain a cautious approach to monetary easing, keeping USD strength intact across major currency pairs. The greenback’s resilience weighed on the EUR/USD, which hovered near 1.0800, while USD/JPY tested support around 135.50.  

Market participants are digesting geopolitical developments, including reports of a ceasefire in key conflict zones, which initially supported riskier assets. However, the USD’s appeal as a safe-haven limited sharp gains in equities and other currencies. Technical traders note that DXY sustaining above 99 could reinforce bullish momentum, prompting cautious trading in GBP/USD and AUD/USD as investors await further Fed guidance.  

Looking ahead, the focus remains on how the central bank’s stance shapes exchange rates and trading strategies. Analysts expect short-term volatility in currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY, with the greenback’s trajectory linked to any updates on interest rate expectations. For forex traders, maintaining awareness of Fed signals and global risk sentiment will be crucial in navigating the current market environment.
