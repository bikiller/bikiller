The USD/CHF currency pair edged higher to hover near 0.7890 in early European trading on Friday, reflecting a rebound in the US Dollar after a sharp retreat the previous session. Traders noted renewed demand for the greenback, lifting its exchange rate against the Swiss Franc, while market sentiment remained cautious ahead of upcoming economic data from both the US and Europe.  

The recovery in USD/CHF comes amid broader currency market shifts, as the US Dollar regained footing against major peers, including the EUR and GBP. Analysts suggested that short-term technical buying and profit-taking in the Swiss Franc contributed to the pair’s upward movement. The 0.7890 level is now being closely watched as a potential resistance point, with traders assessing whether the rebound has enough momentum to extend further.  

Market participants highlighted that global risk sentiment and monetary policy expectations continue to influence trading in USD/CHF. Any surprise moves in US inflation or interest rate signals could sway the pair, while the Swiss Franc’s safe-haven appeal may temper gains. For now, the pair’s near-term trajectory appears tied to the USD’s strength and investor reactions to evolving economic data.
