The AUD/JPY currency pair dropped to 113.50 early in Monday's European session, as escalating tensions between the US and Iran boosted demand for the Japanese Yen. The exchange rate of AUD/JPY weakened from its earlier level of 113.65, reflecting a shift in market sentiment toward safe-haven assets amidst geopolitical risks. 

With US-Iran relations intensifying after the seizure of a ship, traders flocked to the Yen, traditionally seen as a safe haven during periods of instability. The Aussie, on the other hand, faced pressure as risk appetite diminished, pushing the AUD/JPY lower. The situation underscores how geopolitical tensions can drive shifts in forex markets, influencing trading dynamics and currency strength.
