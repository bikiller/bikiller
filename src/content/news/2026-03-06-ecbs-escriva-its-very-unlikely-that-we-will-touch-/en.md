European Central Bank (ECB) policymaker Jose Luis Escriva signaled on Friday that interest rates are unlikely to change at the next ECB meeting. Speaking to Catalan TV3, he noted that current economic conditions, combined with ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, make immediate policy adjustments improbable. Traders reacted cautiously, with the EUR/USD hovering around key technical support near 1.0800, reflecting a market weighing both risk sentiment and central bank guidance.  

The euro struggled to gain traction against the dollar, as safe-haven flows into the USD kept the EUR/USD under pressure. Analysts highlighted that with global energy prices still volatile due to the Middle East conflict, ECB officials may prefer to maintain a steady policy stance rather than risk tightening prematurely. Short-term technical levels suggest EUR/USD could test 1.0750 if risk aversion persists, while a rebound above 1.0850 would require stronger eurozone economic signals.  

Market participants are now closely monitoring comments from other ECB officials for clues on future policy. Any shift toward hawkish rhetoric could trigger a euro rally, while dovish tones may reinforce USD strength in currency pair trading. The combination of geopolitical uncertainty and cautious ECB signaling continues to shape exchange rate movements, influencing trading strategies across EUR, USD, and other major pairs.
