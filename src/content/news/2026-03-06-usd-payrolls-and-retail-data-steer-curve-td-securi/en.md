TD Securities analysts predict a slowdown in February’s US Nonfarm Payrolls, expecting only 60k jobs added, while the Unemployment Rate is expected to remain unchanged at 4.3%. The market will also be watching January’s Retail Sales data, with a modest 0.2% monthly rebound anticipated. This economic data could influence the USD’s trajectory in forex trading.

In response to these expectations, analysts foresee a slight bear flattening in US yields as the market remains focused on geopolitical tensions and incoming data. The USD's movement, alongside the EUR/USD and other key pairs, will likely reflect these developments, with investors eyeing how economic data interacts with global risk sentiment.
