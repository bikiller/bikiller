The Indian Rupee hovered near all-time lows on Tuesday as risk-off sentiment gripped global markets, pushing the USD/INR currency pair higher. During Asian trading hours, the pair extended gains for the third consecutive session, touching a fresh record high of 95.40. Traders noted sustained demand for the US dollar amid safe-haven flows, while domestic factors, including inflation concerns and capital outflows, continued to weigh on the rupee’s performance.  

Market participants are now looking ahead to key economic releases that could influence currency trading. India’s HSBC Composite and Services Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) data, scheduled for Wednesday, are expected to provide insight into domestic economic momentum. Positive readings could support the rupee, while weaker numbers may reinforce USD strength and further pressure the exchange rate, keeping USD/INR elevated near record levels.  

The broader forex market also reflected heightened caution, with USD strength extending against other emerging market currencies. Investors remained focused on global growth concerns and monetary policy expectations, which are likely to dictate near-term trends in the rupee and its trading against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Technical levels suggest the USD/INR could face resistance near 95.50, while support remains around 94.80, signaling continued volatility for traders.
