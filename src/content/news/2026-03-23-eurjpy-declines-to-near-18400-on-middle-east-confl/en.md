The EUR/JPY currency pair is experiencing mild losses, trading at 184.15 during the early Asian session on Monday. The Euro has faced downward pressure as the Japanese Yen strengthens, reflecting the market's flight to safe-haven assets amid rising tensions in the Middle East. This geopolitical uncertainty has driven investors toward the Yen, traditionally seen as a safer bet during periods of global instability.

As the conflict in the region escalates, risk-off sentiment continues to dominate, pushing the exchange rate of EUR/JPY closer to the critical 184.00 level. This decline is further fueled by the strengthening of the Yen against the Euro, with traders focusing on the risk factors and potential impacts on global markets. Investors are likely to monitor these developments closely as they adjust their positions in the face of ongoing uncertainty.
