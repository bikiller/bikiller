The Bank of Japan released its latest review on Friday, highlighting that Japan's estimated natural rate of interest sits between roughly -0.9% and +0.5%. The report also assessed the degree of monetary accommodation, signaling the BoJ’s continued commitment to ultra-loose policy despite gradual shifts in global yields. Traders noted that the findings could influence the JPY, especially against the USD and EUR, as markets reassess the outlook for interest rate differentials.  

In forex trading, the Japanese yen saw subtle movements following the report, with the USD/JPY pair testing key support near 135.50. Analysts suggest that any upward pressure on U.S. Treasury yields could weigh on the yen, keeping the pair in a cautious range. Similarly, EUR/JPY remained sensitive to European bond developments, reflecting cross-market reactions to the BoJ’s policy stance and Japan’s persistent low-rate environment.  

Market participants are weighing the implications for long-term exchange rates, as the BoJ’s natural rate estimate underscores the challenges of normalizing policy. Short-term trading may remain range-bound, but speculative flows could intensify if global rate shifts accelerate. For now, the yen’s trajectory against major currencies like the USD and EUR will hinge on the interplay between Japan’s policy signals and overseas rate trends, offering both risk and opportunity for currency traders.
