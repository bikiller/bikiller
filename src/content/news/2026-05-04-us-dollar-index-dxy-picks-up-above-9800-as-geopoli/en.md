The US Dollar (USD) gained modestly on Monday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) climbing back above the 98.00 mark after finding support near 97.70 last Friday. The rebound comes as geopolitical tensions intensify, prompting investors to seek safe-haven assets. Despite the uptick, the DXY remains within the lower portion of its recent trading range, signaling a cautious market sentiment amid mixed global cues.  

In currency pair action, the EUR/USD continued to hover near recent lows, reflecting the USD’s underlying strength. Other major pairs, including USD/JPY and GBP/USD, showed limited volatility, as traders weighed economic data against rising geopolitical risks. Exchange rate movements suggest a defensive bias, with market participants closely monitoring central bank commentary and geopolitical developments for further direction.  

Technical analysts note that a sustained move above 98.00 could pave the way for further USD gains, while a fall back below 97.70 might signal renewed consolidation. Traders remain focused on short-term trading opportunities, balancing risk appetite against safe-haven demand. The USD’s performance is likely to continue influencing global currency markets, with investors tracking both macroeconomic indicators and geopolitical headlines for guidance.
