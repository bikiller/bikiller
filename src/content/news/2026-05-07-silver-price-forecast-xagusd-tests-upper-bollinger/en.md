Silver (XAG/USD) retraced some of its early gains on Thursday, testing the upper Bollinger Band amid subdued momentum. The currency pair initially rallied as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East triggered cautious buying in precious metals, but profit-taking and mixed USD performance capped the upside. Traders are watching the exchange rate closely for signs of sustained strength above key technical levels.  

Market participants noted that XAG/USD remains sensitive to shifts in the USD index, with a firmer greenback limiting further gains. EUR/USD and other major currency pairs have shown muted volatility, which has also constrained silver’s trading range. Analysts suggest that while silver’s bounce reflects safe-haven demand, momentum indicators signal caution for short-term positions.  

Looking ahead, the exchange rate could face resistance if global risk sentiment stabilizes or the USD strengthens further. Investors are balancing geopolitical risks with broader market liquidity, keeping trading cautious. A breakout beyond the upper Bollinger Band may attract renewed buying, but subdued momentum suggests consolidation is more likely before any decisive move.
