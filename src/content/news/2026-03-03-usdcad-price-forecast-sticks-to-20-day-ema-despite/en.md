The USD/CAD currency pair is currently trading in a narrow range around 1.3670 during the late Asian trading session on Tuesday. The Canadian Dollar (CAD) has shown resilience, bolstered by rising oil prices amid escalating tensions between the United States, Israel, and Iran. This dynamic has provided some support to the CAD, offsetting the generally strong performance of the US Dollar (USD).

As the pair consolidates, the 20-day exponential moving average (EMA) remains a key technical level to watch. Traders are closely monitoring geopolitical developments that could further influence oil prices and, consequently, the exchange rate between the USD and CAD. The ongoing conflict underscores the interconnectedness of global markets, particularly how geopolitical events can sway currency movements and trading strategies.
