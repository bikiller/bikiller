West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil continues its upward momentum, extending gains for a third consecutive session and trading near $69.00 per barrel during Asian hours on Monday. The move reflects steady buying interest in USD-denominated commodities, as traders reassess short-term supply dynamics. Broader forex markets remain attentive to shifts in energy prices, which often influence USD exchange rate sentiment and risk-linked currency pairs.  

Despite recent OPEC+ production hikes, crude benchmarks have held firm, suggesting markets are balancing supply increases against persistent geopolitical concerns. Flows through the Strait of Hormuz remain a key focus, with traders pricing in potential disruptions. The stability in physical supply has not fully offset speculative positioning in oil futures, keeping WTI supported around current USD levels.  

Oil strength continues to ripple through forex markets, with the US dollar showing mixed reactions as energy-driven inflation expectations adjust. Commodity-linked currencies and risk-sensitive pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY reflect cautious sentiment. Traders monitor whether sustained WTI gains above $69.00 could reinforce USD support or trigger profit-taking in broader exchange rate positioning.
