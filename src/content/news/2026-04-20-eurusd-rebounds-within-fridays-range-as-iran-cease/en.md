EUR/USD edged higher on Monday, rising roughly 0.20% as the currency pair recovered from overnight lows near 1.1730. Traders saw the exchange rate climb to around 1.1790 during the European session, marking a modest rebound within Friday’s established trading range. Market participants cited cautious positioning ahead of key geopolitical developments as supporting the short-term uptick in the euro against the USD.  

The rebound comes as investors weigh the looming ceasefire deadline in Iran, adding a layer of uncertainty to risk sentiment. The EUR found some support after testing near-term technical floors, while the USD faced mixed demand amid a lack of fresh macroeconomic catalysts. Momentum indicators suggest that trading could remain range-bound unless decisive news triggers a breakout above resistance levels.  

Analysts note that the currency pair’s movements reflect broader market caution, with traders balancing geopolitical concerns against stable eurozone data. A sustained move above 1.1790 could attract further euro buying, while a retreat toward 1.1730 might reinforce the pair’s short-term floor. For now, EUR/USD trading remains sensitive to both headline risks and technical levels, highlighting the interplay between geopolitical developments and currency market dynamics.
