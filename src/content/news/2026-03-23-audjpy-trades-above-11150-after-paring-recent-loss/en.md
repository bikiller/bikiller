AUD/JPY edged above 111.50 on Monday, trading near 111.70 during Asian hours as the currency pair recovers slightly from earlier losses. The Australian Dollar remains under pressure, reflecting broader market caution amid rising geopolitical tensions. Traders are weighing risk sentiment, with the pair struggling to sustain gains despite intermittent buying interest against the Japanese Yen.  

The exchange rate movement comes amid mixed flows in major currencies, including USD and EUR, as investors adjust positions across global markets. The Japanese Yen continues to benefit from safe-haven demand, limiting AUD/JPY’s upside. Analysts note that the cross may face resistance around 112.00, while support is expected near 111.20, creating a narrow trading range for the session.  

Market participants remain focused on economic indicators and risk catalysts, including U.S. data releases and developments in Asia-Pacific geopolitics. Any shift in investor sentiment toward riskier assets could prompt renewed AUD demand, whereas further uncertainty may favor the Yen, keeping the currency pair under pressure. Traders are positioning carefully, balancing potential upside against ongoing market volatility.
