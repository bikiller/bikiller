Societe Generale analysts highlighted comments from Brazil’s central bank (BCB) officials regarding the March 25 basis point (bp) cut to the Selic rate. The BCB emphasized that the move should be seen as the beginning of a "calibration process" rather than the start of an easing cycle. This has limited support for the Brazilian Real (BRL) in the face of US dollar strength, which has kept the USD/BRL exchange rate relatively elevated.

Despite the 25bp Selic cut, the Brazilian central bank's cautious approach has kept the Real under pressure, with the USD/BRL pair staying within a higher range. Investors are closely watching any further signals from the BCB that could indicate a shift toward broader easing measures. The BCB's stance suggests that while the central bank may be looking to adjust policy, it’s doing so carefully to avoid unsettling the currency further, keeping the BRL on a steady but restrained path against the dollar.
