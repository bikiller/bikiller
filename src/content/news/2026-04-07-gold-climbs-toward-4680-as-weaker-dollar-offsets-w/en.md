Gold edged higher on Tuesday, with XAU/USD climbing roughly 0.63% as the precious metal approached $4,680. The uptick came as a softer US Dollar provided support, easing pressure on the yellow metal despite lingering geopolitical tensions. Traders monitored oil markets closely, noting a pullback from daily highs amid speculation of stalled US-Iran negotiations, though Iranian media quickly dismissed claims of broken talks.  

The weaker USD against major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD bolstered risk appetite, encouraging buying in safe-haven assets like gold. Currency traders observed the exchange rate dynamics, with the greenback retreating from recent highs, which provided a favorable environment for XAU/USD to test technical resistance levels near $4,680. Analysts highlighted that short-term trading sentiment remained cautious, given the uncertainty in energy markets and ongoing geopolitical headlines.  

Market participants also weighed broader implications for trading strategies, noting that currency fluctuations could continue to drive gold volatility. With oil prices retreating, pressure on inflation expectations eased slightly, influencing USD movements and, in turn, gold pricing. Investors remained alert to central bank cues and potential shifts in global monetary policy, which could sway both forex markets and commodities like gold in the coming sessions.
