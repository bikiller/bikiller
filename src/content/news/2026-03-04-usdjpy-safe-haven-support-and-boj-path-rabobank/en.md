The USD/JPY currency pair has retreated from its recent highs near 158, driven by profit-taking and hawkish remarks from Bank of Japan (BoJ) Governor Ueda. The yen found support as market participants reacted to Ueda's comments, which pointed to a cautious approach to policy tightening. Despite Japan’s vulnerability to rising energy prices, the yen continues to hold its position as a safe haven asset in uncertain times.

The pullback in USD/JPY signals a shift in market sentiment, with traders adjusting their positions in response to the potential impact of global economic developments. While the yen remains susceptible to external pressures, its safe haven appeal could help stabilize the exchange rate in the near term, offering support around the 158 level. As energy costs weigh on Japan’s economy, the BoJ’s future actions will likely remain a key factor in the pair’s movement.
