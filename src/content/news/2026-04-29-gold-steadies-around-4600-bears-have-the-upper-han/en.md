Gold (XAU/USD) hovered near the $4,600 level in early Asian trading, reflecting cautious sentiment as investors brace for the Federal Reserve’s two-day FOMC policy meeting, set to conclude Wednesday. The yellow metal showed signs of consolidation after recent volatility, with bears maintaining a subtle edge as traders weigh the potential impact of interest rate decisions on the USD and broader currency markets.  

The exchange rate dynamics suggest that a hawkish Fed stance could strengthen the dollar, putting downward pressure on gold. Conversely, a dovish shift might fuel trading interest in precious metals as an alternative hedge. Technical analysts note key support near $4,580 and resistance around $4,620, signaling limited room for aggressive moves until the Fed announcement clarifies monetary policy direction.  

Meanwhile, forex markets remain sensitive to risk sentiment, with USD/EUR and USD/JPY exchange rates reflecting broader reactions to anticipated policy shifts. Traders are adjusting positions across currency pairs, balancing gold exposure against potential shifts in the dollar. Volatility is expected to rise, making trading decisions cautious as market participants digest both economic indicators and central bank guidance in the coming sessions.
