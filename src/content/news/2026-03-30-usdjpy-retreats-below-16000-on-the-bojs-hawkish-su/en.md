The USD/JPY exchange rate retreated on Monday, falling 0.38% to around 159.70 after briefly surging to a new year-to-date high of 160.50 earlier in the session. The pullback came as the market digested the Bank of Japan's hawkish summary, ahead of the release of Tokyo's Consumer Price Index (CPI) data later this week. 

Despite hitting key technical resistance near the 160.00 level, the currency pair remains sensitive to shifts in market expectations surrounding Japan's monetary policy. A stronger-than-expected Tokyo CPI could further support a bullish outlook for the yen, potentially keeping USD/JPY under pressure as traders adjust their positions in anticipation of potential central bank actions.
