The USD/JPY currency pair edged higher on Thursday, trading around the 159.20 level after rebounding roughly 100 pips from its intraday low. Investors weighed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East alongside the ongoing divergence in monetary policy between the Federal Reserve and the Bank of Japan, which continues to support the US Dollar against the Japanese Yen. Daily gains remain modest but reflect cautious risk sentiment.  

Traders noted that the uptick in the USD/JPY exchange rate underscores a flight to safety, with the greenback benefiting from its safe-haven status amid uncertainty. Meanwhile, speculation around upcoming Fed rate decisions has bolstered USD strength, contrasting with the BOJ’s commitment to ultra-loose policy, keeping pressure on the yen. Market participants are closely monitoring price levels near 159.20 for potential continuation or pullbacks.  

Across the broader forex market, USD demand is influencing other pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, as investors recalibrate positions in light of geopolitical risks and central bank guidance. Technical traders are watching intraday support and resistance levels, noting that a sustained move above 159.20 could signal further USD gains, while any reversal might create short-term yen buying opportunities. Volatility is expected to remain elevated in the coming sessions.
