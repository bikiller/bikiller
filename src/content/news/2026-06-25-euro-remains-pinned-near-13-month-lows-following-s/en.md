The euro (EUR) continues to trade under pressure against the US dollar (USD) on Thursday, extending its losing streak to a fourth consecutive session. The currency pair remains pinned near 13-month lows as sellers maintain control in the forex market, with EUR/USD edging lower amid persistent downside momentum. Trading activity reflects cautious sentiment as the exchange rate struggles to recover.

Pressure on the euro is being driven by disappointing German consumer confidence, which has weighed on sentiment across the Eurozone and reinforced concerns over economic fragility in the region. Traders have reacted by favoring the US dollar, supporting broader USD strength in the currency market as risk appetite remains subdued.

From a trading perspective, EUR/USD remains vulnerable as the exchange rate hovers near multi-month lows, with market participants closely watching whether the currency pair can stabilize or extend losses further. Focus remains on upcoming data releases and central bank expectations, which could determine near-term direction for the euro against the USD in volatile forex conditions.
