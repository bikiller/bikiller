BNP Paribas analysts forecast that the US economy will outpace expectations in 2026, with GDP growth projected at 2.7% and inflation at 3.1%. This robust outlook suggests a steady monetary policy, with the Federal Reserve likely to maintain its current stance. The Fed Funds target range is expected to remain between 3.5%-3.75%, keeping the USD relatively stable in the near term.

The outlook for the USD is one of gradual depreciation as global factors weigh on the currency. Despite the expected economic strength, the USD could face downward pressure against major peers like the EUR, especially as other central banks potentially tighten their policies. Traders may look to adjust their positions as the year progresses, anticipating a more dovish Fed amid global market shifts.
