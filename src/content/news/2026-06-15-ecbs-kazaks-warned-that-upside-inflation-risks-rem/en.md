European Central Bank Governing Council member Martins Kazaks signaled on Monday that the ECB remains prepared to raise interest rates if inflation pressures persist, stressing that upside inflation risks remain intact. Comments followed a US–Iran deal, which lifted sentiment but did not alter the euro area inflation outlook according to his remarks.

Currency markets showed a cautious response, with the euro currency pair EUR/USD holding within a narrow range as traders weighed ECB tightening expectations against improving geopolitical sentiment. The US dollar remained supported by safe-haven flows, while euro exchange rate volatility stayed contained. Investors recalibrated interest rate expectations, focusing on inflation trajectory and ECB forward guidance.

Looking ahead, the outlook for the EUR/USD exchange rate and broader forex trading activity hinges on whether inflation remains elevated enough to justify further ECB tightening. Persistent upside inflation risks could support the euro, while a softer outlook would favor the US dollar. Traders will closely monitor incoming data and central bank communications for directional cues.
