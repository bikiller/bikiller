The ongoing conflict between Iran and global powers could undermine the petrodollar system, a key pillar supporting the USD's position as the world's reserve currency, according to Deutsche Bank's Mallika Sachdeva. This disruption could lead to shifts in global trading dynamics, particularly affecting USD-based currency pairs. As tensions escalate, traders are closely watching the EUR/USD exchange rate for potential volatility, with the dollar's dominance facing challenges from alternative currencies.

Should Iran successfully reduce reliance on the USD for oil transactions, it may signal the beginning of a broader trend, shaking investor confidence in the dollar's supremacy. This scenario would impact major pairs like USD/JPY and USD/GBP, with technical levels of 1.1000 in EUR/USD and 1.2500 in GBP/USD drawing attention. The market's reaction to geopolitical events will be crucial in determining the dollar's future role in global trading and foreign exchange markets.
