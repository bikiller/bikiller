WTI Crude Oil surged by 3% on Tuesday, climbing back above the $90.00 per barrel mark after a sharp 11% decline on Monday. The rebound came as market doubts over the strength of the recent sell-off emerged, with traders reassessing potential factors influencing the commodity. As oil prices recover, traders are keeping an eye on global economic indicators and OPEC's future decisions, which could further impact price movements.

The bounce also had implications for the USD, as crude prices and the dollar often move in opposition. With rising oil prices, the EUR/USD exchange rate showed subtle shifts, reflecting broader market sentiment. Traders are likely to maintain a cautious stance in the short term, monitoring both oil price trends and geopolitical factors that could sway the forex markets in the coming weeks.
