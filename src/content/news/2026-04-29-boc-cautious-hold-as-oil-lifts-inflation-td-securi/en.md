TD Securities anticipates the Bank of Canada (BoC) will hold its policy rate steady at 2.25% in the upcoming April decision, signaling a cautious approach amid rising energy costs. The April Monetary Policy Report is expected to revise headline inflation higher, driven by elevated oil prices, while core inflation projections remain largely unchanged. Analysts suggest this could reinforce the Canadian dollar’s resilience against the USD and EUR in the near term.  

Market participants are closely watching the CAD/USD and CAD/EUR currency pairs, with traders pricing in limited BoC tightening. The exchange rate may see modest volatility as investors digest the updated inflation forecasts and the BoC’s cautious stance. Higher energy prices supporting inflation could sustain upward pressure on the loonie, though muted core revisions might temper aggressive market reactions.  

Technically, CAD/USD has found support around 0.7350, while resistance near 0.7450 could cap short-term gains. EUR/CAD is hovering near 1.4450, with trading influenced by oil-driven inflation expectations and central bank messaging. Overall, the BoC’s measured tone is likely to maintain steady trading conditions, keeping the loonie supported but leaving room for range-bound movements against major currencies.
