Gold (XAU/USD) surged at the start of the week, opening with a bullish gap as geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran intensified. The growing conflict has led to a spike in safe-haven demand, pushing the precious metal to new highs. As of now, XAU/USD is trading near $5,386, its highest level in over a month, reflecting investor flight to safety.

The heightened uncertainty in the Middle East has sparked a rush into gold, which is traditionally seen as a hedge during times of geopolitical instability. With the US dollar (USD) under pressure, the XAU/USD exchange rate has benefited, and traders are closely watching for further developments in the US-Iran conflict. If tensions persist, gold could continue to climb, with potential resistance levels now eyed at higher price points.
