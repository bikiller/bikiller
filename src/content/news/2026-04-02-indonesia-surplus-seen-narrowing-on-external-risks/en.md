Indonesia's trade surplus expanded slightly in February 2026, reaching USD 1.27 billion, marking its 70th consecutive month of growth. According to UOB’s Global Economics & Markets Research, led by Enrico Tanuwidjaja and Vincentius Ming Shen, the surplus was driven by steady export performance, although external risks are likely to narrow future gains. The ongoing global uncertainty, particularly in major trading partners, could impact Indonesia's export demand, influencing the country’s economic outlook.

As the USD/IDR pair remains sensitive to shifts in Indonesia’s external trade dynamics, this surplus may offer limited support for the rupiah in the coming months. Traders should monitor global trade tensions and commodity price movements, as these factors are expected to weigh on Indonesia’s economic performance. The continued risk of volatility in key markets, coupled with a narrowing trade surplus, could see the rupiah facing additional pressure, affecting trading strategies in the broader forex market.
