Brent crude oil prices have surged significantly as the ongoing Middle East conflict escalates, and the Strait of Hormuz remains effectively closed. The disruption in one of the world’s most crucial oil shipping routes has heightened supply concerns, sending Brent to near $95 per barrel, a sharp increase of nearly 4% over recent days. The tight supply outlook is also weighing on global markets, driving further volatility in currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY, where the U.S. dollar benefits from safe-haven demand.

The uncertainty surrounding Middle East tensions is expected to keep oil prices elevated, potentially influencing broader market dynamics. As crude oil remains a key input for global inflation, elevated prices are likely to put pressure on central banks, with implications for exchange rates and monetary policies. Traders are closely watching technical levels for signs of resistance, with $100 per barrel in sight if the geopolitical crisis persists. The U.S. dollar is expected to remain resilient amid these risks, while the euro faces pressure from rising energy costs in the region.
