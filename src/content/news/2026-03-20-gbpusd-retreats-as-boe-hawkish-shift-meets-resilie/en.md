GBP/USD pulled back on Friday, trading around 1.3380, down 0.39% on the day, after surging in the previous session. Thursday’s rally followed the Bank of England’s unexpected hawkish stance, which initially boosted the pound against major currencies. However, the resilient US dollar regained footing, pressuring the exchange rate lower and capping gains in the currency pair. Traders weighed BoE policy signals against persistent USD strength.  

Technical indicators suggest the pound faces resistance near recent highs, while support levels around 1.3350 may provide short-term stability. The retreat reflects market caution, as investors digest divergent central bank policies, with the Federal Reserve showing persistent hawkishness that continues to underpin the dollar across FX markets. GBP/USD trading remains sensitive to economic data and interest rate expectations on both sides of the Atlantic.  

Market participants are monitoring upcoming US and UK releases for clues on inflation and growth, which could sway the currency pair further. Analysts note that sustained USD strength could limit further pound gains, while any shift in BoE messaging may trigger renewed volatility. Traders are balancing positioning in GBP, USD, and other major currencies such as EUR, looking for opportunities amid the ongoing tug-of-war in forex markets.
