EUR/USD is climbing on Thursday, pushing back toward the 1.1700 mark as traders respond to ongoing market dynamics. The euro is showing resilience against the US dollar despite fresh Eurozone data signaling weak economic growth coupled with persistently high inflation. At the time of writing, the currency pair hovers just below 1.1700, with investors weighing the implications for monetary policy and potential interest rate adjustments.  

Market participants are navigating a mixed backdrop: Eurozone growth remains subdued, yet inflationary pressures persist, prompting speculation on whether the European Central Bank may maintain or even tighten policy further. Meanwhile, the USD is facing headwinds from softer domestic data, which is fueling demand for the euro in the forex market. This interplay is keeping the exchange rate elevated, even as macro indicators paint a cautious economic picture.  

Technically, EUR/USD is testing resistance near 1.1700, with short-term trading momentum favoring the euro. Analysts suggest that if the currency pair sustains levels above 1.1680–1.1690, further upside toward 1.1720 could be possible. Traders are closely monitoring Eurozone inflation trends and US dollar performance, understanding that shifts in either could quickly alter the forex landscape and create fresh volatility in this major currency pair.
