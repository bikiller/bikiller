The GBP/USD currency pair saw a slight uptick as weak US jobs data helped offset broader market risk aversion. The Pound Sterling edged higher, trading at 1.3190, up 0.04%, after earlier sentiment was weighed down by geopolitical tensions. Iran's IRGC threatened to target US companies starting April 1, prompting concerns that soured investor sentiment.

While the US Dollar initially trimmed some of its losses, the overall impact of weak employment numbers weighed on USD strength. The trading range remained tight as the GBP/USD tested technical support levels, holding steady just above 1.3190. Despite the market's initial caution, the impact of geopolitical developments was short-lived, with traders maintaining a cautious outlook on both currencies amid ongoing global uncertainties.
