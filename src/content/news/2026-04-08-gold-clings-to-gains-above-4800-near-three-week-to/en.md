Gold (XAU/USD) continues to hold its gains above the $4,800 level, hovering near a three-week high recorded on Wednesday. The precious metal has shown resilience through the first half of the European session, benefiting from a broadly softer USD. Traders note that the dollar’s retreat against major currency pairs, including the EUR/USD, has provided additional support to gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset.  

Market participants attribute the weaker USD to ongoing concerns over U.S. economic data and shifting expectations around Federal Reserve policy. As the greenback loses ground, gold trading has picked up momentum, reflecting investor appetite for alternative stores of value. Technical analysts point to strong support near $4,780, while the next resistance zone remains around $4,820, indicating potential for continued upside if the dollar remains under pressure.  

The current exchange rate dynamics highlight a sensitive balance between currencies, with traders closely watching both USD movements and broader macroeconomic cues. With gold maintaining above $4,800, market sentiment suggests cautious optimism, as volatility in currency pairs could continue to influence precious metals. Short-term traders are likely to monitor EUR/USD and other major pairs for signals that might drive further gold trading activity.
