The Indian Rupee (INR) weakened against the US Dollar (USD) in early Thursday trading, with the USD/INR pair rising to 92.85 from the previous day’s three-week low of 92.20. The rebound follows the announcement of a ceasefire agreement between the United States and Iran, which sparked concerns about the sustainability of the truce.

Despite the positive news, market participants remain cautious about potential geopolitical risks. The INR’s recovery reflects investor uncertainty surrounding the long-term implications of the ceasefire. Traders are closely monitoring developments, with further fluctuations in the USD/INR likely as the situation evolves.
