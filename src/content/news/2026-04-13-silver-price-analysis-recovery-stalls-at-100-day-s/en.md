Silver (XAG/USD) staged a brief recovery on Monday, rebounding from daily lows of $72.61. However, the precious metal is set to finish the day with a slight loss of 0.33%, trading at $75.58 at the time of writing. The initial drop came over the weekend as news of a deteriorating US-Iran conflict raised concerns in the market, leading to risk-off sentiment.

The recovery remains capped at the 100-day simple moving average (SMA), suggesting that selling pressure is still prevalent. Traders are eyeing the $75 level closely, with further downside risk if the exchange rate fails to maintain its footing. A break below $75 could open the door for a deeper pullback in XAG/USD.
