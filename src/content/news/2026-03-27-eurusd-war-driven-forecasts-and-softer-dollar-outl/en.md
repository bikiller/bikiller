Commerzbank has revised its outlook for the Eurozone, lowering its 2026 growth forecast amid ongoing geopolitical tensions. Chief Economist Jörg Krämer highlighted that weaker economic momentum is likely to temper the European Central Bank’s rate hikes, suggesting the EUR/USD exchange rate could face limited upside pressure. The bank now anticipates fewer ECB tightening steps than futures currently price in, signaling a more cautious trading environment for the euro.  

At the same time, Commerzbank expects the US Federal Reserve to implement more rate cuts than the market currently predicts. This divergence in monetary policy paths could weigh on the USD initially, offering potential support for the EUR/USD pair in coming sessions. Traders are watching how shifting expectations on interest rates and growth data influence short-term currency pair volatility and broader market sentiment.  

Market participants should consider these dynamics when trading the euro against the dollar, as subdued eurozone growth and an evolving Fed outlook create a complex backdrop. Technical levels may see increased testing, with the EUR/USD exchange rate sensitive to both geopolitical developments and central bank signals. The combination of softer eurozone growth forecasts and an anticipated Fed easing cycle sets the stage for a nuanced currency market landscape in 2026.
