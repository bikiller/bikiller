The British Pound slipped against the US Dollar on Thursday, with the GBP/USD currency pair losing around 0.20% amid renewed uncertainty over a potential US-Iran deal. Sterling retreated from an intraday high near 1.3454, trading at 1.3406 as investors reacted to mixed signals in the geopolitical arena. The market’s risk sentiment leaned toward the USD, supporting broad dollar strength across major currency pairs.  

Weaker UK business activity compounded Sterling’s struggles, as a soft PMI report highlighted cooling growth in the domestic economy. Traders noted that sluggish manufacturing and services output may keep the Bank of England on hold regarding future monetary policy, weighing on the exchange rate. Market participants also eyed the relative resilience of the US economy, which bolstered the USD against the Pound.  

Technical levels suggest that 1.3400 could act as near-term support for GBP/USD, while resistance remains around 1.3450. The combination of geopolitical uncertainty and muted UK economic indicators indicates continued volatility in the currency pair, prompting cautious trading strategies. Forex traders are watching for fresh data and developments that could shift the balance between Sterling and the greenback.
