The Australian Dollar edged lower against the US Dollar on Thursday, with the AUD/USD currency pair slipping after a four-day rally. The Greenback regained footing following a period of weakness, boosting the exchange rate and putting pressure on risk-sensitive currencies. Traders noted that the AUD/USD struggled around recent intraday highs, with the rebound in USD driving the pair toward support levels near 0.6800.  

Market participants are also keeping a close eye on crude oil prices and geopolitical tensions, which have influenced risk sentiment. Gains in the US Dollar were underpinned by safe-haven demand as uncertainties in global markets prompted cautious trading. Meanwhile, the Australian Dollar faced headwinds from slower-than-expected domestic data, limiting its ability to extend gains against major currencies like the USD and EUR.  

Technically, AUD/USD faces resistance near 0.6850, while immediate support sits at 0.6800. Traders are monitoring momentum indicators and intraday volumes for signs of continuation or reversal. The broader outlook suggests that the currency pair may remain range-bound unless significant shifts occur in commodity markets or geopolitical developments, which could steer trading sentiment and influence near-term exchange rate movements.
