The Canadian dollar fell 0.40% on Tuesday as the US dollar strengthened, refreshing year-to-date highs, while weaker oil prices added pressure. In forex trading, USD/CAD broke above the 1.4200 level for the first time since April 2025, signaling renewed bullish momentum in the currency pair.

Broad USD strength dominated currency markets, pushing major currency pairs lower against the greenback. Commodity-linked currencies, particularly the CAD, underperformed as crude oil softness reduced support for Canada’s export outlook. Traders continued to favor the dollar amid tightening expectations and uneven global risk sentiment, reinforcing upward pressure on USD/CAD exchange rate dynamics.

From a technical perspective, USD/CAD holding above 1.4200 suggests a potential shift in near-term trend structure, with momentum traders eyeing follow-through buying. Continued oil weakness could further weigh on the Canadian dollar, while sustained USD strength may keep volatility elevated across forex markets, especially in North American trading sessions.
