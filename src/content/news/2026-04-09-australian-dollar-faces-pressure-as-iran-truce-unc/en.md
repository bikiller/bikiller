The Australian Dollar (AUD) is under pressure during Thursday's European session, trading 0.2% lower against the US Dollar (USD) at around 0.7030. The currency pair’s decline reflects a slight revival of the risk-off sentiment in the market, triggered by renewed uncertainty surrounding the truce in Iran. As global investors react to geopolitical concerns, demand for safer assets, like the USD, continues to rise.

The AUD's retreat comes as global risk appetite falters, with market participants seeking refuge in traditionally stable currencies. The pair has struggled to maintain momentum above key technical support levels, reflecting broader market unease. In the absence of fresh bullish catalysts, the Australian Dollar faces continued pressure, leaving traders cautious and the outlook uncertain.
