The Japanese Yen (JPY) is struggling to attract buyers as concerns over fiscal policy overshadow a modest recovery against the US Dollar (USD). After dipping during the Asian trading session, the JPY managed to reverse its losses, but bulls are hesitant to commit further. The USD/JPY exchange rate remains under pressure, highlighting the mixed fundamental factors affecting market sentiment.

Tokyo's consumer price index (CPI) data has provided some support, yet the broader fiscal concerns continue to weigh heavily on the JPY. As traders navigate these complexities, the currency pair is experiencing limited follow-through in upward momentum. With the current exchange rate hovering around the 147.00 level, market participants are closely monitoring developments for clearer direction.
