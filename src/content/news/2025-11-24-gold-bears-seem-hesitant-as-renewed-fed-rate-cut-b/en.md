Gold (XAU/USD) is struggling to gain momentum in early European trading on Monday, trading just above the $4,050 mark, down 0.35% for the day. The metal's bearish sentiment appears restrained as renewed expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts limit the recent strength of the USD.

This hesitant performance comes amid fluctuating exchange rates, as traders weigh the implications of potential monetary policy shifts. While the dollar has seen a rally, the impact of these Fed rate cut bets is keeping gold bears cautious, indicating a complex interplay between currency movements and precious metal trading.
