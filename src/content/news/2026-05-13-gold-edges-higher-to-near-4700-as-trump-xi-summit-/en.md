Gold held steady near $4,700 in early Asian trading on Thursday, showing modest gains as investors tread cautiously ahead of the high-stakes summit between US President Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping in Beijing. The safe-haven metal’s movements coincided with a softer USD tone, as traders weighed potential geopolitical developments against ongoing currency pair dynamics across EUR/USD and USD/JPY.

Market participants are eyeing the summit’s outcome for hints on trade negotiations and tariffs, factors that could influence global liquidity and FX volatility. Technical support around $4,680 remains a focus, while resistance near $4,710 could cap short-term advances. Trading volumes in XAU/USD suggest a cautious stance, with hedging strategies in other currency pairs like GBP/USD and AUD/USD reflecting broader risk sentiment.

Analysts note that any breakthroughs or escalations in the Trump-Xi discussions could trigger rapid shifts across commodities and currency markets. A positive resolution may ease pressure on the USD, prompting traders to rotate into riskier assets, while a deadlock could boost gold’s appeal as a hedge. Investors are monitoring real-time exchange rates and cross-asset correlations to adjust positions ahead of the anticipated policy signals.
