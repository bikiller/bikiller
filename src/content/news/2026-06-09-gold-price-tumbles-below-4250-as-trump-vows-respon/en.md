Gold (XAU/USD) tumbled sharply on Tuesday, sliding nearly 2% as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East spiked. The precious metal breached the $4,250 level, down 1.93% on the day, following US President Donald Trump’s warning of a response to Iran’s downing of a US helicopter near the Strait of Hormuz. Traders reacted swiftly, with safe-haven demand for gold giving way to heightened risk aversion in currency markets.  

The sudden sell-off in XAU/USD also rippled across major currency pairs. The US dollar gained modest traction against the euro and other key currencies as investors sought liquidity amid uncertainty. Forex traders noted that the exchange rate movements were closely tied to headline risk, with market positioning in gold, USD, and EUR/USD reflecting rapid shifts in sentiment.  

Analysts caution that further escalation could intensify volatility across commodities and forex markets. A rebound in USD trading could pressure other safe-haven assets, while gold may test key technical support below $4,250. Market participants are closely watching developments near the Strait of Hormuz, with trading volumes in XAU/USD and major FX pairs likely to remain elevated in the near term.
