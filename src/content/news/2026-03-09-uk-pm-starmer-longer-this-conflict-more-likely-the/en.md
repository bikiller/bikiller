UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer warned Monday that the longer the conflict in the Middle East persists, the greater the potential economic fallout. Speaking during European trading hours, he emphasized ongoing discussions with international partners to mitigate risks to global markets. The news heightened volatility across major currency pairs, with the USD and EUR seeing notable swings as traders reassessed risk sentiment.  

The GBP/USD exchange rate briefly dipped below 1.2700 amid growing uncertainty, reflecting investor caution over possible supply chain disruptions and higher commodity prices. Meanwhile, EUR/USD trading held near 1.0950, with the euro facing pressure from rising geopolitical risk premiums. Analysts noted that prolonged instability could fuel demand for safe-haven currencies like the USD and JPY, pushing yields and exchange rates in unpredictable directions.  

Market participants remain alert to any policy responses from the UK and its allies, with Starmer signaling that coordinated measures may be implemented to shield the economy. Forex traders are watching technical levels closely, including GBP/USD support around 1.2650 and EUR/USD resistance near 1.1000. The combination of geopolitical tension and proactive diplomacy is expected to drive heightened trading activity and sustained volatility in the coming sessions.
