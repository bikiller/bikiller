The USD/JPY currency pair edged higher on Monday, trading around 158.10, marking a 0.10% gain for the day. Investors reacted to rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which added a risk premium to the market. The Japanese yen remained under pressure as energy prices surged, with oil’s sharp advance prompting traders to reassess safe-haven flows and currency exposures.  

Market participants noted that the exchange rate’s rise reflects a broader risk-on sentiment in forex trading. The combination of Middle East uncertainties and climbing oil prices has put the yen at a disadvantage against the dollar, while the USD continues to show resilience amid global demand for safe-haven assets. Analysts suggest the pair may face further testing near key technical levels if these conditions persist.  

Traders are also keeping an eye on the euro and other major currencies, as shifts in risk sentiment could influence cross-currency flows. The USD/JPY’s current trajectory signals potential volatility ahead, with momentum supported by the ongoing energy market swings and geopolitical concerns. Strategic positioning in forex markets is likely to remain cautious as investors weigh these dual pressures.
