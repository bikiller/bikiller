GBP/USD extended gains during Thursday Asian trading hours, climbing toward the 1.3290 area and briefly testing the psychological 1.3300 level. The currency pair showed renewed bullish momentum as the British Pound strengthened against the US Dollar in early trading. The exchange rate reflected improved risk sentiment in the forex market, with traders positioning ahead of key macro catalysts.

Sentiment around the British Pound improved after reports that UK’s likely next Prime Minister Andy Burnham has committed to maintaining strict fiscal discipline. This stance helped ease concerns over potential fiscal slippage, supporting demand for the currency. As a result, the GBP benefited broadly across major currency pairs against the USD and other peers in trading.

Looking ahead, investors remain focused on upcoming US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) data, which could drive near-term USD volatility and influence the GBP/USD exchange rate. A stronger-than-expected US labor report may revive USD demand and cap further upside, while softer data could extend pound gains above the 1.3300 resistance level in forex trading.
