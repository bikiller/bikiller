Gold (XAU/USD) faced renewed selling pressure on Friday, following a failed attempt to break the $4,800 mark the previous day. The precious metal slid to the $4,738-$4,737 range during the Asian session, as concerns over escalating tensions in the Hormuz Strait fueled inflationary fears. This uncertainty is contributing to expectations of tighter U.S. monetary policy, with traders increasingly betting on hawkish moves from the Federal Reserve.

The current pullback in gold prices highlights the market's sensitivity to geopolitical risks and the Federal Reserve's stance on inflation. As gold struggles to maintain bullish momentum, traders are positioning for potential volatility ahead of the upcoming U.S. CPI data release, which could further influence the USD exchange rate. With inflation and interest rate expectations in focus, the outlook for gold remains closely tied to broader market dynamics.
