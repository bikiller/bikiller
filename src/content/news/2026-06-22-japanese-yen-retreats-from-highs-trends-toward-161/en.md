USD/JPY currency pair slid to an intraday low of 161.07 on Monday, easing back from highs near 161.90 as the Japanese Yen staged a modest recovery from extreme weakness. The exchange rate remains pinned near a four-decade peak zone, keeping forex trading conditions highly volatile as market participants reassess directional momentum in USD-driven flows.  

Sentiment across the forex market is being shaped by heightened caution over potential Japanese intervention, as the currency pair hovers close to levels historically associated with official action. Meanwhile, broader USD strength and divergent yield expectations continue to support the dollar, while EUR and other majors remain sensitive to shifts in risk appetite and liquidity conditions.  

Technically, traders are eyeing the 161.00 handle as a key psychological level for USD/JPY, with break risks potentially accelerating downside volatility if intervention fears intensify. For now, trading activity remains reactive, with the exchange rate consolidating gains after testing extreme highs and leaving market participants alert to sudden policy-driven swings.
