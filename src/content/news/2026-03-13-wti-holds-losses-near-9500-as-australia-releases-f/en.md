WTI crude oil prices are holding modest losses near $95.20 per barrel after a strong 9% rally in the previous session. The price has been under pressure amid Australia’s decision to release fuel reserves, adding bearish sentiment to the market. This development comes as oil traders digest global supply concerns and fluctuating demand forecasts, with WTI trading closely tied to broader risk sentiment in the financial markets.

The U.S. dollar continues to influence oil markets, as fluctuations in the USD can affect global commodity pricing. As WTI remains near the $95 mark, oil traders are closely monitoring the broader economic landscape, including currency pair movements and geopolitical developments. The recent surge in WTI was spurred by supply fears, but with fuel reserves being tapped, a reversal could be in sight if market sentiment shifts.
