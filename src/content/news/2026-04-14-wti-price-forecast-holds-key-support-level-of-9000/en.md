West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures on NYMEX edged lower by nearly 0.6%, trading around $92.00 in the European session on Tuesday. Despite the dip, oil prices remain above the key support level of $90.00. Traders are weighing optimism surrounding a potential second round of US-Iran talks, which is prompting profit-taking and adding volatility to commodities and related currency pairs, particularly those sensitive to energy prices.  

The US dollar (USD) has shown mixed trading against major currencies like the euro (EUR) and yen (JPY), as the market digests the implications of oil movements. A stronger USD could weigh on crude demand in dollar-denominated markets, while fluctuations in oil can feed through to energy-linked currencies such as the CAD and NOK, influencing their exchange rates in short-term trading.  

Market participants are monitoring technical levels closely, with $90.00 serving as a critical floor for WTI. A break below could trigger broader risk-off sentiment, affecting forex pairs and commodities alike. Meanwhile, optimism around diplomatic talks supports sentiment, keeping traders cautious but attentive to sudden spikes in volatility and potential ripple effects across USD-based trades.
