The US Dollar Index (DXY) fell to 98.80 as market reaction to the latest US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) report revealed persistent inflation pressures. Despite a slight decline in labor market conditions, inflation’s persistence solidified the Federal Reserve's cautious stance, leading to a dip in the USD's strength. This mixed economic data highlighted the complexity of the Fed’s decision-making process in the coming months.

As inflation continues to be sticky, trading in major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY saw increased volatility. EUR/USD gained some traction, with the exchange rate moving higher, while USD/JPY fluctuated within a tight range. The Fed's ongoing balancing act between inflation control and labor market concerns could keep the USD under pressure in the short term, influencing broader forex market sentiment.
