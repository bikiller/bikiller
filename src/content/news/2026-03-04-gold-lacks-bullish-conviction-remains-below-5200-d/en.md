Gold (XAU/USD) struggled to maintain bullish momentum on Wednesday, remaining below the critical level of $5,200 amid ongoing tensions in the Middle East. Despite experiencing slight intraday gains, the yellow metal has failed to attract significant buying interest, reflecting a cautious market sentiment as traders weigh geopolitical risks against economic data.

The prevailing uncertainty in the region has not translated into stronger upward movement for gold, suggesting that investors remain hesitant. As the European session progresses, the lack of conviction in gold's price action may have implications for trading strategies, particularly for those looking to hedge against currency fluctuations in the USD and EUR exchange rates. Gold's resistance at $5,200 continues to act as a barrier, indicating that a sustained breakout may require additional catalysts.
