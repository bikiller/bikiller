Standard Chartered analysts Chong Hoon Park and Nicholas Chia expect the USD/JPY currency pair to continue on a higher trajectory into March. With the Bank of Japan maintaining its policy rate at 0.75% on March 19, the outlook for the Japanese yen remains subdued. The Bank's cautious stance reflects concerns over uneven growth and rising oil prices, which are likely to continue weighing on the yen.

As the USD/JPY trading range shifts upward, market participants will monitor any shifts in the Bank of Japan’s policy stance. A steady rate may provide limited support for the yen, while the strengthening U.S. dollar could fuel further upward movement in the exchange rate. Higher oil prices are a crucial factor, influencing inflation and Japan's economic recovery.
