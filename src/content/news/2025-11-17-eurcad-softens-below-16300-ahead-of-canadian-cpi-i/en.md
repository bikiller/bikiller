The EUR/CAD exchange rate has dipped below the 1.6300 mark, trading around 1.6275 during the early European session on Monday. This decline comes ahead of the Canadian CPI inflation release, which is anticipated to impact market sentiment and trading strategies. 

While the currency pair has softened, analysts suggest that the potential for further downside may be constrained by the European Central Bank's cautious approach. With the ongoing economic developments, traders are closely monitoring inflation indicators that could prompt shifts in monetary policy from both the ECB and the Bank of Canada.
