The British Pound edged higher against the US Dollar on Thursday, with the GBP/USD currency pair climbing above the 1.3350 mark and reaching around 1.3385 during Asian trading hours. The move reflected modest demand for Sterling as traders assessed the latest developments in global currency markets. Despite the advance, gains remained measured as investors continued to monitor shifts in interest-rate expectations on both sides of the Atlantic.

Market sentiment was influenced by growing expectations that the US Federal Reserve could keep interest rates elevated for longer than previously anticipated. Rising Fed hike bets have supported the USD in recent sessions, limiting the upside potential for the GBP/USD exchange rate. Higher US yields generally strengthen the Dollar by increasing its appeal relative to other major currencies, including the British Pound and the EUR.

For forex traders, the latest price action highlights the balance between Sterling resilience and persistent Dollar strength. While the currency pair has managed to hold above the key 1.3350 level and trade near 1.3385, any further gains may face resistance if US economic data continues to reinforce expectations of a higher-for-longer policy stance from the Federal Reserve.
