WTI crude oil prices surged above $92.50, trading around $92.80 during Asian hours on Thursday. This uptick followed heightened tensions between the US and Iran, as Iran accused the US of violating parts of a two-week ceasefire agreement. The market response reflects growing concerns over stability in the Middle East, which has implications for global oil supply and pricing dynamics.

The rising crude prices also weigh on forex markets, particularly in relation to the USD. With oil prices climbing, the USD may face pressure as traders adjust their positions. As the US continues to navigate geopolitical issues, currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY may experience increased volatility. Investors are closely monitoring any developments that could influence the balance between oil supply and demand, along with broader market sentiment.
