The AUD/USD currency pair edged lower toward the 0.6960 level as traders digested mixed Australian inflation data. Core consumer prices showed a modest uptick, while broader inflation figures fell short of expectations, fueling uncertainty over the Reserve Bank of Australia’s next move. This divergence has left the exchange rate under pressure, with risk sentiment influencing flows alongside domestic economic signals.  

Global oil volatility added another layer of caution, prompting market participants to reassess positions in commodity-linked currencies such as the Australian dollar. As investors weigh the implications of energy-driven inflation against central bank guidance, trading activity in the AUD/USD remains choppy, with short-term momentum showing vulnerability near key support levels. The pair’s movement also reflects broader USD strength, supported by safe-haven demand and ongoing geopolitical concerns.  

Technically, AUD/USD faces resistance near 0.7000, while support holds around 0.6950–0.6960. Traders are watching for shifts in both domestic policy cues and global risk appetite, which could drive further swings against the USD or even impact EUR and other major currency pairs. Volatility in commodities and inflation surprises are expected to keep trading ranges tight, with the market poised for decisive moves once the next RBA signals emerge.
