Canadian dollar strengthened against the US dollar on Thursday as a weaker-than-expected US employment report weighed on the greenback, driving the USD/CAD currency pair lower. The exchange rate slipped to 1.4180, down 0.26% on the day, after briefly touching 1.4150, its lowest level in more than a week, reflecting renewed selling pressure on USD in forex trading.

Market participants reacted to the soft US jobs data by reassessing Federal Reserve rate expectations, which added downside pressure to the US dollar across major currency pairs. In trading sessions, USD/CAD extended its decline as sellers tested the 1.4150 level, now seen as a near-term support zone after the exchange rate broke below recent consolidation ranges.

Looking ahead, forex traders will monitor upcoming US economic releases for further direction, with sustained USD weakness potentially allowing the Canadian dollar to maintain gains. The pair’s movement below 1.4180 signals continued volatility in the currency market, and any break below 1.4150 could open the door to deeper losses in USD/CAD.
