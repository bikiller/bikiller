GBP/JPY staged a slight recovery on Friday, after earlier falling under pressure, largely due to ongoing market intervention by Tokyo to stabilize the weakening Japanese Yen (JPY). The currency pair found support at the 100-day simple moving average (SMA), which helped curb further losses. However, the overall momentum remains subdued as traders remain cautious amid global uncertainty.

The Bank of Japan's intervention strategy appears to be a key factor driving price action in GBP/JPY, with Tokyo stepping in for the second consecutive day to prevent excessive Yen depreciation. As the pair hovers near critical technical levels, including the 100-day SMA, traders are closely monitoring any further signs of government action, which could influence future trading dynamics in the JPY and other currency pairs such as USD/JPY.
