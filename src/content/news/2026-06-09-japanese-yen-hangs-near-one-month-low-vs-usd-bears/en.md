The Japanese Yen hovered near a one-month low against the US Dollar on Tuesday, as the USD/JPY currency pair struggled to extend modest gains from the Asian session. Spot prices remained above the key 160.00 psychological level, despite a softer backdrop for the US Dollar across global markets. Traders appeared cautious, weighing intervention risks that could curb further Yen weakness.  

Market participants noted that the USD/JPY failed to sustain the previous day’s peak, its highest since April 30, reflecting a mix of technical resistance and lingering uncertainty. While the US Dollar broadly softened against other major currencies, including the Euro and the British Pound, the Yen showed limited follow-through, suggesting hesitancy among bears. Trading volumes remained moderate as investors monitored policy signals and geopolitical developments.  

Analysts highlighted that the Yen’s recent softness could fuel intervention speculation, keeping aggressive selling in check. Any decisive break above 160.00 could invite renewed attention from policymakers, while a reversal might offer relief for the USD/JPY. Investors continue to watch closely for cues from US inflation data, ECB commentary, and risk sentiment shifts, which could drive near-term exchange rate movements in major currency pairs.
