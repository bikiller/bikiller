TD Securities strategist Pooja Kumra highlights that ongoing disruptions in Qatar and Iran justify a higher oil price baseline for 2026 compared to pre-crisis levels. The bank maintains its expectation for oil prices to remain in the mid-$90s range. These geopolitical tensions have shifted the market's outlook, suggesting that oil prices could remain elevated, pushing inflationary pressures globally.

The potential for even a 10% increase in oil prices could have significant ripple effects, notably boosting headline inflation. This dynamic has implications for key currency pairs, with traders closely monitoring the USD/EUR and other crosses for shifts in risk sentiment. Oil prices remain a crucial driver in forex markets, influencing exchange rate movements and overall market sentiment.
