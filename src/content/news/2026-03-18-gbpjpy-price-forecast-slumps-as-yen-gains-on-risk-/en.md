The GBP/JPY exchange rate saw a decline of 0.20% on Wednesday, reflecting growing risk aversion among investors. The pair traded lower as market participants awaited the Bank of Japan's (BOJ) monetary policy decision scheduled for Thursday. With the yen strengthening against the pound, traders are cautious, monitoring any signals from the BOJ regarding potential changes in its monetary stance.

This drop in GBP/JPY is part of a broader market shift as the yen gains appeal amid concerns over global economic risks. The market's focus now shifts to the BOJ's decision, which could influence short-term movements in the currency pair. Investors are particularly attentive to any hints of future tightening, which would likely bolster the yen further, adding pressure to the pound in the short term.
