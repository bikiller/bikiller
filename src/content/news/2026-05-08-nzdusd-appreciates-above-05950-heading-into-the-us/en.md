The New Zealand Dollar strengthened against the US Dollar on Friday, with the NZD/USD currency pair climbing above the 0.5950 level ahead of the closely watched US Nonfarm Payrolls report. The exchange rate touched an intraday high of 0.5970 after rebounding from 0.5930 earlier in the session, reflecting renewed demand for risk-sensitive currencies during European trading hours.

Market participants remained cautious before the US labor market data release, which is expected to influence Federal Reserve policy expectations and broader USD trading sentiment. The softer tone in the Greenback encouraged buying interest in higher-yielding currencies, including the NZD, while traders also monitored movements across major forex pairs such as EUR/USD and AUD/USD for additional directional cues.

From a technical perspective, the recovery above 0.5950 signals improving short-term momentum for NZD/USD, with bulls attempting to maintain control near session highs. Analysts noted that sustained trading above the 0.5970 region could open the door toward further upside resistance, while failure to hold gains may see the pair retest support around 0.5930 as volatility increases following the US NFP release.
