Gold (XAU/USD) climbed toward $4,500 in early Asian trading on Friday, rebounding sharply from a two-month low touched in the previous session. The recovery follows reports that the United States and Iran reached a preliminary ceasefire extension, easing geopolitical tensions that had pressured safe-haven assets and influenced currency pairs such as USD/EUR and USD/JPY.

Traders noted that the surge in gold prices came amid broad USD weakness, as investors reassessed risk appetite. The precious metal’s rebound highlights its sensitivity to macro developments, with trading activity accelerating across forex markets. Analysts pointed out that a stabilization in the USD exchange rate could support further upward momentum in gold, while cross-currency volatility remains a key factor for portfolio hedges.

Market participants are now closely monitoring follow-up negotiations and regional developments, as the outlook for energy markets and global interest rates continues to intersect with forex trading. A sustained ceasefire could bolster investor confidence, potentially easing pressure on other safe-haven currencies and influencing trading strategies across major currency pairs and commodities. Gold’s near-term technical support is now seen near $4,450, with resistance around $4,520.
