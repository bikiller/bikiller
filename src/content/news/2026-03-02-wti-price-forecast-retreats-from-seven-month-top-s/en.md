WTI Crude Oil prices retreated from a seven-month high, easing from over $73.00 to trade near the $71.00 mark. The price surge earlier this week was driven by escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which triggered concerns over supply disruptions. Despite the pullback, the market remains firmly supported above the $70.00 level, maintaining a bullish outlook for the near term.

The rise in WTI prices has also impacted related markets, including the USD, as the stronger oil prices support the greenback. Traders are closely watching the developments in the Middle East, with any further intensification potentially driving prices higher. As crude oil continues to trade within a broad range, key technical levels like $73.00 and $70.00 will likely act as important support and resistance zones for the next phase of trading.
