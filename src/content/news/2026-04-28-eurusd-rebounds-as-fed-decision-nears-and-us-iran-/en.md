The EUR/USD currency pair staged a modest rebound on Tuesday, recovering some of its earlier intraday losses as the US Dollar showed signs of easing pressure. Traders are adjusting positions ahead of the Federal Reserve’s highly anticipated interest rate decision on Wednesday, which is expected to influence USD liquidity and broader market sentiment. The euro gained from oversold levels, while the dollar’s momentum slowed amid cautious trading.  

Market participants are also keeping a close eye on geopolitical developments, particularly tensions between the US and Iran, which have capped further USD strength. The exchange rate fluctuated near key technical levels, with EUR/USD trading around recent intraday lows before rebounding, reflecting the interplay between safe-haven flows and risk appetite. Analysts suggest this cautious trading highlights market sensitivity to both policy signals and external geopolitical risks.  

Technically, EUR/USD remains within a short-term range, with support and resistance levels guiding intraday strategies. The currency pair’s movements indicate traders are balancing expectations for the Fed’s policy outcome against ongoing global uncertainties. As USD momentum eases, EUR trading could see increased volatility, with potential implications for forex positions and cross-currency strategies ahead of central bank guidance.
