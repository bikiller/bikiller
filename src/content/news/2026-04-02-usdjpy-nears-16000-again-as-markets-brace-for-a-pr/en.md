The US Dollar (USD) is showing broad strength on Thursday, pushing the USD/JPY currency pair closer to the psychologically significant 160.00 level. Trading activity indicates a roughly 0.6% gain for the greenback against the Japanese Yen, as investors seek safe-haven assets amid rising geopolitical tensions. Market participants are adjusting positions cautiously, reflecting concerns over a potential protracted conflict involving Iran.  

Risk sentiment is driving flows across major currencies, with the Yen under particular pressure due to its traditional safe-haven role being offset by the dollar’s momentum. The USD is outperforming not only JPY but also trading stronger against the Euro (EUR) and other major peers, highlighting persistent appetite for US assets. Analysts note that the exchange rate approaching 160.00 may trigger technical selling or prompt central bank monitoring.  

Traders are weighing implications for both carry trades and cross-currency strategies, as the surge in USD/JPY could influence broader forex markets. With the geopolitical backdrop uncertain, currency pair volatility is expected to remain elevated, potentially affecting EUR/USD and other liquid pairs. Market watchers suggest that positioning ahead of key economic data could reinforce the current bullish bias in the dollar, keeping trading activity focused on technical thresholds and risk developments.
