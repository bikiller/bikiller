The International Energy Agency has issued a stark warning regarding potential disruptions in the oil supply, driven by rising tensions in Iran and the possibility of a blockade in the Strait of Hormuz. As a result, traders are witnessing increased volatility in Brent crude options, with prices soaring amid rising crack spreads.

These developments are influencing forex markets, particularly the USD and EUR exchange rates. The heightened risk in energy markets could lead to a stronger USD as investors seek safe-haven currencies amidst uncertainty. With oil prices surging, currency pairs sensitive to commodity fluctuations may experience amplified movements, underscoring the interconnectedness of energy security and forex trading dynamics.
