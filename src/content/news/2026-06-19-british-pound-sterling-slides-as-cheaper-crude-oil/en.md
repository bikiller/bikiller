The British Pound Sterling weakened sharply on Thursday despite a hawkish hold from the Bank of England, with GBP/USD slipping below the 1.3300 level and approaching 1.3200, its lowest point since early April. The central bank kept interest rates unchanged as expected, while a second member of the Monetary Policy Committee backed an increase in the Bank Rate. However, Sterling failed to capitalize on the policy stance, reflecting broader market concerns.

A key factor weighing on the currency pair was the decline in Crude Oil prices, which eased fears about inflationary pressures in the UK economy. Cheaper energy costs are reducing the inflation pipeline that previously supported expectations for tighter monetary policy. As a result, traders reassessed the likelihood of aggressive future rate hikes, prompting selling pressure on the Pound despite the BoE's relatively hawkish tone.

In forex trading, the move highlights a growing disconnect between central bank rhetoric and actual exchange rate performance. While higher rates typically support a currency, investors are increasingly focused on inflation trends, global demand, and risk sentiment. GBP/USD remains vulnerable around the 1.3200 technical area, while traders also monitor movements in EUR, USD, and broader cross-market signals for clues on the next directional move in Sterling.
