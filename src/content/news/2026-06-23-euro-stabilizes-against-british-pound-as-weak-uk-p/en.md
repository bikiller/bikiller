EUR/GBP traded little changed on Tuesday, stabilizing after a sharp slide in the previous session. The currency pair had come under pressure following the unexpected resignation of UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer, which triggered broad-based gains in the British pound and pushed the exchange rate lower across major crosses, including against the euro and USD-linked pairs. Trading activity reflected a brief reset after the volatility spike.

Market sentiment remained shaped by diverging economic signals, with weaker UK PMIs highlighting slowing domestic momentum, while Eurozone activity data proved more resilient. This contrast offered underlying support for the euro within broader forex trading conditions, though it was largely offset by political-driven strength in GBP flows. As a result, EUR/GBP stayed range-bound despite intraday fluctuations across the currency market.

Looking ahead, traders are focusing on upcoming UK economic releases and Eurozone indicators for clearer direction in the currency pair. Persistent weakness in UK data could reintroduce downside pressure on the pound and lift EUR/GBP, while shifts in risk appetite and USD movements may continue to steer broader forex exchange rate trends.
