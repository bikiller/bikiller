US Dollar gains extended in recent trading as the DXY index broke out of its prior consolidation range, according to HSBC strategists. The move reflects a broader shift in FX trading sentiment across major currency pairs after an interim US–Iran peace agreement reduced geopolitical risk premiums and encouraged renewed USD demand in exchange rate markets, with volatility easing across G10 currencies.

Fed-driven repricing has further supported the dollar, with traders adjusting expectations around the policy outlook following recent Federal Reserve communication. HSBC notes that this shift has contributed to USD strength versus peers, including EUR/USD and other G10 currency pairs, as investors reassess interest rate differentials and reposition in FX trading strategies amid changing exchange rate dynamics across global markets.

Looking ahead, the breakout in the DXY suggests continued USD resilience if risk sentiment remains stable and Fed rhetoric stays supportive. Market participants will monitor further developments in US–Iran relations and upcoming macro data for direction in currency trading. For EUR, GBP, and other majors, exchange rate volatility may persist as positioning adjusts through Q3 trading sessions across FX markets.
