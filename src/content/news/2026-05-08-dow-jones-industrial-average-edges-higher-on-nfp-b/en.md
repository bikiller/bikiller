Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) futures rebounded Friday, May 8, after Thursday’s late selloff pushed the cash index toward 49,500. Overnight trading in Asia and Europe held a narrow range just above 49,600, as investors remained cautious ahead of the U.S. nonfarm payroll (NFP) report. The upside momentum was modest, reflecting a market digesting stronger-than-expected job data while weighing geopolitical risks, including Iran’s awaited response to ongoing tensions.  

In currency markets, the USD maintained its strength against the EUR and JPY, supported by the NFP beat. The EUR/USD pair hovered near 1.1020, while USD/JPY tested 134.50, reflecting a mix of safe-haven flows and dollar-driven trading. Traders noted that short-term technical levels around 1.1000 for EUR/USD and 134.00 for USD/JPY could act as key support, with volatility likely to rise once Iran’s reply and broader economic signals emerge.  

Analysts said market participants are balancing solid U.S. labor data with geopolitical uncertainty, keeping both equities and currency pairs in tight ranges. Momentum indicators suggest DJIA may face resistance near 49,700, while currency traders monitor USD strength amid thin liquidity. With the NFP results confirming resilient employment, expectations for the Federal Reserve’s next moves remain a central driver for USD trading and cross-asset positioning.
