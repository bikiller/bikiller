Societe Generale economists have downgraded Eurozone growth forecasts for 2026, noting cuts exceed those for the US in percentage terms. This divergence is reinforcing expectations of continued US outperformance, putting pressure on the EUR/USD exchange rate. Traders are closely watching how these revisions affect euro liquidity, with the EUR hovering near key support levels against the USD.

The softer outlook comes amid concerns over subdued domestic demand and slower investment across the Eurozone. Analysts suggest that the revised GDP projections may prompt the European Central Bank to maintain a cautious stance, potentially limiting upward pressure on EUR/USD. Market participants are adjusting positions as the currency pair responds to shifting macro dynamics and risk sentiment.

For forex traders, the implication is a heightened potential for volatility in EUR/USD trading. With US growth projections comparatively resilient, the USD is likely to retain strength, challenging the euro at technical levels around recent lows. Investors are weighing these fundamentals alongside global risk flows, shaping short- and medium-term trading strategies in major currency pairs.
