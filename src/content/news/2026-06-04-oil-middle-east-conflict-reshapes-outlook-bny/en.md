The Middle East conflict is now the dominant factor shaping the global economic outlook, with BNY analysts noting that surging oil and energy prices are fueling inflation and pressuring growth. Brent crude has climbed sharply, pushing USD-based energy costs higher, which is prompting traders to watch currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY for volatility as central banks weigh policy responses.  

Rising input costs are translating into broader market caution, with EUR and other major currencies experiencing uneven trading against the dollar. The USD has strengthened in response to inflationary concerns, while risk-sensitive currencies such as AUD and NZD are under pressure amid energy-driven market jitters. Technical levels around 1.0650 in EUR/USD and 149.00 in USD/JPY are now key points for traders monitoring short-term moves.  

Investors are positioning for potential central bank interventions, with forex markets reflecting the uncertainty from oil price swings. The heightened inflation environment is expected to influence both exchange rates and commodity-linked trading strategies. Analysts caution that continued Middle East instability could keep energy costs elevated, sustaining pressure on global currencies and creating persistent volatility across major pairs.
