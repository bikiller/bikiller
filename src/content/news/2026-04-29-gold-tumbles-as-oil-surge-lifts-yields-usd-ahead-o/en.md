Gold (XAU/USD) slid more than 1% on Wednesday, retreating as a surge in oil prices pushed US Treasury yields higher. The move strengthened the USD, reflecting market expectations that the Federal Reserve is unlikely to cut rates when it announces its decision around 18:00 GMT. Traders are watching how energy-driven yield pressures influence both precious metals and currency pairs across global markets.  

The rise in yields is weighing on gold, which often competes with interest-bearing assets, making the safe-haven metal less attractive. Meanwhile, the USD has gained traction against major counterparts like the EUR, with traders adjusting positions ahead of the Fed’s policy statement. Market participants are closely monitoring technical levels in XAU/USD as volatility increases amid shifting risk sentiment.  

Analysts note that the interplay between commodity prices, Treasury yields, and the Fed outlook is shaping currency markets. Higher energy costs continue to support USD strength, affecting exchange rates across G10 currencies and emerging markets alike. As trading activity intensifies, gold’s retreat underscores the sensitivity of metals and FX markets to macroeconomic indicators and central bank signals.
