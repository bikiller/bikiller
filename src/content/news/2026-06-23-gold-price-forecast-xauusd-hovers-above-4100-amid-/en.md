Gold (XAU/USD) currency pair reversed Monday’s gains on Tuesday, resuming a broader bearish trend as selling pressure returned to the trading market. After failing to extend upside momentum beyond the $4,220 resistance level in the previous session, the exchange rate slipped back and is now testing key support around $4,100 against the USD.

Market sentiment has shifted as traders reassess recent gains in gold amid fluctuating USD strength and broader forex trading conditions. The inability to sustain levels above $4,220 suggests weakening bullish momentum, with short-term positioning favoring sellers. The currency pair remains sensitive to movements in US dollar demand and risk appetite across global markets.

Going forward, the $4,100 level is seen as a crucial support zone for XAU/USD, with a break potentially opening the door to further downside pressure. Conversely, a rebound could see another retest of $4,220 resistance. Traders are closely monitoring USD trends and broader exchange rate dynamics for directional cues. The overall bias in intraday trading remains tilted to the downside amid prevailing USD influences.
