NZD/USD is trading at 0.5880 on Monday, slipping by 0.06% as tensions between the US and Iran fuel a rally in the US Dollar. The New Zealand Dollar is under pressure, seeing modest losses as investors seek safe-haven assets amidst the rising geopolitical risks. This shift has overshadowed the upcoming economic data from New Zealand and the US, which could further influence market direction.

The New Zealand CPI report and US Retail Sales figures are due this week, adding a layer of uncertainty to the NZD/USD exchange rate. Traders are cautious, with the pair remaining vulnerable to broader market sentiment and geopolitical developments. As the USD strengthens, further fluctuations in NZD/USD are expected depending on the impact of these economic releases.
