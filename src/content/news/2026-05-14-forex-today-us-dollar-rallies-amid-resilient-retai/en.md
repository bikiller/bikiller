The US Dollar gained traction on Wednesday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) climbing toward the 98.80 zone, marking a fresh two-week high. Strong April retail sales, which rose 0.5%, underscored resilient consumer spending despite elevated borrowing costs. This upbeat data fueled USD demand across major currency pairs, pushing the EUR/USD lower as traders recalibrated expectations for interest rate policy and economic resilience.

Treasury yields contributed to the greenback’s strength, with benchmark rates edging higher, reinforcing the appeal of USD-denominated assets. Forex trading showed increased volatility as the market reacted to these fundamentals, with USD/JPY and GBP/USD also reflecting the dollar’s renewed momentum. Market participants noted that persistent retail activity could support further USD gains if growth data continue to surprise to the upside.

Looking ahead, the exchange rate outlook remains influenced by both macroeconomic indicators and yield movements. Analysts highlight that EUR/USD may struggle near current levels without a shift in eurozone sentiment, while USD strength could extend into other currency pairs if the US economy maintains its spending vigor. Traders are positioning accordingly, weighing retail consumption against global monetary conditions and central bank guidance.
