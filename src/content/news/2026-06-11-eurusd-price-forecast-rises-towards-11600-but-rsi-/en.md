The EUR/USD currency pair climbed 0.36% amid a softer USD, trading at 1.1579 as geopolitical tensions eased. Market sentiment shifted after US President Donald Trump cancelled planned strikes on Iran, noting that both countries are finalizing a peace agreement. This development bolstered the euro’s exchange rate, attracting buyers and lifting trading activity across the pair.

Technically, the EUR/USD has been pushing toward the 1.1600 level, but momentum indicators such as the RSI remain bearish, signaling that underlying selling pressure persists. Traders are weighing the short-term upside against potential pullbacks, monitoring key resistance levels closely. The market’s reaction reflects both fundamental geopolitical relief and cautious technical positioning.

Looking ahead, exchange rate movements for the EUR/USD will likely hinge on updates from US-Iran negotiations and broader USD performance. A sustained move above 1.1600 could trigger further euro buying, while any renewed US dollar strength might cap gains. Forex traders are keeping an eye on both political cues and technical signals to navigate the current market dynamics effectively.
