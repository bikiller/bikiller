Gold (XAU/USD) maintained a positive bias during the early European session on Friday, trading near $2,025 per ounce, yet it struggled to extend gains as renewed US Dollar strength emerged. Traders noted that the bullish USD, supported by hawkish signals from major central banks, pressured gold, limiting upside momentum despite initial buying interest in the precious metal.  

The USD’s rebound against major currencies, including the euro and yen, reflects expectations for tighter monetary policy. The euro-dollar exchange rate hovered around 1.0940, showing the USD’s relative resilience. Market participants linked the currency moves to recent central bank commentary emphasizing persistent inflation risks, which weighed on gold’s appeal as an alternative asset.  

Technically, XAU/USD faces resistance near $2,030, with support around $2,015 holding for now. Trading activity suggested cautious positioning ahead of upcoming US economic data, which could further influence USD strength and gold prices. Analysts indicate that sustained USD momentum may cap gold gains, while any softening in US policy signals could revive bullion buying, offering short-term trading opportunities in the currency pair.
