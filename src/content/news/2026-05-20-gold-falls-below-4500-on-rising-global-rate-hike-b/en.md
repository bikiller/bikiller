Gold (XAU/USD) dipped below $4,500 in early Asian trading on Wednesday, falling to around $4,480, its lowest level since March 30. Traders cited persistent inflation fears that are keeping expectations for global interest rate hikes elevated. Higher U.S. Treasury yields have strengthened the USD, prompting selling pressure in the precious metal as investors seek yield-bearing assets.  

The currency pair’s movement reflects broader market concerns over central banks’ tightening policies. With the USD gaining against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, gold’s appeal as a safe-haven diminishes, reinforcing the downward pressure. Technical analysts note that a sustained break below $4,500 could open the door to further declines toward key support levels near $4,460.  

Market participants remain attentive to upcoming economic data that could influence monetary policy expectations. Traders watching the XAU/USD exchange rate are weighing the interplay between inflation readings, Treasury yields, and USD strength. Any shift in these factors could quickly reshape trading sentiment, with gold likely to remain sensitive to moves in global rates and major currency pairs.
