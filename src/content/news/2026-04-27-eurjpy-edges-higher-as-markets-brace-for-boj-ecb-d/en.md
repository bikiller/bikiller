EUR/JPY inched higher on Monday, trading around 186.95, marking a modest gain of 0.07%. Investors are approaching the session cautiously, reflecting a wait-and-see attitude ahead of critical monetary policy updates from the Bank of Japan and the European Central Bank. The currency pair’s trading remains influenced by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which have added a layer of uncertainty to global risk sentiment and capital flows.  

The euro’s relative strength against the yen is underpinned by expectations of potential ECB guidance, while the yen remains sensitive to BoJ signals regarding policy continuity. Traders are closely watching technical levels, with 186.95 serving as a near-term reference point, as market participants position ahead of the central bank announcements. Movements in the broader USD, EUR, and JPY exchange rates are also contributing to cautious trading behavior.  

Market watchers suggest that volatility could pick up once the BoJ and ECB deliver their statements. Any shifts in interest rate guidance or forward-looking commentary could prompt sharper moves in the EUR/JPY pair and ripple across related currency pairs. For now, trading reflects a balance between geopolitical concerns, policy anticipation, and risk appetite, keeping investors alert as key levels hold and momentum remains moderate.
