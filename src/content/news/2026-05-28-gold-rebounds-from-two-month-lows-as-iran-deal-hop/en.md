Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded sharply on Thursday, climbing over 1.20% from two-month lows around $4,366 to trade near $4,500. The rally in this key currency pair comes amid a weaker USD, fueled by speculation over a potential peace deal between the US and Iran. Traders saw renewed appetite for gold as a safe-haven asset, pushing exchange rates higher across the precious metals market.

Market sentiment was bolstered as the softer dollar eased pressure on other commodities and major forex pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD. The sudden surge in gold prices reflects increased trading activity, with investors positioning ahead of potential geopolitical shifts. Technical analysts note that a breach of the $4,500 level could pave the way for further upside, signaling strong momentum for XAU/USD in the near term.

The rebound also underscores the sensitivity of forex markets to political developments, where even tentative progress on international agreements can prompt sharp moves in exchange rates. As traders monitor the evolving situation, volatility in gold trading is likely to persist, influencing USD performance against other major currencies and shaping portfolio strategies across global markets.
