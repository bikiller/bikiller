Federal Reserve Bank of San Francisco President Mary Daly suggested that the U.S. central bank could keep interest rates steady if inflation remains high. In an interview with Reuters, Daly emphasized that while current inflationary pressures are significant, a quick resolution to the ongoing Iran conflict and a drop in oil prices could provide room for a rate cut. This uncertainty has left traders cautious, with the USD/EUR exchange rate hovering around 1.0800, reflecting market indecision.

Daly’s comments have sparked mixed reactions among traders. The potential for a rate cut, depending on geopolitical developments, has caused volatility in the forex market, particularly in USD currency pairs. The possibility of lower oil prices influencing inflation further complicates predictions. As oil prices ease, the EUR/USD may test key support levels around 1.0750, while the USD/JPY remains resilient above the 133.50 mark. The market will be closely watching any updates on the geopolitical situation, which could shift trader sentiment and impact global currency trading strategies.
