The NZD/USD pair is trading at around 0.5814, down 0.71% after the Federal Reserve's decision to hold interest rates steady on Wednesday. This move, combined with a forecast of just one rate cut in 2026, strengthened the US Dollar, putting pressure on the New Zealand Dollar. The Fed's stance has solidified expectations of a strong USD, impacting the NZD/USD exchange rate.

Market participants are now turning their attention to New Zealand's upcoming GDP data, which is expected to provide more insights into the country's economic performance. Any surprises in the GDP figures could influence the NZD/USD pair, potentially driving further volatility in the exchange rate. For now, the focus remains on the Fed's cautious approach and its effect on the broader forex market.
