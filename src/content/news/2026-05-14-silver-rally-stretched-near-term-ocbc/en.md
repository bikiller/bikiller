Silver extended its recent rally, with prices approaching the USD87–88/oz range, driven primarily by strong technical momentum, according to OCBC strategist Christopher Wong. Traders have noted that industrial metals broadly are better bid, suggesting broader market support. The precious metal’s gains have coincided with fluctuations in the USD, with key currency pairs such as EUR/USD reflecting ongoing risk sentiment and influencing trading flows in metals.  

Wong highlighted that softer US–China rhetoric could further bolster Silver, as easing geopolitical tensions often encourage inflows into commodities priced in USD. Exchange rate movements are playing a role, as a weaker USD tends to lift dollar-denominated assets like Silver, making it more attractive for international buyers. Momentum-driven buying has reinforced short-term technical levels, keeping the market in a bullish stance near the current price range.  

For traders, the rally signals continued attention to both technical triggers and macro developments. Currency pair volatility, particularly in USD-linked pairs, remains a key factor shaping trading strategies. If industrial metals maintain their strength and geopolitical tensions remain muted, Silver could sustain its current advance, potentially influencing cross-asset flows and hedging strategies in forex and commodity markets alike.
