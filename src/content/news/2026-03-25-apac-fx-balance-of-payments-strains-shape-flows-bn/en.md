APAC currencies are feeling growing balance-of-payments pressures as geopolitical tensions and energy dynamics reshape flows. Geoff Yu of BNY points to the Iran conflict and shifts in energy by-products as key drivers influencing the Malaysian ringgit (MYR), Thai baht (THB), Australian dollar (AUD), and Philippine peso (PHP). Traders are closely monitoring these currencies against the USD, with heightened volatility in spot and forward markets as central banks weigh policy responses.

The MYR and THB have shown sensitivity to capital movements, with exchange rates reflecting both commodity-linked exposure and external financing needs. The AUD continues to track global risk sentiment, while the PHP is impacted by remittance inflows and trade balances. Market participants are analyzing technical levels for key currency pairs, seeking entry points amid widening spreads and shifting liquidity conditions in regional forex markets.

Implications for trading are significant as currency pairs including USD/MYR, USD/THB, AUD/USD, and USD/PHP navigate these pressures. Investors are adjusting positions to manage exposure to external shocks, while cross-currency strategies are adapting to the evolving macro backdrop. With balance-of-payments strains driving flows, the region’s FX landscape is expected to remain reactive to geopolitical and energy-related developments in the near term.
