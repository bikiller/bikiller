In a note, Societe Generale strategist Kit Juckes describes the EUR/USD currency pair as acting almost like a mirror of the US Dollar Index, with the exchange rate closely tracking broader USD moves. The analysis suggests that trading in euro-dollar increasingly reflects shifts in dollar strength, rather than standalone euro-specific drivers, reinforcing its role as a global forex benchmark pair.

Juckes links this relationship to periods of US dollar weakness, particularly under President Trump, when policy uncertainty and risk sentiment helped drive swings in the currency pair. During those phases, EUR/USD tended to move inversely with the Dollar Index, highlighting how forex trading conditions were dominated by broad USD dynamics rather than euro-area fundamentals.

According to SocGen, the relationship is now shifting as EUR/USD recouples with relative interest rates between the US and eurozone, making rate differentials a key trading driver. For forex traders, this implies the currency pair may respond more to central bank policy expectations, narrowing divergence with the Dollar Index and reinforcing rate-driven strategies in the exchange rate outlook.
