Gold (XAU/USD) extended its decline on Tuesday, falling to its lowest level since March as demand for safe-haven assets weakened and the US Dollar (USD) regained strength. The move followed renewed uncertainty over prospects for a ceasefire in the Middle East after comments from US President Donald Trump clouded expectations for a near-term peace agreement. A stronger greenback lifted the USD exchange rate against major peers, adding pressure to bullion prices during active trading sessions.

The precious metal also remained under pressure from the Federal Reserve’s hawkish policy stance. Expectations that US interest rates could stay elevated for longer continued to support Treasury yields and the USD, reducing the appeal of non-yielding assets such as gold. Market participants closely monitored developments across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, as shifting rate expectations influenced broader forex and commodity markets.

The combination of geopolitical uncertainty and firm monetary policy signals has created a challenging environment for gold traders. While heightened tensions would typically support safe-haven demand, the current rebound in the USD has outweighed those flows, pushing XAU/USD lower. Investors are now watching upcoming economic data and central bank commentary for clues on future exchange rate movements and whether gold can stabilize after reaching its weakest level in several months.
