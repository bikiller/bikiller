The USD/INR currency pair surged to a fresh monthly high, climbing 0.5% to near 91.80 as the US Dollar strengthened amid geopolitical tensions. The ongoing conflict between the United States and Iran has led to a risk-off sentiment in global markets, driving investors toward safe-haven assets like the USD. This push has further pressured the Indian Rupee, which has struggled to maintain stability.

Rising oil prices, fueled by the US-Iran war, are exacerbating the situation for the INR, as India is a major oil importer. With crude oil prices soaring, the INR faces additional downward pressure, amplifying the volatility in the forex market. Traders are now closely monitoring any developments in the geopolitical landscape, as further escalations could continue to support the USD while weighing heavily on the Rupee’s performance.
