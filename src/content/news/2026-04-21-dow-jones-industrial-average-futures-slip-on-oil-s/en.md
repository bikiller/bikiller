Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) futures saw a modest decline on Tuesday, after hitting a fresh overnight high near 49,800. The index quickly reversed as traders reacted to stronger-than-expected US Retail Sales data, which exceeded forecasts, signaling a potential uptick in consumer spending. This surge in retail activity raised concerns about rising inflation, prompting a shift in sentiment across financial markets.

The market’s reaction was further fueled by hawkish comments from Federal Reserve Chair-designate Kevin Warsh. Warsh's testimony hinted at a possible tightening of monetary policy, which has implications for the USD. In forex markets, the EUR/USD pair saw some volatility, with the exchange rate dipping as the US dollar gained momentum. Traders are now closely monitoring US economic indicators and Fed rhetoric, which could drive further fluctuations in both equity and currency markets.
