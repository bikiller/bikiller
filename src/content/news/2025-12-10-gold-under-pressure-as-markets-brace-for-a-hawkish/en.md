Gold (XAU/USD) is experiencing slight downward pressure as traders prepare for the Federal Reserve's interest rate decision. Currently, the currency pair is trading around $4,194, a drop from the intraday peak of $4,218. Market sentiment remains cautious, with investors closely monitoring indications of a hawkish stance from the Fed.

This consolidation phase reflects uncertainty in the market regarding future monetary policy, potentially impacting the exchange rate of gold against the USD. As the Fed's decision looms, fluctuations in gold prices may influence broader trading activities in the forex market, particularly for those holding positions in the EUR/USD and other major pairs.
