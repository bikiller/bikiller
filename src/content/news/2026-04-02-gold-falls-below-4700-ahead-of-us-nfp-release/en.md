Gold slipped below $4,700, with XAU/USD testing $4,675 during early Asian trading on Friday, as investors showed caution ahead of the US Non-Farm Payroll (NFP) release. The precious metal’s decline came amid heightened risk sentiment after US President Donald Trump’s remarks on potential conflict with Iran sent oil prices surging. Trading volumes remained subdued, reflecting the market’s quiet mood on Good Friday.  

The exchange rate dynamics highlighted USD strength, with traders repositioning ahead of key economic data. Gold’s retreat pressured nearby support levels, suggesting potential follow-through if the USD continues to firm. Currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD also saw subdued volatility, reflecting the broader market’s tentative stance. Analysts noted that geopolitical tensions could sway commodity-linked currencies and safe-haven flows, impacting both forex and metal markets.  

Market participants are eyeing the NFP report for clues on US employment trends and possible Federal Reserve moves. Any surprise in payroll numbers could quickly shift trading sentiment across XAU/USD and other major pairs, while oil-driven inflation fears may further influence USD demand. With liquidity thin, even modest shifts in risk appetite could amplify movements in gold and related currency pairs in the coming sessions.
