Asian currencies showed resilience yesterday, holding steady even as the U.S. Dollar weakened amid renewed geopolitical tensions over the fragile two-week ceasefire in the Middle East. Traders appeared cautious, balancing risk appetite with uncertainty, keeping major currency pairs like USD/JPY and USD/SGD range-bound. Michael Wan, MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst, noted that despite sporadic volatility, the overall exchange rates reflected measured positioning rather than aggressive directional bets.  

The USD/JPY pair hovered near key technical support at 148.50, while USD/SGD maintained levels around 1.3520, signaling market reluctance to push beyond short-term thresholds. The cautious trading environment has kept the EUR/USD pair largely sideways, testing 1.0850 resistance but struggling to gain sustained momentum. Wan emphasized that investors are watching geopolitical developments closely, as any escalation could trigger sharp currency swings, particularly in Asia-focused trades.  

Looking ahead, market participants are expected to monitor the Middle East situation and broader risk sentiment, which could drive sudden adjustments in currency valuations. A sustained USD weakness might favor regional currencies, but tight ranges indicate traders remain wary. Analysts suggest that maintaining flexible strategies and closely tracking exchange rate levels across key pairs will be crucial for navigating this uncertain trading landscape.
