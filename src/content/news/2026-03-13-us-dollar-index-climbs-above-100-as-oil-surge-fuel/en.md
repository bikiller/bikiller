The US Dollar Index (DXY) extended its upward momentum on Friday, climbing above the key 100 level to trade around 100.10, marking a daily gain of 0.35%. The dollar’s strength comes amid rising oil prices, which have stoked renewed concerns over inflation and prompted investors to favor the USD over other major currencies. Currency pairs like EUR/USD are feeling the pressure, with the euro slipping against a firmer greenback.  

Traders noted that surging commodity costs are influencing central bank expectations, potentially accelerating interest rate adjustments. The stronger USD has intensified selling in risk-sensitive currencies, including the AUD and NZD, while safe-haven demand has lifted the Japanese yen. Market participants are watching technical levels closely, as a sustained move above 100 on the DXY could signal further gains for the dollar in the coming sessions.  

Forex market activity is increasingly driven by inflation outlooks and geopolitical tensions, affecting exchange rates across multiple pairs. Analysts suggest that USD trading could remain elevated if oil prices continue their upward trajectory, reinforcing the greenback’s appeal. Investors are also monitoring EUR/USD and GBP/USD closely, seeking key support and resistance levels that may define short-term trends amid ongoing volatility.
