The Chinese Yuan remained supported in recent trading, with USD/CNY hovering near 6.78 as market participants assessed the latest policy signals from Beijing. According to Commerzbank analysts, their valuation model points to a slightly stronger People’s Bank of China (PBoC) fixing compared with the previous session, suggesting authorities continue to favor exchange rate stability despite ongoing global market volatility.

A key factor influencing the currency pair is China’s decision to draw on domestic oil reserves rather than compete aggressively for crude supplies in international markets. By relying on strategic reserves, Beijing may reduce the immediate impact of elevated energy prices on import costs, easing pressure on the Yuan and limiting potential deterioration in the country’s trade balance.

For forex traders, the move highlights how commodity policy can shape currency market dynamics beyond traditional monetary factors. A reduced need for large-scale USD purchases to fund energy imports could help underpin the Yuan in the near term, while supporting a more stable USD/CNY exchange rate.
