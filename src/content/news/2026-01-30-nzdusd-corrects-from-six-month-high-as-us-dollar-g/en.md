NZD/USD retreated from a six-month peak on Friday, trading around 0.6035 at the time of writing, down roughly 0.70% on the day. The pullback snapped a winning streak that had been in place since mid-January, as the exchange rate eased after testing 0.6094 in the previous session. Trading activity reflected a shift toward consolidation after recent gains.

The correction was driven largely by profit-taking in the New Zealand Dollar alongside a firm rebound in the US Dollar. Broad-based USD demand resurfaced as investors reassessed positioning across major currency pairs, lending support to the greenback against both the NZD and peers such as the EUR.
