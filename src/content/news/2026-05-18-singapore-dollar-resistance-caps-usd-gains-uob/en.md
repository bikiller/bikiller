The USD/SGD currency pair has rebounded from recent lows near 1.2660, signaling renewed upward momentum in trading activity, according to Quek Ser Leang of United Overseas Bank. Market participants are seeing the pair as broadly supported above 1.2735, suggesting that any dips toward this level may attract buying interest and stabilize the exchange rate. This rebound reflects cautious optimism among USD traders amid mixed regional cues.  

Technical analysts highlight that major support remains anchored at 1.2660, which could act as a floor if the USD faces pressure. The short-term resistance around current levels is capping gains, indicating that while USD strength is building, further advances may be gradual. Traders are closely monitoring moves against other major currencies like the EUR and JPY, as these could influence USD/SGD trading flows and sentiment.  

Looking ahead, the pair’s trajectory will likely depend on global market risk appetite and shifts in monetary policy expectations. Sustained trading above 1.2735 could reinforce bullish momentum, but any failure to break key resistance may trigger profit-taking. For forex traders, keeping an eye on technical levels alongside macroeconomic data remains crucial for navigating USD/SGD positions in the near term.
