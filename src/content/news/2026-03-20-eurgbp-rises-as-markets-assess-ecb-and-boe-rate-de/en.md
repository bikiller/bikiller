EUR/GBP gained ground on Friday, retracing some of Thursday’s losses as traders digested fresh signals from the European Central Bank (ECB) and the Bank of England (BoE). The currency pair climbed modestly, with the exchange rate hovering around 0.8775, reflecting renewed confidence in the euro following the ECB’s cautious stance on interest rates. Analysts noted that shifts in inflation expectations across the eurozone and the UK were key drivers behind the move.  

Market participants closely monitored the ECB’s comments on eurozone inflation and the BoE’s outlook, which suggested a potential pause in rate hikes. The cautious tone weighed on sterling, while the euro found support from resilient economic data, prompting increased trading activity in EUR/GBP. Technical analysts highlighted resistance near 0.8790 and support around 0.8750 as critical levels for upcoming sessions.  

The euro’s modest rebound against the pound comes amid broader currency market volatility, with USD movements also influencing sentiment. Investors remain focused on central bank cues and inflation trends, as these factors could guide future exchange rate trajectories. Traders are weighing positioning ahead of next week’s economic releases, assessing whether EUR/GBP can sustain gains or return to recent lows.
