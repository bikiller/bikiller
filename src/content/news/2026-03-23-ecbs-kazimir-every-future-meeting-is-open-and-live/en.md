European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member Peter Kazimir, also Governor of the National Bank of Slovakia (NBS), stated on Monday that every ECB meeting remains "open and live," signaling potential future rate hikes. His comments come as the EUR/USD pair continues to trade around 1.0600, with the euro under pressure amidst the ongoing inflation battle across the eurozone.

Kazimir’s remarks suggest that the ECB is prepared to adjust monetary policy further if necessary to curb inflation, signaling a hawkish stance. With inflation still above the ECB's target, investors are watching closely for any changes in the central bank's approach, especially as the EUR/USD exchange rate remains vulnerable. The market expects continued volatility as the euro faces pressure from both global economic uncertainties and domestic inflation concerns.
