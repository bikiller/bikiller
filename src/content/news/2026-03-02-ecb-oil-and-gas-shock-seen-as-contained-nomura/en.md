Nomura’s Senior European Economist Andrzej Szczepaniak suggests that the recent surge in oil and gas prices, driven by tensions between the US, Israel, and Iran, is unlikely to significantly impact the European Central Bank's (ECB) outlook on inflation. Despite crude oil prices pushing above $95 per barrel and natural gas rising over 10%, the European economy's resilience and the ECB's policy stance suggest that the oil and gas shock will be contained, keeping the euro area’s Harmonized Index of Consumer Prices (HICP) within expected ranges.

In forex markets, the EUR/USD pair has experienced volatility, with the exchange rate dropping to 1.05 before stabilizing. The strengthening of the USD, supported by rising oil prices and market uncertainty, has led to cautious trading. With the ECB maintaining a tight monetary policy, the effects on the euro’s exchange rate may be muted, but the overall market sentiment will likely remain sensitive to geopolitical developments in the Middle East.
