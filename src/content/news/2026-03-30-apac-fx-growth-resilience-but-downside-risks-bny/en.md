BNY’s Head of Markets Macro Strategy, Bob Savage, noted that Asia-Pacific currencies are showing resilience amid improving regional PMI momentum. South Korea’s recent inclusion in the WGBI and strong export data are bolstering investor confidence, supporting trading flows in KRW and other regional currencies. These fundamentals suggest underlying growth momentum, helping exchange rates withstand broader market volatility despite external pressures.  

However, geopolitical tensions and rising oil prices are presenting notable headwinds. The USD has strengthened against several APAC currencies, with USD/KRW testing near-term resistance levels and AUD/USD showing signs of retracement. Traders are closely monitoring how these external factors could influence central bank policies, potentially affecting future currency pair valuations.  

Savage highlighted that while the region’s growth outlook remains constructive, FX markets may remain sensitive to sudden shocks. Trading strategies are likely to favor selective exposure, balancing resilient currencies like JPY and KRW against those vulnerable to commodity swings. Overall, the interplay of solid economic data and global uncertainties is shaping exchange rate dynamics across the Asia-Pacific corridor.
