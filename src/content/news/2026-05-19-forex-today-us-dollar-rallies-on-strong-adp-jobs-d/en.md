The US Dollar strengthened sharply on Tuesday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) climbing toward the 99.30 region, as traders reacted to robust US labor data and geopolitical developments. The ADP employment report revealed that private employers added an average of 42,250 jobs per week over the past four weeks, the strongest reading since the weekly series began in October 2025. The upbeat figures bolstered USD demand across major currency pairs, pushing the EUR/USD lower and USD/JPY higher.

Market participants also reacted to comments from former President Donald Trump on Iran, which heightened geopolitical risk sentiment and fueled safe-haven flows into the USD. The combination of strong US job creation and increased geopolitical uncertainty prompted a broad-based rally in the currency, with traders repositioning ahead of upcoming economic releases. Technical analysts note that DXY approaching 99.30 could signal further upside if momentum persists.

Currency traders are closely watching exchange rate movements, with EUR/USD testing support levels near 1.0950 and GBP/USD also showing vulnerability against the greenback. The USD’s advance is likely to influence commodity-linked currencies and emerging market pairs, as investors weigh both domestic economic strength and external geopolitical pressures. Overall, the USD rally underscores ongoing market sensitivity to employment data and risk-related headlines.
