Canadian Dollar strengthened against a softer US Dollar at the start of the week, with the USD/CAD currency pair trading on the back foot on Monday and snapping a four-day winning streak. The exchange rate eased after failing to hold earlier gains, slipping from an intraday high near 1.3820 to around 1.3803 at the time of writing as USD selling pressure dominated short-term trading flows.

Market sentiment was shaped by ongoing developments around US-Iran negotiations, which kept the US Dollar slightly weaker across forex markets. Despite pressure from falling oil prices, the Canadian Dollar maintained relative resilience, limiting USD/CAD upside momentum. Traders noted that currency pair volatility remained contained as investors weighed geopolitical risk against broader USD exchange rate softness.

Traders now focus on whether shifting geopolitical headlines and commodity trends will drive further direction in the forex trading landscape, particularly for the USD/CAD exchange rate. With the pair hovering around key short-term levels, market participants are watching for a sustained break beyond recent intraday highs to confirm near-term momentum.
