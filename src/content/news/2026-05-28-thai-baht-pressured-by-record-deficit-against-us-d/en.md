The Thai baht came under renewed pressure as USD/THB climbed to 32.55, even amid recent portfolio inflows supporting the local currency. Analysts at Commerzbank highlight that the baht’s resilience is being tested by fundamental weaknesses, notably Thailand’s widening trade imbalance. The April trade deficit surged to a record $10.0 billion, underscoring persistent import pressures and a lag in export growth that is influencing exchange rate dynamics.  

Forex traders note that while inflows provided temporary relief, the baht faces structural headwinds against the dollar. The sharp trade shortfall has implications for capital flows, potentially prompting further USD strength and elevated volatility in the currency pair. Technical trading levels around 32.50–32.60 now act as key resistance zones, with market participants closely watching for whether this range holds amid ongoing external pressures.  

Market implications extend to broader trading strategies in the region. Investors and forex desks are weighing hedging options as Thailand’s external imbalance continues to shape USD/THB movement. With the baht pressured by record deficits, momentum may favor USD appreciation, while short-term corrections could offer tactical trading opportunities. Exchange rate trends in the near term will likely remain sensitive to trade data releases and portfolio shifts in both USD and regional currencies.
