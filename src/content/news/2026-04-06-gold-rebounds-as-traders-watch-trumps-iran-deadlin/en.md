Gold (XAU/USD) staged a rebound in early Asian trading on Tuesday, climbing back toward $4,660 as market participants closely monitored US President Donald Trump's looming deadline for potential military action against Iranian infrastructure. The precious metal edged higher amid heightened geopolitical risk after Tehran’s forces reportedly closed the Strait of Hormuz, a key artery for global oil shipments, sparking demand for safe-haven assets amid volatile USD trading.  

Traders noted that the XAU/USD exchange rate reacted to a mix of risk sentiment and broader currency movements. The rebound in gold came as the US dollar softened against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, providing additional support. Investors were keenly watching technical levels near $4,650, which analysts cited as a short-term floor, while bullish momentum could target $4,680 if the risk-off sentiment persists.  

The market response highlights the interplay between geopolitical events and currency pair fluctuations, with safe-haven flows influencing both gold and USD exchange rates. Should tensions escalate, trading in commodity-linked currencies and USD-denominated assets may see further volatility, prompting forex traders to reassess hedging strategies. Gold’s movement underscores its role as a hedge amid uncertainty, while the broader forex market remains sensitive to headline-driven catalysts.
