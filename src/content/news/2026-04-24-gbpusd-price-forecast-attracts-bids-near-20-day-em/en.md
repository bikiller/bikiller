GBP/USD recovered early-session losses during Friday’s European trading, turning positive around the 1.3490 level as the currency pair attracted fresh buying interest. The exchange rate moved higher as the US Dollar corrected after a three-day winning streak, easing pressure on the Cable. The rebound reflects shifting short-term trading sentiment in the forex market.

Price action also found support near the 20-day EMA, where dip buyers stepped in and helped stabilize the GBP/USD exchange rate. The technical level continues to act as a key dynamic support zone, suggesting that bullish traders are defending the trend despite recent US Dollar strength. Market participants remain focused on whether momentum can extend beyond this area in ongoing trading sessions.

Looking ahead, the GBP/USD currency pair remains sensitive to further US Dollar fluctuations following its recent corrective move. A sustained break above the 20-day EMA could reinforce short-term bullish sentiment, while failure to hold gains may reopen downside pressure. Traders continue to monitor broader forex market cues, including risk appetite and USD direction, for clearer guidance on the next move in the exchange rate.
