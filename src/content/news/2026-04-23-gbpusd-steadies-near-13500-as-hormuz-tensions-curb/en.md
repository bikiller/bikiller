GBP/USD remains stable around the 1.3500 level as rising tensions in the Middle East weigh on the currency pair. The ongoing conflict between the US and Iran has intensified, with both nations seizing ships and oil vessels near the critical Strait of Hormuz. This geopolitical uncertainty has contributed to a cautious trading atmosphere, limiting the potential for upward movement in the exchange rate.

Amid this backdrop, a Pakistani official indicated that negotiations between Washington and Tehran have stalled, further complicating the situation. As traders monitor developments in the region, the impact on the USD could reverberate through other currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/EUR. The current dynamics suggest that the 1.3500 threshold will be pivotal for GBP/USD in the near term, as market participants weigh the implications of geopolitical risks on the forex landscape.
