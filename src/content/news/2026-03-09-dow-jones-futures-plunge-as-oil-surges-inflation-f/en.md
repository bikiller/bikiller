Dow Jones futures fell 1.74%, dropping below 46,700 during European trading hours, signaling broader market concerns. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures followed suit, slipping 1.61% and 1.75%, trading below 6,650 and 24,250, respectively. The market’s decline comes amid rising fears over inflation, driven by surging oil prices, which intensified concerns over potential interest rate hikes by the Fed. 

As inflation fears resurface, currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY may experience heightened volatility. The USD remains under pressure, with traders cautious about the broader implications of higher energy costs on consumer prices. With oil prices on the rise, the market sentiment is teetering, and traders are watching closely for any signs of further tightening from central banks, particularly the Federal Reserve. The outlook for risk assets remains uncertain as inflationary pressures continue to weigh on investor sentiment.
