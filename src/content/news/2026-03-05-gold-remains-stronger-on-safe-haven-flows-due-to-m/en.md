Gold (XAU/USD) extended its rally for a second consecutive session on Thursday, fueled by safe-haven demand amid escalating tensions in the Middle East. Traders increased exposure to the precious metal as geopolitical risks prompted caution across global markets. The spot price surged, reflecting investor concerns, while USD weakness added momentum, with traders adjusting positions in major currency pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD.  

Market participants noted that the ongoing conflict has shifted risk sentiment, encouraging inflows into gold and other defensive assets. Technical levels around $2,000 per ounce acted as a psychological anchor, with traders monitoring potential breakouts that could influence short-term trading strategies. Currency pairs closely linked to risk appetite, such as AUD/USD and NZD/USD, saw increased volatility as capital flowed toward perceived safer havens.  

Analysts suggest that sustained uncertainty could prolong bullish pressure on gold, impacting forex exchange rates as investors hedge USD exposure. Continued conflict may trigger shifts in equity markets and commodity-linked currencies, reinforcing gold’s role as a liquidity and value-preservation tool. Traders are watching closely for developments that could dictate both precious metals performance and broader currency market dynamics.
