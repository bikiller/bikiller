The EUR/GBP currency pair traded in a narrow range around 0.8625 during early European hours on Wednesday, with the exchange rate showing little direction as market participants refrained from taking significant positions ahead of the European Central Bank’s policy announcement on Thursday. Cautious trading conditions prevailed across major currency markets, with investors closely monitoring expectations for the ECB’s next move and its potential impact on the EUR.

The euro remained broadly steady against the British pound as traders weighed the outlook for interest rates and monetary policy in the Eurozone. With the ECB decision approaching, volatility across EUR-linked currency pairs was subdued, reflecting uncertainty over the central bank’s guidance on inflation, growth, and future policy adjustments. Investors also kept an eye on broader market sentiment, including developments involving the USD and other major currencies.

A decisive ECB signal could influence the near-term direction of the EUR/GBP exchange rate, potentially triggering stronger trading activity after several sessions of consolidation. Any shift in rate expectations may alter yield differentials between the Eurozone and the UK, affecting demand for both the euro and sterling. Until then, traders are expected to remain cautious, keeping the currency pair anchored near the 0.8625 level.
