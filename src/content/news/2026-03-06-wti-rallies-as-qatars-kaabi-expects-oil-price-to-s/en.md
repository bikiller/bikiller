West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil surged to $82.80 per barrel during Friday's European trading session, marking its highest level since July 2024. The rally comes amid heightened tensions surrounding Iran, with Qatar's Energy Minister Saad al-Kaabi predicting a potential spike in oil prices to $150 per barrel. The uncertainty in the Middle East has intensified investor interest in oil, driving prices higher.

The WTI price movement has had significant implications for the forex market, especially with USD-related currency pairs. The surge in oil prices often puts upward pressure on the USD, as the currency is heavily linked to oil trading. As a result, the EUR/USD exchange rate saw slight volatility during the trading session. Oil price fluctuations remain a key factor for traders, influencing broader market sentiment and potential shifts in global inflation expectations.
