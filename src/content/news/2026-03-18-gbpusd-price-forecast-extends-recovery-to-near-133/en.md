The Pound Sterling extended its rally against the US Dollar for a third consecutive session on Wednesday, with the GBP/USD currency pair climbing toward 1.3370. Traders noted growing momentum in the exchange rate as the Pound capitalized on soft US economic data, while the Dollar faced pressure ahead of the upcoming Federal Reserve and Bank of England policy meetings. The pair’s recovery reflects heightened market sensitivity to central bank signals and interest rate expectations.  

Momentum in GBP/USD was supported by broader risk sentiment, with investors weighing the outlook for global growth alongside US inflation dynamics. The Pound’s strength against the USD and, to a lesser extent, the EUR, underscores shifting flows in the forex market, as traders adjust positions ahead of potentially pivotal monetary policy announcements. Technical analysts highlight 1.3370 as a near-term resistance level, noting that a break above could pave the way for further upside in the currency pair.  

Market participants remain cautious as both the Fed and BoE meetings loom, with expectations for rate guidance and economic commentary set to influence trading decisions. The GBP/USD exchange rate has become a key barometer for sentiment in the G10 forex space, attracting speculative and hedge-driven flows. Short-term volatility is likely to persist, offering opportunities for traders to capitalize on swings in the Dollar and Sterling amid evolving macroeconomic data.
