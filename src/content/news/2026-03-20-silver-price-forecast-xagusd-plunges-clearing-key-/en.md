Silver (XAG/USD) tumbled sharply in the North American session, with the currency pair dropping more than 6.80% on the day. The exchange rate has fallen below the critical $70 mark, currently trading at $67.89, signaling a continuation of bearish momentum. This move places silver on track for weekly losses exceeding 15.70%, marking its second-largest weekly decline since the 17.39% drop recorded at the end of January.  

Market participants attribute the selloff to a stronger USD, which has pressured precious metals across the board. Rising treasury yields and expectations of tighter monetary policy have also weighed on silver trading, as investors shift toward yield-bearing assets. The XAG/USD pair’s retreat below key support levels highlights mounting selling pressure amid a challenging backdrop for non-yielding assets.  

Traders and analysts suggest the current trend could influence short-term positioning in both the USD and EUR currency pairs. With silver testing multi-week lows, technical levels under $70 may now act as resistance if the market attempts a recovery. The decline in silver reflects broader market sentiment, emphasizing the sensitivity of precious metals to currency fluctuations and macroeconomic developments.
