The Australian Dollar weakened against the US Dollar early Monday, with the AUD/USD currency pair trading near 0.7145 in the Asian session. Market participants reacted cautiously as fragile peace talks in the Middle East fueled risk-off sentiment, prompting investors to favor the safe-haven USD. Trading volumes remained moderate, reflecting heightened uncertainty ahead of key global developments.  

Investors are also eyeing the Federal Reserve’s interest rate decision scheduled for Wednesday, which could further influence the AUD/USD exchange rate. Analysts note that any indication of a hawkish stance by the Fed may extend USD strength, potentially pushing the Australian Dollar lower. Conversely, signs of a pause or dovish tilt could offer the AUD some relief.  

Technical observers point to support levels around 0.7130, while resistance is seen near 0.7175. The broader forex market shows mixed trends, with the EUR and other major currencies also reacting to geopolitical tensions and central bank cues. Traders remain positioned for potential volatility, balancing short-term trading strategies against fundamental risks.
