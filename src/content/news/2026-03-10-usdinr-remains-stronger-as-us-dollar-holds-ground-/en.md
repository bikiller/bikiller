USD/INR edged higher on Monday, rebounding slightly after modest losses in the previous session, as the US Dollar maintained strength amid a broader risk-off sentiment in global markets. The currency pair surged to a record high of 92.81, reflecting persistent selling pressure on the Indian Rupee. Traders cited elevated crude oil prices and growing geopolitical concerns as key factors fueling demand for the greenback.  

Market participants noted that the sharp rise in the USD/INR exchange rate underscores the vulnerability of emerging market currencies during periods of heightened uncertainty. The Rupee’s weakness is compounded by capital outflows and higher import costs, particularly in energy, which intensify trading volatility. Analysts suggested that the pair could face resistance near current levels while support may emerge around recent lows, creating a narrow range for short-term movements.  

The ongoing USD strength is mirrored across other currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, as investors seek safe-haven assets amid risk aversion. With central banks signaling cautious stances and global economic data showing uneven recovery, forex traders remain attentive to macroeconomic cues and oil price trends. The elevated USD/INR level is likely to influence import-export balances and could prompt policymakers to monitor currency interventions closely.
