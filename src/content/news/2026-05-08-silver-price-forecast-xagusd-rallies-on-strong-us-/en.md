Silver (XAG/USD) surged to around $80.70 on Friday, marking a daily gain of 2.98% as traders reacted to a weaker US Dollar. The currency pair’s upward momentum was fueled by a robust US jobs report, which unexpectedly bolstered investor confidence in the labor market while easing pressure on the USD. Traders noted that demand for safe-haven assets remained strong amid ongoing geopolitical tensions, adding further support to the precious metal’s exchange rate.  

Market participants highlighted that the softening of the USD against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY contributed to silver’s rally. As the USD index retreated, XAG/USD found additional traction, prompting increased trading volumes in both spot and futures markets. Analysts suggested that this pattern reflects broader investor sentiment favoring hedges against currency volatility and inflation concerns, influencing short-term positioning in the forex space.  

Technically, silver is testing resistance levels near $81, with support around $79.50, indicating potential consolidation before the next directional move. Traders remain attentive to upcoming US economic releases and central bank commentary, which could sway USD strength and, consequently, XAG/USD dynamics. The current rally underscores how currency fluctuations and macroeconomic signals are closely intertwined with precious metals trading strategies.
