The USD/CHF currency pair retreated on Thursday from a three-day high of 0.8120, sliding toward 0.8035, its lowest level in about ten days. The move came after weaker-than-expected US economic data pressured the US Dollar, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) falling 0.55% and triggering broad USD selling across forex markets.

Technically, a shooting star pattern has emerged on the chart, signalling potential bearish continuation as sellers regain control below recent highs. The exchange rate is now testing support near 0.8035, while traders increasingly eye the psychological 0.8000 level as the next downside target amid fading USD momentum and cautious risk sentiment in currency trading.

Broader forex markets continue to reflect renewed pressure on the US Dollar, with investors reassessing expectations for the US economic outlook following soft data releases. Safe-haven flows have offered mild support to the Swiss franc, keeping USD/CHF under pressure as traders monitor whether the currency pair can hold above the 0.8000 handle in coming sessions.
