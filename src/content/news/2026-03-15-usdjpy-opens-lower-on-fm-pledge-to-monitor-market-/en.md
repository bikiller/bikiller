USD/JPY opened lower on Monday, falling to ¥147.37, as Japan's Finance Minister, Shunichi Suzuki, pledged to "monitor" market developments amid escalating tensions in the Middle East. The war in the region triggered broad risk aversion, affecting global currency markets. The Japanese yen strengthened slightly as traders sought safe-haven assets, with the USD/JPY exchange rate dipping by 0.5% from its previous close.

The unrest also sparked volatility across other major pairs, with the EUR/USD dropping to 1.0530, a level last seen in late September. With global markets reacting to the ongoing geopolitical situation, traders are closely watching the USD's performance, particularly against safe-haven currencies like the yen. The uncertainty could keep the USD/JPY within a narrow range as market sentiment fluctuates.
