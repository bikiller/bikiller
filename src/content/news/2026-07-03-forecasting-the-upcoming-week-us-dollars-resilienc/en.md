The coming week is set to test major currency pairs as traders return from the US Independence Day holiday. The US dollar continues to show resilience, though recent weaker US labor market data has raised doubts about momentum. Forex markets are expected to react as liquidity returns, with USD exchange rate trends influencing EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and broader trading sentiment across FX desks.

The focus now shifts to upcoming Federal Reserve minutes and weekly jobless claims, both key catalysts for USD direction. Traders will scrutinize policy cues for interest rate expectations, while labor data will help confirm whether the recent slowdown is deepening. Currency pair volatility may rise as markets reassess USD strength versus EUR and JPY counterparts.

Overall, the USD’s resilience will be tested as traders balance safe-haven demand with shifting rate expectations. Major currency pairs may consolidate in tight ranges before a clearer trend emerges. Any surprise in Fed minutes or jobless claims could quickly reshape exchange rate direction, influencing short-term trading strategies across FX markets into the new trading week ahead.
