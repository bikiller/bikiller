ECB Governing Council member Alexander Demarco, Governor of the Central Bank of Malta, said Wednesday the ECB can delay decisions on additional monetary tightening until the next set of macroeconomic projections. The comments came as traders reassess the outlook for euro currency pair exchange rates, with attention on how policy guidance may shape EUR/USD trading and broader forex market sentiment across major assets.

The remarks suggest policymakers are leaning toward a data-dependent stance, reducing urgency for immediate rate hikes. In forex markets, this keeps the euro sensitive to US dollar strength, as USD movements continue to dominate major currency pair volatility. Traders are closely watching inflation trends and growth indicators to gauge whether ECB tightening will resume or remain on hold.

For currency traders, the guidance implies range-bound price action in EUR pairs until fresh projections provide clarity. Any delay in tightening could limit euro upside against the USD, while unexpected hawkish signals may trigger repricing across forex exchange rates. Overall, market participants remain cautious, adjusting trading strategies to shifting ECB policy expectations.
