The Indian Rupee (INR) saw a modest rebound against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, with the USD/INR exchange rate retracing to 94.30 after reaching a record high of 94.75 the previous day. The shift in the currency pair comes amid improving market sentiment, largely driven by hopes for a potential ceasefire in the Middle East.

This positive development helped to ease investor concerns and brought some relief to the INR, which had been under pressure due to the strength of the USD. The retracement from the lifetime high signals a brief stabilization in the INR's trading, although global geopolitical tensions remain a key factor for the currency pair's near-term outlook.
