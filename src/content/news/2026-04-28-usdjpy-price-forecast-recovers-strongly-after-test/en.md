The USD/JPY currency pair gained momentum on Tuesday, climbing 0.17% to hover around 159.70 during the European session. Traders noted a strong rebound following the pair’s test of a Descending Triangle breakout, signaling renewed bullish sentiment. The exchange rate's uptick reflects broader USD strength as investors position ahead of the highly anticipated two-day Federal Reserve policy meeting, set to influence interest rate expectations.  

Market activity shows the US Dollar outperforming key peers, including the EUR and GBP, as traders weigh potential Fed guidance on monetary tightening. Technical charts suggest the pair may face near-term resistance around 160.00, while support remains intact near the breakout zone. Analysts highlight that momentum above the triangle could spur further gains, emphasizing the USD/JPY’s sensitivity to both fundamental cues and technical signals.  

Trading volumes indicate that market participants are closely monitoring Fed commentary for clues on inflation and policy direction. Any hawkish signals could reinforce USD strength, pushing the USD/JPY higher, while dovish tones might prompt a pullback. Overall, the currency pair’s recent recovery underscores the interplay between technical patterns and macroeconomic drivers, making it a focal point for forex traders navigating the volatile environment ahead of the central bank event.
