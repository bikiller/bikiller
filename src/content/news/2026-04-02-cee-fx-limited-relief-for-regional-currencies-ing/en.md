Central and Eastern European (CEE) currencies are facing continued pressure despite recent improvements in some of the region's major currencies. ING's Frantisek Taborsky points out that although the Hungarian Forint, Polish Zloty, and Czech Koruna have made gains, the overall market sentiment remains cautious. Trading in these currencies has been volatile, with exchange rates for EUR/HUF, EUR/PLN, and EUR/CZK still under strain against the USD.

The slight recovery seen in the past few days is not enough to reverse the broader negative trend. USD strength, combined with market concerns over regional economic stability, continues to weigh heavily on CEE currencies. As the EUR/USD exchange rate remains firm, with the pair holding above key technical levels, the outlook for CEE currencies remains uncertain, with any further gains expected to be limited unless significant changes occur in global trading conditions.
