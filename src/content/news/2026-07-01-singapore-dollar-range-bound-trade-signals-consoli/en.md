Singapore dollar is trading in a consolidation phase against the US dollar after Monday’s dip, with the USD/SGD currency pair showing limited directional conviction. Intraday momentum remains flat as spot trading holds within a tight band. The exchange rate is expected to fluctuate between 1.2925 and 1.2955, reflecting subdued volatility in broader forex market conditions and cautious positioning among traders.

According to UOB analyst Quek Ser Leang, the currency pair’s recent price action suggests ongoing consolidation following the earlier pullback, with intraday signals offering little directional bias. The US dollar exchange rate remains contained, as trading activity lacks strong catalysts. This environment has reinforced a short-term neutral tone in the USD/SGD pair amid broader calm across FX markets.

Over a 1–3 week horizon, the bank maintains a neutral outlook, projecting USD/SGD to remain confined within a broader 1.2870–1.2970 range. For forex traders, this suggests range-bound strategies may dominate, with limited breakout risk unless USD sentiment shifts materially. While EUR and other majors continue to influence global FX sentiment, the Singapore dollar is expected to track a stable trading pattern against the US dollar.
