Pound Sterling came under renewed pressure on Tuesday as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East prompted cautious trading across major currency markets. The GBP/USD currency pair, which had posted modest gains in the previous session, slipped to trade around the 1.3400 level during the Asian session. Traders highlighted risk aversion, with investors favoring the relative safety of the USD amid mounting uncertainty in global markets.  

Analysts noted that the recent decline reflects both technical selling and broader sentiment shifts. Sterling struggled to maintain momentum against the greenback, as the exchange rate failed to hold above key resistance levels from the previous day. Meanwhile, the EUR/USD pair remained relatively stable, suggesting the dollar's strength is selective, concentrated against risk-sensitive currencies like GBP.  

Market participants are closely watching upcoming economic data and central bank commentary, which could influence short-term exchange rate trends. Any escalation in Middle East tensions may further boost USD demand, potentially extending Sterling's retracement. Traders are weighing positions carefully, with stop-loss orders near technical support levels, as volatility in the GBP/USD pair remains elevated.
