The coming week is expected to deliver heightened volatility across major currency pairs as markets focus on US labor data, particularly Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP). Traders are closely monitoring USD exchange rate direction, with EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and USD/JPY likely to react sharply to any surprise in job creation, wages, or unemployment trends, shaping short-term forex trading sentiment.

Beyond US employment figures, ISM Manufacturing data and Eurozone inflation releases will also guide currency pair movements. A stronger US manufacturing print could reinforce USD strength, while softer Eurozone inflation may weigh on the EUR exchange rate. These macro signals are likely to influence cross-asset forex trading flows, particularly in EUR/USD and broader dollar-index positioning.

Central bank commentary from the Federal Reserve, European Central Bank, and Bank of England will further shape expectations for interest rate paths. Any shift in tone could amplify volatility across major currency pairs, especially GBP/USD and EUR/USD. Traders will remain sensitive to forward guidance, with USD direction hinging on whether policymakers signal persistent inflation risks or easing momentum.
