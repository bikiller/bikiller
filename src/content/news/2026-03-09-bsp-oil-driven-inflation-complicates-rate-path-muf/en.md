MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst, Michael Wan, warns that rising oil prices and the prolonged closure of the Strait of Hormuz are complicating the policy path for the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP). Higher oil prices have led to inflationary pressures, increasing the likelihood of further rate hikes by the BSP. This dynamic could impact the Philippine Peso (PHP) as traders factor in inflation risks and monetary tightening.

The ongoing volatility in the oil market, with Brent crude hovering around $90 per barrel, is straining the BSP’s inflation control measures. The tightening environment has made the USD/PHP exchange rate more sensitive to these developments, with the pair trading near 56.50. As oil prices continue to rise, market participants are likely to adjust their expectations, potentially affecting trading in both the PHP and other regional currencies like the EUR/GBP. The impact of higher energy costs may keep the pressure on the BSP's decision-making.
