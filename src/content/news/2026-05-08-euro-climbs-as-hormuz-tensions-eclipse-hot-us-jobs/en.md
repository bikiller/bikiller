The euro edged higher against the dollar during Friday’s North American session, with the EUR/USD currency pair trading at 1.1775, up 0.44%. Market participants reacted to escalating tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, which have fueled demand for safe-haven assets and supported the euro despite strong US labor data. Traders are weighing geopolitical risks against economic fundamentals, keeping the exchange rate volatile.  

The latest US jobs report showed solid employment growth, which could limit the Federal Reserve’s flexibility to cut interest rates in the coming months. This dynamic is creating a push-and-pull in currency markets, with the USD receiving support from robust economic data while the EUR gains from risk-off sentiment tied to Middle East uncertainties. Traders remain alert to headlines that could shift market positioning rapidly.  

Technical analysts note that EUR/USD is testing near-term resistance around 1.1780, with potential upside if geopolitical concerns persist. A sustained move above this level could attract further buying, while a pullback may see the pair revisit support near 1.1740. Currency traders are closely monitoring both macroeconomic releases and regional tensions, which continue to shape exchange rate movements in the short term.
