Silver (XAG/USD) continued its upward momentum on Friday, with the currency pair reclaiming the $60.00 level for a second consecutive session. The exchange rate extended weekly gains to more than 5.50% as broader US Dollar weakness supported demand for the white metal, pushing trading to $62.42 and marking an eight-day high at $62.89.

Market sentiment in the broader forex space remains tilted against the USD, with investors rotating into precious metals as a hedge. The XAG/USD currency pair is benefiting from technical momentum, as RSI recovery signals suggest further upside potential. Traders are now eyeing a potential breakout toward the $65.00 resistance zone if buying pressure persists.

However, volatility risks remain elevated across currency markets, with traders closely monitoring US macroeconomic signals that could influence USD direction against peers such as the EUR. Any shift in interest rate expectations may impact silver’s exchange rate dynamics. For now, bullish momentum in XAG/USD continues to dominate short-term trading conditions.
