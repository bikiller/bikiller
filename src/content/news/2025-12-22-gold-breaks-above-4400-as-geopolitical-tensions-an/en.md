Gold (XAU/USD) soared to a new record high of $4,424 on Monday, reflecting a 2.0% increase for the day. This surge comes as escalating geopolitical tensions have heightened investor interest in safe-haven assets. The precious metal broke through the previous peak established on October 21, which was around $4,381, signaling strong bullish momentum in the market.

The rising demand for gold can be attributed to a combination of factors, including uncertainty surrounding global events and a shifting outlook on Federal Reserve policies. As traders adjust their positions in response to these dynamics, the exchange rate of XAU/USD continues to reflect heightened volatility, with implications for both currency pair traders and broader market sentiment.
