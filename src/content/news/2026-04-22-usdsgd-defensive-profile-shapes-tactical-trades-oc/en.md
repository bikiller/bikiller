USD/SGD edged higher as stalled US–Iran ceasefire negotiations weighed on market sentiment, prompting a defensive trading stance. According to OCBC’s Christopher Wong, the currency pair has seen bearish momentum diminish, while the relative strength index (RSI) has climbed, signaling potential for tactical positioning. Traders remain alert to geopolitical developments that could influence short-term swings in the USD/SGD exchange rate.  

Technical indicators suggest the pair may test nearby resistance levels, with momentum indicators hinting at a cautious but steady rebound. Market participants are weighing USD strength against regional currencies, including the SGD, while global risk appetite remains muted. Wong highlights that fading bearish pressure may encourage traders to explore measured long positions, particularly amid fluctuating sentiment toward safe-haven assets like the USD.  

In broader forex trading, the USD’s performance against the EUR and other major currencies will likely impact USD/SGD dynamics. Investors are closely monitoring headline risks from Middle East developments, assessing their implications for exchange rates and portfolio strategies. The tactical environment emphasizes short-term responsiveness, as technical and geopolitical signals converge, shaping trading decisions in a volatile but manageable USD/SGD landscape.
