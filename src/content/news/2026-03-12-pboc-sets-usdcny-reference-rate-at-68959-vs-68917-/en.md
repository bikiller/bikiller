The People’s Bank of China (PBOC) set the USD/CNY reference rate at 6.8959 for Thursday’s trading session, edging higher from Wednesday’s fix of 6.8917. This came slightly above Reuters’ median estimate of 6.8853, signaling a modest depreciation of the yuan against the US dollar. Traders noted that the move reflects ongoing cautious monetary policy adjustments amid stable domestic economic indicators.  

Market participants see the slight uptick in the USD/CNY exchange rate as part of broader dynamics affecting Asian currency pairs. The yuan’s performance is being closely watched against the backdrop of global USD strength and shifting risk sentiment. Investors are monitoring capital flows and import-export pressures, which could continue to influence trading ranges and technical levels in the near term.  

The softer yuan may weigh on cross-currency moves, with EUR/CNY and other major pairs potentially experiencing volatility in line with USD strength. Analysts suggest that traders should watch key psychological levels around 6.90, as sustained trading above this point could prompt renewed speculation on PBOC interventions or adjustments in China’s foreign exchange strategy. Overall, the market remains attentive to both domestic policy signals and international macroeconomic developments.
