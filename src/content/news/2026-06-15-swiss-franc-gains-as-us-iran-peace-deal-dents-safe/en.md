Swiss Franc strengthens against the US Dollar on Monday as risk sentiment improves following the US-Iran peace agreement aimed at de-escalating tensions in the Middle East. The easing geopolitical backdrop has reduced demand for traditional safe-haven assets, putting pressure on the Greenback across major currency pairs. At the time of writing, USD/CHF trades around 0.7923, marking a 0.60% decline on the day in the exchange rate.

Market participants reacted to improving global risk appetite, shifting capital away from USD as a defensive currency. The Swiss Franc benefits from its traditional safe-haven status, gaining traction in forex trading sessions. Lower geopolitical risk premiums have weighed on USD positioning, leading to broad US Dollar weakness against European currencies and tighter intraday volatility in USD/CHF trading.

Traders now watch whether USD/CHF can hold below the 0.7950 resistance area, with momentum signals pointing to potential further downside if risk-on sentiment continues. However, any breakdown in the peace agreement could quickly revive safe-haven demand for the US Dollar. For now, forex markets remain highly sensitive to geopolitical headlines and shifting risk appetite across global trading sessions.
