Markets are increasingly pricing in a more assertive European Central Bank policy path, with roughly three rate hikes anticipated by December 2026. This outlook suggests investors expect the ECB to respond to the Iran war–driven energy shock in a manner broadly aligned with the Bank of England. The shift is influencing EUR trading dynamics, particularly in the EUR/USD currency pair, as rate expectations feed directly into exchange rate positioning.

The energy shock has complicated the ECB’s policy calculus, as rising costs risk reigniting inflationary pressures across the euro area. Traders are adjusting positions to reflect a scenario where tighter monetary policy offsets energy-driven price spikes. This has supported the EUR in recent sessions, with the currency pair stabilizing against the USD as markets reassess divergence between major central banks.

For forex participants, the key implication lies in relative policy trajectories. If the ECB follows through with the implied tightening path, the EUR/USD exchange rate could find near-term support, especially if the Federal Reserve adopts a more cautious stance. However, volatility remains elevated, with energy developments continuing to drive sentiment and trading flows across major currency pairs.
