NZD/USD bounced back on Monday, climbing 1.42% to around 0.5850 after several days of decline. The currency pair's recovery was fueled by positive Chinese economic data, which helped bolster risk sentiment in global markets. The upbeat figures contributed to renewed optimism for the New Zealand Dollar (NZD) as traders turned their attention to higher-yielding assets.

The rebound in NZD/USD was also supported by growing expectations of an interest rate hike by the Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ). Market participants anticipate tighter monetary policy from the RBNZ in the coming months, adding further strength to the Kiwi. As a result, the exchange rate is experiencing upward momentum, with key technical levels closely monitored by traders for potential further gains.
