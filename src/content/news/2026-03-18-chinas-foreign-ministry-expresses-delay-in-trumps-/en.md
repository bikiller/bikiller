China’s foreign ministry confirmed Wednesday during European trading hours that updates are expected regarding the postponement of a planned meeting between US President Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping. The delay comes amid heightened tensions over trade and economic policies, prompting traders to reassess risk sentiment. In response, the USD saw mixed movements against major currencies, with the USD/JPY hovering near recent support levels and the EUR/USD testing the 1.0950 mark.  

Market participants are weighing the potential impact on global trade flows and investor confidence. Safe-haven currencies like the Japanese yen and Swiss franc experienced modest gains as uncertainty increased, while risk-sensitive currencies, including the Australian and New Zealand dollars, faced downward pressure. Technical analysts note that the USD/JPY remains above the 108.50 support zone, while EUR/USD could find resistance around 1.1000 if sentiment shifts.  

Forex traders are closely monitoring developments for clues on future US-China negotiations, as delays may affect capital flows and carry trades. Exchange rate volatility is likely to persist until the meeting is rescheduled, with short-term trading strategies favoring defensive positioning. Overall, market dynamics reflect caution, with the USD maintaining relative strength amid geopolitical uncertainty and the broader currency market adjusting to evolving risk factors.
