The Swiss Franc (CHF) strengthened against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, pushing the USD/CHF currency pair to a two-month low. At the time of writing, the exchange rate hovers around 0.7766, marking its weakest level since March 10. Traders pointed to renewed optimism over a potential US-Iran peace deal as a key factor weighing on the Greenback, prompting a shift toward safer and alternative currencies like the Swiss Franc.  

Market participants noted that the decline in USD/CHF comes amid broader USD softness, with investors recalibrating positions in response to geopolitical developments. The Swiss Franc, traditionally seen as a haven currency, has attracted buying interest as traders anticipate reduced risk premiums in the event of progress on the diplomatic front. Meanwhile, the US Dollar’s pressure was echoed in other major pairs, including EUR/USD, reflecting a cautious tone across global forex markets.  

Technically, USD/CHF’s fall below recent support levels signals potential further downside if momentum persists, with traders closely watching key thresholds near 0.7750. Continued movement in response to US-Iran negotiations could influence short-term trading strategies, as well as portfolio hedging decisions involving CHF and other major currencies. Analysts suggest the market remains sensitive to news flows, with the currency pair likely to remain volatile until clarity emerges on geopolitical developments.
