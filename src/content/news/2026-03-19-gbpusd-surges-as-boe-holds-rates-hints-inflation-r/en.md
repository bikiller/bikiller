GBP/USD surged during Thursday's North American session as the Bank of England (BoE) decided to keep interest rates unchanged, citing persistent inflationary pressures, particularly driven by the ongoing Middle East conflict. The pair rose by 0.76%, reaching a new trading level of 1.3356. Market participants are closely monitoring the BoE's stance amid the inflation risks, which continue to weigh on the UK economy.

The decision to hold rates surprised many traders, as the BoE’s cautious tone signals that inflation concerns are far from over. As a result, GBP/USD has gained traction against the USD, with the exchange rate climbing to 1.3356. The continued geopolitical risks and inflation concerns have added volatility to the market, leaving investors uncertain about future rate decisions and the broader economic outlook.
