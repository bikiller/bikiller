The People’s Bank of China has scrapped the 20% reserve requirement on foreign currency forward positions, a move flagged by Commerzbank analysts Charlie Lay and Moses Lim as a clear signal to temper recent yuan strength. By removing the 20% rule, Beijing lowers the cost of building short CNY positions, potentially slowing the currency’s appreciation even as USD/CNY and USD/CNH have drifted lower in recent trading.

The adjustment comes amid a broader decline in the USD across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, which has weighed on the USD/CNY exchange rate. With both onshore and offshore yuan gaining ground, authorities appear keen to prevent excessive one-way trading and speculative positioning. The policy shift effectively injects two-way risk back into the market, influencing forward pricing and hedging demand.

For traders, the move underscores official discomfort with rapid CNY gains and suggests policymakers are prepared to lean against appreciation pressure. While the broader USD trend remains a key driver, near-term price action in USD/CNY may stabilize as positioning adjusts. The decision highlights how regulatory tweaks can quickly alter sentiment and reshape exchange rate dynamics in Asia-focused FX trading.
