Gold (XAU/USD) holds steady above the $4,000 level on Friday, maintaining a firm tone in the forex trading session as a softer US Dollar and easing US Treasury yields provide support. The latest US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) inflation report broadly met market forecasts, tempering expectations for near-term Federal Reserve interest rate hikes and stabilising the exchange rate dynamics.

With inflation data aligning with expectations, traders scaled back aggressive Fed tightening bets, weighing on the US Dollar and pulling Treasury yields lower. In forex markets, the weaker USD has helped gold maintain its position above the $4,000 psychological level, while the XAU/USD currency pair shows resilience amid shifting interest rate expectations and subdued volatility.

Looking ahead, market participants will closely watch upcoming US economic releases and Fed commentary for further clues on policy direction. Sustained softness in inflation could reinforce expectations of a pause in rate hikes, potentially supporting gold’s upside bias in the medium term, while continued USD weakness may keep the currency pair trading near recent highs.
