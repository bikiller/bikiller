EUR/GBP remains elevated, trading above levels suggested by the Bank of England (BoE) and European Central Bank (ECB) rate differentials. This divergence signals a significant risk premium for the Pound (GBP), as noted by Deutsche Bank’s Shreyas Gopal and Sanjay Raja. Political uncertainty surrounding the UK continues to weigh on the currency, creating volatility in the currency pair’s exchange rate.

The persistent gap in EUR/GBP reflects ongoing concerns over the UK's economic stability amid shifting political dynamics. As the BoE and ECB continue their respective monetary policies, the Pound faces upward pressure, trading at levels that imply heightened risk. This premium underscores the market’s cautious stance, with the exchange rate fluctuating in response to external factors like political developments and broader global market trends.
