West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil eased on Thursday, retreating 3.70% to trade at $101.45, following a three-day rally. The pullback comes despite ongoing supply concerns, with geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz continuing to threaten global oil flows. This decline, while notable, keeps US crude above the $100 threshold, signaling that the market remains under pressure from supply risks.

Despite the drop, the broader market sentiment around WTI remains firm, with traders eyeing further developments in the Middle East. The persistent tensions in Hormuz, a crucial shipping route for oil, suggest that volatility in the oil market could persist. The price action is having a knock-on effect on USD-based currency pairs, with the dollar maintaining strength amid broader global uncertainties.
