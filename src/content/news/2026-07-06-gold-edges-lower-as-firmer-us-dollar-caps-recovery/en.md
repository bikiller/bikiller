Gold (XAU/USD) traded slightly lower on Monday as a firmer USD limited the precious metal's recovery, while mild profit-taking also weighed on prices after last week's rebound. The move followed a recovery from a more than seven-month low of $3,941, highlighting cautious trading conditions as investors balanced demand for safe-haven assets against renewed strength in the US Dollar and changing exchange rate expectations across major currency pairs.

The stronger USD reduced the appeal of non-yielding gold by making the metal more expensive for holders of other currencies, including the EUR and other major counterparts. Currency pair movements reflected continued confidence in the US Dollar, encouraging traders to lock in profits after the recent rally. Although Gold managed to recover from last week's lows, the firmer greenback prevented buyers from extending gains, leaving the market without a clear directional catalyst.

Market participants will continue to monitor upcoming US economic data and central bank signals for clues about the outlook for interest rates and the USD. Any changes in exchange rate expectations or broader forex trading sentiment could influence demand for Gold and other safe-haven assets. For now, XAU/USD remains under pressure near recent recovery levels, with the $3,941 low serving as an important technical reference for traders assessing the next market move.
