Crude oil prices edged lower on Tuesday despite heightened geopolitical tensions, after former President Donald Trump claimed Iran had downed a U.S. Apache helicopter over the Strait of Hormuz. Traders reacted cautiously as Trump warned of a possible military response, yet Brent crude slipped 0.8% to $84.62 a barrel, while WTI fell 0.7% to $81.45, signaling that markets were not spooked by the news.  

In currency markets, the USD showed relative strength as safe-haven flows picked up. The EUR/USD pair retreated to 1.0925, testing short-term support near 1.0900, while USD/JPY climbed to 146.10, driven by increased demand for the dollar amid geopolitical uncertainty. Commodity-linked currencies like the AUD and CAD struggled, with AUD/USD sliding 0.6% and USD/CAD rising toward 1.3700, reflecting oil’s modest decline.  

Traders weighed the geopolitical headlines against broader market fundamentals, noting that the threat of U.S.-Iran escalation had yet to trigger a sustained risk-off move. Crude’s muted response suggested confidence in supply stability, while forex markets continued trading in familiar technical ranges. Analysts highlighted that any further escalation could push USD pairs higher and extend pressure on oil-linked currencies, reinforcing the importance of monitoring both geopolitical developments and central bank policy cues.
