WTI oil prices dropped on Tuesday, trading at $82.30, marking a 1.15% decline. The retreat follows concerns over global oil supply, with the G7 considering the release of strategic reserves. Market participants are now reassessing risks, as the move by major economies signals potential supply adjustments that could affect the broader energy market.

The International Energy Agency (IEA) has also called for an emergency meeting, further fueling uncertainty. This development weighs on crude prices, impacting the USD and related currency pairs. Traders are closely monitoring the situation, with any significant shift in oil supply dynamics likely to affect exchange rates and global inflationary pressures.
