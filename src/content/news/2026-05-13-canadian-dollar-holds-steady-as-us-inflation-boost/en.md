The USD/CAD currency pair is trading in a narrow range around 1.3700 on Wednesday, reflecting a tug-of-war between a resilient US Dollar and supportive Oil prices for the Canadian Dollar. Investors remain cautious as US inflation data keeps the greenback firm, while high crude prices continue to cushion the CAD, maintaining its appeal amid global market volatility.

Traders are monitoring the exchange rate closely, with the USD showing strength against major counterparts including the EUR, keeping USD/CAD in check despite attempts by the loonie to gain ground. The interplay between macroeconomic indicators and commodity prices is creating a sideways trading environment, with neither bulls nor bears gaining a decisive edge.

Looking ahead, market participants will weigh upcoming US economic data against energy market trends to gauge potential shifts in the pair. Technical levels around 1.3700 are proving pivotal, serving as a reference for intraday moves, while continued fluctuations in oil could provide CAD support, influencing trading strategies and hedging decisions across North American currency markets.
