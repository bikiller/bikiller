The Indian Rupee (INR) declined sharply against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, hitting an all-time low as the USD/INR currency pair approached 92.80. This significant move was driven primarily by a surge in oil prices, which has put additional pressure on the Indian economy and currency, intensifying concerns about inflation and trade deficits.

As the exchange rate continues to reflect these economic challenges, traders are closely monitoring the market for further developments. The ongoing oil shock not only impacts the INR but also poses broader implications for India's import costs and overall economic stability, prompting speculation about potential interventions by the Reserve Bank of India.
