BNP Paribas economist Hélène Baudchon has signaled a milder inflation impact from the latest energy price surge compared with the 2022 shock. The rise in oil and gas costs, triggered by escalating tensions in Iran, has driven crude benchmarks higher, but analysts see a more contained effect on consumer prices this time. Market participants are weighing the implications for USD strength, with the DXY index testing recent support levels.  

In forex trading, the EUR/USD pair has reacted to the news with modest gains, hovering around 1.0950, as traders balance energy-driven inflation expectations against Federal Reserve policy signals. Commodity-linked currencies like the NOK and CAD have seen sharper moves, reflecting their sensitivity to oil price fluctuations. Technical levels around 1.0920 support and 1.0980 resistance are drawing attention as short-term traders adjust positions.  

The broader implication is a market cautious but less reactive than during 2022. Exchange rates may stabilize unless geopolitical risks escalate further, influencing USD demand and euro sentiment. Traders are closely monitoring Brent crude near $87 per barrel and natural gas prices, assessing whether the energy-driven USD strength will persist or ease, shaping currency pair dynamics in the coming sessions.
