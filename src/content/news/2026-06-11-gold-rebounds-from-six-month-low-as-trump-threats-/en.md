Gold (XAU/USD) is rebounding after hitting a six-month low, with the currency pair trading at $4,091, up 0.58% on Thursday. Earlier, XAU/USD dipped to $4,023 as heightened geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran rattled markets. Traders are weighing the implications of ongoing exchanges of fire, which threaten to delay any resolution in the Middle East conflict and influence USD flows.

The rise in gold comes amid cautious trading across global forex markets, with investors seeking safety in precious metals amid uncertainty. The exchange rate movements highlight increased volatility in USD pairs, as risk sentiment shifts. Market participants are closely monitoring developments in the Middle East for cues that could drive further currency pair fluctuations, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, alongside commodities like gold.

Analysts suggest that gold’s rebound could persist if tensions escalate, reinforcing its role as a hedge against geopolitical risk. For forex traders, XAU/USD price action provides insight into broader currency market sentiment, with shifts in USD liquidity likely to impact other major pairs. Technical levels near $4,100 could act as resistance, while continued volatility may keep traders focused on safe-haven assets.
