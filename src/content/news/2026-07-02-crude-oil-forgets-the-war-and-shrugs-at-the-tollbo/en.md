Crude oil extended its decline on Thursday, settling at its lowest level in 18 weeks as investors continued to unwind the geopolitical risk premium that had supported prices earlier this year. The market has now erased virtually all of the gains linked to the conflict, leaving crude back at levels seen before the outbreak of hostilities. Trading activity reflected improving risk sentiment, with participants shifting their focus from geopolitical headlines to broader macroeconomic fundamentals and demand expectations.

The retreat in oil prices has also influenced the forex market, where commodity-driven flows remain closely watched alongside major currency pair movements. Lower energy costs can ease inflation pressures, affecting expectations for central bank policy and shaping the exchange rate outlook for the USD and the EUR. Currency pair performance has increasingly been driven by changing interest rate expectations rather than geopolitical developments, reinforcing the view that investors are rotating toward fundamental economic indicators.

With the conflict premium largely erased, market participants are now assessing whether crude oil can stabilize at pre-war levels or face additional downside pressure. The shift in sentiment highlights how quickly geopolitical concerns can fade when supply disruptions fail to materialize. Traders across oil and forex markets are expected to monitor incoming economic data, central bank signals, and USD performance for fresh direction, while broader trading conditions remain sensitive to changes in global growth and energy demand.
