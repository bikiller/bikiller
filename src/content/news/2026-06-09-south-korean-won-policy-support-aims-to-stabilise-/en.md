South Korea’s authorities have unveiled a fresh package of measures aimed at stabilising the Korean Won, responding to persistent volatility in the foreign exchange market. According to Commerzbank analyst Charlie Lay, the government plans to tighten oversight of offshore FX derivatives trading, increase scrutiny of suspected market misconduct, and strengthen monitoring of activity affecting the KRW exchange rate. The steps reflect growing concern over speculative pressures and sharp movements in the currency market.

A key element of the package involves the National Pension Service expanding its foreign-exchange hedging operations through USD forward selling. By increasing hedging activity, the pension fund could boost the supply of USD in the market, providing additional support for the Won and helping smooth fluctuations in the USD/KRW currency pair. Market participants will be watching whether these flows translate into more stable trading conditions in coming sessions.

The measures underline policymakers’ determination to limit excessive exchange-rate swings and reinforce confidence in South Korea’s financial markets. For forex traders, the announcement signals a more proactive stance from authorities, which could influence positioning across Asian currencies as well as broader USD and EUR trading. While the effectiveness of the initiatives remains to be tested, the policy package is designed to reduce volatility and support a more orderly foreign-exchange environment.
