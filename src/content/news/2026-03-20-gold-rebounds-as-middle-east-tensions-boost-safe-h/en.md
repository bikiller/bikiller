Gold (XAU/USD) surged to around $4,735 during the Asian session on Friday, breaking its seven-day losing streak. The rebound came as tensions in the Middle East, fueled by the ongoing US-Iran conflict, sparked an increase in demand for safe-haven assets. Gold's rise is a direct response to growing market uncertainty, with traders closely monitoring geopolitical developments for further direction.

The precious metal's rally reflects broader market sentiment as investors seek shelter in traditional safe-haven currencies like the USD. As gold prices rebound, attention is also shifting to the potential impact on the broader forex market, with the EUR/USD exchange rate and other currency pairs likely to see volatility. Any escalation in the Middle East could continue to influence trading dynamics across multiple assets.
