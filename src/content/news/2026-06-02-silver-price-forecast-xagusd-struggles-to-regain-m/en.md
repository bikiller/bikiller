Silver (XAG/USD) retreated from intraday highs on Tuesday, struggling to reclaim momentum as it trades below its 50-day simple moving average (SMA). The pullback comes amid a rebound in the US Dollar (USD), which has strengthened across major currency pairs, adding pressure to precious metals. Traders are closely monitoring the exchange rate dynamics, as XAG/USD hovers near key technical support levels.  

Market participants are weighing geopolitical risks, particularly the uncertainty over a potential deal between the United States and Iran to end the three-month-old conflict. This uncertainty has reinforced demand for the USD as a safe-haven currency, while silver trading has slowed, reflecting caution among investors. The currency pair’s short-term trend remains sensitive to developments in both the forex market and broader geopolitical news.  

Technically, XAG/USD faces resistance near the 50-day SMA, and any sustained rally above this level could prompt renewed buying interest. Conversely, a continued USD rebound against the euro (EUR) and other currencies could weigh further on silver prices, keeping traders alert to volatility in both forex and commodity markets. Analysts suggest monitoring these technical and macro factors to gauge the currency pair’s next move.
