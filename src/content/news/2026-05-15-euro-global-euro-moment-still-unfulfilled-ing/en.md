The euro has yet to achieve the “global euro moment” envisioned by European Central Bank President Christine Lagarde in 2025, according to ING’s Carsten Brzeski. Despite periods of stability against the USD, the EUR/USD currency pair continues to trade within familiar ranges, showing limited traction in challenging the greenback. Market participants are watching technical levels closely, with 1.1000 and 1.1200 acting as key resistance points.

Traders attribute the euro’s restrained performance to uneven economic growth across the Eurozone and persistent geopolitical uncertainties. Exchange rates have been sensitive to ECB commentary, yet the absence of a decisive rally suggests that EUR liquidity and global usage remain below levels needed to assert a truly dominant international position. Short-term trading shows oscillations around support near 1.0900, highlighting investor caution.

The implications for forex markets are notable: without a breakout, EUR/USD may continue to fluctuate, constraining options for traders seeking long-term euro positions. Brzeski emphasizes that structural reforms and stronger fiscal coordination could be pivotal in elevating the euro’s status. Meanwhile, the USD remains the benchmark for global trading, with EUR movements largely reactive, reflecting the ongoing struggle for Europe to establish a lasting, influential role in currency markets.
