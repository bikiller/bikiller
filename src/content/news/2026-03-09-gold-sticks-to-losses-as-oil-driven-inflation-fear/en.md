Gold (XAU/USD) extends its losses, trading just below the $5,100 mark during the first half of the European session, though it remains above a four-day low reached earlier Monday. The precious metal is under pressure as oil-driven inflation concerns weigh on markets, tempering expectations for aggressive rate cuts and keeping the USD supported across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD.  

Traders note that renewed inflation fears linked to rising crude prices have boosted the dollar, pushing gold lower despite its appeal as a safe-haven asset. Exchange rate movements show EUR/USD holding steady but sensitive to shifts in risk sentiment, while USD strength continues to influence other commodity-linked currencies and trading flows in Asia and Europe.  

Technically, gold faces resistance near the $5,110–$5,120 zone, with support observed around the $5,090–$5,095 range. Market participants are watching whether persistent USD gains will limit gold’s rebound or if softer inflation data could reignite buying interest. Currency traders are closely monitoring central bank signals, as any adjustments in interest rate expectations could trigger broader shifts across the FX market.
