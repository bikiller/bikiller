Brent crude has slipped back below the USD 100 mark as markets weigh a potential Iran nuclear deal, according to Commerzbank’s commodity analysts led by Barbara Lambrecht. While the prospect of increased Iranian exports exerts downward pressure, the team notes that a swift normalization of Gulf output remains unlikely. Traders are closely watching oil-linked currency pairs, with the USD showing resilience against the EUR amid these supply concerns.

The gradual recovery in Gulf exports, coupled with restrained US drilling, suggests a measured market adjustment rather than a sharp price reversal. This dynamic has influenced energy-linked exchange rates, prompting some volatility in USD-denominated commodity trades. Market participants are weighing the implications for long-term positions, while the EUR/USD and other major pairs respond to shifting oil sentiment and geopolitical developments.

Commerzbank highlights that, despite the pullback, technical levels around USD 100 could act as a key support for Brent. Forex traders are factoring in both geopolitical and macroeconomic signals, balancing energy market flows with currency movements. As the situation unfolds, the interplay between crude prices and USD strength is expected to guide trading strategies across commodities and forex markets.
