Gold prices surged to an all-time high near $4,300 during Asian trading hours on Monday, driven by safe-haven flows amid growing expectations of interest rate cuts by the US Federal Reserve. The XAU/USD currency pair gained momentum as traders reacted to indicators of softer inflation and a slowdown in job growth in the US, prompting a flight to the precious metal.

As investors seek refuge in gold amid uncertainty in the USD, the implications for the forex market are significant. A looming shift in Fed policy could weaken the dollar's exchange rate against major currencies like the EUR, while boosting demand for gold as a hedge against potential economic downturns. The recent movements highlight the interconnectedness of commodities and currency trading, positioning gold as a focal point in the current market landscape.
