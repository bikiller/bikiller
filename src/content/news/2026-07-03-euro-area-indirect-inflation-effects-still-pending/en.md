Euro area inflation has yet to show indirect spillover effects from the recent energy shock into food or broader goods prices, according to Societe Generale economists Sam Cartwright, Michel Martinez and Jorge Garayo. They note that while energy volatility has been evident, downstream pricing pressures across the consumer basket remain contained, limiting immediate inflation passthrough in the region.

Market participants are closely watching the EUR/USD currency pair as the absence of secondary inflation pressures may reduce expectations for aggressive European Central Bank tightening. This dynamic can weigh on the euro against the US dollar, especially as relative yield differentials continue to favor USD strength. Trading sentiment remains sensitive to incoming inflation data and energy price volatility.

From a forex trading perspective, subdued indirect inflation transmission could support a more stable euro area outlook, but it also reinforces the divergence with US inflation resilience, keeping the US dollar firm. Investors in EUR/USD may therefore see range-bound conditions unless energy shocks reaccelerate price pressures or shift European Central Bank rate expectations materially in coming sessions.
