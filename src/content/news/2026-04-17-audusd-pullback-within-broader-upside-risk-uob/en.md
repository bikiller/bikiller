AUD/USD has pulled back after testing a high of 0.7197, as traders reassess near-term momentum. United Overseas Bank strategists Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann highlight that despite the retreat, the currency pair remains within a broader upside risk profile. Intraday trading is likely to hover between 0.7130 and 0.7180, with the US dollar showing mixed pressure against major currencies like the euro and yen.  

The Australian dollar’s recent strength was supported by resilient domestic data and risk-on sentiment, but profit-taking and USD rebounds have capped gains. Traders are watching technical levels closely, as a move above 0.7197 could signal further upside, while a dip below 0.7130 may trigger short-term retracements. Currency markets remain sensitive to shifts in US Treasury yields and commodity-linked flows that influence AUD positioning.  

Market participants are balancing optimism in the Australian economy with ongoing USD support, keeping trading ranges contained. Volatility is expected to persist, especially around economic releases from both Australia and the US. As the currency pair navigates this intraday corridor, traders are likely to weigh technical levels alongside macro signals, seeking opportunities amid fluctuating exchange rate dynamics.
