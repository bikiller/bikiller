The EUR/JPY currency pair extended its gains above the 185.00 level during early European trading on Thursday, briefly reaching 185.20. The Japanese Yen came under pressure as markets reacted to concerns over the government’s fiscal expansion plans, which could weigh on the currency. Traders noted increased demand for the Euro, with the exchange rate reflecting broader risk sentiment and USD correlations.  

Market participants remain cautious ahead of key economic data, with German Industrial Production figures for February scheduled later on Thursday. Analysts suggest that stronger-than-expected industrial output in Germany could support the Euro, potentially reinforcing EUR/JPY upside. Conversely, weaker data might slow the pair’s momentum, leaving traders focused on technical levels around 185.00 and near-term support zones.  

The broader forex landscape shows the Yen struggling against other major currencies amid fiscal stimulus expectations. EUR/USD trading also remains firm, hinting at sustained Euro strength. Investors are weighing risk-on flows, monetary policy expectations, and macroeconomic data, all of which continue to shape currency pair movements and exchange rate dynamics across Asian and European sessions.
