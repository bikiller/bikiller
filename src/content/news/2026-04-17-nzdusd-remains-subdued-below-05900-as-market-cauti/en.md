NZD/USD extended its subdued trading for a second consecutive day, hovering near 0.5890 during Friday’s Asian session. The currency pair remains under pressure as the US Dollar gains modest ground, reflecting cautious market sentiment ahead of the weekend’s high-stakes US-Iran meeting. Traders appear reluctant to take aggressive positions, keeping the exchange rate below the psychologically significant 0.5900 level.  

Market participants are closely watching the impact of geopolitical tensions on broader currency flows. The USD’s strength is supported by safe-haven demand, prompting a mild pullback in risk-sensitive currencies like the New Zealand Dollar. Technical traders note that sustained trading under 0.5900 could expose NZD/USD to further downside, while short-term resistance remains near the 0.5900 mark.  

Analysts suggest the cautious tone may persist through the end of the week, with volatility likely around key announcements or developments from the US-Iran dialogue. The exchange rate dynamics between NZD, USD, and other major currencies such as EUR may remain sensitive to geopolitical cues, influencing positioning in forex markets globally. Traders are expected to monitor support levels and market sentiment for directional cues in the coming sessions.
