The EUR/GBP currency pair extended its gains above the 0.8600 level, trading near 0.8630 during Friday’s early European session. Traders responded to disappointing UK GDP data, which weighed on the Pound Sterling, prompting a shift toward the Euro. Market participants noted that the weaker GBP fueled short-term trading opportunities, with the exchange rate maintaining its upward momentum against its European counterpart.  

Analysts highlighted that the Euro’s resilience was supported by broad USD and EUR strength in global markets. Investors are keeping an eye on potential retracements, as EUR/GBP remains technically above key support at 0.8600. Currency traders are also monitoring the pair for breakout signals, considering both fundamental data and short-term technical indicators that could influence intraday moves.  

Attention now turns to the Eurozone Industrial Production report for January, scheduled later on Friday. Positive readings could reinforce the Euro’s gains, potentially extending the EUR/GBP rally, while weaker output might cap upside momentum. With volatility in GBP stemming from the UK’s economic slowdown, market participants anticipate heightened trading activity and cautious positioning ahead of further macroeconomic releases.
