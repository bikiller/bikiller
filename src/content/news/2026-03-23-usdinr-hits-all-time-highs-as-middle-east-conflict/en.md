The Indian Rupee (INR) weakened against the US Dollar (USD) to hit record lows, with the USD/INR exchange rate soaring to new all-time highs. The ongoing conflicts in the Middle East have exacerbated the currency’s decline, leading to increased investor uncertainty. The INR’s downtrend intensified in early trading this week, with the currency trading near 83.25 against the USD, a sharp drop from previous levels.

This latest decline comes as Indian markets brace for a holiday on Thursday due to the Shri Ram Navami festival. The weakened INR has raised concerns about inflationary pressures and the broader economic impact. Traders are closely watching developments in the geopolitical landscape, as the ongoing volatility could further pressure the rupee. With the USD continuing to strengthen, the outlook for the INR remains uncertain in the near term.
