The British Pound has shown notable strength, emerging as the third best-performing G10 currency since the onset of the conflict. This resilience is largely driven by a significant 35 basis point rise in UK 2-year yields, which has provided crucial support for GBP/USD trading. Additionally, market expectations surrounding a rate cut by the Bank of England have diminished, bolstering the Pound's performance against major currencies.

As a result, GBP/USD has maintained its upward momentum, supported by this yield-driven rebound. The reduced rate-cut pricing reflects growing confidence in the UK's economic outlook, further strengthening the Pound’s position. This shift is also evident in the EUR/GBP exchange rate, which has been impacted by the Pound’s relative outperformance, despite the broader market uncertainty tied to energy concerns. The Pound's technical strength remains a key focus for traders as the economic dynamics evolve.
