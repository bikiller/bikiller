Gold prices (XAU/USD) saw a slight recovery on Monday, pulling back from a significant drop earlier in the session. The precious metal fell to $4,098, marking its lowest point since November and bringing it close to the 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA). Despite the rebound, gold remains down nearly 3% for the day, reflecting broader market volatility.

The price movement followed geopolitical developments, as U.S. President Donald Trump delayed planned military strikes on Iran, easing immediate tensions. This prompted a temporary lift in risk sentiment, supporting a modest recovery in gold. However, with the ongoing strength of the USD, trading sentiment remains cautious, leaving gold vulnerable to further price fluctuations in the coming days.
