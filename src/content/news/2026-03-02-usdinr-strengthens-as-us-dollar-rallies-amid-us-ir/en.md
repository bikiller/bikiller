The USD/INR currency pair opened the week with a 0.25% gain, rising to nearly 91.80, as the US Dollar strengthened amid escalating tensions between the United States and Iran. The surge in oil prices, driven by the ongoing conflict, further dampened market sentiment, putting pressure on the Indian Rupee.

Traders are closely watching the situation as rising oil prices often lead to a wider trade deficit for India, which typically weakens the Rupee. With global risk appetite soured by the geopolitical turmoil, the USD remains the preferred safe-haven currency. Market participants will continue to monitor developments for further volatility in the INR.
