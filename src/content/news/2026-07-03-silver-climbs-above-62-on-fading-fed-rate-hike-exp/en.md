Silver (XAG/USD) extended its rally on Friday, climbing above the $62 level to trade at $62.35, marking a 2.32% daily gain. The currency pair’s movement reflects strong inflows into precious metals markets, with trading activity showing renewed bullish momentum against the US dollar. The exchange rate strength underscores growing demand for safe-haven assets in the broader commodities space.

Sentiment shifted after a weaker-than-expected US employment report, which reinforced expectations that the Federal Reserve may pause further rate hikes. The softer labor data weighed on the USD and supported gains in XAG/USD, as traders adjusted positioning across forex and metals markets. Lower yields and reduced tightening expectations have encouraged renewed buying interest in silver and other precious metals.

Market participants continue to monitor USD volatility and upcoming macroeconomic releases for direction, with silver benefiting from its dual role as an industrial and defensive asset. The current exchange rate trend highlights how changes in Fed policy expectations can quickly influence trading flows between the USD, EUR, and commodity-linked currencies, sustaining upward pressure on XAG/USD in the short term.
