Gold (XAU/USD) extended its steep decline for the fourth straight day on Monday, falling below the $4,300 mark to hit a fresh year-to-date low. The precious metal has been under pressure for the past three weeks, with investors reacting to hawkish central bank signals and the firming US dollar. Early European session trading saw continued follow-through selling as momentum favored bears.  

The broader FX market reflected these dynamics, with the USD strengthening across major currency pairs. EUR/USD eased amid dollar strength, while other commodities-linked currencies showed mixed reactions. Traders highlighted rising yields and persistent inflationary concerns as key drivers, fueling demand for the greenback and weighing on non-yielding assets such as gold.  

Analysts note that the technical picture remains bearish for XAU/USD, with the $4,300 support now turning into resistance. Short-term traders may watch for further downside if USD strength continues, while a stabilization in central bank policy could offer some relief. Market participants remain cautious, adjusting positions across forex and commodities markets in response to central bank communications and ongoing geopolitical risks.
