The US Dollar remained supported in forex trading after TD Securities reiterated its higher-for-longer Federal Reserve outlook, a stance that continues to underpin the USD exchange rate against major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD. The bank said persistent inflation pressures and elevated geopolitical risks are likely to delay any meaningful shift toward monetary easing, reinforcing demand for the greenback across global markets.

According to TD Securities’ US Economic Outlook, stagflationary risks linked to the Iran conflict, higher Oil prices, and ongoing supply chain stress could keep inflation elevated well into 2026. That backdrop may prevent the Federal Reserve from cutting interest rates as quickly as markets previously expected, helping maintain higher US Treasury yields and supporting broader USD trading momentum against rival currencies.

The outlook has implications for forex investors positioning around interest-rate differentials and safe-haven demand. A prolonged restrictive Fed stance could limit upside in EUR/USD while sustaining volatility across emerging-market currency pairs sensitive to energy costs and global trade conditions. Traders are also monitoring whether persistent inflation pressures will further strengthen the Dollar’s relative appeal in the near term.
