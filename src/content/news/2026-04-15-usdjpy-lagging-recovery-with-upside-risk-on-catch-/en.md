USD/JPY is currently trading slightly weaker around the middle of its recent range, underperforming its G10 counterparts during the broader market recovery. As the sentiment improved across global markets, the Japanese Yen failed to capture the same momentum, trading at a disadvantage compared to other major currencies like the USD and EUR.

Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret highlight the upside risk for USD/JPY in the near term, suggesting potential for a catch-up rally. While the Yen remains within its recent range, the recovery in risk sentiment may prompt further gains in the USD/JPY exchange rate, especially if global conditions continue to favor the USD.
