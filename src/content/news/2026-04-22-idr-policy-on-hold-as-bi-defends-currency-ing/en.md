Bank Indonesia kept its benchmark policy rate unchanged at 4.75%, prioritising stability in the Indonesian rupiah over further tightening, according to ING economist Deepali Bhargava. The decision underscores a cautious policy stance as policymakers balance inflation management with currency defence amid shifting global interest rate expectations. Markets had broadly priced in a pause at this meeting.

Following the decision, trading in the USD/IDR currency pair is expected to remain range-bound as rate stability reduces volatility expectations. The rupiah’s defensive policy bias may offer near-term support, limiting sharp exchange rate swings. For forex markets, the hold reinforces expectations that Bank Indonesia will avoid aggressive tightening unless external USD strength intensifies further.

Broader Asian FX sentiment may also stabilise as Indonesia’s steady policy stance supports regional risk appetite. However, relative USD strength continues to be a key driver across major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY, keeping emerging market currencies sensitive to global yield differentials. Traders will watch future BI signals for any shift in bias.
