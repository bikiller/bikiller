The US Dollar Index (DXY) steadied near the 100.00 mark on Tuesday during European trading hours, recovering slightly after losses in the previous session. Traders remain cautious amid heightened geopolitical tension following Iran-related developments and a looming deadline tied to former President Donald Trump. The index, which tracks the USD against a basket of six major currencies, has shown resilience around key technical levels despite volatility.  

EUR/USD and other major currency pairs mirrored the cautious sentiment, with the euro trading under pressure as investors sought safe-haven assets. The USD maintained support, bolstered by expectations of continued demand in risk-averse trading conditions. Analysts note that movements around the 100.00 level could set the tone for near-term trading, influencing cross-rates and broader forex strategies.  

Market participants are weighing potential fallout from geopolitical risks alongside macroeconomic indicators, keeping the USD index range-bound for now. Traders are closely monitoring price action, with the 100.00 psychological level acting as a pivot. Should the index break decisively, it could trigger shifts across major currency pairs, including GBP/USD, USD/JPY, and commodity-linked currencies, shaping global trading sentiment in the days ahead.
