Expectations of progress towards peace in Iran are fueling a bond rally, with implications for the US Treasury market and the USD. According to BNY’s Geoff Yu, easing oil prices are contributing to a revaluation of real rates, creating favorable conditions for the US dollar as exporter surpluses and reserve management strategies come into play.

As oil prices decline, the strengthened USD could influence currency pairs such as EUR/USD, potentially driving the exchange rate higher. Market participants are closely monitoring these developments, as shifts in reserve flows and bond market dynamics could lead to increased trading activity in the USD, reinforcing its position against other major currencies.
