Silver (XAG/USD) edged lower on Monday, slipping more than 1.50% as trading activity remained subdued across commodity-linked currency pairs. The white metal has struggled to hold above the psychological $60.00 per troy ounce level, with sellers maintaining pressure throughout the session. On a monthly basis, silver is on track to end June down over 22.50%, reflecting sustained downside momentum.

Technical pressure intensified as XAG/USD slipped below the $60.00 threshold, raising concerns of a looming death cross formation on key moving averages. The weakness suggests growing USD-driven demand shifts and broader risk-off sentiment in precious metals trading. Market participants are watching whether momentum indicators confirm further downside extension in the exchange rate.

Looking ahead, the currency pair remains vulnerable to heightened volatility as traders reassess exposure to USD strength and correlated assets such as EUR/USD. A sustained break below $60.00 could accelerate bearish positioning in silver trading, while any recovery would depend on renewed demand for safe-haven assets amid shifting macroeconomic expectations and central bank policy outlooks.
