AUD/USD fell sharply toward the 0.6920 level on Tuesday, with the currency pair hovering near a three-month low as the US dollar strengthened across the board. The exchange rate came under pressure amid risk-off sentiment, as traders reduced exposure to higher-beta currencies. Broader forex market flows continued to favor USD buying, keeping upside attempts in AUD/USD limited.

Investors are now focused on the upcoming Australian Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, a key inflation indicator that will shape expectations for the Reserve Bank of Australia’s (RBA) policy path. Trading activity has turned cautious ahead of the release, with participants awaiting clearer signals on whether inflation remains sticky enough to support a tighter stance from the central bank.

In the near term, AUD/USD volatility may rise depending on the CPI outcome. A softer reading could reinforce expectations of policy easing and extend downside pressure on the exchange rate, while stronger data may offer temporary relief. Traders are closely watching the 0.6900 psychological support level for potential breakdown or stabilization.
