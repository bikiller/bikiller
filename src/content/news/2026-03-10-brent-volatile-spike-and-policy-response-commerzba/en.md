Brent crude prices surged dramatically to $120 per barrel, driven by heightened geopolitical tensions. The sharp spike was attributed to fears surrounding escalating tensions in Iran, which saw the commodity push through key technical levels. However, the rally was short-lived as prices pulled back following signals from President Trump that the conflict could be resolved soon, easing market concerns.

This volatility had a ripple effect on forex markets, influencing the USD/EUR exchange rate. The dollar initially strengthened against the euro as oil prices rose, with traders pricing in higher inflation risks. However, the subsequent retreat in oil prices allowed the euro to recover, stabilizing the EUR/USD exchange rate. Traders will continue to monitor geopolitical developments as well as any potential policy responses from central banks.
