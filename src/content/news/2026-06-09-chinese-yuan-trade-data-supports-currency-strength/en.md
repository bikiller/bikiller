The Chinese Yuan found additional support after China reported a wider-than-expected trade surplus of USD 105.4 billion in May, according to Societe Generale. The strong external balance was driven by a 19.4% year-on-year increase in exports, highlighting the resilience of the country’s manufacturing sector despite ongoing global economic uncertainties. Demand for AI-related equipment was a key contributor to the export surge, reinforcing confidence in China’s high-tech industries.

For forex markets, the robust trade performance strengthens the outlook for the Yuan’s exchange rate against major counterparts, including the USD and EUR. A larger trade surplus typically increases foreign currency inflows, creating supportive conditions for the domestic currency. Traders monitoring the USD/CNY currency pair may view the latest figures as a signal that underlying demand for the Yuan remains firm, even as broader market sentiment continues to be influenced by global growth and monetary policy expectations.

The data could also have wider implications for regional currency trading, as stronger Chinese export activity often supports confidence across Asian markets. While external risks remain, the combination of rising exports and sustained foreign demand for advanced technology products suggests the Yuan may retain a relatively stable footing in the near term. Market participants will continue watching upcoming economic releases for further clues on the currency’s trajectory.
