Vietnam faces a potential stagflationary shock, according to MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst Michael Wan. Ongoing disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz could drive higher oil and energy costs, significantly impacting economic growth and pushing inflation higher. This situation risks a rise in the USD/VND exchange rate, which could increase volatility in Vietnam’s currency markets as the economy grapples with these supply-side pressures.

The energy crisis is expected to lift inflation rates, further straining Vietnam’s growth prospects. As energy prices surge, the Vietnamese dong (VND) may weaken against the US dollar (USD) due to higher import costs. This would likely exacerbate inflationary pressures, making it harder for the central bank to manage the economic slowdown. Traders should monitor oil price fluctuations closely as they may continue to influence VND trading in the near term.
