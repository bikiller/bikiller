Gold (XAU/USD) held steady on Monday, recovering from an early gap-down as it traded around $4,717, bouncing back from an intraday low near $4,632. Despite the recovery, the upside remained constrained, with market sentiment wary of escalating tensions between the United States and Iran. This geopolitical uncertainty is keeping investors on edge, limiting bullish momentum in gold prices.

The lack of significant movement also stems from concerns about the Federal Reserve's monetary policy outlook. The broader risk-off environment, coupled with expectations of further tightening from the Fed, is weighing on the appeal of gold. As traders closely monitor developments in US-Iran relations and the Fed's next moves, XAU/USD's near-term outlook remains uncertain, with the precious metal finding support but struggling to gain clear upward momentum.
