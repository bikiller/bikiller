The US Dollar (USD) continues to struggle, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering just above 98.00, marking its lowest point since the onset of the Middle East conflict on February 28. Amid a broad risk-on sentiment in global markets, the greenback is underperforming across the board, losing ground against major G8 currencies. Investors are favoring higher-yielding and more volatile assets, putting additional pressure on the USD.

Currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD have benefited from the dollar's weakness, with the euro and pound showing steady gains in trading sessions. The depreciation in the USD exchange rate has reinforced a shift in capital flows toward equities and commodities, while safe-haven demand for the greenback softens. Technical levels near 98.00 in the DXY are being closely watched, as a break below could trigger further downside.

Traders are weighing geopolitical risks alongside central bank signals, balancing the Fed’s hawkish stance against global risk appetite. Continued USD weakness may influence cross-currency strategies, prompting adjustments in forex portfolios. Market participants remain alert for volatility in the coming sessions, with the interplay between risk sentiment and macroeconomic indicators set to dictate short-term exchange rate movements.
