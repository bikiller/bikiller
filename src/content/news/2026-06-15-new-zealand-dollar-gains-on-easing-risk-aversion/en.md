NZD/USD extended gains on Monday, trading around 0.5850 during Asian hours, recovering after minor losses in the previous session. The currency pair benefited from improved risk sentiment as easing risk aversion supported higher-yielding currencies against the USD. Traders monitored broader forex market flows as the exchange rate stabilized near recent ranges.

New Zealand Dollar strength was underpinned by recent domestic economic data, which helped the currency hold ground against the USD. The release reinforced expectations of steady economic momentum, encouraging buying interest in the NZD within the broader forex market. Compared with major pairs such as EUR/USD, sentiment toward risk-sensitive currencies improved modestly.

Looking ahead, traders are assessing whether the NZD/USD exchange rate can sustain gains above the 0.5850 handle amid fluctuating USD demand. Broader forex trading activity remains sensitive to shifts in US dollar sentiment and global risk appetite. Cross-currency moves in EUR and other majors may also influence near-term direction in the pair.
