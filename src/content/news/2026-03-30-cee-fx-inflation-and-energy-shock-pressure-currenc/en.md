Central and Eastern European (CEE) currencies are coming under pressure as rising oil prices and geopolitical tensions reinforce a risk-off tone across the region, according to ING.  notes that markets are increasingly sensitive to inflationary pressures, which are keeping central banks in a hawkish stance and sustaining elevated rate expectations.  highlights that this backdrop is weighing on broader FX sentiment.

The spike in oil is feeding into inflation dynamics, which in turn complicates the outlook for regional central banks already priced for further tightening. Higher energy costs are influencing exchange rate behavior, particularly against the USD and EUR, as traders reassess interest rate differentials. In this environment, CEE currency pairs are more vulnerable to downside moves, especially when global risk appetite deteriorates and safe-haven demand strengthens.

From a trading perspective, the combination of sticky inflation and external shocks suggests heightened volatility across CEE FX markets. Currency pairs in the region may remain under pressure if energy prices stay elevated, while any escalation in geopolitical tensions could amplify flows into USD and away from higher-yielding currencies. For now, the balance of risks continues to favor cautious positioning and tighter risk management.
