European aluminium producers face rising cost pressures as Commerzbank’s Norman Liebke highlights the impact of the EU Emissions Trading System (ETS) reform. Changes in benchmark calculations could increase expenses for recyclers and refineries, a development likely to reverberate across EUR/USD trading, as investors weigh the potential for higher production costs against currency exchange rate movements.

The reform, aimed at tightening emissions allowances, may push aluminium prices higher, influencing commodity-linked currencies and affecting USD liquidity. Traders are watching the EUR closely, anticipating potential volatility as European industrial output adjusts to the new cost structure. Spot rates and futures contracts are likely to reflect these shifts in market sentiment, with technical levels around $2,300 per tonne gaining attention.

For forex participants, the tightening of EU ETS rules could trigger a recalibration of trading strategies. EUR/USD pairs may experience fluctuations as importers and exporters factor in higher aluminium costs, while cross-asset trading could see correlation effects with other commodities. Investors are advised to monitor both the exchange rate trends and aluminium pricing closely to navigate the evolving market landscape.
