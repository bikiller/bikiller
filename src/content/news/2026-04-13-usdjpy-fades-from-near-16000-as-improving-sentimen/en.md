The USD/JPY currency pair saw a volatile session on Monday, trading in a broad intraday range as market sentiment influenced the exchange rate. The pair briefly surged to a session high near 159.86, driven by short-term USD strength, before retracing steadily. By the close, USD/JPY settled around 159.35, effectively unchanged on the day, reflecting a tug-of-war between risk appetite and safe-haven flows.  

Traders noted that improving investor sentiment globally weighed on the US Dollar, prompting a softening in USD/JPY despite earlier gains. The pair’s intraday swings highlighted the sensitivity of the exchange rate to risk-on dynamics, with equity market optimism supporting the yen’s steadier footing. Technical watchers pointed to the 160.00 level as a psychological barrier, which capped USD advances during the session.  

Looking ahead, analysts suggest that USD/JPY could continue to oscillate near current levels unless a decisive catalyst emerges. With the US Dollar under modest pressure and the yen benefiting from risk-positive sentiment, trading may see short-term corrections around 159.30–159.90. Broader trends in global currencies, including EUR/USD movements, will likely influence the pair, keeping market participants alert to shifts in macroeconomic sentiment and policy expectations.
