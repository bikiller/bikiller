The USD/PHP exchange rate surged to 60.10 following a national state of emergency declared by President Marcos. The move comes as the Philippines grapples with soaring energy prices, triggering heightened market concerns. The Peso's decline reflects the growing pressures on the Philippine economy, with the emergency powers expected to address these economic challenges.

As the Peso weakens, traders are keeping a close eye on the implications for the broader market. The USD remains strong against the Peso, with the exchange rate moving past significant resistance levels. Analysts are also monitoring the potential for further volatility, as the government's response to rising energy costs could influence future trading dynamics for the currency pair.
