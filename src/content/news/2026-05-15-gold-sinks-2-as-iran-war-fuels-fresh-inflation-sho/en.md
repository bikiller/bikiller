Gold tumbled sharply on Friday, with the XAU/USD pair dropping more than 2.30% amid heightened geopolitical tensions. The metal dipped to around $4,511 before rebounding slightly to trade near $4,551, as traders reacted to escalating fears of prolonged conflict between the US and Iran. The sudden retreat in gold reflects growing concern over a potential surge in inflation that could ripple through global markets.  

Currency markets have shown volatility alongside precious metals, with the USD strengthening against major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD as investors sought safe-haven liquidity. Analysts note that renewed inflation pressures could prompt central banks to accelerate interest rate hikes, adding a layer of complexity for forex trading and exchange rate dynamics. This environment has amplified risk sentiment, pushing both commodities and currencies into reactive trading patterns.  

For traders, the move highlights the interplay between geopolitical events and macroeconomic policy expectations. Gold’s drop below technical support near $4,511 signals potential further retracements, while currency pairs tied to the USD may continue to see upward pressure. Market participants are closely monitoring inflation indicators, Fed and ECB policy statements, and the evolving Middle East situation to gauge the next major shifts in both forex and commodity markets.
