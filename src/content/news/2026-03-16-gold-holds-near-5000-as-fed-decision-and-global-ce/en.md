Gold (XAU/USD) is trading near the $5,000 mark on Monday, holding steady despite a slight pullback in the US Dollar (USD) and Treasury yields following their recent rally. The precious metal’s sideways movement reflects cautious market sentiment ahead of the Federal Reserve’s upcoming policy decision, as investors weigh potential interest rate adjustments and their impact on the broader currency and commodities markets.  

The USD’s mild retreat has offered some support to gold, which often benefits when the greenback softens. Meanwhile, major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD are seeing modest fluctuations as traders position for central bank signals. Analysts note that gold’s technical levels remain resilient, with $4,980 providing near-term support, while resistance hovers just above $5,020, suggesting a consolidation phase rather than a clear directional trend.  

Global central bank policies continue to influence trading dynamics, with investors monitoring rate expectations across the Eurozone, Japan, and emerging markets. Any shift in monetary policy could ripple through forex markets, affecting currency pairs and exchange rates, and consequently, the appeal of safe-haven assets like gold. Traders are closely watching for volatility, as gold’s reaction to USD strength or weakness will likely dictate short-term trading opportunities in the coming sessions.
