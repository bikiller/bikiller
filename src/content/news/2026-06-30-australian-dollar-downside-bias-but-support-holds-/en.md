The Australian Dollar (AUD) remains under mild pressure against the US Dollar (USD), with United Overseas Bank strategist Quek Ser Leang noting a cautious tone in recent trading. The AUD/USD currency pair has been moving within a narrow range, reflecting subdued volatility and a slightly negative intraday bias as traders react to steady USD conditions and broader forex market sentiment.

Price action in the AUD/USD exchange rate is expected to stay contained between 0.6865 and 0.6900, suggesting a consolidative phase rather than a directional breakout. While downside pressure persists, the pair continues to attract buying interest near support, limiting deeper losses. This range-bound behavior indicates that market participants are hesitant to commit to aggressive positioning without clearer macroeconomic catalysts driving USD strength or weakness.

Looking ahead, a sustained break below 0.6865 is considered unlikely in the near term, reinforcing the importance of this level as a key support zone for AUD/USD trading. Any meaningful shift in direction will likely depend on changes in US Dollar momentum and overall risk appetite across global forex markets, with spillover effects potentially influencing sentiment in related pairs such as EUR/USD.
