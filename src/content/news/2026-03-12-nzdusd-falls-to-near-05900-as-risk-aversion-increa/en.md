NZD/USD continues its downward trend, reaching near the 0.5900 level during Thursday’s Asian trading session. The pair has seen three consecutive days of losses, pressured by the stronger US Dollar. As risk aversion rises due to escalating tensions in the Middle East, investors are flocking to safer assets, pushing the USD higher.

The US Dollar’s strength is further supported by surging energy prices, which have intensified inflationary concerns. This uptick in inflation expectations has reduced the market’s appetite for Federal Reserve rate cuts, bolstering the USD’s appeal. With these factors in play, NZD/USD faces continued headwinds, leaving the currency pair vulnerable to further declines in the short term.
