EUR/USD retreated 0.21% on Wednesday, pressured by the Federal Reserve’s decision to keep interest rates steady. The central bank’s Summary of Economic Projections (SEP) indicated that policymakers foresee only a single 25-basis-point rate cut by year-end, reinforcing a broadly hawkish stance. Traders reacted to the Fed’s cautious outlook, adjusting positions in the currency pair amid expectations of limited USD easing.  

The euro struggled to gain traction against the dollar, with the exchange rate dipping in early trading as market participants weighed the implications of a restrained Fed policy. Analysts noted that the Fed’s message signals confidence in the U.S. economic recovery, keeping USD demand elevated and challenging EUR/USD upward momentum.  

Looking ahead, EUR/USD trading is likely to remain sensitive to U.S. monetary policy cues. Any shift from the Fed toward more aggressive easing could prompt a rebound in the euro, while persistent hawkish signals may continue to cap gains. Technical levels around the 1.08–1.09 range could provide short-term support and resistance, guiding traders’ strategies in a cautious market environment.
