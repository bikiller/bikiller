The Australian Dollar (AUD) has gained momentum against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, as AUD/USD trading sees an upward push towards the 0.6950 resistance level. This move follows a rebound from the 100-day Simple Moving Average (SMA), reflecting stronger demand for the Aussie amid a softening Greenback. The market sentiment remains fragile, with traders staying cautious ahead of a looming deadline set by US President Donald Trump regarding Iran's negotiations.

The USD is facing downward pressure as global risk appetite weakens, largely influenced by geopolitical uncertainties. With the US President's ultimatum for Iran to either secure a deal or reopen the Strait of Hormuz by 8:00 p.m. ET, market participants are wary of potential disruptions. This uncertainty is boosting demand for risk-sensitive assets like the AUD, contributing to the bullish momentum in the AUD/USD exchange rate.
