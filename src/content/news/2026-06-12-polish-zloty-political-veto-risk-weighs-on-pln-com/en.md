The Polish zloty remained under pressure in forex trading as investors assessed growing political risks in Poland. According to Commerzbank analyst Tatha Ghose, President Karol Nawrocki has quickly built a record of vetoes, blocking legislation covering crypto assets, taxation and healthcare. The development has raised concerns about the pace of reforms, weighing on sentiment toward the PLN and influencing the exchange rate against major currencies such as the USD and EUR.

Market participants are increasingly focused on the implications for the EUR/PLN and USD/PLN currency pair outlook. Nawrocki’s willingness to veto key government initiatives has introduced additional uncertainty into Poland’s political and economic landscape, reducing confidence that structural reforms can be implemented smoothly. As a result, traders are factoring a higher political risk premium into zloty valuations.

Ghose expects the president to continue obstructing reform efforts until the 2027 elections, a scenario that could keep pressure on the Polish currency over the medium term. For forex markets, prolonged political gridlock may limit support for the PLN, while encouraging more cautious trading strategies. Investors will closely monitor future policy disputes and their impact on Poland’s economic prospects, as well as potential spillover effects on PLN exchange rates against both the EUR and USD.
