Silver (XAG/USD) extended its downside for a second straight session on Thursday, slipping below the key $70.00 psychological level during trading. The precious metal’s exchange rate against the USD weakened as risk sentiment shifted, with market participants reacting to fading optimism over a potential Middle East ceasefire. The move reflects a broader recalibration in safe-haven demand across currency pairs and commodities.

The decline comes as geopolitical tensions remain unresolved, prompting volatility across global markets while simultaneously supporting the US Dollar. A firmer USD often pressures silver prices, given their inverse correlation, making the XAG/USD pair more sensitive to shifts in macro sentiment. Traders are also watching EUR/USD and other major currency pairs for clues on broader USD strength and capital flows.

From a technical perspective, a sustained break below $70.00 could expose lower support zones, potentially accelerating bearish momentum in the near term. Market participants are closely monitoring upcoming economic data and geopolitical headlines, as these factors will likely influence trading direction. Any stabilization in risk appetite or USD weakness could offer silver a chance to recover lost ground.
