The Canadian Dollar slipped against the US Dollar on Tuesday, with the USD/CAD currency pair edging higher to trade around 1.3930 during Asian hours. The pair recovered modestly after registering slight losses in the previous session, reflecting the USD’s resilience as risk sentiment shifted. Traders monitored geopolitical tensions, particularly uncertainty over the Middle East peace truce, which supported safe-haven demand for the greenback.  

Despite rising oil prices, typically a boost for the Canadian Dollar, the loonie struggled to hold gains. Market participants noted that elevated risk aversion continued to overshadow commodity support, leaving the CAD under pressure. Technical levels around 1.3920-1.3950 have emerged as a short-term resistance zone, with momentum indicators signaling cautious trading amid thin liquidity in the early Asian session.  

Analysts suggest that the USD/CAD exchange rate may continue to fluctuate near current levels if geopolitical concerns persist. Traders are closely watching US economic data, oil inventories, and potential shifts in risk sentiment, which could dictate intraday direction. The broader forex market remains sensitive to safe-haven flows, with the USD attracting bids against commodity-linked currencies such as the CAD, while the EUR and other major pairs reflect mixed risk appetite across global trading venues.
