Silver (XAG/USD) remained confined within a narrow range on Monday, with prices hovering around $25.34 per ounce. Traders appeared cautious as ongoing US-Iran tensions kept financial markets on edge. The exchange rate showed limited movement, reflecting a delicate balance between safe-haven demand for precious metals and pressure from a firmer USD across global currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD.  

Technical levels acted as additional barriers, with resistance near $25.50 and support at $25.20 containing intraday fluctuations. Despite heightened geopolitical risk, market participants avoided aggressive trading positions, leaving silver’s short-term trend sideways. Analysts note that even minor headlines on US-Iran negotiations are now driving temporary spikes or dips in the currency pair, highlighting the sensitivity of XAG/USD to broader risk sentiment.  

Looking ahead, any significant escalation in Middle East tensions could push silver higher as investors seek USD-denominated safe havens, while easing news might reinforce current consolidation. Traders monitoring XAG/USD alongside key currency pairs will likely focus on the interplay of technical levels and USD strength, making trading ranges critical for short-term strategy. Volatility is expected to remain subdued until clearer signals emerge from both geopolitical developments and broader forex market trends.
