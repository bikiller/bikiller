Gold (XAU/USD) edged lower on Tuesday, showing a mild negative bias as markets paused for direction amid uncertainty over the resumption of US-Iran talks. Trading volumes remained subdued, with investors hesitant to take aggressive positions following weekend tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, which briefly heightened geopolitical risk. The yellow metal hovered near $1,975 per ounce, struggling to gain clear traction against the US dollar.  

The cautious mood in precious metals mirrored broader forex market sentiment, where the USD held steady against major currencies, including the EUR and JPY, amid safe-haven flows. Analysts noted that any confirmed developments in the US-Iran diplomatic front could spark sharp swings in gold’s exchange rate, as traders adjust positions in both commodities and currency pairs. Technical levels around $1,970 support and $1,985 resistance are being closely watched for breakout signals.  

Currency traders are also monitoring how potential geopolitical escalations could ripple through emerging market currencies and cross pairs. While gold remains sensitive to USD strength and geopolitical headlines, its muted trading highlights a market in wait-and-see mode. A resolution or renewed tensions in the Gulf could decisively influence short-term gold prices and broader forex positioning in USD, EUR, and commodity-linked currencies.
