West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude held firm near $103.80 per barrel in early European trading on Friday, building on a surge of more than 10% this week. Market participants attributed the rally to escalating supply fears after US President Donald Trump issued fresh warnings over Iran, intensifying geopolitical tensions. Traders noted that any disruption in Middle East output could further strain global oil inventories, supporting higher prices in the near term.  

The strengthened WTI has ripple effects across forex markets, with the USD gaining against major currencies amid risk-driven flows. Currency pairs such as EUR/USD saw increased volatility, as traders weighed the potential for tighter energy-linked inflation and its impact on Federal Reserve policy. Higher oil prices often bolster the dollar, influencing exchange rates and cross-asset trading dynamics globally.  

Analysts suggest traders keep an eye on technical levels, as WTI near $104 could test resistance around $105, while broader market sentiment remains sensitive to news from the Middle East. The interplay between crude prices and currency movements highlights the close connection between commodities and forex markets, with energy shocks potentially shaping USD performance and EUR/USD trends in the coming sessions.
