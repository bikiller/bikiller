The US Dollar slipped sharply on Monday as markets embraced a wave of relief following the reported US-Iran ceasefire. According to Brown Brothers Harriman’s Elias Haddad, risk assets rallied, with Brent crude climbing above $88 per barrel and equities posting solid gains across major indices. Safe-haven demand eased, allowing the USD to retreat against a basket of currencies, while Treasury yields stabilized, reflecting diminished geopolitical uncertainty.  

In forex trading, the USD/EUR pair fell back toward the 1.0950 level, while the USD/JPY eased below 146.50, highlighting broad-based dollar weakness. Haddad notes that the shift in sentiment has reduced upside pressure on the USD, giving traders room to consider long positions in risk-linked currencies such as the AUD and NZD. Exchange rates responded swiftly as investors rebalanced portfolios in favor of equities and commodities.  

Market participants are weighing the sustainability of the relief rally amid ongoing geopolitical tensions. Technical analysts observe that key support zones for the USD, including 1.0920 on the EUR/USD and 145.80 on USD/JPY, could be tested if risk appetite persists. Overall, Haddad emphasizes that the recent de-escalation offers a window for currency traders to adjust positions, with the USD likely facing short-term downside while commodity-linked currencies gain traction.
