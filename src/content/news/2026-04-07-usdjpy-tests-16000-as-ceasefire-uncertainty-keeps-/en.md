The USD/JPY currency pair is testing the critical 160.00 level as geopolitical uncertainty continues to pressure the Japanese Yen. The market remains volatile due to stalled ceasefire talks and looming threats of escalation in the Middle East. With President Trump's 8 pm ET deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz approaching, risk sentiment is shaky, keeping the Yen under strain against the stronger USD.

The oil market is also in focus, with prices topping $100 per barrel, further fueling concerns about inflation and economic instability. This combination of rising crude prices and heightened geopolitical tensions is driving the JPY lower, despite Japan’s generally lower inflation levels. Traders are watching for any significant moves in the USD/JPY pair, as the market braces for potential market shifts if the geopolitical situation worsens further.
