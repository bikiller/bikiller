European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member and Bank of France Governor, François Villeroy de Galhau, signaled that traders should not expect a rate hike at next week’s meeting. Speaking amid heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, he emphasized a cautious approach, suggesting that the bank will monitor economic developments closely before adjusting policy. The remarks weighed on the euro, with the EUR/USD currency pair dipping toward 1.0850.  

Market participants reacted to Villeroy’s comments by reassessing short-term expectations for ECB tightening. Traders increased bets on a pause in interest rate hikes, shifting focus to potential fiscal and geopolitical risks rather than immediate monetary action. EUR trading saw moderate volatility, as investors balanced euro weakness against a broadly stronger USD, with technical levels near 1.0820 acting as interim support for the currency pair.  

Analysts note that the ECB’s cautious stance could keep the euro under pressure in the near term, while the USD continues to benefit from safe-haven demand. Currency traders are watching key exchange rates and EUR/USD momentum for signals of further directional moves. Any escalation in Middle East tensions could reinforce USD strength, potentially prompting EUR/USD to test lower support levels around 1.0800, influencing broader forex market dynamics and trading strategies.
