Gold (XAU/USD) traded near $5,190 early on Wednesday in the Asian session, holding steady after experiencing significant volatility in recent days. The precious metal's price stabilized as market sentiment was influenced by indications of potential de-escalation in Middle East tensions. This relief eased investor concerns and provided some support to gold, although prices remained vulnerable to upcoming market catalysts.

Market participants are also keeping a close eye on the upcoming U.S. Consumer Price Index (CPI) inflation report. Any significant deviation from expectations could impact the USD's strength and, in turn, gold prices. With the exchange rate for XAU/USD holding near $5,190, traders are positioning for volatility, closely monitoring both geopolitical developments and U.S. economic data for future trading direction.
