The Eurozone's retail sales unexpectedly fell by 0.1% month-on-month (MoM) in January, diverging from forecasts of a 0.3% increase. This decline comes after December’s revised positive growth of 0.2%, up from an earlier reported drop of 0.5%. The unexpected contraction in consumer spending may signal a slowdown in economic activity, potentially weighing on the EUR/USD exchange rate.

Traders are now reassessing the outlook for the Eurozone economy, particularly as the disappointing retail sales figure could influence the European Central Bank’s policy stance. With inflation concerns still in play, a weaker retail sales reading could prompt market participants to adjust their expectations for further ECB rate hikes. This development adds pressure on the EUR, potentially affecting its performance against the USD in the coming sessions.
