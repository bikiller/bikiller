The British Pound has been the second-best performing G10 currency, following the US Dollar, since the onset of the Middle East conflict. According to Rabobank's Senior FX Strategist Jane Foley, this surge in GBP is largely due to a significant repricing of Bank of England (BoE) policy expectations, which has driven the currency higher against its peers.

GBP/USD has seen notable gains, reflecting the market's revised outlook on UK interest rates. As trading picks up, the Pound's strength has continued to outpace many of its counterparts, including the Euro, with GBP/EUR trading at elevated levels. The BoE’s stance on monetary policy has added momentum to the exchange rate, as traders price in the potential for further tightening ahead.
