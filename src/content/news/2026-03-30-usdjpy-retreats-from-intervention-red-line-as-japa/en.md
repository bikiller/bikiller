The USD/JPY currency pair pulled back on Monday, trading at 159.60, a 0.44% decline on the day. Earlier, the pair had surged to a nearly 20-month high above the 160.00 mark, reflecting strong momentum. However, the retreat was prompted by fresh warnings from Japanese authorities, signaling concerns over continued yen weakness and potential intervention in the foreign exchange market.

The intervention threat weighed on the pair's momentum, as market participants reassessed the strength of the US dollar against the Japanese yen. With the USD/JPY hovering just below 160.00, traders are closely monitoring Japan’s stance, as any intervention could have significant implications for both the yen and broader market sentiment.
