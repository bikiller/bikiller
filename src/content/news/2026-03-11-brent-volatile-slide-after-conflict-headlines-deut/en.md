Brent crude experienced a sharp decline, with analysts at Deutsche Bank noting an 11% drop in a single day. This reversal came amid growing optimism over supply risks linked to Iran, causing significant price fluctuations. Overnight weakness continued, fueled by news surrounding Saudi Aramco’s pipeline, U.S. political statements, and speculation about potential IEA reserve releases. These events have contributed to volatile market conditions, impacting both oil prices and broader commodity trading.

As the market reacts to these developments, the USD/EUR exchange rate remains sensitive to changes in global energy prices, which affect inflation expectations and risk sentiment. Traders closely monitor geopolitical developments and supply disruptions, as they influence demand for safe-haven assets like the U.S. dollar. This volatility underscores the importance of geopolitical risks in shaping the broader forex market, particularly for currency pairs sensitive to commodity price movements.
