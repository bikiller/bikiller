EUR/GBP is holding steady in Friday trading, hovering around 0.8675 as the currency pair struggles to build momentum after Thursday’s recovery. The exchange rate remains capped below the 0.8680 resistance level, which has repeatedly limited upside attempts. Earlier in the week, the pair briefly dipped to 0.8654 before bouncing modestly, reflecting cautious sentiment across European forex markets.

Stronger-than-expected UK Retail Sales data has provided underlying support for the British Pound, keeping EUR/GBP under pressure and preventing a sustained breakout higher. The improved consumer spending outlook has reinforced expectations of relative UK economic resilience, strengthening GBP demand in the currency market and limiting euro gains despite broader eurozone stability.

From a trading perspective, EUR/GBP remains locked in a tight consolidation range, with 0.8654 acting as immediate support and 0.8680 as firm resistance. Market participants are closely monitoring euro and pound divergence alongside USD-driven flows for directional cues. A decisive break above resistance or below support may define the next short-term move in the currency pair.
