The US Dollar Index (DXY) has slipped below the key 100 level, currently trading around 99.40, as traders digested the Federal Reserve's latest interest rate decision. Despite the Fed holding rates steady, concerns over inflationary pressures from rising energy prices dominated comments from Chair Jerome Powell, adding downward pressure on the USD. 

The EUR/USD exchange rate saw a noticeable reaction, with the euro strengthening in response to the dollar's decline. As the market adjusts to the Fed's stance, the DXY's drop signals potential shifts in the broader forex market. Traders will closely monitor upcoming data for further insights into future rate hikes and inflation trends.
