The Pound Sterling edged lower in European trading on Thursday, with the GBP/USD currency pair slipping toward the 1.3350 mark. Traders cited renewed risk-off sentiment following Iran’s rejection of a ceasefire as a key driver, prompting cautious positioning across major forex markets. The British pound also showed slight weakness against the euro, reflecting broader market sensitivity to geopolitical tensions.  

Market participants highlighted that the exchange rate movement comes amid thin liquidity, as investors adjust portfolios in response to heightened uncertainty. The US Dollar benefited from its safe-haven appeal, with buyers stepping in across USD pairs. Analysts note that near-term trading may remain choppy, with the GBP/USD pair facing resistance above 1.3400 and potential support around 1.3320.  

Strategically, forex traders are keeping a close eye on geopolitical developments, which continue to influence currency flows and volatility. Any escalation in Middle East tensions could sustain demand for the USD, while the pound may struggle to regain traction. The broader implication is cautious trading sentiment, with GBP exchange rates under pressure until clearer signals emerge.
