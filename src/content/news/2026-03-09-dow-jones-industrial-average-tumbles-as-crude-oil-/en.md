The Dow Jones Industrial Average slumped at the open on Monday, reacting to a weekend escalation in tensions between the US and Iran. Crude oil prices spiked past $100 per barrel, stoking fears of rising energy costs and weighing heavily on industrial and transportation stocks. Investors quickly shifted to safe-haven assets, with the USD strengthening against major currencies and the EUR/USD pair slipping toward key support levels.  

In forex trading, the US dollar index (DXY) climbed as traders sought refuge from heightened geopolitical risks. The EUR/USD pair edged lower to around 1.0800, while USD/JPY tested the 135.50 level amid heightened volatility. Commodity-linked currencies like the AUD and CAD felt pressure from the oil surge, reflecting growing concerns over potential inflationary impacts and central bank responses.  

Market participants are watching for further developments in the Middle East, as extended conflict could amplify supply disruptions and fuel additional volatility across both equities and currencies. Technical traders are focusing on support and resistance levels in major currency pairs, while the DJIA may continue to experience sharp swings. With crude trading above $100, risk sentiment remains fragile, suggesting a cautious approach for both forex and equity markets in the near term.
