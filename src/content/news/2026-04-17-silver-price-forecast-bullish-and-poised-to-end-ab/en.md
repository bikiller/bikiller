Silver climbed sharply on Friday, jumping more than 4% to reclaim the $81.00 per troy ounce mark. The rally came as the USD weakened across major currency pairs, reflecting a surge in risk appetite following positive developments in the Middle East. Traders noted that the softer dollar boosted precious metals, while the EUR/USD and GBP/USD exchange rates showed broad strength, signaling a shift in market sentiment.  

Momentum in silver trading was underpinned by both technical and geopolitical factors. The $81.00 level now stands as a key resistance, with bullish traders eyeing further gains if the metal maintains above this threshold. Market participants highlighted how the interplay between currency volatility and commodity prices often drives short-term trading opportunities, particularly in USD-denominated assets like silver.  

Analysts suggest the current move may set the stage for sustained upward pressure, as investors monitor global developments and central bank signals. A weaker USD tends to make silver more attractive to holders of other currencies, while EUR/USD and other major pairs continue to react to macroeconomic cues. With momentum building, traders are closely watching whether silver can consolidate above $81 and extend the recent bullish trend.
