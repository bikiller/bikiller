The euro has come under pressure as markets reassess the European Central Bank's (ECB) stance on inflation. Commerzbank's Christoph Rieger highlighted that the recent oil-driven sell-off has caused a significant shift in market expectations. Investors now anticipate a more aggressive, zero-tolerance approach from the ECB, leading to higher projections for year-end interest rates.

This shift in sentiment has fueled a sharp repricing of ECB policy, influencing the EUR/USD exchange rate. Traders are adjusting their positions, with the euro weakening against the US dollar. As markets price in tighter ECB policy, the focus remains on the central bank's next moves, particularly in response to the inflationary pressures amplified by surging oil prices. The USD continues to benefit from this recalibration, strengthening as traders favor the greenback amid the changing interest rate outlook.
