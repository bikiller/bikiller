USD/CAD exchange rate continues its upward advance, with Société Générale’s Kenneth Broux noting a decisive break above a key descending trendline and the upper boundary of a multi-month base. The move signals renewed bullish momentum in the currency pair, reinforcing an uptrend that is now extending toward the 1.4150 level in trading.  

The breakout strengthens short-term USD demand against the Canadian dollar, with traders interpreting the move as confirmation of a structural shift in USD/CAD trading conditions. Technical indicators suggest the pair is transitioning from consolidation into trend expansion, while the exchange rate benefits from sustained momentum above former resistance zones in the near term trading environment.  

Looking ahead, sustained trading above broken resistance would keep focus on 1.4150 as the next upside target for USD/CAD, while any pullbacks may find support near the former base structure. Overall sentiment remains tilted in favour of USD strength versus CAD, though volatility around broader FX drivers such as EUR/USD and risk sentiment could still influence direction.
