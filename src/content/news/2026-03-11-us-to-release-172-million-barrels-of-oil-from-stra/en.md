The Trump administration has announced plans to release 172 million barrels from the US Strategic Petroleum Reserve, aiming to curb surging crude and gasoline prices amid the ongoing tensions in Iran, Bloomberg reported Wednesday. The move comes as part of a coordinated global effort, with major economies seeking to stabilize energy markets. Oil traders reacted swiftly, pushing WTI crude futures down by over 3% in early US trading, while gasoline benchmarks also softened.  

The release is expected to influence broader financial markets, including forex. The USD strengthened against the EUR and JPY as investors anticipated lower inflationary pressures from easing fuel costs. Key currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY saw increased trading volume, with EUR/USD testing support near 1.0900 and USD/JPY approaching resistance around 110.50. Analysts note that sustained oil price relief could support risk sentiment, potentially shifting flows into higher-yielding assets.  

Market participants are weighing the scale and timing of the release, assessing its impact on energy-linked currencies and commodities. Hedging activity in crude and related derivatives is likely to intensify, affecting short-term exchange rate volatility. Forex traders remain cautious, closely monitoring crude benchmarks and USD movements, while technical levels suggest potential retracements in commodity-linked currencies like CAD and NOK as oil stabilizes.
