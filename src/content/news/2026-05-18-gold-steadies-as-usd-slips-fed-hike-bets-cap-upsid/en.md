Gold prices stabilized on Monday after dipping below $4,500, reflecting a cautious market sentiment. The US Dollar is weakening amid concerns that the Middle East energy shock could spark another inflation wave, influencing currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY. Traders are closely watching the XAU/USD exchange rate, which is showing consolidation around key support levels after recent volatility.

Market participants are balancing expectations for future Fed policy with geopolitical risks. While the softer USD is supporting gold, Fed rate hike bets are capping the upside, limiting bullish momentum. Trading volumes in the precious metal and related forex pairs remain elevated as investors hedge against potential inflationary pressures from energy markets.

Analysts suggest that gold’s near-term trajectory will depend on both the USD’s performance and incoming economic data. Any renewed strength in the greenback or signals of tighter monetary policy could weigh on XAU/USD, while escalating geopolitical tensions may provide support. Currency traders are also monitoring EUR/USD and other major pairs for correlated movements that could impact gold trading strategies.
