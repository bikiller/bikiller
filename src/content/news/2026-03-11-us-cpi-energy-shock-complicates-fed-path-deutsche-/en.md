Deutsche Bank's economists are closely watching the February US CPI report, which is poised to impact Federal Reserve policy expectations. The recent surge in oil prices has delayed market pricing for rate cuts, with inflationary pressures rising from higher energy costs and tariff-related strength in core goods. This could push headline inflation higher, complicating the Fed's decision-making process as traders monitor the USD’s movement.

Core CPI is expected to remain steady, indicating that underlying inflation pressures may be more controlled. However, the uptick in energy costs and goods prices is likely to influence short-term market sentiment. As traders adjust their positions, the USD’s strength against the EUR and other major currencies could face renewed pressure depending on how the Fed reacts to these inflationary signals. The exchange rate outlook remains fluid as investors weigh these inflation dynamics and their potential effect on future monetary policy.
