The Indian Rupee opened stronger against the US Dollar on Tuesday following Monday’s holiday for Dr. Baba Saheb Ambedkar Jayanti. The USD/INR currency pair edged lower as traders reacted to a sharp drop in global oil prices, fueled by optimism over renewed talks with Iran on crude exports. Early trading saw the rupee gain momentum, with the exchange rate moving toward near-term support levels around 82.50.  

Oil markets led the sentiment shift, with Brent crude dipping more than 2% amid expectations of increased Iranian supply. The weaker crude backdrop eased inflation concerns in India, supporting the rupee against the USD. Traders noted that the currency pair’s fall could prompt short-term technical buying, particularly if USD/INR tests key intraday support near 82.45-82.50.  

Meanwhile, broader forex markets showed mixed moves, with the EUR/USD pair holding steady amid muted European data. Analysts suggested that USD strength could be capped as dovish cues from global oil dynamics continue to influence emerging market currencies. Market participants are now eyeing upcoming economic releases and RBI signals to gauge the sustainability of the rupee’s early gains.
