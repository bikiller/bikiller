The AUD/USD exchange rate dropped significantly on Wednesday, tumbling by around 1% to approach the key support level of 0.7100. The move came after the Federal Reserve decided to leave interest rates unchanged, with Fed Chair Jerome Powell signaling that he plans to stay on the board even after his term ends. This development fueled a surge in demand for the US Dollar, strengthening its position against the Australian Dollar.

Market traders reacted to Powell’s comments, which contributed to an increased demand for the USD as investors anticipated the Fed’s future policy direction. The sharp decline in the AUD/USD pair highlights the ongoing impact of US monetary policy decisions and underscores the USD's continued dominance in global forex markets. As the pair nears 0.7100, further volatility could follow depending on upcoming economic data and Fed statements.
