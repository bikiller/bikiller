The Canadian Dollar strengthened against the US Dollar on Thursday, benefiting from a surge in oil prices amid ongoing tensions in the Middle East. As a result, USD/CAD saw a decline in its prior session gains, with the currency pair trading close to 1.3720 during the Asian trading hours. The positive correlation between the loonie and oil prices supported the Canadian Dollar as the rally in crude boosted market sentiment.

The exchange rate for USD/CAD faced downward pressure as oil prices continued to climb, reflecting geopolitical risks. With the Canadian economy heavily reliant on oil exports, the rise in crude prices further bolstered the Canadian Dollar’s value. As USD/CAD hovers near the 1.3720 level, traders are keeping a close eye on global oil market dynamics and Middle East developments, which could drive further movements in the currency pair.
