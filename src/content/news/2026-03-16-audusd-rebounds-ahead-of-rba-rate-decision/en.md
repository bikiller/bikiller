The AUD/USD currency pair rebounded by approximately 1.25% on Monday, recovering from last week's lows to trade around 0.7070. This upward movement comes ahead of the Reserve Bank of Australia's (RBA) upcoming rate decision, as traders remain cautious and monitor any potential shifts in monetary policy. The pair has been navigating a volatile range since its peak near 0.7190 in early February.

Despite frequent pullbacks toward the 0.7000 mark, the Australian dollar has shown resilience, making steady recoveries each time. This choppy trading pattern reflects a balance of market uncertainty and the influence of global economic factors, with the USD continuing to exert pressure on commodity-linked currencies. Traders are likely to remain focused on the RBA's stance on interest rates, which could further shape the short-term outlook for AUD/USD.
