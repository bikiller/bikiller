Rising Brent crude prices are increasingly influencing eurozone inflation dynamics, prompting fresh scrutiny of the ECB’s policy stance. ABN AMRO economists highlight that oil surging above $95 per barrel could feed through to consumer prices, keeping core inflation sticky. The EUR/USD has responded to these developments, trading around 1.0870, as traders weigh tighter monetary policy against the backdrop of energy-driven cost pressures.  

Market participants are factoring in potential ECB rate adjustments, with higher energy costs suggesting a more hawkish tone could persist. Technical levels in EUR/USD near 1.0850–1.0900 are proving key, as short-term trading reflects both euro strength on inflation resilience and dollar support from global risk aversion. Analysts note that continued Brent volatility could amplify swings in exchange rates, affecting EUR crosses and broader FX liquidity.  

The implications for forex trading are clear: investors are monitoring not only headline inflation but also sector-specific shocks, such as energy, which can accelerate policy divergence. EUR/USD is likely to remain sensitive to both ECB guidance and Brent movements, while USD liquidity and risk sentiment play a balancing role. Traders should watch 1.0800 as a key support, with 1.0920 offering near-term resistance, as the market navigates the interplay of commodity shocks and monetary signaling.
