The Japanese Yen slipped further as the US Dollar extended its rally, with the USD/JPY currency pair trading around 158.90 during Asian hours on Monday. This marks the sixth consecutive day of gains, highlighting persistent strength in the greenback. Investors continue to weigh a more hawkish Federal Reserve, fueling broad USD appreciation against major currencies, including the euro and yen.

Traders cite the Fed’s shift toward a tighter monetary stance as a key driver behind the rising exchange rate. Market participants are adjusting positions in anticipation of potential rate hikes, supporting USD demand. Technical charts show USD/JPY steadily pushing higher, reflecting both momentum trading and safe-haven flows into the dollar amid inflation concerns.

The yen’s weakness against the USD may pressure exporters and impact global currency markets, while EUR/USD and other major pairs react to the Fed’s policy trajectory. With volatility remaining elevated, analysts suggest close monitoring of central bank signals and economic data, as continued hawkish rhetoric could extend USD gains and maintain the yen at multi-month lows.
