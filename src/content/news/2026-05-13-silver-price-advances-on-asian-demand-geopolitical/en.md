Silver extended gains on Wednesday, trading around $88.65, up 2.40% on the day, as strong Asian demand underpinned the metal despite a firm US Dollar. The currency pair XAG/USD showed resilience even against rising US Treasury yields, which typically weigh on non-yielding assets. Traders cited geopolitical tensions and renewed interest from Asian investors as key factors driving the uptick in silver exchange rates.  

Market participants noted that the rebound in USD against major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD did little to deter silver buying. Technical support around $87.50 provided a floor for the metal, while momentum indicators suggest further short-term trading interest. Investors remain alert to fluctuations in currency markets, where a stronger dollar could pressure commodities priced in USD.  

Analysts suggest that silver’s current rally highlights the interplay between safe-haven demand and forex market dynamics. As the US Dollar continues to oscillate, XAG/USD trading may see further volatility, offering potential opportunities for traders hedging currency exposure. Sustained Asian demand could keep silver prices buoyant, even if Treasury yields maintain upward pressure, signaling a complex environment for both forex and commodity markets.
