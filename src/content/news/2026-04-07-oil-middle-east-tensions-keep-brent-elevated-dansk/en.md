Brent crude oil prices remain elevated at $111 per barrel, driven by ongoing tensions in the Middle East and disruptions around the critical Strait of Hormuz. As traders assess the risk of further supply disturbances, oil markets continue to experience volatility. These geopolitical tensions are fueling uncertainty, leading to a stronger demand for safe-haven assets and impacting global energy trade.

The surge in oil prices has broader implications for currency markets, with the USD strengthening against commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian dollar (CAD) and the Russian ruble (RUB). The EUR/USD exchange rate is also reflecting the ripple effects, as the European economy faces increased costs from higher energy prices. This market movement highlights how oil price fluctuations can influence global trading dynamics and currency valuations.
