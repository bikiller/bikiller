The USD/JPY currency pair edged higher on Thursday, March 26, trading near the 159.70 level as the US Dollar retained strength against a softer Japanese Yen. Market participants noted a sustained bullish bias, with the greenback finding support amid renewed optimism from comments by former US President Donald Trump. Traders reacted to the remarks, boosting USD demand while JPY remained under pressure due to persistent risk sentiment and lower safe-haven flows.  

Momentum in the exchange rate was further reinforced by technical signals, with USD/JPY holding above key intraday support levels. Analysts highlighted that the pair’s upward trajectory could face resistance near 160.00, but the current trend suggests continued USD strength in early trading. The move comes amid mixed performance in other major currency pairs, with EUR/USD showing modest consolidation and risk-driven trading patterns influencing broader FX markets.  

Investors are watching closely for economic indicators and geopolitical developments that could sway sentiment. Any shifts in US Treasury yields or Bank of Japan policy expectations may further impact USD/JPY trading dynamics. For now, market activity reflects cautious optimism for the Dollar, suggesting that bullish pressure may persist if the Yen remains under duress and global risk appetite holds steady.
