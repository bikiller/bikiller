The USD/CAD currency pair continued its upward trajectory on Thursday, as the US Dollar maintained broad support amid escalating geopolitical tensions. Investors remained cautious over the uncertain US-Iran negotiations and rising Middle East risks, pushing the exchange rate higher. Traders observed the pair testing intraday highs near 1.3620, signaling persistent demand for the Greenback against the Canadian Dollar.  

Market participants noted that the CAD faced pressure from both risk-off sentiment and weaker oil prices, limiting its ability to counter the USD’s strength. Technical charts indicate that immediate support for USD/CAD is around 1.3580, while resistance sits near 1.3650. Analysts suggest that a sustained break above these levels could reinforce bullish trading momentum, keeping the pair in focus for short-term traders.  

Across global forex markets, the US Dollar remained resilient versus major currencies, including the EUR and JPY, reflecting safe-haven flows amid uncertainty. Traders are closely monitoring economic data and central bank signals, as any shift could influence USD strength and broader currency pair movements. With geopolitical concerns unlikely to abate soon, the USD/CAD exchange rate may continue to see elevated volatility in the near term.
