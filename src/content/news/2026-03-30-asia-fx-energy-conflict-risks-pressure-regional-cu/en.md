Prolonged US-Iran tensions are beginning to pressure Asian currencies, as highlighted by MUFG Senior Currency Analyst Lloyd Chan. The ongoing conflict, particularly the risk to Middle Eastern energy infrastructure, has dampened market sentiment. This geopolitical risk is contributing to a stronger USD, with USD/JPY testing levels near 134.50, as traders seek safe-haven assets amid the uncertainty.

As regional currencies face mounting pressure, the outlook for the Japanese yen, Australian dollar, and other Asian currencies remains uncertain. The EUR/USD exchange rate has also been affected, with the pair consolidating around 1.0700. Traders are closely monitoring developments in the Middle East, as any escalation could further impact risk sentiment and drive the USD higher, intensifying volatility across the forex market.
