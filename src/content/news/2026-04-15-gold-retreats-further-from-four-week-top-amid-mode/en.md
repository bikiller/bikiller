Gold (XAU/USD) slipped further from its four-week peak as the USD showed signs of modest recovery. During Wednesday’s Asian session, gold had briefly touched its recent high, but selling pressure emerged, pushing the price down to around $1,815 in late trading. Traders noted that while the retracement is notable, the downside appears limited, with buyers still eyeing support near key technical levels.  

The currency market dynamics suggest that the USD’s slight rebound influenced gold’s retreat, as risk-off flows eased and the greenback gained traction against major pairs like the EUR/USD. Forex traders are closely monitoring exchange rate movements, particularly after recent data that hinted at firmer US economic fundamentals, which could underpin USD strength and temper gold’s upward momentum.  

Market watchers are weighing gold’s technical outlook against currency pair trends, with XAU/USD consolidating after the spike to four-week highs. Short-term trading strategies focus on resistance around prior highs and support near $1,800, while shifts in USD strength or EUR exchange rates could trigger renewed volatility. Overall, the market shows cautious optimism, balancing retracement pressure against persistent safe-haven demand.
