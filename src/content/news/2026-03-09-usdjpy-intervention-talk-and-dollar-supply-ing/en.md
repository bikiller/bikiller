USD/JPY has returned to levels that put it squarely in Japan’s FX intervention territory, according to ING’s Chris Turner. The currency pair has been propelled higher by a mix of global shocks and surging oil prices, intensifying demand for the US dollar. Traders are watching closely as the exchange rate hovers near technically sensitive zones, adding pressure to Japan’s currency authorities to consider action.  

Despite speculation, Turner sees a coordinated US–Japan intervention as unlikely in the near term. The dollar’s broad strength, supported by robust liquidity and ongoing market flows, makes a joint move less feasible. Still, any unexpected intervention could trigger a sharp repricing in USD/JPY, potentially knocking several big figures off the exchange rate in a short span.  

Market participants are positioning for volatility across major currency pairs, including USD/EUR and USD/JPY, as shifts in risk sentiment and commodity prices ripple through trading desks. The current backdrop of strong dollar supply and external shocks underscores the delicate balance central banks must maintain while monitoring speculative pressures on key FX levels. Traders remain alert for sudden movements that could reshape intraday and short-term trends.
