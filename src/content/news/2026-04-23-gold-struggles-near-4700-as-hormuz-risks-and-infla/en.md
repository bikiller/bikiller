Gold (XAU/USD) failed to build on its modest gains from the previous session, facing fresh selling pressure in the Asian session on Thursday. The precious metal struggles near the $4,700 mark as inflation-driven expectations of a more aggressive Federal Reserve tighten their grip on the market. The ongoing concerns surrounding geopolitical risks, particularly in the Strait of Hormuz, have further lifted the US Dollar, keeping gold under pressure.

The USD continues to benefit from shifting market sentiment, with traders factoring in higher interest rates from the Fed in response to persistent inflation. As the exchange rate for the EUR/USD hovers near 1.09, investors are increasingly cautious about holding gold in a higher yield environment. With inflation fears intensifying and the US Dollar on the rise, XAU/USD’s upside potential remains limited, while the dollar’s strength keeps weighing heavily on the precious metal.
