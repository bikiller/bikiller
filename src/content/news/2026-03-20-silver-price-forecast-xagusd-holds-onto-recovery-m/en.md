Silver prices (XAG/USD) maintained their recovery around $74 during Friday’s Asian session, following a rebound from Thursday’s gains. The white metal climbed after briefly testing February lows near $64.00, suggesting short-term support at that level. Traders noted that the exchange rate remains sensitive to USD strength, with market participants closely monitoring U.S. dollar movements and broader currency pair dynamics for further guidance.  

Analysts highlight that XAG/USD’s bounce reflects risk-on sentiment after heavy selling pressure earlier this week. Despite the uptick, the outlook for silver remains cautious as the currency pair faces resistance near key technical levels, while the USD’s performance against major currencies such as EUR continues to influence trading flows. Volatility in related commodity markets and forex pairs could shape near-term price action.  

Market watchers suggest that while the recovery provides temporary relief, sustaining gains above $74 will require stronger momentum in risk appetite and softer USD trends. Cross-market factors, including fluctuations in EUR/USD and other major currency pairs, may dictate whether silver continues its rebound or reverts toward February lows. Traders remain alert to central bank signals and macroeconomic data impacting both forex and commodity markets.
