The USD/CHF currency pair is trading higher this week, holding steady near 0.7925 after recovering from a near three-week low of 0.7855 reached on Friday. The exchange rate's resilience comes amid renewed concerns over US-Iran talks, which failed to make progress, and ongoing inflation fears. These factors continue to bolster demand for the US dollar across the forex market.

The US dollar remains supported by these geopolitical tensions, pushing the USD/CHF higher despite fluctuating risk sentiment. As inflationary pressures persist, traders are eyeing further moves in the currency pair, with technical levels around 0.7925 and 0.7855 likely to play key roles in determining the short-term direction. Market participants will continue to watch for any developments on the inflation front and geopolitical events that may impact the USD’s strength.
