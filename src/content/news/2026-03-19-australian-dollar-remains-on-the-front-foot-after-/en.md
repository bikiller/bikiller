The Australian Dollar (AUD) showed resilience against the US Dollar (USD) during Thursday's Asian session, with the AUD/USD pair edging higher. This uptick reversed some of Wednesday's retracement from the weekly high, where the pair had faced resistance near the 0.6700 mark. Despite this minor recovery, bulls appear hesitant, lacking strong conviction to push the currency higher.

The mixed jobs report from Australia, which showed slight improvements in employment but a drop in the participation rate, has left traders uncertain about the next direction. As the AUD/USD hovers around 0.6670, the market is caught in a range-bound phase, with volatility driven by both global risk sentiment and domestic economic data. Without a clear catalyst, the pair remains susceptible to fluctuations.
