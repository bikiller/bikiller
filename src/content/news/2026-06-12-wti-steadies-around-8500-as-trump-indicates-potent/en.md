West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil stabilized near $85.00 per barrel on Friday during Asian trading hours, following a sharp decline of over 5.5% in the previous session. The drop had rattled energy markets, briefly affecting risk sentiment across major currency pairs as traders reassessed USD and EUR positions in light of shifting geopolitical developments. 

The recent volatility comes amid signals from former President Trump regarding a potential deal with Iran, which has traders speculating on future crude supply levels. Forex markets responded cautiously, with the USD seeing modest gains against major currencies, while commodity-linked currencies reflected the softer oil pricing in their exchange rates. Trading desks noted that any confirmed progress on Iran negotiations could influence both crude benchmarks and associated currency pairs. 

Analysts suggest that the current consolidation around $85.00 will be closely watched, as oil’s trajectory often impacts broader financial markets, including forex volatility. Short-term traders are balancing technical levels with fundamental news flow, monitoring how USD, EUR, and other major currencies react to energy price shifts. The market remains sensitive to further announcements, underscoring the link between oil dynamics and global trading sentiment.
