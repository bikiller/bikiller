The Euro (EUR) has shown a modest recovery against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, as EUR/USD pares some of its earlier losses. After hitting intraday highs, the Greenback pulled back slightly following the release of the latest S&P Global Purchasing Managers Index (PMI) data. The exchange rate for EUR/USD moved away from the day’s lows, reflecting this shift in market sentiment.

The data release, which showed signs of economic slowing in the US, helped ease upward pressure on the USD. Traders reacted to the softer-than-expected PMI figures, and as a result, the EUR/USD currency pair regained some ground. The pullback in USD allowed the Euro to trim losses, but the broader trend remains volatile as market participants continue to monitor upcoming economic indicators for further clues on the direction of the currency pair.
