Central and Eastern European currencies remain under pressure as escalating tensions between the US and Iran continue to push oil prices higher, adding strain to regional markets. ING’s Frantisek Taborsky notes that the EUR/HUF exchange rate has been particularly sensitive, with traders closely watching for any spikes linked to geopolitical developments. USD inflows into safe-haven assets have further supported volatility in local currency pairs.  

Market participants are preparing for upcoming economic releases from Hungary, Turkey, and Poland, though Taborsky expects geopolitics to overshadow domestic data. EUR/HUF and USD/TRY trading has reflected heightened risk sentiment, with technical levels being tested as oil-driven stress feeds through regional currencies. The interplay of global energy markets and geopolitical uncertainty is increasingly dictating intraday moves.  

Traders eyeing Central European markets should monitor the reaction of exchange rates to both oil prices and geopolitical headlines, as these factors may continue to dominate trading strategies. Short-term positioning in EUR, USD, and local currencies appears heavily influenced by risk-off flows, suggesting further bouts of volatility until the geopolitical landscape stabilizes or new macroeconomic signals emerge.
