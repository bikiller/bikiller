AUD/JPY held near 109.50 in early Asian trading on Tuesday, stabilizing after losses in the previous session. The currency pair traded around 109.70, reflecting cautious investor sentiment as traders digested fresh economic data. Market participants are eyeing potential downside pressure on the Australian Dollar, following weaker-than-expected readings from China’s National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI), which added to concerns over regional growth.

The Japanese Yen showed resilience amid Japan’s latest CPI figures, which reinforced expectations of a gradual inflation trend rather than aggressive monetary tightening. This backdrop kept AUD/JPY range-bound, as the exchange rate balanced between AUD weakness and Yen stability. Traders noted that key technical levels around 109.50 could serve as a pivot, with the next support and resistance points guiding short-term trading strategies.

Looking ahead, AUD/JPY movements may hinge on broader USD trends and risk sentiment, especially given ongoing volatility in Asian equities. A sustained decline in the Australian Dollar could push the pair below current levels, while a rebound in risk appetite or stronger domestic data might lift AUD against the Yen. Market watchers remain focused on PMI revisions, CPI updates, and global trading flows to gauge potential swings in the currency pair.
