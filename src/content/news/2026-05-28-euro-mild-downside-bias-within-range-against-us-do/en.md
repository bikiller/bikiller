EUR/USD ended the latest session little changed near 1.1625 after briefly climbing to 1.1660, as traders weighed mixed momentum in the currency pair against the broader strength of the USD. Analysts at UOB noted that while the exchange rate showed resilience during intraday trading, price action suggests a mild downside bias is beginning to build in the near term.

According to UOB’s Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann, slightly firmer bearish momentum could push EUR/USD toward the 1.1610 technical level. However, the analysts do not expect the pair to break decisively below the more significant 1.1590 support for now. The view reflects cautious sentiment in forex trading as investors continue monitoring US Dollar demand and broader macroeconomic signals.

The euro’s inability to sustain gains above 1.1660 may also indicate that upside momentum remains limited despite periods of volatility in the foreign exchange market. For traders, the current range-bound movement highlights a market still searching for direction, with short-term trading likely to remain focused on technical support and resistance levels as the EUR and USD battle for near-term control.
