Gold (XAU/USD) is trading higher on Monday, supported by moderate risk appetite after US and Iran comments hinting at progress in peace negotiations, which weighed on the USD. The currency pair’s exchange rate strengthened as traders reduced safe-haven demand for the US dollar in forex trading sessions across major markets.

Spot gold’s bullish momentum is driving XAU/USD towards the $4,600 level, with investors positioning for further upside in the currency pair’s exchange rate. Trading activity remains sensitive to geopolitical headlines, while easing tensions continue to undermine USD strength. Technical traders note sustained bids supporting the broader gold rally in global forex markets.

Looking ahead, forex markets may see continued USD softness if peace talks advance, potentially benefiting risk-sensitive currencies such as EUR alongside gold. The exchange rate dynamics in XAU/USD suggest traders are balancing risk appetite with safe-haven flows, keeping trading ranges volatile as macroeconomic and geopolitical signals drive short-term positioning sentiment.
