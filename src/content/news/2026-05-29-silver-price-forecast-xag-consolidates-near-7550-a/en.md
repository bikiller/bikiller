Silver (XAG/USD) edged slightly lower on Friday, retreating 0.16% as it hovered in the $75.00-$76.00 range. Trading remained largely contained near the 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at $75.70, reflecting a period of consolidation after recent volatility. The currency pair’s exchange rate movement suggests cautious sentiment among traders amid mixed signals from USD strength and broader market risk appetite.

Analysts note that the 200-day SMA has emerged as a key level for bearish traders, with potential pressure if silver fails to sustain gains above the current consolidation zone. USD dynamics, particularly in relation to major currencies like EUR, are influencing precious metals, as a firmer dollar tends to weigh on silver and gold prices. Market participants are monitoring these technical benchmarks closely for trading opportunities.

Looking ahead, the XAG/USD pair may continue to navigate within this tight band unless triggered by macroeconomic data or shifts in USD liquidity conditions. Short-term traders are eyeing both the 50-day and 200-day SMAs for signals, while longer-term investors weigh geopolitical and monetary factors affecting commodity prices. The subtle interplay between USD movements and silver’s technical support could shape the near-term trading landscape.
