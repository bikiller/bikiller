Gold (XAU/USD) climbed nearly 2% on Wednesday, extending gains in a volatile session as traders reacted to mixed US economic data. The currency pair advanced despite a firmer US Dollar and elevated US Treasury yields, which typically weigh on non-yielding assets. Persistent uncertainty in macro indicators supported demand for the precious metal in forex trading.

Fed Chair Kevin Warsh reiterated the central bank’s commitment to price stability, reinforcing expectations for tighter monetary policy. The stronger USD and rising Treasury yields added pressure across currency pairs, while traders evaluated the implications for exchange rates and gold pricing within broader forex markets. Market participants remained focused on upcoming US data releases and Fed policy signals.

Outlook for XAU/USD remains sensitive to USD strength, real yields, and risk sentiment. Traders are watching key resistance and support levels as volatility persists in major currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY, with gold acting as a hedge amid shifting expectations for global interest rates. Broader market sentiment continues to be driven by US dollar trends and bond yield movements.
