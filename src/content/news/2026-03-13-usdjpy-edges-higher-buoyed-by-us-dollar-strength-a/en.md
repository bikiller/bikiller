USD/JPY inched higher on Friday, trading around 159.50, marking a 0.10% gain for the day. The currency pair remains near recent peaks, underpinned by sustained US Dollar (USD) strength. Investors continue to weigh the broad interest rate differential between the United States and Japan, which has reinforced USD demand against the yen and kept trading activity focused near key technical levels.  

Market participants are also monitoring potential intervention risks in Japan, as authorities have historically stepped in to temper rapid yen depreciation. Despite these concerns, the USD’s resilience has allowed the exchange rate to maintain its upward momentum, while trading volumes suggest a cautious approach from yen sellers.  

The broader forex landscape shows the USD maintaining strength against major currencies, including the euro, as global investors react to economic data and interest rate expectations. Analysts note that the USD/JPY pair may continue to test psychological thresholds if the current US-Japan rate gap persists, making it a focal point for traders balancing risk, carry trade opportunities, and intervention scenarios.
