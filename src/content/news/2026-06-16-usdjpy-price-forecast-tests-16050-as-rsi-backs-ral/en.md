USD/JPY extends its advance on Tuesday as traders position ahead of the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy decision, which began during the session. The currency pair’s exchange rate climbs steadily, with USD/JPY trading at 160.47 while briefly testing the 160.50 level. Momentum remains supported by technical indicators, as RSI backs the ongoing rally in broader USD trading sentiment.

Market attention remains centered on the Federal Reserve’s policy outlook, with USD strength underpinning the USD/JPY currency pair. The exchange rate holding above 160.00 keeps the market in Japan’s perceived intervention zone, raising caution among traders. Volatility may increase as trading desks assess potential policy signals and the risk of official action to slow further USD/JPY gains.

Short-term outlook for USD/JPY remains tied to Fed guidance and any signals of tighter financial conditions. As long as the currency pair holds above key support near 160.00, buyers may continue to dominate trading flows. However, intervention rhetoric from Japanese authorities could cap further upside in the exchange rate and trigger sharp intraday reversals.
