The US Dollar maintained a firm tone across global currency markets during Thursday’s American session, supported by heightened risk aversion. Persistent concerns over potential oil supply disruptions have weighed on investor sentiment, boosting demand for safe-haven assets like the USD. The EUR/USD currency pair slipped to around the 1.1520 zone, approaching its 2026 low of 1.1507 as traders reassess market risk.  

Trading activity showed the USD gaining broadly, with euro weakness reflecting both technical pressures and caution over broader economic signals. The exchange rate’s proximity to its 2026 trough suggests continued sensitivity to geopolitical and commodity-driven developments. Market participants are watching closely for any shifts that could push the pair below critical support levels.  

Analysts note that the EUR/USD’s current trading behavior underscores the influence of global risk sentiment on currency markets. A sustained rebound in oil supply concerns or renewed risk-off sentiment could further strengthen the USD, keeping the euro under pressure. Traders remain alert to potential catalysts, with the 1.1507 level serving as a key technical benchmark for short-term trading strategies.
