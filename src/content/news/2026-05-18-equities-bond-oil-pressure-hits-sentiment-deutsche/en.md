U.S. equities saw a sharp intraday pullback as the S&P 500, despite extending its streak to seven consecutive weekly gains—the longest since 2023—reacted to rising bond yields and climbing oil prices. Deutsche Bank strategists highlighted that the surge in Treasury yields weighed on sentiment, with the index recording its steepest single-day decline since March. This volatility has stirred cautious trading in USD and EUR currency pairs as investors reassess risk exposure.

Oil’s recent rally added pressure on equities and influenced forex flows, particularly pushing USD/JPY higher amid safe-haven demand. Rising commodity costs are feeding into broader market concerns, prompting traders to rotate capital into currencies like the USD while rebalancing EUR positions. Technical levels around the S&P 500’s recent highs are being closely monitored, as any breach could amplify cross-asset reactions in major currency pairs.

Looking ahead, market participants are weighing macroeconomic indicators against geopolitical factors, with bond yields and commodity trends shaping trading strategies. The interplay between equities, oil, and forex markets suggests that investors may continue to favor defensive USD positions, while EUR and other risk-sensitive currencies could see heightened volatility. Traders are advised to track both equity and bond developments for cues on short-term exchange rate movements.
