The U.S. Dollar (DXY) continues to rise, bolstered by the surge in energy prices triggered by the ongoing Middle East conflict. As crude oil prices climb, energy-exporting nations, especially in the Middle East, are benefitting, further strengthening the greenback. The rise in energy prices has led to a divergence in global economic conditions, with Europe and Asia, reliant on energy imports, feeling the pressure.

The EUR/USD pair remains under pressure, trading near recent lows, reflecting concerns about the economic impact on the Eurozone. The ongoing energy crisis places significant strain on European economies, further eroding the euro’s value. Meanwhile, the USD benefits from higher yields and safe-haven flows, reinforcing its position in the forex market. Traders will closely monitor any shifts in energy prices and geopolitical developments, which could dictate the next moves in the exchange rates.
