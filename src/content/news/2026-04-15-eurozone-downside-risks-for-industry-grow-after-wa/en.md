Eurozone industrial output showed modest growth in February, rising just 0.4% month-on-month, according to ING’s Bert Colijn. Despite the increase, production remains below most of the levels seen in 2025, signaling mounting pressures on the bloc’s industrial sector. Analysts point to elevated energy prices and the ongoing Middle East conflict as key factors dampening momentum, particularly for energy-intensive industries. Traders are watching the EUR/USD closely as these pressures could influence the exchange rate.  

The combination of slower industrial activity and geopolitical risk has stirred caution in forex markets. EUR trading against the USD has shown volatility, with investors weighing the potential for subdued Eurozone growth against the backdrop of resilient US economic data. Higher energy costs are likely to curtail investment in key sectors, which could limit the euro’s upside and prompt market participants to adjust trading strategies accordingly.  

Looking ahead, Colijn emphasizes that downside risks are likely to persist, with the Middle East war and energy inflation continuing to shape the industrial outlook. Currency traders are factoring these headwinds into their positions, with technical levels in EUR/USD being closely monitored. Market participants are preparing for potential fluctuations as investment sentiment and economic data intersect with ongoing geopolitical uncertainty.
