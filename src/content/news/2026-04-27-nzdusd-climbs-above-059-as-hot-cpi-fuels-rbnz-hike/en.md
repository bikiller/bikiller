NZD/USD edged higher on Monday, climbing above the 0.59 mark as stronger-than-expected CPI data bolstered expectations of a Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ) interest rate hike. The currency pair gained roughly 0.4%, settling near 0.5905, though it struggled to breach the 0.5925 resistance that capped last week’s rebound. Traders noted that the uptick reflected growing appetite for risk-sensitive currencies amid dovish signals from major central banks.  

Market participants highlighted that the exchange rate’s advance was underpinned by fresh inflation metrics showing persistent price pressures, reinforcing bets that the RBNZ may tighten monetary policy sooner rather than later. Against a broadly firmer USD, NZD/USD’s move was modest but technically meaningful, suggesting short-term support around 0.5880. The pair’s trajectory continues to attract attention as traders weigh potential divergence with the Federal Reserve’s policy stance.  

Analysts pointed to a cautious trading environment, where gains are limited by technical resistance and the looming Fed decision. Investors are monitoring broader forex flows, with the EUR and other major currencies influencing NZD/USD volatility. Any sustained break above 0.5925 could open the way toward 0.5950, while failure to hold current levels may see a retreat toward 0.5870, keeping short-term momentum in focus.
