US equities rebounded on Tuesday, with the Dow Jones Industrial Average climbing back above the 49,000 mark as oil prices eased from recent highs. Investors appeared encouraged by a stream of better-than-expected first-quarter earnings, reinforcing the view that corporate profits, rather than policy shifts, are currently driving market momentum. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq also posted modest gains, lifting broader risk sentiment across global markets.  

The easing of crude supported the US dollar, keeping the USD index stable against major counterparts. In forex trading, the EUR/USD pair hovered near 1.1010, while USD/JPY found support around 134.20. Traders noted that equity gains alongside a firm USD suggest continued appetite for risk assets, yet caution remains ahead of upcoming economic data and central bank commentary.  

Market participants are weighing strong corporate performance against potential macro headwinds. Positive earnings helped lift cyclical sectors, which in turn supported related commodity-linked currencies like the AUD and CAD. Technical levels remain critical: a sustained Dow above 49,000 could encourage further buying, while EUR/USD resistance near 1.1050 may cap upside in forex markets. The interplay between equities, crude, and currency pairs is likely to remain central to trading decisions in the near term.
