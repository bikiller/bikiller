Gold (XAU/USD) is trading just above the $4,000 psychological level, struggling to regain bullish momentum after sliding to fresh year-to-date (YTD) lows at $3,941 earlier in the session. The currency pair remains under pressure as traders reassess demand for safe-haven assets, with the exchange rate hovering near a critical technical zone that continues to attract attention from short-term participants.

The weakness in the precious metal reflects shifting trading sentiment across global markets, where USD strength and evolving expectations around interest rates are influencing flows. In forex markets, the interplay between the U.S. dollar and gold remains central, with XAU/USD reacting sharply to changes in risk appetite and broader macroeconomic positioning. Sellers have maintained control below recent resistance levels.

From a technical perspective, the $4,000 mark now acts as a key psychological support, while the $3,941 YTD low stands as the immediate downside reference. A sustained break below this region could expose further losses, while recovery attempts will likely depend on renewed demand and a softer USD environment in the broader currency pair landscape.
