The GBP/USD currency pair opened the new week with a bearish gap, retreating after a five-day winning streak. The pair, which had reached its highest point since late February at 1.3485, struggled to maintain momentum as it failed to hold above 1.3400. The bearish trend came amid a stronger USD, which capped any potential upside movement in the exchange rate.

As the dollar strengthened, the GBP/USD saw limited follow-through beyond the 1.3400 level. Traders are closely monitoring the pair, with the 1.3485 high now acting as a key resistance point. The lack of buying interest at higher levels reflects growing caution in the market, particularly as broader dollar strength remains a dominant factor in determining the pair’s movement in the short term.
