Gold (XAU/USD) trades range-bound on Wednesday, giving back earlier gains after a brief rebound from a weekly low. The precious metal remains sensitive to USD dynamics and broader risk sentiment in the currency pair space, with traders reluctant to commit amid shifting geopolitical headlines. Price action suggests consolidation rather than a clear directional breakout in forex trading.

Market sentiment is being driven by ongoing US-Iran tensions, even after US President Donald Trump extended a ceasefire agreement just hours before its expiration. The development has reduced immediate tail-risk fears but not fully restored confidence, keeping volatility contained. In FX markets, USD positioning remains cautious as investors reassess safe-haven flows and geopolitical risk premiums.

Looking ahead, gold’s exchange rate outlook against the USD suggests continued range-bound trading unless fresh catalysts emerge. Traders are closely monitoring geopolitical headlines and US dollar strength for direction, while EUR and other major currency pairs reflect similar caution in broader forex markets. Until clarity improves, XAU/USD may remain locked in consolidation.
