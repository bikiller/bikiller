The UK market eyes a busy economic day on Thursday, with the Office for National Statistics (ONS) scheduled to release the monthly GDP figures, Trade Balance, and Industrial Production at 06:00 GMT. Traders will scrutinize the data for signs of momentum in the economy, as stronger-than-expected GDP growth could lift the GBP/USD exchange rate, while weaker figures may trigger a shift toward the USD and safe-haven flows.  

Industrial Production and the Trade Balance will add nuance, influencing short-term trading in the currency pair. A widening trade deficit or contraction in industrial output could weigh on the pound, prompting technical traders to watch key support near 1.2600 and resistance around 1.2750. Conversely, a positive surprise could see GBP regain traction against the dollar and even affect cross-rates with the EUR.  

Market participants are likely to react quickly, with GBP/USD volatility expected in the early London session. Momentum traders may capitalize on breakout moves, while institutional flows could reinforce trends depending on the relative strength of the data. Overall, Thursday’s releases are poised to shape trading dynamics, offering insight into the pound’s resilience and the broader market sentiment versus USD and EUR positions.
