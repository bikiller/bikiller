The Australian Dollar weakened against its major peers during Wednesday’s European session, with the AUD/USD currency pair falling 0.25% to trade near 0.7010. The move reflects growing market conviction that the Reserve Bank of Australia’s next policy adjustment will be an interest-rate cut rather than a hike, weighing on demand for the Australian currency and pressuring its exchange rate against the USD.

Investor sentiment toward the AUD has softened as expectations of easier monetary policy continue to build. Traders increasingly believe that slowing economic momentum and easing inflation pressures could give the RBA room to lower borrowing costs in the coming months. As a result, the Australian Dollar has underperformed across the forex market, while the US Dollar has remained relatively supported.

The shift in interest-rate expectations is a key driver for currency pair movements, as lower rates generally reduce the appeal of a currency to international investors. Market participants will closely monitor upcoming Australian economic data and RBA commentary for confirmation of the policy outlook. Any further signs supporting a rate cut could keep the AUD under pressure against the USD and other major currencies, while influencing broader trading conditions across forex markets, including key crosses against the EUR.
