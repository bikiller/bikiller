China’s economy kicked off 2026 on a stronger-than-expected note, with robust gains in Industrial Production and Exports. Fixed-Asset Investment also rebounded, bolstered by quasi-fiscal policy measures, signaling resilience amid external headwinds. Analysts at TD Securities highlight that these developments have supported the yuan, keeping the USD/CNY pair under pressure as traders reassess China’s growth trajectory.  

However, risks are mounting from elevated oil prices and escalating tensions with the United States. Energy costs are feeding into inflationary pressures, while geopolitical frictions weigh on investor sentiment. EUR/CNY and USD/CNY exchange rates have shown increased volatility in early trading, with the market closely monitoring policy responses and potential interventions to stabilize capital flows.  

For forex traders, these dynamics underline a cautious approach to China-linked positions. Strong domestic activity may continue to support the yuan, but external shocks could trigger sudden swings. Market participants are eyeing technical levels, with USD/CNY near 6.95 and EUR/CNY hovering around 7.50, as indicators of sentiment shifts and potential trading opportunities in the coming weeks.
