Gold (XAU/USD) held steady above the $4,500 mark during Thursday’s Asian session, maintaining its sideways consolidation after the overnight retreat from the 100-day Simple Moving Average (SMA). The precious metal’s exchange rate has struggled to gain momentum, reflecting cautious trading sentiment as investors weigh the impact of ongoing hawkish expectations from the Federal Reserve.  

Market participants remain attentive to USD strength, which has acted as a headwind for gold. The currency pair’s performance continues to influence risk appetite, with traders monitoring how shifts in U.S. interest rate expectations could steer gold and broader commodity-linked currencies. Technical levels, including the $4,500 psychological threshold and the 100-day SMA, remain key markers for potential short-term reversals or continuation.  

Analysts suggest that while gold’s consolidation signals a temporary pause, volatility may pick up if the Fed reinforces its hawkish stance or if USD movements intensify. Exchange rate fluctuations against major currencies like EUR/USD could further shape trading strategies, with investors adjusting positions to hedge against USD-driven pressures. Overall, gold’s flat trading above $4,500 highlights the delicate balance between technical support and macroeconomic drivers.
