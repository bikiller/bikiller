The USD/CAD currency pair climbed back above the 1.3700 mark on Friday as the Canadian Dollar faced broad-based weakness. Canada’s latest employment data came in below expectations, underscoring a slowdown in the labor market and raising concerns about domestic economic growth. Traders quickly reacted, pushing the exchange rate higher as the Loonie struggled to find support against its US counterpart.  

Firm demand for the US Dollar added further momentum to the rally. Heightened geopolitical tensions surrounding the ongoing US-Iran conflict boosted safe-haven flows, strengthening the USD across major pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD. The combined effect of disappointing Canadian data and persistent USD strength kept USD/CAD trading near key resistance levels, prompting traders to reassess short-term positions in North American currencies.  

Technically, the pair’s rebound above 1.3700 opens the door for further upside, with 1.3750 and 1.3800 representing near-term targets if momentum continues. Market participants remain cautious, balancing Loonie vulnerabilities with potential shifts in US economic signals. The forex market is likely to see continued volatility as traders monitor employment trends, central bank commentary, and global geopolitical developments that could sway USD demand.
