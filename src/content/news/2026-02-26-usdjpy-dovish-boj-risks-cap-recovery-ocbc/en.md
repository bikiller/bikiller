The USD/JPY exchange rate faces resistance as the dovish stance from new Bank of Japan (BoJ) nominees raises concerns over delayed policy normalization. Despite Japan's currency being fundamentally undervalued, the ongoing cautious approach from the BoJ is putting downward pressure on the yen. Analysts from OCBC, including Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, suggest that this dynamic may limit any significant recovery in the pair.

As a result, the yen continues to struggle against the US dollar, which benefits from a more hawkish Federal Reserve. The persistent dovish outlook for Japan could prevent substantial upside in USD/JPY, especially as traders monitor the broader global economic conditions and any potential shifts in monetary policy from the BoJ.
