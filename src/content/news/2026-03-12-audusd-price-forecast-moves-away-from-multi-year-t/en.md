The AUD/USD currency pair reversed course on Thursday, retreating to 0.7125 after hitting a multi-year peak near 0.7185 during Wednesday’s Asian session. This move ended a four-day winning streak, as traders reacted to a firmer USD and shifting market sentiment. The exchange rate’s pullback suggests profit-taking and heightened caution after the pair reached levels not seen since June 2022.  

Trading activity shows the AUD under pressure against the greenback, reflecting broader strength in the USD amid robust U.S. economic data. The pair’s recent rally had been fueled by risk-on sentiment and a weaker USD, but the pullback highlights ongoing volatility and the influence of global macro factors on currency markets. Market participants are closely watching technical support near 0.7120–0.7130 for potential stabilization.  

Looking ahead, the AUD/USD may remain sensitive to developments in U.S. monetary policy and risk appetite across global markets. A sustained rebound above 0.7180 could renew bullish momentum, while a breach below 0.7120 might open the door for further declines. Traders are also comparing movements against other major pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD to gauge broader USD trends and the implications for cross-currency strategies.
