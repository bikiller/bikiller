The USD edged lower as global market sentiment improved, with easing geopolitical tensions and a rebound in technology stocks encouraging investors to take on more risk. According to OCBC’s Sim Moh Siong, the shift in risk appetite reduced demand for the US Dollar as a defensive asset, leading to modest weakness in the currency during trading. The move was driven primarily by changing market positioning rather than a major shift in underlying economic fundamentals.

Even as the USD softened, OCBC noted that the downside may remain limited, with scope for modest upside as market risks continue to recalibrate. Currency pair performance is therefore likely to remain sensitive to shifts in investor sentiment, particularly as traders assess the balance between improving risk appetite and lingering macroeconomic uncertainties. The exchange rate outlook for major pairs such as EUR/USD and other USD crosses will continue to depend on incoming economic data and broader market developments.

For forex participants, the latest price action highlights how quickly trading conditions can change as risk sentiment evolves. While stronger appetite for risk has weighed on the USD in the near term, any renewed uncertainty could revive demand for the US Dollar and support a modest recovery. Investors are expected to monitor geopolitical developments, equity market performance, and macroeconomic catalysts closely as they evaluate the next direction for major currency pair movements and exchange rate trends.
