The US Dollar traded in a narrow range on Wednesday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering near the 99.00 mark as investors weighed conflicting developments surrounding tensions involving Iran. The index remained stuck around the midpoint of its weekly trading band, reflecting cautious sentiment across the broader forex market. Traders largely refrained from taking aggressive positions as uncertainty over geopolitical risks continued to cloud short-term direction for the USD and major currency pairs.

Market participants kept a close eye on safe-haven flows, which helped stabilize the Greenback despite the lack of a clear bullish catalyst. The EUR/USD exchange rate and other major forex pairs saw subdued movement, while Treasury yields and risk appetite remained mixed throughout the session. Analysts noted that the Dollar’s sideways trading pattern signals hesitation among investors waiting for clearer geopolitical guidance before committing to fresh USD exposure.

The muted price action around the 99.00 technical level suggests the DXY could remain range-bound in the near term unless fresh headlines trigger volatility. Currency traders are also monitoring upcoming economic data and central bank expectations for clues on future exchange rate direction. Until stronger catalysts emerge, forex markets are likely to see cautious trading conditions, with the USD maintaining a defensive but stable tone against its major peers.
