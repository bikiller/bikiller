The EUR/USD currency pair remained broadly stable near 1.1640 during Wednesday’s early Asian trading session, with the exchange rate hovering close to the 1.1650 mark as traders weighed conflicting macro drivers. While expectations of a more hawkish stance from the European Central Bank continued to support the EUR, renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East limited bullish momentum against the USD.

Market sentiment turned cautious after Iran threatened retaliation following recent US strikes targeting launch sites and boats, prompting investors to seek safety in the US Dollar. The geopolitical uncertainty dampened risk appetite across forex markets, keeping the EUR/USD pair trapped within a narrow trading range despite underlying support from ECB rate outlook expectations and resilient Eurozone policy signals.

Currency traders are now closely monitoring whether the EUR/USD pair can sustain gains above the 1.1650 technical area, as broader market direction remains tied to geopolitical developments and central bank expectations. A prolonged escalation in Iran–US tensions could strengthen demand for the USD as a safe-haven currency, potentially capping further upside in the euro exchange rate over the near term.
