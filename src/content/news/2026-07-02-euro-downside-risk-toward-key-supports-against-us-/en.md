EUR/USD traded in a choppy range between 1.1361 and 1.1423 before closing softer at 1.1376 in recent trading, reflecting renewed pressure on the euro against the US dollar. The currency pair struggled to sustain intraday gains as forex market sentiment tilted slightly in favor of the USD, keeping the exchange rate under mild downside pressure heading into the close.

According to United Overseas Bank analyst Quek Leang, short-term momentum in EUR/USD is turning lower, suggesting continued downside risk in the near term. The pair may retest the 1.1360 level amid ongoing selling pressure, although broader technical support at 1.1335 is expected to remain intact and limit deeper losses in the currency pair.

Market participants are closely watching whether sustained US dollar strength will keep EUR/USD under pressure, with trading conditions likely to remain range-bound but skewed to the downside. A break below immediate supports could accelerate selling, while failure to breach 1.1360 may keep the currency pair consolidating within a narrow forex trading range.
