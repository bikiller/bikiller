The USD/CAD currency pair gained traction on Wednesday, with traders stepping in on dips as the exchange rate climbed toward 1.3765-1.3770 during the European session. This move brings the pair closer to its highest levels since April 14, reflecting sustained bullish interest and positioning ahead of the U.S. Federal Reserve’s FOMC minutes. Market participants are closely watching technical levels for signs of further momentum.  

Price action shows the pair testing resistance near the 200-day EMA, a key technical barrier around 1.3770. A breakout above this level could attract additional USD buying against the CAD, reinforcing upward trading trends. Conversely, failure to breach the EMA may encourage profit-taking and short-term retracements, keeping traders cautious in the lead-up to high-impact economic data.  

Broader market conditions are also influencing USD/CAD trading. Strength in the USD against other majors like the EUR, combined with muted risk appetite in global markets, supports the pair’s bullish bias. Investors are digesting both macroeconomic cues and central bank signals, using technical indicators and support levels to guide positioning. Overall, the pair remains poised near critical resistance, with momentum likely to hinge on the upcoming FOMC insights.
