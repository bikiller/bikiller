The Australian Dollar (AUD) fell against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, trading at 0.7083, down 0.37%. The decline came after geopolitical tensions in the Middle East sparked a flight to safety, pushing investors towards the USD. The AUD/USD currency pair saw downward pressure as risk-off sentiment took hold, reflecting broader market concerns over the situation in the region.

In addition to the geopolitical concerns, strong US economic data added support to the Greenback. The firm data raised expectations of further tightening by the Federal Reserve, further boosting the USD's strength. As the market digests these developments, the AUD remains under pressure, with the exchange rate hovering near the 0.7080 level.
