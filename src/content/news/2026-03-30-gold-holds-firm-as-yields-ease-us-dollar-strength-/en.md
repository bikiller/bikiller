Gold (XAU/USD) remained largely steady on Monday, retracing some of its earlier gains as traders navigated a mix of geopolitical tensions and evolving interest rate expectations. The precious metal found support from escalating unrest in the Middle East, which typically fuels safe-haven demand, yet its upside was limited by the continued strength of the US Dollar. Market participants noted that the exchange rate hovered near key technical levels, keeping trading cautious.  

The USD index maintained firm footing, exerting downward pressure on gold and other commodities priced in dollars. Investors weighed the potential impact of upcoming Federal Reserve commentary on rate policy, which added volatility to currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Analysts highlighted that while lower yields supported bullion, the persistent demand for USD-denominated assets restrained significant upward momentum.  

Traders are watching XAU/USD for signs of a decisive breakout, as the tug-of-war between geopolitical risk and currency strength continues. Currency markets reflected this balance, with EUR/USD trading in a narrow range amid uncertainty. With global liquidity conditions sensitive to both rate expectations and geopolitical developments, gold’s movements will remain closely linked to shifts in major currency pairs and Treasury yields, influencing trading strategies across forex markets.
