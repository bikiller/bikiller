Societe Generale’s latest report highlights China’s vulnerability amid rising U.S.–Iran tensions. Despite Iran being a key oil supplier to China, disruptions in oil supply are considered manageable, allowing China to mitigate risks. The market has reacted, with oil prices showing volatility, influencing USD/CNY and EUR/USD pairs. Traders are keeping a close eye on geopolitical developments, adjusting positions based on expected market shifts.

The U.S. Dollar remains the focal point in global markets, fluctuating in response to both oil price trends and geopolitical uncertainty. For the EUR/USD pair, recent resistance near the 1.1000 mark has been challenged as volatility intensifies. Societe Generale suggests that while oil supply disruptions could cause short-term spikes, China’s strategic reserves and alternative sources may help stabilize its energy needs, keeping the yuan relatively insulated from long-term effects.
