Colombia’s economic outlook is benefiting from higher oil prices, which are providing a boost to growth and fiscal resilience. Societe Generale strategist Dev Ashish notes that this recovery is expected to return Colombia’s GDP closer to potential levels by 2026–27. Inflation expectations are gradually stabilizing, although the pass-through from elevated oil costs could create temporary upside pressures, influencing both local market sentiment and monetary policy expectations.  

The tighter stance from BanRep has added support to the Colombian peso, with traders closely watching the USD/COP exchange rate. Strong oil revenues are underpinning the currency, and the peso’s performance against the USD and EUR is likely to attract speculative flows as the market weighs domestic inflation trends against external developments. Short-term swings could emerge from oil price volatility, with technical levels around key support and resistance points guiding intraday trading.  

For forex participants, the interplay between higher commodity prices and BanRep’s cautious tightening signals a more favorable backdrop for COP trading. The currency’s correlation with oil prices remains pronounced, and a resilient growth outlook may entice carry trade strategies. EUR/COP and USD/COP positions are expected to see heightened activity, especially as investors adjust to both global market conditions and Colombia’s evolving macro trajectory.
