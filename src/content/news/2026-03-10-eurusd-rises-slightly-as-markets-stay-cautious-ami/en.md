The EUR/USD currency pair edged higher on Tuesday, continuing a modest rebound as traders weighed ongoing geopolitical tensions. The Euro (EUR) extended gains for a third consecutive session, climbing to around 1.1645, recovering from Monday’s near four-month low of 1.1507. Market participants cited a softer US Dollar (USD) as a key factor supporting the exchange rate, while caution persisted amid global uncertainties.  

Trading activity remained measured, with investors balancing risk amid the US-Iran conflict. The softer USD helped relieve some downward pressure on the EUR/USD pair, allowing the Euro to stabilize after its recent sharp decline. Analysts noted that technical support around the 1.1500 level provided a floor for the rebound, while momentum indicators suggested that the pair could face resistance near 1.1650-1.1700.  

Market implications suggest cautious optimism for the Euro, although volatility is likely to persist. Traders are closely monitoring geopolitical developments and USD movements, which could dictate short-term swings in the currency pair. A continued retreat in the USD may further support the EUR/USD exchange rate, while renewed risk-off sentiment could cap gains and keep trading ranges tight in the near term.
