The British Pound (GBP) plunged on Monday, reaching a four-month low against the US Dollar (USD). The GBP/USD currency pair dropped by over 0.50%, trading at 1.3184. The spike in Middle East tensions prompted investors to flock to the safe-haven USD, driving its strength across the markets. 

Meanwhile, oil prices continued to rise for the fourth consecutive day, adding pressure to the GBP. The ongoing volatility in the region is likely to keep the USD in demand, overshadowing any potential recovery for the Pound in the near term.
