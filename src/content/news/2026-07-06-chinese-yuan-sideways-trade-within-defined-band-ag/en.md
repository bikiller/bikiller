The USD/CNH currency pair continued to trade within a well-defined range as the Chinese Yuan showed limited movement against the US Dollar, reflecting subdued market momentum. According to United Overseas Bank (UOB) analyst Quek Ser Leang, the recent strength in USD has eased slightly, leaving the exchange rate confined to a narrow intraday band between 6.7800 and 6.7930. The muted price action points to balanced trading conditions and a lack of strong directional catalysts in the foreign exchange market.

Although momentum has softened, UOB does not expect a significant shift in the near term. The bank noted that the earlier upward momentum in the US Dollar has faded, reducing the likelihood of an immediate breakout for the USD/CNH currency pair. Instead, traders are likely to continue monitoring technical levels and macroeconomic developments for fresh signals that could influence exchange rate direction and broader forex market sentiment.

Looking ahead over the next one to three weeks, UOB expects USD/CNH to remain in a broader trading range between 6.7750 and 6.8080. This outlook suggests continued sideways trading rather than a sustained bullish or bearish trend. For forex participants, the defined support and resistance levels may offer useful reference points for short-term trading strategies while highlighting the market's current preference for consolidation over decisive directional moves.
