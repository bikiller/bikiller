The Mexican Peso advanced against the US Dollar on Thursday, rising more than 0.62% after Banco de México (Banxico) delivered a unanimous decision to keep its benchmark interest rate unchanged at 6.50%. The hawkish hold reinforced expectations that monetary policy will remain restrictive, supporting the currency in broader forex trading.

USD/MXN traded at 17.49 following the decision, pulling back from a one-and-a-half-month peak near 17.67. The exchange rate reflects renewed peso strength as traders adjusted positions in the currency pair, with the US Dollar losing short-term momentum against emerging market currencies in the forex market during late trading sessions on Thursday.

Looking ahead, the peso’s resilience may depend on future Banxico signals and US Federal Reserve policy expectations. If interest rate divergence persists, USD/MXN could remain pressured below recent highs, while EUR and other major currencies also respond to shifting global yield differentials and risk sentiment across forex markets globally.
