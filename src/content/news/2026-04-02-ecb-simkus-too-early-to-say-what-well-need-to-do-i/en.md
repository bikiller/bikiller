European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member and Lithuanian central bank Governor Gediminas Simkus signaled caution on Thursday, emphasizing that it is too early to determine what policy measures might be needed in April. Speaking during European trading hours, Simkus noted that shifting economic conditions require a careful assessment of interest rates. His comments weighed on the euro, with the EUR/USD currency pair trading slightly lower amid renewed uncertainty over the ECB’s next steps.  

Investors monitored the EUR/USD exchange rate closely, which edged toward the 1.0900 support level following Simkus’ remarks. Market participants interpreted his statement as a signal that the ECB may pause or adjust its pace of tightening, contrasting with earlier hawkish expectations. Trading volumes in major forex pairs, including EUR/GBP and EUR/JPY, reflected cautious positioning, as traders weighed the impact of slower policy action against ongoing inflationary pressures.  

Analysts suggest that the euro’s performance will hinge on upcoming data releases and ECB commentary. A more dovish outlook could limit EUR gains against the USD, while any stronger-than-expected signals on rate hikes may boost the euro. For forex traders, technical levels near 1.0950 and 1.1000 in EUR/USD remain key, as market sentiment continues to fluctuate in response to policy uncertainty.
