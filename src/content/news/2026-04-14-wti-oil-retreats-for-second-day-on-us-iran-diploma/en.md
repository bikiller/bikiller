WTI crude oil fell for the second consecutive day, with prices slipping to $89.10 per barrel, down 3.93% on Tuesday. The decline is driven by optimism surrounding US-Iran diplomatic efforts, which could ease supply concerns. While the potential for a breakthrough in talks offers some relief to the market, risks in the Strait of Hormuz continue to cap any significant downside.

The market remains sensitive to developments in US-Iran relations, but geopolitical tensions, particularly in the Middle East, are likely to keep oil prices volatile. Despite the positive diplomatic signals, traders are cautious, aware that any escalation could quickly reverse recent gains. The USD remains the dominant factor in oil trading, with fluctuations in the US dollar exchange rate also influencing oil’s appeal against other currencies.
