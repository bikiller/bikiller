Brent crude retreated below the $100 mark on growing optimism over a potential US–Iran nuclear agreement, signaling a possible easing of global energy supply concerns. Analysts at Deutsche Bank highlighted that the futures curve now points to a reduced risk of a stagflationary shock, suggesting traders are recalibrating positions across oil-linked assets and related currencies. The pullback has supported modest strength in the USD, as investors weigh lower inflation pressures.  

In currency markets, the euro-dollar pair has responded to shifting sentiment, with EUR/USD testing recent support levels near 1.0900 amid dollar resilience. Commodity-linked currencies, particularly the Canadian dollar and Norwegian krone, saw subdued trading after oil’s retreat, reflecting sensitivity to Brent’s pricing dynamics. Market participants are closely watching exchange rates for hints of broader risk appetite shifts, with safe-haven flows favoring the greenback in short-term trades.  

The easing in crude prices may influence central bank expectations, impacting interest rate differentials that drive forex trading. Short-term technical levels around $99.50–$100.50 are under scrutiny, and traders are adjusting hedges in energy-sensitive portfolios. Deutsche Bank emphasizes that Brent’s movement below the psychological $100 threshold could dampen volatility across commodity-linked currency pairs, while reinforcing USD liquidity amid a recalibration of global market risk.
