Gold (XAU/USD) clings to modest intraday gains near the $4,200 mark heading into the European session on Monday, attempting to recover after snapping a three-day losing streak. The metal rebounded from a more than one-week low touched on Friday, with price action stabilizing as traders reassess near-term momentum in the currency pair against the USD.

Crude Oil prices have turned lower after a modest bullish weekly gap earlier in the session, easing inflation worries and indirectly supporting Gold by softening expectations for sustained USD strength. Softer energy prices reduce inflation pressure, influencing interest rate outlooks and shaping broader forex trading sentiment across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD.

Overall, XAU/USD trading remains sensitive to shifts in inflation expectations and risk sentiment, with gold holding above the $4,200 level as traders watch USD direction and bond yields. The easing in oil-driven inflation pressures may limit dollar upside, keeping precious metals supported in near-term forex market conditions, with spillover effects into EUR/USD volatility and broader FX flows.
