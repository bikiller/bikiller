Euro declines during Tuesday’s North American session as heightened geopolitical risk in the Middle East supports the US Dollar. Exchange of fire between US and Iranian forces near the Strait of Hormuz has unsettled risk sentiment, prompting traders to rotate into safer assets. The currency pair remains under pressure amid fragile market conditions, with appetite for risk assets fading across equities and commodities.

EUR/USD trades at 1.1622, down 0.15%, reflecting steady USD demand across forex markets. The exchange rate weakness highlights cautious trading as investors weigh ongoing diplomatic negotiations against escalating tensions. Dollar strength is reinforced by safe-haven flows, while the euro struggles to maintain momentum in a risk-off environment. Traders remain hesitant to build long euro positions ahead of further geopolitical clarity.

Market participants remain focused on developments around the Strait of Hormuz, with volatility expected to persist if tensions escalate further. The currency pair outlook hinges on geopolitical headlines and broader USD sentiment, leaving traders cautious about directional conviction in the near term. Liquidity conditions may tighten further if risk-off flows into the dollar accelerate across global markets.
