Renewed optimism over a potential peace deal with Iran, coupled with a sharp slide in Brent crude oil prices, is weighing on the US Dollar (USD) across major currency pairs. Analysts at MUFG highlight that the greenback is under pressure as risk sentiment improves, bolstered by gains in global equities and AI-related sectors. The USD’s exchange rate against the euro and yen has softened, reflecting investor appetite for higher-yielding, riskier assets.  

Traders note that the EUR/USD pair has edged higher, testing recent resistance levels, while USD/JPY is trading lower amid broader risk-on flows. Brent crude’s retreat is prompting commodity-linked currencies such as the CAD to underperform, reinforcing the dollar’s selective weakness. Market participants are watching technical thresholds closely, with key support and resistance levels guiding intraday trading strategies.  

The combination of geopolitical optimism and falling energy prices is reshaping short-term currency dynamics. While the USD remains central to global liquidity, its near-term outlook may stay subdued if oil continues to slide and equities maintain momentum. Investors are balancing safe-haven hedges with exposure to growth-oriented currencies, making trading decisions sensitive to both macro developments and technical signals.
