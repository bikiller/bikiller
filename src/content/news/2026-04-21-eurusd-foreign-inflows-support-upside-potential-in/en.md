EUR/USD currency pair is drawing attention after ING analyst Chris Turner pointed to robust foreign demand for Eurozone assets. Investors purchased about EUR280bn in equities and debt in the first two months of the year, signalling steady capital inflows. This trend is seen as offering marginal support to the euro, helping stabilize trading against the US dollar.

Foreign inflows into Eurozone bonds and equities are reinforcing bullish sentiment around the EUR/USD exchange rate. Market participants view the EUR280bn allocation as a signal of resilient appetite for euro-denominated assets, which can limit downside pressure on the euro versus the US dollar. In trading sessions, this dynamic helps the single currency maintain a firmer tone.

ING’s view suggests the impact remains marginal rather than transformative, though sustained foreign demand could continue to underpin the euro in broader FX trading. For the EUR/USD pair, the balance between US dollar strength and euro inflows will remain key, with investors closely monitoring capital flow data for further direction.
