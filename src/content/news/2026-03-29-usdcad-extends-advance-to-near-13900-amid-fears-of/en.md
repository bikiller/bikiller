The USD/CAD currency pair extended its upward momentum for a sixth consecutive trading day on Monday, climbing toward the 1.3900 level in Asian markets. This marks the highest exchange rate for the pair in over two months, reflecting sustained demand for the US dollar amid escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Traders are closely monitoring the situation, which is fueling safe-haven flows and driving USD strength against major currencies, including the Canadian dollar.  

Market participants note that the surge comes as investors reassess risk amid potential disruptions to oil supply and regional stability. The Canadian dollar, often sensitive to energy price swings, has lagged as crude markets remain jittery. Analysts suggest that the near-term trajectory of USD/CAD could hinge on both geopolitical developments and US economic data, with 1.3900 serving as a key technical level to watch.  

In broader trading, the US dollar retains its defensive appeal, influencing not only USD/CAD but also other major pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY. Momentum in the currency markets highlights the interplay between geopolitical risk and currency valuations, prompting traders to weigh potential carry trade adjustments and hedging strategies. With six days of gains, USD/CAD’s advance underscores the resilience of the greenback amid global uncertainty.
