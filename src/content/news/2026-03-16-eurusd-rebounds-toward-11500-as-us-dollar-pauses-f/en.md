The EUR/USD currency pair saw a rebound on Monday as the US Dollar paused after its recent rally, giving the Euro room to recover. The pair bounced from the seven-month lows it reached on Friday, moving closer to the 1.1500 mark. Traders are eyeing upcoming decisions from the Federal Reserve (Fed) and European Central Bank (ECB), which could provide further direction.

The Euro’s recovery came amid a broader softening of the USD, as investors reassessed expectations for interest rate hikes. With the Fed's stance on monetary policy remaining in focus, the EUR/USD exchange rate is expected to remain volatile, with market participants anticipating how both central banks will navigate economic challenges. The pair's movement near the 1.1500 level suggests a critical resistance point, making upcoming events crucial for its next move.
