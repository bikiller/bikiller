The US dollar held steady against major currencies on Wednesday as traders awaited the release of key US inflation data later in the day. The EUR/USD pair traded around 1.0970, hovering near a two-week low, while USD/JPY edged slightly higher to 143.15 amid safe-haven demand for the greenback. Analysts note that any surprises in the Consumer Price Index could trigger sharp moves across forex markets.  

The Canadian dollar came under pressure ahead of the Bank of Canada’s interest rate decision. USD/CAD climbed to 1.3625, testing resistance near the 1.3630 level, as markets debated whether the central bank will hold rates steady or signal further tightening. Investors are closely watching BoC commentary for clues on the outlook for inflation and growth, which could influence trading in commodity-linked currencies.  

Elsewhere, the British pound remained fragile, with GBP/USD trading near 1.2560 as market participants weighed economic data and global risk sentiment. Technical levels continue to guide short-term moves, with EUR/JPY bouncing slightly off 157.20 support. Overall, forex markets remain cautious, with traders positioning ahead of high-impact US and Canadian releases that could reshape exchange rate trends in the coming days.
