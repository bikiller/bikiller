The USD/CAD currency pair extended its gains for the third straight day on Monday, trading just below the mid-1.3900s in the Asian session. The pair remains close to a nearly four-month high reached last week as a firmer US Dollar supports the exchange rate. Traders are eyeing technical resistance levels while monitoring the interplay between North American economic data and global risk sentiment.  

The Canadian Dollar continues to underperform against the USD, pressured by persistent strength in the greenback. However, bullish crude oil prices are capping further losses, providing some support to the loonie. Market participants note that oil-related sectors and commodity-linked currencies, including CAD, often react to shifts in energy prices, which are currently bolstering trading activity despite broader USD strength.  

Analysts suggest that the USD/CAD may remain sensitive to US economic indicators and Fed policy signals in the coming sessions. Meanwhile, traders are also watching correlations with other major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD to gauge broader dollar momentum. The near-term outlook points to continued volatility, with the pair likely to oscillate around the mid-1.3900s unless a decisive breakout occurs.
