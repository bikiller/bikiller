Gold prices briefly surged past USD 5,400 per troy ounce on Thursday, as it outperformed the Dollar and US Treasuries. The rally was driven by a flight to safety, amid market uncertainty. However, the precious metal retraced its gains, settling back to levels seen on Friday. This pullback reflects the shifting dynamics in the forex market, where a repricing of interest rates is impacting demand for safe-haven assets.

The USD's strength, driven by hawkish signals from the Federal Reserve, played a pivotal role in the decline of gold. As the exchange rate between USD and major currencies like EUR and JPY adjusts in response to the rate outlook, gold's allure as a hedge against inflation diminishes. Traders are closely monitoring this interplay between global rates and commodity prices, as it continues to shape market sentiment in the forex and commodities space.
