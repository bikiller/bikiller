Gold prices rallied on Monday as renewed optimism surrounding a potential Hormuz-related agreement weighed heavily on the US Dollar, driving fresh demand for the precious metal during subdued Memorial Day trading. The XAU/USD currency pair climbed more than 1.30%, reaching $4,570 after rebounding sharply from intraday lows near $4,519. Despite thin market liquidity, bullish momentum remained intact as investors reduced exposure to the USD amid improving global risk appetite.

The weaker Greenback also came as US equity index futures pushed toward fresh all-time highs, reflecting stronger sentiment across financial markets. Currency trading activity showed broad pressure on the Dollar’s exchange rate against major peers, including the EUR and other safe-haven assets. Lower Treasury yields and speculation over easing geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz further supported gold’s upside, reinforcing demand for alternative stores of value.

Market participants are now closely watching whether XAU/USD can sustain gains above the $4,570 level, which could open the door for another leg higher in the near term. Traders expect volatility to remain elevated once full US market participation resumes, particularly if USD weakness persists across forex markets. Continued softness in the Dollar exchange rate may keep gold supported as investors reassess global risk conditions and central bank expectations.
