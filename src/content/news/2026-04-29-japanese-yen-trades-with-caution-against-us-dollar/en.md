The Japanese Yen (JPY) edged cautiously against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, with the USD/JPY currency pair hovering around 159.65 during the European session. Traders showed limited conviction, keeping the exchange rate in a narrow range as market participants awaited the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy decision scheduled for 18:00 GMT. The Yen’s muted moves reflect the market’s anticipation of potential shifts in interest rate guidance from the Fed.  

In the broader forex landscape, the USD remains broadly supported against major currencies, while risk-sensitive pairs like EUR/JPY and GBP/JPY mirrored the Yen’s subdued trading. Analysts noted that the currency pair’s current technical posture suggests consolidation, with immediate support near 159.50 and resistance around 160.00. Any significant Fed signal could quickly propel the USD/JPY out of this quiet range.  

Investors are closely monitoring global liquidity conditions, as the Fed’s policy statement could trigger volatility across the forex market. Traders are also keeping an eye on safe-haven flows into the Yen, which historically strengthen during periods of uncertainty. A decisive move in the USD/JPY could set the tone for broader market sentiment in the coming sessions, influencing trading strategies across major and cross currency pairs.
