WTI crude oil surged to $89.40 on Wednesday, marking a 2.33% gain as market attention refocused on geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. U.S. President Donald Trump’s warnings to Iran added a risk premium, while the Energy Information Administration reported a deeper-than-expected draw in U.S. crude inventories, fueling buying momentum. Traders noted heightened volatility in the USD, with the DXY index reacting to risk-on sentiment.  

The energy-driven rally rippled across currency markets, with commodity-linked pairs like USD/CAD and AUD/USD showing increased trading activity. The stronger oil price supported the CAD against the USD, while safe-haven flows briefly boosted the JPY. EUR/USD oscillated near key technical levels, reflecting cautious positioning as traders balanced crude gains against macroeconomic data.  

Analysts suggest that continued Middle East tensions and tighter U.S. stock draws could sustain WTI above $88, influencing both oil and forex markets. For traders, monitoring crude futures alongside USD exchange rates remains critical, as shifts in oil pricing could trigger corresponding moves in commodity-sensitive currencies, impacting overall trading strategies in the near term.
