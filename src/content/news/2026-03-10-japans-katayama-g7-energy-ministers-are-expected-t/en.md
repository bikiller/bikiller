Japan’s Finance Minister Satsuki Katayama confirmed that G7 energy ministers are set to meet virtually on Tuesday to discuss a potential release from strategic oil reserves. The move comes as global markets react to supply concerns following the escalation of conflict in Iran, which has driven Brent crude prices above $95 per barrel. Traders are closely watching how this could influence USD-based commodity flows and broader risk sentiment across currency pairs.  

In forex markets, the USD/JPY pair has seen increased volatility, with the yen weakening as investors weigh the implications of higher energy costs on Japan’s import-driven economy. EUR/USD has also shown swings as traders reassess Eurozone inflation expectations, reflecting the potential knock-on effects of elevated oil prices on central bank policy outlooks. Technical levels around 1.0900 in EUR/USD and 145.50 in USD/JPY are under scrutiny, with breakouts likely to trigger short-term trading adjustments.  

Analysts suggest that any coordinated release of oil reserves could temporarily ease supply pressures, providing relief to commodity-linked currencies like the CAD and NOK, while supporting risk sentiment in equities. Forex traders are monitoring both energy market cues and G7 communications, as expectations for intervention could drive sharp intraday moves across major pairs. Market positioning ahead of the G7 meeting highlights the sensitivity of currency exchange rates to geopolitical tensions and energy market developments.
