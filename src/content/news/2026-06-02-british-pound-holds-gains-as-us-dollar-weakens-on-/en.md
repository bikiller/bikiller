The GBP/USD currency pair is holding firm in European trading on Tuesday, recovering after slight losses in the previous session and hovering near 1.3470. The exchange rate is benefiting from a softer US Dollar as easing risk aversion supports demand for higher-beta currencies. The move reflects a steadier market tone after news of a partial ceasefire between Hezbollah and Israel reduced safe-haven flows into the USD.

With the dollar losing traction, sterling is finding room to extend its recent advance against major peers, including the EUR and other G10 currencies. The near-term outlook for GBP/USD remains tied to risk sentiment and dollar demand, while traders watch whether the pair can build on current gains and hold above the 1.3470 area. A sustained break higher could encourage further upside in the currency pair.
