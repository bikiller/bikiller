The USD/JPY pair continues its upward momentum for a second consecutive day, rising to the mid-159.00s during the Asian session on Monday. This marks a sustained push beyond the recent peak of 159.50, a level not seen since July 2024. The pair's strength is driven by continued demand for the USD, supported by expectations of higher U.S. interest rates.

Despite the bullish sentiment, fears of Japanese government intervention to curb the yen's depreciation remain a key risk. While the USD/JPY exchange rate has surged, the possibility of intervention could limit the extent of further gains. Traders will closely watch for any signs of action from the Bank of Japan as the pair tests technical resistance levels.
