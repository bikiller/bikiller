EUR/JPY has paused its four-day rally, trading around 184.20 during Thursday's European session. The currency pair has seen a pullback, as the Japanese Yen strengthens amid growing market expectations of an imminent rate hike by the Bank of Japan (BoJ). This shift in sentiment has weighed on the Euro, halting its recent gains.

The Yen's appeal has been boosted by speculation that the BoJ may soon raise interest rates, a move that could impact the EUR/JPY exchange rate in the short term. As EUR/JPY hovers near 184.00, traders are closely monitoring any developments regarding the BoJ's policy stance, which could dictate the pair’s next direction.
