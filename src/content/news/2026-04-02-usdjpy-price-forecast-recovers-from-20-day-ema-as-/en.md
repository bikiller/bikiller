The USD/JPY currency pair climbed 0.4% to hover around 159.50 in early European trading on Thursday, rebounding sharply from a brief two-day correction. Traders cited renewed safe-haven demand for the US Dollar, driven by geopolitical tensions, as the United States signaled intentions to respond forcefully to Iran in the coming weeks. This surge helped the pair recover above its 20-day EMA, reinforcing technical support.  

Market participants are closely monitoring the exchange rate, with the USD attracting flows as investors seek stability amid heightened uncertainty. The corrective dip earlier this week had allowed the pair to test short-term support, but the rebound underscores the Dollar’s defensive appeal relative to the Japanese Yen. Traders note that momentum may remain influenced by geopolitical developments and risk sentiment.  

Analysts suggest that sustained safe-haven demand could push USD/JPY higher in the near term, with the 20-day EMA now serving as a crucial floor for trading activity. The combination of technical support and external risk factors positions the currency pair for potential further gains, while market watchers continue to weigh global developments against broader USD and EUR trends.
