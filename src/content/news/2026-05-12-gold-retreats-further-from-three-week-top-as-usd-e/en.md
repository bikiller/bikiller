Gold (XAU/USD) retreated from a three-week high in early Asian trading on Tuesday, easing back toward the $4,700 level. The precious metal's pullback comes as the US dollar shows mild strength, with traders positioning ahead of the US consumer price index (CPI) release. Market participants are closely watching exchange rate movements as USD gains can weigh on non-yielding assets like gold.

Currency pairs across the board saw fluctuations, with the USD edging higher against major rivals including the EUR. Analysts note that gold’s intraday retracement is technically normal after rallying to multi-week highs, while trading volumes remain moderate. The market is factoring in expectations of inflation data that could influence Fed policy signals, which in turn impacts both currency and commodity trading.

Looking ahead, investors are weighing the implications of the upcoming CPI report. A stronger-than-expected inflation reading could further support USD exchange rates and pressure gold lower, while softer data may boost the metal as traders seek a hedge against price pressures. Traders are monitoring technical levels and momentum, keeping a close eye on XAU/USD movements for signs of trend continuation or reversal.
