AUD/USD climbed on Thursday, marking its fourth consecutive day of gains as traders reacted to the US–Iran ceasefire. The easing of geopolitical tensions has kept the US Dollar under pressure, prompting a rebound in risk-sensitive currencies like the Australian Dollar. The currency pair extended its intraday move, testing recent resistance levels as market participants weighed broader dollar weakness against supportive economic data from Australia.  

The exchange rate saw AUD/USD trading higher, reflecting sentiment-driven flows rather than major macro surprises. Investors noted that while the USD struggles amid reduced safe-haven demand, the AUD has benefited from firm commodity prices and regional growth indicators. Technical traders highlighted the pair’s ability to hold above key short-term support, suggesting that momentum could favor continued upside in the near term.  

Market analysts expect the currency pair to remain sensitive to shifts in global risk appetite. Any reversal in the US–Iran situation or shifts in US economic signals could quickly reshape USD trading dynamics. Meanwhile, AUD’s resilience hints at potential broader implications for EUR/USD and other major pairs, as a softer Dollar typically supports cross-asset gains across risk-linked currencies. Traders are watching closely for follow-through above resistance or signs of a pullback to test support zones.
