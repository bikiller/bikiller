WTI crude oil continued its upward trajectory on Tuesday, trading near $95.80 during Asian hours, marking the second consecutive day of gains. Prices are being propelled by rising concerns over Middle East tensions, which threaten the stability of key energy transit routes. The market’s focus on supply security has also bolstered the U.S. dollar, as investors recalibrate positions across major currency pairs.  

Forex traders have noted heightened volatility in the EUR/USD and USD/JPY exchange rates as oil-driven risk sentiment feeds into broader currency markets. Commodities-linked currencies like the Canadian dollar and Norwegian krone are seeing correlated moves, reflecting the direct impact of crude price swings. Analysts suggest that WTI approaching the $96.00 mark could test investor confidence, prompting shifts in short-term trading strategies.  

The supply-driven rally in oil has implications beyond commodities, influencing forex liquidity and USD strength. Market participants are watching how geopolitical risks translate into higher energy costs, potentially affecting inflation expectations and central bank policy outlooks. With traders adjusting exposure to both oil and associated currency pairs, the interplay between commodity markets and forex continues to shape short-term trading dynamics.
