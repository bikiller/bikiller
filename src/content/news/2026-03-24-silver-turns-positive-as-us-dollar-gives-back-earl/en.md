Silver (XAG/USD) rebounded during Tuesday’s European session, reclaiming early losses to trade near $69.70. The white metal’s recovery comes as the US Dollar (USD) relinquished its initial gains, easing pressure on precious metals. Traders noted that the pullback in the USD exchange rate offered support for silver, reinforcing its role as a safe-haven asset amid cautious market sentiment.  

The currency pair’s movements reflected broader forex dynamics, with the USD index retreating against major currencies including the euro (EUR) and yen. Market participants attributed the shift to mixed economic data and lingering uncertainty over interest rate trajectories, prompting investors to reassess risk positions. The price action underscores silver’s sensitivity to currency fluctuations and highlights potential volatility in the near term.  

Technical observers noted that silver’s rebound above $69.50 could open the door to further upside, though resistance near $70 remains a key hurdle. Traders are keeping a close eye on USD exchange rate trends and euro strength, as shifts in the currency pair could influence both intraday trading and medium-term positioning in XAG/USD. Overall, silver’s gains are tentative, reflecting the interplay between forex markets and precious metals demand.
