The USD/CAD currency pair continues to show resilience, with the Canadian Dollar slightly softer as the US Dollar stabilizes in early trading. According to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret, the modest CAD weakness comes amid steady USD demand, yet they emphasize that the longer-term bearish trend in USD/CAD remains intact. Traders are closely watching key technical levels to gauge potential shifts in momentum.  

Market participants note that the exchange rate movements reflect a mix of domestic Canadian data and broader USD strength. While the CAD has shown minor pullbacks against the greenback, the underlying trend is still dominated by USD support. Analysts suggest that short-term fluctuations may offer trading opportunities, but the dominant downtrend for the USD/CAD pair continues to guide strategic positioning in forex markets.  

Looking ahead, Scotiabank highlights that any rally in the USD/CAD is likely to be limited unless new macroeconomic data shifts the outlook. Forex traders are advised to monitor USD movements against other major currencies, including EUR and JPY, for cues that could influence the Canadian Dollar. The persistence of the bear trend signals cautious trading, with the potential for controlled pullbacks rather than sustained reversals.
