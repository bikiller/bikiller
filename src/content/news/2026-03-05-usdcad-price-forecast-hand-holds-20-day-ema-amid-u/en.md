The USD/CAD currency pair trades near 1.3645 during Thursday’s European session, holding close to its 20-day EMA as traders weigh recent market developments. The pair remains largely flat, reflecting a consolidation phase where the US Dollar’s modest rebound after a brief pullback offsets the underlying strength of the Canadian Dollar. Market participants are closely watching technical levels for cues on short-term direction.  

Trading activity shows the Loonie stabilizing against the greenback amid ongoing geopolitical concerns, including tensions related to the US-Iran conflict, which continue to influence risk sentiment. Investors are balancing safe-haven flows into USD with steady demand for CAD, leaving the exchange rate in a narrow range. Analysts suggest that any break above the 20-day EMA could signal a potential upside move, while a dip below may reinforce consolidation.  

With the broader forex market reacting to both technical signals and geopolitical risk, USD/CAD traders are navigating subdued volatility. The interplay between the US Dollar’s recovery and the Canadian Dollar’s resilience indicates a cautious trading environment. Market watchers remain alert for catalysts from economic data or geopolitical headlines that could shift the pair’s trajectory in the coming sessions.
