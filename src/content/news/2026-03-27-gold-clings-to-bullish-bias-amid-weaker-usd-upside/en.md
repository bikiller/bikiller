Gold (XAU/USD) extended its steady intraday climb during Thursday’s early European session, reaching a fresh daily high near $4,465. The precious metal continues to benefit from a softer USD, which has weakened against major currencies including the EUR and JPY. Traders noted that safe-haven demand remains supportive, while technical momentum keeps the exchange rate buoyant despite limited volatility earlier in the day.  

Market participants are closely watching the U.S. dollar’s performance, as a weaker USD is fueling gold’s appeal in currency markets. However, the upside appears capped, with hawkish bets on the Federal Reserve restraining broader gains. Expectations of higher interest rates are prompting investors to weigh the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like gold against currency-trading positions in the USD.  

Analysts suggest that XAU/USD may continue oscillating near current levels unless significant shifts occur in risk sentiment or monetary policy outlooks. Resistance around the $4,465 mark could slow further advances, while any renewed dollar strength might trigger short-term retracements. Traders remain active across forex markets, balancing gold, USD, and major currencies like EUR, GBP, and JPY in anticipation of upcoming economic data.
