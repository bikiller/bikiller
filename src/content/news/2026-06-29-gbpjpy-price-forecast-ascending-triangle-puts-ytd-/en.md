The GBP/JPY currency pair extended gains on Monday, rising 0.59% as the Pound Sterling strengthened against the Japanese Yen. The exchange rate climbed to 214.78 after rebounding from daily lows of 213.41. Trading activity was supported by renewed speculation over possible intervention by Japanese authorities in the FX market, limiting aggressive yen appreciation and keeping volatility elevated.

Technically, the pair continues to trade within an ascending triangle pattern, keeping the year-to-date high in play as a key resistance zone. Market participants see the structure as a bullish continuation setup, with higher lows supporting upside momentum. Meanwhile, broader risk sentiment in USD and EUR markets also influences cross-currency flows, indirectly supporting GBP strength versus the yen.

Looking ahead, traders remain focused on potential Japanese intervention risks, which could cap further yen weakness and trigger short-term reversals in the currency pair. Sustained trading above 214.00 keeps bullish momentum intact, though profit-taking near recent highs may emerge. Volatility across global FX markets remains elevated as participants track central bank signals and cross-asset flows.
