British Pound is trading higher against US Dollar for second consecutive day on Friday, with GBP/USD holding above 1.3200. The currency pair is hovering just above this threshold as buyers maintain momentum, while the year-to-date lows at 1.3140 remain close and act as a nearby technical support zone. Traders remain cautious as USD volatility eases across major currency markets today.

The latest move in the GBP/USD currency pair reflects a softer US Dollar after its recent rally lost momentum, with improved risk appetite and easing geopolitical tensions contributing to the shift. Oil prices returning to pre-war levels have also influenced broader forex trading sentiment, supporting risk-sensitive currencies and keeping USD demand in check across global exchange rate markets. EUR also steadies.

From a technical trading perspective, the exchange rate remains supported above 1.3140, while a sustained move above 1.3200 could open the door for further upside in GBP against USD and potentially influence EUR cross flows as traders reassess near-term positioning. Market participants will closely watch whether the British Pound can maintain momentum above the psychological 1.3200 level amid fluctuating USD sentiment.
