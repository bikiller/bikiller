West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil hovered around $97.30 on Thursday, rising 0.34% amid heightened market volatility. Investors remain cautious following the latest Trump-Xi discussions, weighing potential impacts on global trade flows. The International Energy Agency (IEA) warned of a persistent supply deficit, which has added a bullish undertone to energy markets despite geopolitical uncertainties. Traders are closely watching oil-linked currencies, particularly USD/JPY and USD/CAD, as energy price swings influence exchange rates.  

The USD showed mixed performance, with the dollar index holding steady against a basket of major currencies as traders digested the oil-driven risk sentiment. EUR/USD trading remained range-bound, reflecting cautious positioning ahead of potential market-moving announcements. WTI’s modest gains have fueled short-term speculative trading, as investors adjust positions across commodity-linked currency pairs, balancing the risk of higher oil costs against broader economic growth concerns.  

Market participants noted that persistent oil deficits could bolster the USD in the near term, given the United States’ role as a major crude producer and importer. Currency traders are monitoring technical levels, with USD/JPY approaching key resistance and EUR/USD testing support around 1.1050. The interplay between energy markets and forex trading continues to underscore the global ripple effects of crude price fluctuations, influencing both exchange rates and investor sentiment.
