The British Pound (GBP) advanced against the US Dollar (USD), with the GBP/USD currency pair rising 0.5% to trade near 1.3340 during Thursday’s European trading session. The move reflects steady buying interest in sterling as traders position ahead of key US labor market data, keeping exchange rate momentum tilted higher in early trading.

The US Dollar remains on the defensive as investors await the upcoming Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report, a key catalyst for currency pair volatility. Expectations of shifting Federal Reserve policy continue to influence USD demand, while improved risk sentiment supports the British Pound across major exchange rates. Traders are also reassessing short-term positioning in anticipation of stronger US labor figures or potential downside surprises.

Overall, GBP/USD remains supported, with traders monitoring whether the currency pair can sustain gains above recent levels ahead of data release. Increased volatility is expected as US NFP approaches, with short-term flows likely dictating direction across EUR crosses and broader forex markets. Market participants will watch key technical levels around 1.3340 for confirmation of further upside momentum or rejection.
