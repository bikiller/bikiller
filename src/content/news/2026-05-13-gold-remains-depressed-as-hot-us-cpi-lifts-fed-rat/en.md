Gold (XAU/USD) held a negative tone for the second consecutive day on Wednesday, hovering above the multi-day low of around $1,638 reached yesterday. Traders are cautious as the US dollar strengthened, supported by hotter-than-expected US CPI data that fueled expectations of additional Federal Reserve rate hikes. The currency pair shows limited upside, with resistance looming near $1,650.  

The robust US inflation reading lifted the USD across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, putting pressure on commodities priced in dollars. Market participants are weighing the potential impact of tighter monetary policy, with gold trading subdued despite minor pullbacks. Technical levels suggest the yellow metal may test support around $1,630 if selling pressure continues.  

Analysts note that sustained USD strength could dampen gold demand as investors seek higher-yielding assets. Meanwhile, the interplay between interest rate expectations and inflation data remains a key driver for exchange rates and trading flows. Currency traders are closely monitoring economic releases and Fed signals to gauge the trajectory of both the USD and safe-haven assets like gold.
