China’s formal invocation of its 2021 Blocking Statute marks a notable shift in global trade risk, according to MUFG’s Michael Wan. The move directly counters recent US sanctions on five Chinese refineries linked to Iranian oil, signaling Beijing’s readiness to shield domestic firms from foreign regulatory pressure. Analysts say this development could heighten geopolitical uncertainty, affecting major currency pairs such as USD/CNY and EUR/CNY as traders reassess risk exposure in emerging markets.  

In forex trading, the immediate impact has been a modest appreciation of the yuan, with USD/CNY dipping toward the 6.88 mark, while EUR/CNY edged lower to 7.40. Market participants are recalibrating positions, factoring in potential retaliatory measures and trade frictions. Liquidity in Asian sessions showed heightened volatility, reflecting cautious investor sentiment. The move also underscores how geopolitical events increasingly influence exchange rates, particularly in currencies sensitive to US-China relations.  

Looking ahead, traders are monitoring technical levels closely. A sustained breach below 6.88 in USD/CNY could trigger further yuan strength, while resistance near 6.92 may cap upside momentum. EUR/USD and other major pairs are also under pressure as investors weigh global growth concerns alongside currency-specific developments. The Blocking Statute’s activation may prompt hedging strategies, with market participants adjusting long and short positions to navigate evolving risk landscapes.
