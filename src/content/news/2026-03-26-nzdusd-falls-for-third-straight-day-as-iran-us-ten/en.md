NZD/USD continued its decline on Thursday, trading around 0.5770, down 0.65% for the day. This marks the third consecutive loss for the currency pair after it failed to maintain its position near the 0.5900 level reached last week. The sustained drop reflects ongoing pressure on the New Zealand dollar amid broader market dynamics.

The recent fall in NZD/USD is driven by increased demand for the safe-haven US dollar, as tensions between Iran and the United States have escalated. The rising geopolitical uncertainty has spurred risk-off sentiment, with investors flocking to the USD. This shift in market mood has weighed heavily on risk-sensitive currencies like the NZD, which remains under selling pressure in the current environment.
