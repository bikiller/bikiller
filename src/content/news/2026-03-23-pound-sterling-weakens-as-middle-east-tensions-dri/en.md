Pound Sterling came under pressure on Monday as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East pushed oil prices higher, boosting safe-haven demand for the US Dollar. The GBP/USD currency pair slid to around 1.3315 during early European trading, with traders reacting to a firmer USD and increased risk aversion in global markets. Investors are closely watching developments that could affect commodity-linked currencies and cross-border flows.  

Market participants are also awaiting key economic data, with preliminary Purchasing Managers Index (PMI) readings from the UK and the US scheduled for Tuesday. Analysts suggest that stronger-than-expected US PMI figures could further support the USD, potentially pressuring the GBP/USD exchange rate below current levels. Conversely, resilient UK PMI data might provide temporary relief to the British pound amid broader risk-off sentiment.  

Technical traders note that the GBP/USD pair is testing early support near 1.3315, while the euro-to-dollar exchange rate remains under watch for broader trends. Continued volatility in oil markets and USD strength could shape trading strategies, influencing currency pair positions across forex markets. Investors are advised to monitor geopolitical developments and macroeconomic indicators for clues on near-term exchange rate movements.
