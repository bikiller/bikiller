Asian currency markets are showing restrained movement as a firm US Dollar and elevated US Treasury yields continue to weigh on regional currencies. Despite renewed optimism around US–China trade talks, broader Asia FX remains soft, with most major pairs struggling to gain traction. The USD remains the dominant driver, keeping trading ranges tight across the region.  

The Renminbi (RMB) stands out as the main outperformer, supported by lower USD/CNY fixing rates and policy-tolerated appreciation. Traders have pushed the onshore exchange rate slightly higher, reflecting a market that is receptive to central bank guidance. The combination of political stability and cautious optimism around trade discussions underpins RMB strength against both the USD and other Asian currencies.  

Broader implications for Asia FX suggest limited upside without a decisive shift in US interest rate expectations. Currency pairs like USD/SGD and USD/KRW are trading near technical resistance levels, while sentiment-driven flows remain fragile. Investors are closely watching US Treasury yields, regional monetary policies, and ongoing US–China negotiations for cues that could trigger more sustained movements across Asian markets.
