The Dow Jones Industrial Average futures dropped 230 points, or 0.5%, on Thursday, reversing gains from the previous session. Rising oil prices and renewed tensions in Iran dampened investor sentiment, pushing risk appetite lower. This sell-off weighed on the broader market, with the S&P 500 down 0.8% and the Nasdaq Composite falling 1.1%. The flight to safety boosted demand for the USD, with the EUR/USD trading near 1.0700.

Oil prices surged amid escalating geopolitical risks, further pressuring global equities. As market participants fled to safer assets, the USD strengthened against major currencies. The EUR/USD remained under pressure, with a breakout below 1.0700 potentially signaling further downside. In the forex market, the risk-off sentiment also saw the JPY gain against the USD, as traders sought refuge in the yen, while crude's volatility kept currency pairs in tight ranges.
