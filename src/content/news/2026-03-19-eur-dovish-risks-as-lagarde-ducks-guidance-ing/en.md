EUR faced downward pressure after ECB President Christine Lagarde sidestepped giving explicit guidance, raising dovish concerns among traders. ING’s Francesco Pesole noted that recent mixed signals from major central banks are likely prompting the ECB to remain cautious, particularly given its heightened sensitivity to oil price swings following the 2022 shocks. The euro struggled against the USD, with the EUR/USD pair hovering near key support levels.  

Market participants are closely watching the exchange rate, as EUR trading activity reacts to central bank ambiguity. Short-term technical indicators suggest increased volatility, with EUR/USD testing its 1.0800 support and resistance near 1.1000. Traders are factoring in both energy-driven inflation risks and broader monetary policy uncertainty, keeping the pair range-bound for now.  

The lack of clear guidance may limit EUR gains against major currencies. USD strength is likely to persist as investors seek safe-haven assets amid central bank divergence. Analysts highlight that EUR traders should monitor oil price trends and ECB commentary for potential catalysts, as any shift in policy tone could drive renewed exchange rate swings and trading opportunities.
