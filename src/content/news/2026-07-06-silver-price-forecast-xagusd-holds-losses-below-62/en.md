Silver price (XAG/USD) trades around $62.30 per troy ounce during European hours on Monday, slipping below the $62.50 mark and halting a four-day winning streak. The currency pair edges lower as trading momentum fades, with the exchange rate reflecting mild selling pressure in early sessions across global markets amid cautious sentiment in the broader forex market.  

Market pressure stems from rising expectations that the Federal Reserve may deliver an interest rate hike later this year, supporting the US dollar and weighing on precious metals. As USD strengthens in the currency market, non-yielding assets like silver lose appeal, while EUR/USD and other major forex pairs reflect shifting risk sentiment across global trading sessions this week outlook overall.  

Looking ahead, traders will monitor whether XAG/USD can reclaim the $62.50 level, which may act as near-term resistance if bullish momentum returns.
