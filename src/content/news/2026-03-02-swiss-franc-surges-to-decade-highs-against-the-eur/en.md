The Swiss Franc surged to decade-high levels against the Euro on Monday as geopolitical tensions escalated. Following US-Israeli military strikes on Iran, a wave of risk aversion gripped global markets, driving investors into safe-haven assets. The EUR/CHF currency pair dropped sharply, reaching a low of 0.9150, a level not seen in over ten years. The Swiss Franc also gained against other major currencies, outperforming the USD.

However, the rally was short-lived as the Swiss National Bank (SNB) intervened with strong warnings to cool the CHF markets. The SNB's remarks helped reverse some of the Franc's gains, particularly against the Euro, which pushed the EUR/CHF exchange rate back up to 0.9200. Despite the SNB's efforts, the Swiss Franc remains a key player in the ongoing market volatility, with traders eyeing further geopolitical developments and SNB policy moves.
