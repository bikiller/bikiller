Philippine inflation is poised to rise further above the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas’ (BSP) target, driven by persistent pressures from food, fuel, and a weaker peso. Analysts at DBS Group Research, led by Radhika Rao, highlighted that while sequential inflation pressures have eased slightly due to lower fuel and utility costs, the overall upward trend remains intact, keeping the PHP/USD exchange rate under scrutiny in forex trading circles.

The Philippine peso has seen volatility against major currencies such as the USD and EUR as traders weigh inflation risks against BSP policy. Higher domestic prices are likely to influence market expectations for BSP rate adjustments, potentially supporting further currency strength if tighter monetary measures are signaled. Technical levels for the PHP/USD pair are being closely watched, with investors assessing short-term support near 55.50 and resistance around 56.20.

Currency traders are positioning ahead of anticipated central bank communications, with the market factoring in the impact of sustained inflation on interest rates and the exchange rate. Persistent food and fuel-driven inflation could spur the BSP to maintain or raise rates, creating trading opportunities in the PHP against USD and other regional peers. Market participants remain alert to shifts in price trends and BSP guidance that could sway both short-term trading and broader sentiment across Asia’s forex markets.
