The Australian Dollar (AUD) traded in a mixed fashion ahead of the upcoming release of the Australian Consumer Price Index (CPI) and the Federal Reserve's policy meeting. The AUD/USD exchange rate slipped slightly to 0.7180 during the European session on Tuesday, reflecting subdued market sentiment as traders await key economic data. The Australian currency's performance was muted as investors gauged the potential impact of inflation figures on the Reserve Bank of Australia’s future decisions.

In contrast, the AUD showed more resilience against other major currencies, with little movement against the Euro (EUR). The market’s focus remains on the forthcoming CPI report, which is expected to provide further insight into Australia's inflationary trends. The outcome could influence expectations regarding the RBA’s stance on interest rates, while the broader Forex market keeps a close eye on the Federal Reserve’s next policy steps. The current trading environment suggests cautious positioning as both central banks prepare to make critical announcements.
