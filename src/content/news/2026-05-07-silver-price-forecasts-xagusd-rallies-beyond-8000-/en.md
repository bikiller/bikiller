Silver (XAG/USD) surged past the $80.00 mark on Thursday, driven by a broader risk-on mood in global markets. Traders moved into precious metals as U.S. Treasury yields declined, easing concerns over aggressive Federal Reserve rate hikes. The currency pair extended gains against the USD, with market participants weighing lower oil prices and a weaker dollar as key support for silver trading.  

The pullback in oil has cooled expectations for further Fed tightening, fueling a rally in XAG/USD. Technical charts show strong momentum above $80, with short-term traders eyeing consolidation near this round number. The shift has also influenced other currency pairs, as the USD softened against the EUR and JPY, reflecting lower real yields and renewed appetite for risk assets.  

Analysts note that silver’s exchange rate could remain elevated if Treasury yields stay subdued and equity markets maintain their upward trajectory. Trading volumes indicate sustained interest from speculative investors, while the broader forex market monitors USD movements closely. This dynamic may continue to support precious metals, highlighting the interplay between macroeconomic signals, rate expectations, and currency fluctuations.
