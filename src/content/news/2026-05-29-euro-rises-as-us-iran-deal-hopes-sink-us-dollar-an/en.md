The EUR/USD currency pair advanced on Friday, with the exchange rate climbing 0.12% to 1.1664 as the US Dollar weakened amid shifting sentiment surrounding developments in the Middle East. While regional hostilities continued to generate uncertainty, market participants focused on reports that a potential US-Iran agreement is awaiting approval from the White House and senior Iranian officials. The prospect of easing tensions reduced demand for traditional safe-haven assets, putting pressure on the USD during trading.

At the same time, hopes for diplomatic progress weighed on crude oil prices, further influencing broader market sentiment and currency flows. Lower oil prices and reduced geopolitical risk encouraged investors to move away from defensive positions, supporting the euro against the greenback. As a result, the EUR gained modest ground, allowing the EUR/USD pair to extend its upward move during the session.

For forex traders, the latest price action highlights how geopolitical developments can quickly influence exchange rates and trading conditions. The softer USD environment provided support for the euro, while expectations surrounding a potential US-Iran deal remained a key driver of market direction. Investors will continue monitoring political headlines and risk sentiment for clues on whether the EUR/USD currency pair can sustain gains above the 1.1664 level in the near term.
