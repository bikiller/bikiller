USD/CHF trades flat on Thursday around 0.7845, showing choppy price action with two-way swings. Traders are avoiding aggressive positioning in the currency pair as broader forex markets remain cautious. Ongoing US-Iran geopolitical tensions continue to weigh on risk sentiment, limiting conviction in USD trading and keeping the exchange rate confined within a narrow intraday range.

The pair’s upside attempts remain capped below the 100-day and 200-day simple moving averages, reinforcing a technically bearish ceiling for USD/CHF. Repeated failures to break above these key indicators suggest sellers are defending higher levels, keeping the currency pair under pressure despite occasional USD strength. Weak momentum near these technical barriers has limited upside momentum and contributed to sideways trading conditions.

Looking ahead, USD/CHF direction will likely remain tied to risk sentiment and developments in geopolitical tensions, with traders closely watching US dollar demand across major currency pairs. Until a decisive breakout above the moving averages occurs, the exchange rate may continue to consolidate around current levels near 0.7845. Volatility is expected to persist as forex markets react to shifts in USD flows and global risk appetite.
