The Mexican Peso (MXN) has posted mixed performance against the US Dollar (USD) so far this year. While the currency gained ground early in 2026, Rabobank strategists Molly Schwartz and Christian Lawrence note recent softening, reflecting a moderation in carry trades and shifting investor sentiment. The USD/MXN currency pair has oscillated around key technical levels, with traders monitoring both domestic fundamentals and global risk trends.

Despite the pullback, the peso retains relative support versus peers, benefiting from Mexico’s higher interest rates and stable macroeconomic indicators. Schroder and Lawrence highlight that, in cross-currency terms, the peso remains resilient against the euro (EUR) and other G10 currencies, even as short-term trading sees increased volatility. Market participants are weighing potential central bank moves and external shocks that could influence near-term exchange rates.

Looking ahead, the MXN’s trajectory will hinge on both domestic policy signals and broader USD dynamics. Sustained investor confidence could reinforce support, while global risk-off episodes may pressure the currency lower. Traders are closely watching technical thresholds for the USD/MXN pair, using these as reference points for entry and exit in both carry and speculative trades, as liquidity conditions and risk appetite evolve.
