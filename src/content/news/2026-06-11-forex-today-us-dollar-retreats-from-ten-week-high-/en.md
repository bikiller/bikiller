The US Dollar pulled back from a ten-week peak as the DXY slipped to 99.60, following a surge earlier in the session to a three-month high. Traders booked profits despite the release of May’s Core PPI, which rose 0.4% month-on-month and held steady at 4.9% year-on-year, signaling persistent inflation pressures but not enough to sustain the USD’s momentum. The dollar’s retreat weighed on key currency pairs, with USD/EUR and USD/JPY seeing increased volatility.

The euro faced pressure after European Central Bank officials highlighted a cautious outlook, keeping investors wary of aggressive policy shifts. The EUR/USD exchange rate fluctuated as market participants weighed ECB guidance against US inflation data. Technical levels around the 1.09–1.10 zone became focal points for trading, with short-term momentum indicators suggesting potential consolidation before the next directional move.

Traders remain attentive to both US inflation metrics and ECB commentary, as these continue to drive currency market dynamics. USD strength may resume if upcoming data confirms resilient domestic growth, while EUR could rebound if ECB maintains accommodative signals. Exchange rate swings highlight the delicate balance between profit-taking, monetary policy expectations, and broader risk sentiment across global forex markets.
