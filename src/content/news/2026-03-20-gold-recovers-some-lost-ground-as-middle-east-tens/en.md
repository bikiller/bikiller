Gold (XAU/USD) regained some ground on Friday, climbing back toward $4,725 after a seven-day slide, as heightened tensions between the US and Iran fueled safe-haven demand. The rebound in the precious metal comes amid cautious trading in major currency pairs, with the dollar index showing resilience against the euro and yen, reflecting investors’ flight to perceived safety. Traders are positioning ahead of potential geopolitical developments.  

The renewed appetite for gold comes alongside modest movements in forex markets, with USD/EUR and USD/JPY exchange rates seeing subdued volatility as investors weigh the impact of Middle East unrest. Analysts note that continued escalation could sustain gold’s upward momentum, potentially pressuring risk-sensitive assets like equities while boosting USD demand as a benchmark for safe-haven flows.  

Technically, XAU/USD is testing resistance near $4,725, with market watchers eyeing whether the level holds or prompts further gains. Active trading volumes suggest participants are hedging against geopolitical uncertainty, influencing broader trading strategies across forex and commodities markets. The combination of geopolitical risk and defensive positioning is likely to keep traders focused on short-term shifts in both precious metals and major currency pairs.
