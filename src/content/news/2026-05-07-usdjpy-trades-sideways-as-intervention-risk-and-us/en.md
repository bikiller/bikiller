The USD/JPY currency pair held steady around 156.30 on Thursday, edging down just 0.05% as traders weighed the possibility of Japanese intervention. The Japanese Yen has found underlying support amid speculation that authorities may act to limit further weakness, keeping the exchange rate range-bound despite ongoing US dollar strength. Market participants remain cautious ahead of key US jobs data that could influence USD momentum.  

Trading activity has been muted, with the USD showing resilience against major peers like the EUR and JPY. Analysts note that any sharp move above 156.50 could intensify calls for intervention, while a dip toward 155.80 may attract buying interest. Investors are balancing global risk sentiment with domestic monetary signals, leaving the pair oscillating in a narrow band.  

The outlook for USD/JPY depends heavily on both macroeconomic releases and policy expectations. US employment figures will likely shape near-term USD trading, while Yen intervention risk constrains significant moves. Traders are positioning for volatility around key technical levels, as the interplay between safe-haven flows and dollar strength continues to dictate the pace and direction of the currency pair.
