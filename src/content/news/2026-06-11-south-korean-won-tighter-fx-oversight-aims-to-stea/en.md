South Korean authorities are stepping up oversight of the foreign exchange market to support the Korean Won, Commerzbank notes. Banks are now subject to more frequent FX position reviews, and joint inspections with the Bank of Korea are intensifying. These measures aim to curb excessive volatility as the USD/KRW currency pair hovers around 1,520, a level not seen since 2009.

Traders report that the Korean Won is consolidating amid heightened monitoring and broader market caution. The central bank’s engagement signals a commitment to stabilizing the exchange rate, particularly against the US dollar, which has strengthened in global markets. EUR/KRW and other major currency pairs are showing modest retracements, reflecting a cautious trading environment.

Market participants are weighing the impact of these regulatory moves on short-term USD/KRW trading strategies. Sustained intervention could temper sharp swings, while investor sentiment remains sensitive to global USD strength and domestic liquidity conditions. Analysts suggest that monitoring these FX measures is key for forecasting potential movements in both Korean Won and regional currencies over the coming weeks.
