Silver (XAG/USD) faced a sharp drop on Wednesday, tumbling nearly 2.80% as the currency pair breached its 50-day Simple Moving Average at $75.77. The breakdown through this key technical level opened the path for XAG/USD to fall below the $75.00 mark, signaling increased selling pressure in the market. Traders watched the daily low hit $73.44 before a modest rebound brought the exchange rate to $74.74.

The decline comes amid a stronger USD, which pressured silver as investors shifted toward safe-haven and higher-yielding assets. Momentum traders reacted to the breach of the SMA, reinforcing the bearish sentiment. Forex market activity highlighted elevated trading volumes around $75.00, suggesting the level now acts as near-term resistance for the precious metal against the greenback.

Looking ahead, bears appear focused on a potential move toward $73.00, with the broader trend showing vulnerability to further USD strength and shifts in risk appetite across EUR and other major currency pairs. Market watchers will monitor upcoming economic data and USD sentiment, as these factors could influence XAG/USD’s trading range and investor positioning in the coming sessions.
