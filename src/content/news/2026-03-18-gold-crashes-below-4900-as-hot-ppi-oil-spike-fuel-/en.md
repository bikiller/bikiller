Gold (XAU/USD) slumped over 2.20% on Wednesday, dropping below $4,900 amid a renewed USD rally. The precious metal fell to $4,878 after briefly peaking at $5,016, as stronger-than-expected US PPI data and rising oil prices pushed investors toward the dollar. Traders reacted swiftly, pricing in the possibility that persistent inflation could keep the Federal Reserve on a tighter monetary path, limiting easing prospects.  

The US dollar strengthened across major currency pairs, with the EUR/USD and GBP/USD retreating as risk sentiment shifted. Rising Middle East tensions added a layer of uncertainty, but the immediate market focus remained on inflationary pressures and energy-driven volatility, influencing both commodity and forex trading. Technical levels around $4,900 proved insufficient to support gold, highlighting the susceptibility of XAU/USD to macroeconomic triggers.  

Analysts suggest that if US inflation continues to surprise to the upside, the USD may maintain its momentum, pressuring gold further. Forex traders are closely watching exchange rates and commodities for directional cues, balancing geopolitical risk with monetary policy expectations. For now, XAU/USD faces immediate resistance near $5,016 and support around $4,870, framing short-term trading strategies for market participants.
