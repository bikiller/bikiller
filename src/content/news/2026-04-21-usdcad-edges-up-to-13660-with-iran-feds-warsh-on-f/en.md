The US Dollar (USD) saw modest gains against the Canadian Dollar (CAD) on Tuesday, recovering slightly after a six-day decline. The USD/CAD currency pair reached session highs just above 1.3660, though it remains near the five-week low of 1.3635 set the day before. This slight uptick follows a period of selling pressure, with market sentiment still cautious.

Attention is now turning to geopolitical and central bank developments. With ongoing tensions surrounding Iran and comments from Federal Reserve Governor Christopher Waller, traders are weighing potential impacts on USD's outlook. The current exchange rate indicates a sensitive market, where any further volatility in oil prices or U.S. economic signals could drive additional fluctuations in USD/CAD trading.
