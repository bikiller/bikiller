GBP/USD currency pair came under renewed selling pressure during the Wednesday Asian session, slipping below the 1.3250 level after failing to break above the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement. The exchange rate had earlier climbed to a nearly two-week high around 1.3275 in the previous trading session, but upside momentum faded as USD demand strengthened across forex markets.

Technically, the rejection at the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement reinforced short-term resistance near the 1.3275 zone, triggering profit-taking and fresh short positions. Trading activity suggested buyers lost control as the currency pair struggled to hold gains above key intraday levels, while broader USD stability continued to weigh on risk-sensitive currencies like GBP in global trading.

Looking ahead, traders are watching whether GBP/USD can maintain support above the 1.3250 mark, with a break lower potentially exposing the 1.32 handle. Market participants remain focused on USD dynamics and upcoming US economic data, which could drive broader exchange rate volatility across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and other GBP crosses.
