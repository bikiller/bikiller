The Australian Dollar edged lower against the US Dollar on Wednesday, reflecting softer-than-expected inflation data from Australia. Traders noted that the AUD/USD currency pair slipped to around 0.6640, as market participants digested the subdued CPI figures, which raised concerns about slower domestic price pressures. Technical analysts highlighted that the pair is testing key support levels near 0.6625, prompting cautious trading ahead of the upcoming Federal Reserve decision.  

Meanwhile, the US Dollar gained traction amid escalating geopolitical tensions, particularly ongoing uncertainties around US-Iran relations. The stronger Greenback lifted the USD index, reinforcing its appeal as a safe-haven currency. Investors increasingly favored USD liquidity, leading to notable trading volumes across major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Market sentiment suggests that any further escalation could sustain USD strength and keep commodity-linked currencies, including AUD, under pressure.  

Looking ahead, traders are closely monitoring the Fed’s policy signals, with expectations that interest rate guidance could influence short-term currency movements. The AUD/USD exchange rate may continue to face volatility, with potential rebounds limited unless inflation data shows improvement or geopolitical risks ease. For now, market watchers anticipate cautious trading, watching for technical breakouts above 0.6680 or further declines toward 0.6600.
