GBP/USD opened the new week with a sharp risk-on surge, briefly climbing toward 1.3450 as news of a US-Iran ceasefire and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz eased geopolitical tensions. The move triggered a drop in crude oil prices and supported risk-sensitive currencies, with the British pound gaining alongside broader FX sentiment.

However, the rally quickly reversed as the initial geopolitical optimism faded, sending GBP/USD back toward 1.3400, effectively completing a full round-trip to its starting level. The dollar regained footing ahead of key Federal Reserve expectations, with traders reassessing risk exposure and short-term positioning across major currency pairs.

Looking ahead, FX trading remains highly sensitive to geopolitical developments and central bank signals, particularly with the Fed in focus. The correlation between oil prices and risk currencies continues to drive volatility, leaving GBP/USD vulnerable to further swings around the 1.3400 area as liquidity conditions shift. Traders will watch 1.3450 resistance and 1.3400 support closely.
