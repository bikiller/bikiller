Bank of England Deputy Governor Sarah Breeden warned during a U.S. program on Friday that the ongoing conflict in the Middle East has increased the likelihood of market stresses converging. The uncertainty surrounding the war has heightened risks in global financial markets, with investors closely watching developments in both the Middle East and broader geopolitical tensions.

This has had a notable impact on currency markets, with the EUR/USD exchange rate dipping below key support levels. Trading volatility has surged, reflecting investor anxiety. The USD strengthened as a safe-haven currency, while the EUR struggled to maintain ground. With the war's potential to disrupt oil prices and supply chains, further pressure on global risk assets is expected, keeping traders on edge and influencing forex movements across major pairs.
