Geopolitical tensions, particularly Iran’s control over the Strait of Hormuz, continue to place pressure on global oil supply chains, according to Rabobank's Bas van Geffen. The risk of disruption in one of the world’s most crucial oil shipping routes keeps oil prices volatile, driving up the costs of energy. As market uncertainty persists, oil prices remain elevated, directly influencing currency pairs like USD/JPY and EUR/USD, which are highly sensitive to global commodity price shifts.

The ongoing regional instability has led to heightened risks for energy markets, creating fluctuations across commodity-linked currencies. For instance, the Australian Dollar (AUD) and Canadian Dollar (CAD) are particularly vulnerable to shifts in oil prices, as they are closely tied to energy exports. With oil trading at over $80 per barrel, these geopolitical factors are expected to drive continued volatility in both oil and forex markets, keeping traders on edge as they monitor developments in the region.
