West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil extended its decline during Friday’s Asian trading session, hovering near $97.60 per barrel after recording modest gains a day earlier. The commodity remained under pressure as traders monitored developments around the Strait of Hormuz, where nearly 30 vessels reportedly continued navigating through the critical shipping corridor. Despite geopolitical concerns, market participants appeared cautious, limiting aggressive bullish positioning in energy markets and broader USD-linked trading activity.

The pullback in oil prices also influenced forex sentiment, particularly among commodity-linked currency pairs and emerging-market exchange rates. A softer crude market can weigh on oil-exporting currencies while offering support to major importers tied to the USD and EUR. Investors continued assessing whether stable shipping flows through Hormuz could ease immediate supply disruption fears, reducing demand for traditional safe-haven assets and tempering volatility across global trading markets.

Meanwhile, the US Dollar remained broadly supported as traders focused on inflation expectations and energy-driven price pressures. Lower WTI levels near the $97.50-$97.60 zone may slightly ease concerns over accelerating fuel costs, though persistent geopolitical risks continue to underpin cautious sentiment. Currency pair movements involving the USD, EUR/USD, and commodity-sensitive currencies are expected to remain closely tied to fluctuations in crude oil prices and broader risk appetite.
