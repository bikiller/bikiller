The USD/JPY currency pair saw a modest rise of nearly 0.25% on Tuesday, hovering around 158.07, 80 pips higher than its opening price. The uptick came as risk sentiment soured during the late hours of the New York session, despite US President Donald Trump hinting at a potential de-escalation of ongoing geopolitical tensions. The pair’s movement reflects growing caution in global markets, with traders responding to risk-off dynamics.

As the USD/JPY clings near the 158.00 mark, market participants are closely watching the broader geopolitical landscape and its impact on the exchange rate. While Trump’s remarks were initially seen as a potential calming factor, the ongoing uncertainty has kept the Japanese yen in demand as a safe-haven currency. With the pair’s technical support around the 158.00 level, further risk aversion could drive additional volatility in the short term.
