The Federal Reserve held its key interest rates at 3.50%-3.75% in March, maintaining its stance despite inflationary pressures from rising energy prices. Deutsche Bank economists anticipate a 25 basis point cut in September, but the market is pricing in only slight easing. This caution is reflected in the USD's recent movements against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, with traders awaiting clearer signals on the Fed's future actions.

Rising oil prices, driven by concerns over a potential blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, could further complicate the outlook. Deutsche Bank warns that this could push Brent crude toward USD 120 per barrel, adding to inflationary pressures. This development may affect the USD's performance in currency pairs, potentially leading to greater volatility as traders adjust positions in response to both energy price fluctuations and the Fed's next moves.
