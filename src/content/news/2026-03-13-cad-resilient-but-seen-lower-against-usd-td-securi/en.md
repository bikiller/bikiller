TD Securities analysts predict the Canadian Dollar (CAD) will remain relatively strong against non-USD currencies, thanks to its close ties to oil. With crude prices holding steady, CAD benefits from this link, positioning it favorably in comparison to currencies like the Euro (EUR) and the Japanese Yen (JPY). Oil's resilience is expected to support CAD's strength despite broader market uncertainties.

However, the outlook against the US Dollar (USD) remains less optimistic. TD Securities suggests that CAD is likely to weaken against the greenback, reflecting a lower sensitivity to risk-off market sentiment. The USD's dominance, combined with cleaner positioning in the Canadian Dollar, could see the currency pair (CAD/USD) struggle to gain traction. Traders are advised to watch closely for any shifts in oil prices or risk appetite, as these could further influence CAD's movements.
