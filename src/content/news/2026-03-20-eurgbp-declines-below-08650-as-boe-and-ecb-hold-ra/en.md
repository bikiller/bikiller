EUR/GBP fell below the 0.8650 level during early European trading on Friday, with the currency pair hovering near 0.8620. The Pound Sterling gained momentum against the Euro after both the Bank of England (BoE) and the European Central Bank (ECB) opted to maintain their interest rates, fueling a shift in market sentiment.

The decision from both central banks came amid growing geopolitical uncertainty, contributing to fluctuations in the EUR/GBP exchange rate. While the ECB held steady, the BoE’s move seemed to offer support for the British currency. Traders now focus on upcoming economic data, with 0.8600 seen as a key technical level for the cross.
