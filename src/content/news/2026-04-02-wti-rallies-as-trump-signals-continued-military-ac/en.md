West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil surged more than 8% on Thursday, as renewed geopolitical tensions boosted risk premiums across commodity and forex markets. Prices climbed sharply after signals of continued US military action against Iran heightened concerns over supply disruptions through the Strait of Hormuz, a key global energy chokepoint influencing trading sentiment and USD-linked assets.

The rally in crude supported the US Dollar (USD) against several major currency pairs, as higher energy prices fed into inflation expectations and reinforced demand for USD-denominated commodities. Traders monitored exchange rate movements closely, with EUR/USD facing pressure amid rising oil-driven volatility and shifting risk appetite across global markets.

Market participants remain focused on geopolitical developments and their implications for supply stability, with sustained disruptions likely to keep oil prices elevated. This environment may continue to influence forex trading flows, particularly in USD and energy-sensitive currencies, as investors reassess risk exposure and adjust positions across key currency pairs in response to ongoing uncertainty.
