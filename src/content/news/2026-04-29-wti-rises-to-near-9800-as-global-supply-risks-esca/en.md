WTI crude oil extended its rally for a third consecutive day, rising to approximately $96.90 per barrel during Asian trading on Wednesday. The gains come amid growing concerns over global supply risks, particularly from key oil-producing regions. As a result, oil prices continue to climb, pushing the commodity closer to the $98.00 mark, highlighting increasing market volatility and tightening supply dynamics.

The surge in oil prices has implications for the forex market, especially against the USD. Rising energy prices often bolster demand for the US dollar, given its role as the primary currency for oil trading. Meanwhile, the EUR/USD exchange rate could face downward pressure if oil prices continue to rise, as the eurozone is heavily impacted by higher energy costs. Traders are closely monitoring these movements, with the risk of further volatility in the oil and currency markets.
