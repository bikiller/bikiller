WTI crude oil surged over 7.5% on Thursday, driven by escalating concerns over potential disruptions to global oil supply through the Strait of Hormuz, a key shipping route. The market is responding to rising tensions in the US-Iran conflict, which could intensify the volatility in oil prices. As a result, the USD saw notable movements, with the EUR/USD exchange rate reflecting the broader risk sentiment.

Technically, WTI’s bullish momentum appears poised to continue, with traders eyeing further upside potential. Key resistance levels are now being tested, suggesting that the ongoing geopolitical risks could fuel more price gains. This surge in crude oil prices has significant implications for currency pairs such as USD/JPY and EUR/USD, where rising oil costs typically lead to shifts in global economic sentiment and investor appetite for riskier assets.
