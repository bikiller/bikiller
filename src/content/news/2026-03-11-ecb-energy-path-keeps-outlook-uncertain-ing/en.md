EUR exchange rates remain volatile as energy market developments continue to cloud the European economic outlook, according to ING strategists Michiel Tukker and Benjamin Schroeder. They highlight that EUR/USD trading is still highly sensitive to shifts in energy prices, which could dictate the pace of inflation and influence the ECB’s policy path. Markets currently price in the first ECB rate hikes around 2026, leaving currency traders cautious amid uncertainty.  

The strategists note that persistent energy volatility may trigger short-term swings in the euro against the USD and other major currencies. Technical levels around 1.08–1.10 for EUR/USD are under watch, with trading activity reflecting heightened responsiveness to headline energy data and broader macro signals. Analysts emphasize that these dynamics could impact carry trades and EUR positioning, especially as investors weigh the delayed impact of ECB guidance.  

Looking ahead, the EUR/USD currency pair may face sporadic spikes or retracements, with traders monitoring energy-linked inflation metrics closely. The delayed 2026 ECB hikes suggest a prolonged period of subdued euro strength versus the USD, creating tactical trading opportunities for forex participants. Investors are advised to track energy price developments, as these remain the dominant driver of exchange rate fluctuations and market sentiment across European assets.
