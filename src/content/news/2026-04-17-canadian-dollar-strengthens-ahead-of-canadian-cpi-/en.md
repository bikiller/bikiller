The Canadian Dollar (CAD) is strengthening as the USD/CAD pair drops further to 1.3685 during early European trading on Friday. The move reflects reduced safe-haven demand for the US Dollar (USD) after reports surfaced of a ceasefire agreement between Israel and Lebanon. This development eased geopolitical tensions, diminishing the USD's appeal.

Traders are also positioning themselves ahead of the upcoming Canadian CPI inflation report, which is expected to influence the Bank of Canada's future monetary policy decisions. As market participants anticipate potential rate hikes, the CAD is benefiting from increased demand, contributing to the downtrend in USD/CAD. The pair is likely to see heightened volatility in the session as traders react to inflation data and broader risk sentiment.
