The USD/JPY currency pair slid 0.62% on Tuesday, marking its second consecutive decline. The pair closed around 158.70 after an initial attempt to push towards the key 160.00 level was swiftly rejected. This downturn is attributed to broader market reactions, with risk sentiment strengthening following hopes of peace in Iran, which weighed on the US Dollar.

The move lower in USD/JPY comes amid shifting market dynamics, as geopolitical developments fuel optimism about a potential resolution in the Middle East. As a result, traders have reduced their positions in the US Dollar, leading to declines across USD-related pairs. The exchange rate’s rejection at 160.00 suggests a resistance zone, with further downside potential if the risk-on sentiment persists.
