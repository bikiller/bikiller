The EUR/USD currency pair faced a volatile session, climbing briefly to 1.1848 before a sharp reversal left the Euro under pressure. Analysts at UOB, including Quek Ser Leang Lee and Sue Ann, noted that this spike failed to sustain momentum, suggesting the exchange rate is currently overextended. Market participants are now assessing whether the Euro can maintain support amid ongoing USD strength.  

UOB forecasts further downside potential for EUR/USD, though they expect firm support at 1.1715 to prevent a deeper sell-off. The level of 1.1665 appears unlikely to be tested in the near term, indicating a bounded range for the currency pair. Traders are advised to watch for consolidation and range-bound trading opportunities while monitoring key technical levels.  

Market observers highlight that the failed push higher reflects a mix of profit-taking and cautious positioning ahead of economic data releases. With the USD showing resilience, EUR/USD may continue to fluctuate within the defined range, offering both short-term trading opportunities and signals for broader market sentiment. Investors are weighing the Euro’s vulnerability against firm USD demand, creating a delicate balance in forex trading dynamics.
