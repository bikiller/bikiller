The USD/CAD currency pair slipped during Friday trading as the Canadian Dollar gained ground against the US Dollar following weaker-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data. The softer labor market report weighed on the USD, prompting traders to reassess expectations for US economic momentum and potential Federal Reserve policy direction. As a result, the exchange rate moved lower, reflecting renewed demand for the Canadian Dollar in the forex market.

At the same time, rising Oil prices provided additional support for the Loonie, given Canada’s status as a major crude exporter. Stronger crude benchmarks tend to boost the Canadian Dollar by improving the country’s trade outlook and increasing foreign demand for CAD. The combination of a softer Greenback and stronger energy markets created downward pressure on the USD/CAD currency pair during the session.

In forex trading, the shift highlights how commodity dynamics and US macroeconomic data can quickly reshape currency pair movements. Market participants will continue monitoring US economic indicators and Oil price trends for further direction. If energy prices remain elevated while US data continues to soften, the Canadian Dollar could maintain strength against the USD in the near term.
