TD Securities expects the Bank of Canada to maintain its overnight rate at 2.25%, adopting a cautious stance due to weaker-than-expected growth and inflation data. With the Canadian economy running below projections, the central bank is likely to keep monetary policy steady, signaling a wait-and-see approach for the coming months. The outlook for the CAD remains uncertain, as market players monitor the impact on currency pairs such as USD/CAD and EUR/CAD.

The ongoing conflict in Iran, coupled with rising oil prices, poses new inflationary risks that could influence the BoC's policy decisions. A surge in crude prices might put upward pressure on the Canadian dollar, as oil remains a key export for Canada. However, this potential boost could be offset by broader global economic concerns, leaving the CAD vulnerable to volatility in the exchange rate, particularly against the USD and EUR.
