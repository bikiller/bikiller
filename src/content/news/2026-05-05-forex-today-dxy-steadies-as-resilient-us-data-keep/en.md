The US Dollar Index (DXY) is holding steady near the 98.50 mark, underpinned by safe-haven demand and persistently high US Treasury yields. Despite recently stronger-than-expected US economic data, trading has been choppy, with investors weighing the outlook for interest rates against ongoing geopolitical uncertainty in the Middle East. Market participants remain cautious, keeping currency pair movements in a narrow range.  

The euro-dollar (EUR/USD) exchange rate has reflected this hesitancy, hovering near key support and resistance levels as traders digest the implications of resilient US data. Rising US yields continue to support the USD against major peers, while fluctuating oil prices and regional tensions inject volatility into other FX markets. Analysts suggest that these dynamics could keep short-term trading ranges tight across most major currency pairs.  

Investors are closely monitoring how fresh headlines from the Middle East may shift safe-haven flows and influence the broader USD trend. While the DXY remains anchored, the interplay between macroeconomic indicators, central bank expectations, and geopolitical developments will likely dictate the near-term direction for USD, EUR, and other major currencies. Traders are positioned for cautious moves, with technical levels around 98.50 for the DXY acting as a focal point.
