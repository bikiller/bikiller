AUD/JPY holds its gains on Monday during Asian trading hours, hovering near 113.30, after posting slight losses in the previous session. The currency pair’s exchange rate stabilised as traders reassessed short-term positioning across major FX markets. Broader sentiment in the forex market remained mixed, with USD and EUR movements offering limited directional cues for the cross.

Support for the Australian Dollar came after China’s latest monetary policy update from the People’s Bank of China (PBoC), which reinforced expectations of steady regional liquidity conditions. As China is a key trading partner for Australia, the policy decision helped lift AUD demand, strengthening the AUD/JPY currency pair despite cautious risk appetite across Asian equities and FX trading.

Looking ahead, traders will monitor further signals from Chinese economic data and global interest rate expectations to gauge momentum in the exchange rate. The AUD/JPY pair remains sensitive to shifts in risk sentiment and commodity-linked flows, while USD and EUR fluctuations may continue to influence broader forex positioning across G10 currencies in the near term.
