AUD/USD came under pressure on Wednesday as traders reacted to softer-than-expected Australian inflation data. The release weighed on the Australian Dollar, with momentum indicators signaling weakening buying interest. The currency pair tested support near the 0.7100 level, prompting sellers to dominate early trading as investors recalibrated positions ahead of key economic updates.  

Meanwhile, the US Dollar remained supported amid ongoing uncertainty around a potential US-Iran peace deal. This geopolitical risk kept demand for safe-haven currencies elevated, limiting AUD/USD upside. Traders are closely watching exchange rate movements against other major currencies such as EUR and JPY, with market sentiment favoring USD strength in the short term.  

Technically, AUD/USD’s intraday trading shows a downward bias, with the 0.7100 support acting as a key pivot. Should the pair break below this threshold, it could attract further selling pressure, while a rebound above recent resistance levels may require renewed confidence in Australian economic data. Currency traders are weighing these dynamics as they adjust positions and monitor broader forex market momentum.
