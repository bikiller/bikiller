Eurozone inflation is expected to rise to 1.8% year-on-year in February, according to Danske Bank’s research team. Core inflation, however, remains stable at 2.2%. While national data shows mixed results, the uptick in services inflation suggests underlying price pressures, especially after a tax-induced dip in January. These inflationary trends could influence EUR/USD trading, as investors watch for further signs of inflation momentum.

The rising inflation in the Euro area comes as energy shocks continue to impact the region. The mix of rising costs and resilient demand in services could lead to greater volatility in the EUR/USD exchange rate. As energy prices drive inflation, the ECB’s monetary policy decisions will be closely scrutinized by traders, potentially affecting market sentiment and the future direction of the euro against the dollar in the coming months.
