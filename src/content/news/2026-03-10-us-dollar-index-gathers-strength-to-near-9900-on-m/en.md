The US Dollar Index (DXY) is currently trading around 98.90, edging closer to the 99.00 mark during early European trading on Tuesday. The increase in the DXY reflects rising uncertainty and ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which have driven investors towards the safety of the USD. The index has gained momentum as global markets remain on edge over the region's instability.

As traders await the release of key US CPI data, expectations for inflationary pressures continue to affect market sentiment. The upcoming figures could influence the Federal Reserve’s stance on monetary policy, with potential implications for USD strength. Currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD are particularly sensitive to these developments, as any shift in the USD could cause notable fluctuations in exchange rates across the forex market.
