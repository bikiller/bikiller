The US Dollar Index (DXY) held firm near 101.60 on Wednesday, marking a one-year high as USD buying persisted across major currency pairs. The greenback’s strength reflected cautious trading ahead of key US inflation data, with investors reducing risk exposure in forex markets, particularly in EUR/USD and other G10 currency pairs.

Market participants are closely watching Thursday’s US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) Price Index, the Federal Reserve’s preferred inflation measure, which could reshape expectations for interest rates. The US dollar has benefited from stable Treasury yields and cautious positioning, while EUR/USD and GBP/USD remain under pressure as traders favor the USD in short-term trading flows ahead of data release.

Looking ahead, the US Dollar outlook remains data-driven, with volatility likely to increase around the PCE release. A stronger-than-expected reading could reinforce USD bullish momentum, while a softer print may trigger profit-taking across major currency pairs and support a rebound in EUR/USD and other risk-sensitive FX markets in the near term.
