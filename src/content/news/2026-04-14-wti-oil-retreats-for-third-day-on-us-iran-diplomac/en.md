WTI US Oil slipped for a third consecutive session on Tuesday, with prices hovering around $89.10 per barrel, marking a 3.93% drop on the day. The retreat comes amid renewed hopes for US-Iran diplomatic engagement, easing concerns over potential supply disruptions through the Strait of Hormuz. Traders noted that bullish momentum has been capped as market participants weigh the geopolitical risks against improving dialogue.  

Currency markets reflected the shift, with the USD gaining modestly against major peers. The EUR/USD pair dipped below 1.0950, while USD/JPY held near 134.20, indicating that risk sentiment is favoring the dollar amid softer oil prices. Commodity-linked currencies such as the AUD and CAD faced pressure, with AUD/USD sliding toward 0.6600 as investors adjusted exposure to energy-linked assets.  

Analysts suggest that if diplomatic progress continues, crude could stabilize near current levels, limiting further downside. However, technical traders warn that a breach below the $88.50 support could intensify selling pressure. Market participants remain attentive to oil-driven exchange rate fluctuations, noting that sustained weakness in WTI could keep USD strength in play while pressuring commodity-sensitive currencies across forex trading desks.
