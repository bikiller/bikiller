Silver prices are showing consolidation near the $75.00 mark, slipping 0.36% as sellers cap gains around the 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at $75.98. Trading activity suggests a cautious sentiment among investors, with the USD’s relative strength against major currency pairs like EUR/USD influencing the white metal’s limited upside. Traders are closely watching the short-term trendline for signs of a breakout or further retracement.

The precious metal faces its next key resistance at the 100-day SMA of $81.00, a level that could act as a pivot point for market participants weighing long versus short positions. Exchange rates and risk sentiment in global markets remain crucial, as silver often tracks shifts in the USD, offering opportunities for forex traders to hedge or capitalize on correlated movements.

Market analysts note that the current pattern reflects a tug-of-war between bullish momentum and technical selling pressure. With XAG hovering near the 50-day SMA, momentum traders may look for signals from other currency pairs, such as USD/JPY or EUR/USD, to gauge potential directional strength. The interplay of macroeconomic data, dollar index movements, and trading flows will likely dictate whether silver can reclaim upward momentum toward the $81.00 resistance.
