Gold (XAU/USD) edged lower at the start of the week, slipping about 0.20% on Monday as investors reacted to rising crude oil prices. The uptick in oil fueled concerns of sustained inflationary pressure, prompting market participants to reassess the trajectory of central bank policy. Traders noted that higher energy costs could reinforce a “higher-for-longer” rate environment, influencing both the USD and other major currencies.  

In forex markets, the retreat in gold coincided with a firmer USD, with exchange rates showing modest strength against the EUR and other commodity-linked currencies. Analysts highlighted that technical support levels around $1,980 per ounce are under scrutiny, as any breach could trigger further selling, while safe-haven demand remains a key driver for short-term trading strategies in currency pairs.  

Crude’s rally also weighed on broader risk sentiment, prompting currency traders to monitor the impact on commodity-linked FX, including the AUD/USD and CAD/USD pairs. Market watchers noted that the Fed and other central banks might remain cautious in cutting borrowing costs, with implications for both dollar liquidity and cross-currency flows. Overall, gold’s decline reflects a delicate balance between inflation expectations, energy markets, and the evolving USD trend.
