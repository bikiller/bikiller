US Energy Secretary Chris Wright predicts the ongoing US-Israel conflict with Iran will likely conclude in the coming weeks, potentially stabilizing global oil markets. With the end of the conflict, oil supplies are expected to rebound, leading to a drop in energy prices. This anticipated recovery could significantly impact inflation rates and influence currency trading, especially in energy-dependent pairs such as USD/CAD and EUR/GBP.

As oil prices stabilize, the market may see a shift in investor sentiment, particularly for the USD, which has been volatile due to energy price fluctuations. The decline in energy costs could ease inflationary pressures, strengthening the USD against other major currencies. Traders will closely monitor the situation, with the potential for increased volatility in commodity-linked currency pairs like AUD/USD and NZD/USD.
