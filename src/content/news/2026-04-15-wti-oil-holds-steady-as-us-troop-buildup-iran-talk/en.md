WTI crude oil steadied around $89.10 on Wednesday, recovering slightly after an earlier dip to a three-week low near $85. Market participants attribute the muted movement to a mix of geopolitical tensions and supply considerations, with US troop deployments in strategic regions offsetting optimism from renewed Iran nuclear discussions. Traders are closely watching how these developments influence the USD and broader commodity-linked currency pairs.  

In forex markets, the USD remains sensitive to oil swings, with the EUR/USD trading near 1.0970 as investors weigh inflation risks against energy price pressures. Oil-linked currencies such as the CAD and NOK also saw modest trading shifts, reflecting the balance between supply concerns and easing geopolitical risk. Technical analysts note that key support around $85 could attract buying interest, while resistance near $90 may cap upside momentum in the near term.  

The stability in crude prices suggests a cautious outlook for both commodities and related currency pairs. Investors are monitoring not only the direct effects on exchange rates but also broader implications for US inflation expectations and monetary policy signaling. Continued negotiation progress with Iran or further military developments could trigger renewed volatility, making careful position management essential for traders navigating oil-sensitive currency pairs.
