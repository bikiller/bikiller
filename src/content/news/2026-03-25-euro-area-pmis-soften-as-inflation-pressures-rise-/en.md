Deutsche Bank analysts report that economic activity in the Euro Area is showing signs of slowing, with the composite PMI falling to a 10-month low. Despite remaining slightly above the 50 threshold, which signals expansion, the data suggests weakening momentum in the region. The downturn points to a potential shift in the market, with traders keeping a close eye on further economic developments.

Survey details indicate rising input prices, signaling renewed inflationary pressures. This has led to cautious trading behavior in EUR/USD, with exchange rates remaining volatile. As inflation concerns rise, the market anticipates that the European Central Bank may adjust its policy stance, which could impact the EUR in the coming weeks.
