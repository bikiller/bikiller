Gold prices extended their decline in early Wednesday trading, with XAU/USD falling below the $4,250 mark and touching around $4,235, its lowest level since March 23. The precious metal came under renewed selling pressure during the Asian session as traders reacted to escalating US-Iran tensions and reassessed risk sentiment across global financial markets. The move strengthened demand for the USD, weighing on non-yielding assets such as gold.

Market participants also adjusted positions ahead of key US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, a release closely watched for clues on the Federal Reserve’s policy path. Growing expectations that the US central bank could maintain a hawkish stance or even consider another interest rate hike this year boosted US Treasury yields and supported the greenback. As a result, the exchange rate dynamics favored the USD against major counterparts, including the EUR and other leading currency pairs.

The latest pullback highlights how sensitive gold remains to both geopolitical developments and monetary policy expectations. Traders are now focused on incoming US inflation figures, which could influence near-term trading conditions across commodities, currencies, and broader financial markets. A stronger-than-expected CPI reading may further reinforce USD strength and keep pressure on XAU/USD, while softer data could help the precious metal stabilize after its recent losses.
