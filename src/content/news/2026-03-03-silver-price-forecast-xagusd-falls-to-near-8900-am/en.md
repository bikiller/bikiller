Silver (XAG/USD) saw a sharp decline early in the Asian session on Tuesday, falling to approximately $89.25. The white metal has been highly volatile, driven by the strength of the US Dollar (USD) and rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The escalation of conflicts between the US and Israel, with strikes on Iran, has heightened market uncertainty.

The stronger USD continues to weigh on silver prices as the exchange rate reflects growing demand for safe-haven assets. Traders are closely monitoring the evolving situation in the Middle East, with risk sentiment likely to remain fragile. At current levels, silver faces significant pressure, and further declines below the $89.00 mark may signal increased bearish momentum in the near term.
