Commerzbank anticipates a gradual appreciation of the Chinese Yuan (CNY) against the US Dollar (USD) over the coming years, while the currency remains undervalued relative to most other major peers. Analyst Volkmar Baur forecasts the USD/CNY exchange rate to reach 6.90 by June 2026 and slide further to 6.70 by late 2027, suggesting steady but moderate gains for traders monitoring this key currency pair.  

The bank expects EUR/CNY to remain broadly stable, reflecting a relative balance in trading flows between Europe and China. Market participants may interpret this as a sign that the Yuan’s strength against the Dollar is unlikely to disrupt Euro-linked positions, although shifts in capital flows or monetary policy could alter the trajectory.  

Investors and forex traders should watch the USD/CNY pair closely, as the projected gradual gains highlight potential opportunities in Dollar hedging and Yuan exposure. With the Yuan still considered undervalued, movements could influence broader currency markets, particularly for commodity-linked trades and cross-border investment strategies, underlining the importance of monitoring central bank signals and macroeconomic indicators.
