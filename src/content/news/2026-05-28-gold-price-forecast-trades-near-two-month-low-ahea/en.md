Gold price (XAU/USD) extended its decline during Thursday’s European trading session, falling 1.43% to around $4,390 and hovering close to the fresh two-month low of $4,366.56 reached earlier in the day. The precious metal remained under heavy pressure as traders reduced bullish positions ahead of the US PCE Inflation data, a key indicator closely watched by the Federal Reserve for interest-rate guidance and USD exchange rate direction.

Market sentiment was also shaped by renewed tensions in the Middle East, which triggered a sharp rise in oil prices and strengthened demand for the US Dollar against major currency pairs, including EUR/USD. A firmer USD typically weighs on non-yielding assets such as gold, making the metal less attractive for overseas investors. The stronger greenback and cautious risk sentiment combined to intensify selling pressure across commodity-linked trading markets.

Investors are now focused on upcoming US inflation figures, which could influence expectations for future Federal Reserve policy moves and broader forex market volatility. If the PCE Inflation report supports a higher-for-longer interest-rate outlook, the USD may continue to gain ground against the EUR and other major currencies, potentially pushing XAU/USD below the $4,366 support zone and toward fresh technical lows.
