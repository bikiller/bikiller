West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil pulled back slightly during Thursday’s trading session, trimming part of its strong intraday advance while still holding firm above the mid-$93.00 region. Prices eased to around $94.75–$94.80 after earlier gains, but the commodity remained more than 6% higher on the day. The move reflects persistent bullish momentum in energy markets, with traders continuing to price in tightening supply conditions.

Market sentiment has been driven largely by growing concerns over potential supply disruptions, which have supported aggressive buying activity across commodity-linked assets. The sharp rally in WTI has also drawn attention in the broader forex market, where energy prices often influence currency pairs tied to commodity exports. Rising oil prices tend to support currencies such as the Canadian dollar against the USD, while also shaping global inflation expectations.

Despite the modest intraday pullback, the overall trading structure remains constructive, with prices comfortably above the $93.00 area that now acts as a near-term support level. Investors are closely watching how the energy rally may spill over into foreign exchange markets, particularly through shifts in the USD exchange rate and cross-currency flows involving the EUR and other major pairs as macro sentiment adjusts to higher commodity prices.
