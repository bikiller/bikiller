Gold (XAU/USD) remains under pressure in Friday trading, hovering just above the $4,658–$4,657 zone, a two-week low that reflects persistent bearish momentum. The metal is on track to snap a four-week winning streak as sellers dominate price action. A firmer USD exchange rate, supported by safe-haven demand, continues to weigh on the commodity-linked currency pair.

The US Dollar strengthens amid rising geopolitical tensions surrounding Iran and renewed inflation concerns, both of which are reshaping market sentiment. Traders are increasingly positioning around expectations that the Federal Reserve may keep interest rates elevated for longer, boosting Treasury yields and the USD. This dynamic reduces the appeal of non-yielding assets like gold in global trading flows.

Looking ahead, the near-term outlook for XAU/USD will hinge on incoming US inflation data and broader risk sentiment across forex markets, including EUR/USD movement. A sustained break below the $4,650 level could open the door for deeper losses, while any pullback in the USD may offer temporary relief for gold bulls attempting to stabilize the exchange rate.
