Sterling has been grinding lower against the euro, with the GBP/EUR currency pair under pressure as UK political developments weigh on sentiment. Rabobank Senior FX Strategist Jane Foley highlights that speculation around potential leadership changes involving Burnham, alongside questions about adherence to existing fiscal rules, is shaping near-term direction in the exchange rate.

Market participants are assessing how political uncertainty could influence fiscal credibility and broader GBP trading dynamics. Concerns over policy continuity are limiting demand for sterling versus the euro, while cross-currency flows remain sensitive to shifting expectations in UK governance. In broader FX markets, the dollar (USD) remains a key benchmark influencing relative currency pair positioning.

Outlook for sterling remains closely tied to developments in UK fiscal policy and leadership signals, with traders watching for clearer guidance on budget discipline. Continued uncertainty may keep the GBP/EUR exchange rate volatile in the near term, while forex investors adjust positioning across major currency pairs as macro and political risks evolve.
