The British Pound extended its rally against the Japanese Yen on Monday, pushing the GBP/JPY currency pair to a six-day winning streak. Traders cited surging Oil prices as a key driver, putting pressure on the Yen and lifting the exchange rate to around 214.87, the highest since February 4. Market participants noted that safe-haven flows into USD and EUR remain subdued, reinforcing GBP strength in Asian and European trading sessions.  

Analysts highlighted that the persistent rally reflects broader risk-on sentiment, with the Pound gaining traction amid weakening JPY demand. Technical observers pointed out that a sustained move above 215 could signal further upside potential, while traders closely monitor Oil markets for cues on the Yen’s near-term vulnerability. Currency markets overall have shown heightened volatility, with GBP/JPY outperforming other major pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY.  

Looking ahead, the interplay between energy prices and monetary expectations is likely to shape market direction. A continued rise in Brent and WTI could keep the Yen under pressure, while the Pound may benefit from positive UK data or dovish signals from the Bank of Japan. Investors are weighing whether the current bullish momentum in GBP/JPY is sustainable or a short-term correction within broader forex trends.
