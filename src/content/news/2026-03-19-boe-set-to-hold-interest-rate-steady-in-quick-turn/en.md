The Bank of England (BoE) is set to keep its key Bank Rate at 3.75% for the second consecutive meeting, after a significant shift in the economic landscape. Despite concerns over oil-driven inflation, which initially pressured the BoE to consider more aggressive action, recent data indicates that the macroeconomic environment has cooled, prompting a pause in tightening measures. 

The GBP/USD exchange rate has remained sensitive to these developments, with traders closely monitoring BoE decisions for further direction. The EUR/GBP pair has seen fluctuations, reflecting market uncertainty and shifting expectations surrounding the BoE's stance. As the global inflationary pressure eases, BoE’s decision to hold could signal a cautious approach, balancing between growth concerns and price stability.
