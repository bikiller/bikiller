Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret report that the British Pound (GBP) is trading softer against the US Dollar (USD), with the GBP/USD currency pair under modest pressure. The move follows mildly disappointing UK Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI) and CBI data, although overall market reaction has remained relatively muted across forex trading desks.

Fiscal worries continue to cap the Pound’s recovery attempts, as investors reassess UK budget dynamics and growth prospects. Sterling sentiment remains fragile, limiting upside in the GBP/USD exchange rate even during periods of broader USD consolidation. Trading activity suggests a cautious tone, with participants reluctant to build strong bullish positions.

Looking ahead, traders are watching whether upcoming UK data or shifts in US dollar sentiment can provide clearer direction for the currency pair. For now, GBP remains sensitive to fiscal headlines and macroeconomic releases, while EUR and other major currencies show relatively steadier positioning against the USD in broader forex markets.
