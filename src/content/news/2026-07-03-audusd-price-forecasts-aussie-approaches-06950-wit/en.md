The AUD/USD currency pair extends its advance on Friday, rising for a second consecutive session as the Australian Dollar strengthens against a broadly weaker US Dollar. The exchange rate is edging toward the 0.6950 level, with bullish momentum building after Thursday’s disappointing US Nonfarm Payrolls report undermined USD demand across forex trading markets.

USD weakness persists after payrolls miss, reducing expectations for near-term tightening and prompting repositioning in currency trading. Investors are rotating into higher-yielding assets like the Australian Dollar, supporting the AUD/USD exchange rate. Broader forex sentiment remains cautious, though risk appetite improves slightly, with EUR/USD also finding modest support amid softer USD conditions ahead of upcoming data releases.

From a technical standpoint, the AUD/USD pair now tests resistance near 0.6950, a key level that could determine near-term direction. A sustained break higher may signal further upside momentum in forex trading, while failure to breach it could trigger consolidation. Traders remain focused on US Dollar dynamics and broader risk sentiment across major currency pairs.
