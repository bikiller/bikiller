The USD/JPY currency pair is hovering near the psychologically significant 160.00 level on Friday, holding steady after bouncing off intraday lows. Traders reacted to a stronger-than-expected US employment report, which bolstered the US dollar against the Japanese yen. The exchange rate’s resilience around this level signals market sensitivity to any signs of intervention from Japanese authorities.

The upbeat US jobs data renewed demand for USD, prompting trading activity across major pairs, including USD/EUR and USD/JPY. Market participants are weighing the impact of higher US employment on interest rate expectations, with the yen facing pressure as investors seek returns in dollar-denominated assets. Technical traders are watching the 160.00 mark closely as a potential trigger for central bank response.

While the USD/JPY has rebounded, volatility remains elevated as investors digest macroeconomic data and geopolitical developments. Any sustained move above 160.00 could prompt intervention or speculative hedging, influencing broader forex market flows. Meanwhile, the dollar's performance against other major currencies like the euro reflects ongoing concerns over inflation and economic growth dynamics in the US.
