USD trading is under pressure as inflation dynamics continue to shape Federal Reserve policy expectations. DBS Group Research economist Eugene Leow notes that the consensus anticipates US CPI at 2.4% year-on-year and 0.3% month-on-month for February, leaving markets highly sensitive to any upside surprises. Stronger-than-expected inflation could constrain Fed easing, supporting the dollar, while softer data may fuel USD weakness across major currency pairs.  

The EUR/USD exchange rate has shown volatility amid these dynamics, with traders closely watching how CPI outcomes influence market positioning. A higher-than-expected print could trigger a rebound in USD trading, potentially pushing EUR/USD lower toward key technical support levels, while muted inflation would likely weigh on the greenback, allowing the euro to gain traction in forex markets.  

Investors are navigating USD moves with caution, weighing inflation risks against broader economic signals. Short-term trading strategies are likely to focus on CPI surprises and their implications for interest rate expectations, with ripple effects across pairs such as USD/JPY and GBP/USD. Market participants remain alert for shifts that could recalibrate exchange rates and influence global currency flows.
