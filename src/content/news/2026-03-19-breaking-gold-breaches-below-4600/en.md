Gold tumbled below the critical $4,600 per troy ounce level on Thursday, extending its decline to a seventh consecutive day and marking fresh multi-month lows. The precious metal is facing sustained selling pressure as the US dollar strengthens, with the USD index pushing higher and weighing on gold’s appeal as an alternative asset. Traders noted increased volatility in spot trading, with momentum favoring further downside.  

The firming USD has influenced key currency pairs, particularly EUR/USD, which continues to hover near its recent lows, reflecting broad dollar strength. Analysts link gold’s slide to rising Treasury yields and hawkish sentiment from US Federal Reserve officials, which bolsters the USD and makes non-yielding assets like gold less attractive. Traders remain cautious ahead of major economic data releases that could further sway exchange rates and trading sentiment.  

Market participants are watching technical levels closely, with $4,600 now acting as a crucial support for gold. Breaching this threshold may trigger additional stop-loss orders and prompt further selling. Meanwhile, EUR/USD and other major currency pairs are being closely monitored for spillover effects, as the correlation between currency strength and commodity prices continues to guide trading decisions in both forex and metals markets.
