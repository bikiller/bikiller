Silver prices (XAG/USD) extended their losses, dipping to around $72.90 during the Asian session on Thursday. The precious metal’s decline reflects waning safe-haven demand as market sentiment improves, particularly with rising optimism surrounding potential peace in the Middle East. This optimism has diminished investor interest in assets like silver that typically benefit during periods of uncertainty.

As silver continues to slide, market participants are adjusting their expectations, focusing on global economic recovery signs. The latest retreat brings the currency pair closer to a critical support level of $72.00, highlighting the ongoing shifts in risk sentiment. Should the downtrend persist, traders will likely monitor further developments in Middle Eastern peace talks for additional price direction.
