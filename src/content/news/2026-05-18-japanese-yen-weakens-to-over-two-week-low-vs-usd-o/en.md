The Japanese yen slipped to a more than two-week low against the US dollar on Monday, as escalating tensions in the Middle East drove safe-haven flows toward the USD. The USD/JPY currency pair climbed past the 159.00 level, continuing its streak of gains for the sixth consecutive day and marking seven positive sessions in the last eight trading days. Market participants cited geopolitical risks as the key driver behind the yen’s retreat.

Trading activity in Asia pushed USD/JPY to a two-and-a-half-week high during the session, reflecting persistent demand for the greenback amid broader risk-off sentiment. Analysts note that the pair’s momentum is reinforced by recent US economic data, which has bolstered expectations for sustained dollar strength relative to major peers like the EUR and JPY. Technical observers highlight the significance of the 159.00 threshold as a pivotal resistance level that traders are closely monitoring.

The weaker yen has implications for export-driven markets and currency trading strategies, as investors reassess positions amid global uncertainties. With the USD showing resilience across key currency pairs, trading desks anticipate heightened volatility in the short term, particularly as geopolitical developments unfold. Momentum indicators suggest that USD/JPY could test higher resistance levels if current trends persist, while EUR/JPY and other cross rates may also reflect shifts in risk sentiment.
