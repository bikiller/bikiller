WTI crude oil prices fell more than 3% on Monday, dipping from an opening level near $100 per barrel to close below $95. The decline was driven by a cooling of the Iran risk premium, which had previously kept prices elevated. Traders saw less immediate threat from potential disruptions to supply, prompting a wave of profit-taking and selling pressure throughout the trading session.

The drop in WTI crude also impacted currency markets, with the USD gaining strength against oil-sensitive currencies like the Canadian dollar (CAD) and the Norwegian krone (NOK). As oil prices retreated, the USD's appeal grew, strengthening its position in the exchange rate. The shift in market sentiment suggests that traders are recalibrating their expectations for future oil price moves, influencing broader forex trends.
