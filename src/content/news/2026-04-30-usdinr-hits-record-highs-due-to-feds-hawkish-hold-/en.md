The USD/INR currency pair surged to record highs on Thursday, with the Indian Rupee hitting fresh lows against the US Dollar at the opening bell. Traders reacted sharply to the Federal Reserve’s hawkish stance, signaling no immediate cuts to interest rates, which reinforced demand for the USD. The exchange rate breached key psychological levels, intensifying trading activity as the Rupee struggled to maintain stability.  

Rising crude oil prices added further pressure on the INR, given India’s heavy reliance on imports. The spike in oil costs has widened the trade deficit, forcing investors to reassess positions in emerging market currencies. Against major counterparts like the EUR, the USD maintained strength, reflecting global market caution and the appeal of safe-haven assets amid persistent macroeconomic uncertainty.  

Market analysts suggest the combination of Fed policy and external commodity shocks could prolong volatility in the USD/INR pair. Short-term traders are closely watching resistance and support levels, while importers and exporters recalibrate strategies to manage currency risk. The broader implication points to heightened hedging activity and potential intervention measures by the Reserve Bank of India if downward pressure on the INR persists.
