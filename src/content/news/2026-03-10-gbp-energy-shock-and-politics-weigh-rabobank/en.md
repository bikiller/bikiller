The British Pound has shown resilience in recent trading, but UK risks remain significant, according to Rabobank's Senior FX Strategist, Jane Foley. Despite this, she cautions that an energy shock could drive UK inflation higher by an additional 65 basis points (bps) by mid-year, putting pressure on the Bank of England (BoE) to limit any further easing. This scenario could hinder the GBP's ability to rally further against major currencies like the USD and EUR.

The ongoing geopolitical tensions and energy concerns continue to weigh on the GBP/USD exchange rate, which has been volatile. Market participants are closely watching the BoE's response to inflationary pressures, as these risks could keep the central bank cautious in its approach to policy adjustments. As energy costs surge, the UK’s economic outlook remains uncertain, keeping traders on edge as they navigate potential shifts in the currency pair's direction.
