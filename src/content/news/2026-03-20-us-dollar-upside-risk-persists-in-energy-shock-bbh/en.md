Renewed risk aversion has lifted the US Dollar across major currency pairs, supported by gains in oil prices and US Treasury yields, while equities remain under pressure. Analysts at Brown Brothers Harriman, led by Elias Haddad, highlight that recent political remarks temporarily steadied market sentiment, but the underlying energy shock continues to fuel upside risk for the USD. Trading volumes remain steady, with investors eyeing safe-haven flows.  

The EUR/USD exchange rate has edged lower, reflecting broad USD strength, while oil prices continue their rebound, amplifying concerns over inflation and policy responses. Bond yields are climbing, reinforcing the appeal of the Dollar in forex markets. Market participants are balancing these developments against the absence of major US economic data this week, keeping focus on Fed officials’ comments for guidance on future monetary policy.  

Traders are watching technical levels closely, with USD/JPY testing recent highs and EUR/USD approaching support near $1.0850. The interplay between energy-driven inflation concerns and risk sentiment is likely to dictate short-term forex movements. With no significant releases on the calendar, central bank rhetoric and market reactions to geopolitical events remain key drivers for the USD and its cross rates.
