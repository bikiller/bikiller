WTI crude oil prices surged over 6% on Tuesday, crossing the $75 per barrel threshold, driven by heightened tensions in the US-Iran conflict. The risk of oil supply disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical global shipping route, has sparked concerns among traders. This spike in crude prices has significantly impacted the USD, pushing up the value of the currency as investors flock to safe-haven assets.

The market’s reaction to these geopolitical developments has led to increased volatility across global financial markets. The EUR/USD exchange rate saw downward pressure as the USD strengthened in response to the oil price rally. As the situation evolves, oil traders and forex markets are bracing for further fluctuations, with oil prices potentially reaching new highs if the conflict intensifies.
