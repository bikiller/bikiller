The US dollar rally paused on Thursday, June 25, as traders positioned ahead of critical US inflation data. The USD, which had strengthened across major currency pairs in recent sessions, showed signs of consolidation in forex trading. Investors reduced aggressive USD long positions, awaiting clarity on inflation trends that could influence Federal Reserve policy expectations and near-term exchange rate direction.

Major currency pairs such as EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and USD/JPY traded within tight ranges as volatility eased. The euro stabilized against the USD after recent declines, while the yen remained under pressure amid cautious risk sentiment. Forex traders focused on intraday support and resistance zones, with price action reflecting hesitation rather than directional conviction in the exchange rate markets.

Market participants now look to US inflation figures as the key catalyst for the next move in USD trading. A stronger-than-expected reading could revive the dollar rally and push it back toward recent resistance levels, while softer data may support a rebound in EUR and other majors. Overall forex market sentiment remains data-driven, with volatility expected to rise following the release.
