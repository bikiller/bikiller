The USD/JPY pair has encountered a slowdown in its upward momentum following the landslide victory of Japan's Liberal Democratic Party (LDP) in the Lower House elections. According to MUFG's Teppei Ino, the market had already priced in expectations surrounding the "Takaichi trade" and potential intervention warnings, leading to a stabilization of the exchange rate. This has resulted in diminished pressure for the Dollar against the Yen as of early March.

Despite the pause in the Dollar’s advance, the outlook for the USD/JPY remains cautiously bullish. The current trading environment reflects a delicate balance, with the market closely monitoring potential government actions. The pair’s movement hinges on any further intervention signals or changes in broader market sentiment, which could reignite volatility and push the exchange rate beyond recent technical levels. Traders will be watching for clear indicators of policy shifts in the coming weeks.
