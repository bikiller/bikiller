Expectations for the Federal Reserve’s easing cycle in 2026 have been trimmed as US inflation shows unexpected resilience, according to ING’s James Knightley. Strong near-term price pressures and steady economic growth are making early rate cuts less likely, prompting traders to adjust USD positions. The repricing has influenced the dollar across major currency pairs, with the USD seeing renewed strength against the EUR, JPY, and GBP in recent sessions.  

In forex trading, the EUR/USD pair has reacted sharply, with the exchange rate testing support near 1.0800. Similarly, USD/JPY has climbed toward 150.50, reflecting the market’s anticipation of a less accommodative Fed. Knightley emphasizes that the revised expectations are shifting sentiment, with traders recalibrating hedges and positioning for a stronger greenback amid persistent inflation and resilient US growth data.  

The implications extend to broader currency markets, where central bank divergence and relative growth trajectories are driving flows. Emerging market currencies have come under pressure as the USD gains traction, while EUR and GBP traders are weighing the Fed’s stance against the ECB and BoE. Overall, USD trading remains closely linked to inflation dynamics, with technical levels around 1.0800 in EUR/USD and 150.50 in USD/JPY serving as focal points for market participants.
