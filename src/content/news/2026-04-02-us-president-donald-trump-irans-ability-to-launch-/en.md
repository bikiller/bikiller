US President Donald Trump stated Thursday that Iran’s capability to launch missiles and drones has been significantly curtailed, a development that could ease geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The remarks lifted risk sentiment in global markets, prompting traders to adjust positions in key currency pairs. The USD found support against the EUR and JPY, as investors weighed the implications for oil supply and regional stability, while Brent crude prices edged slightly lower on reduced geopolitical risk.  

Trump also emphasized that the United States does not require oil from the Middle East, signaling potential resilience in energy markets. This reassured investors, leading to a modest rally in the USD, with the EUR/USD exchange rate testing support near 1.0800. Safe-haven currencies such as the Swiss franc and Japanese yen retraced earlier gains, reflecting reduced demand amid lower perceived geopolitical threats.  

Traders remain focused on the broader impact of US-Iran relations on currency movements and commodity-linked flows. The restrained Iranian missile and drone capacity could lessen oil market volatility, influencing USD trading patterns and cross-currency flows. Market participants are monitoring technical levels closely, with USD/JPY hovering near 136.50 and EUR/USD eyeing a potential rebound if risk sentiment continues to improve. This backdrop underscores the intersection of geopolitics and forex dynamics in shaping exchange rates.
