Silver (XAG/USD) rallied sharply on Friday, climbing to around $82.60, marking a 5.40% gain as the US Dollar (USD) softened across major currency pairs. The currency’s retreat fueled buying in precious metals, with traders reacting to a renewed expectation that the Federal Reserve may ease interest rates sooner than previously anticipated. This surge pushed silver toward its recent technical highs, signaling increased market appetite amid shifting monetary sentiment.  

The exchange rate movement reflects broader currency market trends, with the USD retreating against the euro (EUR) and other major currencies. Analysts note that this weakness in the greenback has enhanced silver’s appeal as an alternative store of value, driving robust trading volumes. Market participants are monitoring how inflation data and Fed commentary will shape further USD performance, which could sustain or reverse the recent metal rally.  

Traders are now watching key support and resistance levels closely. With XAG/USD breaking above psychological thresholds near $80, a test of $85 could be on the horizon if the USD continues to underperform. Conversely, any rebound in the dollar could pressure silver prices, creating a volatile trading environment. Investors remain attuned to currency pair dynamics, central bank signals, and risk sentiment as drivers of short-term silver market behavior.
