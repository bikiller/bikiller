Silver (XAG/USD) continues to trade under pressure on Thursday, holding a bearish bias as it struggles below the $75 mark. The currency pair’s downside momentum is influenced by broader market caution, with investors weighing geopolitical tensions from the ongoing US–Israel conflict with Iran. These macro headwinds are fueling a risk-off sentiment, driving demand for the USD while weighing on silver’s appeal as a safe-haven asset.  

Technical indicators show XAG/USD struggling to reclaim key resistance levels, suggesting continued vulnerability in the near term. Traders are closely watching the exchange rate for potential support around $74.50, with a sustained breach possibly opening the door to further declines. The current trading pattern reflects a cautious stance among market participants, who remain wary of escalating geopolitical risks and their potential impact on commodity-linked currencies.  

The silver market’s performance is also intertwined with broader forex trends, as USD strength exerts additional pressure on XAG/USD. Investors are balancing exposure across major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD while recalibrating positions in commodities. As sentiment remains sensitive to geopolitical developments, trading strategies may shift toward defensive positions, highlighting silver’s role as both a hedge and a reflection of broader risk appetite in the forex market.
