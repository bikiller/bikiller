Japanese Yen (JPY) staged a rebound on Thursday, recovering from levels near four-decade lows against major peers, including the USD/JPY currency pair. The move came without any direct catalyst from the Bank of Japan (BoJ), surprising traders who had been closely watching policy signals. Exchange rate action suggested shifting positioning in a market heavily skewed toward yen weakness.

Sentiment shifted after Tokyo appeared less explicit in verbal guidance, with officials no longer “telegraphing their punches” on potential intervention. That reduced certainty around the yen trade and triggered short-covering across yen pairs. Traders in USD, EUR, and JPY crosses reacted to the prospect of stealthier policy responses, alongside broader dollar-driven volatility.

Despite the rebound, underlying fundamentals still favor a structurally weak yen in FX trading, driven by persistent interest rate differentials versus the US dollar and euro. Market participants remain alert to sharp swings in USD/JPY and EUR/JPY exchange rates, especially near key psychological levels. The outlook now hinges on whether Japanese authorities tolerate volatility or step in more decisively in currency markets.
