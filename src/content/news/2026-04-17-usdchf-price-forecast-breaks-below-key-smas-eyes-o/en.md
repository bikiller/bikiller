The USD/CHF currency pair closed the week lower, down 0.87% overall, and dropped 0.27% on the final trading day. The exchange rate faced pressure as market sentiment shifted positively toward a potential US-Iran deal over the weekend. Traders reacted to this optimism, which dampened demand for the US dollar and supported the Swiss franc.

Technical indicators also showed a bearish trend, with USD/CHF breaking below key simple moving averages (SMAs). The pair now eyes the 0.7800 support level, a critical threshold that could signal further declines if breached. As markets digest the implications of global events, traders will closely monitor any developments that may influence the USD's strength against the franc.
