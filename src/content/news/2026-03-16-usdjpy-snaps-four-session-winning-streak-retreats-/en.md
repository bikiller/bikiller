USD/JPY retreated to the 159.00 area on Monday, ending a four-day winning streak as the currency pair lost roughly 0.4%. After a period of steady gains, traders took profits, and the exchange rate pulled back amid otherwise subdued market conditions. The pullback reflects a temporary pause in the USD’s recent strength against the yen, with market participants cautious ahead of upcoming economic data.  

Trading activity was generally muted, with the yen finding support near the psychologically significant 159.00 level. Analysts noted that short-term technical resistance around recent highs may have triggered the reversal, while the broader USD trend remained supported by steady investor appetite. Movements in other major currencies, including EUR/USD, suggested a cautious tone across the forex market, keeping volatility low.  

The drop in USD/JPY may influence trading strategies in the near term, as investors assess whether the pullback is a minor correction or a sign of a more extended consolidation phase. With no major economic releases driving the market, momentum-driven trading dominated, highlighting the sensitivity of currency pairs to even modest shifts in sentiment and positioning. Traders are watching for signs of renewed strength or further declines around key technical thresholds.
