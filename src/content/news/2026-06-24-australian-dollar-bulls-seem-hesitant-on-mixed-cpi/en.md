AUD/USD attracted modest buying interest during the Asian session on Wednesday after softer-than-expected Australian consumer inflation data, though Australian dollar bulls remained hesitant. The currency pair continues to hover near its April lows against the USD as traders reassess the inflation outlook and its implications for monetary policy, keeping trading sentiment cautious across the forex market.

Price action showed the AUD/USD reversing part of the previous day’s slump, climbing back toward the 0.6900 level, which also marks its lowest point since early April. The move followed the inflation release and reflected a tentative stabilization in the currency pair, as traders weighed whether softer CPI could limit future upside in Australian yields and pressure the Australian dollar versus the USD.

Looking ahead, the currency pair remains sensitive to shifts in USD strength and broader risk sentiment, with EUR and other majors also reflecting cautious trading conditions. Market participants will closely monitor further Australian data and central bank commentary, as the mixed CPI reading keeps expectations for the Reserve Bank of Australia’s policy path uncertain and caps conviction among AUD bulls.
