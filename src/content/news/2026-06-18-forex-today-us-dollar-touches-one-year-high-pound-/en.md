The US Dollar extended its advance on Thursday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) firming near 100.80, its strongest level in over a year since May 2025. The move followed the Federal Reserve’s decision to keep interest rates unchanged at 3.50%-3.75% during Wednesday’s policy meeting, the first chaired by Kevin Warsh, reinforcing expectations of a steady USD funding backdrop across major currency pairs.

Stronger USD pressured major currency pairs, with EUR/USD and GBP/USD edging lower in early trading. The Pound slipped after the Bank of England maintained its policy stance, as traders reassessed interest rate differentials against a firming US yield outlook. Market participants noted reduced appetite for risk-sensitive currencies amid persistent dollar demand across forex markets.

Looking ahead, traders will watch whether the DXY can sustain momentum above the 100.80 threshold, which now acts as a near-term reference level for USD strength. Any break higher could reinforce bullish positioning in the dollar, while softer rate expectations or improved risk sentiment may cap gains across major forex currency pairs.
