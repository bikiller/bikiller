Central and Eastern European (CEE) currencies came under renewed pressure as a stronger US Dollar and softer oil prices weighed on sentiment, according to ING strategist Frantisek Taborsky. The shift in global exchange rate dynamics has tightened conditions across regional FX markets, reinforcing USD strength and weakening risk-sensitive currencies in the basket.

Taborsky notes that currency pairs such as EUR/PLN and EUR/CZK are increasingly influenced by repricing of interest rate expectations. Markets have largely outpriced further rate hikes in Poland and the Czech Republic, reducing support for local currencies. This adjustment in trading expectations has amplified sensitivity to external drivers, particularly USD direction and commodity-linked inputs like oil.

With the US Dollar maintaining upward momentum, regional FX trading faces persistent headwinds, as lower oil further dampens inflation-linked support. The combination reduces carry appeal and leaves CEE currencies more vulnerable to volatility. Investors now watch EUR and USD flows closely, with broader implications for exchange rate stability and positioning across emerging European currency markets.
