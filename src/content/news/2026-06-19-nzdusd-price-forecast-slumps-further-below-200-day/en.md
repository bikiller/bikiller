The NZD/USD currency pair extends its decline for a third consecutive trading session, reflecting sustained bearish pressure against the US Dollar. The exchange rate has fallen more than 1.48% over the week after touching a two-month low at 0.5722. It currently trades at 0.5738, down 0.25% on the day, as sellers maintain control in short-term trading.

Technically, the pair has slipped below its 200-day simple moving average, reinforcing a bearish outlook in the broader forex trend. The break signals weakening momentum for the New Zealand Dollar as USD demand strengthens across major currency pairs. Immediate focus remains on the psychological 0.57 support level, which could be tested if selling pressure persists in upcoming sessions.

Market participants remain cautious as the NZD/USD exchange rate struggles under sustained USD strength, with traders watching whether the pair can stabilize above the 0.57 handle. Broader risk sentiment in forex markets, including EUR and other major currencies, continues to favor the US Dollar, leaving the New Zealand Dollar vulnerable in near-term trading conditions.
