The EUR/GBP currency pair edged lower toward the 0.8650–0.8660 zone in early European trading on Thursday, as the Euro came under pressure following weaker-than-expected German Retail Sales data. The soft figures reinforced concerns about sluggish consumer demand in the Eurozone’s largest economy, weighing on the EUR exchange rate against a relatively steady Pound.

Market participants are also positioning cautiously ahead of upcoming policy decisions from both the European Central Bank and the Bank of England. Expectations around interest rate guidance are driving near-term trading flows, with investors closely watching signals on inflation and growth. The EUR/GBP pair remains sensitive to any divergence in monetary policy outlooks between the two central banks.

Broader currency markets, including USD crosses, reflect a cautious tone as traders assess global economic momentum. The Pound has found modest support amid resilient UK data, while the Euro struggles to gain traction. In the near term, the 0.8650 level acts as a key technical support, with further downside possible if bearish sentiment persists.
