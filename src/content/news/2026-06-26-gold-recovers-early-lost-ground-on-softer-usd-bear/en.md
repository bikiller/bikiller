Gold (XAU/USD) recovers earlier losses on Friday, building on a modest intraday rebound from the $3,983–$3,982 support region. The precious metal pushes toward the upper end of its daily trading range as the European session approaches, with exchange rate action reflecting a softer USD backdrop across major currency pairs in early Friday trading conditions.

Despite the rebound, the broader bias for XAU/USD remains tilted to the downside as expectations of further Federal Reserve interest rate hikes support the US dollar. Higher yields weigh on gold's appeal, limiting upside momentum even as short-term trading flows stabilize around intraday support levels across major currency pairs in the forex market.

Market participants will continue to monitor USD direction and incoming US economic data for fresh cues on the exchange rate outlook. EUR and other major currency pairs may also influence risk sentiment, but gold trading remains sensitive to Fed policy expectations, with volatility likely to persist within the current range-bound structure.
