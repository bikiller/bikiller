GBP/USD continued its downward trajectory on Thursday, driven by a combination of geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and stronger-than-expected US jobs data. As traders anticipate Friday's Nonfarm Payrolls report, the solid employment figures gave the US Dollar an added boost, pushing the exchange rate lower. At the time of writing, GBP/USD is trading at 1.3337, down by 0.25% on the day.

The strengthening USD has pressured the British Pound, which faces a challenging environment ahead of key economic data. With the Nonfarm Payrolls report looming, traders are closely monitoring any further signs of strength in the US labor market, which could further weigh on the GBP. As the market adjusts, the outlook for the GBP/USD currency pair remains tilted toward further downside risks, barring a shift in the broader market sentiment.
