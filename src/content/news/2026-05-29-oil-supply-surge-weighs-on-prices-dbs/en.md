Philip Wee of DBS Group Research said Brent and WTI crude prices have come under significant pressure as traders increasingly price in a surge of supply from Gulf producers. Expectations of ample output have weakened sentiment across commodity markets, prompting a broad pullback in oil benchmarks. The decline reflects growing confidence that supply will remain sufficient despite ongoing geopolitical uncertainties and concerns over the global demand outlook.

The sharp fall in oil prices has also reverberated across currency markets, influencing trading activity in major currency pairs and commodity-linked exchange rates. Lower crude prices tend to weigh on oil-exporting economies and their currencies, while providing support to net energy importers. As a result, forex participants are closely monitoring the USD, EUR, and other major currencies for signs of shifting capital flows driven by changing energy market dynamics.

Looking ahead, the prospect of abundant Gulf supply could continue to cap oil price gains and shape broader market positioning. Investors are expected to remain focused on production trends, inventory data, and macroeconomic developments that could affect both energy demand and currency valuation. The interplay between oil prices, the USD exchange rate, and global risk sentiment is likely to remain a key theme for traders in the weeks ahead.
