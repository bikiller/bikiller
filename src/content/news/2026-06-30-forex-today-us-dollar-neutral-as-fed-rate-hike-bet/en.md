The US Dollar Index (DXY) traded on a neutral footing near the 101.20 level on Tuesday as traders weighed mixed United States economic data alongside hawkish Federal Reserve commentary. In forex trading, the USD held steady against major currencies, with overall exchange rate movements subdued as market participants reassessed short-term direction.

Rate hike expectations from the Fed continued to underpin the US dollar, as hawkish remarks suggested inflation concerns remain unresolved. Currency pair movements such as EUR/USD reflected limited momentum, with traders cautious ahead of further US data releases that could influence near-term USD demand and broader foreign exchange positioning in global markets.

Looking ahead, the USD remains supported as long as rate hike bets persist, keeping the DXY around key technical levels like 101.20. In currency trading, investors are likely to monitor incoming US macro data and Fed guidance for clearer direction, with volatility in major currency pairs expected to stay contained in the near term.
