The Japanese Yen (JPY) continues to underperform against major currencies, with the USD/JPY pair rising to 157.75 in late Asian trading on Friday. This marks a notable increase in the pair's value, reflecting growing uncertainty around the Bank of Japan's (BoJ) policy stance amidst ongoing geopolitical tensions, particularly the escalating conflict in Iran.

The yen's weakness comes as global risk sentiment is impacted by the geopolitical situation, which could lead to shifts in monetary policy. As the BoJ faces pressure to adapt to these changes, traders are reacting by pushing the USD/JPY pair higher. With the exchange rate nearing key resistance levels, further volatility in the yen is expected, depending on the progression of global events and BoJ policy moves.
