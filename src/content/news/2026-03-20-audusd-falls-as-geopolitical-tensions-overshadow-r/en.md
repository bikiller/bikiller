The AUD/USD currency pair traded lower at 0.7040 on Friday, reflecting a 0.68% drop for the day. The Australian dollar's gains, bolstered by a recent rate hike by the Reserve Bank of Australia, were overshadowed by a resurgence in safe-haven demand for the US dollar. Despite strong domestic economic factors supporting the AUD, global geopolitical tensions weighed heavily on market sentiment, leading to the downward pressure.

As the pair continues to face downward momentum, traders are focusing on the broader risk-off environment that has dominated the markets. This shift in sentiment has led to a preference for the US dollar, traditionally viewed as a safer asset during times of geopolitical uncertainty. With global market volatility rising, the AUD/USD exchange rate may struggle to regain its footing unless the risk appetite returns or further positive domestic data emerges from Australia.
