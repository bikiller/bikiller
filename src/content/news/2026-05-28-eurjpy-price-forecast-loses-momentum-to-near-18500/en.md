The EUR/JPY currency pair lost momentum during Thursday’s early European trading session, slipping toward the 185.10 area as demand for the Japanese Yen strengthened. Rising tensions surrounding the US-Iran conflict boosted safe-haven flows into the JPY, weighing on the exchange rate despite broader resilience in the Euro. The pair nevertheless remained close to the psychological 185.00 level, suggesting traders are still cautious about shifting away from bullish positioning.

Market participants continued to monitor geopolitical developments alongside expectations for monetary policy signals from both the Eurozone and Japan. Increased risk aversion encouraged investors to rotate into defensive assets, limiting upside momentum for EUR/JPY and creating short-term pressure on the cross. At the same time, steady EUR demand and softer sentiment around the USD helped prevent deeper losses during European trading hours.

From a technical perspective, the broader bias for EUR/JPY remains bullish even after the latest pullback. The currency pair continues to trade above key support zones near 185.00, indicating that buyers remain active on dips. Traders will likely watch whether the pair can stabilize above current levels before attempting another move higher, while further escalation in Middle East tensions could continue supporting the Japanese Yen in the near term.
