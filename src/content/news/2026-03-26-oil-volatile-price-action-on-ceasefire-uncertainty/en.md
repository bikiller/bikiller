Oil prices surged about 2% as markets grappled with uncertainty over a potential US-Iran ceasefire. NYMEX WTI and ICE Brent bounced around the $100/bbl mark, reflecting investor caution amid mixed signals from Washington and Tehran. The sharp swings in crude have fueled broader risk sentiment, influencing USD pairs, particularly USD/EUR and USD/JPY, as traders weigh geopolitical risk against central bank policies.  

Energy-related currencies like the CAD and NOK also saw heightened volatility, tracking crude’s swings. The Brent-WTI spread remained a key focus, with traders watching technical levels near $100 for signs of further momentum. Forex traders adjusting positions in EUR/USD and GBP/USD reflected growing caution, balancing oil-driven inflation expectations with potential shifts in interest rate outlooks.  

Short-term trading has been dominated by reactionary flows, as investors seek safe havens amid geopolitical ambiguity. Analysts caution that sustained price swings could trigger broader USD liquidity adjustments, affecting cross-currency exchange rates. Market participants are keeping an eye on both geopolitical headlines and technical charts, with oil’s behavior increasingly tied to currency volatility in global trading.
