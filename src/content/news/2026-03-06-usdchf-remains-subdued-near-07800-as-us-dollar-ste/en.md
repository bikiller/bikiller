USD/CHF held steady near the 0.7800 mark on Friday as the US Dollar consolidated recent gains. After climbing 0.25% in the previous session, the currency pair edged slightly lower, trading around 0.7810 during Asian hours. Market participants cited a cautious tone ahead of key US economic data, with traders weighing the outlook for interest rates and broader risk sentiment across forex markets.  

The exchange rate’s subdued movement reflects a broader stabilization in the USD against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Analysts noted that the pair’s modest pullback came after sharp short-term gains, suggesting that profit-taking and technical resistance near current levels are tempering further upside. Liquidity in the Asian session remained light, keeping the trading range narrow.  

Investors continue to monitor macroeconomic indicators, including inflation data and central bank guidance, for clues on future USD momentum. Any shift in sentiment could trigger renewed volatility in USD/CHF and related currency pairs. Traders are advised to watch key support near 0.7800 and resistance levels around 0.7850, as these technical zones may guide near-term trading strategies in the forex market.
