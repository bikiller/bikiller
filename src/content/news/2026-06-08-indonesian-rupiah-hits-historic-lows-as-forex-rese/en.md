The Indonesian Rupiah continued its slide on Monday, with the USD/IDR currency pair hitting a record high of 18,210 during Asian trading hours before stabilizing around 18,200. This marks the fifth consecutive day of gains for the US dollar against the Rupiah, reflecting growing concerns over Indonesia's dwindling foreign exchange reserves. Market participants cite persistent capital outflows and rising global interest rates as key pressures on the local currency.

Traders note that the exchange rate volatility has intensified, with the Rupiah underperforming against both the USD and major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Analysts suggest that the central bank may face heightened pressure to intervene in the forex market to stem further depreciation, especially as investor sentiment remains cautious. The weakening Rupiah raises the cost of imports and could stoke inflationary pressures in the coming months.

The continued sell-off underscores the vulnerability of emerging-market currencies amid global monetary tightening. Forex strategists are closely monitoring technical levels, with 18,200 acting as a critical threshold for market reactions. Sustained USD strength may trigger further Rupiah losses, influencing cross-border investment flows and trading strategies across the region.
