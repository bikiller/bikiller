The Japanese Yen struggled to gain meaningful traction on Monday, even as the US Dollar remained under pressure following a bearish gap at the weekly open in the USD/JPY currency pair. The exchange rate stabilized in early trading as investors reacted to growing optimism surrounding potential progress in negotiations between the United States and Iran, a development that could eventually lead to the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz and ease global supply concerns.

Despite the softer USD tone across broader forex markets, elevated energy costs continued to weigh heavily on the Japanese currency. Japan’s dependence on imported fuel kept sentiment toward the Yen fragile, limiting downside momentum in USD/JPY and encouraging cautious trading activity. Market participants also monitored movements in EUR/USD and other major currency pairs for broader direction as geopolitical headlines continued to influence risk appetite and safe-haven flows.

Traders now expect near-term volatility in the USD/JPY exchange rate as shifting expectations around energy prices, Federal Reserve policy, and geopolitical risks shape market positioning. While a weaker US Dollar typically supports the Yen, concerns over Japan’s trade balance and higher import costs have reduced demand for the currency. Investors remain focused on whether improving global risk sentiment can sustain pressure on the USD in the sessions ahead.
