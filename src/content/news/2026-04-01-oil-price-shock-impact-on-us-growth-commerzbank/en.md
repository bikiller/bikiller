Commerzbank economists Bernd Weidensteiner and Christoph Balz highlight the impact of the recent surge in Brent and WTI crude oil prices on the US economy. Despite the sharp gains in oil, the analysts note that the United States is structurally more resilient than during the 1970s energy shocks, thanks to lower oil intensity and stronger domestic production. This resilience limits the immediate pressure on consumer spending and inflation, factors that influence USD exchange rate dynamics against major currencies like the EUR and JPY.  

Rising oil costs have already affected market sentiment, triggering trading volatility in both energy and forex markets. Investors are closely watching the USD as Brent hovers near $95 per barrel and WTI trades around $92, with the EUR/USD pair reacting to shifts in risk appetite and inflation expectations. The combination of elevated energy prices and solid domestic output suggests a muted impact on the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy path, supporting USD strength in short-term trading.  

Currency traders are balancing the oil-driven inflation narrative with underlying US economic stability. While higher energy costs could pressure consumer budgets, the reduced dependency on imports dampens broader macro risks, keeping USD exchange rates relatively stable. Technical analysts are noting key support around 1.0800 for EUR/USD, with potential resistance near 1.0950, as oil-related volatility continues to influence market positioning across major forex pairs.
