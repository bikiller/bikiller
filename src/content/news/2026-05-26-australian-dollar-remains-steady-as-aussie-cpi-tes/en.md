The Australian Dollar held steady during the North American session, with AUD/USD hovering near Tuesday’s opening level and trading flat around 0.7170. Market participants remained cautious ahead of Wednesday’s release of Australia’s Consumer Price Index (CPI), a key inflation indicator that could influence expectations for monetary policy and drive the next move in the currency pair’s exchange rate.

Trading activity was subdued as investors refrained from taking large positions before the inflation data. The CPI report is expected to provide fresh insight into price pressures in the Australian economy, potentially shaping the outlook for interest rates and broader demand for the AUD. As a result, currency markets have adopted a wait-and-see approach, limiting volatility despite ongoing shifts in global risk sentiment.

At the same time, geopolitical uncertainty continued to weigh on the Australian Dollar, offsetting support from stable domestic fundamentals. The risk-sensitive AUD often reacts to changes in global market confidence, and lingering geopolitical concerns have tempered buying interest. Against the USD, the currency pair remained confined to a narrow range near 0.7170, while traders also monitored movements in major currencies such as the EUR for broader signals on market direction and capital flows.
