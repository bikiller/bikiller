The Japanese yen weakened against the U.S. dollar as the USD/JPY currency pair approached the 158.90 level, reflecting cautious trading ahead of Japan’s first-quarter GDP report. Investors are positioning themselves amid heightened uncertainty in the bond market, with Bank of Japan board member Kihara warning that volatility could intensify, influencing both short-term exchange rates and broader market sentiment.

Market participants noted that the yen’s retreat came despite broader stability in Asian currencies, as traders weighed the potential impact of economic growth figures on monetary policy expectations. The USD/JPY exchange rate has shown resilience, with technical levels near 158.90 acting as a focal point for both speculative and hedging strategies. Analysts suggest that any surprising GDP data could trigger sharp adjustments in currency trading.

The outlook for the yen remains sensitive to domestic economic signals and global dollar trends, with the EUR/USD and other major pairs providing context for cross-currency flows. Traders are closely monitoring developments in Japan’s bond market and the implications for interest rate differentials, which continue to shape intraday and longer-term trading dynamics across the forex market. Volatility is expected to remain elevated until the GDP figures provide clearer guidance on the country’s economic trajectory.
