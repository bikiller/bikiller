The Japanese Yen continues its downward trend against the US Dollar, with the USD/JPY exchange rate hovering near 160. This marks a significant move as Japanese Government Bond (JGB) yields reach multi-decade highs, putting pressure on the Yen. The currency pair’s rise reflects ongoing market sentiment that the Bank of Japan (BoJ) will maintain its hawkish stance, particularly amid expectations for further tightening.

The BoJ’s continued commitment to its hiking bias is a key factor in the Yen’s struggle. With global interest rates rising, investors are increasingly favoring higher-yielding assets, weakening the demand for JPY. As a result, USD/JPY has experienced a sharp climb, with the currency pair now testing levels not seen in years. The BoJ's policy, coupled with higher JGB yields, suggests potential further downside for the Yen.
