The USD/INR plunged sharply in early trading on Thursday as the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) stepped in to stabilize the Indian Rupee. The exchange rate dropped significantly, with the INR gaining momentum against the US Dollar after the RBI's intervention. Market participants noticed a swift reversal from the previous day's levels, signaling that the central bank was actively managing the currency to prevent further depreciation.

The move saw the INR rally to 82.70 per USD from a weaker opening around 82.95, marking an intraday surge of nearly 0.30%. Traders are now closely monitoring the pair, especially as the rupee faces pressure from global market trends and domestic economic conditions. The RBI's action underscores its ongoing efforts to support the rupee amidst a volatile forex environment.
