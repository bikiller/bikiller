European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member and Bank of France Governor, François Villeroy de Galhau, addressed concerns over the potential impact of the ongoing Middle East tensions on the French economy. Speaking during European trading hours on Tuesday, Villeroy emphasized that the economic exposure to the region’s conflicts remains limited. This statement comes as the market watches the EUR/USD exchange rate, which has seen modest fluctuations, staying within a tight range amid global geopolitical uncertainties.

Despite the volatility in global markets, driven by U.S. and Iranian tensions, the euro remains resilient, with EUR/USD trading near key support levels. Investors have remained cautious, yet the euro's performance has not been significantly affected by the escalating situation. Villeroy's remarks suggest that the economic outlook for France, and by extension the eurozone, should not face substantial risks from the Middle East crisis, allowing traders to maintain their focus on domestic factors influencing the EUR/USD exchange rate.
