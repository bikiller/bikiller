The USD/CAD currency pair is struggling to regain traction after hitting a near one-month low around 1.3525 earlier this week. Despite a softer USD, Canadian Dollar bulls are showing caution as falling crude oil prices weigh on the loonie. During Wednesday’s Asian session, the exchange rate remains under pressure, reflecting limited upward momentum and lingering market uncertainty ahead of key US inflation data.  

Traders note that the softer greenback has provided only temporary relief for the loonie, with USD/CAD trading confined to a narrow range. The pair’s inability to recover above 1.3550 highlights the cautious stance of market participants, who are monitoring oil dynamics closely. Any sustained rebound in crude could reignite Canadian Dollar strength, while continued declines may prolong the pair’s weakness against the USD.  

Looking ahead, attention shifts to the upcoming US CPI release, which could determine near-term USD direction. Investors are weighing the interplay of oil market trends and inflation expectations, as these factors influence both trading sentiment and risk appetite. With USD/CAD hovering near its recent low, traders remain alert for any signals that could prompt a decisive move in the currency pair, affecting broader North American and global forex flows.
