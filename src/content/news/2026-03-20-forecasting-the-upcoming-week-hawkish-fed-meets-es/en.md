The US Dollar retreated this week, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) slipping below the key 100.00 level to 99.60 on Friday. Earlier in the week, the DXY had surged following the Federal Reserve’s decision to keep interest rates unchanged in the 3.50%-3.75% range, signaling a hawkish stance amid persistent inflation concerns. Traders reacted to the Fed’s guidance, weighing the potential impact on currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY.  

Market participants are now navigating heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, as the escalating conflict in Iran adds uncertainty to global risk sentiment. The combination of a strong Fed and geopolitical risk has created a volatile environment for USD trading, influencing exchange rates across major pairs. EUR/USD gained modestly as the dollar softened, while safe-haven currencies like the Japanese yen saw intermittent strength in intraday trading.  

Looking ahead, the market is expected to remain reactive to both central bank cues and geopolitical developments. Key technical levels, such as the 99.50 support for the DXY and resistance near 100.50, will guide short-term trading strategies. Investors are closely monitoring currency pair movements, assessing whether the USD can regain momentum or if renewed risk aversion will continue to weigh on the greenback.
