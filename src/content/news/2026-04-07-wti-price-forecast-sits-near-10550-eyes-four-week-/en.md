WTI crude oil prices extended their rally in the Asian session on Tuesday, climbing closer to the four-week high of $105.50 reached the day before. The fresh bids come as the market eyes a key deadline related to former President Trump’s stance on Iran, adding uncertainty to global oil supply dynamics. Traders remain focused on this geopolitical factor, potentially impacting future price fluctuations.

The upward movement in WTI is also supported by the broader market sentiment, with a weak USD aiding commodity demand. As oil prices near a critical resistance zone, attention shifts to potential price consolidation or a breakout, particularly as the US dollar's exchange rate adjusts. The market is anticipating volatility ahead of key events this week, with possible price swings linked to any developments regarding US-Iran relations.
