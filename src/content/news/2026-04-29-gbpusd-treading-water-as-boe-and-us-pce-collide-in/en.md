GBP/USD trading turned cautious midweek as the currency pair struggled to hold above the 1.355 level, a key technical support zone. After attempting to stabilize early in the week, the exchange rate slipped as market participants reduced positions ahead of a critical 90-minute window featuring the Bank of England decision and US PCE inflation data, both pivotal for USD direction.

The convergence of these events heightened volatility expectations across forex markets, with traders wary of sharp moves in both GBP and USD. The BoE’s policy stance could influence rate differentials, while the US PCE reading—closely watched by the Federal Reserve—may shape expectations for future tightening. This clash leaves GBP/USD vulnerable to sudden repricing.

In the near term, failure to reclaim the 1.355 level keeps downside pressure intact, with sentiment tilting in favor of the USD if US data surprises to the upside. Conversely, a hawkish BoE could lend support to the pound. For now, the currency pair remains range-bound, reflecting uncertainty as traders await clearer signals from both central banks.
