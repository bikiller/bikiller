The US Dollar Index (DXY) dropped on Monday, reflecting a risk-off sentiment across global markets. This decline came amid news of unsuccessful peace talks between the US and Iran, which raised tensions. Additionally, President Donald Trump authorized the US Navy to move towards the Strait of Hormuz, a development that traditionally triggers safe-haven demand. Despite these geopolitical concerns, the USD weakened against major currencies.

In the forex market, the EUR/USD exchange rate saw a modest rally, trading above 1.1000, while the USD/JPY declined sharply, hitting key support levels near 107.50. The US dollar's retreat occurred even as risk aversion typically drives investors toward the greenback. As markets continue to digest the fallout from these tensions, volatility is expected to remain elevated, especially in safe-haven assets like gold and the yen.
