WTI Crude Oil saw a sharp pullback on Tuesday, trading around $99.60, down 2.30% for the day. The decline ended a four-day rally as markets responded to easing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Investors shifted focus to hopes of a de-escalation in Iran, reducing concerns over potential disruptions to global oil supplies.

This price movement comes as traders anticipate a potential draw in API oil inventory data, which could provide further direction for the commodity. The pullback in oil prices has had a ripple effect across forex markets, with the USD seeing some pressure. However, traders remain cautious, as any unexpected inventory data could fuel renewed volatility in both crude oil and currency pairs like USD/EUR.
