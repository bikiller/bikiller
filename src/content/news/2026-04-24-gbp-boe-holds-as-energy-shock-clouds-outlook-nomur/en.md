Sterling traders are bracing for the Bank of England to keep Bank Rate unchanged at 3.75% at its upcoming meeting, according to Nomura economists George Buckley and team. The outlook is increasingly clouded by an ongoing energy shock, which continues to weigh on growth expectations and complicate the inflation path across the UK economy and broader financial markets.

Nomura expects a likely 8-1 vote in favour of holding policy steady, with Huw Pill seen as the sole dissenting hawk. For FX trading desks, the expected split reinforces the view of a cautious central bank, limiting immediate volatility in the GBP/USD currency pair while keeping the pound sensitive to shifting interest rate expectations versus the USD and EUR.

Market participants see the decision as largely priced in, with trading activity focused on forward guidance and macro signals rather than the headline rate. Stability at 3.75% could keep short-term exchange rate moves contained, though any deviation in tone may still ripple across European FX markets and broader risk sentiment in global currency trading sessions.
