Oil prices continue to rise sharply as the ongoing conflict in Iran disrupts supply chains, with Brent crude climbing significantly. The International Energy Agency has underscored that the global natural gas market is likely to remain tight for at least the next two years, further supporting the bullish sentiment in energy markets. This upward pressure on oil prices could influence currency pairs, particularly the USD, as traders adjust their expectations around inflation and interest rates.

In the forex market, the rising oil prices may impact the exchange rates of currencies closely tied to oil exports, such as the Canadian dollar (CAD) and the Norwegian krone (NOK). As traders monitor these developments, the implications for major currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY could become more pronounced, particularly if inflationary pressures from energy costs prompt shifts in monetary policy outlooks. The current geopolitical landscape adds another layer of complexity as traders navigate the potential for increased volatility in both commodities and forex markets.
