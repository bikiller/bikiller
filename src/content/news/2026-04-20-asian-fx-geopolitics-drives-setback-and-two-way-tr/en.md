Asian currencies retraced Friday’s gains as renewed geopolitical tensions rattled markets. Following Iran’s closure of the Strait of Hormuz, traders shifted away from risk-sensitive assets, with high-beta currencies taking the brunt. The South Korean won (KRW) led the decline, while the Taiwanese dollar (TWD), Indian rupee (INR), Thai baht (THB), and Philippine peso (PHP) also softened, reflecting heightened sensitivity to rising oil prices. USD/Asian FX pairs saw a resumption of two-way trading as volatility spiked.

The shift in sentiment impacted broader trading flows, with the USD regaining strength against regional currencies. EUR/USD remained volatile, influenced by both oil-driven risk aversion and mixed European data. Technical levels around previous support and resistance points saw increased attention, as traders weighed potential reversals against the backdrop of escalating Middle East tensions.

Market participants are positioning cautiously ahead of next week, balancing the risk of further geopolitical disruptions against underlying fundamentals. Oil-sensitive currencies are expected to remain under pressure, with KRW and INR particularly vulnerable, while safe-haven flows into USD could sustain broader dollar strength. Traders are closely monitoring exchange rate swings, seeking opportunities amid heightened two-way volatility across Asian currency pairs.
