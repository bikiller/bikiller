The U.S. dollar maintained its strength as markets digest the Federal Reserve’s latest policy stance, with TD Securities highlighting a “higher-for-longer” approach. Strategist Bart Melek noted that the Fed’s recent FOMC decision, combined with the Iran-driven oil supply shock, is steering monetary policy toward a neutral or even restrictive bias. This dynamic is delaying expectations for any near-term rate cuts, keeping the USD supported across major currency pairs.  

EUR/USD struggled to regain footing, trading below 1.1000 as investors weighed the Fed’s firmness against softer European data. Commodity-linked currencies like the AUD and CAD saw heightened volatility, responding both to oil price surges and the broader risk-off sentiment that favors the greenback. Traders are adjusting positions in anticipation of tighter U.S. liquidity, which could continue to influence short-term exchange rate trends.  

Gold prices came under pressure as the prospect of prolonged higher rates boosts the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets. Technical analysts highlight resistance around $2,050 per ounce, with support near $2,000. Market participants remain cautious, balancing geopolitical developments with the Fed’s stance, while forex traders closely monitor USD/JPY, EUR/USD, and other key pairs for signals on the next directional moves.
