West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil extended its rebound on Thursday, climbing more than 2% to trade around $71.50 after a sharp recovery from a more than three-month low. The move snapped a three-day losing streak as short covering emerged across energy markets, with traders reacting to oversold conditions following the recent selloff. Broader trading sentiment remained tied to USD dynamics, as fluctuations in the US dollar continue to influence commodity pricing and cross-asset flows.

Market participants are also closely monitoring developments in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil supply. Heightened geopolitical sensitivity has added a risk premium to crude, supporting bids despite earlier downside pressure. In forex markets, this has contributed to shifting risk sentiment across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, as energy price volatility feeds into inflation expectations and interest rate outlooks.

Looking ahead, traders are assessing whether WTI can sustain gains above the recent lows or remain range-bound amid competing macro forces. Strength in oil often supports commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian dollar versus the USD, while also influencing broader USD strength through inflation channels. As a result, both oil and forex trading conditions remain tightly interlinked, with volatility expected to persist.
