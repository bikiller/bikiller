Gold prices edged lower as the USD gained traction, pushing XAU/USD down to $4,518, a 0.50% decline. Traders reacted to hawkish remarks from Fed official Christopher Waller, which reinforced expectations of a potential interest rate hike later this year. The stronger dollar also pressured other precious metals, while safe-haven demand softened amid lingering doubts over a US-Iran resolution.  

The uptick in the USD influenced key currency pairs, with EUR/USD and GBP/USD feeling the weight of a firmer greenback. Market participants are increasingly factoring in higher borrowing costs, adjusting trading strategies in both forex and commodity markets. Technical indicators suggest gold faces resistance near $4,540, with support levels around $4,500 under scrutiny if the USD momentum continues.  

Analysts highlight that the combination of geopolitical uncertainty and monetary policy expectations keeps volatility elevated across currency and commodity markets. Investors are closely monitoring Fed communications and US-Iran negotiations, as any shift could quickly sway exchange rates and trading flows. For now, gold’s retreat reflects the broader USD recovery and market caution heading into the next Fed policy signals.
