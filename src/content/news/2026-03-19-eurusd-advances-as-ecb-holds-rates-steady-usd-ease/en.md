EUR/USD rose slightly on Thursday, driven by the Euro's strength following the European Central Bank’s decision to keep interest rates unchanged. The pair traded at $1.0700, edging higher after the ECB's announcement. The Euro’s gains were also supported by expectations of prolonged hawkishness in the region. As a result, the EUR/USD exchange rate experienced a modest rally, reflecting confidence in the Eurozone's monetary policy stance.

Meanwhile, the US Dollar weakened across the board, retreating from its post-Fed highs. The Dollar's decline followed a series of hawkish central bank decisions, including those by the Bank of Japan and the Bank of England, which fueled optimism in global markets. As USD eased, the broader market sentiment shifted, contributing to further losses for the currency pair, with EUR/USD moving above key resistance levels.
