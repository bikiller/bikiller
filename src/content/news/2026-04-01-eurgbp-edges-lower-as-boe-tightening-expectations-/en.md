EUR/GBP traded at 0.8725 on Wednesday, losing 0.10% on the day. The currency pair has been pressured by stronger expectations of further tightening from the Bank of England (BoE), which is weighing on the pound. Despite positive Eurozone PMI data supporting the euro, the overall outlook for GBP remains more favorable in the current environment of rising UK rates.

The EUR/GBP exchange rate's slight pullback comes amid a broader market focus on potential BoE rate hikes. With the euro supported by recent data, the pound is still benefiting from higher interest rate expectations, keeping EUR/GBP under pressure. This trend highlights the continuing divergence in monetary policy between the ECB and BoE, shaping near-term trading sentiment for the pair.
