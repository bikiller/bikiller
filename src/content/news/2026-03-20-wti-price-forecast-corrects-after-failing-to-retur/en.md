West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures on NYMEX retreated more than 1% early Friday, hovering around $93.10 as European markets opened. The setback comes after oil failed to sustain a rebound above the $100 mark, signaling lingering pressure on energy markets despite previous bullish momentum. Traders are weighing supply concerns against weakening demand expectations, with the USD strength adding further headwinds for commodity-linked currencies.  

The correction in WTI is influencing broader trading flows, with the USD index gaining ground against the EUR and other major currencies. The decline in oil prices may impact energy-sensitive pairs, prompting cautious positioning in EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Market participants are closely monitoring technical levels, as a sustained break below $93 could accelerate selling, while a rebound above $95 may offer short-term relief.  

Analysts note that the exchange rate movements in commodity-linked currencies are increasingly tied to WTI trends, as lower crude prices can weigh on CAD and NOK. Investors remain attentive to geopolitical developments, OPEC+ output signals, and economic data that could sway both oil and forex markets. Overall, the interplay between energy prices and currency pairs is shaping trading strategies as markets digest the latest correction.
