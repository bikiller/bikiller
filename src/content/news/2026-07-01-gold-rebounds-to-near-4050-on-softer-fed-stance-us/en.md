Gold (XAU/USD) rebounds to around $4,045–$4,050 during early Thursday Asian trading, recovering from a seven-month low as the exchange rate stabilizes after recent selling pressure. The move comes as traders reassess Federal Reserve Chair Kevin Warsh’s latest remarks, which signaled a softer policy tone and prompted repositioning in USD-denominated assets.

Market sentiment is increasingly driven by expectations surrounding US labor data, with the upcoming Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report acting as a key catalyst for USD volatility. A softer Fed stance has reduced dollar strength, supporting gold demand across forex trading desks and encouraging renewed interest in safe-haven positioning from investors globally.

From a technical perspective, XAU/USD is attempting a recovery after touching its recent seven-month low, with resistance seen near the $4,050 level. Sustained buying above this threshold could extend gains, while broader forex markets remain sensitive to USD index movements and shifting EUR and commodity currency correlations in the near term.
