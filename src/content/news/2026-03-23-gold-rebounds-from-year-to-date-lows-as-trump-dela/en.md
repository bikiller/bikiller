Gold prices (XAU/USD) rebounded on Monday, recovering from year-to-date lows as bargain hunters took advantage of the recent sell-off. The precious metal found support after U.S. President Donald Trump postponed his planned strikes on Iran's energy infrastructure. This news eased geopolitical tensions, offering a temporary reprieve for gold, which had dropped sharply in recent days. As of the latest trading, gold was up 1.2% to $1,455.

The price action also saw the currency pair gain traction against the USD, with the U.S. dollar (USD) weakening slightly as markets digested the news. Gold’s recovery came amid renewed risk appetite in the broader financial markets, as concerns over an escalation in Middle Eastern tensions eased. With technical support at $1,450, investors are eyeing further price movements, with the focus now shifting to any developments in global political risks that could influence the XAU/USD exchange rate.
