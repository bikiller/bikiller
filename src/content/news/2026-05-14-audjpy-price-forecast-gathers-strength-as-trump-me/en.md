The AUD/JPY currency pair edged higher to 114.65 in early European trading on Thursday, maintaining a bullish bias amid cautious market sentiment. Traders are positioning ahead of the high-profile meeting between US President Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping in Beijing, with hopes that progress on trade negotiations could influence risk sentiment and boost commodity-linked currencies like the Australian dollar.

Market participants are also weighing broader FX trends, including the relative strength of the USD and movements in EUR pairs, as global investors digest the potential for coordinated economic measures. Technical charts suggest AUD/JPY may test key resistance levels near 115.00 if optimism from the summit holds, while any setbacks could trigger retracements toward support around 114.20.

Overall, trading activity in the AUD/JPY pair reflects a mix of geopolitical caution and speculative positioning. Investors are closely monitoring exchange rate fluctuations, with the summit outcome likely to drive short-term volatility across major currency pairs. Forex traders are keeping an eye on volume and momentum indicators to gauge potential breakout or reversal scenarios as the US-China dialogue unfolds.
