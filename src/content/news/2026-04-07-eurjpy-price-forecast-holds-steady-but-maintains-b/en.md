The EUR/JPY currency pair is holding steady near 184.35 in early European trading on Tuesday, showing resilience above its 100-day exponential moving average (EMA). Traders remain cautious as the exchange rate maintains a bullish bias, suggesting that buyers are still willing to defend support levels despite broader market uncertainty. The pair’s technical setup points to potential upside, but momentum appears restrained.  

Market attention is focused on geopolitical developments, particularly rising tensions between the United States and Iran. Such concerns tend to favor safe-haven currencies like the Japanese Yen, which could limit the euro’s gains against the JPY. Investors are weighing risk sentiment carefully, with any escalation in conflict likely to spur demand for the Yen, influencing intraday trading and exchange rate volatility.  

Analysts suggest that while EUR/JPY retains its bullish structure above the 100-day EMA, traders should monitor both technical levels and global news flow. Short-term trading may see choppy price action as the market digests geopolitical headlines and USD movements, with the broader trend hinging on whether the euro can overcome safe-haven pressures or remains capped by risk-off flows.
