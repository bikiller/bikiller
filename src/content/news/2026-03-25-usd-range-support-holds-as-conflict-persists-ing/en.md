The USD remains supported against a backdrop of ongoing Middle East tensions, which continue to elevate energy prices and bolster demand for safe-haven assets. ING’s Chris Turner notes that while there is speculation over a potential Dollar sell-off, it is premature to expect a sustained downturn. The USD’s strength persists as investors seek stability amidst geopolitical uncertainty, with energy prices keeping inflation concerns alive.

EUR/USD has been trading within a tight range, with technical support around 1.0600, while the USD/JPY has shown resilience near the 145.00 mark. As global markets continue to digest the impact of elevated energy prices and broader market risk, the exchange rate dynamics suggest that the USD will maintain its ground. The Dollar’s safe-haven appeal is likely to persist as long as the geopolitical situation remains fluid.
