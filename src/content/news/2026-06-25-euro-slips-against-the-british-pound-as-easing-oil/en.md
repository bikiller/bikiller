EUR/GBP currency pair trades on the back foot around 0.8620 on Thursday, with the exchange rate hovering near the lower boundary of a multi-month range that has held since July 2025. The euro is extending losses against the British pound in subdued trading conditions across major FX markets, including USD crosses, as sentiment softens.

Easing oil prices are weighing on expectations for further European Central Bank rate hikes, reducing demand for the euro and keeping EUR/GBP trading biased to the downside. Softer energy costs ease inflation concerns in the eurozone, prompting investors to reassess the ECB policy outlook, while the Bank of England stance remains comparatively steady.

Traders are now watching whether EUR/GBP can stabilise above the 0.8600 handle or extend its decline toward deeper support within the range that has contained price action since July 2025. Overall, the currency pair remains range-bound, with euro weakness against the pound dominating short-term forex trading sentiment amid broader USD-driven volatility in FX markets.
