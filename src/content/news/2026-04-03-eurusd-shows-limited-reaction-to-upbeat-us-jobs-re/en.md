EUR/USD remains range-bound on Friday, with the currency pair trading within a narrow band as the US Dollar gains support from a stronger-than-expected Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report. The US economy added 236,000 jobs in March, surpassing the anticipated 230,000, boosting confidence in the dollar. Despite the positive data, EUR/USD holds steady at around 1.0870, reflecting a muted reaction due to reduced market liquidity from the Good Friday holiday.

The Euro (EUR) remains relatively resilient, with traders cautious amid thin holiday trading conditions. The limited movement in EUR/USD highlights the impact of the holiday market lull, where fewer participants are active in driving price action. Although the USD is supported by solid economic data, the lack of liquidity prevents significant shifts in the exchange rate, keeping EUR/USD trapped within its recent range.
