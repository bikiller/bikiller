European Central Bank (ECB) official and Finnish Central Bank Governor Olli Rehn signaled on Wednesday that monetary policy should not hinge solely on rising oil prices. Speaking during European trading hours, Rehn emphasized that while energy shocks are significant, the ECB must weigh their broader economic implications before adjusting interest rates. The remarks come amid heightened EUR/USD volatility, with the pair trading near recent 1.0950 support.

Rehn noted that policymakers need a measured approach, considering how energy costs ripple through inflation and exchange rates. EUR trading has seen modest gains against the USD following his comments, reflecting market perception that the ECB is unlikely to react hastily. Analysts highlight that crude oil prices hovering above $85 per barrel may influence sentiment but not directly dictate rate moves.

The central bank’s focus remains on assessing the full scope of energy-driven shocks on growth and inflation. Traders are watching EUR/USD for potential breaks above 1.1000 or slides toward 1.0900, while oil-driven volatility continues to underpin USD/commodity pair fluctuations. Rehn’s caution underscores a careful balancing act, aiming to support economic stability without overreacting to temporary price swings.
