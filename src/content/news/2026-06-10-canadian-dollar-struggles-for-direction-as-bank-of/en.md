The Canadian Dollar (CAD) gave back part of its earlier gains against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday after the Bank of Canada (BoC) left interest rates unchanged, a decision that initially sparked a brief wave of buying interest. However, the move proved short-lived as traders reassessed the policy outlook, allowing the USD/CAD currency pair to rebound from an intraday low of 1.3899. At the time of writing, the exchange rate was trading near 1.3925.

The BoC’s decision to maintain borrowing costs signaled a cautious approach amid ongoing uncertainty surrounding economic growth and inflation trends. While the unchanged rate provided temporary support for the Canadian Dollar, market participants remained reluctant to extend bullish positions, leading to renewed demand for the US Dollar during North American trading hours.

For forex markets, the price action highlights the fragile balance between monetary policy expectations and broader risk sentiment. The recovery in USD/CAD from the 1.3899 low toward 1.3925 suggests traders continue to favor the greenback despite the BoC’s steady stance. Investors will now watch upcoming economic data and central bank signals for clues on the next direction of the currency pair, while movements in major currencies such as the EUR and USD remain key drivers of overall trading sentiment
