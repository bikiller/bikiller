The USD/JPY currency pair climbed to the 157.10 region on Monday, with the US Dollar gaining momentum as risk sentiment shifted. Traders reacted sharply after US President Donald Trump dismissed Iran’s latest peace proposal as “totally unacceptable,” boosting demand for safe-haven USD assets. The move pushed the Japanese Yen lower, underlining its vulnerability amid heightened geopolitical tensions and reinforcing USD strength across major currency pairs.  

Market participants are also eyeing the upcoming US Consumer Price Index (CPI) report, which could further sway trading sentiment. Analysts note that the USD/JPY exchange rate may test higher levels if inflation data surprises to the upside, while technical charts indicate that a break above 157.50 could trigger additional bullish momentum. EUR/USD and other major pairs are similarly sensitive, as traders adjust positions ahead of potentially market-moving economic releases.  

The Yen’s decline reflects a combination of political uncertainty and broad USD strength, prompting traders to recalibrate positions in global forex markets. Investors are closely monitoring flows into USD-denominated assets, with safe-haven buying supporting the exchange rate. Continued volatility is expected as markets digest both geopolitical developments and US economic indicators, with the USD/JPY pair poised for further testing of resistance in the near term.
