The EUR/USD currency pair continued to consolidate above the 1.1600 mark, with traders closely monitoring resistance at 1.1682, a level that aligns with the closely watched 200-day moving average. Despite expectations for tighter monetary policy from the European Central Bank (ECB), market sentiment toward the euro remained cautious, reflecting lingering downside risks against the USD in forex trading.

According to Brown Brothers Harriman strategist Elias Haddad, comments from ECB policymaker Isabel Schnabel reinforced expectations of a potential June rate hike, offering near-term support for the EUR exchange rate. However, the prospect of higher borrowing costs has yet to fully offset broader concerns surrounding eurozone growth and persistent demand for the US Dollar as a defensive currency.

In the near term, the EUR/USD exchange rate may remain sensitive to both ECB policy signals and shifts in USD strength. While the euro has managed to hold above 1.1600, traders are watching whether the currency pair can break above 1.1682 resistance or face renewed downside pressure, particularly as technical indicators continue to shape market positioning.
