West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude eased to around $85 per barrel early Friday in Asian trading, marking its lowest level since mid-April. The dip came as the US Dollar (USD) weakened following President Donald Trump's announcement of a settlement with Iran, fueling optimism that geopolitical tensions in the Middle East may be easing. Oil markets responded swiftly, with Brent futures also showing modest declines.

The easing risk premium prompted currency pairs such as USD/EUR and USD/JPY to retreat slightly, as traders reassessed safe-haven demand. WTI’s retreat reflects broader shifts in investor sentiment, with markets pricing in a potential reduction in geopolitical-driven supply concerns. Exchange rates across major pairs exhibited increased volatility, highlighting how oil and forex markets remain intertwined amid global uncertainty.

Analysts suggest that continued progress toward resolving the Middle East conflict could sustain pressure on oil prices, potentially impacting commodity-linked currencies like the Canadian Dollar (CAD). Traders are monitoring key technical levels near $85, as breaches could trigger further adjustments in both oil futures and related currency pairs, affecting trading strategies across energy and forex markets.
