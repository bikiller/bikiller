The recent spike in oil and natural gas prices has altered market expectations for the Bank of England's monetary policy. Rabobank’s Senior Macro Strategist, Stefan Koopman, notes that the surge in energy prices has pushed back the anticipated rate cuts, now pushing the BoE's policy rate on hold through 2026. This shift has impacted the GBP, with the exchange rate facing pressure against major currencies like the USD and EUR as traders adjust to the changing outlook.

Energy prices, including oil, have soared in recent weeks, complicating the BoE's path toward easing. With inflation risks rising alongside these energy costs, the UK’s central bank is unlikely to cut rates as previously expected. As the GBP/USD continues to trade under pressure, the market will likely focus on any new policy signals, especially with the EUR/GBP exchange rate also reflecting the broader uncertainty in the market.
