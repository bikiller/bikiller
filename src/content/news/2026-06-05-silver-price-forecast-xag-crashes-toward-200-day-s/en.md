Silver (XAG/USD) plunged sharply on Friday, testing the 200-day Simple Moving Average near $67.79 as the precious metal posted a daily loss of almost 8%. Traders reacted to a stronger-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls report, which bolstered the USD and pressured risk-sensitive assets, including silver. The exchange rate now hovers near key technical support, signaling heightened volatility in the XAG/USD currency pair.  

The drop leaves XAG/USD on track for a weekly decline approaching 10%, marking one of the steepest moves in recent months. Market participants are weighing the implications of robust US employment data, which tends to lift Treasury yields and dampen appeal for non-yielding commodities. Momentum indicators suggest that a breach of the 200-day SMA could open the path toward $61.00, attracting both short-term traders and swing positions.  

Currency markets are closely watching the ripple effects across USD-linked pairs, with EUR/USD and other major exchange rates reacting to the stronger greenback. Silver trading reflects broader investor sentiment, where rising USD strength pressures commodity-linked currencies and metals alike. Analysts caution that XAG/USD could face further downside unless upcoming US economic data softens expectations for monetary tightening.
