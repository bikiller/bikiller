EUR/USD is trading near the 1.17 level as market participants await the upcoming Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meeting. Investors are pricing in expectations that the Federal Reserve will hold interest rates steady and avoid committing to firm forward guidance, leaving the dollar range-bound against the euro. This cautious stance has kept the currency pair’s short-term momentum subdued, with traders closely watching central bank signals for directional cues.  

The euro has seen limited upside despite recent economic data, as uncertainty over the Fed’s messaging weighs on the exchange rate. Market participants note that any deviation from the expected policy stance could trigger volatility in EUR/USD trading, potentially pushing the pair toward key technical levels. Analysts at Danske Bank highlight that the pair’s proximity to 1.17 reflects balanced sentiment between bullish euro flows and defensive USD positioning.  

Looking ahead, EUR/USD could react sharply to commentary from either the Fed or the European Central Bank, as investors recalibrate expectations for interest rate paths. Technical watchers point to 1.17 as a critical pivot, with moves above or below potentially setting the tone for broader currency trends. Traders remain attentive to market liquidity and cross-currency flows, which could influence short-term trading dynamics and the euro’s performance versus the dollar.
