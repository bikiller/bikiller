USD/JPY bounced back to trade around 158.70 on Friday, rising 0.61% after Thursday’s sharp decline. The currency pair found support as the US Dollar strengthened, recovering from recent volatility. Traders are recalibrating expectations for US monetary policy, weighing the impact of upcoming economic data and Fed commentary on the greenback’s near-term trajectory.  

The rebound in USD/JPY reflects a broader rally in the USD, with the dollar gaining against major peers such as the EUR and GBP. Market participants cite safe-haven flows and renewed demand for US assets as key drivers. Technical levels near 158.50 acted as a floor, helping the pair stabilize after testing lower support in prior sessions.  

Despite the upside, the Bank of Japan’s subtle hawkish signals continue to temper gains. Analysts note that any further appreciation could face resistance near recent highs, with trading volumes remaining cautious amid geopolitical and economic uncertainty. Investors are monitoring both US inflation trends and BoJ policy hints to gauge whether the currency pair can extend its recovery.
