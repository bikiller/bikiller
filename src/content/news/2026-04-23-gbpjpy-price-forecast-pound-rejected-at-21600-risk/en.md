GBP/JPY struggled to extend gains on Thursday, stalling around 215.70 after being firmly rejected at the 216.00 resistance level. The currency pair, a key cross in forex trading between the British pound and Japanese yen, failed to sustain upward momentum as broader exchange rate flows weakened. Early attempts to build on bullish sentiment faded, leaving price action choppy and range-bound.

Risk appetite deteriorated sharply as Middle East headlines unsettled global markets, prompting renewed caution across forex trading desks. The shift weighed on higher-yielding currencies and supported safe-haven demand, contributing to the pullback in GBP/JPY toward the 215.00 psychological level. Traders also monitored USD and EUR movements, as broader sentiment remained sensitive to geopolitical developments and shifting expectations for rate paths.

At the time of writing, GBP/JPY trades at 215.06, hovering just above immediate support as bearish pressure builds below 215.00. A sustained break under this level could deepen downside risks, exposing further technical weakness in the currency pair. Market participants remain cautious, with volatility in GBP/USD and EUR/JPY also reflecting uneven risk sentiment across major forex markets into Friday sessions.
