The AUD/USD currency pair maintained its bullish momentum during Thursday’s Asian session, finding support near 0.7250. Traders stepped in on minor dips after the pair retreated slightly from the previous day’s highs around 0.7275-0.7280, levels not seen since June 2022. Spot rates are hovering in the mid-0.7200s, marking a third consecutive day of gains as the US dollar softened.  

The softer USD continues to underpin the Australian dollar, with investors eyeing broader market sentiment and risk appetite. The exchange rate’s resilience comes amid mixed global economic data, while momentum indicators suggest buyers remain in control. Traders are monitoring technical support around 0.7250, which may provide a springboard for further advances if the USD weakness persists.  

Market participants also note that the AUD/USD’s climb reflects wider shifts in forex trading, with the pair outperforming some major counterparts, including the EUR/USD. Sustained buying pressure could push the exchange rate back toward multi-year highs, while short-term volatility remains possible as traders react to US economic updates and central bank commentary.
