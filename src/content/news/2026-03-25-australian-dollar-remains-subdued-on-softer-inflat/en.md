The Australian Dollar (AUD) continues to face downward pressure, with the AUD/USD exchange rate slipping further during the European session on Wednesday, hovering around 0.6980. This marks the second consecutive day of losses for the currency pair, primarily driven by weaker-than-expected inflation data from Australia. The market’s reaction reflects growing concerns over the country's economic outlook and inflationary pressures.

Adding to the bearish sentiment surrounding the Aussie, geopolitical uncertainty continues to cloud the market. Ongoing talks between the US and Iran have created additional volatility, keeping traders cautious. The combination of soft domestic inflation and external geopolitical risks has dampened investor appetite for the Australian Dollar, leaving the currency pair vulnerable to further declines in the near term.
