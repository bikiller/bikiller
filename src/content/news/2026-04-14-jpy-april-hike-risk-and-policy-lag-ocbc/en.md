The Japanese Yen (JPY) struggled to gain traction despite a weaker US Dollar (USD), as the Bank of Japan (BoJ) maintained a quiet stance. The lack of clear communication from the BoJ left the Yen isolated, with traders scaling back expectations for an April rate hike. As a result, the USD/JPY exchange rate remained relatively resilient, holding steady around ¥134.80.

Despite market skepticism, OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong argue that a rate hike by the BoJ in April is still likely. This stance is based on the ongoing risk of inflation pressures in Japan, which could prompt the central bank to adjust its policy sooner rather than later. Such a move would likely impact the JPY/USD trading dynamic, potentially pushing the currency pair towards key resistance levels.
