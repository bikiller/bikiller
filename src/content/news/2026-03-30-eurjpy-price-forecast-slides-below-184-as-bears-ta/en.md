EUR/JPY saw a sharp decline on Monday, dropping 0.78% as the pair slid below the 184 level. The Japanese yen strengthened after remarks from the country’s currency chief, Atsushi Mimura, who warned of "bold action" to address the yen’s rapid appreciation. The verbal intervention sent shockwaves through the forex market, raising expectations that Japan might step in to curb further yen strength.

At the time of writing, the EUR/JPY is trading just above 183, with bears eyeing the 100-day simple moving average (SMA) as the next key support level. The yen’s gains are also bolstered by a more hawkish tone from Japanese authorities, which could trigger volatility in the pair if intervention materializes. Market participants will closely monitor any signs of action, which could significantly impact the EUR/JPY exchange rate in the short term.
