The USD/CAD currency pair showed limited upside on Tuesday, with the US Dollar trading around 1.3515, slightly off recent highs but holding above the 1.3600 mark. The pair had rebounded from Friday’s lows at 1.3550, yet the recovery struggled to gain momentum. Traders noted that the Loonie benefited from steady oil prices, which underpinned the Canadian Dollar amid moderate risk aversion in global markets.  

Market participants highlighted that the US Dollar remained broadly supported against major currencies, though cautious sentiment capped gains. Exchange rates reflected a blend of lingering safe-haven demand and commodity-driven flows, with the Canadian Dollar responding to crude prices that remained resilient. Technical levels near 1.3600 acted as resistance, preventing USD/CAD from extending its rebound despite the Dollar’s general stability.  

Analysts suggested that short-term trading could see the pair oscillate between 1.3550 support and 1.3600 resistance, as investors weigh oil market developments and US economic indicators. Movements in other major currencies, including EUR/USD, may also influence USD positioning, while CAD’s sensitivity to commodity prices continues to shape its performance. Overall, the market remains in a cautious holding pattern, awaiting clearer directional cues.
