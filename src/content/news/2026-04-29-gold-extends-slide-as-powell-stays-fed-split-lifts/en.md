Gold (XAU/USD) continued its downward trajectory, shedding over 1% as investors digested the Federal Reserve’s latest decision to hold interest rates steady. The precious metal fell under pressure from higher U.S. Treasury yields, which strengthened the dollar and weighed on non-yielding assets. Traders noted that gold is testing support levels near $1,960, with resistance around $1,985 proving difficult to breach amid cautious market sentiment.  

Fed Chair Jerome Powell signaled he intends to remain on the board until ongoing legal matters are fully resolved, adding a layer of uncertainty to policy outlooks. The mixed messages from the Fed have fueled a split in market expectations, pushing USD exchange rates higher against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. This dynamic has led forex traders to recalibrate positions in gold, silver, and other commodities sensitive to interest rate trends.  

Market participants are closely monitoring technical levels and yield movements as trading volumes pick up. Short-term momentum suggests XAU/USD may face continued selling pressure if Treasury yields remain elevated and the USD strengthens further. Currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD are also reacting, with the greenback’s resilience limiting upside for risk-sensitive assets, while traders assess implications for global forex markets.
