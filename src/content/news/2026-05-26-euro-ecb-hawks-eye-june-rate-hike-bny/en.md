Euro sentiment in the currency pair market turned more hawkish after reports that ECB policymakers are considering a June rate hike. The exchange rate outlook has been shaped by tighter monetary policy expectations, with traders reassessing EUR positioning against USD. BNY notes that interest-rate expectations remain a key driver of euro trading as inflation concerns persist across the bloc.

BNY research highlighted comments from ECB Executive Board member Isabel Schnabel, who signaled support for a potential June rate increase. The statement reflects growing concern that Middle East energy shocks are feeding persistent inflation pressures across the eurozone, complicating the central bank’s easing path and reinforcing a more cautious stance among policymakers.

Market participants are now watching EUR/USD trading dynamics closely, as shifting rate expectations could widen volatility in the exchange rate. Against the USD, the euro may remain sensitive to incoming inflation data and ECB communication, with traders pricing in a higher probability of action at the June policy meeting outcome.
