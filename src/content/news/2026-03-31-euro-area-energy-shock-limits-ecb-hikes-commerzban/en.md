Euro area inflation surged to 2.5% in March, primarily driven by rising energy costs linked to the ongoing conflict in Iran. This sharp increase in energy prices significantly impacted the inflation rate, while core inflation, excluding volatile items, eased to 2.3%. The energy shock has added pressure to the Eurozone economy, influencing expectations for the European Central Bank's (ECB) monetary policy.

As energy prices continue to rise, the ECB faces challenges in implementing aggressive interest rate hikes. The EUR/USD exchange rate may be sensitive to these developments, with traders keeping an eye on future inflationary pressures and ECB responses. With core inflation decelerating, the market may adjust its expectations for tightening cycles, which could limit the upside potential for the euro against the USD in the near term.
