The USD/JPY currency pair surged to a near three-week high of 157.35 in the early Asian session on Tuesday. The US Dollar gained strength against the Japanese Yen following stronger-than-expected economic data from the United States. This upbeat data has bolstered market sentiment, driving the exchange rate higher as traders anticipate continued US economic resilience.

Later in the day, market participants will focus on comments from Bank of Japan (BoJ) Governor Kazuo Ueda. His speech could provide further direction for the JPY, which has weakened below the key 157.50 level. With the BoJ maintaining its ultra-loose monetary policy, any hints on future policy adjustments could impact the trading of the Yen against major currencies.
