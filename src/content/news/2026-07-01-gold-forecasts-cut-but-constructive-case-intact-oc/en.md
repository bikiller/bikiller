Gold and Silver outlooks have been revised lower by OCBC analysts Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, reflecting a more challenging macroeconomic environment heading into 2026. The analysts cited a combination of elevated real yields, continued strength in the US Dollar (USD), and slower exchange-traded fund (ETF) inflows as key factors weighing on precious metals valuations. The stronger USD exchange rate environment has also reduced the near-term appeal of non-yielding assets across global trading markets.

Despite the downward revision to their end-2026 forecasts, OCBC maintains a constructive longer-term stance on both Gold and Silver. Market participants continue to monitor the interaction between Federal Reserve policy expectations, US Treasury yields, and broader currency pair movements, particularly involving the USD and EUR. These macro drivers remain central to precious metals trading and portfolio allocation decisions.

The revised outlook highlights growing caution over near-term price performance while preserving the broader bullish case for precious metals. Analysts noted that persistent geopolitical uncertainty, reserve diversification trends, and potential shifts in monetary policy could continue to provide underlying support for Gold and Silver markets, even as stronger USD dynamics and higher real yields create headwinds for investors and traders.
