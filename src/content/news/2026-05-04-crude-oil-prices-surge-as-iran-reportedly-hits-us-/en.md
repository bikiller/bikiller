Crude oil prices surged in early European trading on Monday, fueled by reports that Iran reportedly targeted a U.S. warship, raising concerns of heightened tensions in the Middle East. Brent crude climbed above $92.50 per barrel, while WTI gained to $88.30, marking intraday increases of roughly 2%. The geopolitical risk pushed safe-haven demand, lifting the USD against several major currencies.  

The EUR/USD pair dipped to 1.0730 as traders sought shelter in the greenback, while USD/JPY climbed toward 145.10, reflecting risk-off sentiment. Commodity-linked currencies such as the AUD and CAD saw mixed trading, with USD/CAD touching 1.3450 amid stronger crude dynamics. Technical charts show Brent facing resistance at $93.00, and a break above could signal further bullish momentum, potentially reverberating across forex markets.  

Traders remain cautious as Middle East tensions threaten supply routes, impacting both energy prices and currency markets. Increased oil volatility is likely to influence central bank considerations, with the USD strengthening against major peers. Market participants are closely monitoring developments, positioning around safe-haven currencies, and adjusting exposure in risk-sensitive assets as crude continues its upward trajectory.
