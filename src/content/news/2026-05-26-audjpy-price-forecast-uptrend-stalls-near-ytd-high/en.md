The AUD/JPY currency pair edged higher on Wednesday, with the Australian Dollar gaining 0.14% against the Japanese Yen as improving market sentiment boosted demand for risk-sensitive assets. The exchange rate climbed to 114.17 after rebounding from an intraday low of 113.78, while traders continued to monitor broader movements across USD and EUR markets for directional cues.

Despite the advance, the uptrend appeared to lose momentum near the year-to-date high, as technical indicators suggested bullish pressure was beginning to fade. The Relative Strength Index flattened during Asian trading, signaling that buying interest may be cooling after the pair’s recent rally. Market participants are now watching whether AUD/JPY can sustain gains above the 114.00 level or face a corrective pullback.

The softer tone in the Japanese Yen was largely driven by fading safe-haven demand, with investors shifting toward higher-yielding currencies amid stronger risk appetite. Equity market stability and improved sentiment across global trading sessions also supported the Australian Dollar. Analysts noted that continued volatility in USD and EUR exchange rates, alongside expectations for central bank policy, could influence the next move in the AUD/JPY pair over the near term.
