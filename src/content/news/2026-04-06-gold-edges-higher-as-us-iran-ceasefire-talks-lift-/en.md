Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher on Monday as the US Dollar softened, with the currency pair trading around $4,691. Investors reacted to renewed optimism over US-Iran ceasefire talks, which bolstered market sentiment and prompted safe-haven buying to ease. Earlier in the session, XAU/USD had dipped to an intraday low near $4,600 before rebounding, highlighting renewed volatility in response to geopolitical developments.  

The softer USD weighed on other major currency pairs as well, with traders noting increased trading activity in EUR/USD and GBP/USD as market participants adjusted positions. The rebound in gold reflects a cautious risk-on sentiment, with investors balancing geopolitical concerns against expectations for US monetary policy and currency movements. Technical levels around $4,600 continue to act as support, while $4,700 remains a short-term resistance target.  

Market analysts suggest that continued diplomatic progress between Washington and Tehran could further temper USD strength and sustain demand for precious metals. Traders are watching exchange rate fluctuations closely, as gold often responds inversely to USD movements, influencing broader currency pair dynamics. With XAU/USD hovering near $4,691, short-term trading could remain choppy amid geopolitical headlines and shifting sentiment in forex markets.
