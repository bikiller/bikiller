AUD/USD traded sideways during Friday’s Asian session, struggling to build on the previous day’s rebound from below the 0.7100 mark, its lowest level in a week. The currency pair remained trapped within a two-week-old trading range, although the exchange rate continued to hold above the key 0.7150 level. Despite the lack of strong follow-through buying, spot prices stayed resilient, reflecting a cautious but constructive tone among forex traders.

The Australian dollar’s ability to remain above 0.7150 suggests that downside pressure has eased for now, even as USD demand limits further gains. Market participants continue to monitor broader risk sentiment, central bank expectations, and movements across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, for fresh direction. The pair’s recent recovery from sub-0.7100 levels indicates buyers are still active near short-term support zones.

From a technical perspective, AUD/USD remains range-bound, with neither bulls nor bears gaining decisive control. Holding above 0.7150 keeps the prospect of additional upside intact, while the broader consolidation pattern points to continued cautious trading. If the pair sustains current levels, it could be on track to post modest weekly gains, marking its first positive performance in three weeks and signaling improving sentiment toward the Australian dollar.
