West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude slipped below the $93.00 mark in early Asian trading, struggling to sustain the modest gains seen on Tuesday. Despite rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, supply pressures and cautious investor sentiment weighed on oil prices, pushing WTI closer to a weekly low. Traders noted that short-term technical levels around $93.50 acted as resistance, limiting upside momentum in commodity markets.  

The softer oil tone influenced currency markets, with the USD gaining against commodity-linked currencies such as the AUD and CAD. The USD/CAD exchange rate climbed modestly as weaker crude reduced Canadian dollar demand, while EUR/USD trading remained steady, reflecting a broader risk-off mood among forex investors. Analysts highlighted that energy market fluctuations often ripple through forex pairs, affecting both exchange rates and cross-asset positioning.  

Investors continue to monitor geopolitical developments and inventory reports for guidance, as any escalation could quickly shift market sentiment. Currency traders remain sensitive to oil price swings, balancing positions between safe-haven currencies and commodity-linked pairs. Technical indicators suggest a cautious trading environment, with key levels near $92.80–$93.50 defining near-term WTI support and resistance for both energy and related forex markets.
