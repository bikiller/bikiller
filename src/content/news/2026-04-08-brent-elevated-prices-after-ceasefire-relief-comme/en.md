Brent crude prices experienced a sharp drop of $15 following the announcement of a two-week ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran, coupled with the reopening of the crucial Strait of Hormuz. Despite this significant dip, the commodity remains firmly positioned near $95 per barrel, well above the pre-conflict price levels, reflecting the market's ongoing resilience.

The ceasefire relief has provided some temporary stability to oil markets, yet Brent continues to trade above $90, signaling that geopolitical tensions still exert a significant influence on pricing. The USD remains a key player, as oil price fluctuations directly impact USD-based transactions, affecting exchange rates across major pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY.
