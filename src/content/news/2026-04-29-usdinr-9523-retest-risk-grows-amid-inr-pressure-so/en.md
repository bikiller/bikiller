The USD/INR currency pair faces increasing pressure as analysts from Societe Generale highlight persistent headwinds for the Indian Rupee (INR). With India grappling with large import bills, particularly for Oil and Gold, the INR is under significant strain against the US Dollar (USD). As a result, the risk of a retest of the 95.23 level for USD/INR is rising.

This pressure on the INR comes at a time when global market conditions remain volatile, contributing to the currency’s ongoing struggles. With import costs continuing to rise, the exchange rate for USD/INR may experience further depreciation, as traders closely monitor the currency’s movements. The Indian Rupee’s weakness against the Dollar could lead to increased costs for imports, further impacting India's economic landscape.
