The AUD/USD currency pair is showing limited directional movement during Friday’s North American session, trading within a narrow range defined by key technical levels. The exchange rate currently hovers near the 20-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at 0.7187, while the 50-day SMA provides support at 0.7095. Traders are weighing this range as momentum indicators signal caution.  

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) has turned bearish, hinting at potential downward pressure despite the pair’s sideways behavior. Market participants are monitoring the interplay between short-term and medium-term SMAs, as any decisive break above or below these levels could trigger more pronounced moves. The AUD/USD remains sensitive to USD strength, broader risk sentiment, and cross-market flows including EUR/USD correlations.  

Overall, trading in AUD/USD suggests consolidation, with buyers and sellers in a tug-of-war between support and resistance. While short-term swings remain limited, technical traders may look for breakout opportunities near 0.7187 and 0.7095, as shifts in momentum could influence positioning. The pair’s performance continues to reflect global market dynamics, central bank cues, and shifts in USD demand.
