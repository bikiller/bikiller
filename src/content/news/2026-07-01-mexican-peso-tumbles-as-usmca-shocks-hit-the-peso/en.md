The Mexican peso extended losses against the US dollar on Wednesday, with the USD/MXN currency pair climbing to 17.55, up 0.37% in the session. The exchange rate reflected renewed demand for the greenback, as traders positioned amid shifting macro expectations. In forex trading, the pair showed steady upward momentum throughout the day, signaling continued pressure on the peso across spot markets.

The decline came as speculation intensified over a potential cancellation of the USMCA free trade agreement signed in 2020, raising concerns about Mexico’s trade outlook. Combined with broader US dollar strength, risk aversion weighed on emerging market currencies. Forex markets reacted as investors reassessed exposure, with USD demand increasing against the peso in volatile trading conditions.

For forex traders, the move underscores heightened sensitivity of the USD/MXN currency pair to political developments and trade policy headlines. Continued uncertainty around USMCA could sustain volatility and keep the exchange rate elevated, while US dollar strength remains a key driver across major currency pairs. Market participants will watch for further signals affecting short-term trading direction.
