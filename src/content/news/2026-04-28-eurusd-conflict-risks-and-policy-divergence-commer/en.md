EUR/USD is expected to trade in a narrow range in the short term as investors weigh geopolitical tensions and central bank policy signals. Commerzbank’s Antje Praefcke highlights that the unfolding Iran conflict has added a layer of uncertainty, prompting cautious trading. The exchange rate has seen limited directional momentum, with market participants hesitant to commit ahead of key monetary policy meetings from both the ECB and the Federal Reserve.  

The currency pair has remained resilient around current levels, as EUR strength is balanced by the USD’s safe-haven appeal. Traders are closely monitoring technical support and resistance zones, which are shaping intraday trading decisions. Any escalation in the Middle East could push the USD higher, while signals from central banks on rate expectations may tilt EUR/USD either way.  

Market participants are positioning for potential volatility around upcoming policy statements, but near-term swings are likely to be contained. The interplay between conflict risk and policy divergence is expected to guide exchange rate movements, keeping the pair confined to a defined range. Strategic traders may look to capitalize on short-term price oscillations while awaiting clearer directional cues.
