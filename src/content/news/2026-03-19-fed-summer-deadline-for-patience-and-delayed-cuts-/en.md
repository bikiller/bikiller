The Federal Reserve left interest rates unchanged at its latest FOMC meeting, signaling continued patience on inflation despite recent volatility. Chair Jerome Powell downplayed the significance of the Summary of Economic Projections, suggesting that policy adjustments remain data-dependent. TD Securities economists note that while rates hold for now, the Fed’s tolerance for delayed inflation normalization may expire by late summer, particularly as an oil-driven surge lifts headline inflation.  

Currency markets reacted to the Fed’s stance with the USD showing resilience across major pairs. The EUR/USD traded near 1.1020, pressured by the prospect of sustained Fed support, while the USD/JPY hovered around 146.50, reflecting the risk-adjusted demand for dollars. Traders are monitoring energy price developments closely, as any persistent oil shock could strengthen the USD further and reshape short-term trading strategies.  

Analysts highlight that delayed rate cuts, combined with elevated inflation, could increase volatility across forex markets. USD strength may persist against commodity-linked currencies, while safe-haven flows could push pairs like USD/CHF higher. Investors are positioning for potential summer tightening, and exchange rate trends suggest that the market is pricing in a narrower window for policy easing than previously expected.
