Gold (XAU/USD) is experiencing a downturn on Tuesday, retreating from Monday's six-week high as traders capitalize on recent gains. Currently, the precious metal is trading around $4,190, marking a decline of over 1% on the day. This price adjustment reflects a natural profit-taking phase following a significant rally that had piqued market interest.

Despite prevailing expectations of a dovish stance from the Federal Reserve, which could typically bolster gold prices, the currency pair is struggling to maintain its upward momentum. The current exchange rate dynamics highlight the delicate balance between profit realization and market sentiment, as investors weigh potential Federal Reserve policy shifts against ongoing economic uncertainties.
