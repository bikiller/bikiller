WTI US Oil extended its steep decline on Wednesday, sliding further in Asian and European trading sessions. West Texas Intermediate hovered near $69.70 per barrel, down 4.40% on the day at the time of writing, marking its weakest level since March 2. The move reflects sustained selling pressure across energy-linked assets as risk sentiment softens.

Prices fell as easing geopolitical tensions in the Gulf reduced supply disruption fears, prompting traders to unwind risk premiums built into crude benchmarks. Improved expectations around stable shipping routes and output resilience weighed on the commodity currency complex, with oil-sensitive FX pairs showing muted volatility. Market participants also reassessed demand outlooks amid mixed global growth signals and shifting inventory expectations.

On the forex side, the decline in crude prices offered mild relief to import-heavy economies while influencing USD strength dynamics through inflation expectations. A softer oil outlook can temper inflation pressures, potentially affecting USD and EUR exchange rate expectations. Traders continue to monitor WTI trading patterns as correlations between energy markets and currency pairs remain elevated ahead of macro data releases.
