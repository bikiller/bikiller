Gold prices steadied near key technical levels as traders awaited the upcoming US Consumer Price Index (CPI) report, while geopolitical tensions in the Middle East added a cautious undertone. The XAU/USD currency pair found support after closing the week’s initial bearish gap, buoyed by a softer US Dollar. Market participants monitored the USD index closely, as its mild pullback helped sustain gold around current exchange rates.  

In forex trading, safe-haven flows into gold typically intensify amid global uncertainties, influencing other major pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY. Analysts noted that while the USD showed relative weakness, expectations of higher US inflation could prompt renewed demand for the currency, potentially pressuring gold prices. Technical charts highlighted consolidation near $2,040 per ounce, with short-term support at $2,030 and resistance near $2,060, signaling limited directional momentum ahead of the CPI release.  

Investors also weighed broader market implications, including potential shifts in cross-currency positions and commodity-linked trades. A spike in CPI above forecasts could strengthen the USD, prompting adjustments in XAU/USD and related forex pairs, whereas softer readings might favor continued gold stability. Trading desks emphasized careful positioning, balancing hedging strategies with anticipation of volatility stemming from both macroeconomic data and geopolitical developments.
