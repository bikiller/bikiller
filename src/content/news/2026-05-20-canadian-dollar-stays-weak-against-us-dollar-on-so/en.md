USD/CAD is trading around 1.3773, with the Canadian dollar under pressure after a downside surprise in domestic Consumer Price Index (CPI) data. According to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret, the weaker inflation reading has weighed on CAD sentiment, leaving the currency on the back foot against the US dollar in broader forex trading sessions.

Market participants noted that the currency pair held near 1.3773 as the US dollar maintained firmer footing, benefiting from relative economic divergence. The exchange rate reflected cautious risk sentiment, with CAD lagging peers. In forex trading, USD demand remained supportive, while weaker inflation expectations in Canada continued to dampen upside momentum for the Canadian dollar.

Looking ahead, the USD/CAD currency pair may remain sensitive to further Canadian inflation signals and US dollar trends, with traders closely monitoring macroeconomic data for direction. Continued CAD underperformance could keep the exchange rate elevated, especially if US yields stay supportive. Overall, sentiment in forex markets favors near-term USD strength against the Canadian dollar.
