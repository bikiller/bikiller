The US Dollar Index (DXY) climbed to a 13-month high near 101.40 on Tuesday, extending its recent bullish momentum as traders reacted to global flash PMI data. Strength in the US currency reflected broad demand for USD across major currency pairs, with investors reassessing growth differentials and positioning in forex trading sessions amid shifting macroeconomic signals.  

The latest S&P Global US Purchasing Managers Index (PMI) reinforced the upbeat tone, with the Manufacturing PMI rising to 55.7 in June, the strongest reading since May 2022. The data signaled sustained business expansion, supporting the US dollar exchange rate as resilience in economic activity contrasted with softer expectations in other major economies.  

In currency trading, the stronger data backdrop bolstered USD demand against peers such as EUR and GBP, keeping downside pressure on the euro-dollar pair. Market participants now weigh whether persistent US strength could delay easing expectations, sustaining elevated volatility across forex markets and reinforcing the dollar’s dominance in near-term positioning.
