The US Dollar's recent rebound has slowed, with market sentiment shifting as US-Iran tensions show signs of easing. As geopolitical risks subside, traders are recalibrating their positions, leading to a pullback in the greenback's gains. The EUR/USD exchange rate has moved closer to 1.0900, reflecting the shift in investor appetite away from the USD.

Oil prices have also contributed to the dollar's retreat, falling amidst concerns over global demand. This dynamic has further pressured the USD, especially against commodity-linked currencies like the Canadian Dollar. With USD/CAD testing support near 1.3400, markets are adjusting their outlook for the greenback in the short term.
