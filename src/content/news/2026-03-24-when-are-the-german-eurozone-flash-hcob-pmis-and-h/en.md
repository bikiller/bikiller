Traders are eyeing the German and Eurozone flash HCOB Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI) for March, set for release today at 08:30 and 09:00 GMT, respectively. The preliminary readings will provide early insight into manufacturing and services activity, which could influence the EUR/USD currency pair. Analysts anticipate that stronger-than-expected data may support the euro, while weaker numbers could pressure the exchange rate against the USD.  

EUR/USD trading has been hovering near key technical levels, with recent support around 1.0800 and resistance near 1.0900. A surprise uptick in the PMIs could push the pair toward the upper boundary, attracting short-term buyers, whereas disappointing results might accelerate a decline back toward support. Traders are closely monitoring these releases for clues on the euro’s near-term strength and potential USD resilience.  

Market sentiment ahead of the data reflects caution, with the USD showing relative stability amid mixed risk appetite. Investors are weighing the euro’s performance against broader economic signals, including inflation trends and ECB policy expectations. The flash PMIs may act as a catalyst for intraday volatility in EUR/USD, guiding trading strategies and shaping the next directional move for this major currency pair.
