US President Donald Trump announced a new round of tariffs on European Union (EU) vehicles, specifically targeting cars and trucks. This move, revealed via Truth Social, is expected to increase trade tensions between the US and the EU, which could have a significant impact on the EUR/USD exchange rate. The new tariffs are likely to fuel volatility in global markets, with traders closely monitoring the potential effects on the EUR as it struggles to hold ground against a strengthening USD.

The latest tariff increase could further weaken the euro, especially if the US dollar continues its bullish momentum. Market sentiment already favors the USD amid concerns over global trade, and with this fresh development, the EUR/USD pair could test technical support levels around 1.0500. If the tariffs escalate tensions further, the pair may experience additional pressure, with the euro potentially falling to new lows. Traders will be looking for any signs of retaliation from the EU, which could add further complexity to the forex market.
