Chinese yuan has strengthened, with USD/CNY trading below the 6.80 level, marking its strongest performance since February 2023, according to Societe Generale analysts. The move reflects sustained appreciation in the currency pair as the exchange rate drifts lower, signalling firmer demand for the yuan in FX trading ahead of key macro developments.

Ahead of the upcoming US–China summit, market positioning has shifted in favour of the yuan, supported by improved risk sentiment and expectations of more stable bilateral economic dialogue. Traders in the foreign exchange market have reduced USD exposure, contributing to softer US dollar dynamics against major peers including EUR and CNY. The yuan’s strength also reflects tighter onshore liquidity conditions.

For currency traders, the break below 6.80 in USD/CNY highlights a technically significant level, potentially reinforcing further downside momentum in the pair. If sustained, it could influence broader Asian FX trading trends and spill over into USD index pressure. Investors remain alert to policy signals from both central banks, as volatility in the exchange rate may rise around summit headlines.
