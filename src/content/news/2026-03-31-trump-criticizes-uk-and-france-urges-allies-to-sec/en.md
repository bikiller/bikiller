US President Donald Trump sharply criticized the United Kingdom and France on Truth Social, urging allies to secure their own oil supply routes amid rising Middle East tensions. The remarks coincided with heightened trading volatility in the forex market, with the USD gaining against major currencies. The EUR/USD currency pair slid to 1.0820, pressured by safe-haven demand for the greenback, while GBP/USD fell to 1.2265 as uncertainty weighed on European markets.  

Traders noted that Trump’s comments added to geopolitical risk sentiment, driving USD/JPY above 150.00 and lifting the USD index to 105.3. Oil price fluctuations also fueled currency moves, with Brent crude briefly topping $88.40 a barrel. Market participants closely monitored technical support at 1.0800 for EUR/USD and 1.2200 for GBP/USD, as short-term trading momentum favored USD strength across major pairs.  

Analysts suggest that persistent geopolitical tensions could prolong USD gains while keeping the euro and pound under pressure. Forex traders may see heightened intraday swings as investors adjust positions ahead of potential Middle East disruptions. Risk appetite appeared fragile, reinforcing USD’s role as a safe-haven currency, while EUR/USD and GBP/USD await clearer directional cues from both oil markets and diplomatic developments.
