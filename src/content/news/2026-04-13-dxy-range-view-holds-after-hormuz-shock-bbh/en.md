The US Dollar maintained its range-bound stance after geopolitical tensions flared in the Middle East. Following the US naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz, Brent crude surged back above $100 a barrel, triggering renewed risk aversion in global markets. Traders responded by favoring safe-haven currencies, sending the USD higher against major pairs including the EUR, JPY, and GBP, while equities faced pressure from heightened uncertainty.  

Analysts at Brown Brothers Harriman highlight that the currency pair dynamics remain influenced by oil price swings and geopolitical risk. The USD’s strength was particularly noticeable in EUR/USD trading, where the exchange rate tested key support levels near recent lows. Market participants are weighing the potential impact of sustained oil above $100, with implications for inflation expectations, interest rate differentials, and central bank policy positioning.  

Technical signals suggest the DXY index is holding within its established range, despite the Hormuz-induced shock. Traders remain cautious, monitoring whether continued Middle East tensions could further lift the USD and pressure commodity-linked currencies. Overall, the interplay between Brent, USD exchange rates, and risk sentiment is dictating short-term trading flows, with volatility likely to persist until the geopolitical picture clarifies.
