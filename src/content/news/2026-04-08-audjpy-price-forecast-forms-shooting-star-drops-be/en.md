The AUD/JPY currency pair slipped below the 112.00 mark on Thursday, following the formation of a shooting-star candlestick that signaled potential bearish pressure. Traders noted that the Australian Dollar’s earlier gains versus the Japanese Yen were partly fueled by a brief surge in risk appetite, supported by a two-week pause in the US-Iran tensions in the Middle East.  

Market participants remain cautious as the exchange rate tests technical resistance, with the shooting-star pattern suggesting sellers are gaining influence. While the AUD had been buoyed by global risk sentiment, the current retreat reflects traders locking in profits amid mixed economic signals from both Australia and Japan.  

Analysts expect the pair’s movements to remain sensitive to shifts in USD trends and broader forex sentiment. A sustained drop below 112.00 could invite further downside, while a rebound might hinge on renewed appetite for higher-yielding currencies like the AUD. EUR and USD trading activity is also likely to influence the short-term dynamics, as traders weigh global risk factors against domestic fundamentals.
