Gold (XAU/USD) continues to weaken, dipping below the $5,200 mark and reaching a fresh daily low during the European trading session on Wednesday. The precious metal has struggled to gain traction as traders remain cautious ahead of the highly anticipated US CPI data release. This retreat comes after a brief attempt to stabilize above the critical support level.

The US Dollar (USD) has seen renewed buying interest, with the greenback gaining ground against a basket of currencies. A bounce from a one-week low has further pressured gold prices, as the USD’s strength dampens the appeal of dollar-denominated assets. As traders await key inflation data from the US, the market's focus remains on potential shifts in monetary policy, adding to the uncertainty surrounding gold's near-term outlook.
