Gold (XAU/USD) remained range-bound on Thursday as traders weighed the fragile ceasefire between the US and Iran, keeping markets cautious. Prices hovered near $1,985 an ounce, with the metal struggling to break key technical levels amid heightened geopolitical uncertainty. The USD maintained broad strength, limiting gold’s upside, while safe-haven demand provided intermittent support during intraday trading sessions.  

In currency markets, the euro edged slightly lower against the dollar, with EUR/USD dipping toward 1.0960 as investors favored USD assets amid rising tensions. Traders noted that any escalation in the Middle East could spur volatility across major currency pairs, pushing safe-haven flows into both gold and the greenback. Technical analysts highlighted resistance near $1,990 and support at $1,975, framing potential intraday ranges.  

Market participants remain cautious ahead of further news on the ceasefire, with gold trading constrained by a mix of risk-off sentiment and dollar resilience. The combination of geopolitical risk and USD strength continues to influence both commodity and forex markets, leaving traders focused on key levels and exchange rate movements for potential trading opportunities. Monitoring these dynamics will be crucial for positioning in XAU/USD and major currency pairs over the coming sessions.
