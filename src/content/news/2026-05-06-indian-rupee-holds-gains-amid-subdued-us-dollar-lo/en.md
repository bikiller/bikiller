The Indian Rupee maintained its gains on Wednesday as the US Dollar softened and global oil prices retreated. The USD/INR currency pair extended losses for the second consecutive session, trading near the 95.00 mark during Asian hours. Market participants cited a combination of subdued US Dollar sentiment and easing crude costs as key drivers behind the Rupee’s resilience against the greenback.  

Traders are closely monitoring India’s upcoming HSBC Composite and Services PMI releases, which could influence near-term rupee dynamics and broader trading sentiment. Any upside surprise in the PMI data may support the domestic currency further, while weaker-than-expected readings could invite renewed USD buying pressure. Technical analysts note that the 95.00 level is acting as a psychological support, with the next resistance seen around 95.50.  

In the wider forex market, the Rupee’s movements are being tracked alongside other major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD, as global risk appetite remains cautious. Lower oil prices are providing an additional cushion for the INR, potentially easing import-related pressures. As trading continues, investors are weighing both domestic indicators and external factors, shaping exchange rate expectations and short-term positioning in the USD/INR currency pair.
