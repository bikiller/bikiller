The Bank of Japan (BoJ) decided to keep its policy rate unchanged, maintaining the current ultra-loose monetary stance. This decision has left the Japanese yen (JPY) under pressure, with the USD/JPY trading near 131.50. Despite ongoing global inflation concerns, the BoJ's commitment to loose policy signals continued weakness for the yen against major currencies like the US dollar and euro.

Gold prices surged towards $5,000 amid increasing market uncertainty and inflation fears. Investors flocked to the precious metal as a hedge, driving prices up over 2% on the day. This rise reflects growing concerns over economic stability, pushing the XAU/USD exchange rate closer to key resistance levels. The broader forex market remains cautious, with the USD continuing to dominate, especially against the JPY and EUR.
