Gold extended gains in Wednesday’s North American session, with the XAU/USD currency pair climbing to $4,530, up 1%. Traders reacted to the Federal Reserve’s latest meeting minutes, which indicated that most officials favored setting the stage for a rate hike. The announcement weighed on the USD, allowing gold to attract buying interest as a hedge against potential monetary tightening.  

The US Dollar saw broad weakness against major currencies, including the EUR, amid market speculation over the timing and pace of future Fed rate adjustments. Currency pair trading showed elevated volatility as investors reassessed risk assets, with gold benefiting from its safe-haven appeal. Analysts note that technical levels near $4,500 provided support, fueling momentum for short-term upward swings in XAU/USD.  

Market participants are closely monitoring Fed communications for hints on interest rate policy, which continues to influence exchange rates and cross-asset flows. Rising gold prices may signal caution among traders regarding USD strength, while EUR/USD and other currency pairs react to shifts in sentiment. As trading unfolds, XAU/USD could test higher resistance points, reflecting the interplay between central bank guidance, currency movements, and investor positioning.
