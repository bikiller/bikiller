USD/CHF currency pair holds firm around 0.8070 on Tuesday, following a 0.29% decline on Monday. The exchange rate remains under pressure as broad US dollar weakness persists, driven by month-end portfolio flows and improving global risk appetite. Despite the softer USD tone, trading activity suggests buyers are still defending current levels, limiting downside momentum in early-week forex sessions.

Technically, the pair continues to hold a key support zone near 0.8070, a level that traders are closely monitoring for directional cues. Bulls are still targeting a retest of the year-to-date high, provided USD selling pressure stabilises. Sentiment across currency markets remains sensitive to shifts in USD and EUR dynamics, with risk-on flows helping to cushion downside moves in the short term.

Looking ahead, USD/CHF trading conditions are likely to remain range-bound unless a decisive break below support triggers fresh downside momentum. Conversely, sustained USD stabilisation could reopen the path toward higher resistance levels. Traders are expected to watch incoming US data and broader forex sentiment for confirmation, as liquidity flows and risk appetite continue to dictate near-term price action.
