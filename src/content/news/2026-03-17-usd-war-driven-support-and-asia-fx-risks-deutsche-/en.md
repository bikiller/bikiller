The ongoing Iran conflict has significantly impacted global markets, driving a correlation between energy prices and the US Dollar. With rising oil prices and weakening global growth, the Dollar has found support, strengthening against major currencies. The USD is particularly benefiting from this dynamic, with increased demand from risk aversion and energy-driven uncertainty.

Asian currencies, however, are under severe pressure as the broader Dollar trend unfolds. Emerging market currencies in Asia are seeing sharp declines, reflecting concerns over regional vulnerabilities amid the global economic slowdown. The EUR/USD exchange rate is hovering just above 1.05, with traders eyeing further weakness in the Euro as energy and geopolitical risks continue to overshadow market sentiment.
