The US Dollar (USD) has struggled to gain momentum despite rising oil prices and growing geopolitical uncertainty. ING strategist Francesco Pesole highlights that the USD's upward potential remains limited as resilient US equities and month-end flows dampen its performance. The EUR/USD exchange rate remains relatively stable, with the currency pair hovering around 1.0900, showing little reaction to global market shifts.

Oil price surges, driven by supply concerns, have failed to provide significant support to the USD. Although oil impacts inflation and interest rate expectations, the dollar’s strength is capped as strong risk sentiment keeps US equities buoyant. The ongoing month-end flows are also contributing to the muted movement in the USD, keeping the currency pair trading within narrow ranges. Traders remain cautious, awaiting further signals from economic data and geopolitical developments.
