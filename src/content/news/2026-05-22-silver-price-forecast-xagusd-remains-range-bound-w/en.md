Silver (XAG/USD) stayed trapped in a narrow trading range on Friday as investors refrained from taking strong positions amid lingering uncertainty over US-Iran negotiations. The precious metal hovered near $76.00 at the time of writing, putting the USD-denominated commodity on track for a largely unchanged weekly performance as cautious sentiment limited volatility across currency and commodity markets.

Technical signals continued to reflect subdued momentum in the XAG/USD currency pair. Both the Relative Strength Index (RSI) and MACD pointed to weak directional conviction, suggesting that buyers and sellers remain evenly matched. The lack of a decisive trend has kept trading activity restrained, with market participants waiting for a stronger catalyst to drive the exchange rate beyond its recent range.

Broader market caution, tied to geopolitical developments and shifting expectations around the USD outlook, also influenced sentiment toward silver and related safe-haven assets. In the absence of fresh macroeconomic drivers from the US or Europe, traders in USD and EUR markets are closely monitoring geopolitical headlines for clues on the next move in XAG/USD and wider cross-asset trading conditions.
