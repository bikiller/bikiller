European equities saw a strong rebound, with the Stoxx 600 rising 2% and OMX Nordic gaining nearly 3%. In contrast, US indices ended the day slightly lower. The rally in European markets was driven by tech and growth stocks, which outperformed their value counterparts despite a modest uptick in US 10-year yields. The positive sentiment in equities supported a more favorable environment for risk-sensitive currencies.

The movement in global stocks coincided with fluctuating oil prices, which added volatility to the broader market. As oil prices swung, the EUR/USD exchange rate remained relatively stable, reflecting investor caution. With growth stocks leading the way, the dollar's performance against the euro was less influenced by yields, indicating that market sentiment was more focused on equities than bond market movements. This dynamic is likely to continue shaping currency pairs in the short term.
