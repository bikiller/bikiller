EUR/JPY, the currency pair tracking the euro against the Japanese yen, pared its daily losses during Asian trading hours on Thursday but remained in negative territory. The exchange rate was hovering around 183.80, recovering from recent weakness as buyers stepped in near key technical areas. The move reflects cautious market sentiment toward the euro while traders monitor broader USD and global currency trends.

The pair’s rebound followed a bounce from a symmetrical triangle pattern, a technical formation often viewed as a signal of potential volatility ahead. Traders are watching whether EUR/JPY can regain momentum above the 184.00 level, which could strengthen the bullish outlook and attract further buying interest in the forex market.

However, continued pressure below 184.00 may indicate that sellers remain in control, keeping the currency pair vulnerable to further declines. Market participants are assessing economic signals, central bank expectations, and risk sentiment that could influence the next direction for EUR/JPY trading.
