Thailand’s Baht (THB) remains under pressure as weak domestic output and rising energy costs weigh on sentiment, according to Commerzbank. February manufacturing production was flat year-on-year, reflecting refinery maintenance disruptions and softer external demand. A relatively strong THB has also dampened export competitiveness, influencing the USD/THB exchange rate and limiting upside in currency pair trading.

Energy prices have added further strain, with authorities reducing fuel subsidies and cutting excise taxes in response to the oil rally. However, limited fiscal space constrains the government’s ability to cushion the shock, raising concerns among forex traders about Thailand’s macro stability. Elevated oil import costs could widen the current account deficit, a key factor for THB valuation against USD and EUR.

In the near term, the Baht’s outlook remains fragile as external demand struggles to recover and policy flexibility narrows. Market participants are likely to monitor global oil price trends and capital flows closely, as these will shape THB exchange rate dynamics. Persistent headwinds may keep USD/THB biased higher, especially if risk sentiment deteriorates.
