Canadian households experienced a notable boost in wealth in 2025, with net worth climbing 5.8% to an all-time high, according to National Bank of Canada analyst Matthieu Arseneau. Financial assets drove the increase, outpacing moderate credit growth, while the S&P/TSX posted a 31.7% total return. Rising gold prices supported equity gains, attracting investor interest and influencing portfolio allocations across both domestic and international markets.  

The stronger Canadian financial position has implications for the CAD/USD and CAD/EUR exchange rates, as higher household wealth tends to support domestic spending and bolster the Canadian dollar. Forex traders have noted increased demand for CAD, with technical levels showing consolidation near recent highs. Market sentiment reflects a more resilient economic backdrop, with equities and commodity-linked currencies reacting positively to asset gains.  

Analysts suggest that the wealth surge could encourage further trading activity, especially in equities and currency pairs tied to Canada’s resource sector. The correlation between gold and the Canadian dollar remains significant, with gold’s rally enhancing CAD strength. For forex traders, monitoring net worth trends alongside technical levels in USD/CAD and EUR/CAD could provide key insights for short- and medium-term positioning.
