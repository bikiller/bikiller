Rising global oil and gas prices are pushing Thailand toward a higher inflation environment, according to UOB economists Enrico Tanuwidjaja and Sathit Talaengsatya. The surge in energy costs is lifting the risk of a cost-push shock, eroding the low-inflation backdrop that had supported the Thai baht. Traders are now closely watching the USD/THB exchange rate, which has shown increased volatility amid concerns over imported energy expenses.  

The baht’s trading dynamics are also influenced by broader forex flows, as investors weigh USD strength against regional currencies. UOB notes that persistent crude price gains could pressure domestic fuel prices, prompting adjustments in monetary policy expectations. This has made EUR/THB and USD/THB ranges more sensitive to global commodity swings, with technical levels near 36.50 for USD/THB drawing particular attention from short-term traders.  

Market participants are recalibrating positions as oil-related cost shocks ripple through inflation forecasts. Stronger USD demand amid global energy uncertainty may weigh on regional currencies, while the baht faces a balancing act between export competitiveness and import-driven inflation. Analysts suggest that sustained oil price pressure could keep the Thai central bank cautious, impacting forward-looking exchange rate strategies and shaping trading patterns in both spot and derivative markets.
