Canadian Dollar weakens on Monday even as oil prices edge higher, with the USD/CAD currency pair extending its advance for a second consecutive session. The exchange rate trades near 1.4230 at the time of writing, up 0.20% on the day. The move reflects sustained demand for the US Dollar in forex trading, keeping the pair bid despite supportive crude oil dynamics for the Canadian currency.  

Underlying strength in the US Dollar continues to dominate forex markets, offsetting the traditional positive correlation between the Canadian Dollar and rising oil prices. Even as crude benchmarks firm, CAD remains pressured as USD buying persists across major currency pairs. Market participants point to broader risk sentiment and relative interest rate expectations as key drivers supporting the greenback against commodity-linked currencies like the Canadian Dollar.  

USD/CAD continues to trade around the 1.4230 exchange rate level, maintaining a short-term bullish bias after back-to-back daily gains. Traders in the forex market are watching whether sustained US Dollar strength can drive further upside momentum, particularly against commodity currencies. In broader FX terms, movements in EUR/USD and risk appetite remain key signals influencing cross-currency flows and near-term positioning.
