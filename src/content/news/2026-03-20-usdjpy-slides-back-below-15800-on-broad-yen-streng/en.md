USD/JPY fell 1.25% on Thursday, slipping below the 158.00 level and closing near 157.80. The decline came amid broad-based strength in the Japanese yen, which gained traction against major currencies. Traders reacted to various global market dynamics, including shifts in risk sentiment and concerns over potential economic slowdowns, which supported the yen’s safe-haven appeal.

The movement below the key 158.00 mark could signal further downside potential for USD/JPY in the short term. With the currency pair facing increased pressure, market participants are eyeing possible technical support at 157.50. Any sustained weakness in the USD could lead to more significant corrections in this currency pair, especially if global economic uncertainty persists, keeping the yen’s bid intact.
