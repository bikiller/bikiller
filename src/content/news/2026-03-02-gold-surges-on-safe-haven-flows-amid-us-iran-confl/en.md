Gold (XAU/USD) has surged at the start of the week, opening with a bullish gap amid rising geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran. The ongoing conflict has fueled a surge in safe-haven demand, driving gold prices higher. At the time of writing, XAU/USD trades around $5,393, marking its highest point in over a month. The US dollar (USD) remains under pressure as investors flock to gold as a store of value.

The recent geopolitical developments have pushed gold to the forefront of global trading. As the US-Iran situation escalates, the demand for safe-haven assets has intensified, benefiting precious metals like gold. The move above the $5,393 level has provided a technical boost for the currency pair, suggesting further upside potential if tensions continue to simmer. Traders are closely watching the developments, with XAU/USD likely to remain volatile in the short term.
