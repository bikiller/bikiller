The US Dollar (USD) experienced a pullback on Wednesday, despite robust employment figures and a positive ISM Services PMI. After a two-day surge that brought the US Dollar Index (DXY) close to the 100.00 level, the Greenback's rally was halted as market sentiment was overshadowed by ongoing tensions between the US and Iran. 

As trading continued, the EUR/USD pair was affected, with the exchange rate reflecting the USD's weakness. Analysts noted that while the labor market data was strong, geopolitical concerns have created a cautious atmosphere among traders, leading to a reduction in the USD's momentum. The market reaction suggests that external factors may continue to influence currency movements, keeping investors on edge.
