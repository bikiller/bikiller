The Indian Rupee extended its losses against the US Dollar on Tuesday, with the USD/INR currency pair climbing toward the 94.50 mark. After a brief pause in early trading, the exchange rate resumed its upward trajectory as sustained pressure from higher crude oil prices weighed on the rupee. Traders noted that the persistent energy cost shock is keeping the local currency under pressure, with market participants adjusting positions accordingly.  

Market sentiment favored the USD, supported by global dollar strength and robust inflows into safe-haven assets. The rupee’s depreciation reflects broader concerns over India’s trade balance and rising import costs, particularly in oil. Analysts highlighted that the USD/INR could test previous resistance levels near 94.70 if crude prices remain elevated, influencing both short-term trading strategies and hedging decisions for exporters and importers.  

Investors are watching the broader forex landscape, with USD trading against other major currencies like the EUR and JPY also showing relative strength. The move in USD/INR underscores how external factors, including commodity prices and global market dynamics, continue to shape exchange rate trends. Market participants are expected to remain cautious, balancing dollar buying with potential intervention signals from domestic authorities.
