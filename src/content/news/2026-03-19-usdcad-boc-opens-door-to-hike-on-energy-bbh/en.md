USD/CAD is currently trading around 1.3735, facing resistance at the 200-day moving average. The currency pair has seen a slight uptick as the Bank of Canada (BoC) held its overnight rate steady at 2.25%. However, the BoC removed its previous guidance that the current policy stance is suitable, signaling a potential shift toward tightening. 

The central bank's shift in tone is largely driven by the rise in energy prices, with the BoC indicating that it may consider hiking rates if energy prices remain elevated. This change in outlook has added bullish pressure to the Canadian dollar, with traders adjusting expectations for future rate moves in response to energy market developments.
