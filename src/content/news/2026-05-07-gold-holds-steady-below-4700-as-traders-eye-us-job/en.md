Gold (XAU/USD) remained steady near $4,685 in early Asian trading on Friday, with investors adopting a cautious stance ahead of the US April jobs report. Market participants are closely watching the employment data for clues on the Federal Reserve’s next move, as stronger-than-expected numbers could boost the USD and weigh on safe-haven assets like gold. The metal has hovered just below the $4,700 mark in recent sessions, reflecting a balance between risk sentiment and inflation concerns.  

The broader forex market shows the USD maintaining strength against major currency pairs, including the EUR and JPY, as traders anticipate potential volatility from the upcoming economic release. Gold’s limited movement suggests that traders are largely sidelined, awaiting clearer direction from both macroeconomic indicators and geopolitical developments, including reports of a potential US-Iran peace deal that could influence risk appetite and demand for precious metals.  

Technical analysts note that sustaining above $4,680 could reinforce short-term support, while a break below $4,670 may open the door to further downside pressure. With trading volumes relatively muted, market watchers remain focused on the interplay between USD strength, global inflation expectations, and safe-haven flows, which will likely dictate gold’s near-term trajectory in both forex and commodities markets.
