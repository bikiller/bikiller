Commerzbank's Tatha Ghose has set a year-end target of 55.0 for the USD/TRY currency pair, reflecting the market's response to the Turkish central bank's decision to maintain its current policy. The bank's worst-case scenario has materialized, as policymakers opted for foreign exchange intervention and ad hoc liquidity tightening rather than implementing necessary rate hikes. 

This decision has implications for the exchange rate, as traders now navigate a landscape marked by volatility and uncertainty. Investors are likely to remain cautious, monitoring further developments in Turkey's monetary policy and its impact on the Turkish lira against major currencies like the USD and EUR. The lack of decisive action raises concerns about inflation and economic stability in the region, potentially influencing future trading strategies.
