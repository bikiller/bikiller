Gold hovered near $4,545 in early Asian trading on Friday, showing little directional movement as markets await updates on US-Iran ceasefire negotiations. Traders are cautious, keeping the precious metal anchored just below the $4,550 mark, while key currency pairs like USD/EUR remain under close observation for signals of risk sentiment shifts that could influence safe-haven flows.

The XAU/USD trading range remains tight, reflecting a market digesting geopolitical headlines without committing to major positions. Analysts note that any breakthrough in talks could trigger volatility, impacting not only gold but also correlated assets such as silver, oil, and broader USD-based exchange rates. For now, technical levels around $4,545 act as support, with resistance near $4,550 holding firm.

Currency markets are showing muted reactions alongside gold, with the USD index trading in a narrow band as investors balance geopolitical caution with underlying economic data. Forex traders are monitoring EUR/USD and GBP/USD for potential spillover effects, while market participants weigh the implications of a ceasefire on global risk appetite and safe-haven demand, which could shift trading strategies in the coming sessions.
