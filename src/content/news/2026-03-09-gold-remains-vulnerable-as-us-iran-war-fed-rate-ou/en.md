Gold (XAU/USD) is showing signs of consolidation on Monday after heavy selling pressure earlier this week, as traders reassess the impact of geopolitical tensions and US monetary policy expectations. The precious metal struggled to hold above key support levels near $1,950 an ounce, with the dollar (USD) strengthening across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, as investors sought safe-haven liquidity amid renewed Middle East concerns.  

Market sentiment remains cautious, with the US-Iran conflict fueling risk aversion and driving flows into the greenback. Gold’s exchange rate continues to react to shifts in the Federal Reserve’s rate outlook, with traders weighing the potential for higher interest rates against the metal’s traditional hedge appeal. Short-term technical levels suggest resistance around $1,970, keeping upward momentum limited.  

Traders are also eyeing broader forex movements, with the EUR/USD pair retreating from weekly highs as USD demand rises. The interplay of macroeconomic data, geopolitical uncertainty, and central bank guidance is likely to keep gold and major currencies in a tight range, prompting traders to monitor key support and resistance levels closely for potential breakout opportunities in the coming sessions.
