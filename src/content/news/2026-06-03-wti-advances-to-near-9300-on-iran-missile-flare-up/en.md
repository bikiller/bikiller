West Texas Intermediate (WTI), the US crude oil benchmark, climbed to around $92.90 during early European trading on Wednesday, extending its recent gains as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East intensified. Market sentiment was driven by renewed concerns surrounding Iran following a missile-related flare-up, which raised doubts about the prospects for a peace agreement between the United States and Iran. The developments supported crude prices as traders assessed potential risks to global energy supplies.

The advance in oil prices also influenced broader financial markets, including forex trading, where energy-linked currencies and major currency pairs remained in focus. Higher crude prices can strengthen inflation expectations and affect the outlook for the USD exchange rate, particularly as investors reassess the implications for US monetary policy. Meanwhile, EUR/USD and other major currency pairs may experience increased volatility as markets react to shifting risk sentiment.

With WTI holding near the $93.00 mark, traders are closely monitoring geopolitical headlines for further direction. Any escalation in tensions could provide additional support for oil prices, while signs of diplomatic progress may ease supply concerns and trigger profit-taking. For now, uncertainty surrounding US-Iran relations remains a key driver of both commodity and currency market movements.
