The EUR/USD exchange rate faces heightened volatility due to Europe's energy crisis and ongoing geopolitical tensions. Disruptions in oil flows via the Druzhba pipeline have raised concerns over energy security, which could weigh heavily on the euro. This, coupled with potential EU financing for Ukraine, keeps traders cautious, as uncertainties around fiscal support and energy stability mount. 

As a result, the EUR has struggled to maintain upward momentum, hovering around 1.0850. The continued pressure from these energy shocks has left traders wary of a deeper sell-off, with key resistance at 1.0900 and support at 1.0800. While geopolitical risks linger, the broader EUR/USD outlook remains fragile, as the market monitors further developments in the EU's fiscal stance and its impact on inflation and growth forecasts.
