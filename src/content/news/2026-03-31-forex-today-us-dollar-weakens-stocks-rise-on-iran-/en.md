The US Dollar Index (DXY) dipped to around 100.00 on Tuesday, reflecting a broader weakness in the greenback. The USD lost its safe-haven appeal as investor sentiment improved, driven by increasing optimism over a potential de-escalation in the Middle East. This shift caused a decline in demand for the USD, traditionally sought during times of geopolitical tension.

In currency trading, the EUR/USD pair saw gains as the euro benefited from the dollar's retreat. Meanwhile, other major currency pairs also rallied against the USD. The weakening of the US Dollar, combined with a rise in stock prices, suggests that traders are positioning for a more stable global outlook. Investors are closely watching geopolitical developments for further clues on the market's next move.
