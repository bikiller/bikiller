The Indian Rupee (INR) turned slightly weaker against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, giving up early gains even as the greenback broadly underperformed ahead of key US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) data. The USD/INR currency pair saw cautious trading, with intraday sentiment shifting after initial rupee strength faded in the foreign exchange market.

Market participants remained cautious as global forex markets positioned for potential volatility around the US jobs report, a key driver of USD direction. While the dollar index showed softness, the Indian Rupee failed to extend gains due to uneven capital flows and lingering import-related demand, keeping the USD/INR exchange rate under mild upward pressure.

Looking ahead, traders expect the USD/INR pair to remain range-bound until the NFP release provides clearer direction for the US Dollar. Short-term volatility may rise, with market participants adjusting positions across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/INR, as they assess interest rate expectations and broader risk sentiment in the global forex market.
