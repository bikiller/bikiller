ABN AMRO economists highlight that the recent surge in oil prices, driven by tensions around Iran, could prompt the Federal Reserve to reconsider its monetary stance. With Brent crude climbing above $90 a barrel, the USD has seen heightened volatility against major currencies. Traders are closely watching the USD/EUR and USD/JPY pairs, where the dollar has gained modestly, reflecting safe-haven demand amid energy-driven market uncertainty.  

The report notes that any Fed reaction, whether a pause or acceleration in rate adjustments, will likely ripple across currency markets. The USD/CHF pair, currently trading near 0.9300, and the GBP/USD, hovering around 1.2650, could experience short-term swings as investors digest shifts in U.S. inflation expectations. Market participants are positioning for potential policy signals, balancing energy costs, and growth risks in their trading strategies.  

ABN AMRO underscores that while the energy shock elevates risk sentiment, technical levels remain key. The USD/JPY faces resistance near 145.50, and a decisive move could influence broader trading dynamics. Meanwhile, EUR/USD support near 1.0850 may hold if the dollar strengthens modestly. Currency traders are monitoring both geopolitical developments and central bank responses to navigate near-term volatility and adjust positions accordingly.
