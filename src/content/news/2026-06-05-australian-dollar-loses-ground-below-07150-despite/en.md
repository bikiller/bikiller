The Australian Dollar (AUD) dipped below 0.7150, slipping to around 0.7120 against the US Dollar (USD) in early European trading on Friday. Despite the Reserve Bank of Australia’s hawkish stance, traders appear cautious, weighing the impact of geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The currency pair’s retreat highlights the market’s sensitivity to global risk sentiment, with investors favoring safe-haven assets amid uncertainty.  

The AUD/USD exchange rate decline comes as traders anticipate the US May employment report later in the session, a key economic indicator that could influence USD strength. Market participants are watching for shifts in job growth and wage data, which may affect the Fed’s policy outlook. Meanwhile, the broader trading environment shows the Australian Dollar under pressure against major currencies, including the Euro (EUR), as risk aversion dominates.  

Technical observers note that a sustained move below 0.7150 could open the door for further downside, testing support levels around 0.7100. Conversely, any upside reversal would require a pickup in demand for the AUD or risk-on sentiment in global markets. Traders remain attentive to both geopolitical developments and US macroeconomic releases as determinants for near-term currency movements.
