Gold (XAU/USD) extended its recovery on Wednesday, rebounding from a sharp decline earlier this week that saw the precious metal hit four-month lows. Buyers reentered the market after the selloff, pushing prices back above key technical levels. At the time of writing, gold is trading around $1,870, a notable recovery from recent lows near $1,850, driven by a cooling in oil prices and ongoing geopolitical tensions.

The market reaction follows a drop in oil prices, which eased inflation concerns and provided some relief to commodity-linked currencies. Meanwhile, tensions surrounding Iran’s resistance to the U.S. ceasefire plan added further uncertainty, supporting gold’s safe-haven appeal. As the USD maintains a strong presence in global forex trading, the XAU/USD pair is closely watched for any signs of further volatility or a shift in investor sentiment toward risk assets.
