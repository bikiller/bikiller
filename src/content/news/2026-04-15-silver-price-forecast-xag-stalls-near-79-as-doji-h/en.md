Silver (XAG/USD) stalled near $79 on Wednesday as a doji candle appeared around the 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at $79.09, signaling hesitation among traders. The indecision reflects a balance between bullish sentiment aiming for the next cycle high at $90.01, set on March 10, and caution over potential retracement toward the 100-day SMA support at $76. Exchange rate swings in USD and broader forex trends have influenced this pause in trading momentum.  

The currency pair’s hesitation comes amid mixed signals from global markets. Strength in the USD has weighed on silver, limiting upward momentum, while EUR and other major currencies continue to fluctuate, impacting commodity-linked currencies. Traders are watching whether technical support at $76 will hold, providing a platform for renewed buying, or if resistance near $79 will cap gains and trigger profit-taking.  

Market participants are assessing both fundamental and technical factors as trading volume remains steady. A decisive break above $79 could accelerate moves toward $90.01, while a fall below $76 may prompt wider corrections. Analysts note that the interplay between USD strength, geopolitical concerns, and macroeconomic data will likely shape XAG/USD exchange rate trends in the near term, keeping traders alert to shifts in momentum.
