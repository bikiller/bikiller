WTI crude oil prices softened on Friday, retreating from earlier gains this week as market sentiment was influenced by diplomatic efforts to resolve the US-Iran tensions. Trading at $71.25 per barrel, the price of West Texas Intermediate fell by 0.6%, reflecting growing optimism around a potential breakthrough in US-Iran talks. The development provided support for risk appetite, leading to reduced demand for safe-haven assets.

The prospect of easing geopolitical tensions also impacted the broader forex market, with the USD weakening against other major currencies. The EUR/USD rose 0.3% to 1.0920 as the US dollar pulled back. The easing of tensions could pave the way for more stability in the region, potentially reducing oil supply risks. Traders will now focus on upcoming developments in US-Iran negotiations, with the exchange rate remaining sensitive to news in the coming days.
