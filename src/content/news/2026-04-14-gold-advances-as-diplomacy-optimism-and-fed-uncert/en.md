Gold (XAU/USD) continued its upward momentum in the Asian session on Tuesday, extending the previous day's rebound from the sub-$1,650 level. The precious metal saw increased buying interest as investors shifted away from the US dollar, which has come under pressure due to growing uncertainty surrounding the Federal Reserve's monetary policy. Gold's gains come despite a mixed economic outlook, with its safe-haven appeal gaining traction.

Diplomatic optimism surrounding global economic recovery has further contributed to the softening of the USD. As the market awaits further cues on Fed actions, traders are cautious, leading to a weakening of the greenback. The EUR/USD exchange rate also benefited from this sentiment, moving higher as the dollar struggled. With global tensions and fluctuating monetary policies in focus, gold remains a preferred choice for risk-averse investors, positioning it for continued strength in the short term.
