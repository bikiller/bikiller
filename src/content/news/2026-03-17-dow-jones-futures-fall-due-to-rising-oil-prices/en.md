Dow Jones futures slipped 0.27% to hover around 46,850 during European trading hours on Tuesday, as rising oil prices weighed on market sentiment. This decline was mirrored in broader U.S. equity futures, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures falling 0.50% and 0.58%, respectively, to 6,670 and 24,530. The oil rally, fueled by tightening supply concerns, put pressure on risk assets, dampening investor appetite.

In forex markets, the USD remained under pressure as rising oil prices often lead to concerns about inflation and potential rate hikes by the Federal Reserve. The EUR/USD exchange rate was seen fluctuating near key support levels as traders adjusted their positions, anticipating any shift in U.S. monetary policy. With these tensions, traders are cautious, keeping a close eye on any further movements in oil prices and economic data to gauge future market direction.
