Gold tumbled sharply on Monday, slipping below $4,550 as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East pushed the US Dollar higher. The precious metal dropped more than 2%, pressured by safe-haven demand for the USD. Investors also reacted to a surge in US Treasury yields, reflecting growing expectations that the Federal Reserve will maintain interest rates through 2026, curbing prospects for policy easing.  

Currency markets saw the USD strengthen across major pairs, with the EUR/USD retreating as traders sought haven assets amid rising geopolitical risk. Rising Treasury yields boosted the appeal of dollar-denominated assets, influencing exchange rates and prompting short-term traders to adjust positions in gold, silver, and other commodities. Market participants cited technical levels near $4,550 as critical support now being tested.  

The combination of stronger USD, elevated yields, and regional tensions reshapes trading strategies, keeping bullion under pressure. Analysts note that unless the geopolitical risk abates or the Fed signals a pivot, gold may struggle to regain momentum. Forex traders are watching key currency pairs closely, as dollar strength impacts cross-rate movements and emerging-market currencies remain sensitive to shifts in US monetary expectations.
