West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil retreated sharply on Monday, sliding from session highs of $106.44 to lows near $101.00 per barrel. The roughly $5 decline came amid growing optimism that the US and Iran are inching toward a potential peace agreement through international mediators. Traders reacted swiftly, adjusting positions in energy-linked currency pairs, including USD-based pairs, as the geopolitical risk premium eased.  

The drop in WTI reverberated through financial markets, influencing USD trading as investors recalibrated expectations for inflation and interest rates. Forex markets saw minor adjustments in major pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD, reflecting shifts in risk sentiment. Commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian dollar also experienced trading fluctuations, with investors balancing oil-driven gains against a softer outlook for crude-related exports.  

Market watchers suggest the volatility could persist until concrete developments emerge from Washington and Tehran. Exchange rates are likely to remain sensitive to energy price swings, especially as central banks factor oil-driven inflation pressures into policy decisions. Traders are closely monitoring technical levels on both WTI and currency charts, preparing for potential retracements or breakouts if the peace talks make tangible progress.
