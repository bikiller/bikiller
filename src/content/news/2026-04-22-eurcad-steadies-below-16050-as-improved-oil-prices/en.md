The EUR/CAD currency pair remained under pressure on Wednesday, hovering near 1.6040 during European trading and extending its decline for a sixth consecutive session. The exchange rate continues to struggle below the 1.6050 level, reflecting sustained bearish momentum as market participants favor the Canadian Dollar amid shifting macroeconomic drivers.

A key factor behind the move is the rebound in oil prices, which tends to support the commodity-linked CAD. Stronger crude markets have improved Canada’s terms of trade, boosting demand for the currency and weighing on the EUR/CAD pair. At the same time, broader USD dynamics and cautious sentiment around the Euro add to downside pressure, keeping rallies limited.

From a trading perspective, the pair’s inability to reclaim 1.6050 suggests near-term resistance remains firm, while continued downside could expose lower technical levels if bearish momentum persists. Traders are closely watching energy markets and global risk sentiment, as further gains in oil or shifts in USD strength could reinforce the current trend in this currency pair.
