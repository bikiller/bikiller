West Texas Intermediate (WTI) US crude is trading around $75.60 on Friday, edging up 0.21% on the day as the market attempts to stabilize after a sharp selloff earlier in the week. Despite the modest rebound, prices remain under pressure, reflecting lingering bearish sentiment and broader risk-off flows in USD-denominated commodities.

Sentiment is being influenced by the resumption of crude shipments through the Strait of Hormuz, easing concerns over potential supply disruptions. The improved flow of barrels has reduced immediate geopolitical risk premiums, weighing on oil benchmarks even as physical demand signals remain mixed across global markets and trading sessions so far this week.

Looking ahead, traders remain focused on geopolitical developments and their impact on USD-priced energy markets, with oil often influencing inflation expectations and cross-asset flows. The stabilization in WTI may also filter into currency pair dynamics, including USD majors such as EUR/USD, as risk sentiment continues to shift across trading desks.
