The Pound Sterling is struggling against its major peers, excluding the antipodean currencies, as tensions in the Middle East weigh on market sentiment. During the late Asian session on Monday, the GBP/USD exchange rate fell 0.6%, nearing the 1.3400 level. This drop reflects a broader risk-off mood, where investors are cautious amid geopolitical uncertainties.

With the Sterling under pressure, the Pound's performance continues to falter, as the market remains wary of potential disruptions. The GBP's weakness extends across most currency pairs, with only the Australian and New Zealand Dollars showing resilience. The current movement suggests that traders are positioning cautiously, watching geopolitical developments closely, which could affect further market volatility in the coming days.
