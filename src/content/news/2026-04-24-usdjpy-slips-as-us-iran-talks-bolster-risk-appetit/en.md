USD/JPY slipped to around 159.50 on Friday, declining 0.14% for the day and breaking a four-day winning streak. This move is attributed to a pullback in the US Dollar amid rising risk appetite following constructive US-Iran talks, which have boosted market confidence and reduced demand for traditional safe-haven currencies.

The decrease in the USD/JPY exchange rate reflects shifting market dynamics as traders increasingly favor riskier assets over the dollar. The Bank of Japan's stance continues to limit any significant upside for the currency pair, suggesting that while the USD may be under pressure, the JPY's performance remains constrained by domestic monetary policy.
