WTI crude prices surged for the second consecutive day, reaching $98.00 per barrel, marking the highest level since April 13. The rise comes amid concerns over stalled peace negotiations between the US and Iran, which has led to uncertainty surrounding the potential for increased oil supply. The market's reaction highlights traders' sensitivity to geopolitical developments, particularly those impacting global oil production.

As WTI oil continues to climb, the broader forex market also sees shifts in major currency pairs. The USD remains supported, with the EUR/USD exchange rate maintaining a steady outlook as oil prices push inflation fears back into focus. With crude reaching these technical levels, traders are closely monitoring any further developments in US-Iran relations, which could drive more volatility in both oil prices and related forex pairs.
