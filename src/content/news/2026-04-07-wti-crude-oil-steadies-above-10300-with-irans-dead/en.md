WTI crude oil held steady above $103.00 per barrel on Tuesday, hovering near four-week highs as traders weighed geopolitical tensions against market fundamentals. Efforts to push prices past the $105.00 level have stalled, with uncertainty mounting ahead of the looming US deadline for Iran. The firming oil price supported risk sentiment, boosting commodity-linked currencies such as the AUD and CAD, while safe-haven flows underpinned the USD.  

The dollar index saw modest gains as investors balanced oil-driven optimism with broader geopolitical caution. EUR/USD traded lower, reflecting a slight USD uptick, while GBP/USD remained range-bound. Traders noted that exchange rates are reacting to both crude price momentum and potential disruptions in Middle Eastern supply, keeping volatility elevated in forex markets.  

Market participants are watching oil and currency pairs closely for signals of broader economic impacts. Sustained WTI above $103.00 could lift oil-linked currencies further, yet any escalation over Iran may trigger sharp USD demand, pushing EUR/USD and USD/JPY lower. Technical levels around $105.00 in crude and key support zones in major currency pairs will likely guide trading in the coming sessions.
