Gold (XAU/USD) continued its upward momentum, marking a fourth consecutive day of gains as it surged to a two-week high near $4,800. At the time of writing, the XAU/USD pair stands at $4,758, up almost 2%. The rally is largely driven by the weakening U.S. Dollar, as investors anticipate a potential resolution to the ongoing Middle East conflict, easing geopolitical concerns.

As the USD falters, market participants are shifting towards safe-haven assets like gold, propelling the price of the precious metal higher. The ongoing depreciation of the Greenback has been a key catalyst for gold's performance, pushing the metal to new highs. If the USD continues to lose ground, gold could extend its rally, with further gains likely if the geopolitical situation stabilizes.
