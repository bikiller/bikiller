Silver (XAG/USD) extended its corrective move on Wednesday, sliding sharply to around $58.75 during trading, marking a 4.62% daily decline. The currency pair hit a fresh low not seen since December 2025, underscoring sustained bearish pressure in the precious metals market as sellers dominate the exchange rate action. Broader forex sentiment remains cautious as traders reassess metal exposure.

The move comes as expectations for additional Federal Reserve rate hikes increase, lifting US dollar (USD) strength and weighing on non-yielding assets like silver. Upcoming PCE inflation data has also added caution across currency trading desks, with forex traders reassessing risk exposure. Rising yields and a firmer USD have intensified selling pressure in the XAG/USD exchange rate.

Implications for trading include heightened volatility and continued sensitivity to US economic releases, particularly inflation metrics and Fed commentary. In forex markets, silver’s correlation with USD pairs such as EUR/USD remains in focus, as further dollar strength could extend downside momentum. Traders are watching whether the currency pair stabilizes or breaks lower in the near term.
