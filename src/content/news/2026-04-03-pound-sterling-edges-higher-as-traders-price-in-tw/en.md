The Pound Sterling made modest gains on Friday, with the GBP/USD currency pair edging higher to 1.3230 during the Asian trading session. This follows a small decline in the previous day’s trading, as traders adjusted their positions amid growing expectations for two rate hikes from the Bank of England. Market activity remained relatively quiet, as the Good Friday holiday contributed to subdued trading volumes.

The GBP/USD exchange rate reflects shifting market sentiment, with the latest movements driven by expectations of tighter monetary policy in the UK. The outlook for further BoE rate hikes has supported the Pound in recent weeks, but with limited trading activity, fluctuations have remained relatively minor. Traders will likely await further economic data or central bank guidance before making significant moves.
