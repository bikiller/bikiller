The AUD/USD currency pair dropped to around the 0.6890 level on Thursday as tensions in the Middle East flared. Escalating conflicts, particularly following heightened rhetoric from former President Trump, led to a surge in demand for the safe-haven US Dollar (USD). Traders flocked to the USD, driving its value up against risk-sensitive assets like the Australian Dollar.

Market sentiment soured, with the rising geopolitical uncertainty pushing the USD higher. The turmoil also fueled a spike in oil prices, further strengthening the USD. As a result, the AUD/USD exchange rate struggled to maintain its footing, reflecting broader risk-off sentiment across global markets. The technical support at 0.6890 now appears critical for any potential rebound in the pair.
