Canada’s trade deficit surged to CAD 5.7 billion in February, significantly exceeding forecasts. Analysts at RBC pointed to a combination of factors driving the wider gap, including a surge in gold imports, strong demand for equipment and consumer goods, and rising motor vehicle exports. The higher import volume, particularly for machinery and vehicles, contributed to a sharp increase in the deficit despite a solid rise in exports.

The widening trade deficit could put downward pressure on the Canadian dollar (CAD) against major currencies like the USD and EUR. With February’s figures deviating sharply from market expectations, traders will likely monitor any adjustments in the outlook for Canada’s economic growth. The increased deficit suggests a trade imbalance that could weigh on CAD’s exchange rate in the short to medium term.
