EUR/JPY is holding steady around 183.50 on Wednesday, showing minimal intraday movement as traders weigh upcoming central bank actions. The currency pair remains in a narrow range, reflecting cautious sentiment ahead of the European Central Bank (ECB) and Bank of Japan (BoJ) policy announcements scheduled for Thursday. Investors are closely monitoring how monetary decisions may influence the euro and yen, with broader implications for USD cross rates.  

Market participants are positioning themselves amid expectations of divergent central bank stances. The ECB is widely anticipated to provide guidance on interest rates, which could support the euro if hawkish signals emerge, while the BoJ’s policy path continues to influence the yen’s strength. EUR/JPY trading has remained technical, with support near 183.20 and resistance around 183.80, highlighting limited momentum as the market awaits clarity.  

Traders are also watching USD/EUR and USD/JPY movements for potential spillover effects, given the interconnectedness of major currencies. Any surprises from either central bank could trigger volatility in EUR/JPY, prompting rapid adjustments in trading strategies. For now, the pair’s sideways action underscores a cautious market mood, emphasizing the importance of policy cues in shaping near-term exchange rate trends.
