Gold (XAU/USD) has fallen to around $4,760 in early trading on Friday, experiencing mild losses as inflation concerns, driven by rising oil prices, weigh on the market. The precious metal’s decline comes ahead of key US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, with traders cautious about the potential impact on future Federal Reserve policy. Ongoing concerns about the fragile US-Iran ceasefire and escalating tensions in the Middle East are also adding to market volatility.

The closure of the Strait of Hormuz has further heightened fears of geopolitical instability, contributing to a risk-off sentiment that has pressured gold. These developments, combined with oil-driven inflation concerns, are likely to keep investors on edge, influencing both the gold market and broader forex trading. Market participants are closely watching the US CPI report for clues on the direction of the USD, with any surprises potentially influencing currency pair movements across the board.
