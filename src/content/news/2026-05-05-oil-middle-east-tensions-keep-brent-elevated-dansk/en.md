Brent crude continued to trade above $110 per barrel as tensions between the US and Iran in the Strait of Hormuz keep oil markets on edge. Danske Bank’s research team highlights that the persistent geopolitical risk is fueling volatility, with traders watching supply concerns closely. The elevated prices are adding pressure to USD-based currencies, influencing exchange rates across key pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.

In forex markets, the risk-on sentiment has driven the US dollar higher, with the USD/JPY and USD/CHF pairs seeing modest gains amid uncertainty over Middle East supply disruptions. Oil-linked currencies such as the Norwegian krone and Canadian dollar are also reacting to Brent’s strength, trading at levels reflecting heightened energy price exposure. Technical levels near multi-week highs are drawing attention from traders seeking to gauge short-term momentum.

Market participants are weighing the broader implications for global trade and inflation. Continued Brent strength could support USD demand as a safe-haven, while pressuring eurozone and emerging market currencies sensitive to energy imports. Traders are closely monitoring news from the region, positioning themselves around key technical and psychological levels, as geopolitical developments remain the primary driver of both oil and currency market movements.
