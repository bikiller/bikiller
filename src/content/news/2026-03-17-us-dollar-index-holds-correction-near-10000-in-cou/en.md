The US Dollar Index (DXY) is holding its ground around the 100.00 level, maintaining its Monday corrective move ahead of the upcoming Federal Reserve policy decision. This shift follows a substantial pullback in oil prices, which helped alleviate concerns about runaway consumer inflation. As oil prices eased, market sentiment stabilized, reducing pressure on the USD in the short term.

The EUR/USD currency pair traded within a tight range, reflecting the dollar's limited movement. The exchange rate was largely influenced by the market’s anticipation of the Fed's stance on interest rates. With inflationary pressures moderating, traders are closely monitoring any signals from the Fed that could indicate the next moves in the broader USD trend. Market volatility is expected to increase as the central bank's policy outlook comes into focus.
