Silver (XAG/USD) slipped on Thursday, retreating to around $68.50, down 3.85% on the day, as traders weighed a stronger safe-haven U.S. dollar against previous geopolitical-driven gains. Earlier this week, silver had climbed on renewed tensions, but the currency pair is now under pressure from rising Treasury yields, which are boosting the USD and prompting profit-taking in precious metals.  

The pullback in silver mirrors broader market dynamics, where exchange rates for major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD are reacting to U.S. interest rate expectations. Investors are rotating away from non-yielding assets, with the dollar’s strength influencing trading sentiment across commodities. Technical levels around $68 have become a focal point, as traders assess whether support can hold amid ongoing USD momentum.  

Market watchers note that while geopolitical concerns continue to underpin safe-haven demand, the combination of higher yields and currency flows is outweighing this effect. For forex traders, the XAG/USD pair’s movements highlight the interplay between interest rates, USD strength, and commodity positioning, signaling potential volatility in the near term. With silver hovering near key technical thresholds, short-term trading strategies may lean toward caution.
