The GBP/USD currency pair slipped on Wednesday as the US Dollar strengthened amid rising risk aversion in global markets. Traders reacted to reports that Iran's Bushehr nuclear power plant had been targeted, sparking concerns over geopolitical tensions. The Pound weakened from earlier gains, with the exchange rate retreating toward key support levels near 1.2550, reflecting cautious sentiment among forex participants.  

Persistent inflation pressures in the UK added to Sterling’s struggles, as investors weighed the potential for further Bank of England tightening against a backdrop of global uncertainty. USD demand rose not only against GBP but also broadly across major currencies, including the EUR, as safe-haven flows drove trading volumes higher. Analysts noted that the move underscores how macroeconomic data and geopolitical headlines continue to influence currency pairs in the short term.  

Technical indicators suggest that GBP/USD may face further downward pressure if support at 1.2550 is breached, while resistance around 1.2650 could cap any recovery attempts. Traders are closely monitoring US Dollar strength, European equities, and UK inflation readings, which may dictate the next leg in trading dynamics. For now, market sentiment favors the USD, with risk-sensitive currencies like GBP remaining under pressure.
