Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded modestly from its one-month low near $1,800, a level last seen on February 6, as traders reacted to easing US Dollar pressures. During the Asian session on Thursday, the precious metal clawed back some ground amid a softer USD, reflecting a slight pullback in the currency pair’s exchange rate and renewed appetite for haven assets amid lingering geopolitical tensions.  

The USD’s dip provided support for gold, highlighting the inverse relationship between the currency and commodity trading. Market participants also cited geopolitical uncertainties as a factor boosting safe-haven demand, with investors closely monitoring the broader forex landscape, including EUR/USD and other major pairs, for signals of risk sentiment shifts that could influence further movements in gold prices.  

Technically, the $1,800 level now acts as a key support, while traders eye potential resistance around recent highs. Volatility in the currency markets, driven by USD fluctuations and central bank cues, could continue to shape gold’s trading range, offering both short-term trading opportunities and a gauge of broader market sentiment in FX and commodity markets.
