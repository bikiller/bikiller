The British Pound is trading in a tight range against the US Dollar ahead of the Bank of England’s upcoming policy decision. According to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret, the currency pair has seen only marginal moves, reflecting a lack of fresh catalysts. Recent data showed a slightly contractionary services PMI, which has kept the GBP under modest pressure against the USD.

Traders are focusing on technical levels, with the GBP/USD hovering near familiar support and resistance points as market participants weigh the implications of BoE policy. The exchange rate remains sensitive to any shifts in interest rate expectations, while broader market sentiment continues to favor range-bound trading. Meanwhile, other major currencies, including the EUR, have also been relatively stable, reflecting a cautious approach across forex markets.

Market participants are positioning cautiously ahead of potential monetary policy signals, keeping trading volumes subdued. Any surprises from the BoE could trigger sharper moves in the GBP, especially against the USD, while cross-currency pairs may see minor adjustments. Overall, the pound remains in a holding pattern, with analysts emphasizing the importance of incoming data and central bank guidance in steering near-term exchange rate movements.
