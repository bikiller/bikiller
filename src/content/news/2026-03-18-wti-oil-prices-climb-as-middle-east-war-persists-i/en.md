West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil extended gains on Wednesday, trading near $97.50 per barrel, up 2.37% on the day. The rally reflects ongoing tensions in the Middle East, which continue to underpin risk premiums in energy markets. Despite rising prices, the recently announced Iraq-Turkey agreement on oil transport has eased some supply concerns, providing a mixed backdrop for traders navigating both geopolitical and logistical factors.  

The USD has seen mixed movements as oil-sensitive currencies respond to the commodity’s uptick. Commodity-linked pairs such as USD/CAD and USD/NOK experienced modest volatility, with investors recalibrating positions in response to higher crude costs. Meanwhile, EUR/USD trading remained steady, supported by broader market stability, as forex markets digested the interplay between energy risk and global liquidity conditions.  

Analysts note that continued geopolitical uncertainty could sustain elevated oil prices, influencing currency markets tied to energy exports. Traders are monitoring technical levels in both oil and key forex pairs, weighing potential headwinds for the USD and EUR. Exchange rates may see further swings if tensions intensify or if supply assurances from regional agreements hold, keeping market participants alert to shifts in both commodities and currencies.
