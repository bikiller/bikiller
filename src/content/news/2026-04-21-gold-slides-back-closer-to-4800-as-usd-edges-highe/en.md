Gold (XAU/USD) failed to maintain momentum after a modest rebound from a one-week low of $4,737. During Tuesday’s Asian session, the precious metal encountered selling pressure, sliding back toward the $4,800 mark. This pullback comes as the USD strengthens, with the greenback gaining ahead of crucial US-Iran peace talks that are set to impact global sentiment.

The exchange rate for XAU/USD struggled to capitalize on earlier gains, and market participants are closely monitoring the situation. As the USD edges higher, traders are increasingly cautious, awaiting developments from the diplomatic talks. The direction of gold prices remains tied to fluctuations in the broader currency market, with the dollar’s movements likely to dictate short-term trends in precious metals trading.
