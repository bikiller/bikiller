EUR/USD surged on Monday, recovering from early losses as the Euro strengthened amid a broad decline in the US Dollar. The currency pair rose from 1.1010 to 1.1050, driven by reduced safe-haven demand after President Donald Trump postponed planned strikes on Iran. This shift in geopolitical tension allowed risk appetite to return, weakening the traditionally defensive Greenback.

The weakening of the USD was further supported by a drop in oil prices, which slid following the delay in military action. As the market recalibrated, traders turned their attention back to economic data and technical levels. The EUR/USD exchange rate managed to maintain support above the 1.1000 level, signaling potential for further gains if the Dollar remains under pressure.
