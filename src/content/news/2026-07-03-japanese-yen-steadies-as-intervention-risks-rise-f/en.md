USD/JPY currency pair traded around 161.10 during Friday’s Asian session, holding steady after recent volatility in the forex market. The exchange rate showed choppy trading as participants reacted to shifting expectations for both US monetary policy and Japanese currency authorities. Despite intraday swings, the pair remained anchored near the 161 handle, reflecting cautious positioning among traders.

Support for the Japanese Yen strengthened on rising speculation that Japanese officials may be preparing another round of currency intervention. This prospect has increased downside pressure on USD/JPY as traders reassess risk of sudden policy action. At the same time, easing bets on further Federal Reserve rate hikes have softened the US dollar, contributing to a more balanced trading environment across major currency pairs.

Looking ahead, forex markets are likely to remain sensitive to any official signals from Japan, while shifts in US yields continue to influence USD direction. If intervention fears intensify, the yen could extend gains and push USD/JPY lower from current exchange rate levels. However, persistent uncertainty may sustain volatility, keeping traders cautious in short-term currency pair positioning.
