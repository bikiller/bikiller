The Australian Dollar weakened against the USD on Friday, with the AUD/USD currency pair trading around 0.7105 at the time of writing, down 0.39% on the day. The decline followed the release of a stronger-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report, which reinforced confidence in the resilience of the US labor market and provided fresh support for the Greenback across major currency markets.

The upbeat employment data boosted demand for the US Dollar, prompting traders to reassess expectations for Federal Reserve policy and driving broad-based USD strength. As a result, the AUD/USD exchange rate came under pressure during the session, reflecting a shift in market sentiment toward the US currency. The move also weighed on other major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, as investors favored the Dollar amid improving US economic indicators.

From a trading perspective, the pullback in AUD/USD highlights the sensitivity of the Australian Dollar to changes in US macroeconomic data and interest-rate expectations. Market participants will continue monitoring upcoming economic releases for clues on the Federal Reserve’s next steps, while the currency pair’s ability to hold near the 0.7100 region could be important for short-term price direction and broader forex market sentiment.
