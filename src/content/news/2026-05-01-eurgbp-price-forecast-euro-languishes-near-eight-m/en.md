The EUR/GBP currency pair is struggling near eight-month lows, with the euro (EUR) trading at 0.8630 against the British pound (GBP) on Friday. After a brief rally to 0.8670 on Thursday, the pair has dropped again, indicating continued weakness in the euro's performance. The current exchange rate of 0.8630 leaves the euro dangerously close to the 0.8610 level, which has not been seen since September.

The euro's decline reflects broader market concerns, with investors cautious amid ongoing economic pressures in the Eurozone. As the EUR/GBP pair lingers near these lows, traders are eyeing potential support at the 0.8610 mark. Any further downward movement could see the euro facing more significant losses against the pound, potentially signaling a shift in market sentiment and increased volatility in European currency markets.
