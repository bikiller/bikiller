Gold has experienced a sharp pullback in 2026, defying traditional safe‑haven behavior despite rising geopolitical tensions and a firming US Dollar. HSBC Asset Management highlights that the metal’s decline aligns more with risk‑asset patterns, reflecting investor appetite for equities and higher-yielding currencies. Traders note that the USD’s strength against major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD has added pressure, making gold less attractive as a hedge.  

The precious metal’s retreat comes as market participants weigh persistent inflation data and interest-rate expectations, prompting a rotation into currencies and assets offering yield. Gold’s reaction contrasts with typical risk-off scenarios, where geopolitical strains usually boost bullion demand. As a result, gold trading has increasingly mirrored equity flows, suggesting that technical support levels around $1,950 an ounce may be tested if the USD continues to firm.  

Looking ahead, the interplay between currency pairs and gold remains critical for traders. A rising USD exchange rate could keep gold under pressure, while any EUR/USD or JPY/USD retracements might offer short-term reprieves. HSBC notes that this risk-asset behavior complicates the case for traditional safe-haven positioning, signaling that investors should monitor both forex trends and geopolitical developments to navigate gold’s evolving dynamics.
