The Indian Rupee (INR) regained ground against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, recovering from the previous day’s holiday due to Shri Mahavir Jayanti. The INR rose as oil prices dropped sharply, following a de-escalation in tensions related to the ongoing Middle East conflict. This led to a 0.2% improvement in the USD/INR exchange rate, bringing it down to 83.56 from earlier levels above 83.70.

The drop in oil prices, which fell below $70 per barrel, provided relief to the INR by easing concerns over India’s oil import costs. As oil is a major expense for India, the decline in prices is seen as a stabilizing factor for the Rupee. Traders are now eyeing the 83.50 level as a key technical support for USD/INR, with further gains possible if oil prices remain subdued and geopolitical risks continue to ease.
