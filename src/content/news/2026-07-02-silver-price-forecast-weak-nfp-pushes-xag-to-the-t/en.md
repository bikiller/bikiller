Silver extended its rally on Thursday, with the XAG/USD currency pair climbing to the top of its weekly trading range as the USD weakened across the broader foreign exchange market. The move followed softer-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data, which pushed the US Dollar to its lowest level in two weeks and encouraged fresh buying interest in precious metals. At the time of writing, Silver trades near $61.15, advancing almost 3.50% on the day.

The weaker employment report prompted traders to reassess the outlook for the US economy and the potential direction of monetary policy, weighing on the US Dollar exchange rate against major counterparts. As the USD retreated, Silver benefited from increased demand, with the XAG/USD pair outperforming and reaching the upper end of its weekly range. The latest trading activity highlights the close relationship between precious metals and fluctuations in the US currency.

Looking ahead, market participants will continue monitoring incoming US economic releases and broader currency market developments for fresh direction. Any sustained weakness in the USD could provide additional support for XAG/USD, while shifts in EUR, other major currency pairs, and overall risk sentiment may also influence trading conditions. For now, the softer NFP figures remain the primary catalyst behind Silver's strong advance and renewed bullish momentum.
