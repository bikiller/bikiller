Silver prices edged 0.16% lower during the latest trading session, with the metal consolidating in the $75.00-$76.00 range after recent volatility. The price remained largely unchanged around the 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at $75.70, highlighting a lack of strong directional momentum. Market participants continue to monitor broader USD movements and shifts in major currency pair dynamics, including EUR/USD, for clues on the next move.

The consolidation phase reflects a balance between buyers and sellers as traders assess technical signals and macroeconomic conditions. While the metal has managed to hold near the 50-day SMA, bearish sentiment remains present, with sellers targeting a potential test of the 200-day SMA if downside pressure intensifies. The stability of the USD exchange rate and overall risk appetite across financial markets are also influencing trading activity.

From a technical perspective, the $75.00-$76.00 zone has emerged as an important short-term range. A sustained break below current support levels could strengthen the bearish outlook and bring the 200-day SMA into focus. Conversely, holding above the 50-day SMA at $75.70 may help stabilize sentiment and encourage renewed buying interest in the sessions ahead.
