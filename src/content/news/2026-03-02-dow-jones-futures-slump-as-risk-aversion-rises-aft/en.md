Dow Jones futures dropped 1.43%, nearing 48,300, as market sentiment turned risk-averse following escalating tensions between the US and Israel over strikes on Iran. This downturn occurred during European trading hours, ahead of the US market open. S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures also faced declines, shedding 1.42% and 1.74%, settling around 6,790 and 24,570, respectively.

The shift in investor sentiment triggered volatility in the forex market, with the USD showing strength amid rising geopolitical concerns. The EUR/USD exchange rate remained under pressure as traders flocked to safe-haven assets, while the broader risk-off environment weighed on global equity markets. With markets cautious, the US dollar is likely to continue its dominance in the short term.
