The Euro (EUR) is holding steady against the US Dollar (USD), trading in a narrow range between 1.1530 and 1.1550 amid a holiday-thinned session, with many markets closed for Good Friday. The EUR/USD currency pair is on track for a modest 0.3% weekly gain, yet price action remains confined near the midpoint of March’s trading range, signaling a lack of clear directional momentum.  

Traders are cautious ahead of the US Nonfarm Payrolls report, which could inject volatility into the EUR/USD exchange rate. With limited liquidity, even minor flows are influencing intraday trading, leaving the currency pair oscillating in its established range. Analysts note that breaking above or below this zone may set the tone for next week’s market trend.  

Technical watchers point to 1.1550 as near-term resistance, while support sits around 1.1530. A decisive move in either direction could trigger broader trading adjustments in EUR/USD positions, reflecting the interplay between European and US economic cues. For now, the euro remains range-bound, with market participants eyeing upcoming data for fresh directional signals.
