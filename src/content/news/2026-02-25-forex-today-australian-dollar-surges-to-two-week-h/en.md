The Australian Dollar (AUD) surged to a two-week high following stronger-than-expected inflation data for January, with the Consumer Price Index (CPI) rising 1.9% month-on-month, surpassing forecasts. This sharp inflationary jump prompted traders to adjust expectations, raising the likelihood of further rate hikes by the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA). The AUD/USD pair climbed to 0.6925, up from 0.6830 earlier in the session, marking a significant rebound.

The stronger AUD also put pressure on the EUR/AUD exchange rate, which fell to a three-week low of 1.6060, as investors sought higher returns from Australian assets.
