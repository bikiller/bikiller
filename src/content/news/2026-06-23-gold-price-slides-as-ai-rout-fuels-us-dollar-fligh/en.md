Gold (XAU/USD) exchange rate fell over 1.30% on Tuesday, erasing Monday’s gains as trading sentiment turned risk-off amid an AI-driven equity rout. The US Dollar (USD) strengthened across major currency pairs as investors sought safety, pressuring bullion. Broader forex markets saw capital rotate away from risk assets, weighing on gold pricing.

Persistent US Dollar strength was underpinned by a hawkish Federal Reserve (Fed) outlook, reinforcing higher-for-longer interest rate expectations in the currency market. The USD advanced against peers including the euro (EUR) in broader forex trading, reducing demand for non-yielding assets like gold. Rising yields further supported dollar flows, intensifying downside pressure on the XAU/USD exchange rate.

Looking ahead, gold’s near-term outlook remains sensitive to shifts in risk sentiment and Federal Reserve signals, with volatility likely to persist across currency pairs. If equity market stress deepens, USD safe-haven demand could remain dominant, limiting any rebound in gold trading. Conversely, stabilization in risk assets may help the XAU/USD exchange rate recover modestly.
