Christine Lagarde, President of the European Central Bank (ECB), confirmed that the bank is well-positioned to manage ongoing economic shocks, despite leaving key rates unchanged at the March policy meeting. The decision to maintain the EUR/USD exchange rate was driven by the current market uncertainty. Traders were expecting a more aggressive stance, but Lagarde emphasized the ECB's readiness to respond if the economic outlook worsens. The EUR/USD remained steady, trading around 1.0700 post-announcement.

The market's reaction was muted, as investors await clearer signals on the ECB's future policy direction. Lagarde’s comments underscore the bank’s cautious approach amidst volatile trading conditions. As the EUR/USD consolidates near technical support at 1.0650, traders are closely monitoring any changes in economic data and geopolitical developments. The decision highlights the ECB's careful balance between combating inflation and addressing potential economic headwinds, with future moves likely influenced by incoming data.
