The USD/CAD currency pair edged higher to around 1.3635 during early European trading on Tuesday, marking modest gains above the 1.3600 threshold. Traders showed caution as uncertainty surrounding US-Iran peace talks weighed on market sentiment, while reports of a potential closure of the Strait of Hormuz boosted demand for safe-haven assets, lending support to the US Dollar against the Canadian Dollar.  

Despite the upward move, the pair remains under a bearish technical bias. Resistance around recent highs and key moving averages continues to cap USD/CAD upside, suggesting that any gains may be limited unless broader risk sentiment shifts. Market participants are closely monitoring geopolitical developments, which could trigger sudden shifts in the exchange rate.  

Trading volumes in European markets have remained steady, reflecting cautious positioning ahead of upcoming US economic data. Analysts note that while the USD retains strength amid global uncertainty, the CAD could regain traction if oil prices stabilize, given its correlation with crude markets. For forex traders, maintaining a watchful eye on technical levels and geopolitical headlines is likely to remain essential for navigating the pair’s near-term moves.
