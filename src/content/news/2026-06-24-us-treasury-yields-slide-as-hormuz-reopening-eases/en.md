US Treasury yields declined across the curve on Wednesday after the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz eased supply disruption concerns, pulling crude oil prices lower and cooling inflation expectations. The move triggered a broad bond market rally, with investors reassessing near-term inflation risks and positioning across short- and long-dated maturities.

In foreign exchange trading, the softer yield backdrop weighed on the USD, as reduced rate differentials diminished its appeal against major currency pairs. EUR/USD and GBP/USD saw firmer tones as traders rotated into higher-risk assets. Market participants also adjusted expectations around Federal Reserve policy, pricing in a slightly less hawkish trajectory following the drop in Treasury yields.

Improved risk sentiment supported commodity-linked currencies such as the CAD and NOK, while oil-sensitive markets reacted to the decline in crude benchmarks. Traders continue to monitor geopolitical developments around energy routes, with volatility expected to persist across both bond yields and forex exchange rates as liquidity conditions shift. Overall outlook remains cautious as traders reassess macro drivers.
