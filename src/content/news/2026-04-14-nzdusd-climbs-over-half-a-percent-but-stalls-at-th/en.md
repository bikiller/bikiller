The NZD/USD currency pair saw notable gains on Tuesday, rising by more than 0.55%. The exchange rate climbed to a session high of 0.5920, driven by a mix of technical factors and market sentiment. However, the upward momentum stalled as the pair faced resistance around the 0.5900 handle, ultimately settling just below this level by the end of the trading session.

Despite the pullback, the New Zealand dollar showed strength against the USD, as market participants continue to weigh the impact of global economic data and central bank policies. The 0.5900 level now acts as a key psychological barrier, with traders closely watching for any developments that might push the pair higher or trigger further retracement.
