USD/JPY continued to grind higher on Wednesday, extending gains in a currency pair that is behaving counterintuitively. The Japanese yen weakened further despite the Bank of Japan raising its policy rate only last week. Typically, a hike supports the domestic currency, yet the exchange rate continues to drift toward multi-year or generational lows against the USD.

Market dynamics suggest the move is being driven more by yield differentials and broader USD strength than by domestic policy shifts. Trading flows continue to favor the dollar, keeping pressure on the yen even after tightening from the BoJ. Investors appear unconvinced that the rate hike marks a sustained shift in Japan’s monetary stance.

For forex traders, the persistent USD/JPY uptrend highlights how fragile yen support remains in current conditions. The exchange rate signals that monetary tightening alone may not reverse structural weakness in the currency. Attention now turns to whether further policy adjustments or external USD softness will be needed to stabilise trading ranges.
