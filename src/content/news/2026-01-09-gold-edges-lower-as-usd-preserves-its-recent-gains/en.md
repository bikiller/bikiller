Gold (XAU/USD) faced downward pressure during the Asian trading session on Friday, unable to maintain momentum from its previous rebound near the $4,400 mark. As fresh sellers entered the market, the precious metal struggled to attract buyers, reflecting a broader trend of caution among investors ahead of the key US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report.

The recent strength of the USD has played a significant role in gold's decline, as the currency preserves its gains against a basket of major currencies. Market expectations surrounding the forthcoming NFP data are influencing trading sentiment, with many traders eyeing potential impacts on the USD exchange rate. As the market awaits this crucial economic indicator, gold's price volatility is likely to continue, reflecting ongoing market dynamics.
