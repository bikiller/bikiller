The Pound Sterling edged higher on Friday, gaining ground against most major currencies as optimism over potential progress in Russia-Ukraine peace talks buoyed market sentiment. The GBP/USD currency pair climbed to around 1.3444 during the European session, reflecting cautious buying interest ahead of key geopolitical developments. Traders noted that Sterling’s advance was more pronounced against the USD, while other European currencies limited broader gains.  

Market participants are also eyeing upcoming US-Iran negotiations, which could influence risk sentiment and capital flows. The GBP’s resilience comes amid mixed trading volumes, with investors balancing safe-haven demand against positive risk catalysts. Technical analysts highlighted that support levels around 1.3380 held firm earlier in the week, allowing Sterling to test resistance near 1.3450.  

The exchange rate dynamics suggest cautious optimism in the currency markets, with Sterling potentially benefiting if diplomatic developments ease geopolitical tensions. Meanwhile, the EUR/GBP and other European crosses remain relatively stable, indicating selective currency movements. Traders are closely monitoring developments in both Eastern Europe and the Middle East, as these events could dictate short-term volatility and trading strategies for major pairs involving the GBP and USD.
