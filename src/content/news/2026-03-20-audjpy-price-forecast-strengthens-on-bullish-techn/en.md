The AUD/JPY currency pair gained traction during early European trading on Friday, climbing toward the 112.20 level as the Australian Dollar strengthened against the Japanese Yen. Traders reacted positively to the hawkish tone from the Reserve Bank of Australia, which reinforced expectations of tighter monetary policy. Technical momentum above the 100-day EMA also supported buying interest, keeping the exchange rate buoyant in the near term.  

Market participants noted that the broader forex environment, including USD and EUR movements, continues to influence AUD/JPY trading dynamics. Risk sentiment favored higher-yielding currencies, encouraging investors to move away from safe-haven JPY. As a result, the pair’s intraday gains reflected both fundamental support from RBA commentary and technical momentum, signaling potential continuation toward resistance levels in the coming sessions.  

Analysts suggest that the AUD/JPY exchange rate could remain sensitive to global market shifts, including USD strength and cross-asset trends. Traders should monitor upcoming economic releases from Australia and Japan, as these could impact the currency pair’s trajectory. The combination of hawkish central bank guidance and bullish technical signals above key EMAs positions AUD/JPY for further upside potential, while JPY softness provides an additional tailwind for trading strategies.
