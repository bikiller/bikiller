Gold (XAU/USD) faces continued pressure on Friday, heading for a second consecutive weekly loss. Rising oil prices, driven by the ongoing US-Iran conflict, have sparked fresh inflation fears, intensifying the hawkish shift in global interest rate expectations. As a result, the non-yielding metal struggles to gain support, with market sentiment leaning towards higher rates.

The surge in oil prices is adding to the broader market’s concerns, leading to a stronger USD. This dynamic is creating a more challenging environment for gold, which traditionally thrives in times of low rates and inflation concerns. Traders are now adjusting their outlook, with key support for gold at $1,890. Should this level break, a deeper decline could be in play, affecting the broader commodity and forex markets.
