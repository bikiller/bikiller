The Dow Jones Industrial Average plunged by 840 points, or 1.73%, to 47,885, erasing all of Wednesday's gains amid rising tensions over the situation in Iran. The broader market followed suit, with the S&P 500 dropping 0.82% to around 6,810, while the Nasdaq Composite fell 0.50% to 22,690. The market’s volatility mirrored the growing geopolitical risk, spooking investors and fueling a retreat from riskier assets.

In forex markets, the USD struggled as safe-haven demand for currencies like the JPY and CHF increased. The EUR/USD traded lower, reflecting the pressure on global risk sentiment. Traders are cautious as geopolitical developments continue to impact market sentiment, with volatility likely to persist in the short term. This unease in equities has prompted significant moves across currency pairs, highlighting the interconnectedness of global markets.
