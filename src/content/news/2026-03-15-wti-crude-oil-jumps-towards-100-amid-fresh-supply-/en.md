West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil surged at the start of the week, climbing roughly 3% and pushing toward the key psychological $100 level. The benchmark US crude traded near $99 per barrel during early trading hours as renewed supply concerns tightened global energy market sentiment. The sharp move higher in oil prices quickly drew attention across commodity and forex markets.

Traders are increasingly focused on potential disruptions to global supply, which have supported bullish momentum in crude markets. Rising oil prices often influence currency pair dynamics, particularly for commodity-linked currencies, while also shaping expectations for inflation and central bank policy. As a result, the USD exchange rate and major forex pairs such as EUR/USD remain sensitive to developments in energy markets.

The advance toward $100 marks a critical technical and psychological threshold for WTI. A sustained break above this level could intensify bullish trading activity and trigger further volatility across commodities and forex markets. Market participants are closely monitoring price action, as elevated crude prices can impact global risk sentiment, inflation expectations, and broader USD trading flows.
