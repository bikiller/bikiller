The USD/JPY currency pair climbed to around 160.55 in early Asian trading on Thursday as the Japanese Yen slid to a near six-week low. Market participants reacted to a hotter-than-expected US inflation report, which bolstered expectations for a “higher-for-longer” stance from the US Federal Reserve. The exchange rate pressure reflects growing concerns over potential intervention to support the yen.

Traders noted that the yen’s weakness against the dollar is driving broader forex market adjustments. With USD strength rising, other currency pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD are also seeing shifts, as investors recalibrate positions in anticipation of continued Fed tightening. Technical levels near 160.50 are being closely watched for potential support.

Analysts suggest that if the yen continues to drift lower, intervention fears could intensify, affecting liquidity and volatility in major currency pairs. The trading environment remains sensitive to US economic data, Fed commentary, and geopolitical developments, making careful monitoring of exchange rates essential for forex traders seeking to navigate this volatile phase.
