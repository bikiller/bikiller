Silver (XAG/USD) saw a sharp rebound on Wednesday, rising more than 3% during the Asian trading session to trade near $85.20 per troy ounce. This recovery follows a steep drop of over 12% in the previous two sessions, highlighting the volatility in precious metals as geopolitical tensions escalate. The rise in silver prices comes as investors seek safe-haven assets amid intensifying conflict in the Middle East.

The ongoing turmoil in the region has driven demand for silver, with traders shifting focus to risk-averse investments. As the geopolitical landscape continues to shift, silver's ability to attract buying interest reflects broader market trends where investors are hedging against potential market disruptions. The currency pair's recent price action could signal further volatility, especially if tensions persist, influencing the USD and broader market sentiment.
