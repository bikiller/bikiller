The Japanese Yen (JPY) continues to weaken against the US Dollar (USD) as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East push the yen lower. As the conflict enters its fifth week, the JPY trades near 150.00 against the USD, a critical psychological level. The yen’s persistent decline reflects the risk-off sentiment among traders, with safe-haven demand shifting towards the USD amid the ongoing turmoil.

The Yen's downward trajectory is exacerbated by Japan's ultra-loose monetary policy, which contrasts sharply with the Federal Reserve's tightening stance. As the USD/JPY pair remains elevated, approaching the 151.00 level, technical analysts are closely watching the next support levels. Market participants are bracing for further volatility, with the conflict's duration potentially keeping the yen under pressure in the near term.
