Gold (XAU/USD) surged over 2% on Monday, reaching an all-time high of $4,442 as heightened geopolitical tensions spurred demand for safe-haven assets. The increase in the price of gold reflects investors' growing concerns about global instability, which often drives traders to seek refuge in precious metals.

Additionally, expectations of further interest rate cuts by the Federal Reserve have contributed to this bullish trend. With US Treasury yields declining, the allure of gold as a non-yielding asset has intensified, prompting a shift in trading strategies among market participants. As the USD faces pressure, this unprecedented surge in gold prices could signal a significant shift in market dynamics.
