Brent crude is trading back near $80 per barrel while West Texas Intermediate hovers around $77, effectively erasing nearly the entire geopolitical risk premium built during almost four months of open conflict with Iran. The move reflects a broader reset in global risk sentiment, with energy markets and USD-linked assets adjusting in tandem across trading desks.  

Pricing action suggests the market may have "bought the Iran deal twice"—first on expectations of de-escalation, and again as confirmation of stabilisation. The unwind has been driven by improved supply outlooks, fading war risk hedges, and a firmer USD backdrop influencing commodity valuation across currency pairs and oil-linked forex trading strategies.  

With crude giving back gains, inflation expectations and rate differentials are back in focus for forex traders. A softer oil impulse eases pressure on USD/CAD and supports risk-sensitive pairs like EUR/USD, while volatility in energy continues to shape short-term trading positioning across both commodity and currency markets in the near term.
