The USD/JPY currency pair extended its bullish momentum on Wednesday, following a rebound from the 20-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at 158.24, tested earlier in the week. The pair has gained 0.36%, trading at 159.27, but struggles to break the key weekly resistance at 159.65, which was reached on Monday. The persistent upward pressure suggests that USD remains firm, but near-term gains could be capped by this resistance level.

Market participants will be watching closely for any potential breakthroughs or pullbacks, as the exchange rate continues to hover around the 159.30 mark. With support holding at 158.24, the outlook for USD/JPY remains positive in the short term, though further momentum will depend on breaking the weekly high. Any further push above 159.65 may see the pair targeting higher levels, while a failure to breach it could prompt a consolidation or retracement.
