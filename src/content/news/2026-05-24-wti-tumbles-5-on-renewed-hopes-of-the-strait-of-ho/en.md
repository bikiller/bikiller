West Texas Intermediate (WTI) plunged sharply on Monday, opening nearly 5% lower and approaching the $90 per barrel mark. The drop comes amid renewed optimism over a potential reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, easing concerns about disruptions to global oil supply. This sudden shift has rattled energy traders, prompting increased volatility in currency pairs sensitive to oil movements, including USD/CAD and USD/NOK.

The bearish move in WTI has reverberated across forex markets, with the USD showing mixed strength against the euro and other major currencies. Traders are monitoring how lower oil prices could influence inflation expectations and central bank policies. Exchange rates like EUR/USD and GBP/USD have seen modest adjustments as participants weigh the impact of falling crude on commodity-linked currencies.

Analysts caution that while short-term trading favors a dip in oil, technical levels around $90 will be closely watched. Should WTI breach this threshold, further downside could pressure USD-paired currencies and commodity exporters. Market participants are advised to track both geopolitical developments and technical signals, as the interplay between energy prices and forex markets continues to shape trading strategies.
