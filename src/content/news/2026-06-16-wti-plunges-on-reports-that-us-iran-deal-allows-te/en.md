WTI crude oil prices plunged sharply after reports suggested a breakthrough US–Iran agreement that could reshape global energy flows. Market participants reacted immediately, with traders pricing in increased supply expectations and reduced geopolitical risk premiums. The move also influenced broader forex trading sentiment, strengthening risk appetite while pressuring safe-haven demand for the USD in early exchange rate activity.

According to a Wall Street Journal report, the proposed US–Iran deal would allow Tehran to resume oil exports immediately, marking a significant shift in sanctions policy. Restrictions on banking and transport would also be waived to facilitate cross-border transactions. Traders are closely watching how this could affect crude supply chains and FX liquidity across major currency pairs.

Market implications are being reflected across forex markets, where USD exchange rate dynamics remain sensitive to energy-driven inflation expectations. A potential increase in global oil supply could ease price pressures, influencing EUR/USD and other major pairs through shifting interest rate outlooks. In trading sessions, volatility is expected to persist as investors reassess geopolitical risk and macroeconomic positioning.
