The USD/CAD exchange rate remains under pressure as higher oil prices provide temporary support to the Canadian Dollar (CAD). Commerzbank’s Michael Pfister highlights that while oil prices have been bolstering the CAD, the currency's upside potential is limited. The exchange rate has seen a slight dip, with CAD benefiting from recent market movements, but this support could fade.

Pfister also cautions that a fragile Canadian economy and upcoming USMCA talks could weigh on the CAD. These factors suggest that any positive momentum for the Canadian Dollar could be short-lived. Traders should keep an eye on the developments between the US, Canada, and Mexico, as they could trigger setbacks and volatility in the currency pair. The outlook for USD/CAD remains uncertain as these factors play out.
