Silver (XAG/USD) rebounded on Wednesday, climbing back above the $60.00 level to trade around $60.35, up 3.19% at the time of writing. The currency pair-equivalent precious metal move reflects broad US Dollar weakness, as softer-than-expected US economic data undermined demand for the USD in global forex trading sessions across major currency pairs.

Market sentiment was further shaped by comments from Federal Reserve (Fed) Chair Kevin Warsh, which reinforced expectations of a softer policy outlook. Traders reacted by adjusting USD positioning, with the weaker dollar boosting demand for precious metals like silver across forex-linked flows and supporting intraday volatility in XAG/USD trading amid cautious risk appetite.

Looking ahead, silver’s position above the $60 threshold will be closely watched as traders assess whether the rebound can extend amid ongoing USD softness. In broader forex markets, EUR/USD and other major currency pairs may also respond to shifting Fed expectations, with precious metals remaining sensitive to US data releases.
