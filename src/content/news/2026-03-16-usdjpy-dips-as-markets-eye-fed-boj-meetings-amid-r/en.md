USD/JPY slipped on Monday, trading near 159.20, down 0.33% as the Japanese Yen gained modest ground. Traders are positioning ahead of a pivotal week for central banks, with policy announcements from the Federal Reserve and the Bank of Japan expected on Wednesday. The currency pair’s retreat reflects cautious sentiment amid mounting inflation concerns in the U.S., influencing expectations for potential interest rate guidance.  

Market participants are closely monitoring the exchange rate, weighing the impact of inflationary pressures on USD performance. The Yen’s recent resilience suggests investors are seeking safe-haven positions, while the dollar remains sensitive to U.S. economic data. Momentum indicators show the pair may test support levels around 158.80 if risk sentiment deteriorates further.  

Analysts note that movements in USD/JPY could ripple across broader forex markets, affecting EUR/USD and other major currency pairs. Any surprises from the Fed or BoJ could trigger renewed volatility, reshaping trading strategies. Investors are advised to track central bank cues carefully, as even minor policy shifts could influence the short-term trajectory of the USD and Yen.
