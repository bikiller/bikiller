The AUD/JPY currency pair extended its upward momentum in Thursday’s Asian trading session, hovering near the 112.45–112.50 region as the exchange rate benefited from a softer Japanese Yen. The move followed the Bank of Japan’s decision to keep interest rates unchanged, in line with market expectations, which limited demand for the JPY and supported higher-yielding currencies like the Australian Dollar.

Market participants largely priced in the BoJ’s steady policy stance, but the absence of any hawkish shift reinforced the divergence between Japan’s ultra-loose monetary policy and relatively firmer outlooks elsewhere. This dynamic continues to underpin carry trade activity, where traders favor AUD over JPY. Broader forex sentiment also reflected stable conditions across major currency pairs, with USD and EUR trading in narrow ranges, offering little resistance to AUD/JPY’s advance.

From a trading perspective, holding above the 112.00 psychological level keeps the near-term bias tilted to the upside, with 112.50 acting as an immediate resistance zone. Sustained gains could invite further bullish positioning, while any shift in global risk sentiment or central bank signals—particularly from the Fed or ECB—may influence the pair’s next directional move.
