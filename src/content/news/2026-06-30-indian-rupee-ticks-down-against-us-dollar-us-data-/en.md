Indian Rupee opened slightly lower against the US Dollar on Tuesday, with the USD/INR currency pair edging higher toward 94.65. The exchange rate reflects a firmer US Dollar as traders remain cautious ahead of key US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data for June due on Thursday, keeping forex trading sentiment subdued.

Dollar strength is supported by cautious risk sentiment and positioning ahead of the labour market report, prompting mild pressure on emerging-market currencies like the rupee. In forex trading, investors are reducing directional bets, leading to range-bound moves as USD demand stays resilient across major currency pairs, including USD and other global crosses.

Looking ahead, the USD/INR pair may remain sensitive to US economic releases, with the upcoming NFP report expected to set the near-term direction. A stronger-than-expected outcome could reinforce USD upside momentum, while weaker data may offer relief to the rupee and stabilise the exchange rate around current levels near 94.65.
