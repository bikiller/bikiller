The Indian Rupee (INR) strengthened against the US Dollar (USD) in recent trading, recovering after posting modest losses in the previous Thursday session. The USD/INR currency pair moved lower as exchange rate dynamics shifted in favour of emerging market currencies. Market participants noted improved appetite for risk across trading desks.

Risk-on sentiment in global financial markets supported the rupee, with investors rotating into higher-yielding assets amid improved equity performance and easing demand for the US Dollar. The shift in the forex trading environment encouraged short-term inflows into emerging markets, helping the Indian currency stabilise after earlier pressure. EUR pairs also reflected similar risk appetite across majors.

Looking ahead, traders are closely monitoring US macroeconomic data and Federal Reserve policy signals alongside Reserve Bank of India guidance for direction in the USD/INR currency pair. Market positioning suggests cautious optimism for the rupee, though volatility in exchange rate movements may persist as forex trading conditions remain sensitive to global risk sentiment shifts.
