The USD/JPY currency pair is holding a slight gain, trading at 159.70 on Tuesday, up by 0.27%. The modest uptick comes as traders react to rising geopolitical risks, notably the US's move to effectively block traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, a key global shipping route. This development has sparked safe-haven demand, with the Japanese yen benefiting from the increased uncertainty.

Markets are closely monitoring the situation, with the USD/JPY pair’s price hovering near technical levels, keeping investors on edge. The heightened tensions in the Middle East have intensified fears over global trade disruptions, providing a boost to the yen. As the situation unfolds, traders will be looking for further developments that could drive additional volatility in the exchange rate.
