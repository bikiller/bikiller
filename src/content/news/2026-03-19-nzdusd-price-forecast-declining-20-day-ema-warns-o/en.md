The NZD/USD currency pair is trading 0.4% higher, reaching near 0.5820 during the European session on Thursday. Despite disappointing Q4 GDP data from New Zealand, which showed weaker-than-expected growth, the Kiwi continues to outperform. This surge is likely driven by broader market sentiment and positioning rather than local economic fundamentals.

However, the 20-day exponential moving average (EMA) is showing signs of a bearish outlook, which may indicate further downside potential for the pair. Traders will closely monitor any shifts in sentiment or further USD strength, as these could weigh on the exchange rate in the coming sessions. The recent rally might face resistance, making the near-term outlook uncertain.
