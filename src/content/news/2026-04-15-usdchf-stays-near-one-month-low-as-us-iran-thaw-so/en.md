USD/CHF remained near a one-month low on Wednesday, trading around 0.7820, up just 0.04% on the day. The currency pair had reached 0.7790 on Tuesday, marking its lowest point in the past month. The slight recovery in today’s session suggests limited bullish momentum as the Swiss Franc continues to hold firm.

The US Dollar faces pressure due to a combination of factors, including a thaw in US-Iran relations and softer-than-expected inflation data. These developments have led to reduced demand for the USD, dampening investor sentiment. As a result, USD/CHF’s exchange rate has stayed subdued, with traders remaining cautious ahead of further economic releases.
