The British Pound extended its decline against the USD on Tuesday, with the GBP/USD currency pair falling toward 1.3444, down nearly 0.43% during the session. Demand for the Greenback strengthened after renewed US military action in southern Iran reignited geopolitical concerns and prompted traders to reassess the outlook for ongoing US-Iran negotiations. The shift in market sentiment pushed investors toward safe-haven assets, supporting the US Dollar across major forex markets.

The weaker Pound also reflected cautious trading conditions as investors monitored broader risk appetite and the potential impact of rising Middle East tensions on global growth and inflation expectations. Currency markets saw defensive positioning emerge, with the USD outperforming against several major peers, including the EUR and GBP. Analysts noted that deteriorating sentiment could keep pressure on the GBP/USD exchange rate in the near term if geopolitical uncertainty continues to intensify.

From a technical perspective, the GBP/USD pair remains vulnerable after slipping below recent intraday support zones, with traders closely watching the 1.3400 region as the next key downside level. On the upside, resistance is seen near 1.3500, where previous buying momentum faded. Market participants now await further developments in US foreign policy and upcoming macroeconomic data for fresh direction in forex trading.
