Brent crude prices surged to $115 as geopolitical tensions in Iran intensified, now entering their fifth week. Market attention has shifted towards the resumption of oil flows through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical route for global oil shipments. This uncertainty has led to volatility, with oil prices becoming a pivotal factor in inflationary pressures and the looming risk of a potential global recession.

The oil market’s developments are impacting major currency pairs, particularly USD and EUR. With oil prices rising, the USD faces upward pressure due to its strong correlation with higher commodity prices, while the EUR remains vulnerable to fluctuations in energy costs. Traders are keeping a close eye on oil price movements, as they could influence central bank policies, especially the Federal Reserve’s next move in managing inflation and maintaining economic stability.
