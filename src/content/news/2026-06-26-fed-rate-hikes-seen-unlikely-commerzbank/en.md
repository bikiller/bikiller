Commerzbank analyst Bernd Weidensteiner argues that further Federal Reserve rate hikes appear increasingly unlikely, despite parts of the market still pricing additional tightening. Expectations for a more hawkish Fed stance have been driven by persistent inflation concerns, yet recent data and commodity trends are shifting sentiment in FX trading and interest rate markets, affecting the USD outlook and broader currency pair positioning.

Falling Oil and gasoline prices are expected to ease U.S. inflation pressures, reinforcing the view that the Federal Reserve may not need further tightening. For forex markets, this dynamic typically weighs on the USD, as lower inflation reduces yield expectations and shifts exchange rate differentials. Traders are watching how energy-led disinflation influences EUR/USD trading sentiment and broader dollar positioning in coming sessions and volatility increases.

Looking ahead, FX trading conditions suggest continued sensitivity to incoming inflation data and Fed communication, with currency pairs such as EUR/USD likely to respond to shifts in rate expectations. If energy-driven disinflation persists, USD strength may soften, supporting euro recovery attempts. However, uncertainty around timing of policy adjustments could maintain volatility across major exchange rates and limit clear directional trends in the near term ahead.
