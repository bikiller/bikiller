The USD/JPY currency pair continued its upward move on Thursday, as the Japanese Yen remained under pressure against the US Dollar in active forex trading. The exchange rate climbed back toward levels that previously prompted official “rate checks” by Japanese authorities on January 23, a signal that revived speculation about possible intervention in the foreign exchange market if volatility intensifies.

The Yen’s weakness is largely linked to shifting expectations around US monetary policy and stronger energy prices. Markets have been repricing the Federal Reserve outlook, supporting the USD across major currency pairs, while rising global Oil prices have added pressure on Japan’s trade balance. Because Japan relies heavily on imported energy, higher Oil costs tend to weigh on the Yen and push the USD/JPY exchange rate higher.

As a result, traders are closely watching whether the currency pair can sustain its current momentum without triggering official action. A further rise in USD/JPY could heighten intervention risks from Japanese authorities, particularly if the exchange rate moves rapidly. In the near term, forex markets will likely remain sensitive to US rate expectations, energy price trends, and signals from policymakers regarding potential currency market intervention.
