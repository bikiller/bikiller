OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong remain optimistic about the Australian Dollar (AUD) despite recent pressure from higher energy prices. They point to key factors supporting the AUD, including above-target inflation, robust domestic economic activity, and a hawkish stance from the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA). These elements suggest a constructive outlook for the AUD/USD exchange rate, potentially driving it toward the 0.75 mark.

Despite global risk-off sentiment affecting currencies, the AUD has held its ground. OCBC strategists believe that the RBA's more aggressive monetary policy, coupled with Australia's strong economic fundamentals, positions the AUD for further gains. With the USD under pressure and a favorable inflationary backdrop, the Australian Dollar may continue its upward momentum in the near term, potentially testing resistance levels near 0.75.
