New Zealand Dollar extends losses against the US Dollar, with NZD/USD trading near 0.5660 during Asian hours on Wednesday. The currency pair marks a sixth consecutive day of declines as sustained USD strength weighs on the exchange rate. Broader forex trading sentiment remains tilted against the Kiwi amid persistent demand for the greenback. Traders note continued downward momentum in spot pricing.

Sentiment toward the US Dollar is supported by rising Middle East geopolitical risks, which boost safe-haven demand for USD assets. At the same time, firmer US economic data reinforces expectations of resilient growth and keeps the currency firm across major forex pairs. The combination pressures risk-sensitive currencies such as the New Zealand Dollar in trading sessions.

Looking ahead, NZD/USD remains vulnerable around the 0.5660 level, with traders watching for a potential break that could extend losses further. Continued USD strength may drive additional downside pressure if risk-off conditions persist. In broader currency markets, attention stays on US data releases and dollar index momentum, shaping short-term forex trading direction.
