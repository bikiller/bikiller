Gold prices paused their recent recovery on Friday, with XAU/USD consolidating near $4,200 as traders refrained from taking aggressive positions ahead of further developments surrounding a potential US-Iran peace agreement. The precious metal briefly advanced to an intraday high of $4,246 before surrendering part of its gains, reflecting a cautious trading environment across global financial markets.

Market participants continue to monitor geopolitical headlines closely, as any progress toward a diplomatic breakthrough could reduce demand for traditional safe-haven assets such as gold. The pause in momentum comes after a strong rebound in recent sessions, with investors weighing shifting risk sentiment against broader macroeconomic factors, including movements in the USD and major currency pairs. A stronger US Dollar exchange rate typically limits upside potential for bullion by making the metal more expensive for overseas buyers.

Despite the current consolidation, gold remains supported by lingering geopolitical uncertainty and ongoing demand for defensive assets. Traders are also keeping a close watch on developments in the EUR/USD currency pair and broader foreign exchange markets for clues about risk appetite and capital flows. For now, XAU/USD continues to trade around $4,200, with the $4,246 intraday peak serving as a key near-term reference level as investors await clearer direction.
