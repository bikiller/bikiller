The EUR/USD currency pair continues to reflect divergent monetary policy dynamics, as Commerzbank notes the European Central Bank still trails the Federal Reserve in tackling inflation. Although recent energy shocks have driven a sharper repricing of ECB tightening expectations, the Euro’s exchange rate response remains muted compared to the USD, limiting bullish momentum in trading.

Analyst Michael Pfister highlights that, in real terms, policy support for the EUR lags behind that of the Dollar. While markets have adjusted rate expectations in Europe, the Fed’s earlier and more aggressive stance continues to underpin USD strength. This imbalance keeps downward pressure on the EUR/USD pair, particularly as traders reassess inflation trajectories and central bank credibility.

For forex markets, the implication is a sustained bias favoring the USD unless the ECB accelerates its tightening path. The EUR may struggle to gain traction despite improved rate expectations, as real yields and policy differentials dominate exchange rate movements. Traders are likely to monitor incoming inflation data and ECB signals closely for any shift in momentum.
