Societe Generale’s Anatoli Annenkov highlights that while rising oil prices and increased inflation risks are influencing the European Central Bank’s outlook, the ECB is expected to maintain its current policy stance in the near term. The ongoing oil shock is adding complexity to the rate path, but market expectations suggest that no immediate changes in ECB policy are forthcoming. This uncertainty is likely to weigh on the EUR/USD exchange rate in the short run, with traders closely monitoring any signs of policy shifts.

Oil prices have been climbing steadily, further fueling inflation concerns across the eurozone. However, with the ECB focused on managing inflation and economic recovery, it appears unlikely to adjust rates soon. As the EUR/USD continues to trade within a narrow range, the central bank’s reluctance to make sudden moves could keep the exchange rate largely stable. The market will be watching for any changes in ECB rhetoric, which could trigger volatility in the currency pair.
