The British Pound (GBP) has seen a notable outperformance against its European counterparts, driven by a shift in UK rate expectations. Initially anticipating rate cuts, markets have now priced in the possibility of a rate hike following a surge in inflation risks, fueled by an energy shock. This change has provided strong support to the GBP, as it strengthens against the EUR and USD in recent trading sessions.

The Bank of England's (BoE) hawkish stance on inflation risks has played a key role in this shift. With UK inflationary pressures rising, traders are now adjusting their positions in favor of further tightening, positioning the Pound for potential upside. This shift in sentiment has helped the GBP rally, with the GBP/USD exchange rate holding steady above key technical levels, while GBP/EUR also reflects a stronger Pound as investors remain cautious on the European economy.
