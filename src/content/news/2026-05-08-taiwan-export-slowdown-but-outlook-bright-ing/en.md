Taiwan’s trade momentum showed signs of slowing in April, with export and import growth trailing market expectations. According to ING’s Chief Economist for Greater China, Lynn Song, the island recorded a trade surplus of USD 14.35 billion, reflecting softer global demand. The data has prompted cautious trading in the TWD, as investors weigh the impact on the USD/TWD exchange rate amid broader USD strength across Asia.  

The slower-than-expected export growth, driven by semiconductor and electronics shipments, combined with moderated imports, suggests supply-chain adjustments rather than structural weakness. Forex traders reacted with modest TWD depreciation against the USD and EUR, while risk-sensitive flows favored regional peers. Technical levels around 30.5–30.7 USD/TWD have become focal points, with trading volumes hinting at potential short-term consolidation.  

Looking ahead, Song highlighted that Taiwan’s medium-term outlook remains positive, supported by resilient demand from key markets and ongoing tech investment. Currency analysts suggest that sustained trade surpluses and fiscal stability could anchor the TWD, even as global USD volatility continues to influence cross-currency moves. Market participants are likely to monitor upcoming data for confirmation before adjusting positions in USD/TWD and related currency pairs.
