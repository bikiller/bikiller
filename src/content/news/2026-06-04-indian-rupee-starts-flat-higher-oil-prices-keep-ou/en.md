The Indian Rupee (INR) opened little changed against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, following a strong performance in the previous session. The USD/INR currency pair remained near 95.72, holding onto Wednesday’s gains as traders assessed persistent strength in crude oil prices and ongoing geopolitical uncertainty. Market participants continued to monitor developments in global energy markets, which remain a key driver of the Rupee’s exchange rate.

Higher oil prices continued to cloud the outlook for the Indian currency, particularly as negotiations between the United States and Iran showed little sign of progress. The deadlock in talks has supported crude prices by raising concerns over future supply conditions. For India, a major oil importer, elevated energy costs can increase import bills and place additional pressure on the INR, limiting the scope for sustained appreciation against the USD.

In the foreign exchange market, trading sentiment remained cautious as investors weighed external risks against recent Rupee strength. The USD/INR pair’s ability to hold around 95.72 suggests underlying resilience in the US Dollar, while broader moves across major currencies such as the EUR and USD continue to influence regional exchange-rate dynamics. Traders are expected to remain focused on oil price trends and geopolitical headlines for near-term direction.
