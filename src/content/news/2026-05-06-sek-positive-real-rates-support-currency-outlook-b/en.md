The SEK has shown resilience this week, supported by Sweden’s positive real interest rates, according to Brown Brothers Harriman’s Elias Haddad. The USD/SEK currency pair has dipped amid broad-based Dollar weakness, reflecting reduced demand for the greenback. April’s disinflation in Sweden came in below expectations, prompting traders to scale back bets on further Riksbank rate hikes, which in turn bolstered the krona.  

Market participants note that USD/SEK trading has edged lower, with momentum favoring the SEK as investors reassess carry trade opportunities. The exchange rate now hovers around recent technical support levels, attracting both short-term traders and longer-term positions. Meanwhile, EUR/SEK has also seen modest gains, tracking the krona’s overall strength versus major currencies in the European forex landscape.  

Looking ahead, the outlook for the SEK remains constructive if real yields stay favorable and inflation trends continue to ease. Traders are watching global Dollar trends closely, as any renewed USD strength could temper SEK gains. For now, market dynamics suggest the currency pair’s movements will remain sensitive to both Riksbank signals and broader risk sentiment across forex markets.
