Gold (XAU/USD) bounced back on Friday, reclaiming the $4,500 mark after a sharp 2.75% decline the previous session. The precious metal is trading near $4,527, marking a gain of over 3% on the day, as a softer US Dollar provides crucial support. Traders are closely monitoring developments in the US-Israel-Iran conflict, which continues to fuel volatility across forex and commodity markets.  

The exchange rate movements reflect heightened risk sentiment, with investors shifting away from USD toward safe-haven assets like gold. Currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD are also reacting to geopolitical tensions, as central banks’ monetary outlooks remain under scrutiny. The rebound in XAU/USD underscores the interplay between geopolitical risk and currency market flows, reinforcing gold’s role as a hedge amid uncertainty.  

Analysts suggest the near-term trading range for gold remains elevated, with $4,500 acting as a key support level. A sustained weakening of the USD could drive XAU/USD higher, while any de-escalation in the Middle East may temper gains. For forex traders, these developments highlight the sensitivity of exchange rates to global events and the potential for abrupt market swings in both currency pairs and commodities.
