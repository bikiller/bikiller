EUR/JPY extended its rally on Monday, trading around 183.20, marking a 0.35% gain for the day. The currency pair saw upward momentum as the Japanese Yen weakened against the Euro. The surge in oil prices contributed to this shift, amplifying inflation concerns and pressuring the Yen further. 

The delay in the Bank of Japan's interest rate hike also weighed on the Yen, leaving it vulnerable to broader market sentiment. This combination of factors, along with Euro stability, has supported EUR/JPY, with the exchange rate now hovering near key resistance levels. Traders are closely watching the pair for further signs of Yen weakness, as the BoJ's stance remains a key factor in future price action.
