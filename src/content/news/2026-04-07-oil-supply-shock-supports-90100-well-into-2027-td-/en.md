TD Securities warns that oil prices could remain elevated in the $90–$100 range well into 2027, citing sustained supply disruptions in the Persian Gulf. With flows through the Strait of Hormuz constrained, traders are pricing in a potential surge, prompting increased volatility across energy-linked currency pairs. The USD has strengthened against commodity-sensitive currencies like the CAD and NOK, reflecting market anticipation of prolonged tightness in crude supply.  

Market participants are watching how this shock ripples through forex trading, as stronger oil prices often support commodity currencies while putting pressure on oil-importing regions. The EUR/USD pair is trading with heightened sensitivity, reacting to shifts in USD strength tied to oil-driven inflation expectations. Technical levels around $1.05–$1.07 for EUR/USD may see increased testing if risk sentiment fluctuates with oil market headlines.  

Analysts highlight that the persistent bottleneck in the Gulf could trigger another leg higher in Brent and WTI benchmarks, influencing global liquidity and hedging strategies. Traders are advised to monitor cross-currency dynamics, as commodity shocks tend to amplify correlations between the USD and oil-linked currencies. With trading desks adjusting positions, volatility in pairs such as USD/CAD and AUD/USD is likely to remain elevated, reflecting the market’s pricing of long-term supply constraints.
