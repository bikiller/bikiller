Pound Sterling slipped below the 1.3300 mark against a broadly stronger US dollar, extending losses for the GBP/USD currency pair to a fourth consecutive session. Earlier, the pair had edged higher toward 1.3370 on Friday, but disappointing UK economic data weighed on sentiment, prompting traders to favor the dollar amid expectations of tighter monetary conditions. Market participants noted increased selling pressure in the pound as UK indicators failed to meet forecasts.  

Trading activity highlighted the USD’s broad-based strength, with the greenback gaining ground not only versus Sterling but also against the euro and other major currencies. Analysts pointed to robust US economic signals and cautious investor positioning ahead of upcoming central bank updates, which have bolstered demand for safe-haven currencies. The pound’s retreat reflects a market recalibration, as traders reassess the outlook for UK growth and interest rate differentials.  

Technical levels suggest the GBP/USD may continue testing support near 1.3280 if negative data persists, while a sustained break above 1.3370 could signal a temporary recovery. For forex traders, the recent movements underscore the sensitivity of the currency pair to economic releases and USD momentum, highlighting the importance of monitoring both macro indicators and broader market flows in short-term exchange rate forecasts.
