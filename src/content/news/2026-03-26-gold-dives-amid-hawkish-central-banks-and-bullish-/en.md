Gold (XAU/USD) is experiencing significant selling pressure as it extends the previous day’s decline, falling below the 100-day Simple Moving Average (SMA). Heading into the European session on Thursday, the precious metal continues to be weighed down by hawkish signals from central banks and a strong USD, which has kept the market on edge. This selling push has sent gold prices closer to the $4,400 mark, with no immediate signs of reversal.

The bullish USD, supported by expectations of higher interest rates, has been a key driver behind gold’s drop. As traders remain focused on central banks' policy stances, the euro (EUR) and other major currencies are under pressure against the dollar. With gold trading lower, the market eyes potential further losses, with support levels close to $4,400 in sight for traders.
