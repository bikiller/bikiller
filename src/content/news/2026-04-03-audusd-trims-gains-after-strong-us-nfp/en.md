AUD/USD saw its earlier gains fade on Friday following stronger-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data, which lent support to the US Dollar (USD). The report highlighted a robust labor market, increasing the likelihood of continued tightening by the Federal Reserve. As a result, the Australian Dollar (AUD) came under modest pressure, pushing the AUD/USD exchange rate lower in early trading.

Despite the market's reaction to the NFP figures, overall price action remained relatively subdued, mainly due to thin liquidity surrounding the Good Friday holiday. This muted movement kept the currency pair within a tight range, with traders awaiting more significant catalysts. At the close, AUD/USD hovered just below key technical levels, maintaining a cautious tone as the market digested the implications of US economic strength.
