The USD/JPY currency pair continues to trend higher as renewed Yen weakness reflects deeper structural pressures, according to MUFG analysts. The exchange rate has been supported by rising energy costs tied to ongoing Middle East tensions, which disproportionately impact Japan’s import-heavy economy. This dynamic has widened trade imbalances and reinforced bearish sentiment around the Japanese Yen in recent trading sessions.

MUFG notes that capital flows are also contributing to the move, with investors favoring USD-denominated assets amid global uncertainty. The divergence in monetary policy between the Federal Reserve and the Bank of Japan remains a key driver, keeping yield differentials wide and encouraging carry trade activity. As a result, USD demand persists while the Yen struggles to regain footing.

If geopolitical risks remain elevated, analysts expect further downside for the Yen, potentially pushing USD/JPY to higher technical levels in the near term. Traders are closely monitoring energy markets and macro developments, as sustained pressure could prolong the current trend. The outlook suggests continued volatility in the currency pair, with implications for broader forex market positioning.
