The US Dollar Index (DXY) held steady above the 100.00 mark, retreating from a near 10-month high of 100.54 reached in the previous trading session. As of Monday's Asian session, the DXY was trading around 100.20, reflecting a slight pullback after its recent surge. The USD’s strength had been driven by a stronger-than-expected economic data release and rising yields on US Treasury bonds.

The pullback in the US Dollar came as market participants assessed the next moves from the Federal Reserve, with upcoming data and monetary policy decisions in focus. The EUR/USD pair, a key gauge for the dollar’s performance, remained under pressure, trading well below the 1.0700 level. The market's attention is now on upcoming economic reports, which could provide fresh insights into the USD’s near-term outlook.
