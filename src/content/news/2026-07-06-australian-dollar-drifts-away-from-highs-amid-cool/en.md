Australian Dollar (AUD) is trading moderately lower against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, drifting away from recent highs as softer-than-expected inflation in Australia eases pressure on the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) to consider further rate hikes. The move reflects shifting expectations in the currency pair as traders reassess policy divergence between Australia and the United States.

Weak employment data has also weighed on the Australian Dollar, reinforcing expectations that the RBA may adopt a more cautious stance in upcoming policy meetings. In forex trading, the AUD/USD currency pair remains sensitive to macroeconomic surprises, while a relatively steady US Dollar provides additional headwinds. Broader currency market sentiment shows investors balancing risk appetite against evolving interest rate expectations across major economies.

Looking ahead, traders are likely to focus on upcoming economic releases and central bank commentary for further direction in the Australian Dollar. Any continued weakness in inflation or labour market indicators could keep the AUD under pressure against both USD and EUR, while shifts in global risk sentiment may drive short-term volatility in the forex market.
