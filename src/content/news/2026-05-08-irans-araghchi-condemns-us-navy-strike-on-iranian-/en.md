Iran’s Foreign Minister Abbas Araghchi condemned the US Navy’s recent actions against two Iranian tankers, calling them aggressive and a breach of the ongoing truce, according to Iranian state media. The remarks came during European trade on Friday, where heightened geopolitical tensions immediately reverberated across global currency markets, pushing the USD/IRR exchange rate higher as traders weighed potential disruptions to oil exports.  

In forex trading, risk sentiment shifted toward safe-haven assets, with the USD gaining against the EUR and other major currencies. Analysts noted that any escalation in the Persian Gulf could tighten oil supply expectations, fueling volatility in commodity-linked currencies. EUR/USD briefly dipped below 1.0850, reflecting investor caution, while the USD index climbed as market participants adjusted positions in response to the geopolitical uncertainty.  

The market implications extend to both short-term trading and broader macro positioning. Brokers reported increased volume in USD/JPY and GBP/USD pairs as traders sought liquidity in more stable instruments. Araghchi’s comments underscore the fragility of regional stability, suggesting that currency pairs sensitive to Middle Eastern risks may continue to experience heightened swings in the coming sessions. Forex traders are advised to monitor developments closely for any shifts that could influence exchange rates and risk appetite.
