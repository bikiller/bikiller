Gold (XAU/USD) plunged to a fresh low of $4,700 during the early European session on Thursday, marking its lowest point since February 6. The precious metal struggled amid a hawkish outlook from the Federal Reserve, which has raised expectations for further tightening. This pressure on gold comes as the USD strengthens, fueled by rising interest rate expectations.

Despite the sharp decline, gold found some support near the $4,700 mark, preventing a deeper drop. As the EUR/USD pair continues to face headwinds from the Fed’s stance, traders are closely monitoring the exchange rate's fluctuations. The risk-off sentiment across global markets has pushed traders towards the USD, undermining gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset.
