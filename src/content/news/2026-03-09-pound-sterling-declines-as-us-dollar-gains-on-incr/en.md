The GBP/USD currency pair retreated during Monday’s Asian session, trading near 1.3300 after posting modest gains in the previous session. Sterling faced pressure as the US Dollar strengthened on heightened risk aversion, driven by escalating tensions in Iran with no immediate resolution in sight. Traders remain cautious, positioning for safe-haven flows, which bolstered the USD across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY.  

Market participants highlighted the exchange rate’s sensitivity to geopolitical developments, noting that renewed uncertainty could sustain USD demand while weighing on risk-sensitive currencies like GBP. Technical levels around 1.3320 offered initial resistance, while support near 1.3280 may guide short-term trading decisions. Analysts suggested that the pair’s movement reflects broader risk sentiment rather than domestic UK factors, signaling that market focus remains on global events.  

With investors closely monitoring the conflict and potential geopolitical spillovers, the Pound may continue to underperform against the Dollar if safe-haven buying persists. Currency traders are watching for key economic data from both the US and UK to assess whether the trend might reverse, as any shifts in risk appetite could trigger rapid adjustments in the GBP/USD exchange rate. Volatility is expected to remain elevated as market sentiment reacts to unfolding events.
