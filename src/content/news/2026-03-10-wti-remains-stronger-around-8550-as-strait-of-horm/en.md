WTI crude oil is holding firm around $85.50 per barrel, maintaining a positive trajectory despite significant volatility in the market. The price remained resilient during Tuesday's European trading session, bolstered by ongoing concerns over disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz. These disruptions have put pressure on global oil supply, keeping market sentiment bullish for the commodity. 

The elevated price of WTI is influencing forex markets, particularly impacting USD-related pairs. With oil prices higher, the USD could see upward pressure, as higher oil prices often contribute to a stronger U.S. dollar due to increased energy revenue. Meanwhile, EUR/USD and other major currency pairs may experience volatility, driven by oil price fluctuations and broader market risk sentiment.
