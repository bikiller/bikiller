GBP/USD fell 0.35% on Tuesday, closing at 1.3350, marking a decline below the 200-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA) for the first time since early December. The currency pair has struggled to maintain upward momentum as geopolitical risks intensify, particularly concerns surrounding European stability.

The dip below the 200-day EMA signals potential shifts in market sentiment, with traders likely to monitor further movements closely. This technical breach, combined with heightened global uncertainty, could weigh on the exchange rate, impacting future trading dynamics. The USD remains resilient as investors seek safe-haven assets amidst growing geopolitical tensions.
