Silver (XAG/USD) edged lower to around $67.90 per troy ounce during Tuesday’s Asian session, retracing part of yesterday’s gains. Traders remain cautious as uncertainty in the Middle East fuels safe-haven demand, yet rising expectations for a US interest rate hike are weighing on precious metals. The exchange rate movement highlights the sensitivity of XAG/USD to both geopolitical risks and monetary policy signals.  

Market participants are closely watching the USD’s strength, which has provided headwinds for silver. The currency pair’s fluctuations mirror broader trends in forex trading, with investors balancing safe-haven buying against a firmer dollar. Technical levels near $68.00 are acting as a psychological barrier, limiting further upside in the short term.  

Analysts note that continued rate hike speculation could keep XAG/USD on the defensive, even amid intermittent demand for silver as a hedge. Currency traders are also eyeing movements in EUR/USD and other major pairs, as shifts in global liquidity and central bank policies influence precious metal prices. For now, silver remains range-bound, sensitive to both macroeconomic signals and ongoing geopolitical developments.
