The closure of the Strait of Hormuz has disrupted Qatari LNG shipments, which typically account for 20% of global supply. This has pushed the TTF natural gas price to nearly EUR 70/MWh. As a result, European energy markets are facing heightened volatility, affecting the EUR/USD exchange rate and broader market sentiment. The disruption adds further pressure to the already tight energy supply, contributing to rising inflationary concerns.

This supply shock is keeping the TTF elevated, with natural gas prices significantly impacting trading activity across energy-related currency pairs. Market participants are closely monitoring developments in the Strait of Hormuz, as sustained LNG shortages could push EUR/USD further below key support levels. Meanwhile, the USD remains strong, driven by global risk aversion and its safe-haven status, further influencing energy prices and forex movements.
