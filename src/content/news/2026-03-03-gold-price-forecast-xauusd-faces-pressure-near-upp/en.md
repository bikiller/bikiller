Gold prices (XAU/USD) have dropped by 2.5%, trading near $5,180 during the European session on Tuesday, as the metal pulls back after a four-day rally. The correction comes after a sharp rise on Monday, when gold saw significant gains as investors sought refuge in safe-haven assets amid the ongoing Middle East conflict. 

The price of gold remains under pressure near the upper boundary of a rising channel, hovering around $5,400. This technical resistance level could cap further upside potential in the near term. As global uncertainty continues to fuel demand for the precious metal, traders will be watching the $5,400 zone closely for signs of a breakout or a reversal. The ongoing geopolitical tensions could continue to drive gold's appeal as a safe haven against risk.
