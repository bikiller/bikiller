Swiss Franc continues to weaken against the US Dollar in forex trading, marking its fifth consecutive losing session. The currency pair USD/CHF rose 0.15% on Tuesday, pushing the exchange rate to 0.8100 as the greenback maintained firm demand across markets. The move highlights sustained USD strength in the broader currency market.

Sentiment in the forex market was shaped by deteriorating risk appetite following the recent AI sector rout, while expectations that the Federal Reserve may raise interest rates later this year further supported the US Dollar. These dynamics added pressure on the Swiss Franc, as traders adjusted positions amid shifting macroeconomic signals and interest rate differentials.

Against the euro, the Swiss Franc showed relative strength, climbing in the EUR/CHF currency pair and reflecting a mixed trading pattern across major European crosses. The divergence between USD and EUR performance underscores uneven market flows, with traders closely monitoring central bank signals and broader risk sentiment for direction in coming sessions.
