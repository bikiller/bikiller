The US dollar strengthened on Wednesday as markets reassessed the Federal Reserve’s hawkish stance, lifting the USD against major peers. The EUR/USD currency pair slipped to 1.0965, while USD/JPY rose to 145.30, reflecting increased demand for the greenback. Traders cited persistent expectations of tighter monetary policy, which supported the exchange rate momentum and sparked renewed trading interest in USD pairs.

Market participants are now eyeing the FOMC minutes for further guidance. Any signals of additional rate hikes could reinforce the dollar’s upward trajectory, while softer-than-expected commentary might ease pressure on EUR and other currencies. Technical levels remain closely watched, with EUR/USD facing resistance near 1.1000 and support at 1.0920, and USD/JPY testing key psychological thresholds above 145.00.

Volatility in forex markets continues as traders digest economic data and Fed communications. Currency pair movements reflect not just fundamentals but also positioning ahead of potential policy cues. For now, the USD remains the center of attention, shaping cross rates and trading strategies, with investors balancing hawkish expectations against broader macroeconomic conditions in Europe, Japan, and beyond.
