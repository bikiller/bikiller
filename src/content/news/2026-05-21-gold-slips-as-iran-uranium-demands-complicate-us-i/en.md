Gold prices edged lower on Thursday, with XAU/USD struggling to extend gains from Wednesday’s rebound. The precious metal trades around $4,504, down roughly 0.85% on the day, as investors digest tensions surrounding US-Iran nuclear talks. Renewed concerns over Iran’s uranium demands have weighed on risk sentiment, prompting a cautious tone in global trading and limiting momentum in the gold market.

Currency pairs linked to safe-haven flows, including USD/EUR and USD/JPY, have seen moderate volatility as traders assess the geopolitical backdrop. The exchange rate fluctuations reflect a mix of risk-off positioning and USD strength, as market participants weigh potential impacts on commodities and cross-currency trades. Analysts note that the gold market’s technical levels remain vulnerable to headline-driven swings in the near term.

The broader implication for traders is a cautious approach to gold and related forex pairs, with XAU/USD sensitive to both geopolitical developments and USD movements. Any escalation in US-Iran tensions could trigger further downside in gold prices, while a resolution or easing of nuclear negotiations might stabilize trading. Market watchers continue to monitor key support and resistance levels for tactical entries across FX and commodity markets.
