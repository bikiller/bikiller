The US Dollar (USD) is showing renewed strength, supported by a hawkish Federal Reserve narrative as markets increasingly price in modest additional tightening for 2026. ING’s Chris Turner points out that short-dated US rates remain elevated, bolstered by persistently high oil prices and ongoing Gulf tensions. This dynamic is helping the DXY index recover, drawing renewed attention from forex traders monitoring the USD’s momentum against major peers.  

Currency pairs involving the USD, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, have seen heightened trading activity as investors reassess positioning. The Fed’s stance, coupled with geopolitical concerns, is sustaining demand for the greenback, keeping exchange rates skewed in its favor. Analysts note that technical levels in key pairs are reacting to these fundamentals, with the USD broadly strengthening while the euro and yen face pressure in the near term.  

Looking ahead, market participants are weighing the likelihood of incremental Fed tightening against global risk factors. Any further escalation in Gulf tensions or shifts in energy prices could extend USD gains, influencing trading flows across forex markets. Investors are closely watching the interplay between monetary policy expectations and geopolitical developments, as these will likely determine the USD’s trajectory and shape exchange rates for major currency pairs in the coming months.
