European Central Bank (ECB) board member Isabel Schnabel warned that the ongoing war in Iran poses new upside inflation risks. Speaking at the 2026 US Monetary Policy Forum in New York, Schnabel noted that the conflict could lead to disruptions in energy markets, further exacerbating inflationary pressures in Europe. Despite this, she emphasized that the ECB remains well-positioned to manage its monetary policy.

In forex markets, the euro (EUR) remained largely steady against the US dollar (USD) following Schnabel’s comments, with the EUR/USD exchange rate holding near the 1.0850 level. Investors are cautious, awaiting further developments on inflation and the geopolitical landscape. Analysts are watching closely for any signs that could influence the ECB's next policy move, particularly as the central bank faces the challenge of balancing inflation control with economic growth.
