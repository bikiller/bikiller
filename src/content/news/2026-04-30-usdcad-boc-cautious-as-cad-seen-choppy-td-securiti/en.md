The Bank of Canada (BoC) maintained its key interest rate at 2.25%, adopting a cautious tone in its latest policy statement. Highlighting ongoing trade uncertainties and a soft labour market, the BoC indicated that it anticipates subdued economic conditions ahead. While it modestly revised Canada's 2026 growth forecast upward, the central bank signaled no immediate changes to policy through 2026, with expectations for two rate hikes in 2027.

For traders in the USD/CAD pair, the BoC's cautious stance is likely to fuel volatility in the Canadian dollar (CAD) as it grapples with domestic and global headwinds. The rate decision and outlook suggest a mixed outlook for CAD, especially in light of fluctuating global trade dynamics and slower domestic growth. The USD/CAD exchange rate is expected to remain choppy, reflecting market sensitivity to BoC decisions and broader economic developments.
