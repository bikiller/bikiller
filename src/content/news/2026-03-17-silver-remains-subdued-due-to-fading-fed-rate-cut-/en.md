Silver (XAG/USD) slipped to around $80.50 on Tuesday, marking a 0.60% decline as traders weighed shifting expectations for US monetary policy. The precious metal remains under pressure amid diminishing bets on near-term Fed rate cuts, with investors reacting to persistent inflation concerns exacerbated by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The softer tone in silver trading reflects broader caution in commodity-linked currencies.  

USD strength has played a role in limiting silver’s upside, as the dollar index maintains firm footing against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Currency pair dynamics continue to influence trading sentiment, with investors favoring USD-denominated assets amid uncertainty. Analysts note that silver may struggle to reclaim higher levels unless inflation expectations ease or risk sentiment improves.  

Market participants are closely monitoring developments in the Middle East, as any escalation could intensify inflationary pressures, impacting both the USD exchange rate and commodity markets. Traders remain alert to technical support near $80, while resistance levels around $82 could test bullish momentum if macroeconomic indicators shift. Overall, silver’s subdued trading highlights the interplay between Fed policy expectations, geopolitical risks, and currency movements.
