EUR/USD retreated below the 1.1600 level on Wednesday, as the Euro posted moderate losses against the US Dollar. The currency pair gave back part of its recent gains as traders reduced exposure ahead of the upcoming US Federal Reserve decision, with overall trading sentiment turning more cautious across major FX markets.

Market focus remained on the US Federal Reserve, with commentary surrounding Kevin Warsh adding to expectations of potential policy shifts and interest-rate direction. The US Dollar found mild support as Treasury yields held firm, while EUR/USD trading activity reflected positioning adjustments among investors ahead of the central bank announcement and related guidance.

From a technical perspective, the EUR/USD exchange rate slipping back under 1.1600 signals renewed downside pressure, with the level now acting as immediate resistance. Traders will watch whether sustained weakness extends toward lower support zones if Fed messaging reinforces a stronger USD outlook, potentially increasing volatility in short-term trading sessions.
