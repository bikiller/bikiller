AUD/USD climbed near the 0.6930 area on Thursday as the US Dollar came under pressure following softer-than-expected United States Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) data. The currency pair’s exchange rate strengthened as traders reassessed USD demand across major forex trading sessions, with risk-sensitive currencies finding support amid shifting sentiment in global markets.

Stronger selling pressure on the US Dollar followed the data, as weaker employment figures dampened expectations for further Federal Reserve tightening. Currency traders rotated into higher-yielding assets, lifting commodity-linked currencies such as the Australian Dollar. Broader forex markets reflected improved risk appetite, with the USD index slipping against major peers including EUR and JPY during late trading hours in global markets.

Ahead of upcoming US economic releases, the AUD/USD currency pair may remain sensitive to shifts in US Dollar sentiment, with traders closely monitoring whether the exchange rate can sustain gains above the 0.6930 area. Short-term trading conditions are likely to remain volatile as expectations for Federal Reserve policy adjust, influencing broader forex market direction and risk flows across major currencies.
