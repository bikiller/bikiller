West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices have rebounded above $79.00, recovering from earlier losses as escalating tensions in the Middle East disrupt global energy flows. During the Asian trading session on Friday, WTI was seen trading around $78.80, reflecting a resilient market that is adjusting to geopolitical uncertainties impacting supply dynamics.

The ongoing conflict in the region has heightened concerns over potential interruptions in oil shipments, prompting traders to reassess their positions. As WTI maintains its upward momentum, the implications for currency pairs such as USD/EUR and broader forex markets could be significant, with oil prices potentially influencing inflation expectations and central bank policies in the coming weeks.
