The Japanese Yen retained a firm risk premium in forex trading after China expanded its export control list to include additional Japanese entities, according to BNY’s Geoff Yu. The move has heightened concerns over supply chain disruptions, particularly in the defense and rare earth sectors, reinforcing demand for the JPY as investors assess potential economic and geopolitical risks. The development has drawn attention across major currency pair markets, including USD/JPY and EUR/JPY, where traders continue to monitor shifts in risk sentiment and exchange rate dynamics.

At the same time, stronger domestic fundamentals provided further support for the Japanese currency. Japanese commercial sales posted robust growth in May, highlighting resilient business activity and suggesting that underlying economic momentum remains intact despite external uncertainties. The combination of solid domestic demand and elevated geopolitical concerns has helped strengthen the case for holding JPY positions against major counterparts such as the USD and EUR.

For currency markets, the dual impact of stronger economic data and rising supply chain risks may continue to underpin the Yen in the near term. Traders are likely to watch incoming economic indicators and policy developments closely, as sustained strength in commercial activity could reinforce expectations for a firmer JPY exchange rate while broader risk trends remain a key driver of market direction.
