Global risk sentiment has improved as US–Iran diplomatic efforts sustain the ceasefire, easing geopolitical pressures that had supported safe-haven assets. Brent crude slipped, reflecting calmer energy markets, while the US Dollar (USD) softened against major peers. According to Brown Brothers Harriman’s Elias Haddad, this environment underpins a range-trading view for the USD, with limited directional bias as investors monitor geopolitical developments and economic data.  

In the forex market, the EUR/USD exchange rate has edged higher, reflecting broad USD softness, while USD/JPY and GBP/USD remain within their recent trading bands. Traders are watching key technical levels, with support and resistance holding firm amid subdued volatility. Haddad notes that range-bound trading is likely to persist unless fresh catalysts—such as shifts in US monetary policy or renewed Middle East tensions—alter market dynamics.  

Market participants are balancing modest dollar weakness with stabilizing risk assets. The interplay between energy prices, geopolitical news, and US economic indicators is shaping intraday trading flows. For currency traders, this means opportunities to trade within established bands rather than chasing breakout trends, emphasizing careful positioning and attention to event-driven shifts that could disrupt the current equilibrium in major currency pairs.
