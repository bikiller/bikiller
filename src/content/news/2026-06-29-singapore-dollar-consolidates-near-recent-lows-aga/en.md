Singapore dollar consolidates near recent lows against the US dollar as USD/SGD slipped to 1.2929 before recovering, according to UOB’s Quek Ser Leang. The currency pair remains in a sideways trading structure, showing limited momentum in forex markets. Price action suggests buyers are defending downside moves despite intraday volatility. Overall sentiment remains neutral.

Intraday trading showed USD/SGD briefly dipping toward 1.2929 before rebounding, reinforcing the view that the exchange rate is locked within a narrow consolidation range. Market participants continue to monitor USD strength and regional currency flows, with the pair lacking a clear breakout catalyst during recent forex sessions. Traders remain cautious ahead of key macroeconomic data.

Overall, USD/SGD continues to trade within a tight band as the Singapore dollar holds near recent lows. The absence of strong directional drivers in global forex trading keeps volatility contained, with the pair respecting support around 1.2929 and resistance near 1.2960. This range-bound behavior underscores subdued risk appetite in the broader currency market environment.
