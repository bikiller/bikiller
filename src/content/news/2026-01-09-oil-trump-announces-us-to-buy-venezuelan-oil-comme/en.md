Oil prices faced downward pressure mid-week as U.S. President Trump announced on social media that the U.S. plans to purchase between 30 to 50 million barrels of sanctioned oil from Venezuela. This unexpected move prompted concerns among traders, affecting the broader commodities market and leading to fluctuations in related currency pairs. The news contributed to a volatile trading environment for crude oil, with prices dipping as market participants assessed potential implications for supply dynamics.

The announcement also reverberated through forex markets, influencing the exchange rate of the USD against other currencies. As traders reacted to the oil price changes, the EUR/USD pair displayed notable movement, reflecting the interplay between commodity prices and currency valuations. With the U.S. decision potentially altering global oil supply and demand, traders are closely monitoring further developments and their impact on both oil and forex trading strategies.
