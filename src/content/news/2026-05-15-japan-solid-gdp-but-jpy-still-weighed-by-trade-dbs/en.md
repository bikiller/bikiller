Japan’s economy is expected to post a solid first-quarter performance, with DBS economists Taimur Baig and Radhika Rao forecasting GDP growth of 1.8% QoQ saar. The expansion is being driven by resilient exports and sustained investment linked to artificial intelligence and semiconductor industries, reinforcing the bank’s full-year GDP forecast of 0.5%. Despite the upbeat macro backdrop, the JPY has struggled to gain traction in forex trading.

The Japanese Yen remains under pressure as trade-related concerns and broad USD strength continue to dominate market sentiment. Investors have largely focused on external risks and yield differentials rather than domestic growth momentum, limiting support for the USD/JPY exchange rate. Currency traders also remain cautious over Japan’s trade outlook, particularly as slowing global demand and geopolitical uncertainty cloud the export sector’s medium-term prospects.

In the broader FX market, the muted reaction in the JPY highlights how monetary policy expectations and global capital flows continue to outweigh positive domestic economic data. While stronger GDP figures could support speculation around future Bank of Japan normalization, analysts believe the currency pair will remain sensitive to US Treasury yields and Federal Reserve policy signals. The EUR/JPY and USD/JPY pairs are therefore likely to stay volatile as investors reassess risk appetite and regional growth trends.
