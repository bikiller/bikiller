The GBP/JPY currency pair reversed earlier gains on Friday, retreating from an intraday high of 215.65-215.70 to the lower bounds of its daily range during the early European session. Despite the pullback, the pair managed to stay above the key 215.00 level, reflecting a mix of cautious trading sentiment and profit-taking after the recent upside momentum. Traders are monitoring how this level holds amid broader market fluctuations.  

Market participants pointed to mixed cues from both the USD and EUR, which continue to influence sterling and yen flows. Strength in the US dollar against major currencies, coupled with softer demand for risk-sensitive assets, weighed on GBP/JPY. Meanwhile, modest EUR/JPY gains offered limited support, leaving the cross vulnerable to sellers near technical resistance points.  

Analysts suggest the pair’s current behavior signals consolidation after its recent advance, with 215.00 acting as short-term support. A decisive break below this level could open the way for further declines, while a rebound toward 215.65-215.70 may attract renewed buying interest. Traders remain attentive to global sentiment, central bank guidance, and economic data from the UK, Japan, and the US for cues on future direction.
