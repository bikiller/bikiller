WTI crude oil prices surged above $92.50 on Thursday, reaching $92.65 during early Asian trading hours. This 6.5% rise is largely driven by renewed concerns over supply disruptions, as fresh attacks on ships in the Strait of Hormuz heighten tensions in the region. The uncertainty surrounding oil shipments has triggered fears of a potential tightening in global supply, pushing crude prices higher.

The market's reaction has ripple effects on the broader forex landscape, with the USD gaining momentum. Investors are closely monitoring the situation as geopolitical risks could exacerbate inflationary pressures, influencing central bank policies and exchange rates. The sharp climb in WTI has fueled speculation on how rising oil prices might impact currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY, with traders adjusting their strategies accordingly.
