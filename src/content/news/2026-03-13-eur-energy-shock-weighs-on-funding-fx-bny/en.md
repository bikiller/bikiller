European currencies are under renewed pressure as soaring energy costs strain current-account-surplus economies, according to BNY’s Head of Markets Macro Strategy, Bob Savage. The euro has struggled to maintain recent gains, with the EUR/USD trading near key technical support around 1.0850. Market participants are weighing the impact of rising natural gas and oil prices, which could weigh on corporate earnings and economic growth across the Eurozone.  

FX traders note that the energy shock is fueling volatility in the EUR, prompting tighter spreads and cautious positioning ahead of upcoming economic data. Short-term funding flows into the euro have slowed, as investors consider the potential drag on monetary conditions. The USD remains supported, benefiting from safe-haven demand and steady U.S. Treasury yields, keeping the EUR/USD exchange rate under pressure.  

Analysts suggest that the persistence of higher energy costs could drive further adjustments in trading strategies, with investors monitoring European inflation and current account figures closely. A sustained EUR weakness may create opportunities for USD-denominated assets, while technical levels around 1.0800-1.0850 are eyed as critical for potential rebounds or continued declines in the currency pair. Market liquidity is expected to remain sensitive to headline shocks, shaping short-term trading patterns in EUR/USD.
