GBP/USD showed a brief surge above the 1.3480 level before easing back to the mid‑1.33s, highlighting a cautious trading environment for the currency pair. Strategists from UOB, Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann, point to this move as an indication of upside risk being contingent on a sustained close above 1.3480. Traders are monitoring whether the exchange rate can maintain momentum against the USD after recent consolidations.  

The near-term trading range for GBP/USD appears confined between 1.3350 and 1.3450, suggesting limited directional bias. Market participants are weighing the impact of broader USD strength and movements in EUR/USD, which may influence GBP flows. Technical levels around 1.3480 are likely to serve as critical resistance, while 1.3350 acts as immediate support, framing potential trading opportunities for short-term positions.  

Investors are balancing macroeconomic signals with technical cues, as GBP/USD remains sensitive to shifts in risk sentiment and interest rate expectations. A decisive break above 1.3480 could trigger further upside, while failure to hold above the mid‑1.33s may keep the pair range-bound. Overall, the current exchange rate action reflects a cautious market, with traders watching both USD strength and key support and resistance levels for guidance.
