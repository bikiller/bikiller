The USD/JPY currency pair extended its upward momentum on Tuesday, rebounding after briefly testing the 20-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at 158.10 on Monday. The US dollar gained traction against the Japanese yen, pushing the exchange rate toward the 159.00 level and registering daily gains exceeding 0.14%. Traders remain focused on technical support around the SMA as a potential springboard for further gains.  

Market participants are eyeing the 160.00 mark as the next significant target, with bullish sentiment driven by a mix of safe-haven flows and firm US dollar strength. Movements in other major currencies, such as EUR/USD, indicate ongoing demand for the dollar, which could reinforce USD/JPY’s upward trajectory. Short-term trading activity reflects cautious optimism, with traders balancing technical indicators against broader macroeconomic signals.  

Analysts suggest that if USD/JPY sustains above 159.00, it may open the door to testing 160.00 in coming sessions. The interplay between US economic data, interest rate expectations, and yen dynamics continues to shape trading strategies. As investors monitor volatility across global currency pairs, the focus remains on momentum-driven trading and the implications for cross-market positioning.
