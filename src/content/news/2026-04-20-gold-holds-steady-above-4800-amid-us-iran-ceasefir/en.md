Gold (XAU/USD) remains steady near $4,825 in early Tuesday trading, holding above the $4,800 mark as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East keep markets on edge. The precious metal's stability comes amid heightened uncertainty surrounding a potential ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran, which has led to fluctuating sentiment in global markets.

While gold is experiencing a relatively flat movement, its safe-haven appeal continues to support demand, especially as the U.S. dollar (USD) remains vulnerable to external risks. The ongoing geopolitical instability keeps traders cautious, leading to cautious positioning in the gold market. Investors are likely to remain attentive to developments in U.S.-Iran relations, as further volatility could lead to shifts in gold's price action and impact its trading dynamics against the USD and other currencies.
