Iran has strongly condemned the U.S.'s decision to impose a blockade on vessels entering and exiting its waters. This move, announced during European trading hours on Monday, has caused volatility in global markets, particularly affecting the USD/IRR exchange rate. The Iranian government criticized the U.S. for escalating tensions and vowed to protect its shipping routes. As a result, the Iranian rial weakened slightly against the dollar, with traders reacting to the heightened geopolitical risk.

The U.S. dollar continued to gain ground amid the unfolding situation, strengthening against most major currencies, including the EUR/USD pair. The euro saw some pressure, falling towards the 1.0750 level, as market participants moved into safer assets. Analysts expect further fluctuations in the exchange rates as tensions rise, with the potential for increased volatility in the global forex markets in the coming days.
