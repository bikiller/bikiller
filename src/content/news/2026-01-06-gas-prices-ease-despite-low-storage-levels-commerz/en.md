European gas prices have fallen below EUR 28/MWh, as traders shift their focus from the current cold snap to forecasts of milder weather. This decline comes despite gas storage levels remaining significantly below average, indicating ongoing supply concerns that could affect the market in the longer term.

The easing of gas prices could influence currency pairs involving the euro, particularly the EUR/USD exchange rate, which has been sensitive to fluctuations in energy costs. As traders digest the implications of lower gas prices amid tight supplies, volatility in the forex market may increase, impacting overall trading strategies.
