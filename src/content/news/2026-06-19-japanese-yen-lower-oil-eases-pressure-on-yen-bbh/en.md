USD/JPY is trading just below its multi-decade high, according to Brown Brothers Harriman strategist Elias Haddad. The currency pair remains elevated in forex trading, reflecting persistent US dollar strength against the Japanese yen. However, Haddad notes that the recent slump in oil prices may begin to ease downside pressure on the yen in the near term.

Falling crude oil prices are significant for Japan, a major energy importer, as they reduce import costs and help improve the trade balance. This dynamic typically supports the Japanese yen by lowering external funding needs. In forex markets, the adjustment may moderate USD/JPY upside momentum, even as US dollar demand remains broadly firm across major currency pairs.

Traders will watch whether lower oil prices can sustain additional yen support or if the exchange rate remains dominated by US monetary policy expectations. USD/JPY could stay near elevated levels, but any continued energy-led improvement in Japan’s terms of trade may limit further upside in the currency pair and increase short-term trading volatility.
