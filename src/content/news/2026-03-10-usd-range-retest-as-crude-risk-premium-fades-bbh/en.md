The USD retreated in early trading as markets reassessed the crude oil risk premium, which had surged amid geopolitical tensions. Comments from President Trump eased fears of a prolonged energy conflict, prompting investors to unwind positions that had supported the greenback. The EUR/USD currency pair climbed back toward key resistance levels near 1.1050, while USD/JPY dipped below 135.50, reflecting renewed risk appetite across global markets.  

Equities and bond markets rallied alongside the softer dollar, with major indices in Europe and the U.S. posting modest gains. Traders noted that the pullback in crude-related risk sentiment reduced safe-haven demand for the USD, encouraging flows into riskier assets. Technical analysts observed that the dollar index is testing its recent range lows around 103.50, highlighting the potential for a short-term reversal if oil prices stabilize.  

The shift in sentiment underscores how geopolitics continue to shape currency trading. For forex participants, the recalibration of the crude oil premium signals potential trading opportunities in major pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY. Market watchers caution that while the immediate pressure on the dollar has eased, any renewed flare in oil tensions could quickly reignite volatility, keeping the USD in a delicate balancing act.
