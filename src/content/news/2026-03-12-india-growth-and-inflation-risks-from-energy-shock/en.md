MUFG's Senior Currency Analyst, Michael Wan, warns that a potential closure of the Strait of Hormuz would severely disrupt India’s economy, especially its energy supply. Such an event would result in significant shortages of crude oil, liquefied petroleum gas (LPG), and natural gas liquids (NGLs), pushing up prices and impacting essential sectors like fertilisers and food production. This energy shock could trigger inflationary pressures, weakening the Indian rupee (INR) against the USD and EUR.

The energy crisis would heighten India’s reliance on imported oil, exacerbating inflationary concerns and possibly leading to a widening trade deficit. This could further weigh on the INR’s exchange rate, with traders closely monitoring key technical levels. A sharp drop in the rupee’s value may spur demand for safe-haven assets like the USD, while rising commodity prices would put additional pressure on India’s inflation trajectory, threatening growth prospects.
