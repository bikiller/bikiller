US Dollar movement is closely tied to geopolitical shifts, as Rabobank's Michael Every highlights in the context of the ongoing Gulf War 3. The conflict’s impact on energy control in the Middle East could significantly alter US strategic dominance. As global tensions rise, the USD's role in global trade and energy pricing remains crucial, with its strength often tied to such geopolitical developments. 

The EUR/USD exchange rate could see notable volatility if these geopolitical dynamics intensify. As investors seek safe-haven assets, demand for the USD may push the currency higher. Technical levels are likely to shift, with key support and resistance zones around 1.0600 and 1.0800, respectively. The data calendar will also play a pivotal role in shaping market sentiment, especially as US economic data releases provide further clues to the Fed's next moves.
