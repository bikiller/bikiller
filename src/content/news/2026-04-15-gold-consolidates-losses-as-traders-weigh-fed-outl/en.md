Gold (XAU/USD) is consolidating losses on Wednesday, holding steady as traders weigh the Federal Reserve's outlook alongside ongoing developments in the Middle East. Despite broad pressure on the US Dollar (USD), gold remains in a tight range. The precious metal is currently trading near $1,975, down modestly by 0.2% on the day, as market participants adjust to potential shifts in US monetary policy.

The USD continues to face headwinds, largely driven by cautious expectations surrounding future Fed rate hikes and the possibility of easing tensions with Iran. As negotiations between the US and Iran progress, global risk sentiment is in flux, keeping the broader market on edge. Gold's ability to maintain its position above the $1,970 level suggests ongoing investor interest in the asset as a safe haven amidst geopolitical uncertainties.
