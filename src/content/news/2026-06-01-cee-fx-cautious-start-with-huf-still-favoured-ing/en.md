Central and Eastern European currencies are entering the new month on a cautious footing, with sentiment showing modest improvement amid a flurry of local economic data. Traders are keeping a close eye on the USD/HUF and EUR/HUF currency pairs, as the Hungarian forint remains the preferred option in the region. Exchange rates have held within tight ranges overnight, reflecting balanced risk appetite and limited directional conviction.

Market participants are digesting key indicators and central bank signals, which are supporting selective trading in CEE FX. The forint’s resilience against the euro and the dollar suggests technical support near the 400 HUF per EUR mark is holding firm, while short-term resistance hovers around 405. Volatility in other CEE currencies, such as the PLN and CZK, has been muted, reinforcing the HUF’s relative appeal.

Analysts anticipate that this cautious trading environment may persist until upcoming local data releases clarify economic trends. Any surprises in inflation or industrial output could trigger sharper movements in the USD/EUR and regional currency pairs. For now, the HUF remains the focus for traders seeking opportunities in Central and Eastern European foreign exchange markets, with broader market sentiment gently tilted toward measured, low-risk positioning.
