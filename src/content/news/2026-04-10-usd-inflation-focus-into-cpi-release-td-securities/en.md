The US Consumer Price Index (CPI) release is set to be the focal point for traders, with core inflation expected to rise by 0.27% month-over-month. Headline CPI is projected to jump by 0.90% m/m, driven by higher oil prices impacting broader costs. This data is critical for forex markets, particularly in influencing the USD's performance against key currency pairs like EUR/USD.

The inflation report could impact USD exchange rates, with traders anticipating potential volatility. A higher-than-expected CPI may prompt further USD strength, especially as markets adjust expectations for Federal Reserve policy. In contrast, weaker-than-expected data might dampen Fed tightening bets, resulting in a pullback in the USD. The outcome of this release will be key for short-term trading strategies, especially in pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.
