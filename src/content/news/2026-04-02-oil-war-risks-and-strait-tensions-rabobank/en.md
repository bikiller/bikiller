Brent crude briefly dipped below the $100 mark amid rising concerns over geopolitical tensions, particularly around the Strait. Traders reacted nervously, with risk sentiment softening until former President Donald Trump’s national address shifted the mood. His remarks triggered a rebound in oil prices, while U.S. Treasury yields climbed, prompting adjustments across major currency pairs, including USD/EUR and USD/JPY, as investors recalibrated their exposure to risk.  

The oil rally, paired with higher bond yields, influenced forex trading dynamics, strengthening the dollar against a basket of currencies. Analysts observed that currency pairs closely tied to commodity flows, such as AUD/USD and CAD/USD, displayed heightened volatility, reflecting sensitivity to energy market swings. Traders noted that these movements could signal short-term trends, encouraging active monitoring of support and resistance levels around key technical points.  

Rabobank’s Senior Market Strategist Benjamin Picton emphasized that ongoing war risks and strait tensions remain central to market sentiment. The interplay between energy prices, bond yields, and USD exchange rates underscores how geopolitical events can ripple through financial markets. Investors and currency traders alike are adjusting positions in response, highlighting the growing interconnection between commodity shocks and forex volatility.
