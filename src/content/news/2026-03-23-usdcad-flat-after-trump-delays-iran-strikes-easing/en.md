The USD/CAD currency pair remained largely flat on Monday, with the Canadian Dollar showing little directional bias against the US Dollar. The market reacted to news that US President Donald Trump had postponed planned strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure, easing safe-haven demand for the Greenback. Traders noted the exchange rate hovering near recent ranges, reflecting caution ahead of further geopolitical updates.  

Investor attention also shifted to broader USD movements, as the Dollar weakened against major peers, including the Euro and the Yen. In trading, the USD/CAD pair tested support levels near 1.3360, while resistance remained around 1.3420. Analysts suggested that subdued momentum may persist unless new developments emerge in the Middle East or fresh US economic data enters the market.  

Technically, short-term trading strategies indicated limited volatility, with the pair oscillating within a narrow channel. Market participants highlighted that easing geopolitical tensions can reduce demand for safe-haven currencies like the USD, benefiting commodity-linked currencies such as the CAD. Forex traders are now eyeing upcoming US economic releases and oil price movements for cues on the next directional move in USD/CAD.
