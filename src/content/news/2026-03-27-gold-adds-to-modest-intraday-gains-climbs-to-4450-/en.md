Gold (XAU/USD) extended its modest intraday gains on Friday, climbing above the $4,450 mark as the market digested a delay in the anticipated Iran strike. The precious metal partially recovered from an overnight dip, with trading showing steady demand amid a softer USD. Investors appear to be weighing geopolitical risks alongside broader currency movements, particularly against major pairs like EUR/USD.  

The currency markets reflected cautious sentiment, with the USD under pressure, allowing gold to strengthen. Traders noted that a weaker dollar often supports higher gold prices, as the metal becomes cheaper for holders of other currencies. Exchange rate fluctuations across key pairs, including USD/JPY and GBP/USD, added to the market’s risk-sensitive tone, keeping traders alert to both geopolitical headlines and technical levels.  

Analysts suggest that if the USD remains subdued, XAU/USD could maintain its upward momentum above $4,450, potentially testing recent intraday highs. Market participants are closely monitoring geopolitical developments and central bank cues, which could influence liquidity and trading patterns in both the forex and commodities arenas. For now, gold’s ascent underscores its role as a hedge amid currency volatility and geopolitical uncertainty.
