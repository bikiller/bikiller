The Pound Sterling (GBP) continues its downward trend, trading 0.3% lower against the US Dollar (USD) during the European session on Tuesday. The GBP/USD exchange rate dips to around 1.3360, reflecting ongoing market uncertainty. The recent underperformance is attributed to geopolitical tensions, specifically the escalating conflict between the US and Iran over the Israel crisis, which has weighed on risk sentiment.

The broader forex market remains cautious as investors monitor the geopolitical situation, with the US Dollar holding firm against most major currencies. The Pound's weakness is compounded by concerns over the UK’s economic outlook and political stability. As the situation unfolds, GBP/USD is likely to remain under pressure, and traders will be looking for any signs of easing tensions or domestic policy shifts to gauge potential support for the currency.
