Oil prices remain elevated near the $100 mark as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and ongoing disruptions around the Strait of Hormuz continue to strain global supply chains. These developments have heightened market uncertainty, keeping traders cautious. The resulting sentiment has buoyed crude oil's value, impacting related currency pairs such as USD/CAD and EUR/USD, with fluctuations reflecting shifts in commodity prices.

With supply risks in focus, the oil market is likely to remain volatile, creating ripple effects across global currencies. The USD, often viewed as a safe-haven asset, could see continued strength against the EUR and other risk-sensitive currencies as traders adjust their positions in response to rising oil prices. As the situation unfolds, the outlook for oil remains tethered to these geopolitical factors, influencing both short-term trading dynamics and long-term economic projections.
