Brent crude’s heavily backwardated curve continues to support risk assets despite heightened geopolitical tensions. Deutsche Bank’s Henry Allen notes that the recent Iran conflict has pushed long-dated Brent prices higher, yet equities and currencies like the USD and EUR have shown resilience in trading. Investors are interpreting the backwardation as a buffer, keeping exchange rates relatively stable even amid oil-driven market jitters.  

In forex markets, major currency pairs have responded cautiously to the crude dynamics. The USD has maintained strength against the EUR and JPY as traders weigh the impact of oil prices on inflation expectations and monetary policy. The backwardated Brent structure reduces immediate supply concerns, encouraging continued equity and risk asset buying while keeping trading activity in pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD orderly.  

Analysts suggest that as long as the Brent curve remains in backwardation, risk appetite may persist. Currency markets are likely to reflect this sentiment, with the USD potentially consolidating gains and commodity-linked currencies such as CAD and NOK tracking oil movements. Traders will monitor technical levels closely, balancing geopolitical risk against the cushioning effect of backwardation in their short- and medium-term strategies.
