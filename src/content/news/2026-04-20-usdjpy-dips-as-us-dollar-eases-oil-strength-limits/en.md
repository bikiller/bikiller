The USD/JPY currency pair edged lower on Monday, reflecting a softening US Dollar as traders weighed hopes for a potential resolution to the US-Iran conflict. The exchange rate, which had seen earlier gains, retreated amid easing risk sentiment, with market participants closely monitoring geopolitical developments. Despite the dip, the Yen’s advance was tempered by firm oil prices, which supported risk assets and limited safe-haven flows.  

Technical trading levels held significance, with USD/JPY hovering near key support points around 136.50. Traders noted that a sustained move below this level could signal further downside, while resistance near 137.20 capped recovery attempts. Market activity remained cautious, as the interplay between geopolitical optimism and commodity-driven pressure influenced short-term USD/JPY positioning.  

Meanwhile, broader currency markets mirrored these dynamics. The USD showed modest weakness against the euro and other major peers, while energy-linked currencies benefited from elevated crude prices. For forex traders, the current environment underscores the sensitivity of the USD/JPY exchange rate to both macroeconomic signals and geopolitical headlines, highlighting the importance of monitoring news flows and technical thresholds in intraday trading decisions.
