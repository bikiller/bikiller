ECB Chief Economist Philip Lane, in remarks reported by Reuters, said further interest rate increases remain justified even under a milder economic outlook. The European Central Bank continues to stress a restrictive monetary policy stance aimed at containing inflation pressures driven by the energy shock across the eurozone. The guidance reinforces the ECB’s willingness to maintain tight conditions despite easing growth momentum.

The comments strengthened expectations that euro area policy will stay relatively tight compared with other major central banks, supporting the euro in currency trading. In the forex market, the EUR/USD currency pair reacted as traders reassessed the timing and scale of future policy easing. The exchange rate remains sensitive to shifting rate expectations, while eurozone bond yields continue to reflect a cautious stance on inflation persistence.

Looking ahead, the ECB’s messaging suggests limited urgency to pivot even if the economic outlook softens further. For EUR/USD trading, this keeps interest rate differentials a key driver of short-term volatility. As a result, the euro may remain reactive to incoming inflation data and central bank commentary, with exchange rate movements closely tied to expectations around the duration of restrictive policy.
