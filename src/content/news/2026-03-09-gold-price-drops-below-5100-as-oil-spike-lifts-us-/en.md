Gold prices (XAU/USD) continued to face downward pressure on Monday, falling over 1.50% below the opening price as geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz sparked a surge in oil prices. West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude jumped more than 30%, hitting $113 per barrel, driving the US Dollar higher across the board. At the time of writing, XAU/USD is trading at $5,090.

The spike in oil prices has had a direct impact on the US Dollar, strengthening its position against major currencies. This, combined with the ongoing disruptions in global oil supply, has kept gold under pressure as traders sought safer, higher-yielding assets. With gold currently trading well below its previous levels, the outlook remains uncertain, and further volatility could be expected as market participants react to evolving oil price movements.
