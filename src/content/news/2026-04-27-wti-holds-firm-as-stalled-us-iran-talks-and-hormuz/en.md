WTI Crude Oil held steady near $95.00 per barrel on Monday, as stalled US-Iran talks and ongoing disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz continued to underpin the market. Traders remain cautious, with geopolitical tensions keeping volatility elevated. The lack of progress in negotiations has fueled concerns over supply, pushing risk sentiment and impacting major currency pairs, including USD/EUR and USD/JPY, as investors adjust positions.  

Market participants noted that the stalled diplomacy adds premium to oil prices, supporting energy-linked currencies like the CAD and NOK. In forex trading, USD strength fluctuated against the euro and yen as commodity-linked flows shifted, reflecting broader market anxiety. Technical levels around $94.50 to $95.50 per barrel are now closely watched for potential breakouts, influencing cross-border trading strategies.  

Analysts warn that persistent disruptions in Hormuz could sustain elevated crude prices, affecting inflation expectations and central bank policy decisions. For forex traders, this translates into a watchful eye on commodity-sensitive exchange rates, with movements in WTI influencing USD liquidity and EUR/USD positioning. Volatility in both oil and major currency pairs could persist until a clear resolution emerges, keeping traders active across global markets.
