Gold (XAU/USD) remained under pressure on Wednesday, extending its downside move as traders stayed cautiously optimistic about ongoing US-Iran negotiations. Despite weaker Oil prices and a softer US Dollar (USD), the precious metal struggled to attract fresh buying interest. Market participants continue to monitor geopolitical developments closely, with hopes that diplomatic progress could eventually reduce tensions in the Middle East and limit demand for traditional safe-haven assets.

The decline in Gold prices also reflected improving risk sentiment across broader financial markets, as investors shifted toward higher-yielding assets and currency pair trading opportunities. A potential breakthrough between Washington and Tehran has encouraged expectations of steadier global energy supplies, weighing on defensive positions while keeping the USD exchange rate relatively stable against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY. Lower crude Oil prices further reduced inflation concerns, limiting support for bullion.

Meanwhile, forex trading activity remained cautious ahead of upcoming US economic data and Federal Reserve commentary, both of which could influence the direction of the USD and Treasury yields. Analysts noted that Gold may remain vulnerable in the near term if diplomatic optimism continues to strengthen, although lingering geopolitical uncertainty could still provide intermittent support for XAU/USD and other safe-haven assets.
