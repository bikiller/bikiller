The US Dollar Index (DXY) is hovering near the 98.00 level as markets digest news of renewed activity in key trading hubs. Safe-haven demand for the USD has softened, with traders shifting focus toward riskier assets amid easing tensions. However, uncertainty around the Strait of Hormuz continues to cap downside momentum, keeping the greenback supported despite recent softness in major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.  

Trading activity reflects cautious positioning, with the USD losing ground against some currencies while maintaining strength elsewhere. Analysts note that while the exchange rate shows tentative weakness, the potential for sudden geopolitical-driven spikes keeps market participants wary. Volatility is expected to persist, with forex investors monitoring oil-linked currencies and emerging market pairs closely.  

Looking ahead, the interplay between geopolitical developments and economic data will shape trading patterns. Currency pairs are likely to react to headlines on Hormuz and US economic indicators, with technical levels around DXY 98.00 serving as short-term reference points. For forex traders, maintaining flexible positions could be key as the market balances between risk appetite and safe-haven demand.
