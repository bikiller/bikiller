The USD/CHF currency pair extended its upward momentum on Wednesday, climbing for the second consecutive session. The exchange rate rose nearly 0.45% after finding support near the 100-day Simple Moving Average (SMA), pushing the pair slightly above the 0.7900 level. Traders noted that the US dollar’s rebound reflects renewed risk appetite and ongoing demand for safe-haven assets amid mixed economic signals from Europe and the US.  

Market participants are closely watching the 200-day SMA, which now looms as a potential resistance level for the USD/CHF pair. Strength in the US dollar against major peers, including the euro, has supported the Swiss franc’s limited downside. Analysts suggest that a sustained move above 0.7900 could signal further bullish trading, while a retreat toward the 100-day SMA may indicate short-term consolidation.  

The broader forex market shows the USD maintaining relative strength amid fluctuating risk sentiment. With investors parsing economic data and central bank signals, USD/CHF trading could remain volatile in the near term. Technical levels such as the 100-day and 200-day SMAs will likely guide intraday strategies, influencing both speculative and hedging positions as traders assess the US dollar’s next directional move.
