Gold (XAU/USD) remained under pressure on Wednesday, trading close to one-month lows as concerns over inflation driven by rising oil prices and escalating tensions between the US and Iran weighed on market sentiment. The precious metal is currently positioned around $1,967, a level not seen since late March, as investors await the Federal Reserve’s upcoming monetary policy announcement later today at 18:00 GMT. 

The heightened geopolitical risks, combined with oil's influence on inflation expectations, have reinforced the USD's strength, further dampening gold's appeal. With the Fed's decision looming, the market is on edge, assessing potential rate hikes or dovish signals that could impact the USD's performance. If the Fed takes a hawkish stance, further pressure on XAU/USD could follow, sending the currency pair lower as traders position themselves ahead of any changes in US monetary policy.
