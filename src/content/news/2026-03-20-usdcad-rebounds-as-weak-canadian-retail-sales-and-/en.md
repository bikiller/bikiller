USD/CAD saw a notable rebound on Friday, recovering from earlier intraday lows. The currency pair found support as weaker-than-expected Canadian Retail Sales data put pressure on the Canadian Dollar (CAD). Sales in Canada increased by just 0.1% in January, missing expectations of a 0.3% rise, which raised concerns over the country’s economic growth outlook. 

At the same time, a broad recovery in the US Dollar (USD) added further strength to the USD/CAD exchange rate. As the USD saw broad-based gains, the pair surged back above 1.3700, with the greenback benefiting from firmer US economic data and rising treasury yields. This shift in market sentiment leaves USD/CAD poised to test resistance levels around 1.3750 in the coming sessions.
