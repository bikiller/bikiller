Gold prices surged nearly 2% on Wednesday, with the XAU/USD trading at $4,556, as investor sentiment was boosted by reports of potential US-Iran talks aimed at resolving the ongoing conflict. This surge came amid falling US Treasury yields, which typically signal reduced risk appetite and a preference for safe-haven assets like gold. The market's attention remains on the possibility of diplomatic progress between the US and Iran.

In the broader commodities market, oil futures experienced a significant decline, driven by growing speculation that negotiations could de-escalate tensions in the Middle East. The movement in gold reflects heightened demand for protective assets, as traders adjust their portfolios in response to geopolitical concerns and shifting US economic signals. As the gold market continues to benefit from these developments, traders are closely monitoring any updates on the talks and the potential impact on the USD.
