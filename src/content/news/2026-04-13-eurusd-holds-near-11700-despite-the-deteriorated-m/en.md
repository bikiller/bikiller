The EUR/USD currency pair edged lower from last week’s highs near 1.1740 on Monday, testing support in the upper 1.1600s amid softening market sentiment. Traders note the euro is holding around 1.1685 at the time of writing, having found early support at 1.1670. Despite broader risk aversion pressuring the market, the exchange rate shows resilience, suggesting a cautious but steady trading environment.  

Analysts point to lingering uncertainty in global markets as a key factor behind the EUR’s pullback against the USD. The retreat from 1.1740 highlights profit-taking and reduced appetite for euro exposure, while the USD remains firm amid safe-haven demand. Short-term technical levels, including the 1.1670 support, are closely watched by traders seeking entry points or gauging potential breakout momentum.  

Market participants are weighing the euro’s near-term outlook against U.S. economic data and central bank signals. Continued trading near 1.1685 indicates that the currency pair may consolidate before the next directional move, with sentiment-driven swings likely to influence intraday price action. Investors remain alert to key resistance around last week’s highs, which could determine whether EUR/USD resumes an upward trend or faces further downside.
