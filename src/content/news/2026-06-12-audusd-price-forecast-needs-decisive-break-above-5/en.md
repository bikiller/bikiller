AUD/USD weakened during early European trading on Friday, slipping 0.22% to around 0.7035 as traders locked in recent gains and reassessed near-term risk sentiment. The currency pair remains under close watch after approaching a key technical barrier, with market participants evaluating whether the Australian dollar can sustain its recovery against the USD amid shifting global market conditions.

From a technical perspective, the exchange rate continues to face resistance near the 50% Fibonacci retracement level at 0.7050. Analysts suggest that a decisive break above this threshold is needed to confirm additional upside momentum. Until then, trading activity may remain cautious, with buyers reluctant to push the pair significantly higher without stronger confirmation from price action and broader market drivers.

The 0.7050 region is therefore emerging as a pivotal level for AUD/USD. A successful move above it could encourage further bullish positioning and potentially open the door to additional gains, while failure to clear resistance may trigger renewed selling pressure. Currency markets, including EUR and USD crosses, are likely to remain sensitive to risk appetite, economic data releases, and central bank expectations, all of which could influence the pair’s next directional move.
