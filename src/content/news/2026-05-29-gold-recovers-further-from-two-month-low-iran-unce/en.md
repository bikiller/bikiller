Gold (XAU/USD) regained traction on Friday, climbing away from a two-month low touched on March 27. Trading saw the precious metal attract buyers for a second consecutive day, with prices stabilizing as uncertainty around Iran’s geopolitical stance and expectations of future Fed moves shaped market sentiment. The USD’s fluctuations against major currency pairs like EUR/USD continued to influence gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset.

Traders noted that gold’s recovery remained capped despite increased buying interest. Investors are weighing ongoing Middle East tensions and the prospect of a more hawkish Federal Reserve, keeping exchange rates volatile. Spot gold traded cautiously above $1,980 per ounce, while the USD index (DXY) held firm, supporting the currency pair’s mixed trading dynamics across global markets.

Analysts suggest that short-term gold movements will likely hinge on currency pair volatility and macroeconomic indicators. Strength in the USD tends to limit upside in XAU/USD, even as geopolitical risk sparks intermittent rallies. Market participants continue monitoring Fed commentary, USD trading trends, and oil-linked geopolitical developments, anticipating potential triggers for renewed buying momentum or a reversal back toward recent lows.
