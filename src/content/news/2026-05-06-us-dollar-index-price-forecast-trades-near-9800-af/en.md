The US Dollar Index (DXY) is hovering near 98.00 during Wednesday’s European session, retreating after three consecutive days of gains. Traders are eyeing the nine-day exponential moving average, which recently capped USD strength, as the index adjusts amid mixed sentiment in global currency markets. The pullback suggests caution among investors after the USD rallied against major counterparts including the EUR, JPY, and GBP.  

In currency pair trading, the USD’s recent strength against the euro has eased slightly, with the EUR/USD exchange rate seeing modest recovery from earlier lows. Analysts note that technical resistance around the short-term EMA is influencing market positioning, while broader risk appetite and data releases continue to sway trading flows. The dollar’s movement reflects ongoing recalibration among investors balancing economic indicators and central bank expectations.  

Market participants remain attentive to potential catalysts that could reignite USD momentum. A sustained breach above the nine-day EMA could signal renewed strength across key currency pairs, while failure to hold near 98.00 may allow the euro and other major currencies to regain ground. Traders are monitoring both technical levels and macroeconomic factors to gauge the short-term trajectory of the US dollar in the global forex landscape.
