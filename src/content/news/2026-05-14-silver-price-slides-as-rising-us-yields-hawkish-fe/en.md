Silver dipped sharply on Thursday, slipping 3.20% to trade around $84.70 as investors booked profits following its recent rally. The XAG/USD currency pair faced downward pressure amid rising U.S. Treasury yields, which boosted the appeal of the dollar versus precious metals. Traders cited a combination of technical resistance and profit-taking as key drivers behind the pullback in the silver exchange rate.  

Market participants are weighing hawkish remarks from the Federal Reserve, which have reinforced expectations of continued rate hikes. A stronger USD generally dampens demand for silver and other commodities priced in dollars, as higher yields make holding non-yielding assets less attractive. EUR/USD and other major currency pairs are also reflecting broader dollar strength, influencing cross-asset flows in trading desks globally.  

Analysts note that the near-term outlook for silver may remain volatile, with the $84.70 level acting as a key support to watch. Any rebound will likely depend on shifts in Fed guidance or unexpected swings in U.S. bond yields, while broader market sentiment continues to favor USD liquidity. Traders are adjusting positions, mindful of potential swings in both forex and commodities markets in response to macroeconomic cues.
