The US Dollar regained momentum on Wednesday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) trading firmly above the 100.00 mark after fresh inflation figures pointed to persistent price pressures in the United States. The latest data showed headline Consumer Price Index (CPI) growth accelerated to 4.2% year-over-year in May from 3.8% previously, while monthly inflation increased by 0.5%, reinforcing expectations that the Federal Reserve may maintain a restrictive policy stance.

The stronger inflation reading supported the USD across the forex market and boosted demand for the greenback during trading sessions. Currency pair movements reflected renewed confidence in the US currency, with investors closely monitoring exchange rate fluctuations against major counterparts such as the EUR and other G10 currencies. The rebound in the dollar came after recent periods of softer sentiment and renewed speculation over the timing of potential interest rate adjustments.

Market participants viewed the inflation figures as a sign that underlying price pressures remain elevated, strengthening the case for higher-for-longer borrowing costs.
