The Swiss Franc (CHF) extended losses against major currencies on Thursday, trading lower against both the Euro (EUR) and the US Dollar (USD) as the American session neared its close. The EUR/CHF pair climbed steadily, reflecting broad demand for the euro, while the USD/CHF exchange rate edged higher, underscoring the franc’s defensive struggles amid heightened market activity. Traders noted technical levels around 0.9870 for USD/CHF and 1.0580 for EUR/CHF as key short-term reference points.  

Market participants cited ongoing geopolitical tensions and risk-off sentiment as factors failing to support the traditionally safe-haven franc. Despite global uncertainties, investor appetite for higher-yielding currencies has pressured the CHF, pushing trading volumes higher in the European and US sessions. Analysts highlighted that the franc’s underperformance could signal further short-term corrections, especially if the EUR and USD maintain strength against the broader basket of currencies.  

Looking ahead, the CHF faces potential headwinds if current trends persist, with technical charts showing limited immediate support below 0.9850 against the USD. For EUR/CHF, a sustained move above 1.0600 could accelerate gains. Traders remain cautious, monitoring geopolitical developments and central bank signals that could sway exchange rates. The franc’s performance continues to be a barometer of risk sentiment, shaping positioning strategies across currency markets.
