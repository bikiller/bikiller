Crude oil prices surged on Friday as tensions in the Middle East escalated, pushing WTI above $85 per barrel. The geopolitical uncertainty fueled a sharp risk-off sentiment, prompting traders to move into safe-haven assets. The dollar index (DXY) gained 0.3%, strengthening against major currencies, while the euro fell to 1.0780, reflecting heightened caution in forex markets. Analysts note that oil-driven inflation pressures could influence central bank decisions, affecting USD liquidity and trading strategies globally.  

Energy-linked currencies also reacted to the spike in crude. The Canadian dollar climbed against the USD, with USD/CAD retreating to 1.3150, as traders priced in higher oil revenue. Conversely, oil-importing nations saw their exchange rates weaken, with USD/JPY briefly touching 136.50. Market participants are closely watching technical levels, noting resistance near $86 for WTI and key support zones for major currency pairs that may guide trading activity in the coming sessions.  

The surge in oil and subsequent currency movements signal broader implications for global markets. Investors are reassessing risk exposure, with emerging markets facing potential capital outflows as the USD strengthens. Traders remain focused on geopolitical developments, with any escalation likely to drive further volatility in both energy and forex markets. Positioning in EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and USD/JPY is expected to remain sensitive to oil prices and Middle East newsflow over the near term.
