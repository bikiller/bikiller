The USD/CAD currency pair climbed to the 1.3930 area on Friday, as the US Dollar gained traction following a stronger-than-expected Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report. The data highlighted robust US job creation, reinforcing expectations of ongoing Fed tightening. Traders reacted quickly, pushing the exchange rate higher despite Canadian labor market figures that also exceeded forecasts.  

Canada’s latest employment report showed solid job growth, yet the Canadian Dollar struggled to maintain strength against the surging USD. The discrepancy suggests investors are weighing US monetary policy more heavily, keeping the CAD under pressure in forex trading. Market participants noted that while domestic fundamentals support the loonie, cross-border capital flows favored the greenback.  

Technically, USD/CAD remains near key resistance at 1.3930, with potential for further upside if US data momentum continues. Analysts suggest that traders monitor upcoming Canadian CPI and US economic indicators, as these could influence short-term volatility. For now, the market sentiment favors the USD, with the exchange rate reflecting a cautious approach toward Canadian monetary resilience.
