Deutsche Bank analysts expect the Federal Reserve to hold interest rates steady at this week's meeting, citing growing geopolitical uncertainties and the persistent threat of oil-driven inflation. These concerns are likely to weigh on the Fed’s decision, as it navigates the potential risks to economic stability. As oil prices continue to climb, the impact on inflation could complicate the Fed's ability to make further rate adjustments.

In the forex market, the USD/EUR currency pair will remain sensitive to any Fed-related developments, with the exchange rate likely to fluctuate within the 1.0800 to 1.0900 range. The central bank's stance on rates is pivotal in determining short-term market movements, especially for USD traders. As global uncertainties persist, traders will be closely monitoring the Fed’s actions for signals of future policy adjustments, which could significantly impact the USD’s strength across multiple currency pairs.
