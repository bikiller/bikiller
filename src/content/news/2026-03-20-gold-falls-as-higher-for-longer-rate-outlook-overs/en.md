Gold prices (XAU/USD) continued their downward trend on Friday, extending losses after a sharp two-day decline. The precious metal fell to its lowest level since early February, hovering just above the $4,500 mark. Despite ongoing tensions in the Middle East, the market remains focused on the "higher-for-longer" rate outlook from central banks, which continues to overshadow geopolitical concerns.

The strength of the USD, bolstered by expectations of prolonged higher interest rates, has been a major factor in gold's struggles. With rising Treasury yields and a robust USD exchange rate, traders have been cautious in their positioning, further pressuring XAU/USD. As a result, the market now watches closely for any changes in central bank rhetoric that could shift sentiment.
