Sterling came under pressure as expectations grow that the Bank of England will hold interest rates steady. Societe Generale’s Kenneth Broux anticipates no change in the policy rate but highlighted upside risks to inflation, suggesting markets may have to adjust quickly if price pressures intensify. The GBP/USD currency pair edged lower, reflecting cautious trading sentiment ahead of the BoE’s decision.  

Traders are weighing the potential impact on exchange rates as markets have begun pricing in a future rate hike. The GBP/EUR exchange rate also showed modest declines, signaling increased caution among investors. With BoE guidance expected to remain neutral, short-term trading activity in both USD and EUR pairs is likely to remain sensitive to inflation updates.  

Analysts note that while immediate monetary policy may stay on hold, sterling’s volatility could rise if inflation surprises on the upside. Currency traders are monitoring technical support and resistance levels, keeping GBP trading in focus relative to major currencies. Overall, the combination of a steady BoE outlook and looming inflation risks is shaping cautious sentiment across forex markets.
