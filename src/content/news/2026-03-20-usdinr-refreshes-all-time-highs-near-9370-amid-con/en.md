The USD/INR currency pair surged to fresh all-time highs near 93.70 on Friday, extending the Indian Rupee’s recent slide against the US Dollar. Trading activity resumed after Thursday’s holiday, with consistent selling by foreign institutional investors (FIIs) weighing heavily on the rupee. Market participants noted strong demand for the USD amid cautious domestic sentiment, keeping the INR under sustained pressure throughout the session.  

The rupee’s weakness comes despite steady inflows in other asset classes, reflecting broader concerns over global liquidity and rising US interest rates. Traders highlighted that persistent FII outflows are exacerbating the rupee’s depreciation, while technical analysts observed the 93.50–93.70 zone as a critical resistance level for the USD/INR. Momentum in trading favored the greenback, with the pair showing limited retracement despite minor corrective moves.  

Analysts suggest that if the USD/INR breaks decisively above 93.70, it could open the path toward 94, reinforcing the trend of rupee vulnerability in the near term. Currency traders are also keeping an eye on the euro and other major currencies, as global risk sentiment and central bank policies continue to influence emerging market exchange rates. With FIIs likely to maintain cautious positioning, INR trading could remain pressured in the coming sessions.
