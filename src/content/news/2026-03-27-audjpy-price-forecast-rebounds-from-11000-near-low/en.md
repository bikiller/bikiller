AUD/JPY edged higher on Friday, climbing to around 110.20 during European trading hours after four consecutive days of losses. The rebound comes as the currency pair tests support near the lower boundary of a descending channel on the daily chart, suggesting that technical traders remain cautious. Market participants are watching closely as the exchange rate hovers around this key level, with potential implications for near-term momentum.  

The broader context shows a persistent bearish bias for AUD/JPY, as the pair continues its downward trajectory within the channel. Analysts note that any sustained break below 110.00 could accelerate selling pressure, while a recovery above 110.50 may offer short-term relief. Trading volumes have been moderate, reflecting a cautious stance amid mixed signals from USD and EUR pairs, which continue to influence risk sentiment.  

Investors are monitoring global risk factors, including commodity prices and central bank signals, which impact the Australian dollar. The pair’s movements against the yen also reflect shifts in safe-haven flows, with traders balancing positions across USD/JPY and EUR/JPY. Technical setups suggest the market remains range-bound for now, but volatility could rise if key support or resistance levels are breached.
