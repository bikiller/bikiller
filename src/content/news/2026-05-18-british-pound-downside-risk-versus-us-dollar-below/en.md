The British Pound is showing signs of vulnerability against the US Dollar, with downside risk noted below its 200-day moving average, according to Societe Generale analysts. Persistent inflation and wage pressures have slowed the Bank of England’s rate-cut trajectory, weighing on long-end Gilts and adding pressure on the GBP/USD currency pair. Traders are closely watching exchange rate movements as BoE policy signals influence market positioning.  

Market participants see the slowdown in rate reductions as a key driver behind weaker sterling trading. Gilts, particularly those with longer maturities, are underperforming, reflecting investor caution amid inflation-driven uncertainties. The currency pair’s technical levels suggest that a breach of the 200-DMA could trigger further selling, amplifying volatility against the USD and EUR.  

Looking ahead, economists highlight that BoE responses to wage and inflation dynamics will be pivotal for both bond and currency markets. Sterling’s performance against major currencies, including the USD, is expected to remain sensitive to policy guidance, while traders weigh potential shifts in monetary policy against broader market sentiment. The next moves in exchange rates could hinge on BoE communications and macroeconomic updates, keeping GBP trading under scrutiny.
