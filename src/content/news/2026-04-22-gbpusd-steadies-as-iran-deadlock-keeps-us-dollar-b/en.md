GBP/USD remained steady on Wednesday, trading near the 1.2850 level as geopolitical tensions linger over stalled US-Iran negotiations. The persistent deadlock has kept the US Dollar under moderate pressure, preventing sharp moves despite its safe-haven appeal. Traders remained cautious, balancing the Greenback’s support with the Pound’s sensitivity to recent UK inflation data, which highlighted lingering energy-driven price pressures.  

In the absence of major US economic releases, market focus shifted toward the UK, where consumer price inflation data reflected the ongoing impact of energy shocks. Analysts note that the exchange rate is holding just above key technical support around 1.2830, suggesting limited downside for the currency pair unless geopolitical risks intensify. Trading volumes in EUR/USD and other major pairs echoed a similar cautious tone, as investors weigh global risk sentiment against central bank outlooks.  

Market participants are eyeing the potential for renewed US-Iran dialogue, which could influence the USD broadly. Any breakthrough may lift the Greenback, putting pressure on GBP/USD, while continued stalemate could support a higher Pound. Technical analysts highlight resistance near 1.2900, with trading strategies likely to focus on short-term momentum and reaction to geopolitical developments rather than domestic fundamentals alone.
