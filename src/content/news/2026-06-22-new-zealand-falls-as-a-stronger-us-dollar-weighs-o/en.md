NZD/USD slipped on Monday, trading lower as the New Zealand dollar weakened against a firmer US dollar. The currency pair hovered near the 0.5720 level, marking its proximity to a two-month low. Selling pressure dominated early-week trading, with the exchange rate reflecting broad USD demand across the forex market.

The decline was largely driven by renewed US dollar strength and cautious risk sentiment in global markets. Risk-sensitive currencies such as the NZD struggled as investors rotated toward safer assets. Forex trading flows favored the USD, weighing on commodity-linked currencies. The broader exchange rate environment also saw pressure across similar pairs, while EUR/USD and other majors remained sensitive to shifting risk appetite.

Looking ahead, traders will monitor whether NZD/USD can stabilize above the 0.5720 area or extend losses toward deeper support levels. Continued USD strength could keep pressure on the currency pair, influencing short-term forex positioning. Market participants will also watch macro data and global risk trends for direction, as volatility in major exchange rates remains elevated across trading sessions.
