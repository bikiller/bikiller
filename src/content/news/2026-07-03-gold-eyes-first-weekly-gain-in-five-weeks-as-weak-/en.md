Gold (XAU/USD) extends its upward momentum on Friday, positioning the currency pair for its first weekly gain in five weeks. The exchange rate benefits from renewed buying interest as traders reassess USD strength following recent macroeconomic signals. In forex trading, the move reflects shifting sentiment across major pairs as dollar weakness supports precious metals broadly.

Weak US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data released on Thursday weighed heavily on the US Dollar, triggering a broad currency reaction in forex markets. The disappointing labour figures reduced expectations of an imminent Federal Reserve interest rate hike, prompting traders to adjust USD positions across major currency pairs including EUR/USD and other dollar crosses.

Looking ahead, gold remains supported as traders reassess US monetary policy outlook, with continued USD softness likely to influence short-term exchange rate dynamics. In forex trading, volatility across EUR/USD and other major currency pairs is expected to persist, while gold’s safe-haven appeal may stay elevated if expectations of Fed tightening continue to ease.
