West Texas Intermediate (WTI) Oil surged on Monday, trading around $94.70, reflecting a robust increase of 3.18% after starting the week with a notable bullish gap. The rally in oil prices was significantly driven by former President Donald Trump's rejection of the proposed Iran peace plan, which raised concerns over potential supply disruptions in the region.

Adding to the bullish sentiment, fears of a possible closure of the Strait of Hormuz have emerged, heightening tensions in the oil market. This closure would severely impact global oil supply routes, intensifying the ongoing volatility in energy prices. As trading continues, the implications for related currency pairs, particularly the USD against major currencies like the EUR, could be significant, reflecting shifts in market confidence and geopolitical stability.
