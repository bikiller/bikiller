Gold (XAU/USD) has rebounded about 5%, as OCBC strategist Christopher Wong said US-Iran de-escalation eased oil-led inflation pressures and reduced rate-shock fears in global trading markets. The move supported risk sentiment and helped stabilize USD dynamics, with the exchange rate impact spilling into precious metals demand across forex-linked positioning.

However, Wong warns that upside momentum may slow ahead of the FOMC meeting, as traders reassess the USD interest rate path. Further gains in gold depend on whether Federal Reserve hawkish repricing has peaked, alongside stabilizing US Treasury yields. In currency markets, shifts in USD strength continue to cap aggressive XAU/USD upside trading moves.

Looking ahead, sustained recovery in gold requires softer oil prices, lower yields, and clearer easing in inflation expectations. If oil-driven inflation pressures return, the USD could regain strength, pressuring precious metals. For forex traders, the balance between USD, EUR risk sentiment, and rate expectations will determine whether gold extends gains or consolidates near recent levels.
