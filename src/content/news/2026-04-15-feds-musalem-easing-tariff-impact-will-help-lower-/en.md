Alberto Musalem, President of the Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis, highlighted on Wednesday that the recent oil shock triggered by the Middle East conflict is exerting upward pressure on core inflation. He noted that, despite these supply-side disruptions, core inflation is expected to hover around 3% through the year. Traders are watching closely, as these inflation signals can influence USD sentiment and currency pair movements, particularly against the EUR and other major currencies.  

Musalem emphasized that easing the impact of tariffs could help mitigate inflationary pressures. In the forex market, such developments are driving active trading in USD/EUR and USD/JPY, with exchange rates reflecting expectations of central bank responses. Investors are weighing the balance between energy-driven price surges and potential policy easing, which could steer USD strength or weakness in the coming sessions.  

Market participants are closely monitoring commodity-linked currencies and safe-haven flows as geopolitical tensions and trade policies intersect. With oil-driven inflation persisting, USD trading shows heightened sensitivity, while the EUR and other currencies adjust to these underlying macro pressures. Analysts suggest that upcoming data releases and tariff adjustments will likely shape exchange rate dynamics and trading strategies through the remainder of the year.
