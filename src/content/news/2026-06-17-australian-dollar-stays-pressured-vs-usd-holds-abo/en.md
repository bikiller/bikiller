The AUD/USD currency pair remains under pressure against the USD, holding just above the 0.7050 exchange rate level on Wednesday. Despite a previous upward bounce driven by hawkish Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) signals, the pair has failed to extend gains and continues trading with a negative bias for a second consecutive session as sellers dominate short-term flows.

Market activity is largely shaped by US dollar strength and cautious positioning ahead of the upcoming Federal Reserve decision, keeping risk appetite limited. Traders are reducing exposure to higher-beta currencies like the Australian dollar, while monitoring global yields and USD demand trends. The exchange rate remains sensitive to macro signals from the US central bank outlook.

Attention now turns to the Federal Reserve outcome, which is expected to drive near-term volatility in the AUD/USD exchange rate. The 0.7050 level acts as a key short-term support zone, with a break potentially exposing further downside pressure, while any dovish surprise could trigger a corrective rebound in the currency pair and broader forex markets.
