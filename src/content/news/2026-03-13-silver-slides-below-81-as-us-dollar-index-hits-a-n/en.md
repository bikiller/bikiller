Silver (XAG/USD) continued its downward streak on Friday, slipping below the $81 mark as the metal posted losses for the third consecutive day, down more than 2.90%. The decline comes amid a firming US Dollar, with the US Dollar Index approaching a nearly four-month high, putting pressure on dollar-denominated commodities. Traders noted that the stronger USD is weighing on silver’s appeal as an alternative investment.  

Oil markets showed signs of recovery, even after US President Donald Trump temporarily lifted sanctions on Russian crude for 30 days. The rebound in oil prices added a layer of complexity, with energy markets reacting to geopolitical developments, while safe-haven flows favored the dollar. The EUR/USD and other major currency pairs saw modest adjustments as traders balanced risk sentiment with currency strength.  

Market analysts suggest that continued USD strength could cap silver’s upside in the near term, with technical support near $80.50 attracting short-term interest. Exchange rate fluctuations across currency pairs remain a key driver for metals trading, and any further shifts in US monetary policy or geopolitical headlines could amplify volatility in XAG/USD and other commodities. Traders are closely monitoring positions amid this mix of currency pressure and commodity market dynamics.
