EUR/USD currency pair struggled to sustain its latest advance as upside momentum against the US Dollar lost traction near recent highs, according to UOB strategist Quek Ser Leang. The exchange rate briefly spiked to 1.1472 but failed to extend gains, suggesting that resistance levels are capping further upside bias in the near-term trading environment.  

Market participants noted that the currency pair remains constrained after the rejection at 1.1472, reinforcing a cautious tone in EUR/USD trading against the USD. With buying pressure fading at higher levels, short-term sentiment indicates limited upside follow-through unless a stronger catalyst emerges from broader US Dollar dynamics. Traders continue to monitor key resistance zones and intraday ranges for signals of direction.  

Looking ahead, analysts expect EUR/USD to trade in a narrow consolidation range of 1.1410 to 1.1455 on an intraday basis as the market digests recent moves. The euro’s upside bias remains limited against the US Dollar, with resistance at 1.1472 continuing to act as a key technical ceiling for short-term trading strategies.
