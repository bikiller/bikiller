The United Kingdom’s economy showed surprising resilience in February, with GDP growth far exceeding market expectations. TD Securities highlights broad-based strength across services, production, and construction sectors, signaling that domestic activity remains robust despite lingering inflationary pressures. The data adds complexity for the Bank of England’s Monetary Policy Committee (MPC), which has signaled caution in adjusting interest rates amid persistent global uncertainty.  

The stronger-than-expected GDP has sparked renewed trading interest in the GBP, pushing the pound higher against major counterparts like the USD and EUR. Analysts note that the GBP/USD currency pair has tested key resistance near 1.2850, while GBP/EUR remains under pressure from the euro’s broader stability. Market participants are weighing whether the MPC will maintain its measured approach or shift more aggressively to tame inflation, creating potential volatility in near-term forex trading.  

Investors are watching for technical levels in the FX market, with the pound’s momentum suggesting room for short-term gains if confidence in UK growth persists. Traders are also factoring in US economic signals, which could influence the USD and cross-currency moves. Overall, the combination of robust domestic growth and central bank caution sets the stage for active positioning in GBP pairs, with swings likely as markets digest further economic releases and policy commentary.
