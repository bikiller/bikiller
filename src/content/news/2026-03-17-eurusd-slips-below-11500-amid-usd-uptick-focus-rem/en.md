The EUR/USD currency pair dipped below the 1.1500 mark on Tuesday, as a firmer USD weighed on the euro. The pair failed to build on Monday’s modest recovery from the 1.1415-1.1410 range, near its lowest level since July 2025. Early Asian session trading saw the euro gradually losing ground, reflecting cautious sentiment ahead of key central bank meetings.  

Market participants attributed the USD strength to safe-haven demand and expectations of tighter monetary policy, putting pressure on the EUR/USD exchange rate. Technical support around 1.1410 remains in focus, while resistance near 1.1500 could cap any short-term euro rebound. Traders continue to monitor flows as volatility persists in response to global economic cues.  

Analysts note that the euro’s struggle to reclaim 1.1500 signals a cautious trading environment, with central bank decisions likely to drive the next leg of movement. Any hawkish signals from the Federal Reserve or dovish hints from the ECB could significantly sway the EUR/USD trajectory. Investors are watching for decisive moves, as the pair remains sensitive to shifts in interest rate expectations and broader USD trends.
