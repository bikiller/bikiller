Silver prices showed a modest recovery after two consecutive bearish sessions, rebounding slightly but failing to break above the $70 mark. The metal’s downside risks remain intact as market focus shifts toward the ongoing tensions in the Middle East. These geopolitical concerns have lessened silver’s appeal as a safe-haven asset, pushing traders to favor the US Dollar (USD) instead, which has strengthened amid global uncertainty.

The shift in market sentiment has kept silver trading under pressure, with resistance seen near $70. The price is currently trading well below this level, with significant downside risks in the near term. Currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY have reflected this risk-off sentiment, as the USD rises against major currencies. Analysts are watching for any signs of further geopolitical developments that could lead to volatility in precious metals and impact their future direction.
