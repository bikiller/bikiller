Oil prices have stabilized after recent losses, with West Texas Intermediate (WTI) trading around $88.20 on Wednesday, marking a 0.40% daily gain. The recovery comes as easing tensions from US-Iran negotiations help to alleviate concerns over potential supply disruptions. The market had previously reacted to fears of restricted supply, but the ongoing talks have calmed investor sentiment, leading to a modest rebound.

Meanwhile, rising inventories have further supported the stability in oil prices, signaling a balance in supply-demand dynamics. As WTI moves back above the $88 mark, traders are closely monitoring developments in both geopolitical negotiations and inventory reports. The stability in crude oil prices could have indirect implications for major currency pairs, including the USD/EUR, as oil prices influence broader market sentiment and inflation expectations.
