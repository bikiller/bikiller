The EUR/USD currency pair is trading in a narrow range around 1.1600 during Wednesday’s European session, reflecting subdued momentum in the exchange rate. Price action remains capped near the 200-day EMA, signaling a lack of directional conviction. Traders are holding back as global risk sentiment stays fragile, limiting both EUR and USD flows in the forex market.

Market participants are closely monitoring geopolitical developments, particularly Iran’s expected response to US President Donald Trump’s proposed month-long ceasefire and 15-point settlement plan. This uncertainty is dampening trading activity across major currency pairs, with the USD seeing mixed demand as a safe-haven while the EUR struggles to gain traction amid cautious positioning.

On the macro front, the European Central Bank has reiterated concerns about persistently high inflation, reinforcing expectations that monetary policy could remain restrictive. This outlook offers some support to the EUR but is offset by broader market indecision. As a result, the EUR/USD exchange rate continues to consolidate, with traders watching for a decisive break above or below the 200-day EMA for clearer direction.
