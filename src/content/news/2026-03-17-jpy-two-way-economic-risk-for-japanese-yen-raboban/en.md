The Japanese Yen (JPY) faces a two-way economic risk, with upcoming central bank meetings and potential policy shifts by the Bank of Japan (BoJ) influencing its outlook. Rabobank's Senior FX Strategist Jane Foley highlights BoJ Governor Ueda’s hawkish stance, despite higher energy costs, signaling continued tightening expectations. As markets await new developments, the USD/JPY currency pair remains volatile, with traders eyeing any policy changes or economic data that could prompt further fluctuations.

The BoJ’s approach contrasts with global G10 central banks, creating a delicate balance for the Yen. The market anticipates potential shifts in the BoJ's policy, as tightening measures may impact the JPY’s exchange rate against major currencies like the EUR and USD. As the trading week unfolds, investors will closely monitor Governor Ueda's comments for indications on future direction, especially with energy prices influencing inflation and growth. These dynamics are expected to drive JPY's movement in the short term.
