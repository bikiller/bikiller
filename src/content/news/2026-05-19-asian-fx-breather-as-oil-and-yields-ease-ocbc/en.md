Asian currencies are showing a mild rebound after a period of volatility driven by rising oil prices and U.S. yields. OCBC’s FX strategist Christopher Wong notes that softer Brent crude and a pause in U.S. Treasury yield gains have helped stabilise key currency pairs. The USD/JPY is trading cautiously around 145.20, while the AUD/USD has edged higher to 0.6680, reflecting a slight recovery in risk appetite.  

The EUR/USD remains under pressure near 1.1030, as traders weigh lingering economic uncertainties against U.S. dollar strength. Asian FX markets are digesting the impact of a sell-off in energy-linked currencies, with emerging market pairs like the SGD/USD hovering around 1.3485. Wong points out that technical support levels are holding for several major pairs, suggesting a pause rather than a reversal in broader trends.  

Market participants are monitoring the interplay between commodity prices, Treasury yields, and central bank signals. Any renewed movement in Brent above $85 or U.S. yields rising past 4.2% could reignite pressure on the regional currencies. For now, trading activity is subdued, offering a breather for investors positioning around USD, EUR, JPY, and other major Asian currency pairs. The cautious mood underscores sensitivity to both macroeconomic data and global risk sentiment.
