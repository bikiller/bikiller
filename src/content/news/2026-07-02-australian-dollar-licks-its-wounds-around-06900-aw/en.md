The Australian Dollar (AUD) traded little changed against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, with the AUD/USD currency pair hovering around the 0.6900 area as investors waited for the upcoming US Nonfarm Payrolls report. The exchange rate continued to stabilize after the Australian currency lost nearly 4% during June, reflecting cautious trading conditions and limited conviction ahead of a major catalyst for global foreign exchange markets.

Throughout the session, the AUD/USD pair remained confined to a broad 65-pip range, highlighting the market’s reluctance to establish fresh directional positions. Sellers repeatedly pushed the currency pair toward support at 0.6865, but downside attempts failed to gain traction. At the same time, buyers struggled to lift prices beyond resistance below 0.6930, keeping the exchange rate trapped within well-defined technical boundaries as volatility stayed relatively subdued.

Market participants are now focused on the US labor market data, which could influence expectations for Federal Reserve policy and drive fresh movement in the USD across major currency pairs, including AUD/USD and EUR/USD. A stronger-than-expected payrolls release could reinforce demand for the US Dollar, while weaker figures may encourage a rebound in the Australian Dollar. Until then, trading is likely to remain range-bound as investors monitor key technical levels and await clearer market direction.
