Euro rebounds on Wednesday after appearing to bottom out below 1.1600, with EUR/USD finding renewed buying interest amid shifting sentiment in currency markets. Growing speculation of a potential US-Iran deal has softened demand for the US Dollar, while a hawkish Federal Reserve outlook from the April monetary policy meeting continues to shape broader forex positioning. The currency pair is regaining traction after recent pressure.

At the time of writing, the EUR/USD exchange rate is trading at 1.1622, up 0.19%, reflecting a modest rebound in the currency pair as the US Dollar remains under pressure. Traders are closely monitoring geopolitical developments and Federal Reserve policy signals, which continue to influence short-term direction. The forex market shows cautious optimism as EUR edges higher against USD in early Wednesday trading.

Market sentiment remains sensitive to both geopolitical headlines and central bank expectations, with the EUR/USD currency pair likely to react to any further progress in US-Iran discussions. A sustained dovish shift in US Dollar sentiment could provide additional support for the euro, especially if the Federal Reserve maintains a cautious stance. For now, traders remain focused on macro drivers shaping forex volatility.
