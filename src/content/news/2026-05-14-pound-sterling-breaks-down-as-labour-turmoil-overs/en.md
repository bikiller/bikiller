Pound Sterling slipped sharply against the US dollar on Thursday, with the GBP/USD currency pair dropping 0.9% to test lows near 1.3395. The exchange rate broke below the key 1.3500 level, extending a multi-week decline from its early-March peak. Trading saw a steady downward staircase pattern through the European session, reflecting increasing bearish momentum despite the UK’s recent GDP strength.  

Investors attributed the decline to ongoing Labour Party turmoil, which overshadowed stronger economic data. Market participants highlighted that political uncertainty weighed heavily on Sterling, curbing investor confidence and fueling selling pressure against the USD. The daily candle closed near session lows, signaling sustained weakness and caution among forex traders.  

Technically, the pair’s drop below 1.3500 suggests potential continuation of the downtrend, with further support levels around 1.3350 likely to attract attention. Analysts note that while the UK economy shows resilience, political instability could keep the pound under pressure in coming sessions. USD strength versus EUR and other major currencies adds an additional headwind for Sterling in global trading markets.
