EUR positioning is showing signs of caution as traders adjust to rising inflation concerns, according to Rabobank strategists Molly Schwartz and Jane Foley. Net long positions on the euro have dropped for the third week in a row, reflecting an uptick in short bets. The shift highlights growing market sensitivity to potential ECB policy tightening and the impact on the EUR/USD exchange rate.  

Trading activity suggests that investors are recalibrating risk, with increased short interest putting downward pressure on the euro against the dollar. The adjustment comes amid persistent inflationary pressures across the eurozone, which could influence upcoming central bank decisions and market sentiment. Technical levels near recent support points are under closer scrutiny as the currency pair tests investor confidence.  

The evolving positioning signals that EUR/USD could face heightened volatility in the near term, with traders monitoring inflation data and policy statements closely. While the euro remains supported by fundamental factors, the rise in bearish bets underscores the market’s cautious stance, suggesting that short-term trading strategies may need to account for wider swings in exchange rates and momentum shifts.
