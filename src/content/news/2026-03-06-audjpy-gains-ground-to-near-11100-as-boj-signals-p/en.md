The AUD/JPY currency pair edged higher toward the 111.00 mark on Friday, trading around 110.90 in the early European session. The Australian Dollar gained ground as the Japanese Yen softened, with traders digesting signals from the Bank of Japan suggesting a prolonged hold on interest rates. Market participants appear cautious, weighing the implications for both risk sentiment and broader FX flows.  

The softer JPY pushed AUD/JPY into a technically notable zone, attracting momentum-driven buyers targeting a break above 111.00. Analysts note that with the BoJ maintaining its dovish stance, the exchange rate could continue to favor higher-yielding currencies such as the AUD. Meanwhile, USD/JPY and EUR/JPY also reflected the yen’s weakness, reinforcing a broader risk-on tone across the FX market.  

Investors are now monitoring upcoming economic data and central bank communications for confirmation of the BoJ’s path. With AUD/JPY testing key resistance levels, traders are eyeing potential extensions toward 111.20–111.50. The combination of Japanese monetary policy and global risk appetite is likely to keep volatility elevated, shaping near-term trading strategies across major and minor currency pairs.
