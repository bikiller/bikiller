The Indian Rupee (INR) opened the week under pressure, with the USD/INR currency pair climbing 0.5% to approach the 95.00 mark. Traders attributed the move to rising oil prices following setbacks in hopes for a US-Iran diplomatic resolution, fueling concerns over India’s import bill and wider macroeconomic impacts. Market participants noted a cautious tone in early trading as the exchange rate tested key technical levels.

Higher crude costs lifted the US Dollar across emerging market currencies, with the INR particularly sensitive due to its oil import dependence. Forex trading activity saw increased demand for USD amid investor risk aversion, while the EUR and other major currencies showed comparatively muted moves against the greenback. Analysts highlighted that any sustained upward momentum in USD/INR could pressure local equities and bond markets.

Looking ahead, traders are watching oil price trends closely, alongside geopolitical developments that could influence USD/INR volatility. Short-term technical indicators suggest the currency pair may face resistance near 95.20, while support levels around 94.60 could offer temporary relief. Market watchers advise active monitoring of both global energy markets and central bank cues to gauge the Rupee’s near-term trajectory.
