The euro is under pressure against the US dollar as traders brace for the upcoming US CPI report. Deutsche Bank analysts, led by Jim Reid, note that a strong US payrolls report has reinforced expectations of a resilient US labor market and ongoing inflationary pressures. EUR/USD has hovered near recent lows, reflecting caution among investors ahead of potentially market-moving data.  

Market participants are weighing the firm US jobs data, which showed sustained hiring momentum, against broader eurozone dynamics. The exchange rate has tested support levels around 1.0850, with technical indicators signaling potential for further downside if the USD remains favored. Trading volumes have picked up, as positioning ahead of the CPI release intensifies, with the greenback benefiting from safe-haven flows.  

Analysts suggest that the currency pair could see heightened volatility depending on the CPI outcome. A stronger-than-expected inflation reading could reinforce USD strength, pushing EUR/USD lower, while a softer print might allow the euro some recovery. For now, traders are keeping a close eye on macro data and market sentiment, as the balance between US economic resilience and eurozone fundamentals continues to shape exchange rate movements.
