The US Dollar has softened amid easing geopolitical tensions, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) retreating toward the 98.000 level. Traders are pricing in expectations of a de-escalation in the Middle East conflict, prompting a modest pullback in USD positions. According to MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst Lee Hardman, this shift reflects a reduction in safe-haven demand, with markets increasingly favoring risk assets and alternative currencies.  

In forex trading, this movement has influenced major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY. The euro has found some support as USD selling picks up, while yen crosses remain sensitive to broader risk sentiment. Exchange rates are responding not only to geopolitical news but also to technical levels, as traders monitor the 98.000 mark on the DXY for potential support or resistance in short-term trading strategies.  

Hardman emphasizes that while the USD remains structurally strong, softer demand in the near term could provide opportunities for other currencies to gain ground. Market participants are watching how easing conflict risk reshapes liquidity and positioning, with implications for both cross-currency flows and USD-based trading strategies in the coming sessions. Risk sentiment and technical momentum will likely guide exchange rate developments as the situation unfolds.
