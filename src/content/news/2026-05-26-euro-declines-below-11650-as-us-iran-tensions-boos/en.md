The EUR/USD currency pair slipped to around 1.1630 in early European trading on Tuesday, with the Euro falling below the 1.1650 threshold as rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East fueled demand for the US Dollar. Fresh US strikes linked to the US-Iran conflict weakened optimism surrounding a potential diplomatic breakthrough, prompting investors to shift toward traditional safe-haven assets and boosting the USD exchange rate across major currency markets.

The stronger US Dollar placed renewed pressure on the EUR/USD exchange rate, as traders reduced exposure to risk-sensitive currencies amid heightened uncertainty. Market participants closely monitored developments in the region, with escalating tensions supporting defensive trading strategies and increasing demand for dollar-denominated assets. The move also reflected broader caution ahead of upcoming economic data and central bank commentary that could further influence forex market direction.

From a technical perspective, the break below 1.1650 added bearish momentum to the currency pair, with traders now watching whether EUR/USD can stabilize near the 1.1630 area. Sustained USD strength could expose additional downside levels for the Euro, particularly if geopolitical risks continue to dominate market sentiment and reinforce safe-haven flows into the US Dollar.
