The EUR/USD currency pair is trading lower, holding near 1.1750 after a sharp decline of nearly 1% on Monday. The pair dropped to around 1.1740 during European trading hours, reflecting increased pressure on the euro. This move follows disappointing data out of Germany, where retail sales fell unexpectedly by 5.3% in January, signaling potential weakness in the region's economic recovery.

The drop in EUR/USD comes amid a stronger USD, as market sentiment shifts in favor of the dollar. The weaker-than-expected retail sales data in Germany has raised concerns about the eurozone's economic resilience, weighing on the euro. Traders are now focused on upcoming economic releases for further direction, with key support at the 1.1700 level likely to be tested in the near term if bearish momentum persists.
