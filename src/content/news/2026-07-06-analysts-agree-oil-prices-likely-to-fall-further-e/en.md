Brent crude oil traded flat near $71.90 during Monday’s European trading session, reflecting a cautious tone across commodity and forex markets. The price action comes amid steady USD demand and muted risk appetite, keeping oil within a tight range after recent volatility. Traders monitored broader currency pair movements, with EUR/USD and other majors showing limited direction as markets weighed macroeconomic signals.

Analysts agree that crude oil is likely to extend its downward trajectory even after briefly returning to pre-war levels, as supply dynamics and demand uncertainty continue to dominate sentiment. Expectations of weaker global consumption and resilient output are pressuring prices, while forex traders assess how energy trends could influence USD strength and broader currency valuations.

Market participants are also watching spillover effects on commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian dollar, Norwegian krone, and Australian dollar, which typically correlate with oil price movements. A sustained decline in Brent crude could shift exchange rate expectations and support USD relative strength, while keeping EUR/USD and other major currency pairs sensitive to energy-driven risk sentiment shifts.
