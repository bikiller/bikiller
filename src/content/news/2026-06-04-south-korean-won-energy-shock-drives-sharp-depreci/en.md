The South Korean Won remained under heavy pressure in forex trading, with the USD/KRW currency pair surging to its highest level since 2009, according to Brown Brothers Harriman. BBH strategist Elias Haddad noted that the Won has emerged as one of the weakest performers in the foreign exchange market, reflecting mounting concerns over South Korea’s external balance and vulnerability to rising energy costs.

The sharp depreciation has been driven largely by an energy shock that has increased import costs for the energy-dependent economy. Higher prices for oil and other energy imports have widened trade imbalances, fueling demand for USD and pushing the exchange rate sharply higher. As a result, investors have reduced exposure to the Korean currency amid heightened volatility across regional markets.

The move underscores the growing divergence between Asian currencies and the stronger US Dollar environment. While major currency pairs such as EUR/USD have also faced pressure from broad USD strength, the Won’s decline has been particularly pronounced. Continued weakness in the Korean currency could raise imported inflation risks and complicate policy decisions, while traders remain focused on whether USD/KRW can sustain gains near levels last seen during the global financial crisis.
