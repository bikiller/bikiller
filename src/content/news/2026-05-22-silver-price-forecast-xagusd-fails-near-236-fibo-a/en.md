Silver (XAG/USD) struggled to hold gains as it hit resistance near the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement of its recent decline. The currency pair failed to break above this technical level, prompting renewed selling pressure during the Asian trading session. As a result, XAG/USD retreated to around $76.00, signaling that bullish momentum remains limited.

Traders note that the broader USD strength is weighing on silver, with exchange rate dynamics influencing short-term trading. Investors are cautious, as attempts to reclaim higher ground above the Fibonacci level have so far been unsuccessful, keeping the metal under pressure. Market participants are watching USD movements closely, especially against major currencies like EUR, for potential directional cues.

Looking ahead, silver’s failure to clear $76.50 could invite further downside, with sellers likely to defend resistance near the retracement level. The technical setup suggests that unless XAG/USD finds fresh buying interest or USD softens, the currency pair may continue to test lower supports, highlighting the interplay between precious metals trading and broader forex market trends.
