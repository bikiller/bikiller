Gold has faced near-term pressure as rising global yields and renewed inflation risks dampen expectations for imminent rate cuts. This shift in market sentiment has triggered outflows from gold-backed ETFs and prompted stress-driven liquidations. As a result, gold prices have dropped, and investors are reassessing their positions in light of a stronger USD and shifting global economic conditions.

The pressure on gold reflects broader market concerns, with rising bond yields making non-yielding assets like gold less attractive. The USD, buoyed by these developments, continues to put downward pressure on the yellow metal, while the EUR/USD exchange rate remains sensitive to shifting expectations surrounding interest rates and inflation. Despite this, gold finds structural support at lower levels, suggesting potential for recovery if macroeconomic factors stabilize.
