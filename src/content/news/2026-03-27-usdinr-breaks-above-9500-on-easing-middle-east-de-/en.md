The Indian Rupee (INR) faced continued pressure against the US Dollar (USD) during afternoon trading hours on Friday, breaking above the key 95.00 level. The currency pair USD/INR saw the Rupee extend its opening losses, influenced by market sentiment amid easing tensions in the Middle East. This shift in geopolitical risk appetite contributed to a weakened INR, with traders reacting to the relief in regional de-escalation hopes.

The move above the 95.00 mark highlights a critical technical level for USD/INR, potentially indicating further downside for the Rupee if the trend continues. As global market participants reassess risk factors, the USD remains in a strong position, with investors adjusting their positions in response to developments in both the Middle East and broader economic conditions. With the Rupee under pressure, market watchers will be closely monitoring upcoming data and policy signals for clues on the next directional move.
