Silver retreated on Thursday, with the XAG/USD currency pair trading at $74.11 per troy ounce, down 1.08% from Wednesday’s $74.92, according to FXStreet data. The decline reflects cautious trading sentiment as investors weighed the strength of the US dollar against other major currencies like the EUR. Higher USD exchange rates often weigh on precious metals, making silver less attractive as a hedge.  

Market participants noted that recent gains in the USD have increased the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like silver. Technical analysts highlighted resistance near $75, suggesting the pullback could test support levels around $73.50. Trading volumes remained steady, signaling that investors are adjusting positions amid fluctuating currency dynamics.  

Looking ahead, the silver market may remain sensitive to USD movements and broader forex volatility. Traders are watching economic indicators and central bank statements for clues on interest rates, which influence currency strength and, by extension, XAG/USD pricing. If the EUR strengthens relative to the USD, silver could see renewed buying interest, while further USD gains may continue to pressure the metal.
