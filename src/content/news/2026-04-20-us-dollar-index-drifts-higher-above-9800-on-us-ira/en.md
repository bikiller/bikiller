The US Dollar Index (DXY) edged higher during Monday’s Asian session, trading around 98.30 as geopolitical concerns between the US and Iran resurfaced. The DXY, which tracks the USD against a basket of six major currencies including the EUR, JPY, GBP, CAD, SEK, and CHF, showed modest gains as investors sought safe-haven assets amid heightened market uncertainty. Currency pairs such as USD/JPY and USD/EUR reflected this cautious sentiment, with the greenback inching stronger.  

Trading activity suggested a mild risk-off mood, pushing the USD slightly above key technical support levels. Market participants reacted to fresh headlines on US-Iran tensions, which drove flows toward the dollar, while risk-sensitive currencies like the AUD and NZD underperformed. Analysts noted that the index hovering above 98.00 signals the USD’s resilience, potentially keeping pressure on major exchange rates if geopolitical developments escalate.  

Broader implications for forex traders include tighter ranges in cross-currency pairs and heightened volatility around US economic releases. With the DXY consolidating near 98.30, momentum indicators point to cautious trading, emphasizing the importance of monitoring developments in the Middle East. Investors and currency traders alike are positioning for potential spikes in the USD against traditionally softer counterparts such as the EUR and GBP.
