Gold (XAU/USD) remains under mild pressure on Thursday as the precious metal hovers near daily lows, though it has yet to trigger significant follow-through selling. Traders note that reduced expectations for aggressive Federal Reserve rate cuts are supporting US bond yields, keeping the dollar stronger and weighing on gold’s appeal as a non-yielding asset. The pair continues to hold above key intraday swing points, signaling tentative support.

The US dollar has gained traction across major currency pairs, with the stronger USD limiting upside in gold. EUR/USD and other major exchange rates reflect similar dynamics, as investors reassess monetary policy prospects and risk sentiment. Market participants are watching bond yields closely, with rising Treasury rates nudging traders toward yield-bearing assets over precious metals, influencing short-term trading strategies.

Technically, gold’s inability to break sharply below support suggests consolidation rather than a decisive downtrend. Traders are balancing between fading Fed rate cut bets and global economic data, keeping volatility contained in XAU/USD. For forex and commodity traders, this environment underscores the interconnection between central bank expectations, currency strength, and safe-haven demand, guiding positions in both spot and derivative markets.
