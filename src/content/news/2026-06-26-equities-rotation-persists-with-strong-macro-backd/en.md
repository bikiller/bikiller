Danske Research Team notes that the global equity market continues to experience persistent sector and regional rotation, supported by a strong macroeconomic backdrop. European and Asian equities have extended gains, while US equities have slipped modestly, reflecting shifting investor preferences and improving risk appetite outside the US equity space amid changing growth expectations.

Foreign exchange markets reflect the same risk rotation, with the USD trading softer against a basket of major currencies as capital flows into Europe and Asia strengthen. The EUR benefits from relative resilience in European equities, while EUR/USD exchange rate movement signals improving sentiment toward non-US assets, though volatility remains contained.

Looking ahead, analysts suggest the rotation theme may persist if macro conditions remain supportive, influencing both equity positioning and currency pair trading strategies. Continued divergence between US equities and European and Asian markets could keep USD under pressure, while EUR and regional Asian currencies may attract further inflows amid broader portfolio rebalancing.
