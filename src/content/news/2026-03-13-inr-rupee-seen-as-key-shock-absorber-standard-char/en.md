Amid rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, Standard Chartered analysts Anubhuti Sahay and Saurav Anand highlighted the Indian rupee’s role as a key shock absorber in currency markets. With oil prices spiking and global interest rates under pressure, the USD/INR exchange rate has seen heightened volatility, prompting traders to closely monitor technical support near 83.50 and resistance around 84.20. The bank notes that the rupee’s relative stability could help cushion India’s macroeconomic risks.  

The euro has also reacted to these developments, with EUR/USD trading near 1.0900 as investors weigh the impact of higher crude prices on growth and inflation. Market participants are positioning for potential RBI policy responses, while the rupee’s performance against the dollar may influence cross-currency flows, particularly in USD/INR and EUR/INR pairs. Traders are eyeing intraday swings and key technical levels to gauge momentum and hedge exposures.  

Standard Chartered sees the rupee absorbing shocks from global rate pressures, with the INR acting as a stabilizing factor amid external volatility. Exchange rate movements remain sensitive to oil market dynamics, Middle East geopolitical risks, and capital flows. For forex traders, monitoring USD/INR and EUR/INR ranges alongside crude price shifts is crucial for navigating near-term market turbulence.
