Canadian Dollar (CAD) trims part of its intraday gains on Friday as the US Dollar (USD) stages a modest recovery. Uncertainty over a potential final US-Iran peace agreement weighs on risk sentiment, while expectations of a more hawkish Federal Reserve (Fed) continue to underpin the greenback across the forex market.

The US Dollar recovery followed Thursday’s broadly in-line US data, which eased immediate downside pressure on the currency. In forex trading, the USD gained ground against major counterparts as investors reassessed risk exposure. The USD/CAD currency pair reflected the shift, with the exchange rate stabilizing after earlier CAD strength. Broader market sentiment remained sensitive to geopolitical headlines.

Looking ahead, Fed policy expectations and geopolitical risks continue to drive forex volatility, keeping both USD and CAD in focus. Traders also watch EUR/USD for broader dollar sentiment signals. If US-Iran tensions escalate, safe-haven demand could further support the USD, while easing concerns may allow the Canadian Dollar to recover additional ground in the exchange rate.
