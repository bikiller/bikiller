USD/JPY currency pair edged higher on Friday, posting modest gains as trading activity remained subdued due to the US Independence Day holiday. With liquidity conditions thin across global forex markets, the exchange rate moved in a narrow range as investors stayed cautious. The Japanese Yen held relatively steady, limiting volatility despite softer overall momentum.

Following Thursday’s sharp decline, the US Dollar (USD) stabilised against the Japanese Yen (JPY) in the currency pair after weaker-than-expected United States labor market data triggered renewed speculation over the outlook for monetary policy. The forex trading reaction highlighted sensitivity to economic releases, with USD demand recovering only modestly as investors reassessed rate expectations.

Overall sentiment in forex markets remains cautious as traders monitor upcoming US data releases and broader risk appetite. The Japanese Yen continues to act as a relatively stable funding currency, while USD/JPY exchange rate direction is likely to remain data-dependent. Cross-currency flows, including EUR/USD, also reflect subdued volatility amid the holiday-thinned trading environment.
