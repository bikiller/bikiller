The USD/CAD currency pair continued its upward momentum for the second consecutive day, trading around 1.3770 during Wednesday's Asian session. The pair has benefitted from a decline in crude oil prices, which has put pressure on the Canadian Dollar. As oil prices soften amidst a more stable geopolitical environment, the commodity-linked CAD has weakened, contributing to the pair's gains.

Traders are closely monitoring the easing of global tensions, which has lessened demand for oil, a key driver of the Canadian economy. This shift in sentiment is likely to keep USD/CAD in a bullish trend, as market participants expect further pressure on the Canadian Dollar. With the exchange rate sitting near 1.3770, the pair could see more upside if oil prices remain subdued in the near term.
