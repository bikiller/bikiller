West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil surged to nearly $105.00 during Monday’s Asian trading session, marking its highest level in almost four years. The spike follows heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, particularly after former U.S. President Donald Trump’s comments suggesting possible attacks on Iranian infrastructure. This threat has raised concerns about potential supply disruptions in the region, which typically impacts global oil prices.

The rise in WTI has contributed to volatility in the forex markets, with the USD strengthening as oil prices climb. The USD is gaining across major currency pairs, including the EUR/USD, which has seen a pullback, while the oil-sensitive currencies like the CAD are also benefiting from the price surge. Traders are closely watching oil developments, as further escalation in the Middle East could push WTI toward higher technical resistance levels, influencing broader market sentiment.
