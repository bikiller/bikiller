Aluminium prices are being heavily influenced by supply disruptions caused by the ongoing Gulf conflict. TD Securities reports that smelting operations in Bahrain and Qatar have been significantly impacted, with force majeure declared at Alba. These disruptions have intensified the supply shock, pushing aluminium prices higher. As a result, market participants are seeing strong support for elevated aluminium prices, with trading activity picking up across key commodity pairs.

The aluminium market’s volatility is also spilling over into the forex market. A surge in demand for safe-haven assets, particularly the USD, is driving fluctuations in currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/GBP. Traders are closely monitoring these developments, as the aluminium supply tightness could continue to impact the broader market. The ongoing disruption in the Gulf region is proving to be a key factor in global price trends, with many traders looking for potential reversals or continuation in the coming weeks.
