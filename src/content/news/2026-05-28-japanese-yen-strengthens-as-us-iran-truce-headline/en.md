The Japanese Yen (JPY) gained ground against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, with the USD/JPY currency pair ending its five-day winning streak. The exchange rate retreated as investors reacted to reports of a preliminary truce extension between the US and Iran, weighing on the Greenback. Trading saw USD/JPY dip back toward key technical support levels, highlighting renewed Yen strength in the forex market.

Market participants cited heightened risk sentiment and the easing of geopolitical tensions as the main drivers behind the Yen’s advance. The USD struggled across other major pairs, including EUR/USD, as traders reassessed positions in light of potential shifts in US foreign policy. Technical charts indicated a pause in the USD’s rally, with the Yen now testing levels that previously acted as resistance, offering short-term trading opportunities for currency traders.

Analysts note that if the truce holds, the USD may continue to face pressure, potentially boosting demand for safe-haven currencies like JPY. Forex traders are closely watching the USD/JPY and EUR/USD pairs for signs of trend reversals, while the market monitors broader macroeconomic cues that could influence USD liquidity and trading volumes in the days ahead. The interplay of geopolitical news and technical levels remains central to ongoing currency pair dynamics.
