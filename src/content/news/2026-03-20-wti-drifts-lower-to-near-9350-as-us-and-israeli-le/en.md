West Texas Intermediate (WTI) slipped to around $93.50 in early Asian trading Friday, reflecting easing market jitters after US and Israeli leaders attempted to reassure investors over the recent attacks on key Persian Gulf energy infrastructure. The decline in crude prompted modest shifts in currency markets, with the USD gaining slightly against the EUR and JPY as traders recalibrated risk sentiment. Oil-linked currencies like the CAD also saw subdued trading amid the lower WTI benchmark.  

Market participants noted that the exchange rate dynamics are influenced by the interplay between energy prices and safe-haven demand. The EUR/USD pair edged down as the USD firmed, while risk-sensitive pairs such as AUD/USD mirrored WTI’s retreat. Analysts suggest that if geopolitical tensions continue to ease, the correlation between crude prices and USD strength may soften, potentially stabilizing major currency pairs.  

Technical levels around $93.50 for WTI now act as a near-term support, with traders monitoring whether the benchmark can rebound above $94.0 amid ongoing geopolitical headlines. Volatility in forex markets remains linked to oil price swings, keeping investors attentive to both energy news and central bank commentary, as movements in USD exchange rates could ripple through commodity-linked currencies and broader trading strategies.
