EUR/GBP edged lower on Monday, with the currency pair trading around 0.8658 after briefly hitting an intraday high of 0.8676. The pound shrugged off early political jitters in the UK, while the euro showed modest strength ahead of key central bank meetings. Traders remain cautious, weighing potential volatility from the upcoming European Central Bank (ECB) and Bank of England (BoE) decisions.  

Market participants noted that the exchange rate’s dip reflects a mix of profit-taking and recalibration after last week’s swings. The pound’s resilience suggests that domestic political noise has limited impact on short-term trading, leaving investors focused on interest rate guidance and economic projections. EUR/GBP technical levels around 0.8650–0.8680 are being closely watched as the cross navigates modest fluctuations.  

Looking ahead, the pair may continue to fluctuate in response to policy signals from both the ECB and BoE. Traders are also monitoring broader USD movements, which could indirectly influence euro and pound dynamics. With the market poised for potential surprises, positioning ahead of central bank announcements will likely dictate near-term trading momentum in the EUR/GBP cross.
