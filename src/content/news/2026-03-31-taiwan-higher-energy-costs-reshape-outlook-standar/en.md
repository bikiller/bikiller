Rising energy costs are prompting Standard Chartered to adjust its 2026 macro outlook for Taiwan. Senior Economist Tommy Wu highlights that surging oil and LNG prices, fueled by Middle East tensions, are pushing up import expenses, reshaping inflation expectations. The bank now forecasts CPI inflation at 2.1%, up from the prior 1.5%, signaling potential pressure on the TWD against major currencies like the USD and EUR in currency markets.  

The revised outlook also reflects slower economic momentum, with GDP growth trimmed to 7.6% from 8.0%. Higher energy bills are expected to weigh on domestic consumption and industrial output, factors likely to influence TWD exchange rate trading. Traders may adjust positions in USD/TWD and EUR/TWD pairs as market participants recalibrate expectations for central bank policy and liquidity flows.  

Currency markets are responding cautiously to these revisions, with traders eyeing technical support and resistance levels. The TWD could face volatility against the USD amid concerns about imported inflation and external pressures, while EUR/TWD trading may see adjustments in line with broader risk sentiment. Investors are monitoring shifts in global energy markets closely, as sustained price pressures could continue shaping Taiwan’s macro trajectory.
