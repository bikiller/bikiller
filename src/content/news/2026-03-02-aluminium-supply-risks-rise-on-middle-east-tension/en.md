LME aluminium surged to a one-month peak this week, as escalating tensions in the Middle East reignited concerns over supply disruptions. ING Commodities Strategist Ewa Manthey highlighted that the price jump is driven more by regional physical premiums than by a broad tightening of global inventories. Traders noted heightened activity in contracts priced in USD, with the aluminium/US dollar exchange rate reacting sharply to geopolitical headlines.  

Market attention has focused on Europe and the US, where physical premiums have spiked amid uncertainties over shipping and refinery operations. Manthey emphasized that while global aluminium stocks remain relatively stable, the localized pressure is pushing traders to reassess short-term risk exposure. The move has coincided with increased hedging in currency pairs such as EUR/USD, as European buyers weigh the impact of higher import costs against a fluctuating dollar.  

Analysts suggest that ongoing geopolitical developments could keep aluminium trading volatile in the near term. Exchange rate swings between major currencies, including USD/JPY and GBP/USD, may influence regional demand as manufacturers navigate rising input costs. For forex traders, the situation underscores the interplay between commodity supply risks and currency valuations, highlighting the need for careful monitoring of both metal prices and cross-border flows.
