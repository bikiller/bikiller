Gold prices (XAU/USD) continue their downward trend, trading around $5,000 during the early Asian session on Tuesday. The precious metal faces mild losses as investor optimism fades over the potential for a US Federal Reserve rate cut this year. As market sentiment shifts, expectations for easing have diminished, leading to pressure on gold prices and influencing broader market sentiment.

With all attention now focused on the upcoming Federal Reserve interest rate decision scheduled for Wednesday, traders are closely watching for any signals that could influence future monetary policy. The fading hopes of rate cuts weigh on gold, traditionally seen as a hedge against inflation, while the USD strengthens, exerting further downward pressure on the XAU/USD currency pair.
