The Indian Rupee (INR) halted a five-day losing streak against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, with the USD/INR currency pair staying near the day's low. The exchange rate traded at 83.11, reflecting a slight dip after the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) intervened in the market to stabilize the INR. This move followed concerns over the weakening currency, with the central bank actively supporting the rupee's value.

Despite the RBI's intervention, the Indian Rupee remains under pressure, impacted by global risk sentiment and elevated oil prices. The USD/INR pair's technical levels suggest a potential for further volatility in the short term, with traders eyeing the 83.20 mark as a crucial resistance level. The market will continue to monitor RBI actions and broader global factors affecting currency trading as the INR looks to regain momentum.
