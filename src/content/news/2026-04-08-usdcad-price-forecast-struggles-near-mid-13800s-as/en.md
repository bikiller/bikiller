The USD/CAD currency pair extended its weekly downtrend for a third consecutive session on Wednesday, slipping to a nearly two-week low. Spot trading hovered in the mid-1.3800s, reflecting a decline of roughly 0.30% for the day. Despite the dip, the pair struggled to gain follow-through momentum, as traders weighed mixed fundamental signals from both the U.S. and Canadian economies.  

Market participants remain cautious near the 200-day EMA, a key technical level that could dictate the next leg of USD/CAD trading. Investors are watching broader dollar movements, including USD strength against major peers like the EUR and JPY, which continues to influence sentiment in the North American pair. Volatility remains subdued as markets await clearer directional cues from upcoming economic data.  

Analysts note that a decisive break below the 200-day EMA could accelerate selling pressure, potentially opening the way for further losses toward the 1.3750 area. Meanwhile, any rebound in the exchange rate is likely to encounter resistance near 1.3850–1.3870. Traders are positioning carefully, factoring in interest rate expectations, oil price trends, and cross-currency flows that could steer USD/CAD momentum in the days ahead.
