The Singapore dollar is trading in a choppy, subdued range against the US dollar, with the USDSGD pair closely tracking broader USD movements, crude oil prices, and U.S. Treasury yields. Market participants are seeing mild bullish momentum lose steam, prompting banks like OCBC to recommend caution. Technical support levels are identified at 1.2720/60 and 1.2650/70, while resistance hovers near 1.2840/50.  

Traders are advised to buy dips rather than chase rallies as the pair oscillates within this narrow band. The subdued volatility reflects a mix of global macro drivers, including shifts in USD sentiment and yield fluctuations, keeping the exchange rate range-bound. Momentum indicators suggest any upside may be limited in the near term, making strategic entry points near support more attractive.  

With the USDSGD range-bound, market watchers are also monitoring correlations with EUR and other major currencies, as well as movements in oil prices and UST yields that continue to influence USD strength. The overall trading tone remains cautious, with a preference for selling rallies, while attention stays on key technical zones to gauge potential breakouts or reversals.
