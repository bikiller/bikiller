DBS Group Research economists Radhika Rao and Chua Han Teng believe that Indonesia’s economic prospects remain resilient despite ongoing tensions in the Middle East. With negligible trade exposure to Iran and a diversified trading relationship with Gulf economies, Indonesia is well-positioned to buffer against oil price shocks. This stability is crucial for maintaining the strength of the Indonesian rupiah (IDR) against major currencies like the USD and EUR.

As a net commodity exporter, Indonesia benefits from high global demand, which supports the exchange rate and keeps inflation in check. The potential impacts on the IDR from fluctuating oil prices are mitigated by the country's solid trade fundamentals. Traders should monitor the USD/IDR exchange rate closely, as continued geopolitical concerns could influence market sentiment and lead to volatility in currency pairs linked to emerging markets.
