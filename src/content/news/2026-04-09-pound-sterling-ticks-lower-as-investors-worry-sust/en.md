Pound Sterling edged lower on Thursday, showing cautious trading against major peers as investors questioned the durability of the Iran ceasefire. The currency hovered around 1.3400 versus the US Dollar (USD) during the European session, reflecting a cautious market mood. Market participants cited geopolitical uncertainty as weighing on risk sentiment, prompting a subdued tone across the GBP/USD and GBP/EUR currency pairs.  

The exchange rate for GBP/USD remained range-bound near key technical levels, with traders hesitant to commit ahead of further developments in the Middle East. The Pound’s modest retreat contrasts with a firmer USD, which benefited from safe-haven demand amid mounting geopolitical concerns. Analysts noted that market positioning may limit sharp movements unless fresh economic data or political signals emerge.  

Across the broader forex market, the Pound’s performance underlined sensitivity to global risk events, with the EUR and other European currencies also reflecting cautious sentiment. Trading volumes remained moderate as investors monitored both the geopolitical situation and upcoming economic releases. Short-term technical indicators suggest that GBP/USD could test support near 1.3380 if uncertainty persists, highlighting a careful approach among currency traders navigating heightened volatility.
