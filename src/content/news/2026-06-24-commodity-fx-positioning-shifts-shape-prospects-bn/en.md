Positioning in forex trading has shifted as IMM volumes concentrate in commodity-linked currencies, with AUD, NZD, and NOK leading recent adjustments. According to BNY’s Geoff Yu, the moves reflect broader repricing across currency pairs against the USD and EUR, as investors reassess carry and growth differentials. Exchange rate volatility has been most visible in commodity FX, where liquidity flows have become more concentrated and reactive to macro signals.

NOK and CAD are seeing outflows tied to oil sensitivity and shifting expectations around Norges Bank tightening, with traders reducing exposure in related currency pairs. The moves have weighed on exchange rate performance versus the USD, as commodity FX remains vulnerable to energy price swings and changing rate differentials in global forex trading conditions.

NZD and AUD have underperformed since May, reflecting softer risk sentiment and repositioning in commodity currency pairs. The weakness has implications for short-term exchange rate trends, particularly against the USD, as investors reassess growth prospects and interest rate paths. In broader forex trading, the shift underscores how commodity FX remains sensitive to global demand signals.
