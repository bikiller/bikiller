The European Central Bank (ECB) is set to announce its monetary policy decision this Thursday, following its April meeting. Analysts expect the ECB to keep interest rates unchanged, with the deposit facility remaining at 2%. This neutral stance comes as markets continue to evaluate the ECB's future direction on rate hikes, particularly in light of ongoing inflation concerns.

In forex markets, the EUR/USD exchange rate remains sensitive to any signals regarding future monetary tightening. Traders are closely watching the ECB's stance, as a shift in policy could drive volatility in the euro. With the central bank maintaining its current rate, the euro could remain range-bound against the USD, with key levels to watch at 1.0900 and 1.1000. Future ECB decisions on interest rates will be pivotal in shaping the euro's outlook in the coming months.
