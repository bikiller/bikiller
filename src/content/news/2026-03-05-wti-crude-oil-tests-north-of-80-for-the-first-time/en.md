WTI crude oil surged past $80 per barrel on Thursday, climbing more than 6% in a single session and marking its first return above this level since June 2024. The rally reflects heightened supply concerns amid geopolitical tensions, pushing one of the sharpest upward moves in recent years. Traders noted that this jump is feeding through to broader markets, with energy-linked currency pairs showing increased volatility.  

The surge in oil prices is weighing on the USD, as higher commodity costs can stoke inflation expectations and influence central bank policy sentiment. EUR/USD and other major exchange rates have responded, with traders recalibrating positions as the energy-driven spike reshapes short-term trading dynamics. Commodity currencies like the CAD and NOK also gained as crude-linked flows intensified.  

Analysts warn that sustained WTI strength above $80 could drive more aggressive trading in futures and spot FX markets, creating ripple effects across equities and bond markets. Market participants are closely watching technical levels, while central banks and forex desks adjust hedges to manage volatility. The combination of price momentum and macroeconomic implications underscores how oil remains a critical driver in both commodity and currency trading landscapes.
