The US Dollar (USD) has gained momentum this week as tensions escalate between the US, Israel, and Iran, with the conflict now approaching its second week. Iran's closure of the Strait of Hormuz has driven oil prices higher, adding to inflationary pressures. This has caused traders to flock to safe-haven currencies like the USD, pushing the EUR/USD exchange rate lower, with the pair falling towards 1.0700. 

Oil's surge has also triggered a risk-off sentiment in global markets, affecting forex trading strategies. The rise in crude prices threatens to accelerate inflation, leading to increased demand for the USD. Investors are recalibrating their expectations for Federal Reserve policy, which could lead to further strengthening of the Greenback. The USD/JPY saw notable gains, moving past 150.00, while other major pairs like GBP/USD remain under pressure, trading around 1.2100 as the market remains cautious heading into next week.
