WTI crude oil held firm above the $100 per barrel mark on Monday, driven by geopolitical tensions following former President Trump's threats to destroy Iran's energy infrastructure. The price of West Texas Intermediate (WTI) briefly surged to $101, before facing a quick, temporary dip back under $98. Despite the volatility, the market remained sensitive to these geopolitical developments, which sparked strong buying pressure.

The fluctuations were notable, with intraday movement showcasing heightened volatility. As crude oil prices surged, this bolstered the USD, which saw an uptick against other currencies like the EUR. Traders are closely watching this dynamic as continued price movements could influence the broader forex market, potentially impacting the USD's strength against major currency pairs. The current price action in WTI highlights the energy market’s vulnerability to political statements and their effect on global trading.
