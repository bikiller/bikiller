Silver prices held steady near $76.50 per troy ounce, showing modest gains of just over 1% amid cautious trading. Market participants are weighing the impact of ongoing developments in the Middle East, particularly around US-Iran negotiations, which are keeping risk sentiment fragile. The narrow trading range suggests investors are reluctant to push positions aggressively without clearer signals from political channels.

Technical indicators, including the Relative Strength Index (RSI), point to potential upside momentum, hinting at a breakout if supportive catalysts emerge. Currency pairs such as USD/EUR are influencing precious metals indirectly, with a firmer USD often putting pressure on silver, while softer exchange rates can provide relief for buyers. Traders are watching these dynamics closely, balancing geopolitical risk with traditional safe-haven demand.

The consolidation phase around $76.50 sets the stage for potential trading opportunities if volatility picks up. Short-term swings could be shaped by both macroeconomic data and shifts in forex markets, where USD movements against major currencies like EUR, JPY, and GBP can affect metal prices. Investors are positioned cautiously, seeking clarity on both the Middle East situation and broader global economic indicators before committing to larger trades.
