Rising oil prices driven by escalating Middle East tensions have prompted Standard Chartered economists Chong Hoon Park and Arup Ghosh to revise South Korea’s inflation outlook higher. The bank now forecasts 2026 CPI inflation at 2.4%, up from 2.0% previously, assuming crude oil averages USD 85 per barrel. The shift reflects growing cost pressures that could influence broader market sentiment and trading conditions.

In the forex market, the updated inflation trajectory may add volatility to the KRW, particularly against the USD, as traders reassess interest rate expectations and external balance risks. A higher inflation path could complicate the Bank of Korea’s policy stance, potentially affecting the USD/KRW exchange rate as markets weigh tightening prospects against growth concerns.

Elevated energy costs also carry implications for South Korea’s trade dynamics, given its heavy reliance on imports. This may widen the current account deficit and put additional pressure on the currency pair, especially in a strong USD environment. Forex participants will likely monitor oil price trends and geopolitical developments closely, as they remain key drivers of exchange rate direction and regional trading flows.
