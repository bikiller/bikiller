The GBP/USD currency pair edged higher on Monday, approaching the 1.3590 level as the Pound Sterling found support amid easing geopolitical tensions. Traders are encouraged by the possibility of a US-Iran conflict resolution, boosting risk appetite and demand for the British currency. In parallel, the US Dollar weakened after softer-than-expected US Producer Price Index data suggested that inflation pressures may be cooling.  

Market participants noted that the USD’s retreat allowed the GBP/USD exchange rate to gain 0.61%, with trading activity favoring the Pound over the Greenback. Analysts highlight that the decline in USD demand reflects investor caution, especially as US inflation data fell short of forecasts for a higher print. This dynamic is likely to influence short-term positioning in forex markets.  

Technically, GBP/USD is testing the 1.3590 region, which may act as a near-term resistance for traders watching the pair closely. Continued USD softness could sustain Sterling strength, while broader market sentiment, including geopolitical developments, will remain a key driver. Investors also keep an eye on EUR and other major currencies for correlated moves in global exchange rates.
