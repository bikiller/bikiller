Asian currencies edged higher as risk sentiment stabilized after remarks from US President Donald Trump suggesting a potential end to the Iran conflict. The improved tone supported regional FX trading, with several currency pairs seeing modest gains against the USD. However, elevated oil prices continued to cap upside, reflecting lingering geopolitical uncertainty in global markets.

According to MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst Michael Wan, the rebound in Asian exchange rates remains fragile. Traders are cautiously positioning in key currency pairs such as USD/JPY and USD/CNH, as doubts persist over whether de-escalation efforts will hold. The USD softened slightly, but safe-haven demand has not fully dissipated, limiting broader FX momentum.

Looking ahead, the interplay between geopolitical developments and commodity prices will be critical for forex markets. Sustained high oil prices could pressure import-dependent Asian economies, weighing on their currencies. As a result, traders are likely to remain defensive, closely monitoring headlines and adjusting positions in major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/Asia crosses, for clearer directional signals.
