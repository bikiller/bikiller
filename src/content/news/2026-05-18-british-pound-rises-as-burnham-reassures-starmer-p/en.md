The British Pound pushed higher in early trading as the currency pair GBP/USD climbed above key technical levels, with the exchange rate gaining ground against both the USD and EUR. Traders attributed the uptick in sterling to growing political pressure on Prime Minister Keir Starmer, which has sparked volatility in UK assets. The advance in GBP saw increased trading activity, with markets eyeing support near recent lows and resistance levels unchanged for now.

Market participants reacted positively after Andy Burnham, a leading challenger in the Labour leadership contest, sought to calm investors by confirming he would uphold Chancellor Rachel Reeves’ fiscal rules if he becomes prime minister. That reassurance helped steady the Pound’s exchange rate and dampened fears of abrupt policy shifts. As GBP pairs continue to price in political risk, traders are monitoring economic data and central bank signals for clues on future direction, especially against major currencies like the USD and EUR.
