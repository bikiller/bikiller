Japan’s Finance Minister, Satsuki Katayama, remarked during European trading hours on Tuesday that the government is closely monitoring the impact of the ongoing Middle East conflict on the Japanese economy. Katayama emphasized that future cost estimates heavily depend on the war’s duration, signaling potential uncertainty for Japan’s economic outlook. This uncertainty comes as global markets, including the USD/JPY currency pair, remain volatile, reflecting investor concerns over geopolitical risks.

The Japanese yen has faced pressure amidst this geopolitical turmoil, with the USD/JPY hovering near significant levels. The currency pair's movements have been largely influenced by risk sentiment, with traders cautious about long-term inflationary impacts. In the broader forex market, the EUR/JPY also displayed fluctuations, as European risk-off sentiment weighed on the euro, while Japan’s yen struggled to recover. These developments highlight the delicate balance the Japanese government must maintain to mitigate economic risks from ongoing global instability.
