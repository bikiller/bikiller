The USD/CAD currency pair edged higher on Monday, with the exchange rate climbing to around 1.3617, marking a gain of roughly 0.22% on the day. Traders cited rising geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, fueled by the ongoing standoff between the US and Iran, as a key driver behind the US Dollar’s strength. Market participants remain cautious, monitoring developments that could impact safe-haven demand for USD.  

The US Dollar’s resilience has also influenced broader forex trading, with investors reassessing positions against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY. Analysts note that the pair’s intraday momentum is supported by risk-off sentiment, which tends to benefit the USD amid geopolitical uncertainties. Technical indicators suggest potential resistance around the 1.3650 level, while immediate support remains near 1.3590, highlighting a relatively contained range for the session.  

Looking ahead, the USD/CAD exchange rate may continue to react to news surrounding US-Iran relations and broader commodity trends, particularly oil prices, given Canada’s status as an energy exporter. Traders are balancing geopolitical risks with macroeconomic data, making the pair sensitive to shifts in sentiment. Any escalation in tensions could further underpin the USD, reinforcing its role as a haven in forex markets, while easing concerns may allow the Canadian Dollar to reclaim some ground.
