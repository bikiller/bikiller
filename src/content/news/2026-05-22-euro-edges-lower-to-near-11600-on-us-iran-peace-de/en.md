The EUR/USD currency pair is trading slightly lower near the 1.1615 level in early European hours on Friday, showing modest weakness against the US Dollar. The euro’s exchange rate continues to face downward pressure amid uncertainty surrounding the US-Iran peace deal, leaving traders cautious. Market sentiment appears fragile, with investors weighing potential geopolitical risks against broader dollar strength.

Mixed reports on the progress of the US-Iran negotiations have added to the euro’s cautious stance. While some updates hint at possible breakthroughs, others suggest delays, keeping the EUR under pressure. Technical analysts note that the next support level for the pair lies around 1.1600, making this a critical point for traders looking for short-term entry and exit signals.

The USD has benefited from safe-haven flows as global markets digest the news, reinforcing its appeal against the euro. Trading volumes remain moderate, but the overall trend signals continued volatility for the currency pair. Investors are closely monitoring developments, aware that any decisive news on the peace deal could trigger sharp moves in the EUR/USD exchange rate.
