The US Dollar has remained broadly supported despite recent softening, as optimism over a potential US-Iran peace deal weighs on demand. According to ING’s Chris Turner, while the DXY index has retreated slightly, key support levels continue to hold, reflecting persistent market caution. Traders are watching the USD/EUR and USD/JPY currency pairs closely amid mixed signals from geopolitical developments and risk sentiment.

Energy markets are a major factor underpinning USD strength. Turner notes that disruptions to oil supply through the Strait of Hormuz continue to pose inflationary risks, suggesting that any relief in crude flows could influence exchange rates and forex trading dynamics. The USD’s resilience is being tested as oil prices remain sensitive to geopolitical headlines, keeping volatility elevated in the currency market.

Investors are balancing the potential easing of conflict with the structural risks that remain. While short-term trading sees a softer dollar against major currencies, underlying concerns about inflation and energy shortages keep market participants cautious. The interplay between the peace deal prospects, oil market pressures, and technical support levels will likely guide USD movements and broader forex exchange rate trends in the coming sessions.
