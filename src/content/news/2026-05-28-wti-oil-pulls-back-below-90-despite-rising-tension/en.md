WTI crude oil slipped back below the $90.00 mark on Thursday, despite renewed geopolitical tensions after reported exchanges between Iran and the United States. Oil prices initially moved higher during early trading, but bullish momentum faded as investors weighed the risk of broader supply disruptions against cautious market positioning. The pullback in crude limited support for commodity-linked currencies, while the USD held firm across major currency pair trading.

Market participants remain focused on how escalating tensions in the Middle East could affect global energy flows and inflation expectations. Rising oil prices typically strengthen safe-haven demand for the USD, while pressuring import-heavy economies tied to the EUR and Asian exchange rates. However, traders appeared reluctant to push WTI decisively above resistance near $90.00, signaling concerns that demand conditions and tighter financial markets may cap further gains.

In forex markets, the stronger US Dollar kept EUR/USD under pressure, as investors shifted toward defensive trading strategies amid geopolitical uncertainty. Analysts noted that sustained oil volatility could influence central bank expectations, particularly around inflation-sensitive monetary policy decisions. While crude prices remain elevated compared with recent weeks, technical support around the $88.50 region is now drawing attention as traders assess whether tensions will translate into a longer-term rally.
