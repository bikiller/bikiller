The EUR/USD currency pair continued its rebound on Wednesday, extending gains from Tuesday and trading around the 1.1600 level during the European session. The pair climbed roughly 0.3%, reflecting renewed investor confidence as risk sentiment improved and demand for safe-haven assets like the USD eased. Traders noted that the euro’s strength against the dollar is supported by diminishing geopolitical concerns.  

Market participants are closely watching developments in the Middle East, where hopes of a ceasefire have lifted risk appetite and encouraged trading in more volatile assets. The improved sentiment has prompted investors to scale back USD positions, allowing the EUR/USD exchange rate to edge higher. Technical traders highlighted that breaking above 1.1600 could open the way for further euro gains.  

Analysts suggest that the currency pair’s current trajectory underscores the sensitivity of forex markets to geopolitical events and market sentiment. While the USD remains under pressure, the euro’s resilience may continue if ceasefire expectations persist. Traders are likely to monitor both political developments and technical levels closely as the pair navigates its near-term range.
