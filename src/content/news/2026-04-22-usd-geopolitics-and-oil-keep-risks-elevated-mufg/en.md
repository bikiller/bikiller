The US dollar continues to navigate elevated risks as geopolitical tensions and surging oil prices weigh on markets. MUFG strategist Derek Halpenny notes that President Trump’s recent ceasefire extension offered only a brief respite, leaving risk sentiment fragile. Oil hovers near the USD 100 mark, keeping energy-linked currencies under pressure while safe-haven flows support the USD across major currency pairs.  

In the forex market, the USD remains resilient, with traders eyeing exchange rate moves against the EUR and JPY. Halpenny points out that uncertainty around global conflicts and persistent commodity-driven inflation is driving cautious trading, limiting the potential for sustained rallies in risk-sensitive currencies. Currency pairs like EUR/USD are testing key support levels, reflecting market jitters.  

The elevated risk environment underscores a potential for increased volatility in the weeks ahead. With oil prices maintaining their lofty levels, market participants are closely monitoring USD positioning, while EUR and other major currencies could face intermittent selling pressure. Traders are balancing short-term geopolitical developments with broader macro trends, shaping exchange rate dynamics in a challenging landscape for global forex trading.
