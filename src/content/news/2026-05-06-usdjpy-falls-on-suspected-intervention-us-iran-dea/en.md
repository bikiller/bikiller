The USD/JPY currency pair rebounded on Wednesday after earlier sharp losses, trading around 156.42 following a dip to an intraday low near 155.00. Market participants pointed to a suspected intervention by Japanese authorities as the main catalyst behind the sudden weakness in the yen. Traders closely monitored technical support levels, with the 155.00 mark acting as a key pivot that helped stabilize the exchange rate.  

The broader USD faced pressure amid renewed optimism over a potential US-Iran nuclear deal, which weighed on demand for safe-haven dollars. Risk-on sentiment lifted other major currencies, including the EUR, while investors adjusted positions in anticipation of further geopolitical developments. Trading volumes in USD/JPY surged during the rebound, highlighting the market’s sensitivity to both central bank signals and international news flows.  

Analysts suggest that the near-term outlook for USD/JPY remains volatile, with intervention risks and geopolitical headlines likely to influence trading. Momentum indicators show the pair testing short-term resistance around 157.00, while support at 155.00 remains critical. Investors remain attentive to the interplay between US monetary policy, yen interventions, and broader risk sentiment, which together continue to shape the dynamics of major currency pairs.
