Gold price (XAU/USD) edged higher toward $4,675.00 during Tuesday’s European trading session, though overall movement remained largely sideways. Traders are closely watching the 20-day exponential moving average (EMA), which currently acts as a significant barrier. Market participants are weighing geopolitical developments, particularly the looming deadline set by former U.S. President Donald Trump regarding Iran, which is adding caution to trading activity across commodities and related currency pairs.  

The USD showed mixed performance against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY, reflecting a cautious market sentiment. Investors appear hesitant to commit to aggressive positions as gold’s exchange rate hovers near key technical levels. Momentum indicators suggest a potential consolidation phase, with traders likely to react sharply to any news that might shift geopolitical tensions or U.S. policy statements, influencing both gold and broader forex markets.  

Analysts highlight that a breach above the 20-day EMA could spark renewed bullish interest in XAU/USD, while a rejection may reinforce sideways trading in the near term. The interplay between gold and USD remains critical, with shifts in the exchange rate impacting hedging strategies and cross-currency trades. Market participants remain alert to volatility as the Trump-Iran timeline approaches, potentially driving sudden moves across metals, USD, and correlated currency pairs.
