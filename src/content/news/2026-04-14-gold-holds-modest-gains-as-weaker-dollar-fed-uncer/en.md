Gold (XAU/USD) edged slightly higher on Tuesday, maintaining a modest positive bias as the US Dollar (USD) softened. Traders noted that renewed optimism over potential US-Iran negotiations lent support to the safe-haven metal, keeping its exchange rate buoyant. Despite these gains, gold remains confined within a narrow trading range, reflecting investor caution amid lingering uncertainty over the Federal Reserve’s next policy moves.  

The currency pair showed resilience near key technical levels, with XAU/USD hovering around $1,995 per ounce. Market participants observed that a weaker USD generally favors gold, as it lowers the opportunity cost for buyers using other currencies such as the EUR or JPY. However, mixed signals from economic data and Fed commentary continued to cap any aggressive upside, leaving the pair in a rangebound pattern for now.  

Analysts suggest that gold’s modest advance may persist if geopolitical optimism continues and the USD remains pressured. Traders are closely monitoring broader forex market trends, including USD/EUR and USD/JPY movements, as they could influence gold’s trajectory. With technical support holding steady and resistance levels intact, short-term trading is expected to stay cautious, balancing safe-haven demand against central bank uncertainties.
