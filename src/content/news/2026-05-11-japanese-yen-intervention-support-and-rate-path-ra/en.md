The Japanese Yen (JPY) is finding support after recent intervention moves by the Ministry of Finance, with expectations of backing from the US Treasury adding further stability. According to Rabobank’s Senior FX Strategist Jane Foley, this endorsement could help prevent the JPY from sliding further against the US dollar (USD), keeping the USD/JPY exchange rate within a more controlled trading range in the near term.  

Market participants are closely watching the currency pair, as interventions signal authorities’ commitment to curbing excessive volatility. Technical levels suggest that USD/JPY may face resistance around 145.50, while support near 144.00 remains key for traders. The combination of rate path considerations and intervention signals is prompting cautious positioning, with investors balancing expectations for policy tightening and currency stabilization.  

Meanwhile, the broader forex market is factoring in JPY strength when assessing EUR/JPY and other cross pairs. A firmer yen could influence trading flows from carry trades, while safe-haven demand may rise if global risk sentiment falters. Foley highlights that near-term JPY dynamics are likely to remain intervention-sensitive, with both domestic and international cues shaping the exchange rate trajectory in coming weeks.
