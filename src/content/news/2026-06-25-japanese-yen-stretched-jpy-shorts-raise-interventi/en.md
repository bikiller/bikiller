ABN AMRO’s Georgette Boele notes that speculative positioning in the Japanese yen is highly stretched, with market participants holding substantial net short positions. This comes as the USD/JPY currency pair trades near levels last seen in 1986, underscoring an extended bearish yen trading environment and increasing the risk of official intervention from Japanese authorities.

Persistent yen weakness has been driven by wide interest rate differentials between the US and Japan, sustaining carry trades that favour USD-denominated assets. Continued US dollar strength has also supported major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and others, reinforcing pressure on JPY. For forex trading desks, volatility risks are rising as momentum-driven positioning dominates short-term exchange rate moves.

Looking ahead, the market is increasingly sensitive to any signs of policy response from the Bank of Japan, with intervention risk rising as USD/JPY remains elevated. A rapid unwinding of crowded short yen positions could trigger sharp corrective moves in the exchange rate, potentially strengthening JPY across major currency pairs and disrupting existing trading strategies.
