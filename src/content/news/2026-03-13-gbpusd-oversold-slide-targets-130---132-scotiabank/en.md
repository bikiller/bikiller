The Pound is showing continued weakness against the US Dollar, with the GBP/USD currency pair under pressure following a surprise contraction in UK industrial production. Market sentiment remains risk-averse, weighing on Sterling trading, as investors digest the implications of weaker economic data and a firmer USD. Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret highlight that the exchange rate is underperforming amid broader market caution.  

Technically, the oversold slide in GBP/USD points to potential support in the 1.30–1.32 range. Traders are closely watching these levels, as a breach could signal further downward pressure on Sterling. The combination of disappointing UK data and resilient Dollar demand is shaping the current trading environment, influencing not just GBP/USD but broader currency movements including EUR/USD.  

Looking ahead, market participants will monitor UK economic indicators for signs of stabilization. The interplay between risk sentiment, USD strength, and Sterling fundamentals will be pivotal in guiding trading strategies. Scotiabank’s analysis suggests that while GBP/USD may find temporary support around 1.30–1.32, broader pressures could keep Sterling subdued against the Dollar in the near term.
