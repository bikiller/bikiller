Gold (XAU/USD) started the week under pressure, trading near one-month lows as escalating tensions in the Middle East failed to provide safe-haven support. The precious metal was weighed down by continued hawkish expectations surrounding interest rates, with markets anticipating tighter monetary policies from major central banks, particularly the Federal Reserve. At the time of writing, gold traded just above $1,940, retreating from recent highs.

The US Dollar gained momentum, benefiting from increased demand amid concerns over geopolitical risks and market volatility. The strengthening USD added further pressure on gold prices, making the non-yielding metal less attractive to investors. As the EUR/USD exchange rate fluctuated around 1.0900, market participants remain focused on the potential for further rate hikes, with the Fed's stance continuing to shape market sentiment in the coming weeks.
