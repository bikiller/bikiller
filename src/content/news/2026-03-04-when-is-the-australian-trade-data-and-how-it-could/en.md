The Australian Bureau of Statistics will release January's trade data on Thursday at 00:30 GMT, with analysts anticipating an increase in the trade surplus to AUD 3,900 million month-on-month, up from December's AUD 3,373 million. This expected widening could bolster the Australian dollar against the USD, potentially impacting the AUD/USD currency pair as traders react to the news.

A stronger trade surplus often signals a healthy economy, which may lead to upward pressure on the exchange rate. If the data exceeds expectations, we could see the AUD strengthen further, challenging key resistance levels against the USD. Conversely, a disappointing figure might prompt a sell-off, with traders reevaluating their positions in the AUD/USD pair as market sentiment shifts.
