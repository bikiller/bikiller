WTI oil prices surged on Monday, climbing 5.90% to $95.70 per barrel as tensions surrounding Iran escalated. Renewed fears of a potential US blockade raised concerns over supply disruptions, driving market volatility. While the price has gained, it remains below the critical $100 mark, following last week’s sharp fluctuations in the oil market. 

The rising oil prices have implications for the USD, especially as traders monitor potential shifts in inflation and interest rate expectations. As the price of crude oil climbs, the USD could experience increased pressure, potentially impacting the EUR/USD exchange rate and broader market sentiment. The volatility in oil prices continues to influence global currency trading, making it crucial for forex traders to stay alert to any further geopolitical developments.
