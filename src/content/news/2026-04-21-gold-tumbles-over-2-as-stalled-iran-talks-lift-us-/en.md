Gold (XAU/USD) saw a sharp decline of over 2% on Tuesday, pressured by a lack of confirmation regarding a second round of US-Iran talks in Pakistan. This uncertainty fueled the US Dollar's strength, driving up yields. The price of gold dropped as low as $1,930, reflecting investor hesitation amid geopolitical concerns.

Meanwhile, Crude Oil prices gained slightly, benefiting the Greenback due to its positive correlation with WTI. The rise in oil prices further supported the USD, as traders viewed it as a sign of growing global demand. As the EUR/USD traded lower near the 1.0900 level, the market sentiment shifted towards the US Dollar, creating pressure on gold and other commodities.
