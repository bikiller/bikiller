West Texas Intermediate (WTI) US Oil extended its rally on Tuesday, trading near $98.00 per barrel at the time of writing. The benchmark crude climbed 3.21% on the day, marking its strongest level since mid-April. The move reflects renewed demand in USD-denominated commodities, with oil markets tracking broader risk sentiment across global trading sessions.

Market sentiment remains under pressure from a prolonged closure in the Strait of Hormuz, which has intensified fears of a global supply shock. As one of the world’s key oil transit routes, any disruption has a direct impact on crude futures pricing and energy security expectations. Traders continue to factor in tighter supply conditions, supporting bullish momentum in WTI despite broader macro uncertainty.

Looking ahead, elevated oil prices are likely to ripple through forex markets, influencing USD strength and cross-currency flows, including EUR and other risk-sensitive currencies. Higher crude costs may add inflationary pressure, potentially shaping central bank expectations and trading strategies across major currency pairs. For now, WTI’s breakout above $98 keeps commodity-linked volatility elevated.
