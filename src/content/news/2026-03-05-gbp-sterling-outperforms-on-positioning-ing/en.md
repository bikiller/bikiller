Sterling has shown notable resilience against major currencies, defying the broad strength of the USD. According to Chris Turner at ING, the Pound’s outperformance is particularly evident against the Euro, with the EUR/GBP currency pair experiencing a sharp decline during recent deleveraging. Traders attribute this move to stretched positioning, as market expectations for Bank of England easing have eased, reducing downward pressure on the Pound.  

The GBP’s rally reflects a shift in trading sentiment, as investors adjust long Euro-short Sterling positions. Technical levels indicate that EUR/GBP dropped swiftly through key support zones, signaling a potential recalibration of risk appetite. The move highlights how positioning imbalances can amplify currency pair volatility, even amid overarching USD strength.  

Looking ahead, market watchers suggest that Sterling’s momentum could persist if risk sentiment remains supportive and expectations of central bank easing stay muted. Exchange rate dynamics now favor the Pound in short-term trading, while EUR/GBP levels may continue to test lower thresholds. Traders are closely monitoring flows for indications of further unwinding and the impact on broader forex markets.
