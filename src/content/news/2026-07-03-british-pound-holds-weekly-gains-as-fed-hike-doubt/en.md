The British Pound steadied against the US Dollar on Friday, with the GBP/USD currency pair consolidating around 1.3350, broadly unchanged on the session. Despite the pause, the exchange rate remains on track to finish the week with gains of more than 1%, reflecting persistent dollar softness and improved risk appetite in forex trading across global markets.

Market sentiment has shifted as investors increasingly doubt the Federal Reserve will proceed with another interest rate hike at its September meeting. The fading USD support has weighed on the US Dollar, lifting major currency pairs such as GBP/USD and influencing broader FX flows across EUR and other majors, as traders reassess monetary policy expectations.

From a trading perspective, the pound’s consolidation near 1.3350 suggests a temporary equilibrium after recent volatility. Forex participants are closely watching incoming US macro data for direction, as renewed divergence in central bank expectations could trigger fresh moves in the exchange rate and define short-term positioning in the currency pair.
