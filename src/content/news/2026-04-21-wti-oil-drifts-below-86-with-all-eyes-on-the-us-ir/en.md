WTI crude oil slipped to $85.75 per barrel on Tuesday, holding below Monday’s peak of $88.50 as investors closely monitor developments in the US-Iran peace talks. The pullback came amid softer risk sentiment, with traders weighing potential geopolitical outcomes that could influence global energy supply. The USD showed mild strength, supporting the commodity’s downward pressure as the greenback traded near recent highs against the EUR and JPY.  

Currency markets mirrored the cautious mood, with the EUR/USD pair retreating to 1.0950 amid a firming USD. Market participants highlighted that any positive breakthrough in the talks could ease oil supply concerns, potentially pushing WTI higher, while setbacks might fuel further volatility. Traders are keeping a close eye on technical support levels around $85, with resistance near $88.50 acting as a short-term ceiling.  

Trading activity suggests the market is digesting geopolitical risks alongside macroeconomic signals. USD/JPY held around 133.50 as investors balanced interest rate expectations against safe-haven flows. Analysts noted that oil price movements often ripple through energy-linked currencies, influencing pairs like CAD/USD and NOK/USD. With key levels in focus, traders are positioning cautiously, awaiting clearer signals from both diplomatic developments and broader currency trends.
