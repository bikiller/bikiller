The AUD/USD currency pair struggled to maintain momentum on Wednesday, drifting lower after the release of Australia’s consumer price index. Traders noted the pair’s inability to breach the 0.7200 resistance level, keeping the exchange rate under pressure in the Asian session. Market participants cited the CPI data as a key factor weighing on the Australian dollar, while USD strength continued to influence broader forex sentiment.  

Investors are now turning their attention to the upcoming U.S. Federal Reserve decision, which could dictate the direction of major currency pairs. With trading volumes picking up, any shift in Fed policy or forward guidance may trigger renewed volatility for AUD/USD, EUR/USD, and other widely watched pairs. Analysts suggest that the pair’s current range highlights the market’s cautious stance ahead of U.S. interest rate signals.  

Technical watchers emphasize that a sustained move above 0.7200 could pave the way for a short-term recovery in the Australian dollar, whereas a break below near-term support may intensify USD-led pressure. Currency traders remain focused on macroeconomic indicators, balancing domestic inflation data with global central bank policies, as the forex market positions itself ahead of key announcements in the coming sessions.
