The British Pound showed limited movement against the US Dollar on Friday, as GBP/USD hovered around 1.3362. The currency pair initially spiked after the release of softer-than-expected US jobs data, which underscored slowing momentum in the labor market. Traders remain cautious, with the exchange rate struggling to maintain direction amid mixed sentiment, leaving the pair on track for a third consecutive weekly decline.  

Market participants are weighing the impact of the weak US employment figures on Federal Reserve policy. While softer jobs numbers typically signal reduced pressure for aggressive USD interest rate hikes, the GBP’s upside remains capped by domestic uncertainty and broader risk sentiment. The brief rally in GBP/USD failed to break key technical resistance, suggesting that short-term trading opportunities may be limited.  

Analysts note that the pound could continue consolidating around current levels unless either UK economic data or US labor statistics provide a fresh directional cue. With the USD remaining supported by global demand for safe-haven assets and EUR/USD also exhibiting subdued volatility, traders are focusing on next week’s economic calendar for potential catalysts. The currency pair’s current trajectory hints at further consolidation, with 1.3350–1.3380 emerging as critical support and resistance zones.
