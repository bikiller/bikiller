Gold (XAU/USD) remains under pressure, hovering below the $4,800 mark as it struggles to sustain gains from its modest rebound. The metal briefly recovered from a one-week low near $4,737-$4,738 recorded during Monday’s Asian session, but renewed selling interest has capped upside. Traders are weighing geopolitical risks, notably heightened US-Iran tensions, against firmer US dollar strength that continues to influence exchange rates.  

The greenback’s resilience, driven by rising US bond yields, supports the USD across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, limiting gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset. Analysts note that trading patterns reflect caution, with investors reluctant to chase higher levels amid persistent market volatility. The intraday movement suggests gold is struggling to break above key resistance near $4,800, leaving short-term momentum subdued.  

Market participants are closely monitoring economic data and geopolitical developments for directional cues. While gold retains underlying demand as an inflation hedge, the interplay of a stronger USD, rising Treasury yields, and regional tensions is shaping a challenging environment for bullion. Exchange rates remain sensitive, with fluctuations in the USD impacting both trading strategies and risk sentiment across global currency markets.
