Heightened risks around the Strait of Hormuz and Bab El-Mandeb are reshaping the outlook for Saudi crude exports, according to TD Securities Senior Commodity Strategist Ryan McKay. The two maritime chokepoints handle a significant share of global oil flows, and any disruption could tighten supply expectations. Energy market volatility often spills into forex trading, influencing USD-linked currency pairs and shifting exchange rate dynamics.

McKay notes that Saudi Arabia’s bypass infrastructure, including pipelines that redirect crude away from vulnerable sea lanes, plays a critical role in limiting export disruptions. However, these routes have finite capacity, meaning prolonged instability could still constrain global supply. Such risks tend to support oil prices, reinforcing demand for petro-currencies while also affecting major pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY through shifts in risk sentiment.

For currency traders, tighter oil supply expectations can amplify USD volatility as energy costs feed into inflation outlooks and central-bank policy expectations. If shipping disruptions escalate, markets may see stronger trading flows into commodity-linked currencies while safe-haven demand lifts the US dollar. As a result, forex participants are closely watching geopolitical developments around these strategic chokepoints.
