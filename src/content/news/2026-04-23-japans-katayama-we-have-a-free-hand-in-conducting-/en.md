During the European trading session on Thursday, Japan’s Finance Minister Satsuki Katayama stated that the government retains a "free hand" to conduct covert interventions against one-sided speculative moves targeting the Japanese Yen (JPY). This remark comes amid heightened volatility in the USD/JPY exchange rate, which has seen traders reacting to global economic uncertainties and shifts in monetary policy.

Katayama's comments underscore Japan's commitment to stabilizing the yen, particularly as it faces pressures against major currencies like the USD and EUR. As speculative trading intensifies, the Finance Ministry is poised to act to curb excessive fluctuations, signaling to the market that it is prepared to intervene if necessary to protect the currency’s value.
