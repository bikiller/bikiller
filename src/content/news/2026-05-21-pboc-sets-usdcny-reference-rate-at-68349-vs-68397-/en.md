The People’s Bank of China (PBOC) set the USD/CNY currency pair central parity at 6.8349 for Thursday’s trading session, compared with the previous fix of 6.8397. The latest reference rate also came in above Reuters’ estimate of 6.7955, pointing to a slightly weaker yuan versus expectations despite a modest strengthening from the prior day’s level, shaping early FX sentiment.

In trading, the USD/CNY exchange rate remains tightly managed within the daily band, and the latest PBOC setting suggests continued preference for stability over sharp appreciation. The marginal move lower versus the previous fix signals limited yuan support, while the gap against market expectations highlights cautious policy calibration. This may influence broader Asian currency trading, particularly risk-sensitive pairs linked to the US dollar.

Global forex markets will watch the fixing as a signal for policy stance, alongside USD strength dynamics and broader EUR and Asia FX performance. Offshore CNH liquidity and onshore-offshore spreads may adjust as traders reassess direction for the Chinese currency. The PBOC’s daily reference rate continues to act as a key anchor for USD trading sentiment and near-term exchange rate volatility in the region.
