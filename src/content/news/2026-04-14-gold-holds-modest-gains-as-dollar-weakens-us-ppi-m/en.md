Gold (XAU/USD) is holding modest gains on Tuesday, trading with a mild positive bias as the US Dollar weakens. The precious metal is supported by a softer USD amid investor optimism over the potential for renewed US-Iran negotiations. As of the latest trading data, XAU/USD is up by 0.2%, reaching $1,945 per ounce. The Dollar’s weakness is driven by softer-than-expected US Producer Price Index (PPI) data, which fell short of market forecasts.

The disappointing PPI release, which showed a rise of only 0.1% in March, has prompted market speculation that the Federal Reserve might ease its aggressive tightening stance. This, combined with easing geopolitical tensions, has encouraged risk sentiment, helping gold maintain its ground. The EUR/USD currency pair is also benefiting from the Dollar's weakness, trading higher at 1.0950, reflecting broad USD softness across the forex market. With investors focusing on potential global negotiations, gold remains supported above key technical levels.
