EUR/USD extended its upward move on Tuesday, marking a second straight session of gains as the US Dollar softened ahead of the Federal Reserve’s interest rate decision. The currency pair’s exchange rate found support from easing USD demand, with traders gradually shifting focus away from geopolitical tensions, including the US-Iran conflict, toward central bank policy expectations.

Market participants are increasingly positioning around the Fed’s upcoming announcement, anticipating signals on the future path of interest rates. A more cautious or dovish tone from policymakers could weigh further on the USD, supporting continued upside in EUR/USD trading. Meanwhile, expectations surrounding the European Central Bank also remain in focus, adding another layer of volatility to the currency pair.

In the near term, EUR/USD price action is likely to remain sensitive to macroeconomic cues and central bank guidance. Traders are closely monitoring technical levels and momentum indicators, as the pair’s recent gains suggest improving sentiment toward the EUR. Sustained USD weakness could reinforce bullish pressure, while any hawkish surprise from the Fed may trigger a reversal in the exchange rate trajectory.
