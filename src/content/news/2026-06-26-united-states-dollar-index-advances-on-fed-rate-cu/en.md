United States Dollar Index (DXY) advances, trims previous losses, trades around 101.50 during Asian hours on Friday. Measures USD vs six major currencies. Rise comes after prior weakness, with traders reassessing Federal Reserve rate cut bets influencing USD exchange rate. Broader market tone remains sensitive to US monetary policy outlook, driving intraday volatility in currency trading sessions across Asia.  

USD strength is supported by shifting expectations for Fed easing. Markets reassess timing and scale of rate cuts, boosting dollar demand in forex trading. Major currency pairs such as EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and USD/JPY see renewed pressure as the greenback stabilizes. Improved sentiment helps the index recover, while volatility persists as traders adjust positions ahead of upcoming US data releases.  

In the short term, the DXY’s recovery near 101.50 signals cautious USD consolidation rather than a clear trend reversal. Currency traders continue to watch Fed signals and macro data for direction. If rate cut expectations intensify, upside may be capped; otherwise, the dollar could extend gains against major forex counterparts. Market participants remain focused on interest rate differentials driving exchange rate moves.
