EUR/GBP continued its downward slide on Wednesday, trading around 0.8680, marking its lowest point since March 31. The pair weakened as UK inflation data surpassed expectations, fueling demand for the British Pound (GBP) and pressuring the Euro (EUR). This marked the second consecutive day of losses for EUR/GBP, with traders reacting to the latest Consumer Price Index (CPI) report.

The move lower is also supported by technical signals, with EUR/GBP slipping below the key 200-day simple moving average (SMA), indicating further downside potential. As the British Pound strengthens on the inflation data, market participants are cautious, with the exchange rate likely to face continued pressure if the trend persists.
