Japanese yen gained ground against the US dollar in early European trading on Wednesday, with the USD/JPY currency pair slipping toward 160.25. The exchange rate reflected cautious sentiment as traders reduced exposure ahead of a key risk event. In forex trading, the pair remained under pressure, with USD softness emerging as participants adjusted positions across major currency pairs during the session.

Market participants remained on the sidelines as attention turns to the US Federal Reserve interest rate decision later on Wednesday under new Chair Kevin Warsh. Uncertainty over the policy outlook has limited conviction in directional trading, keeping volatility subdued across major currency pairs and sustaining demand for the Japanese yen as a safe-haven currency.

Analysts note that USD/JPY hovering near 160.25 remains a technically sensitive level, with traders watching for potential intervention risk and sharp moves if Fed guidance surprises. The currency pair may continue to consolidate in a tight range, as USD strength and yen demand balance each other amid cautious forex trading conditions.
