NZD/USD currency pair climbed on Monday, trading around 0.5840, up 0.17% as the US dollar weakened broadly. The exchange rate benefited from improved risk sentiment after markets reacted positively to a framework agreement between the United States and Iran aimed at de-escalating tensions and ending the conflict, supporting higher-yielding currencies like the New Zealand dollar.

Broader USD weakness persisted as investors positioned ahead of a key Federal Reserve decision, with traders reassessing interest rate expectations. The softer dollar environment supported demand for the NZD in forex trading, while risk-on flows lifted commodity-linked currencies. Market participants remain cautious, however, as volatility may increase around the central bank announcement and any shift in policy guidance.

From a technical perspective, NZD/USD is consolidating near the 0.5840 level, with traders watching for a decisive break to determine near-term direction. Sustained USD softness could extend gains, while resistance and profit-taking may cap upside momentum. The outlook remains closely tied to Fed expectations and evolving geopolitical sentiment in the near-term outlook.
