The US Dollar Index (DXY) edged lower toward the 98.00 level on Thursday as easing tensions in the Middle East boosted risk appetite. Gains in equities and oil markets reflected optimism after Axios reported that Washington and Tehran are inching closer to a diplomatic agreement. While resilient US economic data underpinned the dollar, the broader sentiment shift favored higher-yielding currencies, keeping the DXY from extending losses.  

EUR/USD found support around 1.0900 as traders rotated into the euro, responding to the improved geopolitical outlook. USD/JPY held near 145.50, with the yen benefiting modestly from the softer dollar and risk-on flows. Currency pair movements remained range-bound, reflecting caution ahead of the US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report, which could reset expectations for Federal Reserve policy and influence broader trading strategies.  

Market participants are weighing the implications for USD liquidity, with lower DXY levels potentially supporting commodity-linked currencies like AUD and NZD. Traders are positioning ahead of Friday’s NFP release, which could trigger volatility across major exchange rates. Technical levels around 98.00 for the DXY and key support zones for EUR/USD and USD/JPY are under close watch as forex markets navigate a mix of macroeconomic stability and geopolitical relief.
