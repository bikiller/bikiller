Silver (XAG/USD) is retreating on Tuesday, trading around $76.00, down 2.13% on the day. The precious metal is under pressure as a firmer US Dollar boosts exchange rates, making silver less attractive to holders of other currencies. Investors are also reacting to rising US Treasury yields, which are drawing capital toward fixed-income instruments over non-yielding metals.

Market sentiment in forex and commodities is being influenced by the USD’s strength against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Traders note that a higher USD exchange rate tends to weigh on dollar-denominated commodities, including silver and gold, as international buyers face higher costs. Short-term trading strategies are adjusting to this backdrop, with some speculators trimming positions in silver ahead of potential further USD appreciation.

Technical analysts highlight that the $76.00 level could act as a near-term support, while the downside remains vulnerable if the USD continues its momentum. With yields rising, the opportunity cost of holding silver is climbing, prompting caution among forex traders and commodity investors. Observers are keeping a close eye on central bank signals and currency pair movements to gauge the next phase of market volatility.
