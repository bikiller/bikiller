Gold (XAU/USD) is holding onto its modest gains as the US Dollar (USD) weakens, following growing optimism in global markets. The current exchange rate for gold stands at $1,960, reflecting a steady recovery after its recent dip. The shift in market sentiment comes amid fresh diplomatic efforts toward a US-Iran ceasefire, easing geopolitical tensions that had previously pressured risk assets.

The decline in USD strength has also impacted other major currencies, including the EUR/USD, which trades at 1.0900. Traders are adjusting their positions, responding to potential de-escalation in the US-Iran conflict. As a result, safe-haven demand for gold remains strong, with technical support seen around the $1,940 mark. If the ceasefire talks progress, further weakening of the USD could drive gold toward $1,970 or higher.
