GBP/USD has slipped below the 1.3300 mark, with the currency pair facing increased pressure after UK GDP showed no growth in January. This unexpected stagnation has raised concerns over stagflation risks, leaving the British economy struggling to gain momentum. The lack of growth puts output below the Bank of England’s Q1 projections, signaling potential economic difficulties ahead.

The flat GDP result leaves GBP/USD vulnerable to further declines as traders adjust expectations. With the UK economy underperforming, the pound faces continued headwinds against the USD, especially as global market sentiment remains cautious. As the currency pair hovers around the key 1.3300 level, any further downside could see it testing lower technical levels, reinforcing the bearish outlook for GBP.
