Gold prices (XAU/USD) fell sharply to around $4,800 on Thursday, erasing a two-day rally. The decline occurred during the early Asian session as traders reacted to a surge in oil prices, which dampened expectations for a rate cut. The initial boost in gold was driven by escalating tensions in the Middle East, spurring safe-haven demand, but the shift in oil dynamics reversed the momentum.

As oil prices surged, the prospect of prolonged inflation risks weighed on market sentiment, limiting hopes for a dovish stance from central banks. This shift impacted the broader forex market, with the USD strengthening as inflation concerns pressured risk assets. The XAU/USD exchange rate now faces resistance at the $4,800 level, leaving traders to assess whether gold can regain upward momentum or face further downside pressure in the coming sessions.
