Gold prices (XAU/USD) saw a slight pullback on Monday, trading near the 5,000 mark in a quiet session. After last week's sharp decline, where the precious metal fell from recent highs, the market has shown relatively limited movement. This consolidation reflects a cooling in demand for safe-haven assets, as investors adjust their positions.

The XAU/USD currency pair continues to face pressure amid a stable USD, which has supported the greenback against a basket of major currencies. While gold remains sensitive to broader market risk sentiment, the lack of fresh catalysts and a pause in market volatility left gold trading in a narrow range. Investors are closely monitoring the exchange rate, with expectations for further fluctuations based on upcoming economic data.
