Silver (XAG/USD) climbed 2.2% in late Asian trading on Thursday, reaching around $80.80 as market optimism surrounding Iran bolstered investor sentiment. The currency pair is eyeing a retest of its four-week high at $81.00, signaling renewed momentum after recent consolidation. Traders noted that rising geopolitical confidence has spurred demand for precious metals, while the USD showed signs of mixed performance against major currencies such as the EUR.  

Analysts suggest that the silver exchange rate’s upward trajectory reflects broader market positioning ahead of key economic data. As investors monitor USD strength, movements in other commodities and major forex pairs are influencing trading behavior. The approach toward $81 could attract short-term speculative flows, with technical levels around $80.50–$81 acting as immediate support and resistance for traders seeking entry points.  

Market implications extend beyond silver, as metals and forex traders reassess risk amid geopolitical optimism. With XAG/USD testing multi-week highs, volatility may remain elevated, particularly against USD and EUR pairs. Participants are balancing exposure across currencies and commodities, looking for alignment between technical signals and macroeconomic factors to guide trading decisions in the coming sessions.
