Gold rebounded above $4,550 in early Asian trading on Tuesday, recovering some ground after hitting a one-and-a-half-month low. The XAU/USD pair climbed to around $4,565 as the US Dollar weakened, reflecting softer demand for the greenback across major currency pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Traders noted that the drop in USD exchange rates boosted safe-haven buying in gold.

Market participants highlighted that the modest rally in gold comes amid a broader currency trading environment where the USD is losing traction. Technical analysts pointed to the $4,550 support level as key, while momentum toward $4,580 could attract further short-term buying. The shift in sentiment also influenced commodities and currencies, with traders adjusting positions against the backdrop of central bank signals and economic data.

The recovery suggests cautious optimism for gold amid ongoing market volatility. Currency traders are monitoring the interplay between USD strength, interest rate expectations, and geopolitical developments, which continue to sway XAU/USD. For now, gold trading reflects a balancing act between safe-haven demand and fluctuations in global exchange rates, signaling potential consolidation before the next directional move.
