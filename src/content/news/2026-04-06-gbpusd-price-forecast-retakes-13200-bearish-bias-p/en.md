The GBP/USD currency pair rebounded above the 1.3200 mark on Monday, reversing a two-day slide as dip-buyers stepped in around 1.3175 during the Asian session. Traders noted the slight recovery in the exchange rate, with the pound gaining modest ground against the USD amid ongoing market caution. Despite the bounce, the pair remains under pressure from broader risk-off sentiment and persistent geopolitical uncertainties.  

Market participants are weighing the impact of heightened geopolitical tensions on global trading flows, which continue to weigh on sterling. The USD has shown resilience against most major peers, including the EUR, as investors seek safe-haven assets. Analysts highlight that while GBP/USD has found short-term support near 1.3175, momentum indicators still point toward a bearish bias in the near term.  

Technically, the pair’s retest of 1.3200 offers limited relief, with further declines possible if risk sentiment deteriorates. Traders are watching key levels closely, noting that any sustained move above 1.3220 could signal a temporary recovery, while a drop below 1.3170 might accelerate selling. Overall, GBP/USD trading remains sensitive to macro developments, central bank outlooks, and shifts in USD strength, underscoring caution for short-term strategies.
