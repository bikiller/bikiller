The NZD/USD currency pair climbed 0.48% on Thursday, rebounding sharply after finding support near the 200-day Simple Moving Average at 0.5836. The New Zealand Dollar extended gains for a second consecutive trading session, pushing above the key 0.5900 exchange rate level as traders reacted positively to the Reserve Bank of New Zealand’s hawkish pause on interest rates.

Market sentiment toward the Kiwi strengthened after the RBNZ signaled that inflation risks remain elevated, reducing expectations for near-term rate cuts. The stronger policy outlook boosted demand for the New Zealand Dollar against the USD, while broader forex trading activity reflected improving appetite for risk-sensitive currencies. Investors also monitored movements in EUR/USD and other major currency pairs for clues on global market direction.

From a technical perspective, NZD/USD broke out of its recent consolidation range, with momentum indicators pointing higher. The Relative Strength Index turned bullish, reinforcing expectations for additional upside toward the 0.5950 resistance zone. A sustained move above 0.5900 could encourage further buying interest, although traders remain cautious ahead of upcoming US economic data that may influence the US Dollar’s broader trajectory.
