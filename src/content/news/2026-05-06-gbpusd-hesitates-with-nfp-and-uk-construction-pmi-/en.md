GBP/USD edged higher on Wednesday, climbing roughly 0.4% to hover near 1.3595. The currency pair tested the 1.3645 level earlier in the session but struggled to sustain momentum above the psychologically important 1.3600 handle by the close. Traders cited cautious sentiment ahead of key economic releases, leaving the exchange rate range-bound despite attempts at a breakout.  

Market attention now turns to the US nonfarm payrolls (NFP) report and the UK construction PMI, both expected to influence short-term trading dynamics. The dollar showed mixed signals against major currencies, including EUR and GBP, as investors weigh the potential impact of strong employment data and sector-specific UK activity. Analysts note that the exchange rate’s reaction to these reports could set the tone for GBP/USD in the coming sessions.  

Technically, the pair’s failure to hold above 1.3600 signals resistance in the near term, while support around current levels could attract buying if risk sentiment remains stable. Traders remain focused on market drivers such as central bank commentary and macroeconomic indicators, balancing USD strength against ongoing GBP recovery efforts. Volatility is expected to pick up around these critical data releases, shaping trading strategies across forex desks.
