Oil prices have retraced back to pre-war levels, yet ING strategists Michiel Tukker and Benjamin Schroeder caution that the rally may be premature. They argue that market participants are pricing in an overly smooth and rapid restoration of supply conditions, overlooking persistent structural frictions that could limit the durability of the recovery in energy markets.

According to the ING analysts, the rebound in oil has been driven more by sentiment than by confirmed supply improvements, raising questions about how sustainable current pricing levels are. They suggest that traders may be underestimating lingering logistical bottlenecks and geopolitical risks that continue to shape expectations across global energy and currency markets.

In forex trading, oil stability often feeds into USD dynamics through inflation expectations and risk sentiment, with implications for EUR/USD exchange rate movements currency pair. If supply recovery disappoints, upside risks to energy prices could support the USD in certain cycles while increasing volatility for commodity-linked currencies and short-term trading strategies across global markets.
