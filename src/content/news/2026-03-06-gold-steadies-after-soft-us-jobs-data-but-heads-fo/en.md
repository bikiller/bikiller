Gold (XAU/USD) steadied on Friday after a turbulent session, recouping some ground following soft US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data. Earlier, prices had faced pressure as the US Dollar (USD) strengthened, but weaker-than-expected job growth eased the USD and Treasury yields, giving gold some support. Traders noted the metal was still heading for a weekly loss despite the bounce, reflecting broader caution in commodity markets.  

The NFP report showed employment gains below forecasts, prompting a shift in currency pair dynamics. The USD slipped against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, influencing trading strategies across forex markets. As a result, XAU/USD found traction near key technical support levels, while traders weighed implications for Federal Reserve policy and interest rate expectations.  

Analysts expect gold to remain sensitive to upcoming economic data and currency fluctuations. A weaker USD could sustain upside momentum for XAU/USD, though lingering profit-taking and risk sentiment may cap gains. Market participants continue monitoring exchange rates, Treasury yields, and macroeconomic indicators for signals on near-term trading opportunities, with gold’s weekly trajectory remaining under close watch.
