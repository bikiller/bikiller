Gold (XAU/USD) is experiencing a downturn on Tuesday, trading with a negative bias as it reacts to stronger-than-expected US economic data. Investors are currently adopting a cautious approach, hesitating to make significant moves amid the uncertainty surrounding the potential resumption of US-Iran peace talks, particularly following heightened tensions in the Strait of Hormuz over the weekend.

The lack of strong directional momentum in gold prices reflects broader market sentiment, with traders weighing the implications of Federal Reserve commentary. Former Fed official Kevin Warsh has called for a “regime change” at the central bank, suggesting potential shifts in monetary policy that could impact the USD. As gold struggles for direction, its fluctuations remain closely tied to developments in the geopolitical landscape and the evolving exchange rate dynamics between the USD and other major currencies like the EUR.
