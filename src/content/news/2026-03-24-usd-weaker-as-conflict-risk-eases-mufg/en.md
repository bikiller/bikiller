The US Dollar remains under pressure following a sharp sell-off driven by easing tensions in the Middle East. The Dollar Index struggled to break through the 100.00 level, retreating to 98.88, reflecting investor sentiment shifting away from risk aversion. This shift has impacted the USD’s performance across various currency pairs, with the EUR/USD gaining traction as the Euro strengthened in comparison.

The market's focus on geopolitical stability continues to influence the USD’s direction, with de-escalation in the Middle East providing relief to global markets. As the risk of conflict subsides, traders are adjusting their positions, which has further pressured the greenback. For now, the USD remains vulnerable, with short-term trading likely to center around key technical levels, including the 98.88 mark on the Dollar Index.
