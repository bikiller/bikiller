The Canadian Dollar nudged slightly higher on Monday, with the USD/CAD currency pair trading around 1.3740, down a modest 0.05% on the day. Market participants are weighing geopolitical developments, as easing tensions in the Middle East have prompted a soft pullback in the US Dollar against major peers including the EUR and JPY. Traders are closely monitoring technical support and resistance levels ahead of key economic data.

Investor focus is shifting to upcoming Canadian CPI figures and the FOMC minutes, which could provide fresh guidance on monetary policy. Any surprises in inflation data or Federal Reserve commentary may trigger notable moves in the exchange rate, influencing both short-term trading strategies and broader market sentiment.

Technically, USD/CAD remains near recent intraday lows, suggesting the Canadian Dollar is holding modest gains amid cautious trading. Forex traders are balancing the impact of the softer USD with potential market reactions to economic releases later this week. Momentum indicators and volume patterns suggest that volatility could increase as the market awaits the next catalysts for directional moves.
