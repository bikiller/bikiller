West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil is trading around $68.65 per barrel on Friday, posting a 0.30% daily gain as it recovers part of recent losses. The rebound comes as the US Dollar softens following weaker-than-expected US employment data, improving appetite for USD-denominated commodities and supporting broader trading sentiment across energy and forex-linked markets.

Stronger volatility in currency markets followed the release, with the US Dollar Index easing and major currency pairs such as EUR/USD edging higher. Oil prices also drew support from lingering geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which continue to raise supply risk premiums. Traders are closely watching macroeconomic indicators, as expectations for interest rate cuts influence both forex and energy trading dynamics.

Looking ahead, WTI remains sensitive to USD movements and shifting expectations in the exchange rate environment, particularly across USD and EUR currency pairs. Market participants anticipate continued range-bound trading as traders balance demand concerns with geopolitical risks. Short-term price action is likely to remain volatile, with sentiment driven by incoming US data and broader risk appetite.
