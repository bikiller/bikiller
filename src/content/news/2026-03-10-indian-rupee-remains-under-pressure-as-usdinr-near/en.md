The Indian Rupee (INR) continues to face significant pressure as the USD/INR currency pair rebounds, recovering from minor losses seen in the previous session. On Monday, the pair surged to a new all-time high of 92.81, reflecting heightened selling activity on the Indian Rupee. This marked a notable milestone in the exchange rate as INR struggles against the US Dollar.

The rise in USD/INR comes amid increasing market risk aversion, fueled by geopolitical uncertainties and a global tightening of financial conditions. Furthermore, elevated oil prices have compounded INR's decline, given India's reliance on oil imports. With this backdrop, the INR remains vulnerable to further volatility, leaving traders cautious about potential risks in the currency markets.
