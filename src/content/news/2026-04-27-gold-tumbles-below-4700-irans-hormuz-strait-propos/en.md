Gold (XAU/USD) dropped to around $4,685 in early Asian trading on Tuesday, marking a sharp decline as market participants await the US Federal Reserve’s (Fed) interest rate decision. The price movement comes amid a broader "wait-and-see" sentiment, as traders adjust positions in anticipation of potential shifts in US monetary policy. 

Simultaneously, the ongoing tensions in the Middle East, particularly Iran’s proposal to close the Hormuz Strait, are keeping investors on edge. These geopolitical risks, combined with the Fed's looming rate decision, have created a volatile environment for both gold and other major currencies. As traders monitor both events, gold's near-term outlook remains uncertain, with many awaiting clearer signals from the Fed before making significant moves.
