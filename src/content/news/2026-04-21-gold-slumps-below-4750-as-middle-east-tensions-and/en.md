Gold prices (XAU/USD) dropped below $4,750, trading around $4,720 during the early Asian session on Wednesday. The decline comes amid escalating tensions in the Middle East, particularly concerns related to the Strait of Hormuz, which are fueling inflation worries. This shift has prompted traders to reassess their positions in the precious metal.

Additionally, robust US Retail Sales data has strengthened the USD, reinforcing downward pressure on gold. As the exchange rate shifts, market participants may look for further clarity on economic indicators, which could influence trading strategies in both gold and broader currency markets. With the current geopolitical climate impacting commodity prices, investors are urged to stay vigilant as market dynamics evolve.
