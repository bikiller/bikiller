USD/JPY edged lower on Monday, trading around 146.80 as the Japanese Yen found modest support against a broadly softer US Dollar. Traders digested news of potential US-Iran ceasefire talks, which weighed on the greenback amid lingering geopolitical uncertainty. The currency pair struggled to sustain earlier gains, with market participants closely monitoring developments that could influence safe-haven flows into the Yen.  

Market sentiment was further impacted by the latest US ISM manufacturing PMI, which softened to 48.6, missing expectations and signaling subdued business activity. The weaker-than-expected data added pressure on the USD, prompting some traders to scale back long positions. Technical analysts noted resistance near 147.20, while immediate support hovered around 146.50, highlighting a range-bound trading environment for the pair.  

Broader forex markets saw the USD retreat against major peers, with EUR/USD climbing toward 1.1050 as the euro benefited from the Dollar’s softness. Investors remained cautious ahead of potential policy signals from the Federal Reserve and updates on US-Iran diplomatic talks. With geopolitical headlines and economic indicators driving volatility, the USD/JPY exchange rate is likely to remain sensitive to both fundamental and technical cues in the coming sessions.
