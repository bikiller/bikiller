USD/JPY is hovering just below the 160.00 level as market attention shifts toward the latest data from the Bank of Japan (BoJ). The central bank has released new underlying CPI indicators, which remain below the 2% target, signaling ongoing challenges in Japan's inflationary environment. However, the data shows signs of gradual convergence toward the target, hinting at potential policy normalization in the near future.

This shift in expectations is driving volatility in the USD/JPY exchange rate, with traders speculating on the BoJ’s upcoming moves. As the currency pair remains near the key 160.00 mark, investors are closely watching the April meeting for signals that could impact the yen's performance. The USD’s relative strength continues to play a role, but the BoJ's actions will likely be the decisive factor for USD/JPY's trajectory in the coming weeks.
