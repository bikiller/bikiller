Oil prices tumbled sharply below $100 on easing geopolitical tensions, with Brent and WTI both retreating as a US–Iran two‑week ceasefire dampened fears of Middle East supply disruptions. Traders reacted quickly, pushing Brent down toward $98 a barrel and WTI near $95, as the agreement reduced the risk premium that had supported higher oil valuations. Market sentiment shifted, prompting portfolio adjustments across energy and currency markets.  

The decline in crude has influenced FX trading, as USD pairs reacted to lower oil‑linked inflation expectations. Commodity‑linked currencies such as the CAD and NOK saw mild weakness against the USD, while the EUR/USD exchange rate hovered near 1.095, reflecting a mix of risk appetite and dollar strength. Analysts note that currency pairs closely tied to energy prices may continue to see heightened volatility in the near term.  

Investors are weighing the broader implications for global markets, with traders monitoring whether the ceasefire will hold and how it might affect energy exports. Technical levels around $100 for Brent and $96 for WTI are likely to act as psychological barriers, guiding short‑term trading strategies. FX desks are adjusting hedges and recalibrating exposure as oil-linked inflows into USD assets moderate, signaling a cautious but calmer trading environment.
