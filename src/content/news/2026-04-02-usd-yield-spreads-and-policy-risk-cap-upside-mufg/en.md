The US Dollar has struggled to maintain its momentum following the Middle East-driven energy shock. While the initial surge in demand for USD as a safe-haven currency boosted its value, optimism surrounding a swift resolution of the conflict has curbed this strength. As a result, the USD has faced downward pressure, with yield spreads moving against the currency and limiting its upside potential.

Additionally, an increase in US policy risk premiums has further weighed on the USD. The EUR/USD exchange rate, which has traded around 1.0900, reflects the growing headwinds for the Dollar. Traders are shifting their focus towards global economic stability, and concerns about domestic policy uncertainty are preventing the USD from gaining significant ground. The changing yield differentials and political risks are pivotal in shaping the currency’s near-term outlook.
