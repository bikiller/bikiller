AUD/USD edged higher on Tuesday, climbing 0.25% to hover near 0.7185 as the market digested the latest move from the Reserve Bank of Australia. The currency pair found firm support around 0.7150 during the European session, helping traders regain confidence in AUD positions against the USD. Momentum remained cautious, reflecting uncertainty about the RBA’s future policy path and broader risk sentiment in global trading.  

The exchange rate’s modest rise was underpinned by the central bank’s interest rate hike, which bolstered the Australian dollar in the short term. Market participants noted that while the AUD gained against the USD, the advance was contained by persistent strength in the greenback and lingering concerns over global economic data. EUR/USD and other major currency pairs also experienced mixed reactions, highlighting the USD’s continued influence on global trading dynamics.  

Technical analysts pointed to the 0.7150 support level as a key floor, with the recent 0.7185 highs offering near-term resistance. Traders are weighing potential follow-up moves from the RBA against USD trends, keeping trading ranges relatively narrow. Any further volatility in commodity-linked currencies or shifts in risk appetite could dictate the next leg for AUD/USD, leaving the market poised for cautious but active trading.
