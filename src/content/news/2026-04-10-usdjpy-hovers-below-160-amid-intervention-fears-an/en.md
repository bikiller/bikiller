USD/JPY remains under pressure on Friday, trading just below the 160 level, as the Japanese Yen struggles to recover despite a softer US Dollar. The currency pair has largely erased earlier losses this week, with USD/JPY holding steady amid ongoing concerns over oil supply disruptions tied to Middle East tensions. These geopolitical risks continue to hinder the Yen's recovery, keeping the exchange rate elevated.

The Yen faces additional headwinds as the US Dollar stabilizes after recent fluctuations, but the broader market sentiment remains cautious. Oil price volatility, driven by Middle East instability, provides a floor for USD/JPY, preventing a significant pullback in the currency pair. As traders remain wary of further escalation in the region, the Japanese Yen is likely to remain under pressure in the short term.
