The Indian Rupee (INR) saw slight gains against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, with the USD/INR exchange rate dipping to around 93.28. The movement comes amid growing optimism about a potential permanent ceasefire between the United States and Iran, which has led to significant selling pressure on the USD. Traders are reacting to the possibility of easing tensions, weakening the greenback.

This shift in sentiment has led to a modest uptick in INR trading, as market participants adjust their positions. As the situation evolves, the USD/INR pair remains sensitive to geopolitical developments, with further fluctuations likely depending on news surrounding the US-Iran talks. The improving outlook for a truce is likely to continue influencing the USD's strength in global markets.
