WTI crude oil surged again on Monday as former President Donald Trump's rejection of a proposed Iran peace initiative fueled supply concerns. The benchmark rose past $87 per barrel, pushing the USD weaker against major currencies, with the EUR/USD exchange rate testing 1.1060. Traders are increasingly pricing in tight global supply, with the U.S. Dollar Index (DXY) trading slightly lower amid risk-on sentiment in forex markets.

Markets are now eyeing Wednesday’s EIA inventory release to gauge just how constrained U.S. crude stockpiles have become amid the ongoing Strait of Hormuz disruption. Analysts note that any surprise drawdown could drive further gains in oil prices, affecting energy-linked currency pairs such as USD/CAD and AUD/USD. Trading volumes in the dollar and euro have seen moderate upticks, reflecting positioning ahead of the data.

Technical charts show WTI holding above key support near $85, while resistance levels at $88.50 could define near-term momentum. FX traders are balancing oil-driven USD weakness against broader macro signals, with the EUR/USD hovering near its recent highs and GBP/USD testing 1.2750. The interplay of commodity shocks and currency flows suggests a volatile week ahead for both oil and forex markets.
