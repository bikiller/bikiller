US President Donald Trump on Wednesday stated that the International Energy Agency’s (IEA) recent decision to release oil from strategic reserves is expected to weigh on global crude prices. Traders reacted swiftly, with Brent and WTI futures retreating as the market priced in higher supply. The move also impacted risk sentiment, boosting the US dollar against major currencies as investors anticipated lower energy-driven inflation pressures.

In forex markets, the USD strengthened, with the EUR/USD currency pair testing support near 1.0950, reflecting cautious trading amid the oil-driven market shift. Commodity-linked currencies like the AUD and CAD softened as crude weakness undercut their exchange rates. Analysts highlighted that the technical rebound in USD/JPY above 132.50 could gain traction if oil prices continue to slide, influencing broader USD trading flows.

Market participants are weighing the longer-term implications for inflation and monetary policy. Lower oil prices may ease input costs, supporting the greenback, while currencies tied to energy exports face downward pressure. Traders are monitoring technical levels and economic data closely, with short-term volatility expected in forex pairs sensitive to commodity movements as the IEA release reshapes both oil and currency markets.
