West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil continued its upward momentum on Friday, climbing to $100.90 a barrel, a 3.13% gain that pushed it above the psychologically significant $100 mark and a fresh weekly high. Traders in energy and currency markets reacted to signals from former President Donald Trump that China may increase crude purchases, adding bullish sentiment amid ongoing Middle East supply concerns near the Strait of Hormuz.  

The rally in oil has ripple effects across forex markets, with the USD gaining strength against the EUR and other major currency pairs due to safe-haven flows. Rising crude prices often tighten inflation expectations, influencing central bank outlooks and shifting exchange rate dynamics. Investors watching USD/JPY, EUR/USD, and other pairs are factoring in energy-driven adjustments to carry trades and risk positioning.  

Market participants remain cautious as geopolitical tensions in the Gulf persist, keeping oil volatility high. WTI trading above $100 could pressure energy-importing economies, potentially impacting the EUR/USD and GBP/USD exchange rates. Traders are closely monitoring how sustained gains in crude might reshape USD liquidity and influence broader forex trading strategies in the coming sessions.
