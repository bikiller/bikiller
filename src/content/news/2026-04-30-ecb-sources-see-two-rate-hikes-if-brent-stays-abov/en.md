European Central Bank officials are preparing for at least two interest rate hikes this year, starting in June, according to sources speaking to Reuters. The move hinges on oil prices, with Brent crude holding above $100 per barrel. Markets are watching the euro closely, as any tightening by the ECB could strengthen the EUR against the USD, impacting currency pair trading and capital flows across global markets.  

The escalation in oil prices is linked to ongoing tensions in the Middle East, particularly the unresolved Iran conflict. Traders are pricing in higher energy costs, which could sustain inflationary pressures in the eurozone. As a result, the EUR/USD exchange rate has shown increased volatility, with technical levels near 1.10 attracting attention from forex participants and institutional investors alike.  

Analysts warn that the ECB’s anticipated hikes could further influence trading sentiment across major currency pairs. A firmer euro could pressure USD-based imports and commodities, while reinforcing EUR-denominated assets. Investors are positioning ahead of policy announcements, with momentum indicators and support-resistance zones being closely monitored, reflecting broader concerns over inflation, geopolitical risk, and global market stability.
