The USD/JPY currency pair remains under pressure near the 160 level, with the Japanese Yen continuing to struggle as the weakest G10 currency. As of the latest trading session, the pair is hovering just below the key 160 mark, driven by ongoing concerns over potential intervention by Japan's Ministry of Finance (MoF) to stem further weakness in the Yen. 

The Yen’s vulnerability reflects broader market uncertainty and policy risks, adding volatility to the pair’s movements. The USD has remained resilient amid these concerns, maintaining strength as traders watch for any signs of intervention. As the MoF continues to signal vigilance, market participants are closely monitoring the 160 threshold for potential breakouts or corrective actions.
