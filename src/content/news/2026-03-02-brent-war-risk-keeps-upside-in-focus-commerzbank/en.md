Brent crude oil saw only a modest reaction to the ongoing Middle East conflict and the partial closure of the Strait of Hormuz, briefly pushing prices above $80 before retreating. Despite these geopolitical risks, the upside remains in focus, with traders cautious but monitoring potential escalation. The market’s response has been contained so far, with oil holding steady as concerns persist.

Commerzbank's Chief Economist Dr. Jörg Krämer highlighted the significant risk factors, noting that the war in the region continues to fuel uncertainty in global energy markets. This instability has kept Brent crude in the spotlight, with traders weighing the potential for supply disruptions. The price of oil, although volatile, has not sustained any major breakouts, showing resilience against the broader market fluctuations in USD and EUR exchange rates.
