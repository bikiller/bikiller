The US Dollar Index (DXY), which tracks the greenback against six major currencies, continued its decline on Thursday, dipping below the 98.00 mark. As of the Asian trading session, the DXY was hovering around 97.90, reflecting ongoing weakness since April 6. This marks a notable shift in market sentiment amid easing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, driving investor risk appetite.

The pullback in the DXY is also influencing key currency pairs, including the EUR/USD, which has gained traction as a result. The US dollar's recent underperformance can be attributed to shifting expectations, with market participants betting on a de-escalation of tensions in the region. As the situation unfolds, traders will continue to monitor the DXY’s movement for potential breakouts or reversals, particularly around the 98.00 support level.
