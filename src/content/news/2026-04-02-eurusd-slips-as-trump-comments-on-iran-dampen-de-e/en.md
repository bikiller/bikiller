EUR/USD slipped on Thursday, with the pair trading under pressure as ongoing Middle East tensions boosted the US Dollar. The Euro came under strain as comments from former President Trump on Iran dampened hopes for de-escalation, which fueled concerns about global stability. As a result, the EUR/USD exchange rate saw a decline, hovering around 1.0785, down by 0.3% on the day.

The US Dollar remains broadly supported by heightened geopolitical risks, continuing to outperform the Euro amid risk-off sentiment. While investors were initially optimistic about de-escalation in the Middle East, Trump's remarks reignited concerns, strengthening the USD’s safe-haven appeal. As a result, traders remain cautious, and EUR/USD faces resistance near 1.0800, with further declines potentially targeting the 1.0750 mark.
