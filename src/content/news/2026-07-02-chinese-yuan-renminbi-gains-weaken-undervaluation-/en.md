According to  strategist Geoff Yu, recent renminbi strength is carrying strategic significance as China expands semiconductor imports from Japan, South Korea and Taiwan. In forex trading, the CNY exchange rate has firmed against the NA-3 basket (JPY, KRW and TWD), alongside gains versus USD and EUR, signaling broader currency pair resilience.

The move undermines arguments in the US and EU that China’s currency is undervalued, as sustained appreciation across major currency pairs reduces external pressure. Stronger renminbi dynamics also reflect shifting capital flows and improved import competitiveness for high-tech inputs, reinforcing FX stability in Asian markets. Traders watch USD and EUR legs for confirmation of trend continuation, while strength against NA-3 currencies adds divergence signals in cross-currency strategies.

In the near term, CNY direction will remain closely tied to trade flows and regional semiconductor demand, with FX markets pricing in relative resilience against USD and EUR. If import-driven demand persists, the renminbi may continue to trade firmer against JPY, KRW and TWD, reinforcing the narrative of reduced undervaluation risk in global forex assessments.
