The USD remains well-supported as the ongoing conflict in the Middle East continues to push oil prices higher. ING’s Chris Turner believes this "conflict premium" is bolstering the Dollar, with the energy market's volatility adding upward pressure on USD-based trading. As investors turn their attention to the Federal Reserve’s stance, the FOMC meeting this week is expected to be Dollar-positive, as the Fed is likely to resist current market expectations of a rate cut.

This sentiment underpins a stronger USD against major currencies, especially the EUR/USD pair, which continues to hover near critical levels. The Euro has struggled to gain momentum, reflecting concerns over the Eurozone's economic outlook. In contrast, the USD remains buoyed by the Fed's hawkish tone and the global uncertainty surrounding the Middle East. Investors will likely keep a close watch on upcoming central bank moves, which will influence the exchange rate and provide further direction for the Dollar's performance.
