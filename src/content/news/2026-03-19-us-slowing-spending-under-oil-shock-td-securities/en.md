US consumer spending is showing signs of weakness, with real consumption growth remaining sluggish into early 2026. This slowdown is expected to create a soft base for Q1, potentially limiting the upside for the USD in the short term. TD Securities forecasts slower quarterly consumption growth, though year-on-year figures are set to show a stronger pace, supported by tax refunds in Q2.

This softening of domestic demand could influence market expectations for the USD, particularly against the EUR and other major currencies. With consumption struggles under the weight of an oil shock, the market may begin to reassess the strength of the US economy in the coming months. The slight improvement expected from tax refunds may offer some support to the USD, but a sustained recovery seems unlikely without a sharper rebound in consumer momentum.
