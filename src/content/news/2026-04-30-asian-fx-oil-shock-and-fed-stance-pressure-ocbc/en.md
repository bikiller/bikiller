Asian currencies weakened as Brent crude oil surged toward USD 120 per barrel, adding pressure to inflation risks across the region. The rally in oil prices prompted concerns about rising costs, dampening sentiment and contributing to a stronger USD. As a result, key Asian currency pairs such as USD/JPY, EUR/USD, and SGD/USD saw increased volatility, with the USD maintaining a dominant stance in the market.

The hawkish repricing of U.S. Federal Reserve policy further weighed on the forex market, pushing the greenback higher. The expectation of more rate hikes has kept pressure on risk-sensitive currencies like the Japanese yen and the Singapore dollar, with USD/JPY nearing levels not seen in years. These dynamics have left traders cautious, with Asian FX struggling to maintain momentum amidst these external pressures.
