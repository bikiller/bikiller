Brent crude surged amid escalating Iran–US tensions, with traders closely watching potential geopolitical disruptions and US policy discussions on gasoline tax relief. Commerzbank analysts, including Barbara Lambrecht, note that this spike is driving volatility in currency pairs, with the USD gaining against the EUR as markets factor in tighter oil supply expectations. Exchange rates and commodity-linked currencies are reacting sharply to these developments.

China’s crude oil imports fell sharply in April, highlighting a slowdown in demand that adds complexity to global oil trading. Shipments from major suppliers, including Saudi Arabia and Iran, are expected to decline further, reinforcing bullish pressure on Brent. Market participants are weighing these supply-demand dynamics against forex positions, adjusting their USD and EUR holdings accordingly to hedge exposure.

The combined effect of geopolitical risk and shifting demand patterns is influencing both energy and forex markets. Traders are closely monitoring technical levels for Brent and related currency pairs, while volatility in the USD exchange rate provides opportunities and risks in short-term trading strategies. Analysts caution that ongoing uncertainty may keep oil prices elevated and forex markets reactive for the foreseeable future.
