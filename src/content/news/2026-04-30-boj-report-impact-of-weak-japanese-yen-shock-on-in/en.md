The Bank of Japan (BoJ) highlighted in its Thursday report that the recent depreciation of the Japanese Yen has exerted a stronger influence on domestic inflation than the previous oil price shocks. Analysts noted that the USD/JPY currency pair climbed past 145.00, reflecting the yen’s weakness, while the broader FX market responded with heightened volatility as traders adjusted positions anticipating imported inflation pressures.  

The report emphasized that the yen’s softness is boosting import costs, with prices for energy, food, and industrial goods rising, feeding directly into consumer prices. This contrasts with the more muted impact from historical oil shocks, suggesting that the exchange rate now plays a more decisive role in shaping inflation dynamics. EUR/JPY and other yen crosses also saw increased trading activity as market participants recalibrated risk exposure.  

Implications for forex traders are significant, as a persistently weak yen could sustain upward pressure on USD/JPY and EUR/JPY, influencing monetary policy expectations. Strategists suggest that monitoring BoJ communications and technical levels, such as the 145.00 support-turned-resistance in USD/JPY, will be key for navigating potential volatility spikes, while the yen’s trajectory continues to dominate FX sentiment across Asia and global markets.
