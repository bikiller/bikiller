EUR/CAD slipped on Wednesday as the Canadian Dollar (CAD) gained ground following the Bank of Canada's (BoC) steady stance on monetary policy. The BoC held its key interest rate steady at 4.5%, signaling confidence in the domestic economy despite global uncertainties. This stability boosted investor confidence in the Loonie, driving the EUR/CAD exchange rate lower.

Additionally, rising Oil prices provided further support to the commodity-linked CAD. With crude prices edging higher, the Loonie found additional strength, reinforcing its positive momentum. As a result, EUR/CAD saw a decline in early trading, moving toward key support levels, with the pair hovering near 1.4500. The market remains focused on future central bank actions and commodity price shifts for direction.
