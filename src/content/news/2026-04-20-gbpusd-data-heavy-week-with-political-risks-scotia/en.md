The GBP/USD currency pair is holding steady after bouncing back from early volatility triggered by renewed US–Iran tensions. According to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret, the Pound has regained composure against the US Dollar, with trading reflecting cautious optimism amid a politically charged environment. Investors are closely watching the exchange rate as geopolitical developments continue to influence market sentiment.  

This week is expected to be data-heavy, with key UK and US economic releases likely to drive further movement in the GBP/USD pair. Analysts note that any shifts in inflation, employment, or consumer confidence data could sway traders’ positioning, impacting not only the Pound but also broader currency markets including the EUR and other major currencies. Technical levels remain in focus, as traders assess support and resistance zones for near-term trading strategies.  

Scotiabank emphasizes that while the Pound has recovered from the initial risk-off move, political uncertainties and economic indicators could create heightened volatility. Market participants are weighing both geopolitical and fundamental factors in shaping their USD and GBP positions. As the week unfolds, the interplay of data releases and external risks will be critical in determining the direction of the GBP/USD exchange rate and the broader trading landscape.
