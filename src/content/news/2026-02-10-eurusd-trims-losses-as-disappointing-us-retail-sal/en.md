EUR/USD trimmed earlier losses on Tuesday, recovering from daily lows as softer-than-expected US Retail Sales data put pressure on the US Dollar. The exchange rate for the EUR/USD currency pair found some relief after the report showed retail sales growth of just 0.2% in January, below the anticipated 0.5%. This disappointing data highlighted a slowdown in consumer spending, weakening the greenback.

As a result, the euro gained modest support, pushing the EUR/USD back from a tight range earlier in the day. The pair briefly tested lows near 1.0750 before climbing back to trade above 1.0780.
