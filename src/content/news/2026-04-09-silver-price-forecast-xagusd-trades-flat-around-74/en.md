Silver (XAG/USD) held steady near the $74.00 mark during late Asian trading on Thursday, showing limited volatility as traders awaited developments from the upcoming US-Iran talks. The currency pair struggled to establish a clear direction, reflecting cautious sentiment across global markets. Analysts note that uncertainty over a permanent ceasefire in Pakistan is keeping safe-haven demand, and USD flows, relatively muted.  

Market participants observed that the XAG/USD exchange rate remained confined within a narrow range, with buying and selling pressures balancing ahead of Saturday’s diplomatic event. While silver often reacts to shifts in USD strength, traders are closely watching potential geopolitical catalysts that could trigger sharper moves in metals and related forex pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, as risk sentiment fluctuates.  

Technical traders suggest that immediate support sits around $73.80, while resistance near $74.20 could cap gains unless fresh market triggers emerge. Any breakthrough in the talks might lift investor confidence, influencing both precious metals and key currency pairs, as shifts in USD liquidity and risk appetite ripple through trading desks. For now, silver trading remains cautious, reflecting a market in wait-and-see mode.
