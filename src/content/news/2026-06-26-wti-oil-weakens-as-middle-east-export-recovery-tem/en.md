West Texas Intermediate (WTI) US Oil declined sharply on Friday, falling 3.25% as bearish sentiment returned to energy markets. The benchmark was trading around $69.05 at the time of writing after sliding to $68.48 earlier in the session, its lowest level since late February, reflecting renewed volatility in USD-denominated commodities.

Market pressure stemmed from easing supply fears as Middle East export recovery continued to offset earlier geopolitical risk premiums. Traders reassessed crude positioning in USD-based pricing, with improved flows weighing on short-term sentiment. The move also influenced broader forex trading dynamics, as commodity-linked currencies tracked weaker oil benchmarks against the US dollar.

Looking ahead, WTI remains sensitive to shifts in global supply expectations and USD strength, with traders watching whether prices can stabilize above the $68–$70 range. Continued export normalization from the Middle East could cap upside momentum, while broader forex markets, including EUR/USD, reflect diverging central bank expectations and risk sentiment across commodities.
