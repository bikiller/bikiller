The British Pound remained on the defensive against the USD on Wednesday, with the GBP/USD currency pair consolidating below the 1.3400 mark during the Asian trading session. After rebounding from a three-week low over the previous two days, the exchange rate struggled to attract fresh buying interest and moved within a narrow range. Traders adopted a cautious stance as geopolitical uncertainty resurfaced following renewed tensions involving Iran.

Risk sentiment across financial markets softened, supporting demand for the US Dollar as a traditional safe-haven asset. The stronger USD limited upside potential for GBP/USD despite the pair’s recent recovery. Market participants also refrained from placing aggressive directional bets ahead of the release of the latest US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, a key indicator that could influence expectations for Federal Reserve policy and broader currency market trends.

The focus now shifts to incoming US inflation figures, which may determine the near-term direction of the currency pair. A stronger-than-expected CPI reading could reinforce USD strength and weigh further on GBP/USD, while softer inflation data may provide support for the Pound. Investors are also monitoring developments across major currency markets, including EUR/USD trading, as shifting risk appetite and monetary policy expectations continue to shape exchange-rate movements.
