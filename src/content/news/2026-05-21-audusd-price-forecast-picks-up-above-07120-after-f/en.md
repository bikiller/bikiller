The AUD/USD currency pair edged higher above the 0.7120 level on Thursday after finding support near 0.7100, signaling renewed buying interest in the Australian Dollar. Traders noted that the pair’s intraday recovery comes amid soft Australian employment data, which tempered risk sentiment and bolstered expectations that the Reserve Bank of Australia may pause its hiking cycle following three consecutive rate increases.  

Market participants are watching the exchange rate closely, with the Australian Dollar gaining modest ground against the US Dollar as investors digest domestic economic signals. The weaker employment figures have reinforced speculation that RBA policy could shift toward a more neutral stance, providing room for AUD traders to capitalize on short-term rebounds. Technical analysts highlight 0.7100 as a key support, while 0.7150 could serve as an immediate resistance for ongoing trading.  

Broader forex flows show the AUD also influenced by US Dollar dynamics, as USD strength elsewhere keeps gains in check. Traders are monitoring EUR/USD and other major pairs for cross-market correlations, while sentiment-driven moves in commodity-linked currencies, including the AUD, remain sensitive to central bank cues. The market appears poised for cautious trading, balancing domestic economic signals against global risk appetite and interest rate expectations.
