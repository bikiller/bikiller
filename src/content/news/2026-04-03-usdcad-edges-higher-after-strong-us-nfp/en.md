USD/CAD climbed 0.14% on Friday, buoyed by a robust US employment report that dramatically exceeded expectations. The Bureau of Labor Statistics revealed that nonfarm payrolls surged nearly three times above economists' forecasts, reinforcing the dollar's strength against major currencies, including the Canadian dollar. Traders quickly priced in the likelihood of sustained US economic momentum, driving the USD/CAD exchange rate higher in early North American session trading.  

The strong jobs data pushed the US dollar broadly higher, with investors favoring USD positions over risk-sensitive currencies such as CAD and EUR. Market participants noted that the impressive NFP figures could influence the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy stance, potentially accelerating interest rate expectations. As a result, USD/CAD found support near key technical levels, prompting increased trading volume and short-term bullish sentiment.  

Analysts suggest that this rally may persist if the US labor market continues to outperform, reinforcing the greenback’s appeal in forex markets. Traders are closely monitoring follow-up economic indicators, including wage growth and unemployment trends, for further cues on USD strength. For USD/CAD, the immediate focus remains on sustaining gains above 1.36, while broader dollar dynamics against EUR, GBP, and JPY will continue to shape cross-market trading strategies.
