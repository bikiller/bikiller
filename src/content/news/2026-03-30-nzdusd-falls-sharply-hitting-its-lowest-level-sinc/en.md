The NZD/USD currency pair plunged on Monday, falling to a low of 0.5700, marking its lowest point since November. At the time of writing, the pair is trading around 0.5715, down 0.60% on the day. The New Zealand Dollar remains under significant pressure, weighed down by a strengthening US Dollar in the wake of rising geopolitical tensions.

The USD's broad strength has dominated market sentiment, pushing the NZD/USD lower as traders adopt a cautious stance amid uncertain global conditions. With the US Dollar benefitting from risk aversion, the exchange rate remains vulnerable to further declines, leaving the NZD exposed to additional downside risks.
