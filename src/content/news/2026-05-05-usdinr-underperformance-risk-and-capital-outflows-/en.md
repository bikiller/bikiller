The Indian Rupee faced renewed pressure as the USD/INR currency pair underperformed amid persistent capital outflows, according to Michael Wan of MUFG. The rupee entered the period of heightened geopolitical tension surrounding Iran already vulnerable, with investors pulling funds and weighing safe-haven currencies like the USD. Trading volumes in the USD/INR pair showed elevated activity, reflecting both speculative flows and defensive hedging.  

The Reserve Bank of India is reportedly exploring measures to bolster foreign currency inflows, including a potential FCNR swap scheme and adjustments to tax policies aimed at incentivizing Dollar deposits. These steps come as the rupee's exchange rate faces resistance near key technical levels, while EUR/INR and other major currency pairs remain sensitive to global risk sentiment. Market participants are watching for shifts in capital flows that could stabilize or further pressure the rupee.  

Analysts caution that the combination of geopolitical uncertainty and preexisting outflows leaves the Indian currency at risk of further underperformance. Traders are closely monitoring USD strength, cross-currency correlations, and RBI policy signals, which could influence both spot trading and forward contracts. The near-term outlook suggests a cautious approach, with the rupee vulnerable to volatility in global markets and shifts in investor sentiment toward safe-haven assets.
