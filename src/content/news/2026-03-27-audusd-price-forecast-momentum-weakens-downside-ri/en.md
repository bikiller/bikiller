The AUD/USD currency pair slipped further on Friday, marking a fourth consecutive session of losses as the exchange rate drifted lower toward the 0.6900 level. The Australian Dollar remained under pressure in trading, while the USD stayed broadly firm, supported by persistent geopolitical tensions in the Middle East that boosted demand for safe-haven assets.

Momentum in the pair appears to be fading, with technical signals pointing to increasing downside risks below 0.6900. Traders are closely watching this key level, as a sustained break could accelerate selling pressure. The stronger US Dollar continues to dominate forex flows, limiting any meaningful recovery in the AUD despite relatively stable performance in other major pairs such as EUR/USD.

In the near term, the outlook for AUD/USD remains cautious, with market sentiment tied heavily to global risk dynamics and USD strength. If geopolitical uncertainty persists, the currency pair could face deeper declines, reinforcing bearish positioning. For traders, the focus remains on whether support near 0.6900 can hold or give way to further downside in the exchange rate.
