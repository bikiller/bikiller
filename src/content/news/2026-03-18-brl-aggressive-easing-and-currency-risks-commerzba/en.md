Commerzbank’s Michael Pfister forecasts the Brazilian central bank will initiate its rate-cutting cycle, ending its prolonged 15% hold. The market anticipates an initial reduction of 25 or 50 basis points. This shift could influence the BRL/USD exchange rate, adding volatility to trading and creating potential risks for the currency pair as the central bank adjusts its stance.

Pfister also expects the pace of cuts to increase through 2026, potentially putting further pressure on the Brazilian real. Traders will closely monitor the EUR/BRL and USD/BRL pairs for signs of accelerated market movement as the central bank navigates its monetary policy amidst the ongoing economic challenges. The central bank's actions may also impact broader market sentiment and exchange rate volatility.
