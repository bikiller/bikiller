Gold (XAU/USD) struggles to extend modest Asian session gains, failing to hold above a fresh two-week high. The precious metal briefly touched levels just above $4,200 on Monday but quickly eased back, trading below that threshold as selling pressure emerged around higher levels in the currency pair exchange rate today.

The pullback comes as the US dollar (USD) stages a mild uptick across major forex markets, weighing on bullion priced in USD. As the exchange rate strengthens, gold becomes relatively more expensive for non-USD buyers, limiting follow-through demand. Broader trading sentiment remains cautious, with participants reassessing short-term momentum in USD-linked assets like EUR/USD and other majors.

Despite the retreat, overall trading remains range-bound, with XAU/USD still supported by recent gains. Market participants are watching whether gold can reclaim and sustain levels above $4,200 to confirm bullish continuation or if renewed USD strength will cap upside momentum. Near-term price action may remain sensitive to shifts in currency pair flows and risk sentiment.
