Gold (XAU/USD) regained momentum on Monday, climbing higher after earlier intraday losses, as the US Dollar and Treasury yields retreated from recent highs. The softer USD reduced pressure on the precious metal, boosting its appeal among traders seeking alternatives. Analysts noted that the easing in Treasury yields eased the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like gold, underpinning the exchange rate rebound.  

The currency pair’s recovery came amid broader forex market shifts, with the USD index slipping from multi-week highs against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY. This movement encouraged risk-sensitive trading in commodities, as investors rotated from dollar-denominated assets into gold. Technical indicators suggested that XAU/USD could test resistance levels near $2,030, with support around $2,000 providing a floor for near-term trading activity.  

Market watchers highlighted that the rebound reflects both tactical positioning and fundamental pressures, including expectations for potential monetary policy pauses in the US. With volatility in the FX market persisting, traders remain attentive to USD movements and Treasury yields, which are likely to continue shaping gold prices. The interplay between currency pairs, safe-haven flows, and technical levels positions gold for further trading opportunities in the coming sessions.
