The Indian Rupee (INR) continued its downward trend against the US Dollar (USD), marking its fifth consecutive day of losses. On Friday, the USD/INR exchange rate rose significantly, driven by surging oil prices and persistent selling pressure from foreign institutional investors (FIIs). The currency pair's movement reflects broader market concerns about rising inflation and economic stability in India.

As oil prices remain elevated, the adverse effects on India's trade balance and inflation outlook are contributing to the rupee's decline. The USD/INR pair's technical levels indicate a potential breach above strong resistance, with traders closely monitoring developments in global oil markets and FII activity. Analysts caution that unless there's a reversal in oil price trends or a shift in FII sentiment, the INR may face further depreciation against the dollar.
