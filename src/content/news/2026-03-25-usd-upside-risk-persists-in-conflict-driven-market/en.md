The US dollar continues to show resilience amid shifting risk sentiment, with the DXY consolidating just below the 100.00 mark. According to Brown Brothers Harriman’s Elias Haddad, markets are increasingly pricing in the possibility of a resolution to the Iran conflict, prompting traders to reassess positions across major currency pairs. The USD’s steady performance reflects both safe-haven demand and strategic positioning in a conflict-driven environment.  

EUR/USD and other key exchange rates are experiencing modest volatility as investors weigh geopolitical developments against broader economic indicators. While the euro remains pressured, the USD benefits from its relative stability, influencing trading activity across Asia and Europe. Market participants are closely monitoring technical levels, with the DXY near 100 acting as a potential pivot for short-term directional moves.  

Haddad highlights that upside risk in the USD persists, even as risk sentiment improves globally. Currency traders are navigating this environment with a focus on both fundamental drivers and technical setups, particularly around major pairs like USD/JPY and EUR/USD. With the situation in Iran shaping headlines, the market’s response may continue to drive exchange rate fluctuations, offering opportunities for disciplined trading strategies in the coming sessions.
