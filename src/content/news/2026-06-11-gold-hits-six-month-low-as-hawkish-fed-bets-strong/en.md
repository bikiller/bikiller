Gold (XAU/USD) tumbled to a six-month low on Thursday, erasing all gains recorded earlier this year. Traders attributed the slide to hawkish expectations from the Federal Reserve and a strengthening US Dollar, which pushed the exchange rate higher against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Technical selling intensified, leaving buyers cautious amid volatile trading conditions.

The precious metal faced additional pressure as the USD index surged, reflecting broad-based demand for the greenback. Market participants noted that key support levels were breached, triggering stop-loss orders and further selling in the XAU/USD currency pair. Analysts highlighted that momentum indicators point toward continued downside risks if central bank policy signals remain tight.

Investors now monitor upcoming economic data and Fed communications, weighing the implications for both gold and forex markets. A firmer USD could constrain bullion’s appeal, while shifts in interest rate expectations might spark retracements. Traders are also watching correlated assets, including silver and platinum, as currency pair movements influence cross-commodity trading strategies and portfolio positioning.
