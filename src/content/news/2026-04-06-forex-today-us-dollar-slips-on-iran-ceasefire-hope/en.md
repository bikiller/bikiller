The US Dollar Index (DXY) dropped toward the 100.00 level on Monday as market sentiment shifted in favor of a potential ceasefire between the US and Iran. Hopes for diplomatic progress emerged following President Donald Trump’s latest remarks on the Strait of Hormuz, which weighed on the greenback. The pullback in the DXY also supported a more positive outlook for risk assets.

As oil prices remained elevated and gold steadied, the EUR/USD pair saw some upward momentum, with the euro gaining ground against the dollar. Trading activity remained cautious amid ongoing geopolitical tensions. Despite the DXY’s decline, the broader market outlook remains focused on the developments in the Middle East, particularly the potential for a de-escalation of tensions between the two nations.
