Gold edged slightly below $4,500 per ounce on Thursday, reflecting heightened concerns over a protracted conflict in Iran and rising expectations for aggressive US Federal Reserve rate hikes. Commerzbank strategist Thu Lan Nguyen highlighted that market participants are increasingly pricing in sustained geopolitical tensions alongside steeper USD interest rate trajectories, influencing bullion trading and its correlation with major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.

The US dollar maintained strength across forex markets, with trading activity showing investors favoring USD-denominated assets amid the risk-off sentiment. This bolstered the USD index, while currency pairs like AUD/USD and NZD/USD faced downward pressure, as gold’s role as a hedge against geopolitical instability is offset by the rising opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets in a higher-rate environment.

Technically, gold’s pullback potential is significant if it fails to reclaim $4,500 support, suggesting traders should watch the interplay between USD exchange rates, interest rate expectations, and global risk events. For forex and commodities traders, this dynamic underscores the need to balance positions in precious metals against currency volatility, particularly where USD strength can dampen safe-haven flows and influence cross-market trading strategies.
