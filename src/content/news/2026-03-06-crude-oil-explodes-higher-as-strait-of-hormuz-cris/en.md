WTI crude oil surged by nearly 11% on Thursday, climbing above $87.00, marking its highest level since October 2023. The rally was driven by a single explosive bullish candle that overshadowed the past three months of trading. The sharp increase came amid growing tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil shipping route, escalating concerns over supply disruptions in the global market.

The price jump had a notable impact on the broader forex market, with the USD strengthening in response to rising commodity prices. Oil's surge pushed the EUR/USD exchange rate lower as investors sought the safety of the dollar, anticipating higher inflation and potential rate hikes. This market movement highlights the growing influence of geopolitical events on oil prices and their ripple effect across global currencies.
