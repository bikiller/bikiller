Euro downside against the US Dollar remains limited, according to MUFG strategist Derek Halpenny, as rising European yields continue to signal growing expectations that the European Central Bank will deliver a 25bp rate hike at its 11 June meeting. The outlook is further supported by anticipated upward revisions to baseline inflation projections across the euro area.

European bond yields have climbed as traders price in tighter monetary policy, with the EUR/USD exchange rate finding support on expectations of a less dovish ECB stance relative to the Federal Reserve. The shift in rate differentials is helping the euro stabilise against the US dollar in forex trading, limiting bearish momentum in the currency pair.

Looking ahead, investors will closely monitor the ECB meeting on 11 June, where a 25bp hike and higher inflation forecasts could reinforce euro resilience. For forex markets, this keeps EUR/USD sensitive to US data and Fed guidance, while downside risks remain contained unless USD strength accelerates or policy expectations shift materially.
