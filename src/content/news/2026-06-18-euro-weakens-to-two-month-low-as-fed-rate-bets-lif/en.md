Euro extends losses as the single currency remains under pressure against the US dollar. EUR/USD trades around 1.1470 on Thursday at the time of writing, down 0.22% on the day and hovering near its lowest level in two months. The currency pair continues to drift lower in European trading, with sellers maintaining control as short-term momentum weakens after failed recovery attempts above recent consolidation levels.

US dollar demand strengthens as traders scale back expectations for near-term Fed rate cuts, with US yields remaining elevated and supporting the greenback across major currency pairs. The shift in monetary policy outlook has reinforced USD buying interest, putting additional pressure on EUR/USD and other EUR crosses during the session.

With EUR/USD hovering near a two-month low, traders are watching whether the pair can hold above nearby support levels or extend losses if USD momentum persists. Market participants remain focused on incoming US data and Fed commentary, which continue to shape expectations for the exchange rate direction. Volatility is expected to remain elevated as traders reassess the balance between euro weakness and dollar resilience.
