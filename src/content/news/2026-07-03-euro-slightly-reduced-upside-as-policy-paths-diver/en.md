ABN AMRO expects the USD to weaken against major peers over the medium term, but the bank has trimmed its upside outlook for the EUR/USD currency pair after revising its expectations for the European Central Bank. The updated view reflects a growing divergence in monetary policy paths, suggesting the euro’s exchange rate may face stronger headwinds than previously anticipated despite a broadly softer US Dollar.

The bank’s strategist, Bill Diviney, said the revised forecast also takes into account political uncertainty surrounding both the French and US elections. Those risks could influence investor sentiment, trading activity, and capital flows, limiting the euro’s ability to capitalize fully on any broad-based USD weakness. As a result, the currency pair may post more restrained gains even if the underlying trend continues to favor a softer dollar.

For forex markets, the revised outlook highlights the importance of policy expectations and political developments in shaping exchange rate movements. Traders are likely to monitor upcoming ECB and Federal Reserve signals alongside election-related headlines for fresh direction. While ABN AMRO still sees room for overall USD depreciation, the bank believes EUR/USD now has slightly less upside as diverging policy paths and elevated political risks weigh on the euro’s performance.
