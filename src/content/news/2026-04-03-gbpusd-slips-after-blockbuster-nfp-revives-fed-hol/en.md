The GBP/USD currency pair extended its decline for a second consecutive session, slipping 0.12% to trade at 1.3205. The pullback follows a surprisingly strong US Nonfarm Payrolls report, which has reinforced market expectations that the Federal Reserve may maintain a firm stance on interest rates. Investors are closely watching the exchange rate for signs of continued USD strength amid persistent inflation pressures.  

Trading activity shows the pound under pressure against the greenback, as market participants weigh the implications of higher US job growth on future Fed policy. Analysts note that sustained above-target inflation could prompt the central bank to maintain tighter monetary conditions, keeping the USD buoyant. Short-term technical levels suggest potential support around 1.3180, while resistance may form near 1.3250 if the pair attempts a rebound.  

The broader forex market is also adjusting to the shift in sentiment, with EUR/USD and other major pairs reflecting the dollar’s resilience. Traders are balancing risk appetite against safe-haven flows into the USD, while positioning ahead of upcoming economic releases. As the week progresses, the GBP/USD’s trajectory will hinge on both UK economic signals and ongoing Fed commentary, making the currency pair a focal point for global trading strategies.
