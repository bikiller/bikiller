Standard Chartered economists highlight a cost-push reflationary trend in China, driven by rising oil and commodity prices. The forecast for China’s Consumer Price Index (CPI) inflation in 2026 has been revised to 1.2%, up from the earlier projection of 0.6%. This shift is likely to influence the yuan (CNY) exchange rate as inflationary pressures may prompt adjustments in China’s monetary policy.

The impact of rising commodity prices on the Chinese economy is expected to reverberate through global markets, with the USD/CNY pair possibly experiencing heightened volatility. Traders are watching the yuan closely as inflationary concerns could affect China’s trade balances and the broader Asian market. This shift could also have implications for the EUR/USD, as a stronger CNY may alter global demand dynamics and influence broader forex market trends.
