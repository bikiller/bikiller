Euro fell 0.37% against the US Dollar (EUR/USD) in late North American trading on Monday, as the currency pair weakened amid renewed USD strength. The move came after optimism around US–Iran negotiations boosted risk sentiment and supported the greenback across major FX markets, pressuring the shared currency in the exchange rate session.

ECB President Christine Lagarde’s remarks further influenced trading, as she tempered concerns from hawkish policymakers and downplayed risks of second-round inflation effects. The comments surprised investors and reduced expectations for tighter monetary policy, adding pressure on the euro exchange rate against USD and reinforcing bearish sentiment in the EUR/USD currency pair during European trading flows.

Market participants are now watching whether continued geopolitical optimism and central bank messaging keep the USD supported in near-term trading sessions. The EUR/USD currency pair remains sensitive to macro headlines, with volatility likely as traders reassess ECB policy path versus US economic resilience, influencing short-term exchange rate direction and broader forex market positioning across major currency pairs.
