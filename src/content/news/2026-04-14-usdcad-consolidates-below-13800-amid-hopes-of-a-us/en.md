The USD/CAD currency pair has been consolidating below the 1.3800 mark, with the US Dollar weakening slightly against the Canadian Dollar. On Tuesday, the exchange rate fell to a three-week low of 1.3789, continuing its decline from Monday's high of 1.3878. This marks a modest loss for the USD as market sentiment remains cautious amid geopolitical developments.

The decline in USD/CAD comes as traders anticipate a potential deal between the US and Iran, which has led to some uncertainty in the broader forex market. As speculation grows about an agreement, the USD has faced pressure, while the CAD has found support. The exchange rate's movement suggests traders are balancing geopolitical risks and potential shifts in global energy dynamics.
