The USD/INR currency pair opened Monday on a firm note, extending the Indian Rupee’s (INR) weekly losses against the US Dollar (USD). Traders point to sustained pressure from elevated crude oil prices, which have added to India’s import bill, keeping the exchange rate tilted toward the greenback. Early trading saw USD/INR hovering around 82.45, with market participants eyeing 82.50 as an immediate technical resistance.  

Market sentiment remains cautious, as the Rupee struggles to gain traction despite steady inflows from foreign portfolio investors. The currency pair’s movement reflects broader risk dynamics, with investors weighing global crude trends alongside US dollar strength. Analysts note that any further uptick in oil prices could push USD/INR toward the next resistance near 82.60, while support levels around 82.20 are being closely monitored for potential retracements.  

Currency traders are also tracking cross-market signals, with the EUR/USD pairing holding steady near 1.0920, suggesting dollar resilience across major pairs. The INR’s sensitivity to energy costs and global liquidity conditions indicates continued volatility, making USD/INR a focal point for both hedgers and speculative traders. With key macroeconomic data expected later this week, market participants are positioning for potential shifts in trading momentum.
