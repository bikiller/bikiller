Oil prices are seeing a surge in investor interest, with markets predicting a strong likelihood of crude reaching $100 per barrel. Bob Savage of BNY points out that the risks associated with oil supply disruptions remain undervalued, despite this outlook. As oil prices climb, the commodity is increasingly viewed as a safe-haven asset, attracting significant investor flows into energy equities globally. 

This shift is creating notable impacts on the forex market. As oil strengthens, the USD is benefiting, particularly against currency pairs like the EUR/USD, where the greenback remains supported by rising energy prices. The energy sector’s solid performance is helping shield the dollar from broader risks, with traders closely watching the dynamics between oil, inflation, and central bank policies.
