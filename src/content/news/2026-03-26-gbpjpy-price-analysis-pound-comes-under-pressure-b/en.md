The GBP/JPY currency pair is under pressure on Thursday, with the British Pound (GBP) trading lower against the Japanese Yen (JPY) after a four-day winning streak. The exchange rate has fallen below the 213.00 level, signaling a shift in market sentiment. This marks a significant pullback for the Pound, as traders take profits following recent gains.

The decline in GBP/JPY comes amid broader market volatility and a stronger Yen, supported by the Bank of Japan's continued accommodative stance. Traders are closely watching the 213.00 support level, as a sustained break below this could signal further downside risk for the Pound. The movement also aligns with ongoing caution in global markets, where concerns about economic recovery continue to weigh on risk sentiment.
