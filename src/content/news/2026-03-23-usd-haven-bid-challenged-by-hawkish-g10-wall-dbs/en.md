The US Dollar (USD) has faced challenges despite risk aversion in the wake of the ongoing Iran conflict, which typically boosts its haven status. DBS Group Research economist Philip Weeargues suggests that the USD's appeal as a safe-haven may be temporary. The Federal Reserve's decision to pause rate hikes contrasts with the tightening cycle underway in G10 economies, putting pressure on the greenback.

Furthermore, concerns over US fiscal policy are adding downward pressure on Treasury yields, affecting the USD. As a result, USD pairs like EUR/USD could see increased volatility, especially with technical levels showing resistance around 1.0900 for the Euro. Investors are closely watching these dynamics, with market sentiment shifting in response to global monetary policy trends.
