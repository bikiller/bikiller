The Canadian Dollar (CAD) slid to fresh monthly lows against the US Dollar (USD) on Friday, extending its decline for a fourth straight session. The USD/CAD currency pair is trading in the mid-1.3700s, marking a weekly gain of roughly 0.5% as investors seek safety amid risk-off market sentiment. Traders note that the exchange rate remains pressured by a stronger greenback, which has benefitted from global uncertainty.  

Market participants have attributed the CAD’s weakness to broader risk-averse flows, with investors favoring USD assets over commodities-linked currencies like the Canadian Dollar. Technical analysts highlight that the USD/CAD pair is hovering near key resistance levels, suggesting potential for further upside if the trend continues. Meanwhile, cross-currency moves, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, indicate a general USD strength across major pairs.  

The ongoing momentum in USD/CAD trading could have implications for Canadian exports and monetary policy expectations. With the pair consistently climbing this week, traders are closely monitoring economic indicators, oil price movements, and upcoming central bank commentary to gauge whether the CAD can stabilize. For now, the market remains tilted toward safe-haven demand, keeping the US Dollar firmly in control of major forex flows.
