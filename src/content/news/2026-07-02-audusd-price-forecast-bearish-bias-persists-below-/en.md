The AUD/USD currency pair traded with a firmer tone on Thursday, supported by renewed selling pressure on the USD after the latest US Nonfarm Payrolls report came in weaker than market expectations. The softer labor data prompted traders to reassess the likelihood of an imminent Federal Reserve interest rate hike, easing demand for the greenback and allowing the Australian Dollar to recover ground in early trading.

Despite the short-term rebound in the exchange rate, the broader technical picture continues to favor sellers while AUD/USD remains below the key 0.7000 level. Market participants view this threshold as an important barrier for the currency pair, with repeated failures to regain it reinforcing the prevailing bearish bias across the market. Trading activity therefore remains sensitive to incoming US economic indicators and shifts in Fed expectations.

The latest move highlights the strong influence of US macroeconomic releases on global forex trading conditions. While the weaker payrolls figures offered temporary support to higher-beta currencies such as the AUD, investors remain cautious about the medium-term outlook for the pair. Developments surrounding USD performance, Fed policy signals, and broader movements in currencies including the EUR are likely to shape direction in the sessions ahead.
