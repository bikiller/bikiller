Brent Oil has dropped to approximately $100 as the US Dollar weakened, driven by hopes surrounding potential US-Iran negotiations. MUFG Senior Currency Analyst Michael Wan pointed out that while these developments may have influenced the energy market, the path to a deal remains fraught with challenges. The market's current volatility reflects uncertainty regarding the outcomes of these complex discussions.

The price movement in Brent highlights the delicate balance between geopolitical risks and currency fluctuations. As the USD/EUR exchange rate adjusts in response to these developments, traders remain cautious. Despite the optimism from potential talks, Brent’s price resilience at the $100 mark suggests that further risk factors could keep the energy outlook fragile, with potential for heightened volatility in the weeks ahead.
