Gold (XAU/USD) tumbled sharply on Thursday, dropping more than 4.5% and breaking below the $4,600 level as surging US Treasury yields pressured the precious metal’s exchange rate. The decline reflects a shift in trading sentiment toward the USD, with higher yields reducing the appeal of non-yielding assets like gold. The move also rippled across currency pairs, reinforcing broad USD strength against both EUR and emerging market currencies.

The sell-off was driven in part by renewed concerns over elevated energy prices, which have fueled inflation expectations and pushed yields higher. At the same time, a stronger-than-expected US jobs report signaled continued economic resilience, prompting traders to reassess the Federal Reserve’s policy path. As a result, rate cut expectations for 2026 were largely priced out, tightening financial conditions across forex markets.

Markets now anticipate the Fed’s first potential rate cut only in 2027, a significant delay that continues to support the USD’s dominance in global trading. This outlook may keep downward pressure on gold and influence positioning across major currency pairs, particularly EUR/USD, as investors adjust to a prolonged higher-rate environment.
