Commerzbank's Volkmar Baur highlights a striking trend in Asia's foreign exchange markets: despite significant surges in trade and current account surpluses in China, Taiwan, and South Korea, their currencies remain weak. The Chinese yuan (CNY) and South Korean won (KRW) are trading lower against the euro (EUR), with the won even struggling against the US dollar (USD). This divergence between economic strength and currency weakness is raising questions among traders.

The surpluses in these regions have been growing due to strong export performance, yet the currencies are unable to gain traction. The EUR/KRW pair continues to show resistance above key technical levels, while the CNY remains under pressure despite China's robust trade numbers. The imbalance suggests that other factors, such as monetary policy and investor sentiment, may be outweighing trade fundamentals in determining currency valuations.
