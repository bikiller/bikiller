USD/CAD edged lower on Wednesday as a softening in the US Dollar (USD) boosted demand for the Canadian Dollar (CAD). The currency pair was trading near 1.3891, pulling back from earlier highs not seen since December 2025. Market participants cited improving risk sentiment, which weighed on the safe-haven USD, prompting traders to rotate into riskier assets and commodity-linked currencies like CAD.  

Analysts noted that USD selling was broad-based, with gains in EUR, GBP, and commodity currencies reflecting a more positive global outlook. The exchange rate’s retreat from recent peaks highlights ongoing sensitivity to macroeconomic cues, including US inflation expectations, central bank signals, and oil price dynamics, which often influence CAD flows. Traders remain watchful for technical support near the 1.3870–1.3880 zone.  

Looking ahead, the USD/CAD pair may face further consolidation if risk appetite strengthens, keeping USD under pressure. However, any sudden shifts in US economic data or geopolitical tensions could quickly reverse the trend. The current trading environment underscores the interplay between safe-haven demand, commodity-linked currencies, and the broader sentiment driving exchange rates in global forex markets.
