The EUR/USD currency pair edged higher on Friday, buoyed by a wave of USD selling following Iran’s announcement to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. The move lifted market sentiment, easing fears over Middle East tensions and boosting optimism for a potential US-Iran diplomatic breakthrough. Traders reacted swiftly, pushing the euro above key intraday levels as demand for the greenback softened.  

Oil prices tumbled sharply in response, removing a major source of market uncertainty and allowing risk appetite to improve. The euro gained traction, testing the $1.0950 resistance level, while the USD slipped under pressure across major currency pairs. Analysts noted that technical support around $1.0900 helped anchor EUR/USD gains, with momentum indicators suggesting further room for euro strength if sentiment remains positive.  

Market participants remain focused on geopolitical developments and their impact on currency markets. With oil retreating and the Strait of Hormuz reopening, risk-sensitive currencies like the euro benefited, while the USD struggled to hold its ground. Traders are monitoring whether EUR/USD can sustain its climb above $1.0950, as a firm break could signal renewed trading opportunities and a shift in short-term exchange rate trends.
