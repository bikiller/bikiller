Gold (XAU/USD) holds steady near $4,600 as the US Dollar eases, reflecting a cautious optimism in global markets. The precious metal shows a slight upward bias, fueled by hopes for a de-escalation in the ongoing Middle East conflict. As traders monitor geopolitical developments, gold’s price remains sensitive to shifts in risk sentiment, which is largely influenced by the movement of the USD.

The easing of the US Dollar has provided support for gold prices, as the weaker greenback makes the precious metal more attractive to investors. With the EUR/USD exchange rate also seeing some fluctuations, traders are focusing on the broader macroeconomic backdrop. As the situation in the Middle East evolves, further shifts in gold’s price could occur depending on the impact on global risk sentiment and investor appetite for safe-haven assets.
