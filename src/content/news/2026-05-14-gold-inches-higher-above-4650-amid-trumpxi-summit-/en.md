Gold edged slightly higher in early Asian trading on Friday, hovering around $4,660, as investors reacted to renewed optimism surrounding the Trump–Xi summit. The XAU/USD currency pair saw some recovery after earlier losses, though market participants remained cautious amid fading expectations for US interest rate cuts. Traders monitored the USD closely, weighing its influence on gold’s exchange rate against major currencies like the EUR and JPY.

Market analysts noted that technical levels around $4,650 have offered support, preventing a deeper slide. Despite the uptick, upside momentum appears constrained, as the likelihood of fresh monetary easing by the Federal Reserve diminishes. This dynamic kept forex traders alert, balancing positions across gold, USD, and other major currency pairs, while sentiment was bolstered by geopolitical developments rather than fundamental rate expectations.

The interplay between safe-haven demand and currency fluctuations remains central to gold’s trading outlook. A stronger USD could cap further gains, while positive headlines from the summit may continue to lift prices modestly. Investors are closely watching XAU/USD alongside broader forex markets, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, to gauge whether geopolitical optimism can sustain precious metal gains or if the market will shift back toward USD-driven pressure.
