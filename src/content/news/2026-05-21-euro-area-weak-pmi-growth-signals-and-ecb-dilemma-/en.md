The Euro area’s economic momentum showed further signs of strain in May, as the composite PMI slid deeper into contraction territory. According to Commerzbank’s Dr. Vincent Stamer, both manufacturing and services sectors weakened, with services sentiment notably lagging, signaling potential challenges for Q2 growth. Market participants are eyeing this slowdown closely, as it may weigh on EUR/USD trading and broader currency pair dynamics.

The soft PMI readings have sparked renewed debate over the European Central Bank’s policy stance. Investors are monitoring whether the ECB will maintain a hawkish approach or pivot to support the economy. The weaker sentiment in services, a key driver of eurozone GDP, has already influenced exchange rates, with the EUR drifting against the USD amid growing expectations of cautious ECB guidance.

Traders are positioning for heightened volatility in the FX market as economic data continue to signal subdued growth. EUR/USD exchange rate movements are likely to reflect the balance between ECB rhetoric and incoming macro releases. With both manufacturing and services under pressure, the outlook for the euro remains sensitive to further PMI updates and central bank communications, shaping short-term trading strategies and hedging decisions.
