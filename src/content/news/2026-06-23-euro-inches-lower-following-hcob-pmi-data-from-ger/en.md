Euro selling pressure resurfaced on Tuesday as the EUR/USD currency pair trimmed earlier gains and drifted back toward the 1.1430 area during European trading. The exchange rate had managed to stabilize after posting modest losses in the previous session, but sentiment weakened following the release of Germany’s latest HCOB Purchasing Managers’ Index (PMI) figures. The data prompted traders to reassess the near-term outlook for the EUR, limiting the pair’s upside momentum against the USD.

Market participants closely monitored the German PMI readings for clues about economic activity in the Eurozone’s largest economy. The softer reaction in the euro suggests investors remain cautious about growth prospects, with the data reinforcing concerns that economic momentum may remain uneven. As a result, EUR/USD surrendered its intraday gains despite finding some support earlier in the session.

The move highlights the continued sensitivity of forex trading to incoming economic indicators. While the currency pair remained near 1.1430, the inability to extend higher reflects ongoing demand for the USD and a cautious approach toward the euro. Traders will now look for additional macroeconomic data and central bank signals to determine whether EUR/USD can regain upward traction or face renewed downside pressure in the near term.
