The British Pound remained under pressure on Thursday, with the GBP/USD currency pair hovering near a two-month low around 1.3210. Sterling struggled to attract fresh buying interest after the Bank of England kept its benchmark interest rate unchanged at 3.75%, reinforcing expectations that policymakers remain cautious about the pace of future monetary easing. The decision limited support for the Pound despite recent signs of resilience in the UK economy.

At the same time, the US Dollar continued to find support from the Federal Reserve’s measured policy outlook. Market participants interpreted the Fed’s latest stance as a signal that US interest rates could remain elevated for longer than previously anticipated, helping the Greenback maintain strength against major counterparts. As a result, the GBP/USD exchange rate remained under selling pressure during active trading sessions.

The divergence between the BoE and Fed policy outlooks has become a key driver for the currency pair. Traders are closely monitoring incoming economic data and central bank commentary for clues on the next move in interest rates. If USD demand remains firm, GBP/USD could struggle to recover from current levels, while broader movements in major currencies such as the EUR and USD are likely to continue shaping sentiment across the foreign exchange market.
