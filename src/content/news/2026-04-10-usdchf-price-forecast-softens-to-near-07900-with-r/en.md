The USD/CHF exchange rate slipped to 0.7905 during the early European session on Friday, continuing its downward movement as the market remains in a rangebound state. The pair’s softening is driven by a cautious tone ahead of the US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data release, which is expected to provide clues about future US monetary policy.

A fragile two-week ceasefire between the United States and Iran has also contributed to the strength of the Swiss Franc, a traditional safe-haven currency. This geopolitical development has pushed traders to seek refuge in the CHF, weakening the US Dollar (USD) against the Swiss Franc (CHF). Market sentiment remains cautious, and traders will closely monitor the upcoming economic data for further direction.
