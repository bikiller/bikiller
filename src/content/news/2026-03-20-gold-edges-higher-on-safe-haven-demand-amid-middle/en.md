Gold prices (XAU/USD) rebounded to around $4,705 during early European trading on Friday, breaking a seven-day losing streak. The uptick reflects rising safe-haven demand as tensions escalate between the US and Iran, prompting traders to seek refuge in gold amid heightened geopolitical risk. The move comes alongside cautious trading in major currency pairs, with the USD showing resilience against the EUR and other benchmarks.  

Market participants are closely watching the Middle East conflict for potential spillover effects on currency exchange rates. Rising gold demand often signals risk-off sentiment, which can influence USD liquidity and push the EUR/USD exchange rate lower. Analysts note that sustained geopolitical uncertainty could support further gains in XAU/USD, reinforcing gold’s role as a hedge against market volatility.  

Technical levels around $4,700 are now under focus, with traders assessing whether the rebound can hold above this mark. The combination of geopolitical concerns and shifts in major currencies is driving trading strategies, as investors balance exposure between precious metals and the FX market. Momentum in XAU/USD may continue to respond to both Middle East developments and fluctuations in USD and EUR trading activity.
