GBP/USD regained footing after beginning the week with a bearish gap, as escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East injected fresh volatility into forex trading. Despite the initial sell-off, the currency pair attracted dip buyers, with sterling holding firm above the closely watched 200-day simple moving average, reinforcing the bullish technical outlook for the exchange rate against the USD.

The rebound suggests market participants remain willing to back the pound even as broader risk sentiment remains fragile. Safe-haven demand supported the USD early in the session, while spillover pressure was also evident across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD. Still, GBP/USD’s resilience indicates buyers retain near-term control, particularly while technical support levels continue to hold under heightened market uncertainty.

For traders, the focus now shifts to whether geopolitical risk continues driving defensive USD positioning or whether improving sentiment allows sterling to extend gains. Holding above the 200-day SMA keeps the technical bias constructive for the currency pair, with further upside possible if bullish momentum strengthens. However, renewed headline-driven volatility could quickly test support and reshape short-term forex market direction.
