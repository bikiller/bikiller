TD Securities said the US Dollar (USD) remains neutral in the near term, even as its broader outlook turns bearish through the rest of 2026. The bank sees the greenback holding in a mixed trading range for now, but expects weakening later as markets continue to price in shifting policy expectations and softer US growth momentum. That keeps the USD exchange rate vulnerable against major currencies, especially the EUR and other high-beta peers.

In currency pair trading, the outlook suggests limited immediate direction, but a rising downside risk for the dollar over time. TD Securities’ bias implies that EUR/USD and other major forex pairs could gradually firm if US rates move lower or risk sentiment improves. For traders, the message is clear: short-term USD stability may persist, yet the broader trend could still tilt toward depreciation across 2026.
