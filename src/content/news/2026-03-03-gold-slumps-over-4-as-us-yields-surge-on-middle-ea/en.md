Gold (XAU/USD) saw a sharp decline of over 4% on Tuesday as rising US Treasury yields put significant pressure on the precious metal. The yellow metal, traditionally seen as a safe haven during geopolitical turmoil, struggled as higher yields made alternative assets more attractive. Gold traded around $1,830 per ounce, marking a sharp retreat from recent highs.

The surge in US Treasury yields came amid escalating tensions in the Middle East, which typically drives investors to seek the safety of gold. However, with yields on 10-year US Treasuries climbing to 4.5%, the strength of the USD increased, further weakening gold’s appeal. This dynamic caused volatility across forex markets, with the EUR/USD and GBP/USD currency pairs also feeling the impact.
