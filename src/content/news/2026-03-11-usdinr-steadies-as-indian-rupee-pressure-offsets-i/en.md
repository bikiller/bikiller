The USD/INR exchange rate remained largely stable, as pressure from the Indian Rupee (INR) due to increased hedging demand and foreign outflows countered the positive sentiment from easing oil prices. The currency pair showed little movement, reflecting this balance. The INR's downward pressure continues, despite oil prices retreating after reports suggesting the International Energy Agency (IEA) could release record amounts of oil reserves to stabilize global markets.

As oil prices softened, the outlook for the Indian Rupee improved marginally, supporting the broader market sentiment. However, foreign capital outflows and the hedging activities kept the INR under pressure, preventing any significant upside for the currency. The USD/INR pair's resistance remained firm, with traders monitoring any further developments in oil supply and global liquidity conditions.
