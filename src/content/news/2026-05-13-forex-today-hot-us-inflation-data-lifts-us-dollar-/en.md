The US Dollar Index (DXY) surged toward the 98.50 region, marking its highest level since late April, following unexpectedly strong US Producer Price Index (PPI) data. This uptick in inflation has heightened market expectations ahead of key economic releases, including US Retail Sales and UK GDP figures, which could further influence currency trading dynamics.

As investors digest the implications of the robust inflation data, the USD's strength against other currencies, particularly the EUR, remains in focus. The exchange rate movements suggest heightened volatility in the forex market as traders position themselves ahead of these important economic indicators. The current market sentiment indicates that continued strength in US economic data could support the USD's upward trajectory in the near term.
