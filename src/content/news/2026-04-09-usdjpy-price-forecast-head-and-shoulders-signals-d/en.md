The USD/JPY currency pair edged higher on Thursday, testing the 20-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at 159.19 before retreating slightly. At the time of writing, the exchange rate stands at 158.99, reflecting a modest gain of 0.28%. Traders remain cautious as improved risk appetite dampens the appeal of the US Dollar, limiting the pair’s upside momentum despite the initial push toward the SMA.  

Technical patterns suggest a potential head-and-shoulders formation, signaling downside risk for USD/JPY. Market participants are weighing the impact of broader sentiment shifts, with equities rallying and investors favoring higher-yielding assets over traditional safe-haven currencies. The interplay between risk sentiment and technical pressure points could define near-term trading ranges for the pair.  

Analysts note that a sustained break below key support levels may prompt further weakness in the USD against the Yen. Meanwhile, ongoing developments in global financial markets, including movements in EUR and other major currencies, continue to influence USD/JPY trading. Traders are likely to monitor both technical triggers and macroeconomic signals closely to gauge the next directional move.
