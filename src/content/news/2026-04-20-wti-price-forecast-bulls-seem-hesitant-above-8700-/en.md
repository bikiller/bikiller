West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices have shown hesitation above the $87.00 mark, struggling to extend its bullish momentum despite an initial gap higher. As of Monday's Asian session, WTI remains range-bound, holding modest intraday gains, while the breakdown below the 200-SMA keeps market sentiment cautious. Traders are closely watching this key level, as any further weakness could expose downside risks.

The breakdown in WTI’s technical structure has created a more uncertain outlook, especially with the USD maintaining strength across major pairs. The exchange rate between the EUR/USD remains steady, with the USD acting as a safe haven amidst global uncertainty. If WTI fails to reclaim momentum, it could face additional pressure, influencing broader commodity-linked currencies and sentiment.
