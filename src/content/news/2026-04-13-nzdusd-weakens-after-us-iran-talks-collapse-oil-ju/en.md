NZD/USD opened the week under pressure, trading around 0.5830 on Monday, down 0.15% from Friday’s close. Market sentiment was weighed down by the breakdown of US-Iran negotiations over the weekend, which lifted geopolitical uncertainty and supported the safe-haven USD. The currency pair’s retreat reflects risk-off positioning, with traders adjusting exposure amid heightened tensions in the Middle East.  

Oil prices surged following the stalled talks, amplifying concerns about inflationary pressures in the US. Higher crude benchmarks are feeding expectations of a more hawkish Federal Reserve, reinforcing the dollar’s appeal. Investors are increasingly watching the USD for signals, while NZD traders remain cautious, limiting gains as commodity-linked currencies face selling pressure in early-week trading.  

Technically, the NZD/USD exchange rate struggles to reclaim recent support levels, with resistance around 0.5860 and downside risks toward 0.5800. The market is now digesting both macroeconomic implications and geopolitical risks, keeping volatility elevated. For forex traders, the focus remains on the interplay between USD strength, commodity-driven moves, and broader risk sentiment, which will likely guide short-term trading strategies across major currency pairs including EUR/USD and AUD/USD.
