The EUR/USD currency pair advanced during early European trading on Tuesday, climbing to around 1.1545 and approaching the 1.1550 level as renewed expectations for tighter European Central Bank policy boosted demand for the euro. The EUR gained traction against the USD after market participants interpreted recent ECB signals as relatively hawkish, reinforcing speculation that interest rates could remain elevated for longer than previously anticipated.

The stronger euro reflected growing confidence in the Eurozone outlook, with investors favoring the single currency amid shifting monetary policy expectations. The move pushed the exchange rate higher and kept the currency pair near its recent highs, highlighting persistent buying interest in EUR positions. Trading conditions remained cautious, however, as participants balanced ECB-driven optimism against upcoming economic risks from the United States.

Attention is now turning to the US Consumer Price Index (CPI) report due on Wednesday, a key event that could influence expectations for Federal Reserve policy and drive volatility across major currency markets. A stronger-than-expected inflation reading could support the USD and limit further gains in EUR/USD, while softer data may reinforce the euro’s upward momentum and encourage additional buying near the 1.1550 area.
