Gold (XAU/USD) experienced a modest rebound on Thursday, recovering some of its earlier losses. However, the precious metal remains under pressure due to a stronger US Dollar (USD) and rising oil prices. The exchange rate dynamics reflect a cautious market sentiment, influenced by stalled negotiations between the US and Iran, which contribute to the overall uncertainty in trading conditions.

Despite the slight recovery, gold's upside potential appears limited as traders remain wary of the implications of a higher-for-longer interest rate outlook from the Federal Reserve. As the USD strengthens, the appeal of safe-haven assets like gold diminishes, illustrating the ongoing tug-of-war between inflation concerns and geopolitical tensions that continue to shape market movements.
