The Australian Dollar gained momentum against the US Dollar during early Asian trading on Monday, with the AUD/USD currency pair reaching approximately 0.7215. Market participants are positioning ahead of the Reserve Bank of Australia’s (RBA) policy decision on Tuesday, where expectations of a potential rate hike have lifted the exchange rate. Traders cite rising commodity prices and hawkish signals from RBA officials as supportive factors for the AUD.  

Meanwhile, broader market sentiment remains cautious amid simmering geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz. Safe-haven flows have marginally influenced the USD, while risk-sensitive currencies like the AUD and EUR continue to respond to developments in oil markets. Technical analysts note that the 0.7200 level has acted as a key support, reinforcing short-term bullish momentum in the AUD/USD trading range.  

Looking ahead, the exchange rate could see heightened volatility around the RBA announcement, with traders monitoring both the pace of monetary tightening and comments on inflation outlook. A stronger-than-expected rate move could push the AUD further above 0.7250, while any dovish signals may see the pair retreat toward 0.7180. Market watchers also remain attentive to global cues, including USD strength and shifts in EUR/USD trading, which could influence broader currency flows.
