The USD/THB currency pair has seen upward pressure as sustained high energy prices continue to weigh on risk sentiment across Asian markets. According to MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst Lloyd Chan, the Thai Baht remains particularly vulnerable amid this environment, with investors favoring the US Dollar as a safe-haven. Trading activity has picked up, reflecting broader risk-off flows that are supporting the greenback against regional peers.  

Market participants are closely watching the exchange rate as energy costs feed into inflationary pressures, impacting Thailand’s current account and capital flows. The combination of external shocks and cautious investor sentiment has created headwinds for the THB, while USD liquidity remains ample, encouraging speculative positions. EUR/USD and other major currency pairs are also showing sensitivity to these global risk factors, highlighting interconnected market dynamics.  

Looking ahead, Chan notes that ongoing energy volatility could continue to influence THB trading, with the US Dollar likely to maintain strength in the near term. Traders are advised to monitor technical levels for the USD/THB pair, as short-term moves may be amplified by risk sentiment shifts. Overall, the market’s focus remains on energy-driven developments and their impact on regional currencies.
