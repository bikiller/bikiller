The USD/CAD currency pair saw slight movement on Friday, edging up by less than 0.1%. After dipping to a low near 1.3560 during the early session, the exchange rate recovered, trading around 1.3590 by the close. This upward shift comes amid a broader trend of the Canadian dollar’s strength over the week, continuing the Loonie’s upward momentum against the USD.

The exchange rate’s recovery from a near 1.3560 low highlights the ongoing weakness of the greenback, while Loonie strength remains a key driver in the market. With the USD facing pressure, traders are focused on potential changes in U.S. economic data that could influence future movements. At present, USD/CAD is navigating a narrow range, but this week’s slide has investors closely watching for potential breakouts or reversals.
