Silver (XAG/USD) edged higher on Friday, gaining over 0.50% after finding support at a daily low of $73.95. The metal faced resistance near the $75 technical confluence, where multiple moving averages and prior highs converge. Traders noted the cautious advance as investors balanced safe-haven demand against broader risk sentiment in the currency and commodities markets.  

The uptick in silver coincided with gains in US equities, fueled by renewed speculation over potential Washington-Tehran negotiations. Investors appeared encouraged by the prospect of easing geopolitical tensions, which lifted risk appetite and supported trading across USD and EUR pairs. The market’s reaction highlighted the interplay between macro news and precious metals exchange rates.  

Analysts suggest that while XAG/USD remains in a short-term bullish tilt, the $75 area could cap near-term upside. Continued monitoring of US dollar strength, Treasury yields, and geopolitical developments will likely dictate whether silver can sustain its rally. Traders in forex and commodities markets are watching these factors closely, weighing implications for currency pairs like USD/EUR alongside metal positions.
