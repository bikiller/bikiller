USD/KRW exchange rate has lost upward momentum after encountering interim resistance around 1561 in June, according to Societe Generale analysts. The currency pair is now viewed as transitioning into a rangebound trading phase, reflecting a pause in USD strength against the South Korean won. Broader forex sentiment suggests consolidation as market participants reassess direction amid mixed macro signals.

Analysts note limited evidence of a sustained downside move, suggesting the USD/KRW currency pair is unlikely to see a sharp correction in the near term. Initial support is identified at 1530 on pullbacks, which may act as a stabilizing level for short-term trading activity. Price action above this threshold indicates a broadly balanced exchange rate environment.

Going forward, the pair is expected to remain rangebound, with traders focusing on US dollar dynamics, risk sentiment, and regional growth indicators for direction. In the absence of strong catalysts, USD/KRW trading conditions may stay contained between resistance near 1561 and support around 1530, keeping volatility relatively subdued across the foreign exchange market.
