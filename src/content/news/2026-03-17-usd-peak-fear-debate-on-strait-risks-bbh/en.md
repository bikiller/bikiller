The U.S. Dollar is holding a defensive stance as markets weigh geopolitical tensions in the Taiwan Strait, according to Brown Brothers Harriman’s Elias Haddad. Despite Brent crude staying above $100 a barrel and European gas prices remaining elevated, the USD has shown resilience, with key currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY maintaining support near recent technical levels. Traders are eyeing potential volatility as risk sentiment fluctuates.  

Analysts note that the exchange rate dynamics are influenced by a mix of energy-driven inflation concerns and safe-haven demand. While Brent above $100 signals persistent commodity pressure, the USD’s strength reflects cautious positioning, particularly against the euro. Currency pairs have been trading in tight ranges, as investors weigh the likelihood of central bank responses alongside geopolitical developments.  

Implications for forex markets remain significant, with traders monitoring USD performance against major currencies. Any escalation in the Strait could further support the dollar, reinforcing its role as a haven amid uncertainty. Technical levels around 1.08 for EUR/USD and 135 for USD/JPY are under scrutiny, with potential breakouts influencing short-term trading strategies and broader market sentiment.
