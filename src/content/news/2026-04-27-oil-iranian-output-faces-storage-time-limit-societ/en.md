Analysts at Societe Generale warn that Iran’s oil output may face a practical ceiling due to limits in onshore storage and floating tankers, a constraint that could intensify supply concerns in global markets. Traders are closely watching Brent and WTI benchmarks, which have edged higher amid fears that the U.S. blockade will increasingly strain Iran’s ability to sustain full production over time. This supply pressure has also lent support to the USD, as risk sentiment fluctuates.  

The tightening oil outlook is reverberating through currency markets. The EUR/USD pair is under pressure, slipping toward 1.0800 as energy‑linked USD strength boosts the dollar. Commodity‑linked currencies such as the CAD and NOK have seen modest gains, reflecting their exposure to rising crude prices. Market participants are actively trading the USD/JPY, monitoring shifts in oil‑driven risk appetite alongside broader geopolitical developments.  

Looking ahead, any disruption in Iranian flows could further tilt the balance in energy and currency markets. Forex traders are pricing in the possibility of sustained dollar strength, while speculative positioning in oil futures may amplify volatility in EUR/USD and other major pairs. The interplay between crude constraints and FX dynamics underscores the growing sensitivity of exchange rates to energy supply risks.
