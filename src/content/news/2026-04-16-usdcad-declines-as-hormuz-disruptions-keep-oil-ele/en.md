USD/CAD continued its downward trend on Thursday, marking a fourth consecutive day of losses. The currency pair slipped as elevated oil prices kept the Canadian Dollar (CAD) buoyed, benefiting from its close correlation with crude oil. Despite the US Dollar (USD) gaining ground against most major currencies, the persistent strength in oil prices overshadowed broader USD demand, pulling USD/CAD lower.

The recent disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz have kept oil prices elevated, further strengthening the CAD. Meanwhile, US-Iran tensions and ongoing talks have added volatility to the market, keeping investors cautious. As USD/CAD hovers around 1.3640, traders are eyeing key support levels at 1.3600 for any potential reversal. The outlook remains focused on geopolitical developments and oil price fluctuations, both of which will likely continue to influence the exchange rate.
