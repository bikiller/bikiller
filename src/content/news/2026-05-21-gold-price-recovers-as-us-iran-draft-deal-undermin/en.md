Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded sharply during Thursday’s North American session, climbing as traders reacted to reports from Al Arabiya that a final draft of a US-Iran deal, mediated by Pakistan, is imminent. The news weighed on oil prices and the USD, prompting safe-haven flows into gold. The metal’s recovery comes after recent weakness, testing near-term support levels and signaling renewed buying interest around $1,980 per ounce.

Currency pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD showed heightened volatility as the USD struggled against major peers. Traders noted that an easing of geopolitical tensions typically dampens oil-linked currencies while boosting gold and other non-yielding assets. XAU/USD extended gains to reclaim the $1,985 mark, with technical analysts eyeing a potential challenge toward $1,995 if momentum holds.

Market participants remain cautious, monitoring both the finalization of the US-Iran draft and broader macro data. A confirmed agreement could pressure oil-linked currencies and reduce USD demand, reinforcing gold’s status as a hedge. Traders are also watching trading volumes and intraday exchange rates for signals on positioning, with short-term resistance near $2,000 potentially shaping near-term strategies for forex and commodity markets.
