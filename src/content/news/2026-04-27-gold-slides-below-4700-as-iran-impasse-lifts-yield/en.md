Gold prices retreated during Monday’s North American trading session, with XAU/USD slipping below the $4,700 mark to trade near $4,673, down 0.75% on the day. The move came as the USD regained traction, trimming earlier losses and pushing the exchange rate lower amid renewed demand for the Greenback in forex markets.

The rebound in the US Dollar was supported by a modest rise in Treasury yields and a slight deterioration in overall risk sentiment. Market participants reacted to stalled US-Iran negotiations, which dampened optimism and prompted a shift toward safer assets, boosting USD demand while weighing on gold trading. Currency pair flows reflected this shift, with USD strength pressuring commodities.

The decline in gold highlights its sensitivity to both yield movements and broader geopolitical developments. As the USD firms and risk appetite weakens, traders may continue to favor the Dollar over non-yielding assets like gold. In the near term, XAU/USD price action will likely hinge on further developments in geopolitical talks and shifts in US economic indicators impacting the USD exchange rate.
