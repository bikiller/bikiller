OPEC has maintained its oil demand growth forecasts for 2026 and 2027, with no adjustments to the expected global demand. The organization’s outlook, revealed in a draft of its latest report, suggests that while demand is steady, market sentiment remains volatile. As global oil demand continues to grow at a consistent pace, traders are eyeing any developments that could affect oil prices, such as changes in supply or geopolitical tensions. 

The uncertainty surrounding WTI prices adds another layer of complexity to the market. Despite OPEC's stable demand projections, the price of West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil remains unpredictable, impacting trading pairs like USD/CAD and EUR/USD. A fluctuation in oil prices directly influences the exchange rates of commodity-linked currencies, which in turn could affect broader market movements. As traders assess these dynamics, the outlook for the USD and other major currencies remains closely tied to developments in the oil market.
