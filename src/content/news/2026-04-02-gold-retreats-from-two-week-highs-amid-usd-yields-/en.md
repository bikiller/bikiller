Gold prices retreated from two-week highs on Thursday, with the precious metal pulling back after a four-day rally. Trading at $1,948 per ounce, XAU/USD faced pressure as US President Donald Trump's remarks on the US-Israel conflict dampened initial market optimism. Trump’s indication of continued military action sent shockwaves through the market, fueling a rebound in the USD and bond yields.

The shift in sentiment was particularly evident in the EUR/USD exchange rate, which saw a slight dip, as investors moved towards safe-haven assets like the USD. Gold’s earlier gains were erased as traders re-assessed geopolitical risks and US economic prospects. With technical support at $1,940, further movements in gold will depend on global geopolitical developments and US economic data.
