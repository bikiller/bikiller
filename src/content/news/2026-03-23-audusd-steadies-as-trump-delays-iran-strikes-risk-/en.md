The AUD/USD exchange rate remained largely unchanged on Monday, after briefly falling earlier in the session. The pair found support as US President Donald Trump delayed planned military strikes on Iran’s energy infrastructure. This move reduced immediate geopolitical concerns, leading to a more positive risk sentiment in global markets. The Australian dollar, which had been pressured by rising tensions, was able to stabilize around the 0.6900 level.

The decision to hold off on military action eased investor fears, helping boost broader market risk appetite. The AUD/USD was trading at 0.6902, up slightly from its earlier lows, as traders shifted focus away from geopolitical risks to broader economic data. The overall market mood remained cautious, with traders closely monitoring developments in both the US and global economies. This shift in sentiment is likely to keep the currency pair's range-bound movement intact in the near term, while further volatility depends on evolving political tensions.
