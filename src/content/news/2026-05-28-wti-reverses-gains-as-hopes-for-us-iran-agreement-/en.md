WTI crude oil saw volatile swings Thursday as traders reacted to evolving US-Iran negotiations. The benchmark briefly touched an intraday high of $91.27 before retreating, with the current price hovering around $88 per barrel. Optimism about a potential agreement between the two nations boosted risk sentiment, prompting stronger demand in equities and commodity-linked currency pairs, while the USD softened against the EUR and other major currencies.

Forex markets reflected the shifting risk appetite, with the EUR/USD exchange rate rising modestly as investors moved away from safe-haven USD positions. Commodity currencies like the CAD and NOK experienced mild gains, tracking WTI’s intraday volatility and its implications for global energy supply. Traders highlighted the interplay between oil price swings and currency pair trading strategies, noting that momentum and technical levels were closely watched around key support and resistance zones.

Analysts warn that WTI’s rapid two-way moves may continue as geopolitical developments unfold. Market participants are monitoring headline-driven news, adjusting positions in USD/JPY and GBP/USD accordingly. The crude price fluctuations underscore the sensitivity of forex and commodity markets to diplomatic signals, emphasizing the need for nimble trading and careful risk management amid uncertain geopolitical catalysts.
