The USD/JPY currency pair extended its upward momentum for a third consecutive session on Monday, rising 0.07% to trade at 158.02. Traders continue to favor the US Dollar, drawn by its traditional safe-haven appeal amid lingering global uncertainties. Market participants also priced in expectations of a less dovish Federal Reserve, supporting the exchange rate against the Japanese Yen as risk sentiment remains cautious.  

Technical analysts note that the pair’s steady climb reflects growing demand for USD liquidity, with 158.00 acting as a key psychological level. Trading volumes suggest that investors are positioning for potential Fed-driven volatility, while the Yen struggles to maintain ground amid subdued domestic inflation signals. The current trajectory highlights USD strength relative to other major currencies, including the EUR, as global investors reassess risk-reward dynamics.  

Looking ahead, the USD/JPY exchange rate could face resistance near recent highs if the Dollar’s safe-haven status persists. Conversely, any shift toward more dovish Fed expectations may prompt profit-taking, temporarily easing the pair’s ascent. For now, traders remain attentive to macroeconomic data and central bank cues, keeping the focus firmly on USD movements as the pair navigates short-term volatility.
