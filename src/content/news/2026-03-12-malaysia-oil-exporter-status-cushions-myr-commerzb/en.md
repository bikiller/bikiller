The Malaysian Ringgit (MYR) continues to benefit from Malaysia's oil exporter status, as it remains resilient amid a fluctuating global market. Commerzbank analysts highlight the robust 5.9% year-on-year growth in industrial production for January, marking the strongest increase since mid-2024. The uptick was driven by strong export-oriented manufacturing, particularly in the semiconductor sector, providing further support for the MYR.

This positive industrial performance has helped stabilize the MYR against the USD, despite global uncertainties. As the demand for Malaysia's exports remains solid, particularly in the semiconductor market, the MYR is likely to continue its upward momentum. Traders will closely monitor the USD/MYR exchange rate, with key resistance levels set at 4.45, while any pullbacks could see the currency pair testing support near 4.40. The outlook for the MYR appears cautiously optimistic in the short term.
