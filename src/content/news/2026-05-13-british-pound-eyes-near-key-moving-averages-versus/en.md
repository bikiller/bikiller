GBP/USD trading is seeing a pause in selling pressure as the currency pair hovers near key moving averages, according to Societe Generale strategists. Market participants remain cautious amid ongoing political uncertainty in the UK, with Prime Minister Starmer potentially facing a leadership challenge. This backdrop is limiting further downside momentum for the pound against the US Dollar.  

Technically, Cable’s exchange rate is approaching critical support and resistance levels, where traders are closely watching moving averages for clues on potential trend reversals. The pair’s recent stabilization reflects hesitation among investors to commit amid UK political risks, while USD strength from broader market trends adds complexity to trading decisions.  

Looking ahead, any shifts in UK political sentiment or data releases could trigger renewed activity in the GBP/USD and influence cross-currency moves, including EUR/GBP and other major pairs. Traders are likely to monitor technical levels alongside macro factors to gauge potential volatility and directional bias in the forex market.
