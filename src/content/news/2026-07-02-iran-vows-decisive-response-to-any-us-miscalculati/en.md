Geopolitical tensions intensified after Iran’s joint military command warned that any US interference in the Strait of Hormuz would trigger a decisive and swift response. The statement, reported by Aljazeera, renewed concerns over regional stability and energy supply routes, with traders closely watching implications for oil-linked currencies and broader forex sentiment across USD and EUR pairs.  

Risk sentiment in forex trading turned cautious as investors assessed potential disruptions to the Strait of Hormuz, a critical energy corridor. The USD exchange rate saw mild safe-haven support, while EUR currency pair movements reflected broader risk-off flows. Traders also monitored volatility spillovers into commodity-linked currencies such as CAD and NOK, with expectations of sharper intraday price swings.  

Market participants now await further diplomatic signals as tensions over Iran-US relations continue to influence forex positioning and liquidity conditions. The USD and EUR remain sensitive to geopolitical headlines, while traders reassess medium-term exchange rate trends. Any escalation could amplify risk aversion, strengthening safe-haven demand and reshaping short-term currency pair momentum across global trading sessions.
