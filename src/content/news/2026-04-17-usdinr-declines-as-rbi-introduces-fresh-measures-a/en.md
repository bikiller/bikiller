The Indian Rupee edged higher against the US Dollar on Friday, with the USD/INR currency pair easing amid fresh intervention measures by the Reserve Bank of India. The central bank opened special credit lines for state-run oil importers to manage their foreign exchange requirements, providing the rupee with much-needed support. Traders noted increased demand for the INR as these facilities reduced pressure on the domestic currency.  

Market participants observed USD/INR trading around 82.35, marking a slight decline from previous levels near 82.50. The move comes as the RBI seeks to temper dollar purchases and curb excessive volatility, while other major pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, remain sensitive to dollar fluctuations. Analysts suggest the rupee’s resilience reflects a combination of central bank support and softer dollar sentiment in Asian trading hours.  

The new measures could influence short-term trading, with the rupee potentially consolidating near 82.30–82.40 if demand from importers remains steady. Investors are closely watching global cues, including US Treasury yields and the EUR/USD outlook, as these factors continue to sway USD/INR dynamics. The intervention highlights RBI’s proactive stance in stabilizing the currency amid broader forex market pressures.
