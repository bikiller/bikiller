Pound Sterling advanced against the US Dollar on Thursday after a weaker-than-expected US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report undermined expectations for further Federal Reserve rate hikes. The currency pair reacted sharply to shifting interest rate expectations, with traders reassessing USD strength across major forex markets amid softer labor market data and reduced yield support.

GBP/USD trades at 1.3359, down 0.64% as the US dollar remains sensitive to shifting Fed policy expectations. The disappointing employment data weighed on yields and pressured short-term dollar demand, while currency pair volatility increased across European and US trading sessions as investors recalibrated macroeconomic outlooks amid expectations of a delayed Federal Reserve tightening cycle and shifting rate-cut pricing.

Pound Sterling sentiment remains tied to US macroeconomic releases, with traders watching upcoming inflation and labor indicators for direction. The GBP/USD exchange rate may continue to see two-way volatility as markets reassess USD strength versus European currencies, while broader forex trading conditions reflect shifting expectations for global interest rate divergence.
