USD/CHF pared gains after surging more than 1% in the previous session, holding around 0.7920 in Asian trading on Thursday. The currency pair showed limited movement following the Swiss National Bank’s decision to maintain its benchmark interest rate at 0% for the first quarter of 2026, a move largely anticipated by market participants. Traders appeared cautious, weighing the SNB’s stance against broader global currency trends.  

The stable policy from the SNB has kept the Swiss franc in check against the USD, as the exchange rate hovers near the technical level of 0.7900. Investors are monitoring how this policy alignment impacts USD/CHF trading relative to other major currencies like the EUR and JPY, with the franc’s lack of yield advantage limiting its appeal.  

Market watchers note that while the USD/CHF pair retraced slightly from its recent rally, sentiment remains sensitive to shifts in global risk appetite and macroeconomic data. The SNB’s neutral rate decision suggests no immediate tightening, leaving room for USD strength if U.S. economic indicators continue to outperform expectations. Traders are positioning accordingly, balancing short-term volatility with broader trend expectations.
