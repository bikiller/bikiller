Silver (XAG/USD) is consolidating just above the $79.00 mark, showing limited intraday momentum as trading activity remains subdued. During Wednesday’s Asian session, the currency pair oscillated within a narrow range, reflecting cautious positioning ahead of the upcoming Federal Reserve rate decision. Investors are weighing the outlook for the USD, with broader market sentiment influencing short-term demand for precious metals.  

Technical indicators suggest that despite the sideways movement, a bearish bias persists. Traders are monitoring key support levels around $78.50, while resistance near $80.00 caps upside potential. The exchange rate’s hesitation highlights uncertainty in risk sentiment, as the market anticipates clues on interest rates and potential Fed policy shifts that could sway both USD strength and silver trading dynamics.  

With global markets awaiting the FOMC announcement, XAG/USD may continue to see muted volatility in the near term. A stronger-than-expected USD could pressure silver lower, while any dovish signals from the Fed might lift the currency pair. Traders remain focused on the intersection of macroeconomic developments, technical levels, and USD fluctuations to gauge trading opportunities in the coming sessions.
