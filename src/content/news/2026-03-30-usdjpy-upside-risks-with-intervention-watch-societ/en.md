USD/JPY has regained upward momentum, with the currency pair pushing back above its 50-day moving average (50-DMA), a key technical signal closely watched in forex trading. According to Societe Generale, the exchange rate is now breaking beyond its multi-year range, suggesting renewed bullish pressure on the USD against the Japanese yen. This move reflects sustained dollar strength amid global rate differentials.

The breakout has shifted market focus toward higher technical levels, with analysts pointing to potential upside targets near 162.00. Beyond that, the 163.20 to 163.70 channel top represents a critical resistance zone that could define the next phase of trading. Momentum indicators and price action both support the case for continued gains, although positioning appears increasingly stretched.

However, traders remain cautious as intervention risks from Japanese authorities linger in the background. Rapid depreciation in the yen could prompt policy responses, potentially disrupting the current trend. As a result, while the bullish outlook for USD/JPY remains intact, market participants are balancing technical strength with geopolitical and policy-driven uncertainties in the broader forex landscape.
