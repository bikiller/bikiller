Silver price (XAG/USD) rebounded during Asian trading hours on Monday, halting a three-day losing streak as the metal climbed back toward the $66.00 psychological level. The currency pair traded around $65.90 per troy ounce, with spot demand improving after recent downside pressure, reflecting a stabilisation in the broader exchange rate environment.

Sentiment across precious metals was influenced by fading optimism around US-Iran talks, prompting traders to reassess risk exposure and support demand for safe-haven assets. The XAG/USD currency pair showed resilience as USD volatility eased slightly, while movements in EUR and other majors contributed to shifting liquidity in global forex trading sessions.

Looking ahead, traders will watch whether silver can sustain gains above the $66.00 barrier, with technical positioning in the XAG/USD exchange rate suggesting consolidation after recent volatility. Broader USD direction, alongside EUR/USD fluctuations, is expected to guide near-term momentum in the silver trading outlook. Market participants also remain attentive to US dollar yield dynamics and risk sentiment shifts that could reshape short-term positioning.
