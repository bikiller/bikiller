Gold (XAU/USD) extended its decline on Wednesday, sliding to a fresh seven-month low as hawkish Federal Reserve expectations and rising bets on further rate hikes strengthened the US Dollar. The currency pair remains under pressure as traders reassess monetary policy outlook, with USD demand dominating trading flows across major forex markets.

Spot gold’s weakness is being driven by a firmer US Dollar and elevated Treasury yields, which increase the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets. In the broader currency market, the USD continues to outperform most majors, keeping related exchange rate pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD on the defensive as trading sentiment tilts toward a higher-for-longer interest rate outlook.

Market participants now watch upcoming US economic data and Federal Reserve commentary for clues on the next policy move, with further USD strength potentially extending losses in XAU/USD. If rate-hike expectations persist, gold may remain vulnerable, while forex traders reassess positioning across major currency pairs amid shifting risk appetite.
