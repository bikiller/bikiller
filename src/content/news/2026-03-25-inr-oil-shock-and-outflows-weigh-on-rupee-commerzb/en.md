The Indian Rupee (INR) remains under pressure, as a combination of oil price shocks and capital outflows weigh on the currency. Despite the volatility, India's March PMI flash data suggests a moderation in economic growth rather than a steep slowdown. Manufacturing activity has softened, but resilient external demand helps to cushion the impact, leaving INR trading cautiously near 82.00 against the US Dollar (USD/INR).

The recent price action highlights the INR's vulnerability in the face of global uncertainties, particularly with elevated oil prices driving higher import costs. On the EUR/INR front, the exchange rate is struggling around 89.50, reflecting broader market dynamics. As the rupee faces challenges, traders are closely monitoring PMI figures and global risk sentiment to gauge the outlook for the currency in the near term.
