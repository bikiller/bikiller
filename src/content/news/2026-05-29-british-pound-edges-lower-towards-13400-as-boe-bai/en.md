The British Pound extended its losses against the US Dollar on Friday, with the GBP/USD currency pair slipping to an intraday low of 1.3408 and hovering near the 1.3400 level. The move leaves Sterling on track for a modest weekly decline as traders continue to assess the outlook for UK monetary policy. Market participants remained cautious after recent remarks from Bank of England Governor Andrew Bailey, which were interpreted as an effort to keep policy options open amid persistent economic uncertainty.

The softer Pound reflects a combination of cautious sentiment toward the UK economy and renewed demand for the USD, which has remained broadly supported across major currency pairs. In foreign exchange trading, investors are closely monitoring incoming economic data and central bank signals for clues on the timing of future interest-rate adjustments. Bailey’s measured stance has reduced expectations of any immediate policy shift, encouraging a wait-and-see approach among traders.

From a technical perspective, the 1.3400 area is emerging as a key near-term support level for GBP/USD. A sustained break below this threshold could expose the exchange rate to deeper losses, while a rebound may help stabilize sentiment. Meanwhile, movements in related markets, including EUR/USD, continue to influence broader currency trading dynamics as investors balance growth concerns against interest-rate expectations.
