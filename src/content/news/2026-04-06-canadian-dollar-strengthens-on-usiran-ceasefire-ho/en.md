The Canadian Dollar gained momentum as the USD/CAD currency pair dropped to 1.3940 in early European trading on Monday. Traders reacted to growing hopes of a ceasefire between the US and Iran, which led to a positive market sentiment. As a result, the demand for riskier assets such as the CAD increased, pushing the exchange rate lower.

The decline in USD/CAD was also supported by the relative strength of the Canadian economy, which continues to show signs of recovery. With oil prices remaining stable, the Canadian Dollar benefited from its close correlation with crude, further weighing on the USD. As the market watches geopolitical developments, traders will keep an eye on potential shifts in the USD/CAD pair, particularly if the ceasefire talk progresses.
