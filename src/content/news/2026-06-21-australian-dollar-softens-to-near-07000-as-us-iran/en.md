AUD/USD declines to around 0.7005 in early Asian trading on Monday, slipping back toward the key psychological level of 0.7000. The currency pair reflects renewed pressure on the Australian Dollar against the US Dollar as exchange rate dynamics shift in favor of USD. Broader forex trading activity shows cautious positioning as investors reassess risk exposure across major currencies.

Risk sentiment in global markets has deteriorated after US-Iran peace deal negotiations reportedly stalled, encouraging a mild risk-off tone. As a result, the US Dollar finds support as a safe-haven currency, while risk-sensitive assets such as the Australian Dollar face selling pressure. Traders remain focused on geopolitical headlines that continue to influence intraday forex flows.

Looking ahead, traders will closely monitor further developments in US-Iran discussions for direction in the AUD/USD currency pair. The exchange rate remains sensitive near the 0.7000 threshold, with any sustained break potentially shaping short-term forex trading momentum. Broader USD strength and risk appetite will also influence major pairs, including EUR crosses.
