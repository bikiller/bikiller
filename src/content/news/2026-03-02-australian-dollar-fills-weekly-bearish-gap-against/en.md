The AUD/USD pair has recovered from a bearish gap that opened the week near 0.7030, marking the lower boundary of a three-week trading range. As the US dollar retreats, the Australian dollar has gained momentum, climbing back above the 0.7100 level during the Asian session. This shift comes amid fluctuating global risk sentiment and softer demand for the USD.

The rebound in the AUD/USD exchange rate signals a temporary halt in the greenback's recent strength. As market participants adjust their positions, the Australian dollar is testing key support and resistance levels, with 0.7100 now acting as a psychological barrier. If the pair maintains upward momentum, a move towards higher levels could follow, depending on broader market trends and economic data releases.
