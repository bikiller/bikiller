The New Zealand Dollar (NZD) experienced a decline against the US Dollar (USD) as traders reacted to stronger-than-expected US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data. The NZD/USD currency pair demonstrated volatility throughout the session, initially climbing during the Asian and early European hours before hitting a low of approximately 0.5935 mid-session. This downward movement was influenced by market concerns over inflation and its potential impact on monetary policy.

As the trading day progressed, the NZD recovered some losses but still settled slightly lower. Investors are now turning their attention to the upcoming Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ) inflation survey, which could further influence the exchange rate. The market remains cautious, anticipating how the RBNZ may respond to the evolving inflation landscape amid ongoing global economic pressures.
