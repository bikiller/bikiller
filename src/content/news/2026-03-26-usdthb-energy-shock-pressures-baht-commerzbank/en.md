The USD/THB exchange rate surged to 32.65 as Thailand’s trade balance remained in deficit, exacerbating pressure on the Baht. Economists from Commerzbank, Dr. Henry Hao and Moses Lim, noted that the country’s ongoing trade imbalance has contributed to the weakening of the currency. This movement comes amid rising global energy prices, which have further compounded the situation, impacting Thailand’s overall economic stability.

With the Thai Baht under increased pressure, traders are closely monitoring the USD/THB pair, which has seen notable fluctuations in recent days. The rise in energy costs, combined with the persistent trade deficit, has created a challenging environment for the Baht. If the global energy situation continues to affect trade balances, further depreciation of the currency could be expected, with the USD likely to maintain its strength against the Baht in the near term.
