Singapore’s non-oil domestic exports (NODX) are showing robust momentum, with DBS economists Taimur Baig and Radhika Rao forecasting an 11.5% year-on-year rise for April 2026, following March’s 15.3% growth. This marks the eighth consecutive month of expansion, driven largely by the global AI cycle. The strength in exports is likely to influence trading sentiment, with the SGD gaining relative stability against the USD and EUR as investors anticipate continued demand for Singaporean tech products.  

Forex markets are watching the SGD/USD currency pair closely, as the persistent export gains may support the Singapore dollar, potentially testing key technical levels. Traders may position for tighter spreads, reacting to export-led growth data, while equity-linked forex flows could further influence short-term exchange rate dynamics. The broader implication for USD and EUR exchange rates may hinge on comparative manufacturing strength and monetary policy differentials across Asia and the U.S.  

Analysts suggest that sustained export growth could reinforce Singapore’s trade surplus, offering support for the SGD in the medium term. For forex trading, this backdrop highlights potential bullish momentum for the Singapore dollar, especially versus major peers, while underscoring the importance of monitoring AI-driven demand cycles. Market participants are likely to watch upcoming economic indicators to gauge whether this trend can persist and how it might reshape cross-currency strategies.
