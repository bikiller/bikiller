Gold extended its rally during early Thursday Asian trading, climbing toward $4,775 per ounce as investors responded to a softer US Dollar. The XAU/USD currency pair benefited from broad USD weakness, with traders closely monitoring shifts in global risk sentiment and awaiting further cues from geopolitical developments. Momentum in precious metals markets remains supported as safe-haven demand persists.  

The dollar’s retreat, coupled with easing tensions in the Middle East, has fueled buying interest in gold. Currency markets, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, saw muted volatility, reflecting traders’ caution ahead of high-profile events. Analysts note that the exchange rate dynamics are driving flows into non-yielding assets, providing short-term support for gold prices.  

Looking ahead, traders remain attentive to comments from former US President Donald Trump on potential conflict escalation in the region. Any sharp geopolitical surprises could accelerate trading in XAU/USD, influencing USD exchange rates across major pairs. Market participants are positioning for volatility, with technical levels near $4,775 seen as key resistance, while support is eyed around previous consolidation zones.
