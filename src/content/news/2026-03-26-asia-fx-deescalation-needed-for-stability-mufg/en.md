Asian currency markets are under pressure as geopolitical tensions continue to shape trading dynamics. MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst Lloyd Chan emphasizes the need for de‑escalation to stabilize Asia FX. Rising US Treasury yields and elevated oil prices have strengthened the USD, putting downward pressure on currencies such as the Thai baht (THB), Philippine peso (PHP), and South Korean won (KRW), keeping exchange rates volatile.  

The USD/THB and USD/PHP pairs are showing notable resilience, reflecting a defensive stance among investors wary of regional spillovers from the US‑Iran conflict. KRW is also facing headwinds, as traders weigh higher US yields against local economic fundamentals. These external pressures are limiting risk appetite, prompting cautious positioning in Asia FX trading ahead of potential policy or geopolitical developments.  

Analysts suggest that further appreciation of the dollar could extend losses for regional currencies, affecting cross‑currency flows and regional export competitiveness. Currency pair volatility may persist until clarity emerges on the geopolitical front. Traders are watching technical levels closely, balancing defensive hedges with selective trading opportunities as USD strength continues to influence market direction across Asia.
