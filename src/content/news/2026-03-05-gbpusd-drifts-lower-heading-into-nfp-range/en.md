The GBP/USD currency pair drifted lower on Thursday, slipping about 0.2% and settling near the 1.3350 exchange rate as trading activity remained subdued ahead of key U.S. labor market data. The pair stayed trapped near three-month lows, reflecting cautious positioning across the forex market as investors avoided aggressive bets on either the British pound or the USD.

Market participants largely focused on upcoming macroeconomic catalysts, particularly the U.S. Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report, which often drives volatility across major currency pairs including GBP/USD and EUR/USD. With traders awaiting fresh signals on the strength of the U.S. labor market, the USD maintained a firm tone, keeping the pound under pressure and limiting any meaningful recovery in the exchange rate.

Technically, GBP/USD continues to hover near recent support levels after failing to sustain upward momentum earlier in the week. The currency pair’s inability to move decisively away from the 1.3350 region highlights persistent bearish pressure in short-term trading. If U.S. employment data surprises to the upside, the USD could strengthen further, potentially pushing the pair deeper below its three-month trading range.
