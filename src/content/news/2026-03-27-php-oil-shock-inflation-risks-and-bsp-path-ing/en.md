The Philippine peso is under pressure as surging oil prices and ongoing supply disruptions weigh on the economy. ING’s Deepali Bhargava highlighted that elevated energy costs are intensifying inflationary pressures, threatening both domestic growth and the country’s external balances. Traders have been eyeing the PHP-USD exchange rate, which recently tested the 55.50 level, reflecting market concern over the central bank’s ability to manage these shocks.  

Higher global crude prices are driving input costs across sectors, pushing headline inflation above expectations. This dynamic is prompting the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) to consider tightening measures, with market participants pricing in potential interest rate adjustments. The PHP-EUR and PHP-JPY pairs have also seen volatility, responding to shifts in both commodity prices and broader USD strength.  

Analysts note that the peso’s near-term trajectory depends on oil trends and BSP policy clarity. Sustained supply disruptions could keep the currency on the defensive, while decisive monetary action might stabilize trading. Investors are monitoring PHP liquidity and external flows closely, with short-term traders adjusting positions in the USD-PHP pair and hedging against further inflation-driven depreciation.
