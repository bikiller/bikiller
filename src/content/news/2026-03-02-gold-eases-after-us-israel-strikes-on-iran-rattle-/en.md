Gold (XAU/USD) retreated after a strong start to the week, opening with a bullish gap amid rising geopolitical tensions. The conflict between the US and Iran, following strikes in Israel, triggered an immediate surge in demand for safe-haven assets. Gold surged as investors sought refuge from potential market volatility, pushing the price up near $1,930 per ounce, before easing back to $1,920.

The US dollar (USD) remained volatile, driven by market reactions to the Middle East conflict, which also affected the broader forex market. The geopolitical unrest supported a flight to safety, sending gold prices higher initially. However, as traders digested the situation, gold’s gains moderated, and the XAU/USD pair found resistance at the $1,940 level, reflecting caution among market participants. The ongoing developments in the region continue to influence market sentiment and could keep the precious metal in focus in the days ahead.
