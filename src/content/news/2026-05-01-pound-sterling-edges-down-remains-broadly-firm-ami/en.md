The Pound Sterling (GBP) edged lower against its major peers, dipping slightly to 1.3590 against the US Dollar (USD) during the European trading session on Friday. Despite this minor decline, the British currency remains broadly firm, supported by market expectations of a more hawkish stance from the Bank of England (BoE) in the near term. 

GBP/USD is holding above key support levels, reflecting investor optimism regarding the UK’s economic outlook. As the BoE continues to signal potential tightening of monetary policy, traders are positioning for further strength in the Pound. This sentiment is in contrast to the broader strength of the USD, driven by ongoing interest rate hikes by the Federal Reserve. 

With the current exchange rate hovering around 1.3590, traders are closely watching upcoming economic data for any signs of a shift in market expectations. The Pound's resilience, in the face of a slight pullback, suggests continued confidence in the UK economy, although market volatility may remain elevated ahead of the next BoE meeting.
