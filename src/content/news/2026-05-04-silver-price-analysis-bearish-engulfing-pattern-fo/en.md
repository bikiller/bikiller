Silver (XAG/USD) fell sharply on Monday, dropping over 3% as the US Dollar gained on safe-haven flows. The currency pair touched a high of $76.00 before retreating, and at present trades near $72.74. Rising geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, coupled with the US Navy’s involvement in Donald Trump’s 'Operation Freedom,' boosted demand for USD, putting pressure on silver’s exchange rate.  

Technically, XAG/USD formed a bearish engulfing pattern, signaling potential downside risk toward the $70 level. Traders are monitoring this key support closely, as further USD strength could weigh on the silver market. The sharp intraday swings highlight the sensitivity of precious metals to shifts in broader forex markets, especially amid geopolitical uncertainty affecting EUR/USD and other major currency pairs.  

Market participants are positioning for continued volatility, with trading activity driven by risk-off sentiment. Analysts note that if safe-haven demand persists, silver could face additional pressure, while any de-escalation in US-Iran tensions might provide relief. For now, XAG/USD remains under scrutiny, as technical signals and macroeconomic factors interplay to shape short-term price dynamics.
