Tensions in the Middle East have reinforced the appeal of traditional safe-haven currencies, with the US Dollar and Swiss Franc seeing increased demand. Societe Generale analysts note that while macroeconomic data remains supportive for risk assets, geopolitical uncertainty is dominating trading sentiment. Investors appear to favor the USD in currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY, pushing exchange rates toward key technical thresholds.  

The Dollar index has strengthened amid safe-haven flows, while EUR/USD has slipped below the 1.0850 support level, reflecting cautious positioning ahead of the weekend. Trading volumes suggest that market participants are weighing both economic releases and geopolitical developments, with a lack of immediate resolution in Middle East tensions likely to sustain demand for defensive currencies.  

Looking ahead, analysts expect continued volatility in forex markets as risk sentiment fluctuates. Should hostilities ease, the USD may retreat slightly, allowing higher-yielding currencies to recover. However, without a de-escalation, the focus will remain on safe-haven trading, with exchange rates in EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and USD/JPY likely to reflect defensive positioning, while the Swiss Franc may continue to serve as a parallel refuge in cross-currency pairs.
