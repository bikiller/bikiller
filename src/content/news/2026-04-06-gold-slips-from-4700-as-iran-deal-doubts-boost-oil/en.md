Gold (XAU/USD) retreated from its intraday peak of $4,706 on Monday, weighed down by rising geopolitical tensions and a firmer US Dollar. Traders noted that hopes for a swift Iran nuclear deal faded, as officials signaled that negotiations were unlikely to produce an immediate agreement. The prospect of renewed sanctions and potential military action pushed oil prices higher, drawing safe-haven flows away from gold.  

The yellow metal’s pullback came amid active USD strength across major currency pairs, with the greenback gaining against the EUR and JPY. Technical analysts highlighted that XAU/USD tested support near $4,680 before buyers stepped in, indicating short-term consolidation. Market participants balanced the bearish geopolitical catalyst with underlying inflation hedging, keeping trading volatile through the North American session.  

Investors are closely watching the interplay between oil, the USD, and gold. Rising crude benchmarks, driven by Iran uncertainties, may continue to support energy-linked currencies and risk assets, while gold faces headwinds as the safe-haven demand shifts. Traders are likely to monitor key levels around $4,680–$4,700 for potential breakout or further pullback in the coming days, with exchange rate movements in EUR/USD and USD/JPY shaping broader market sentiment.
