USD/CAD extended its rally to a sixth consecutive day, trading around 1.3910 on Monday, up 0.12% on the session. The currency pair is benefiting from a broadly stronger US Dollar, which has gained traction as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East stoke risk aversion. Traders have favored the greenback over commodity-linked currencies, boosting USD/CAD and maintaining its upward momentum.  

Market participants are closely watching the exchange rate for signs of further acceleration. The recent advance reflects heightened demand for safe-haven assets, with the USD outperforming major peers, including the EUR and JPY. Analysts note that any escalation in geopolitical risks could reinforce the current trend, potentially pushing USD/CAD toward its next technical resistance levels near 1.3950.  

Trading activity shows investors adjusting positions amid uncertainty, while the Canadian Dollar faces pressure from weaker risk sentiment and stable oil prices. The six-day winning streak highlights the USD’s resilience and the sensitivity of USD/CAD to global developments. For forex traders, monitoring geopolitical headlines and broader USD trends will remain crucial for navigating potential swings in this key North American currency pair.
