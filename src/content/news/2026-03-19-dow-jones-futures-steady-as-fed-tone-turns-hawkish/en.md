Dow Jones and S&P 500 futures remained steady during European trading hours on Thursday, holding around 46,530 and 6,670, respectively, ahead of the US market open. This stability came despite a more hawkish tone from the Federal Reserve, which has been influencing market sentiment. Investors are cautious, awaiting further signals on potential tightening, as the Fed's stance continues to impact trading in the broader market.

Nasdaq 100 futures saw a slight dip, edging lower by 0.17% to around 24,600. The movement reflects the broader cautious sentiment in global markets, with traders focused on the upcoming economic data and the Federal Reserve's policy decisions. These fluctuations in the futures market are closely monitored by forex traders, with the USD showing some strength in response to the Fed’s rhetoric, potentially influencing key currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY.
