Gold trading remains volatile as investors react to ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Analysts Ewa Manthey and Warren Patterson from ING note that developments around the US–Iran ceasefire continue to drive price swings. The yellow metal is showing heightened sensitivity, with market participants closely watching USD movements, which often influence gold’s exchange rate. Traders are navigating choppy conditions as safe-haven demand fluctuates amid headlines.  

Recent trading saw gold testing key technical levels, reflecting broader risk sentiment across currency pairs. EUR/USD and USD/JPY movements are also feeding into market dynamics, with the dollar’s strength supporting pressure on gold prices. Manthey and Patterson highlight that headline risks are likely to sustain a trading environment marked by rapid shifts, requiring nimble positioning from investors.  

Looking ahead, market participants remain alert to how news from the Middle East may ripple across currencies and commodities. A sustained escalation could bolster USD demand, tightening correlations with gold and other safe-haven assets. ING strategists emphasize that while technical support levels may offer short-term guidance, geopolitical catalysts remain the dominant force shaping trading behavior in the coming sessions.
