The Japanese yen is showing continued pressure against the US dollar, with USD/JPY trading steadily but remaining elevated near the 162 level. According to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret, recent gains have already surpassed levels that could trigger intervention, highlighting ongoing volatility in this key currency pair. Market participants are closely watching the yen’s exchange rate as it tests significant technical thresholds.  

Investors are factoring in expectations for the Bank of Japan, widely anticipated to deliver a 25 basis point rate hike on Tuesday. Forward pricing suggests markets are also pricing in nearly one additional increase by December. This potential tightening stance is contributing to the yen’s recent weakness against the dollar, as traders weigh the implications for currency flows, capital markets, and broader forex sentiment.  

The dynamics in USD/JPY are impacting broader trading strategies across Asian and global markets. Movements in the yen are influencing cross-currency pairs including EUR/JPY, while traders monitor whether further BoJ action could accelerate capital shifts toward the dollar. The combination of technical pressure, central bank guidance, and market positioning is keeping the yen in focus, reinforcing its role as a pivotal factor in forex trading outlooks.
