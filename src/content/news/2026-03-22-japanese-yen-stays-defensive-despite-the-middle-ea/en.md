The Japanese Yen remained under pressure against the US Dollar in early Asian trading on Monday, despite the ongoing escalation in the Middle East, now entering its fourth week. The USD/JPY currency pair edged higher, reflecting a cautious market mood as investors favored the safe-haven appeal of the dollar amid geopolitical tensions. Traders noted that the yen’s limited response suggests underlying domestic factors may be restraining its typical defensive behavior.

Market participants highlighted that the USD/JPY exchange rate is holding above key technical levels, with short-term resistance near 149.20 and support around 148.50. Analysts pointed out that while geopolitical uncertainty usually boosts the yen, persistent USD strength and interest rate differentials continue to weigh on JPY trading. The European session may see further volatility if risk sentiment shifts, potentially impacting EUR/JPY and other yen crosses.

Investors are closely watching how global risk appetite evolves, as prolonged conflicts often drive fluctuations across major currency pairs. A sustained rise in USD/JPY could influence broader forex positioning, including flows into EUR/USD and other dollar-linked assets. Traders are advised to monitor central bank guidance and economic indicators, as these factors may compound the impact of geopolitical developments on currency movements in the near term.
