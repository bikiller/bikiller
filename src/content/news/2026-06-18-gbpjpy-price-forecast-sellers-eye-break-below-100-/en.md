GBP/JPY retreated during Thursday’s North American trading session, slipping 0.21% as the Pound Sterling weakened across forex markets even after the Bank of England (BoE) kept interest rates unchanged. The currency pair peaked near 214.06 earlier in the session before easing to 213.11 at the time of writing, reflecting a mild pullback in the exchange rate amid broader trading volatility and shifting USD and EUR sentiment.

Pound Sterling weakness persists as traders assess the BoE’s steady policy stance against expectations of global rate divergence. Sellers are now focusing on a potential break below the 100-day Simple Moving Average (SMA), a key technical support level that could accelerate downside momentum in the GBP/JPY currency pair and signal further bearish trading pressure in the near term.

Pushing forward, market participants will watch whether yen strength and risk-off flows extend pressure on the exchange rate, particularly if global equity sentiment weakens. A confirmed breakdown below the 100-day SMA could open room for deeper losses in GBP/JPY, while any renewed Pound Sterling recovery or US dollar softness may help stabilize the pair around current trading levels.
