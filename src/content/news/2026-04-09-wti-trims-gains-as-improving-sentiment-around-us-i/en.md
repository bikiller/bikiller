WTI Crude Oil pared earlier gains on Thursday, with prices retreating as sentiment around the US-Iran ceasefire improved. Investors dialed back risk premiums, weighing geopolitical concerns less heavily, while broader market optimism lifted risk assets. The shift coincided with stronger trading in major currency pairs, as the USD softened against the EUR and JPY, reflecting increased appetite for non-dollar holdings amid easing tensions.  

The exchange rate of EUR/USD climbed toward key resistance at 1.1050, while USD/JPY edged lower to 136.80, signaling traders’ willingness to pivot from safe-haven positions. Commodity-linked currencies, including the CAD and NOK, also saw muted gains as WTI held just below $88 per barrel. Analysts note that these levels are technically significant, suggesting potential consolidation unless fresh geopolitical or macroeconomic catalysts emerge.  

Market participants now weigh the sustainability of this optimism, balancing crude volatility with currency flows. The interplay between oil prices and the USD continues to shape forex trading strategies, with WTI fluctuations influencing commodity-linked pairs. Investors are monitoring whether the ceasefire optimism translates into a broader risk-on sentiment, potentially supporting equities while keeping USD exchange rates under pressure.
