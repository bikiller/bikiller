AUD/USD edged higher on Monday, trading at 0.6750, as traders recalibrated their positions following heightened tensions in the Strait of Hormuz over the weekend. As concerns over US-Iran relations grew, market participants sought refuge in risk-sensitive currencies like the Australian Dollar. The softer US Dollar also supported the bullish outlook, with the greenback remaining under pressure due to ongoing market uncertainty.

The RBA's tightening outlook continues to bolster the Aussie, despite potential volatility from geopolitical factors. With investors closely monitoring any developments in US monetary policy, the market remains cautious yet optimistic. This sentiment, coupled with the US Dollar's weakness, has helped push AUD/USD higher. Technical resistance for the currency pair is seen at 0.6800, with support holding firm at 0.6700, signaling potential further gains if the current trend persists.
