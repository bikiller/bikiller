The NZD/USD currency pair continues to trade in negative territory for a sixth consecutive session, hovering near 0.5655 during early European trading hours on Wednesday, with the exchange rate briefly testing the 0.5650 level. The New Zealand dollar remains under pressure as USD demand strengthens across forex markets amid broad dollar strength.

Market pressure is driven by a hawkish Federal Reserve outlook, which continues to support the US dollar and weigh on high-beta currencies such as the New Zealand dollar. Expectations of higher-for-longer US interest rates have reinforced USD buying, keeping the NZD/USD exchange rate on the defensive throughout the trading week.

From a technical perspective, the currency pair is struggling to hold above the 0.5650 support level, with bearish momentum dominating short-term price action. Traders are closely watching upcoming US data and Federal Reserve commentary for fresh signals, as further USD strength could extend losses in the NZD/USD exchange rate in the near term outlook zone.
