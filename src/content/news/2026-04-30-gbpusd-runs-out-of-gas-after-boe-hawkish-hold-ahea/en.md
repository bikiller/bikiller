GBP/USD extended its gains on Thursday, rallying 0.96% to hover near the 1.3600 mark after a volatile trading session. The currency pair initially tested 1.3455 during European morning trade before finding strong support and accelerating higher in the New York afternoon. Traders attributed the moves to a hawkish hold from the Bank of England, which bolstered the pound against the USD amid cautious positioning ahead of Friday’s data-heavy calendar.  

The pound’s advance against the dollar came amid broader market focus on the US economic agenda, with investors weighing the implications of upcoming inflation and employment reports. The exchange rate action reflected renewed appetite for GBP, though technical resistance around 1.3600 may cap further immediate gains. Market participants noted that volatility remains elevated, with swings amplified by thin liquidity and pre-weekend positioning in the forex market.  

Looking ahead, traders are eyeing the Friday deluge of data, which could dictate the next leg for the GBP/USD pair. Moves above 1.3600 may signal sustained momentum, while any reversal could draw the currency back toward the 1.3455 support zone. Overall, the trading session underscored the sensitivity of exchange rates to central bank signals and the interplay between GBP strength and USD performance in global forex markets.
