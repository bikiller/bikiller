Gold (XAU/USD) climbed to a nearly two-week high during Wednesday’s Asian session, approaching the $4,700 mark as traders weighed the potential easing of geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The yellow metal extended its four-day rally as the US dollar weakened, with the USD index sliding amid hopes that the conflict in Iran could resolve sooner than expected. Trading volumes remained active as investors sought safe-haven assets.  

The softer USD provided relief across major currency pairs, with the EUR/USD and GBP/USD seeing modest gains as traders rotated into riskier positions. Technical analysts noted that a sustained move above $4,700 could open the door for further upside in gold, while any retracement might test support near $4,650. Market sentiment continues to hinge on both geopolitical developments and central bank signals affecting currency exchange rates.  

Traders remain attentive to upcoming economic data that could influence USD strength, including US inflation metrics and treasury yields. Meanwhile, the broader forex market reacted to the geopolitical optimism, with gold’s surge reflecting a typical safe-haven response. Analysts suggest that continued USD softness could support not only XAU/USD but other precious metals and commodity-linked currencies in the near term.
