The Dow Jones Industrial Average dropped further on Thursday, dipping below 46,000 as rising Crude Oil prices fueled growing concerns of stagflation in the US economy. The spike in energy prices has intensified inflationary pressures, impacting investor sentiment. This shift caused the USD to weaken, with the EUR/USD trading higher near 1.0800, reflecting a broader risk-off sentiment across global markets.

As equity markets continue to struggle under geopolitical uncertainty, the fallout from the ongoing Iran conflict further dampened market optimism. The US dollar weakened against most major currencies, with USD/JPY dipping below 145, while the overall risk aversion led to stronger demand for safe-haven assets like gold. The implications of escalating tensions and rising inflation have led traders to adjust their expectations for future Fed rate hikes, with the market now pricing in slower tightening.
