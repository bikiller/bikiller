China's economy kicked off 2026 on a strong note, with fixed-asset investment rebounding sharply, supported by a surge in quasi-fiscal policies. This fiscal stimulus is expected to buffer the impact of the ongoing oil price shock, which has seen crude trading at higher levels. The shift in investment momentum helped stabilize market sentiment, influencing the yuan’s exchange rate, with USD/CNY trading at 6.45, reflecting a moderate uptick.

Despite the oil price rise, TD Securities forecasts that China's targeted fiscal measures will keep the economy on track. This is crucial for the broader forex market, particularly for USD/CNY and EUR/USD pairs, where volatility could emerge as traders adjust expectations. The ongoing support from government policies will likely temper fears over inflationary pressures, providing a cushion for the yuan in the coming months.
