Gold (XAU/USD) faced significant downward pressure on Tuesday, retreating by nearly $100 from its daily peak around $5,380. The shift occurred as the US Dollar (USD) surged to its highest level since January 20, intensifying the bearish sentiment surrounding the precious metal. Despite ongoing tensions in the Middle East, the strengthening of the USD played a pivotal role in driving gold lower.

As the USD continues to climb, bolstered by robust demand in global markets, the exchange rate for XAU/USD slipped below the critical $5,300 mark. Traders are focusing on the Dollar's momentum, with the EUR/USD also feeling the effects of USD strength. The shift suggests that, while geopolitical concerns remain, USD buying is currently overshadowing gold's safe-haven appeal.
