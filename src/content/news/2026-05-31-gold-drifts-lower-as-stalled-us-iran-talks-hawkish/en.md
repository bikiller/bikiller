Gold prices edged lower on Monday, with XAU/USD slipping to near $4,535 during the early Asian trading session and ending a two-day winning streak. The precious metal came under pressure as investors reacted to the continued lack of progress in US-Iran peace negotiations, reducing demand for traditional safe-haven assets. The move also coincided with a firmer USD, which weighed on gold by making the metal more expensive for holders of other currencies.

Market sentiment was further influenced by expectations that the Federal Reserve will maintain a hawkish policy stance for longer. Higher interest rate expectations tend to support the US Dollar and Treasury yields, limiting the appeal of non-yielding assets such as gold. As a result, traders in both the commodities and forex markets closely monitored shifts in the USD exchange rate against major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, for clues on broader market direction.

The decline in XAU/USD highlights the delicate balance between geopolitical developments and monetary policy expectations. While lingering global uncertainties continue to provide some underlying support for bullion, the combination of stalled diplomatic efforts and a resilient USD may keep gold under pressure in the near term. Investors will likely focus on upcoming economic data and Fed commentary for fresh trading catalysts.
