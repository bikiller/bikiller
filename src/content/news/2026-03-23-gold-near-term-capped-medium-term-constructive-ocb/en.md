Gold prices are facing pressure as rising global yields and higher real rates weigh on the precious metal, dampening near-term expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts. The current trading environment reflects renewed inflation concerns, which, combined with ETF outflows and stress-driven liquidations, have added to downside risks. As of now, gold has been capped in the near term, trading lower against the USD.

Despite these challenges, OCBC strategists remain constructive on gold's medium-term outlook. The combination of a still-supportive inflation backdrop and potential future uncertainty around the Fed's monetary policy could lead to upward momentum in the longer run. Gold’s resilience may come into play if market stress persists, particularly if global risk sentiment continues to shift. The current market environment reflects the ongoing tug-of-war between inflationary pressures and broader economic dynamics.
