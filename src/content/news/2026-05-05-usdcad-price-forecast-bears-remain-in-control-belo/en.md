The USD/CAD currency pair remains under pressure, trading in a tight range on Tuesday as bearish sentiment dominates below the 1.3700 level. The pair recently hit an intraday low of 1.3604 and is currently around 1.3619. Traders are closely monitoring commodity-linked dynamics, as the Canadian Dollar (CAD) faces modest headwinds from a slight pullback in oil prices.

Market participants note that the exchange rate’s choppy movements reflect broader uncertainty in the forex market, with USD strength offsetting some of the CAD’s commodity support. Technical indicators suggest that resistance near 1.3700 continues to cap upside attempts, keeping the bears in control. Short-term trading strategies may focus on key support and resistance levels to gauge potential breakout or continuation scenarios.

Looking ahead, any renewed weakness in crude could further pressure the CAD, potentially pushing the USD/CAD higher toward test levels above 1.3700. Conversely, a rebound in oil prices might provide the CAD with enough momentum to challenge near-term resistance. Traders are advised to watch intraday volatility and economic releases from both the U.S. and Canada, as these could drive sudden shifts in the currency pair’s trading behavior.
