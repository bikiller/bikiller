Gold edged lower to hover near $4,605 during early Asian trading on Monday, reflecting mild losses amid rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The XAU/USD pair showed limited volatility, with traders weighing the impact of escalating regional conflicts against safe-haven demand. Market participants are also keeping an eye on broader currency markets, including USD and EUR movements, as risk sentiment continues to shape trading flows.  

Analysts noted that gold’s recent pullback comes despite ongoing uncertainties, as the metal remains sensitive to shifts in USD exchange rates. A stronger dollar typically dampens gold demand, while any escalation in regional tensions could trigger renewed buying. Technical levels around $4,600 are seen as key support, with resistance near $4,620 likely to cap short-term rallies.  

Focus now turns to U.S. monetary signals, with Federal Reserve Bank of New York President John Williams scheduled to speak later Monday. His comments are expected to influence USD sentiment, which in turn could drive trading in gold and other major currency pairs. Traders remain alert to shifts in both geopolitical risk and central bank guidance as they position for the week ahead.
