Gold (XAU/USD) extended its rally on Wednesday, climbing above $4,650 during the Asian session to mark a fresh weekly high. The precious metal gained momentum for a second consecutive day, drawing strong interest from traders seeking safe-haven assets. The advance comes amid growing optimism over potential US-Iran diplomatic progress, which has weighed on the US dollar, prompting investors to rotate into alternative stores of value.  

The surge in gold comes as the USD experiences broad-based softness, pressuring major currency pairs and influencing global exchange rates. Analysts note that the weakening dollar has also supported other commodities and currencies, including the euro, as markets react to geopolitical developments. Traders are closely watching the $4,650 level as a key technical point, with momentum above this threshold suggesting further upside potential in near-term trading.  

Market participants highlight that ongoing US-Iran peace talks could keep sentiment tilted toward risk-sensitive assets, boosting gold and potentially other currencies and commodities linked to USD dynamics. With XAU/USD breaking through weekly resistance, traders may increasingly adjust positions across forex markets, balancing exposure between gold, the dollar, and euro-based trading pairs, while monitoring geopolitical news for clues on the sustainability of current trends.
