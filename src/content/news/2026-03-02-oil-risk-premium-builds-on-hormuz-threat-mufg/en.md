Brent crude prices briefly surged above USD 82, driven by heightened concerns over Middle East supply risks, particularly in the Strait of Hormuz. However, the rally was short-lived, with prices retreating back below the USD 80 mark as traders reassessed the geopolitical threat. The volatility in oil prices has added a risk premium to the market, impacting global trading dynamics.

As a result, the price movement has influenced currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY, with traders adjusting their positions based on the shifting outlook for oil and its potential effect on inflation and interest rates. The brief spike above USD 82 highlighted the sensitivity of oil prices to geopolitical events, while the pullback suggests that traders remain cautious amid broader market conditions. The fluctuations continue to shape exchange rate sentiment as markets digest these developments.
