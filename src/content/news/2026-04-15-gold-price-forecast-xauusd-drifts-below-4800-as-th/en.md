Gold (XAU/USD) experienced a slight pullback on Wednesday after a two-day rally, retreating from session highs above $4,850. The precious metal failed to sustain momentum above this key resistance level and drifted below $4,800 as the US Dollar strengthened. The dollar's recovery was driven by mixed developments regarding Iran, which added uncertainty to global markets.

The US Dollar Index (DXY) saw gains as market participants responded to the geopolitical news. This shift in sentiment dampened demand for gold, traditionally seen as a safe-haven asset. As XAU/USD continues to navigate through fluctuating risk sentiment, traders will be closely monitoring the dollar’s performance and any further shifts in geopolitical tensions. The $4,850 resistance remains a critical level for gold bulls, while support is seen near $4,750.
