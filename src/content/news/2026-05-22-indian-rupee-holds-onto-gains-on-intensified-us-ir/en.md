The Indian Rupee (INR) held onto its gains against the US Dollar (USD) on Friday, extending momentum from Thursday’s session amid renewed optimism over a potential US-Iran deal. The USD/INR currency pair traded near **₹83.45**, reflecting a mild pullback from recent highs, as traders responded to easing geopolitical tensions and improved sentiment across emerging markets. 

Trading volumes remained active in early Asian sessions, with the INR also showing resilience against other major currencies such as the Euro (EUR). Analysts noted that the exchange rate movements were influenced not only by geopolitical developments but also by stable domestic economic indicators and robust foreign capital inflows, which continue to support the rupee’s strength in the forex market.

Market participants are eyeing key technical levels for the USD/INR pair, noting support around **₹83.20** and resistance near **₹83.70**. A sustained move above resistance could signal further upside, while a drop below support might trigger short-term corrections. Forex traders remain cautious yet optimistic, monitoring US-Iran negotiations closely, as any concrete deal could impact not just the INR but broader emerging market currencies in the coming sessions.
