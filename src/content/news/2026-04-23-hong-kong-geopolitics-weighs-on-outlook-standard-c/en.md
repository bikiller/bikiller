The Standard Chartered report highlights a decline in the Hong Kong SME Leading Business Index to 43.3 in Q2, signaling a dip in economic sentiment. This downturn is largely attributed to rising oil prices and escalating tensions in the Middle East, which are impacting investor confidence and market stability.

As geopolitical uncertainties continue to unfold, the implications for currency pairs involving the Hong Kong dollar (HKD) may be significant. Traders are closely monitoring exchange rates, particularly against the USD and EUR, as these factors could influence trading strategies and capital flows in the region. With the index reflecting weaker business outlooks, market participants may brace for potential volatility in the forex market.
