Silver (XAG/USD) staged a modest rebound on Monday after dipping to year-to-date lows in early Asian trading, reflecting cautious optimism amid market uncertainty. The currency pair found temporary support near $23.45 per ounce, recovering slightly as traders weighed the implications of geopolitical headlines concerning escalating tensions between the US, Israel, and Iran. The rebound, however, remains fragile, with bearish momentum still dominating technical charts.

Market participants continue to monitor broader forex flows, with the USD index exerting upward pressure on precious metals. Gains in the greenback have constrained XAG/USD, even as safe-haven demand surged briefly. EUR/USD and other major currency pairs have shown muted reactions, suggesting traders are selectively adjusting positions rather than executing broad risk-off strategies, keeping silver’s recovery limited.

Analysts caution that unless silver can reclaim resistance near $24.10, the prevailing downtrend is likely to resume. Short-term traders may see the bounce as an opportunity for profit-taking, but the combination of elevated USD strength and geopolitical uncertainty could weigh on exchange rates across metals and forex markets. Investors remain attentive to headlines that could spark sudden moves in XAG/USD and related currency pairs.
