The Japanese Yen (JPY) recovered from early losses against the US Dollar (USD) during Tuesday’s European trading session. After dipping to a low of 134.13, the USD/JPY currency pair regained momentum, trading around 133.80 as traders reacted to ongoing developments in the global economic landscape. The Fed's stance on interest rates and the Bank of Japan's (BoJ) monetary policy continue to drive investor sentiment.

As attention shifts toward upcoming Fed and BoJ policy meetings, traders are closely monitoring any signals of tighter US monetary policy or continued easing by the BoJ. The recent moves highlight the diverging central bank strategies, keeping volatility high in the USD/JPY exchange rate. Investors are now positioned for potential shifts in these policy stances, which could lead to further fluctuations in the currency pair.
