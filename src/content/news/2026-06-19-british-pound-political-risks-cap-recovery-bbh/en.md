GBP/USD has staged a modest rebound in the foreign exchange market after holding firm above its late-March low, according to Brown Brothers Harriman strategist Elias Haddad. The currency pair’s exchange rate has stabilised in recent trading sessions, but broader trading sentiment remains cautious as political risks continue to weigh on the British pound’s recovery potential against the US dollar.

Analysts note that lingering political uncertainty in the UK is limiting upside momentum for sterling, even as risk sentiment in global forex trading improves. The GBP/USD currency pair struggles to build on gains, with resistance emerging as investors reassess the outlook for the exchange rate versus the USD amid shifting rate expectations.

Looking ahead, Brown Brothers Harriman maintains a cautious outlook, suggesting risks for GBP/USD remain tilted to the downside. While short-term stability above the late-March low offers some support, sustained recovery in the exchange rate will depend on clearer political signals and improved demand for the British pound against the US dollar in broader forex markets.
