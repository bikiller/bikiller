Gold (XAU/USD) regained momentum on Monday, climbing back after earlier intraday losses as traders reacted to easing US Treasury yields and rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The currency pair traded near $4,576, rebounding from an Asian session low of $4,419. The shift in expectations around US interest rates added fuel to the rally, supporting gold as a safe-haven asset amid market uncertainty.  

Investors also weighed broader currency markets, with the USD softening against major pairs like the EUR and JPY. The decline in Treasury yields reduced the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets such as gold, prompting increased buying activity. Traders noted that the exchange rate movements reflect both risk-off sentiment and the market’s sensitivity to geopolitical developments.  

Technically, XAU/USD’s rebound from $4,419 indicates potential support in the near term, while the climb toward $4,576 suggests upside momentum may persist if tensions escalate further. Analysts highlighted that volatility in both currency pairs and commodity markets could continue, keeping trading ranges wide. Market participants are monitoring US economic data and Middle East developments closely, as these factors are likely to shape short-term direction for gold and related currency pairs.
