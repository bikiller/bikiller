The US Dollar Index (DXY) remains broadly firm above the 100.00 mark, trading near 100.15 in Monday’s Asian session after retreating from an intraday peak of 100.35. The index, which measures the USD against six major currencies including the EUR, JPY, GBP, CAD, SEK, and CHF, hovers close to a two-week high as traders weigh geopolitical tensions. Market participants are closely monitoring exchange rate movements amid rising concerns over potential escalations in Iran.  

The strength in the DXY has pressured key currency pairs, with EUR/USD dipping as the euro faces renewed selling interest. Investors are favoring the safe-haven US dollar, driving liquidity flows into USD trading and influencing technical levels across forex markets. Analysts note that resistance near 100.35 could limit further gains unless geopolitical risks intensify, while support around 100.00 remains a critical level for traders assessing short-term positioning.  

Market sentiment reflects a cautious approach, with investors reacting to broader risk-off dynamics. The greenback’s resilience may impact other major currency pairs, including GBP/USD and USD/JPY, as traders adjust hedges and speculative positions. Exchange rates are likely to remain sensitive to news from the Middle East, making short-term USD trading volatile and closely tied to developments in global geopolitics.
