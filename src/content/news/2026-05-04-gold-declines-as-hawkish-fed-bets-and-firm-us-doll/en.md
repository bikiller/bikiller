Gold (XAU/USD) opened the week under pressure, trading near one-month lows as investor focus sharpened on hawkish Federal Reserve expectations. The non-yielding metal struggled against a firm US Dollar, with trading activity reflecting caution amid ongoing Middle East tensions. Analysts noted that every move in the USD index continues to weigh on gold, limiting upside potential despite safe-haven demand.  

The yellow metal hovered around key technical support levels, with XAU/USD facing resistance near $2,040 per ounce. Market participants are monitoring Fed signals closely, as higher interest rate bets push real yields up, making gold less attractive. In currency markets, the USD strengthened across major pairs, with EUR/USD retreating toward 1.0920, reflecting broad dollar demand that adds pressure to commodities priced in dollars.  

Traders are balancing geopolitical risks with macroeconomic signals, keeping gold trading volatile. A break below the current support could prompt further declines toward $2,020, while any shift in Fed rhetoric might trigger short-term rebounds. Exchange rate movements remain pivotal, as USD strength continues to influence both gold and other precious metals, making XAU/USD a focal point for cross-asset trading strategies this week.
