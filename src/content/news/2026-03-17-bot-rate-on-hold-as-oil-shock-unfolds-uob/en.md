The Bank of Thailand (BoT) is expected to maintain its 1.00% 1-D Repo Rate through at least Q1 2027, despite the pressure from rising inflation driven by the ongoing oil shock. UOB economists Enrico Tanuwidjaja and Sathit Talaengsatya predict the BoT will keep its policy stance unchanged, focusing on long-term stability rather than responding aggressively to short-term price fluctuations. This outlook is shaping trading sentiment in the Thai baht, which could experience some volatility in the near term.

As oil prices continue to surge, inflationary pressures are expected to impact the broader market, with potential ramifications for USD/THB and EUR/THB exchange rates. Higher energy costs are likely to push up inflation metrics, particularly in sectors sensitive to fuel prices. However, BoT's cautious approach suggests that the central bank will prioritize economic growth, rather than tightening monetary policy immediately. Traders are watching these developments closely as market participants adjust their positions based on the BoT’s rate decisions and broader economic factors.
