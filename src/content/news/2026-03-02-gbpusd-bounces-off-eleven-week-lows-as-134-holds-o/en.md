GBP/USD dropped by around 0.5% on Monday, briefly reaching an eleven-week low near 1.3310 during early trading. The decline was attributed to a broader sell-off in global markets, driven by concerns over Iran's geopolitical situation. This pressure pushed the exchange rate lower, as traders sought safe-haven assets.

However, the currency pair managed to bounce back in mid-session, recovering to close near the 1.3400 level. Despite the initial weakness, GBP/USD found support at 1.34, indicating a potential base for further consolidation. The movement highlights the market's sensitivity to external risks, with the U.S. dollar maintaining strength amid ongoing geopolitical tensions.
