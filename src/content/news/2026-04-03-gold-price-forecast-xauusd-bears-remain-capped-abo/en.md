Gold (XAU/USD) continues to face resistance near the $4,600 level, following a reversal from weekly highs around $4,800. The currency pair is trading at $4,665, with bullish momentum limited by previous peak levels. Traders remain cautious as gold struggles to break above these ceilings, while the USD shows resilience, influencing the exchange rate dynamics and keeping upward moves contained.  

Market participants are weighing safe-haven demand against stronger USD pressures. Despite a brief surge to $4,800, XAU/USD failed to sustain gains, signaling potential consolidation in the $4,600–$4,700 range. The precious metal’s trading behavior reflects sensitivity to macroeconomic data, including US inflation and EUR/USD fluctuations, which continue to shape investor sentiment.  

Technical analysts note that gold’s inability to surpass prior highs could limit bullish trading strategies, encouraging short-term selling near resistance. Should the currency pair drop below $4,600, further downside toward $4,550 may be tested. For now, gold remains in a tight trading corridor, with volatility likely tied to USD strength, central bank cues, and broader forex market shifts.
