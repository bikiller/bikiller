Gold prices (XAU/USD) have experienced a sharp decline of 2%, dropping to $4,410 during Thursday’s European trading session. This downturn comes amid renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which are impacting global markets and influencing investor sentiment. The safe-haven appeal of gold had been gaining traction, but fears of escalating instability have led to a flight from riskier assets.

The decline brings the gold price closer to crucial support levels, and traders are closely watching the $4,400 mark for signs of further weakness. A break below this level could signal a deeper selloff, potentially opening the door to further losses in the near term. As the market navigates these uncertainties, gold’s performance will likely continue to reflect shifting risk appetite and broader market reactions to ongoing global tensions.
