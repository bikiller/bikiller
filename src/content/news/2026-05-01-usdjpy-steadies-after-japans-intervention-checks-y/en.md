USD/JPY steadied near 156.67 on Friday after plunging to a daily low of 155.48, following two consecutive days of intervention by Japanese authorities aimed at supporting the Yen. The aggressive measures came after the Yen weakened past the psychologically significant 160.00 mark, prompting heavy buying to curb further depreciation. Trading activity remained cautious as the market digested the interventions and assessed the potential for continued central bank involvement.  

Investors noted that the sudden swings in the USD/JPY currency pair underscored heightened volatility in the FX markets, with the dollar reacting sharply to policy actions. Technical traders observed that the 155.50–156.50 zone has become a key battleground, with support holding around Friday’s intraday low and resistance forming near 157.00. Currency markets also saw the dollar broadly mixed against other major pairs, including EUR/USD, as traders weighed risk sentiment alongside Japan’s defensive moves.  

The interventions signal Tokyo’s commitment to curbing excessive Yen weakness, raising questions about the sustainability of current USD/JPY levels. Market participants are now closely monitoring upcoming economic data and any statements from the Bank of Japan, as these could influence both short-term trading flows and longer-term exchange rate trends. For forex traders, the episode reinforces the importance of monitoring central bank actions when positioning in major currency pairs.
