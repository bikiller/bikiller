Gold’s advance is facing headwinds as positioning washouts and CTA selling weigh on the market, according to TD Securities. Senior Commodity Strategist Daniel Ghali highlights that energy importers and Middle Eastern producers are encountering shocks that reduce official sector demand, limiting the metal’s upside. The yellow metal’s trading dynamics are increasingly tied to USD strength and fluctuations in key currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY.  

Recent market activity shows that gold’s correlation with the US dollar remains a dominant driver, as a firmer USD exerts pressure on bullion prices. Technical levels are coming into focus, with near-term support tested around $1,970 per ounce, while resistance sits near $2,020. Traders are reacting to shifts in risk sentiment, with leveraged funds adjusting positions, contributing to increased volatility in both precious metals and associated currency pairs.  

Implications for forex trading are notable as commodity-linked currencies like AUD and CAD respond to gold price swings. Investors are monitoring USD/CHF and EUR/USD exchange rates for hedging opportunities, while central bank movements in energy-exporting economies further influence gold-linked flows. With demand pressures from traditional official buyers easing, the market could see amplified short-term corrections, shaping both forex strategies and cross-asset positioning.
