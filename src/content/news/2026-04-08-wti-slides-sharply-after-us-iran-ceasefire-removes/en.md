West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude tumbled sharply on Wednesday, dropping over 10% following news of a temporary ceasefire between the United States and Iran. The agreement eased geopolitical tensions, removing the war risk premium that had been underpinning oil prices. Traders reacted swiftly, with WTI retreating toward $75 per barrel, reflecting renewed confidence in supply stability and prompting heavy selling across energy markets.  

The move rippled through forex markets as well, with the USD gaining ground against major currencies. The EUR/USD currency pair slipped below 1.0850 as dollar buying intensified, while USD/JPY traded near 135.20, supported by risk-off sentiment easing and reduced oil-driven inflation concerns. Market participants cited technical support levels being tested in both commodities and FX, signaling potential short-term volatility as traders recalibrated positions.  

Analysts noted that the oil sell-off could relieve inflation pressures in the U.S., potentially influencing the Federal Reserve’s policy outlook. For currencies, commodity-linked pairs like AUD/USD and USD/CAD saw notable swings, with CAD weakening amid lower oil benchmarks. Traders remain focused on follow-up developments in the Middle East and upcoming economic data, which could drive further adjustments in exchange rates and WTI pricing in the days ahead.
