Oil prices edged higher on Thursday, with traders showing limited reaction to recent US–Iran exchanges in the Gulf. UBS Chief Economist Paul Donovan highlighted that markets had largely priced in prior US optimism, shifting attention to Iranian statements. Crude futures held steady, supporting commodity-linked currencies such as the CAD, while USD pairs including USD/EUR remained influenced by broader risk sentiment and geopolitical headlines.  

Market participants noted that the muted price swings reflected narrow margins for speculative trading. The Brent–WTI spread remained within typical ranges, and liquidity conditions kept volatility in check. As investors monitored developments in the Gulf, currencies like the Australian dollar and Norwegian krone saw modest trading gains, underpinned by higher oil and energy-linked inflows. Technical levels for USD/JPY and EUR/USD continued to guide intraday trading strategies.  

Looking ahead, analysts suggest that exchange rates and cross-currency pairs could experience subtle shifts if geopolitical rhetoric intensifies. Commodity-driven FX pairs may react more sharply to confirmed disruptions, while broader USD strength or weakness will hinge on macroeconomic data. Traders remain poised near key support and resistance zones, balancing geopolitical news with central bank outlooks and market fundamentals.
