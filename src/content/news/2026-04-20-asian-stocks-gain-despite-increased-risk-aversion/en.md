Asian stocks surged on Monday, brushing off increased risk aversion driven by escalating tensions in the Strait of Hormuz. Oil prices spiked sharply, with Brent crude rising by 3.5%, intensifying inflation concerns and fueling expectations of further central bank rate hikes. Despite this, major equity indices in Tokyo, Hong Kong, and Shanghai saw solid gains, with the Nikkei 225 advancing 1.2%.

The forex market saw the USD strengthening against several major currencies, with the EUR/USD slipping below 1.05. The rising oil prices added pressure on global inflation, which led traders to adjust their expectations for interest rate hikes by central banks, particularly the Federal Reserve. The USD/JPY reached a two-week high of 142.50, reflecting the increased demand for safe-haven assets amid geopolitical uncertainties. As global markets weigh the implications of higher inflation and tightening monetary policy, forex traders remain cautious in their outlook.
