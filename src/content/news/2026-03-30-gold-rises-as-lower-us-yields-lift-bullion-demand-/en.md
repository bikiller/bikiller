Gold (XAU/USD) extended its gains on Monday, rising nearly 1% as investors sought refuge from falling US Treasury yields. Despite a broadly strong US Dollar, bullion attracted renewed demand, with traders reacting to signals that the Federal Reserve is likely to hold interest rates steady through 2026. The metal’s resilience highlights its role as a safe-haven amid mixed signals in currency markets.  

The dollar’s strength has kept currency pairs like EUR/USD under pressure, yet declining yields have bolstered gold’s appeal. Traders noted that XAU/USD tested key technical levels, maintaining momentum above recent support zones. Market participants are weighing the interplay between exchange rates, Fed policy expectations, and safe-haven flows, driving short-term trading dynamics.  

Analysts suggest that sustained gold demand could influence forex strategies, particularly for USD- and EUR-linked positions. As the Fed’s cautious outlook limits yield-driven opportunities, bullion continues to attract capital seeking stability. For forex traders, watching XAU/USD alongside Treasury movements and USD index trends remains crucial for positioning in the current market environment.
