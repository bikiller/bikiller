The GBP/USD currency pair slid more than 0.55% on Tuesday, retreating amid escalating political uncertainty in the UK. Pressure intensified on Prime Minister Keir Starmer following multiple cabinet resignations, fueling risk aversion in the market. Traders reacted swiftly, sending the exchange rate lower as investors weighed the implications for the British economy and potential policy shifts against the USD and broader FX markets.  

Technical analysis points to a dark-cloud cover formation, signaling potential further losses if sellers gain momentum. A breach below the 1.3500 level could open the door for continued downside in trading sessions ahead, attracting more bearish positioning. Market participants are closely watching price action around this key support, which could define near-term trends in GBP/USD versus the USD and EUR.  

Market sentiment remains fragile, with volatility heightened by political developments and investor uncertainty. Traders in forex markets are likely to adjust positions as the UK political landscape evolves, influencing not just GBP/USD but broader cross-currency flows. Continued monitoring of both technical signals and macro drivers will be crucial for assessing the pair’s trajectory in the coming days.
