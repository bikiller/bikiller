WTI crude oil prices hovered just below $90.00 on Thursday, showing mild gains but largely confined within the previous session’s trading range. Traders remained cautious as renewed optimism over potential US-Iran peace talks tempered bullish momentum. The market reaction suggests investors are weighing geopolitical developments against broader macroeconomic signals, particularly the impact of oil prices on USD strength and global currency pairs.  

The energy market’s cautious tone rippled through forex trading, with the USD index edging higher amid steady crude prices, while EUR/USD and other major currency pairs experienced subdued volatility. Analysts note that sustained oil levels near $90.00 could support USD appreciation, influencing cross-rates across commodity-linked currencies such as CAD and NOK. Momentum indicators point to consolidation, leaving traders alert for breakouts in either direction.  

Market participants are closely monitoring both geopolitical cues and technical thresholds, as oil’s limited movement below $90.00 may keep exchange rates in tight ranges. Any escalation in US-Iran talks or shifts in inventory reports could trigger sharper price swings, affecting forex liquidity and trading strategies. Investors are positioning cautiously, balancing energy-driven market sentiment with broader USD trends and interest rate expectations.
