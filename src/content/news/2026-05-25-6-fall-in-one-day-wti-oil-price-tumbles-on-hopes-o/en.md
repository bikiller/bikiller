West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures on NYMEX plunged 6% to near $90 during Monday’s European trading session, marking the lowest level in more than two weeks as investors reacted to growing expectations that the Strait of Hormuz could reopen. The sharp decline in crude prices weakened demand for safe-haven assets, pressuring the USD exchange rate against major currency pairs while improving sentiment across broader financial markets.

The sell-off in oil triggered fresh volatility in forex trading, with the EUR/USD currency pair edging higher as easing geopolitical tensions reduced concerns over global supply disruptions. Market participants viewed the potential reopening of Hormuz as a signal that crude exports from the Middle East may stabilize, lowering inflation fears and reducing pressure on central banks to maintain aggressive monetary policy tightening.

Traders are now closely monitoring whether WTI can hold above the psychological $90 support level after the steep one-day decline. A sustained recovery in risk appetite could continue to influence USD performance against the EUR and other major currencies, while weaker energy prices may also support global growth expectations. Analysts expect further movement in oil-linked currency pairs as trading volumes pick up through the week.
