The USD/JPY currency pair is showing signs of consolidation after failing to maintain a breakout above its long-term trading range, according to Societe Generale analysts. The exchange rate remains above the 50-day moving average, signaling cautious trading amid a backdrop of hawkish monetary sentiment. Traders are closely watching oil prices, which have provided some defensive support to the dollar, cushioning the pair against sharper declines.  

Technical levels are shaping market expectations, with support identified between 158.30 and 157.50, while resistance remains near 160.50. These zones are likely to influence short-term trading strategies, as investors weigh the dollar’s strength against the yen. The inability to sustain a breakout suggests that momentum may be limited, and market participants are monitoring both commodities and central bank cues for directional guidance.  

For forex traders, the interplay between oil prices and USD/JPY movements highlights the broader market dynamics affecting major currencies. While the dollar maintains its footing, the pair’s struggle to break above 160.50 underscores a cautious approach in trading positions. With key support levels holding firm, the exchange rate could see a period of range-bound activity, offering potential opportunities for both intraday and swing traders.
