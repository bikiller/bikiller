WTI hovers around $80.10 per barrel during Asian trading hours on Tuesday, edging higher after a sharp 3.7% decline in the previous session. The rebound remains modest as traders reassess risk sentiment across commodities and currency pairs, with USD movements influencing broader market liquidity. Despite the uptick, momentum stays cautious, reflecting uncertainty in energy trading conditions.

Ongoing market attention centers on developments in US-Iran peace talks, with traders weighing potential implications for global crude supply and geopolitical risk premiums. Any easing of tensions could pressure oil prices further, while stalled negotiations may support upside volatility. In parallel, shifts in USD strength and broader forex trading dynamics continue to shape commodity-linked currency pairs such as USD/CAD and EUR/USD sentiment.

Overall trading conditions suggest oil remains range-bound near the $80 psychological level, with participants closely monitoring support and resistance zones for short-term positioning. Currency exchange rate fluctuations, particularly in USD-denominated assets, are amplifying cross-market sensitivity. Until clearer geopolitical direction emerges, both energy and forex markets are likely to maintain cautious, event-driven trading behavior.
