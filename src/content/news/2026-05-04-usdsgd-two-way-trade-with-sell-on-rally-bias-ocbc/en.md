USD/SGD traded lower into the New York close, with the currency pair easing from recent highs amid broad market shifts. Strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong of OCBC highlighted that a sharp drop in Brent crude weighed on risk sentiment, while a retreat in USD/JPY alleviated immediate yield and inflation concerns. The combination of these factors supported a pullback in the exchange rate, offering traders opportunities to sell on rallies.  

Market participants noted that the sell-on-rally bias remained intact, with USD/SGD exhibiting two-way trading patterns. The move reflected cautious positioning ahead of key economic data, as investors balanced the appeal of USD strength against easing inflation pressures. EUR/USD and other major currency pairs also saw moderate adjustments, with traders reacting to shifts in energy prices and interest rate expectations.  

Technically, the pair is poised for further volatility as market attention shifts to global growth signals and monetary policy guidance. Analysts suggest monitoring resistance and support levels closely, as short-term fluctuations may provide tactical trading opportunities. With USD/SGD showing sensitivity to both commodity moves and broader FX dynamics, the currency pair’s direction will remain linked to cross-market developments in the coming sessions.
