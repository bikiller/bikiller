The USD/CAD currency pair has maintained a range-bound downside bias, with the Canadian Dollar showing notable resilience amid the ongoing US-Iran tensions. According to Scotiabank analysts Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret, the CAD remains the only major currency that has not weakened against the US Dollar since the conflict escalated. Despite the broader market volatility, the CAD has held its ground.

The USD/CAD exchange rate continues to trade in a narrow range, with technical levels suggesting limited downside potential for the Canadian Dollar. The pair's movement is largely influenced by geopolitical concerns, but the CAD’s stability is a positive signal for the currency. Traders are closely monitoring key support and resistance levels to gauge the pair’s future direction, with the Canadian Dollar showing an unexpected strength in comparison to its peers.
