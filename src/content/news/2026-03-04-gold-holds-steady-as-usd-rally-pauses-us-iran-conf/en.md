Gold (XAU/USD) held steady on Wednesday, recovering some of Tuesday’s sharp losses as the US Dollar (USD) paused after a two-day rally. The precious metal traded at $1,920 per ounce, finding support amid a cooling USD. This pause in USD strength allowed gold to stabilize, while still feeling the pressure from the recent declines.

Geopolitical tensions surrounding the US-Iran conflict continued to underpin demand for safe-haven assets. The persistent uncertainty over the conflict kept traders wary, supporting gold prices despite the USD’s momentary pullback. As investors remain cautious, XAU/USD holds a delicate balance, vulnerable to further USD fluctuations and shifts in geopolitical risk sentiment.
