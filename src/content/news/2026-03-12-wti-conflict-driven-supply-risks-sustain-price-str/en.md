WTI crude oil has maintained its upward momentum, climbing above $90 per barrel, as conflict-driven supply concerns continue to dominate market sentiment. DBS Group Research’s Eugene Leow points out that even with the planned release of strategic reserves and expectations of a brief conflict, traders remain wary of potential disruptions, supporting strong buying interest in energy markets. The USD has broadly firmed against major currencies, reflecting risk-averse positioning amid these uncertainties.  

The resilience in oil prices has influenced commodity-linked currencies, with the CAD strengthening against the USD as crude-linked flows intensify. Similarly, the NOK and RUB have seen more active trading, responding to higher energy valuations. Exchange rates like EUR/USD and GBP/USD have experienced mild fluctuations, reflecting market sensitivity to geopolitical tensions rather than central bank-driven dynamics.  

Analysts suggest that sustained oil strength could underpin broader inflation expectations, impacting USD trading patterns and cross-currency valuations. Technical levels around $90 per barrel serve as a psychological benchmark, with WTI trading above this mark likely to influence hedging and speculative flows. Market participants are closely watching both conflict developments and inventory releases, as these factors continue to shape liquidity and volatility in forex and commodities alike.
