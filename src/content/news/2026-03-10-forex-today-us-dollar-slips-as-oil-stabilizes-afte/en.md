The US Dollar weakened in today’s trading as rising market optimism and stabilizing oil prices eroded demand for safe-haven assets. Oil prices found support following comments from former President Trump, which eased concerns over global supply disruptions. The EUR/USD pair saw a modest rise, climbing to 1.0850, while the USD/JPY dipped to 134.45, reflecting a shift in investor sentiment towards riskier assets.

Oil's stabilization at around $75 per barrel has led to a more favorable environment for currencies tied to risk, dampening the USD’s strength. The Greenback faced additional pressure as global equities gained traction, with the S&P 500 advancing 0.6%. The USD/CHF slipped to 0.9075, marking a decline of 0.5%. As investors shift focus to more profitable positions, the outlook for the USD remains uncertain, with key levels at 1.0900 for EUR/USD and 135.00 for USD/JPY in focus.
