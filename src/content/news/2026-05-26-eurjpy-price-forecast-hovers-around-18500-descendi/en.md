The EUR/JPY currency pair edged lower during Tuesday’s Asian trading session, slipping toward 184.90 after recording modest gains in the previous session. The exchange rate remained close to the psychologically important 185.00 level as traders monitored price action near the upper boundary of a descending channel pattern, a technical zone that continues to cap bullish momentum in the cross.

Market participants remained cautious amid mixed sentiment across global forex markets, with the EUR finding limited support against the Japanese Yen despite steady demand for risk-sensitive assets. Technical traders noted that failure to break above the descending channel top could reinforce near-term bearish pressure, while sustained trading above 185.00 may open the door for a stronger recovery move in the currency pair.

The EUR/JPY cross also reflected broader market positioning around the USD and global bond yields, as investors assessed monetary policy expectations from major central banks. Analysts said the pair’s near-term direction will likely depend on whether buyers can maintain momentum above key support zones, while resistance near the channel ceiling continues to shape short-term trading strategies in the forex market.
