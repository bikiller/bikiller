The EUR/JPY currency pair regained traction early Monday, climbing toward 184.80 after breaking a four-day losing streak. Trading in the European session showed the exchange rate strengthening as market participants reacted to the European Central Bank’s (ECB) hawkish comments, supporting the Euro against the Japanese Yen. Despite this upward momentum, the pair remains capped below the Bollinger midpoint, signaling potential resistance ahead.

Traders are watching the EUR closely, as the ECB’s stance has influenced broader forex flows, with the EUR/USD also reflecting underlying strength. The technical barrier near the Bollinger midpoint suggests that while short-term gains are possible, upside could be limited unless market sentiment shifts further. This dynamic has kept the pair contained, even amid the renewed buying pressure.

Market implications point to cautious optimism for EUR/JPY, with momentum trading likely to continue if support levels around 184.50 hold. A break above the Bollinger midpoint could accelerate gains and attract more USD and EUR-based speculative flows, while failure to surpass resistance may lead to consolidation or retracement. Forex traders are balancing central bank cues with technical signals to gauge the next move in this major cross.
