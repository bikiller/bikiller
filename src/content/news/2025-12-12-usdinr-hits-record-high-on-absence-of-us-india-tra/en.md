The Indian Rupee (INR) continued its downward trajectory against the US Dollar (USD) on Friday, with the USD/INR currency pair reaching a record high of 90.86. This milestone underscores the ongoing challenges faced by the INR, exacerbated by a lack of progress in trade negotiations between the United States and India, which has left market participants cautious.

The persistent weakness in the INR reflects broader concerns surrounding India's economic outlook and trade dynamics. As the exchange rate climbs, traders are closely monitoring the impact on inflation and import costs, which could further influence monetary policy decisions in India. The current trading environment raises questions about the INR's resilience and the potential for corrective measures from the Reserve Bank of India in response to this significant depreciation.
