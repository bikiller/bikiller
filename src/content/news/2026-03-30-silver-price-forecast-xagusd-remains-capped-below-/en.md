Silver (XAG/USD) advanced on Monday, climbing to around $70.50 as traders reacted to a pullback in US Treasury yields. The currency pair gained nearly 1% on the day, reflecting renewed demand amid a cautious reassessment of the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy path. Market participants remain watchful of interest rate signals, which continue to influence the USD and broader precious metals trading.  

Despite the uptick, XAG/USD remains capped below its 100-day simple moving average, keeping downside risks on the table. Technical analysts note that failure to breach this key level could invite further selling pressure, especially if the USD strengthens against other major currencies like the EUR. Traders are weighing near-term support zones against potential resistance, making the exchange rate sensitive to shifts in sentiment and macroeconomic indicators.  

The interplay between Treasury yields, Fed guidance, and USD strength is likely to steer silver trading this week. While short-term momentum supports a cautious rally, persistent technical barriers suggest limited upside. Currency pair movements remain closely tied to macro developments, with market participants carefully monitoring economic data, policy cues, and the evolving dynamics between the USD, EUR, and other major currencies.
