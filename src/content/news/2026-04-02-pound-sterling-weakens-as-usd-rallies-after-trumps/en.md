The Pound Sterling came under pressure against the US Dollar during Thursday’s Asian session, with the GBP/USD currency pair retreating from its recent weekly high near 1.3345. Spot trading showed the pair slipping to the mid-1.3200s as fresh selling interest emerged, reflecting renewed strength in the USD following comments from US President Donald Trump. Traders highlighted the sharp contrast between yesterday’s highs and the current pullback, suggesting short-term resistance around 1.3345 remains intact.  

Market participants attributed the USD rally to risk-on sentiment sparked by Trump’s address to the nation, which boosted investor confidence in the greenback. The exchange rate move signals growing demand for USD in both safe-haven and trading flows, pressuring major pairs like GBP/USD. Analysts noted that technical levels around 1.3200 could act as support, while a sustained break below might open the way for further declines toward 1.3150.  

The broader forex market reflected mixed sentiment, with Sterling facing headwinds against a broadly stronger USD, while the EUR/USD pair held relatively steady amid muted European data. Traders are weighing political developments and central bank cues, balancing the pull of USD strength against Sterling’s resilience. For GBP/USD, near-term trading may hinge on whether bulls can reclaim 1.3300 or if sellers maintain control toward mid-1.3200s.
