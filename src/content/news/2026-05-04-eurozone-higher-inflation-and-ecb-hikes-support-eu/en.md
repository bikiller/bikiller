Eurozone economic growth is set to slow, with BNP Paribas forecasting a dip in GDP growth from 1.5% in 2025 to 1.0% in 2026. While this slowdown may weigh on the broader economy, inflation is projected to rebound, reaching 3.0% in 2026 and 3.3% by 2027. These dynamics are expected to continue supporting the Euro, keeping EUR/USD exchange rates elevated.

The European Central Bank's interest rate hikes have already played a key role in strengthening the Euro. As inflation rises, expectations of further ECB tightening could boost the EUR further against the USD in the near term. Traders will closely monitor these economic indicators, as any shift in ECB policy or inflation projections could trigger significant movements in EUR/USD and other Euro crosses.
