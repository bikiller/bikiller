NZD/USD extended its rebound on Tuesday, with the currency pair trading near 0.5900, up 0.20% on the day after recovering from Monday’s low around 0.5850. The exchange rate’s upward move reflects renewed buying interest in the New Zealand Dollar, as traders respond to stronger-than-expected domestic inflation data that has shifted sentiment in favor of further policy tightening.

Market participants are increasingly pricing in a more hawkish stance from the Reserve Bank of New Zealand, as elevated inflation pressures reinforce expectations of additional rate hikes. This has supported NZD demand against the USD, even as broader forex trading remains sensitive to global risk trends and US monetary policy signals. The shift in rate expectations has helped lift the pair from recent lows.

From a technical perspective, the move above 0.5900 suggests improving short-term momentum, though resistance levels remain in focus if bullish pressure continues. Traders are also monitoring cross-currency dynamics, including NZD performance against EUR and other majors, as well as incoming US data that could influence USD strength and shape the next directional move in the currency pair.
