The Mexican Peso weakened against the USD as the Bank of Mexico (Banxico) slashed interest rates, triggering a 0.13% drop in the currency. The USD/MXN pair climbed to 17.27, recovering from an earlier low of 17.19. Banxico's decision to end its easing cycle added pressure, signaling a shift in the central bank’s stance.

This move, coupled with rising geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, further fueled the Peso’s decline. Traders reacted to Banxico’s announcement, and the market now awaits further developments for clues on the direction of the Peso. The USD/MXN continues to trade in a range as investors reassess risk and policy outlooks.
