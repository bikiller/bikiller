Oil markets are facing intensified pressure as supply disruptions through the Strait of Hormuz deepen, according to Societe Generale strategists Michael Haigh, Ben Hoff, and Jeremy Sellem. The team points to a sharp decline in flows and accelerating shut‑ins, which have sent Brent crude hovering near $92 per barrel, while WTI trades around $87. The tightening supply is fueling volatility, prompting traders to hedge using USD‑denominated instruments.  

The knock-on effects are spilling into forex markets. The euro has softened against the dollar, with the EUR/USD pair sliding toward 1.0725 amid rising energy-linked inflation fears. Currency traders are adjusting positions as central banks weigh potential rate impacts, while commodity-linked currencies like the NOK and CAD show heightened sensitivity to oil-driven swings in exchange rates.  

Technically, Brent’s breach of the $90 support has opened room for further upside, while WTI’s near-term consolidation around $86.50-$88 signals persistent market tension. Forex desks are closely watching these levels, with USD strength reinforcing safe-haven flows. Investors are weighing the broader macro impact, including higher fuel costs potentially influencing trade balances, capital flows, and currency trading strategies across major pairs.
