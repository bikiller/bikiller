EUR/USD continues to face downward pressure, trading around the 1.1600 mark during Wednesday’s European session. This decline marks the third consecutive session of losses for the currency pair, reflecting a bearish sentiment in the market. The release of February's HCOB Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) data from Germany and the Eurozone has contributed to this decline, suggesting a slowdown in economic activity.

As traders digest the implications of the PMI figures, the exchange rate remains subdued, indicating a cautious outlook for the euro against the USD. The continued weakness in the eurozone data could reinforce bearish momentum for EUR/USD, prompting further shifts in trading strategies as investors assess potential impacts on monetary policy and economic growth in the region.
