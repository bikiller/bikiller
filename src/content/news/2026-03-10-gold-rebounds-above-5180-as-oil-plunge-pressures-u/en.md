Gold extended gains on Tuesday, climbing above the $5,180 mark as the US Dollar softened amid falling Oil prices. The currency pair XAU/USD surged to $5,187, reflecting a gain of over 0.50% in early trading. The retreat in the Greenback underscores its close correlation with crude, as investors adjust positions in response to weaker energy markets and broader commodity-driven currency flows.  

The drop in Oil weighed on USD strength, prompting traders to rotate into safe-haven assets such as gold. Market participants noted that the exchange rate movement was influenced by the interplay between crude volatility and the Dollar, which continues to be sensitive to commodity price swings. The XAU/USD pair’s rebound highlights ongoing demand for non-yielding assets amid uncertainty in energy and currency markets.  

Analysts suggest that if Oil remains under pressure, USD could face further softness, potentially supporting additional upside for gold. Traders are watching key technical levels around $5,180-$5,200 for signs of sustained momentum. Currency markets, including major pairs like EUR/USD, remain reactive to these dynamics, emphasizing how commodity trends can ripple through global forex trading.
