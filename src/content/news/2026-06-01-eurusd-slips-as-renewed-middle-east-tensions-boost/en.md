EUR/USD is under renewed pressure as rising tensions in the Middle East weigh on market sentiment. Traders initially cheered the prospect of a US-Iran peace deal, but optimism has faded, triggering a wave of selling. The currency pair is currently trading near 1.1626, down roughly 0.30% on the day, reflecting a stronger USD as investors seek safe-haven assets.

Market participants are closely watching geopolitical developments, with the Greenback benefiting from heightened demand amid uncertainty. Technical indicators suggest that if selling persists, the pair could test lower support levels, while any easing in tensions might offer EUR some relief. Trading volumes have picked up as volatility increases, highlighting the sensitivity of the EUR/USD exchange rate to external shocks.

Analysts note that short-term swings in the currency pair may continue as news headlines drive market behavior. Forex traders are adjusting positions in response to rapid shifts in risk sentiment, emphasizing the USD’s role as a benchmark during periods of instability. The interplay between geopolitical risk and central bank policies remains central to the outlook for EUR/USD in the coming sessions.
