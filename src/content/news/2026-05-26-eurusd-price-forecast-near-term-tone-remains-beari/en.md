EUR/USD edged lower to around 1.1633 in Tuesday’s Asian trading session, with the currency pair remaining under modest pressure as the USD attracted renewed demand. Market sentiment shifted in favor of the greenback amid concerns that US-Iran negotiations may encounter obstacles, prompting traders to seek relative safety in the US Dollar and weighing on the euro’s near-term exchange rate performance.

Technical signals continue to reinforce a bearish outlook for the EUR/USD currency pair, with the downward slope of the 20-day Exponential Moving Average indicating persistent selling momentum. While the pair’s decline remains measured rather than aggressive, the broader trading bias suggests rallies may face resistance unless macro sentiment shifts decisively against the USD in upcoming sessions.

For forex participants, the current market setup points to heightened sensitivity to geopolitical developments and risk sentiment. Any deterioration in diplomatic expectations surrounding the US and Iran could further underpin the USD, pressuring the EUR. Conversely, an improvement in negotiations may ease haven demand, though the euro would still need stronger technical confirmation to reverse the prevailing bearish trend in the exchange rate.
