The Canadian Dollar (CAD) strengthened against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, with USD/CAD edging lower amid shifting headlines surrounding the US-Iran conflict. At the time of writing, the pair was trading around 1.1315, approaching four-month highs. This movement reflects cautious market sentiment as traders monitor developments in the geopolitical landscape.

The evolving situation between the US and Iran continues to weigh on global risk appetite, influencing market decisions. While geopolitical tensions have historically impacted the USD, the Canadian Dollar has been gaining ground, benefiting from a slightly more risk-positive tone in the market. Traders are keenly watching the latest news for any significant shifts in sentiment that could lead to further fluctuations in the USD/CAD exchange rate.
