The Dow Jones Industrial Average fell sharply, losing roughly 380 points, or 0.8%, ending a four-day rally as investor sentiment turned cautious ahead of President Trump’s self-imposed deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Oil prices surged past $116 a barrel, intensifying concerns over energy supply disruptions and fueling volatility across global markets. Traders moved to safer assets, driving the dollar higher against major peers.  

In forex trading, the USD strengthened against the euro and yen, reflecting a flight to safety amid rising geopolitical tensions. The EUR/USD pair edged lower, testing key support levels near 1.0850, while USD/JPY climbed toward 149.50. Currency markets responded to heightened risk sentiment, with traders factoring in potential oil-driven inflation pressures and implications for central bank policy.  

Equity and commodity markets remained sensitive to news from the Middle East, with riskier assets under pressure and safe-haven currencies outperforming. Market participants are closely monitoring geopolitical developments, as any escalation could further support the dollar and yen, while pressuring equity indices and oil-importing economies. Technical indicators suggest short-term resistance for EUR/USD at 1.0920, while USD/JPY may face hurdles near 150.00, keeping traders alert to rapid moves.
