The Pound gained ground after the Bank of England delivered a unanimous hold on interest rates, prompting a sharp repricing at the front end of the curve. MUFG’s Derek Halpenny noted that UK government bond yields rose significantly, lending support to GBP/USD and GBP/EUR trading. The repricing reflects renewed market expectations around the BoE’s future tightening path, pushing the Pound higher in early London session trading.  

Traders saw GBP/USD climbing as investors adjusted positions in response to rising yields, while GBP/EUR exchanged hands near key technical levels. The exchange rate movements underscore the market’s sensitivity to central bank policy signals, with short-term volatility likely as traders weigh the BoE’s cautious stance against persistent inflationary pressures.  

Despite the recent strength, risks remain for sterling. MUFG highlights that further currency gains hinge on economic data and market confidence in the BoE’s inflation outlook. Investors continue monitoring upcoming UK CPI figures and global USD trends, which could prompt shifts in trading flows across major pairs, including GBP/USD and GBP/EUR, affecting both short-term technical setups and broader market sentiment.
