Gold (XAU/USD) gave up its modest intraday gains on Tuesday, retreating towards the lower end of its daily range as market sentiment shifted. The precious metal briefly traded above the key psychological level of $5,000 but struggled to maintain momentum. The pullback came as reduced expectations for a Federal Reserve rate cut rekindled demand for the US Dollar, putting pressure on gold prices.

The currency pair saw a decline as USD demand intensified, following a shift in market expectations regarding US monetary policy. With traders recalibrating their outlook on future Fed actions, gold's gains faltered, and the exchange rate slipped from its earlier highs. As the European session began, XAU/USD found some support, holding steady above the $5,000 mark despite the renewed strength of the USD.
