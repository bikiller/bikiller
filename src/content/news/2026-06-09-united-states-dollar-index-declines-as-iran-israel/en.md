The US Dollar (USD) remained under pressure for a second consecutive session on Tuesday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering around the 100.00 mark during Asian trading hours. Market sentiment improved after Iran and Israel agreed to halt strikes, reducing demand for traditional safe-haven assets and encouraging investors to shift toward higher-risk currency pairs and global markets.

The softer tone in the greenback was reflected across the foreign exchange market, where traders reassessed the outlook for major exchange rates. Easing geopolitical tensions weakened defensive USD positioning, allowing rivals such as the EUR and other major currencies to attract fresh buying interest. The move highlighted how rapidly geopolitical developments can influence trading flows and reshape short-term sentiment.

Despite the decline, the DXY remained close to the psychologically important 100.00 level, which many market participants continue to monitor as a key technical area. Currency traders are likely to watch whether improving risk appetite extends the Dollar's losses or if renewed demand for safe-haven assets supports a rebound. Broader market direction will also depend on incoming economic data and shifts in expectations for US monetary policy.
