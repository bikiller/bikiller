Gold (XAU/USD) trades with a mild negative bias during Wednesday’s Asian session, edging lower as traders adjust positions ahead of the Federal Reserve’s upcoming FOMC policy decision. Despite the soft tone, the currency pair remains firmly supported above the $4,300 level, suggesting underlying bullish resilience. Market participants are broadly lightening long exposure in anticipation of potential USD-driven volatility across forex trading markets.

Recent trading activity reflects cautious sentiment across the broader currency market, with the USD maintaining influence over precious metals pricing and gold exchange rate movements. Investors are repositioning portfolios as expectations around interest rates shift, while EUR and other major currencies remain sensitive to Federal Reserve guidance. The pullback in XAU/USD appears driven more by pre-event consolidation than any decisive trend reversal.

Looking ahead, traders expect heightened volatility around the FOMC announcement, which could reshape USD direction and influence gold trading dynamics. A sustained break above or below key levels around $4,300 will be closely watched in the XAU/USD currency pair. Until then, markets are likely to remain range-bound as participants await clearer policy signals from the Federal Reserve.
