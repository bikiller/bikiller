Gold prices dipped below $4,700 on Tuesday, reversing gains from the previous session as the US Dollar (USD) strengthened amid hotter-than-expected US inflation data. The release of stronger Consumer Price Index (CPI) figures fueled a rise in US Treasury yields, prompting traders to rotate out of non-yielding assets like gold. The XAU/USD currency pair faced selling pressure, reflecting a cautious tone in the precious metals market.

The US Dollar index advanced, lifting major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD, while the rising yield environment drew attention from forex and commodities traders. Gold’s technical support levels around $4,680 were tested as investors assessed the implications of sustained inflation for Federal Reserve policy. Market participants weighed the potential for higher interest rates against lingering geopolitical risks, shaping trading strategies across global forex markets.

Analysts noted that gold’s vulnerability is linked to both USD strength and real yields, with further gains in Treasury yields likely to keep XAU/USD under pressure. Short-term trading could see increased volatility, as shifts in currency exchange rates, particularly in USD versus major currencies, directly influence gold and other safe-haven assets. Investors remain alert for upcoming economic indicators that could alter market sentiment and drive further moves in the trading of precious metals.
