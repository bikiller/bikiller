The US Dollar (USD) has found some footing after news from Iran indicated the ceasefire was breached, injecting fresh volatility into markets. ING strategists Francesco Pesole, Frantisek Taborsky, and Chris Turner highlight that while the USD stabilised, underlying pressures from dual Federal Reserve risks persist, leaving room for potential weakness in the near term. Traders are closely watching how geopolitical tensions interact with US monetary policy expectations.  

Across major currency pairs, the USD showed mixed trading patterns. The EUR/USD exchange rate, for instance, tested key technical levels as investors digested both Fed outlooks and the geopolitical backdrop. Short-term trading flows suggest caution, with the USD under pressure against the euro amid renewed risk appetite, while safe-haven currencies like the JPY continue to attract demand during spikes in uncertainty.  

Market implications remain nuanced. While the ceasefire disruption triggered temporary USD strength, ING analysts see potential for further declines if geopolitical risks ease or market sentiment shifts. Traders are positioning for possible corrections across currency pairs, assessing the interplay between Fed guidance, exchange rate momentum, and geopolitical developments. The focus remains on USD performance in relation to major rivals such as EUR, GBP, and JPY as volatility continues to shape trading strategies.
