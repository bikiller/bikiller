Gold (XAU/USD) holds onto its modest intraday gains, trading just above $5,150 during the Asian session on Wednesday. The precious metal is up around 1.30% for the day, supported by some buying interest, though the upside appears limited as the USD maintains a strong bullish momentum. The dollar’s dominance continues to weigh on gold's price action, limiting its ability to surge higher.

The market's focus remains on the ongoing strength of the USD, which has been a significant factor in capping gold’s rally. Despite a slight upward push, the exchange rate for XAU/USD struggles to break through key resistance levels. With the USD showing resilience, gold’s potential for a meaningful rally appears restricted in the near term, especially as broader market sentiment stays cautious.
