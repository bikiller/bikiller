West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures on NYMEX advanced 1.8% to around $91.20 during Tuesday’s early European trading session, supported by renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East that reinforced concerns over global crude supply. The rebound in oil prices also influenced broader trading sentiment across financial markets, with investors monitoring the impact on the USD exchange rate and inflation-linked currency pairs.

Market participants noted that stronger crude prices could complicate expectations for future Federal Reserve policy, particularly as elevated energy costs tend to support inflationary pressures in the US economy. The move in WTI also attracted attention from EUR/USD and commodity-linked currency pair traders, as higher oil prices often affect risk appetite and demand for safe-haven assets. Despite the bullish momentum, technical indicators suggested caution remained necessary.

From a technical perspective, WTI continues to face resistance from the 20-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA), which has capped recent upside attempts. Analysts indicated that a sustained break above this barrier would be needed to confirm stronger bullish trading momentum. Until then, short-term price action may remain volatile as traders assess geopolitical developments, USD strength, and broader market sentiment tied to energy demand and supply expectations.
