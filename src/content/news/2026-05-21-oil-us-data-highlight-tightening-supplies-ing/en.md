Oil prices saw a sharp pullback on Monday despite clear signs of tightening US supply, according to ING analysts Warren Patterson and Ewa Manthey. Stronger-than-expected data pointed to lower inventories and robust demand, but traders appeared cautious, sending WTI and Brent futures lower. The USD strengthened against major currencies, with the EUR/USD trading near 1.0970, reflecting market preference for dollar liquidity amid oil volatility.

Technical levels in energy markets highlighted resistance around $85 per barrel for Brent, while WTI hovered near $80. The decline prompted some risk-off sentiment across forex markets, pushing commodity-linked currencies like the CAD weaker against the USD. Market participants noted that while fundamentals remain bullish, short-term profit-taking and position adjustments often trigger sharp intraday swings, influencing exchange rates and trading flows.

Analysts warned that these dynamics could continue to impact forex trading, with USD/JPY and GBP/USD seeing increased volatility alongside crude price shifts. With inventories tightening, any geopolitical or supply disruption may quickly reverberate through both oil and currency markets. Traders are keeping a close eye on oil benchmarks and US macro data, as these are likely to steer trading strategies and exchange rate trends in the coming sessions.
