Global benchmark Oil prices moved lower in cautious trading, with WTI drifting toward the key psychological $90/bbl level while Brent retreated into the mid-$90s after briefly climbing above $100/bbl, according to Scotiabank’s Global FX Strategy team. The pullback reflected softer risk sentiment across commodity and currency markets, as traders reassessed demand expectations alongside broader USD strength.

The decline in crude prices also influenced major currency pair flows, particularly USD/CAD and EUR/USD, as energy-linked currencies lost momentum against the US Dollar. A firmer USD exchange rate environment, supported by expectations of tighter financial conditions, added pressure to commodities priced in dollars. Investors remained focused on whether WTI can hold above $90/bbl, a technical and psychological support zone closely watched by forex and commodity traders.

Brent’s move back toward the mid-$90s signaled fading bullish momentum after the benchmark briefly broke above $100/bbl earlier in trading. Analysts noted that sustained weakness in Oil could ease inflation concerns in Europe and the United States, potentially affecting central bank expectations and broader EUR and USD trading dynamics. Market participants are now monitoring whether renewed demand signals or geopolitical developments can stabilize prices near current technical levels.
