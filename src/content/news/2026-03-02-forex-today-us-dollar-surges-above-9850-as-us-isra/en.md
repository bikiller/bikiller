The US Dollar surged in early-week trading, with the Dollar Index climbing above 98.50 as geopolitical tensions escalated following coordinated US–Israel strikes on Iran over the weekend. The attack reportedly killed Iran’s Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, triggering a sharp risk-off reaction across global financial markets. Currency traders rapidly shifted toward safe-haven assets, pushing the USD higher against major currency pairs.

The spike in geopolitical uncertainty drove volatility across forex markets, with the USD strengthening against the EUR, GBP and several emerging-market currencies. The EUR/USD exchange rate slipped as investors rotated into the US Dollar amid heightened demand for liquidity and safety. Market participants also moved to hedge exposure, increasing defensive positioning in USD-denominated assets during early trading sessions.

Analysts note that geopolitical shocks often amplify safe-haven flows into the US Dollar, particularly when tensions involve major Middle Eastern powers and potential disruptions to global energy markets. If the Dollar Index maintains momentum above the 98.50 level, traders expect continued pressure on EUR/USD and other risk-sensitive currency pairs. For forex markets, the unfolding crisis may keep volatility elevated and reinforce the USD’s dominance in near-term exchange rate movements.
