The People’s Bank of China set the USD/CNY central reference rate at 6.8109 for Tuesday’s trading session, compared with the previous fix of 6.8175. The latest midpoint also stands above Reuters’ estimate of 6.7877, highlighting a slightly weaker yuan bias versus market expectations. The fixing guides onshore trading in the USD/CNY currency pair and signals the central bank’s managed approach to exchange rate stability.

Compared with the prior session, the central parity rate reflects a firmer yuan setting against the US dollar, as the USD/CNY exchange rate edges lower from 6.8175 to 6.8109. However, the fix came in stronger than the Reuters consensus at 6.7877, indicating some divergence between official guidance and market pricing in forex trading conditions amid shifting USD sentiment.

Market participants continue to monitor the PBOC’s daily fixing as a key anchor for yuan liquidity, with implications for broader Asian FX trading. A stable USD/CNY midpoint can influence EUR and other major currency pairs indirectly, while traders assess whether the central bank will maintain its managed float policy amid global dollar fluctuations and external economic pressures.
