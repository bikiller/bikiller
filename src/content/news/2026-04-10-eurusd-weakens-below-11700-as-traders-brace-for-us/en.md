The EUR/USD currency pair slipped below the 1.1700 mark, trading around 1.1690 during Friday’s early Asian session. Market participants are treading cautiously ahead of key US CPI data, which could influence the USD’s trajectory. The Euro’s retreat reflects investor uncertainty as traders weigh the potential impact of inflation figures on interest rate expectations and broader monetary policy dynamics.  

Tensions in the Middle East are also influencing trading sentiment. The fragile two-week ceasefire between the United States and ‌Iran remains under scrutiny, keeping markets on edge. Any disruption could trigger safe-haven flows into the USD, further pressuring the EUR/USD exchange rate. Analysts note that sustained weakness below 1.1700 may open the door for additional downside moves toward recent support levels.  

Technical indicators suggest the currency pair could face volatility in the coming sessions. Traders are monitoring intraday ranges, with resistance near 1.1720 and support around 1.1670. The combination of geopolitical uncertainty and looming US economic data is likely to keep trading activity cautious, shaping EUR/USD’s short-term direction as market participants adjust positions in response to evolving risk sentiment.
