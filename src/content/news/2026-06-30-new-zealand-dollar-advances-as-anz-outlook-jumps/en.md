NZD/USD continued its upward trajectory for a second consecutive session, trading around 0.5650 during European hours on Tuesday. The currency pair shows steady demand for the New Zealand Dollar as traders reassess USD strength in the broader forex market. Exchange rate movement reflects improving risk appetite and mild USD softness across major currency pairs.

The advance is largely driven by a sharp improvement in domestic sentiment, with ANZ’s outlook jump signaling stronger confidence in New Zealand’s economic recovery. This boost has supported NZD buying in spot trading, strengthening momentum against the USD and other majors such as the EUR. Forex markets reacted quickly as expectations for growth stabilized.

For traders, sustained gains in NZD/USD may indicate further upside potential if sentiment holds, particularly amid uneven USD demand across global trading sessions. However, volatility remains tied to upcoming macro data and shifts in risk sentiment. Currency pair behavior suggests NZD is regaining short-term strength, though confirmation from broader forex flows will be key.
