The US Dollar Index (DXY) found little follow-through on its recent gains, hovering around 98.10 on Tuesday as trading remained range-bound. Market participants grappled with a mix of US economic data, which offered both supportive and cautionary signals, while Treasury yields sent conflicting cues. The indecision kept major currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY in tight ranges, reflecting the market’s hesitation to commit ahead of geopolitical developments.  

Tensions in the Middle East added a layer of uncertainty, weighing on risk sentiment and limiting the dollar’s upside. EUR/USD stayed near 1.0980, struggling to reclaim momentum, while USD/JPY oscillated around 135.20, testing short-term technical levels. Traders noted that any spike in geopolitical risk could trigger defensive dollar flows, but the current environment has kept exchange rates confined to narrow bands.  

Looking ahead, forex traders are monitoring upcoming US data releases and oil price movements, which could sway sentiment and impact USD trading. With mixed signals from yields and persistent geopolitical risks, major currency pairs are likely to remain volatile yet range-bound, prompting cautious positioning across the market. Analysts suggest that sustained dollar strength will require clearer signals from both macroeconomic indicators and geopolitical developments.
