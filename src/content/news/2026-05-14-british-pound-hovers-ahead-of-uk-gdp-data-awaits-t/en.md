The British Pound steadied near 1.3520 against the USD during Thursday’s Asian session, regaining footing after three consecutive days of declines. Market participants are cautious ahead of the UK’s preliminary Q1 GDP release, with attention also on Industrial and Manufacturing Production figures scheduled later. Traders are weighing the potential impact of these data on the GBP/USD exchange rate amid broader risk sentiment.  

Investors are watching global cues, including updates on the upcoming Trump-Xi meeting, which could sway cross-currency flows and influence USD strength. Analysts note that any signs of progress in US-China trade talks might lift risk appetite, supporting sterling and other major currencies such as the EUR. Conversely, cautious economic data could pressure GBP, prompting short-term traders to monitor technical levels closely.  

In the broader forex market, the GBP/USD is navigating a tight range as liquidity remains moderate. Traders are balancing domestic economic indicators against international geopolitical developments, with the exchange rate’s near-term trajectory hinging on GDP outcomes and trade diplomacy signals. Market watchers suggest that volatility could increase once the data hits, potentially setting up key trading opportunities across the USD, GBP, and EUR currency pairs.
