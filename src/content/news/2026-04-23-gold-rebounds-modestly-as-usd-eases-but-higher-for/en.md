Gold (XAU/USD) staged a modest recovery on Thursday, rebounding from earlier intraday losses as the US Dollar showed signs of weakness. The currency pair experienced fluctuations influenced by the broader market dynamics, with gold moving cautiously higher as traders reacted to changing exchange rates. However, gains remained limited due to persistent expectations for higher interest rates, driven largely by inflation pressures from rising oil prices.

As the market digests this complex interplay of economic indicators, gold's ability to maintain upward momentum faces significant challenges. The higher-for-longer interest rate outlook continues to weigh on market sentiment, curbing potential gains for the precious metal. The ongoing fluctuations in the USD and general trading sentiment around inflation could further impact gold prices in the near term, keeping traders vigilant on key technical levels.
