West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil rebounded in Monday Asian trading, recovering part of the nearly 3% losses recorded in the previous session. Prices moved back above the $95.50 mark, trading around $95.70, as USD-denominated energy markets showed renewed buying interest amid stabilising risk sentiment across broader currency pair trading, supporting short-term recovery after recent selling pressure in commodity trading.

Market sentiment improved after reports that Trump rejected Iran’s latest proposal, reigniting concerns over geopolitical tensions and potential supply disruptions. Traders reacted to the prospect of tighter global oil availability, which supported crude prices despite recent volatility in the USD exchange rate and mixed performance across major forex trading pairs.

The rebound in oil also influenced broader forex markets, with energy-linked currencies gaining modest support while the USD remained sensitive to risk flows. Movements in WTI often impact inflation expectations and central bank outlooks, indirectly shaping EUR/USD and other major currency pairs as traders reassess near-term trading positioning across global markets.
