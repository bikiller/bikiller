Pound Sterling struggled against a stronger US dollar for the third consecutive session on Tuesday, as the GBP/USD currency pair continued to trade with a negative bias. Despite the downward pressure, the pair has found some support above the key 1.3500 psychological level during the Asian session, suggesting cautious trading sentiment among investors. Market participants remain watchful amid heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East.  

Traders noted that the firming USD has limited Sterling’s upside, with investors seeking refuge in safe-haven currencies. While the GBP/USD exchange rate shows persistent weakness, follow-through selling has been muted, indicating that the market is balancing risk sentiment with technical support. Analysts point out that any sustained breach below 1.3500 could open the door for further losses toward 1.3450.  

The broader forex market is also seeing spillover effects, with EUR/USD and other major pairs reacting to US dollar strength and risk-off sentiment. Currency traders are keeping an eye on geopolitical developments and potential central bank commentary that could influence exchange rate trajectories. For now, GBP trading remains cautious, with the market digesting a mix of technical levels, news flow, and global uncertainty.
