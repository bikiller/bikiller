Gold prices rebounded sharply on Monday as easing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East pressured the US Dollar and crude Oil markets. Optimism surrounding a potential US-Iran agreement to end the regional conflict and reopen the Strait of Hormuz boosted demand for the precious metal during early trading. The XAU/USD currency pair climbed to around $4,568, gaining 1.30% on the day as investors adjusted their trading positions.

The softer USD exchange rate also provided additional support for Gold, with traders reducing safe-haven Dollar exposure amid improving risk sentiment. Lower Oil prices further weighed on the Greenback, allowing bullion to recover after recent volatility. Market participants continue to monitor developments between Washington and Tehran, as any breakthrough could significantly influence commodity prices, forex trading flows, and broader investor confidence across global markets.

Despite the rebound, analysts note that Gold trading remains highly sensitive to shifts in geopolitical risk, US monetary policy expectations, and movements in major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY. A sustained decline in the US Dollar Index could reinforce bullish momentum for XAU/USD, while renewed tensions or stronger US economic data may limit further upside in the near term.
