Brent crude prices are expected to remain elevated for a longer period, as ongoing geopolitical tensions between the U.S., Israel, and Iran delay the reopening of the vital Strait of Hormuz. Societe Generale analysts predict that the disruption could stretch into April, maintaining a "higher-for-longer" price path for oil. This situation is expected to support the current price level above $85 per barrel, with potential for further volatility in the energy market.

The delayed reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, a key route for global oil shipments, has significant implications for currency markets, particularly for the USD. Oil prices often influence the USD/EUR exchange rate, with higher oil costs generally supporting the U.S. dollar due to its correlation with energy exports. Traders will closely monitor the geopolitical landscape as it continues to affect both commodity and currency trading, with the potential for shifts in global market sentiment.
