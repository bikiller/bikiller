Asian foreign exchange markets may be positioned for a selective recovery if the recent strength in the USD begins to moderate, according to OCBC currency strategist Christopher Wong. He argues that much of the Federal Reserve’s hawkish policy outlook and the resilience of recent US economic data already appear to be reflected in current exchange rate pricing. As a result, the pace of USD gains could slow, creating room for regional currency pairs to stabilize and attract renewed trading interest.

Wong also notes that relatively contained oil prices should provide additional support for Asian currencies, particularly among economies that rely heavily on imported energy. Lower energy costs can reduce pressure on external balances while easing inflation risks, improving the broader macroeconomic backdrop. These factors may encourage investors to reassess positioning in regional FX markets, especially if expectations for further USD appreciation become less aggressive.

While the USD remains supported by comparatively higher US interest rates and solid economic fundamentals, a fading of upward momentum could shift attention toward relative value opportunities across Asian exchange rates. Market participants are therefore likely to monitor upcoming Federal Reserve signals and US data releases closely, as any moderation in policy expectations could influence currency pair performance, trading flows, and the near-term outlook for both the USD and other major currencies, including the EUR.
