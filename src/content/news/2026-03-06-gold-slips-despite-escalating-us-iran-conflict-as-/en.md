Gold (XAU/USD) edged lower on Friday, paring earlier gains as a stronger US Dollar (USD) and rising US Treasury yields pressured the non-yielding metal. Traders noted that despite escalating geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran, the safe-haven appeal of gold was muted, with the exchange rate retreating from recent highs. The move highlights how currency markets remain sensitive to shifts in USD strength and interest rate expectations.  

The dollar index climbed, underpinning the greenback against major currencies like the euro (EUR) and yen, while US Treasury yields ticked higher. This combination made gold less attractive relative to yielding assets, driving XAU/USD down. Analysts observed that the trading range could remain volatile, with gold prices closely tracking USD exchange rate movements and Treasury market developments in the coming sessions.  

Market participants are weighing geopolitical risks against macroeconomic factors, including the Federal Reserve’s policy outlook and inflation data. A firmer USD tends to limit upside for gold, while spikes in tensions or safe-haven flows could still trigger short-term surges. Traders in forex and commodities are keeping a close eye on XAU/USD, EUR/USD, and other major currency pairs as global events continue to influence liquidity and trading dynamics.
