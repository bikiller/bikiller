WTI crude oil prices dipped below $88.50, with the US benchmark trading at $88.20 during early European hours on Thursday. This decline is largely driven by growing optimism over a potential ceasefire between the US and Iran. Traders are monitoring geopolitical developments, which could signal a de-escalation of tensions in the Middle East and potentially ease supply concerns.

The positive sentiment surrounding the ceasefire has led to a risk-on mood in global markets, influencing the price of oil. As a result, the USD has strengthened against several major currencies, including the EUR, while oil prices face downward pressure. Should this ceasefire optimism continue, traders may adjust their positions, which could lead to further fluctuations in both oil prices and currency pairs like USD/EUR.
