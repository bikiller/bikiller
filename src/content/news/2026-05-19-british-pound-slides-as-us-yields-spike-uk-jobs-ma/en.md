The British Pound edged lower during North American trading as the US Dollar strengthened on rising Treasury yields. GBP/USD slipped 0.31% to trade at 1.3392, after hitting a daily high of 1.3437. Market participants reacted to surging US yields, reflecting expectations of higher inflation amid the ongoing energy shock, pushing investors toward the safe-haven dollar and weighing on sterling demand.

Pressure on the GBP was compounded by signs of fragility in the UK labor market. Weaker-than-expected jobs data intensified concerns over domestic economic resilience, influencing forex trading and prompting some traders to scale back long positions in the GBP. Technical levels around 1.3400 provided limited support, with the pair now testing lower ranges as market sentiment tilts toward USD strength.

Looking ahead, the currency pair may remain volatile as global investors reassess risk amid persistent inflationary pressures. The interplay between US yields, energy costs, and UK employment trends could continue shaping GBP/USD exchange rate movements. Traders are watching closely for economic updates from both sides of the Atlantic to gauge whether sterling can regain footing or slide further against the greenback.
