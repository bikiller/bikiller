Gold (XAU/USD) continued its downward trend in early Asian trading on Thursday, dipping to around $4,730. This move came as traders grew more cautious following the extension of a ceasefire agreement between the US and Iran, which raised hopes for a resolution to the ongoing conflict. The relative stability in the geopolitical landscape resulted in a dip in demand for the safe-haven asset.

The extended ceasefire has alleviated some market anxiety, leading to reduced concerns about further disruptions to global supply chains. This, in turn, has prompted a pullback in gold prices as traders shift focus towards riskier assets. The decline in gold's value also reflects the impact of shifting sentiment around US monetary policy and inflation concerns, keeping the precious metal under pressure in the near term.
