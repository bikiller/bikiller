Jeffrey Schmid, President of the Federal Reserve Bank of Kansas City, warned on Tuesday that inflation poses a significant risk to the economy, particularly as higher oil prices may not be a temporary issue. Speaking to the Rotary Club of Oklahoma City, Schmid emphasized the risk of inflation settling around 3%, highlighting the potential impact on the USD and broader market stability.

The comments could have significant implications for the forex market, particularly with the USD/EUR pair, as traders assess how the Fed might respond to persistent inflationary pressures. Schmid’s remarks suggest the Fed will remain cautious about rate cuts, potentially supporting the dollar against major currencies. Market participants are likely to watch the central bank's next moves closely, with inflation figures and oil prices being key drivers of future exchange rate fluctuations.
