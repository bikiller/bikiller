Gold surged above $4,500 on Friday, with XAU/USD climbing more than 3% as investors sought safety amid escalating geopolitical tensions. The yellow metal rebounded from daily lows of $4,375 to trade around $4,510, highlighting strong demand from dip buyers. The persistent conflict, now in its fifth week, has intensified haven flows, driving safe-haven assets like gold higher against the USD and other major currencies.  

Rising inflationary pressures are also supporting gold’s appeal, as traders weigh the impact on currency markets. With the USD under pressure, XAU/USD has attracted speculative and hedging activity, while EUR/USD and other major pairs show heightened volatility. Analysts note that technical levels near $4,500 now act as a psychological barrier, with potential for further short-term trading opportunities if geopolitical or economic conditions worsen.  

Market participants are closely watching both macroeconomic data and ongoing hostilities, as any intensification could sustain the rally. The surge underscores gold’s role as a hedge against uncertainty, with the exchange rate reflecting investor caution. Forex traders are likely to remain reactive, balancing positions across commodity-linked currencies, safe-haven flows, and broader USD movements in the days ahead.
