The USD/CHF currency pair is extending its losses on Friday, trading around 0.7773 and approaching a two-month low. The spot has dipped toward the lower Bollinger band, signaling increasing bearish momentum. Investors are weighing a combination of broad-based USD weakness and risk sentiment shifts, with the Swiss franc benefiting from its traditional safe-haven appeal amid market volatility.  

Traders note that the US Dollar continues to struggle against major peers, including the EUR and JPY, as sentiment favors higher-yielding or more stable currencies. The current exchange rate decline in USD/CHF marks a second consecutive week of losses, reflecting persistent selling pressure. Technical indicators suggest that if the pair breaks decisively below the lower Bollinger band, further downside toward 0.7750 could be possible.  

Market participants are closely watching US economic data and geopolitical developments for cues. Any signs of slowing US growth or dovish central bank commentary could exacerbate USD weakness, driving further CHF gains. Meanwhile, traders are also monitoring the EUR/USD and other major currency pairs for correlation-driven moves, highlighting how interlinked global forex markets remain in shaping short-term USD/CHF trading trends.
