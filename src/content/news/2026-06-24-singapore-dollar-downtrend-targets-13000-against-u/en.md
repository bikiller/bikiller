USD/SGD extended its advance for a fifth consecutive session, reinforcing the bullish outlook for the currency pair as the Singapore Dollar remained under pressure against the USD. Analysts at UOB, Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann, maintained a constructive view on the exchange rate, arguing that recent price action points to further upside despite increasingly stretched market conditions. The pair’s steady climb reflects persistent demand for the US Dollar amid ongoing trading activity across major forex markets.

While technical indicators suggest USD/SGD is approaching overbought territory, UOB believes the rally has room to continue in the near term. The bank highlighted 1.2980 as the next key level to watch, with the psychologically important 1.3000 mark acting as a major cap. Traders are closely monitoring whether the pair can sustain momentum toward these resistance levels as broader USD strength continues to influence currency market sentiment.

The outlook for USD/SGD also carries implications for regional foreign exchange markets, where movements in the US Dollar often affect trading across Asian currencies. Although attention remains focused on the Singapore Dollar, investors are also tracking developments in other major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, for clues about the broader direction of the USD. A break toward 1.3000 would further reinforce the pair’s ongoing uptrend and underline the current divergence in exchange rate performance.
