Deutsche Bank economists predict a significant slowdown in February’s U.S. Nonfarm Payrolls, forecasting an increase of just 30,000 jobs, down from January’s 130,000. This expected dip is likely to impact the USD, putting downward pressure on the greenback as traders adjust their positions ahead of the report. The unemployment rate is anticipated to hold steady at 4.3%, offering limited support to the currency.

The potential softening in job growth could weigh on the USD/EUR exchange rate, especially if the data fails to meet expectations. A weaker payrolls report might lead to speculation about slower U.S. economic growth, prompting a shift in risk sentiment. As a result, traders may position themselves for a possible pullback in USD strength, with the EUR/USD pair potentially seeing a bounce if the U.S. jobs data falls short.
