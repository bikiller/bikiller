The US Dollar Index (DXY) came under renewed selling pressure near the 100.90 level after softer US employment data reinforced expectations of a cooling labor market. The US dollar weakened across major currency pairs as traders reassessed the outlook for monetary policy and short-term interest rate expectations. Market sentiment shifted toward increased bets on potential policy easing.

Nonfarm Payrolls rose by only 57,000 in June, while May gains were revised sharply lower to 129,000 from 172,000, signaling a clear slowdown in hiring momentum. The data reinforced concerns about US economic resilience and increased sensitivity in USD trading, particularly across EUR/USD, GBP/USD and other major currency pairs, as volatility picked up in early trading.

The softer labor data is expected to keep the US dollar on the defensive in the near-term, while supporting speculation around a less aggressive Federal Reserve stance. In forex trading, investors may see continued fluctuations in USD exchange rates as macroeconomic signals drive positioning ahead of upcoming data releases and policy commentary.
