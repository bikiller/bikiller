The Indian Rupee (INR) extended its losses for a tenth consecutive day against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, pressured by persistently high crude oil prices. The USD/INR currency pair opened the session near 83.55, reflecting traders’ cautious sentiment amid rising import costs. Market participants are watching the Rupee closely as sustained oil-related outflows continue to weigh on exchange rate stability.

Trading activity in the INR has been volatile, with short-term technical support around 83.50 holding for now. Analysts point to the correlation between crude prices and the INR, noting that oil-importing nations like India face widening trade deficits when Brent crude hovers near multi-year highs. The currency’s weakness also mirrors global USD strength, with major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD showing similar momentum patterns in early Asian trading.

The Rupee’s slide raises implications for exporters and importers alike, potentially fueling inflationary pressures as higher energy costs filter into domestic prices. Forex traders are positioning for near-term range-bound movements, while central bank intervention remains a possible stabilizing factor. Investors are also monitoring broader USD trends, as the interplay between global markets and local fundamentals continues to shape INR trading dynamics.
