EUR/USD moved higher on Tuesday as the USD softened broadly, allowing the currency pair to recover after a five-day losing streak. The rebound in the exchange rate reflects a shift in sentiment, with traders trimming long Dollar positions following its recent rally. The EUR gained modest traction as risk appetite improved, supporting demand for non-USD currencies.

The USD’s pullback was largely driven by easing geopolitical concerns, as hopes of de-escalation in the Middle East reduced safe-haven demand. This shift encouraged more balanced trading conditions across forex markets, with investors reassessing risk exposure. The EUR/USD pair responded by stabilizing, as market participants rotated out of defensive USD positions and into higher-yielding alternatives.

From a technical perspective, the currency pair’s recovery suggests near-term support is holding, though upside momentum remains cautious. Traders continue to monitor key resistance and support zones for confirmation of a sustained move. If the USD remains under pressure, EUR/USD could extend gains, but lingering uncertainty keeps the outlook dependent on incoming geopolitical and macroeconomic developments.
