The euro came under pressure this week, slipping across the currency market even as the European Central Bank (ECB) delivered its first interest rate hike since 2023. The unexpected move in the EUR/USD currency pair highlighted a disconnect between policy tightening and the euro’s exchange rate performance, with trading sentiment remaining firmly defensive.

Stronger US dollar momentum and shifting interest rate expectations weighed on the shared currency, as traders reassessed the outlook for monetary policy divergence between the ECB and the Federal Reserve. Despite the rate hike, the euro failed to attract sustained buying interest, with foreign exchange flows favoring USD-denominated assets in broader trading conditions.

Market participants now focus on upcoming ECB guidance and broader inflation data to gauge whether the euro can stabilise against the US dollar. Volatility in the EUR/USD exchange rate remains elevated, with traders monitoring key technical levels for signals of further downside pressure or potential short-term corrective rebounds in forex trading sessions.
