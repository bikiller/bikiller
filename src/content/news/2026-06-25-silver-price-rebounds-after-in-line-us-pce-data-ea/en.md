Silver (XAG/USD) rebounded on Thursday, with the white metal trading around $58.65, up 2.16% on the day, after in-line US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) data eased immediate US Dollar support. The currency pair benefited from a softer USD reaction, as traders reassessed interest rate expectations across major forex markets. Market liquidity improved as volatility in the USD index eased after the data release.

The softer dollar backdrop supported XAG/USD demand, with silver attracting renewed buying interest across spot and derivatives trading. Investors interpreted the inflation reading as broadly in line with expectations, limiting upward pressure on US yields and reducing near-term USD strength. This shift helped precious metals stabilize, while forex markets saw modest repositioning against the greenback in major currency pairs during late trading sessions overall tone.

Looking ahead, XAG/USD remains sensitive to further USD moves and upcoming macro data, with traders watching whether the currency pair can sustain gains above the $58 handle. Broader forex sentiment and interest rate expectations will continue to guide silver’s short-term direction, as markets balance inflation trends against potential policy shifts from the Federal Reserve.
