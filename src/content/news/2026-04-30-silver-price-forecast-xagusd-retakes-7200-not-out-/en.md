Silver (XAG/USD) rebounded during Thursday’s Asian session, drawing renewed buying interest after dipping to the $70.85 region, a three-week low. The currency pair regained momentum in the final hour, briefly surpassing the $72.00 level. Traders remain cautious, as technical indicators suggest the upside may be capped, with the white metal facing resistance around this psychological barrier amid mixed USD strength.  

The recovery comes amid broad USD fluctuations, as the dollar index sees intermittent gains against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Market participants are weighing inflation data and risk sentiment, which influence both precious metals and currency trading. While the XAG/USD bounce signals temporary relief, uncertainty persists, leaving silver vulnerable to renewed selling if the dollar strengthens further.  

Analysts note that silver’s exchange rate movements could mirror broader commodity and forex trends, especially with the USD showing resilience. Traders eye key support near $70.50 and resistance around $72.50, balancing short-term trading opportunities against macroeconomic signals. Volatility in the currency pair highlights the interplay between risk appetite, USD dynamics, and commodity-driven flows shaping market behavior.
