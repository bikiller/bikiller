WTI crude oil prices rebounded from a brief dip during the Asian session, climbing back toward the $72.00 mark. After dropping to around $70.00, the commodity quickly regained strength, trading between $71.70 and $71.75 in the last hour. The rally comes amid rising concerns over potential disruptions to oil supply, particularly with the recent closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a key chokepoint for global oil shipments.

The market's reaction is reflective of broader concerns around supply constraints, pushing oil prices higher and impacting related currency pairs. The USD, already under pressure from global risk sentiment, faces additional challenges as oil price fluctuations may fuel inflation fears and influence future Fed decisions. As WTI approaches key technical levels, traders will be closely watching for any further geopolitical developments that could disrupt the market and affect the USD/EUR exchange rate.
