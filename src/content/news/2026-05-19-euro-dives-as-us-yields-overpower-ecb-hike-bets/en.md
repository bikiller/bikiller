The euro slid sharply against the US dollar on Tuesday, with the EUR/USD currency pair approaching the 1.1600 mark. The exchange rate fell 0.48% as investors favored the dollar amid rising US Treasury yields. Despite signals from European Central Bank officials that rate hikes could be on the table at the June meeting, the greenback regained momentum, putting pressure on the euro in forex trading.  

Market participants pointed to soaring US yields as the main driver behind the EUR/USD decline. Traders reacted to the broader US interest rate environment, which bolstered the USD’s appeal relative to the euro. Technical observers noted that the pair’s near-term losses could prompt further testing of support levels around 1.1600, with volatility likely to persist as central bank signals clash with market expectations.  

Analysts said the euro’s weakness highlights the tension between ECB policy hawkishness and strong US monetary signals. The current trading pattern suggests that the USD may continue to dominate short-term moves, challenging EUR bulls. Investors are closely watching upcoming economic releases and policy statements, which could influence the euro-dollar exchange rate and broader forex market sentiment.
