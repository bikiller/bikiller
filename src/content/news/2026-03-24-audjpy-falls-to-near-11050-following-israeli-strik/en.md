AUD/JPY continued its downward trajectory on Tuesday, falling to around 110.60 during the Asian session, marking its second consecutive day of losses. The currency pair has been under pressure, driven by increased risk aversion in the global markets, triggered by Israel's recent airstrikes on Tehran. The heightened geopolitical tensions have led investors to seek safer assets, undermining demand for the Australian dollar.

The continued decline of AUD/JPY highlights the broader market uncertainty as traders react to the escalating conflict in the Middle East. With the exchange rate nearing a key support level at 110.50, further weakness in the pair may be expected if risk-off sentiment persists. This development adds to the ongoing volatility in the forex market, with USD and EUR also seeing movements in response to global risk appetite.
