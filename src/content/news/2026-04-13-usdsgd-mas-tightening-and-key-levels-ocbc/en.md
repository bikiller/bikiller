USD/SGD trading remains in focus ahead of the Monetary Authority of Singapore’s (MAS) policy review on 14 April 2026, with OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong anticipating a tightening move. They expect MAS to increase the Singapore Dollar Nominal Effective Exchange Rate (S$NEER) slope, a measure aimed at curbing imported inflation, which could support the SGD against the USD and other major currencies like the EUR and JPY.  

Technically, the USD/SGD pair has been testing key levels, with resistance near 1.3600 and support around 1.3400. Traders are positioning for volatility, as any adjustment to the S$NEER slope may prompt sharp intraday swings. Market sentiment is sensitive to inflation data and regional economic indicators, with USD strength influenced by U.S. Treasury yields and risk appetite in Asia.  

If MAS moves as expected, a steeper S$NEER slope could reinforce the Singapore Dollar, potentially attracting flows from EUR and USD positions. For traders, monitoring momentum indicators and breakout levels is critical, as short-term swings could offer trading opportunities. The outlook suggests cautious optimism for SGD bulls, with USD/SGD potentially retracing to support levels before reacting to broader macro developments.
