Gold (XAU/USD) held near $4,400 on Tuesday, showing limited upside as investors weighed rising oil prices, stronger U.S. Treasury yields, and geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The currency pair’s exchange rate remains pressured despite early session gains, as traders consider the potential market impact of the Pentagon’s reported plan to deploy a brigade combat team from the Army's elite Airborne Division.  

Crude oil’s upward momentum continues to weigh on gold, reinforcing the USD’s strength against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Market participants are balancing safe-haven demand with higher yields, which make holding non-interest-bearing assets less attractive. Technical support around $4,380 is under close watch, while resistance near $4,420 caps immediate upside, keeping trading ranges narrow.  

Analysts note that the combination of war risk, energy prices, and U.S. monetary signals is likely to keep XAU/USD volatile but contained. Forex traders are monitoring key USD cross rates for cues, as any shift in yields or Middle East tensions could trigger rapid adjustments. Short-term strategies may focus on range-bound trading, with careful attention to geopolitical headlines and market sentiment shaping exchange rate movements.
