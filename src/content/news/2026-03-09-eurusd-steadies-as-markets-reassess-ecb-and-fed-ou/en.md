EUR/USD bounced back on Monday after opening the week with a bearish gap, as the US Dollar (USD) retreated from earlier gains. The currency pair, which had dropped to its lowest level in over three months, saw a recovery in the European session. The rebound was fueled by a shift in market sentiment, with traders reassessing both the European Central Bank (ECB) and the Federal Reserve's policy outlooks.

The recent surge in oil prices added to the uncertainty, influencing both the EUR/USD exchange rate and broader market dynamics. As the greenback weakened, the Euro (EUR) regained ground, moving away from the 1.0500 support level. Traders are closely monitoring economic data and central bank moves to gauge the next direction for the EUR/USD pair, with key levels in focus around 1.0600.
