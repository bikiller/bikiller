The US Dollar faced broad weakness, with the Dollar index (DXY) sliding for a second consecutive session to 99.57, as traders positioned themselves ahead of the upcoming Federal Reserve decision. Market sentiment was further influenced by heightened geopolitical tensions surrounding Iran, prompting cautious trading in key currency pairs. Analysts at UOB highlighted that the current environment is driving a selective approach, with investors weighing both macroeconomic data and geopolitical risks.  

In the forex markets, the USD’s retreat supported gains in major counterparts, with the EUR/USD climbing as traders sought alternatives amid Dollar softness. The trading dynamics reflect a combination of profit-taking from recent USD strength and anticipation of policy signals from the Fed. Technical levels around 99.50 for DXY are now under scrutiny, as a breach could accelerate further USD depreciation against a basket of currencies.  

Looking ahead, forex traders remain focused on exchange rate movements in pairs such as USD/JPY, GBP/USD, and AUD/USD, monitoring both central bank cues and geopolitical developments. The current Dollar weakness has implications for cross-border trading and commodity-linked currencies, reinforcing the importance of tactical positioning. Analysts suggest that volatility may persist until the Fed provides clarity on interest rate guidance, keeping USD trading in a sensitive, reactive phase.
