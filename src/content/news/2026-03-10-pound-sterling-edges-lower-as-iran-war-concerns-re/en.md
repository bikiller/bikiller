The GBP/USD currency pair edged lower on Tuesday during the Asian session, struggling to build on the previous day’s gains. Spot prices held above the 1.3400 level, reflecting cautious trading as investors weigh geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Renewed concerns over a potential US-Israel-Iran conflict have revived demand for the safe-haven USD, keeping the pound under modest pressure despite earlier bullish momentum.  

Market participants remained hesitant to commit to fresh positions, with the exchange rate hovering near key technical support around 1.3400. Traders noted that while Sterling faces short-term headwinds from rising geopolitical risk, the downside appears limited. The dollar’s strength, underpinned by safe-haven flows, continues to influence major currency pairs, including GBP/USD and EUR/USD, as investors monitor headlines for clues on escalation or potential de-escalation.  

Analysts suggest that any sustained shift in the conflict narrative could quickly trigger renewed volatility in the forex market. For now, trading remains range-bound, with GBP/USD struggling to breach resistance levels established during the previous rally. Currency traders are watching closely for updates, as the interplay between geopolitical developments and broader market sentiment will likely dictate the near-term trajectory of the pound against the USD.
