Gold prices have hit a record ten-day losing streak, breaking below $4,100 per ounce. The precious metal's decline is largely driven by higher real yields and a stronger USD, which have overshadowed any geopolitical concerns that traditionally support gold. The ongoing strengthening of the U.S. Dollar, particularly against key currency pairs like EUR/USD, continues to weigh heavily on the precious metal.

As gold struggles to maintain upward momentum, traders are increasingly focused on rising U.S. Treasury yields, which enhance the appeal of dollar-denominated assets. This combination of higher yields and a firmer dollar has left gold vulnerable to further declines, even amid global uncertainties. With the market's attention shifting toward the strength of the USD, the outlook for gold remains bearish in the short term.
