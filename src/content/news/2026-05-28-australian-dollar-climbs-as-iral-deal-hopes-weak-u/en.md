The Australian Dollar strengthened against the USD on Thursday, with the AUD/USD currency pair climbing around 0.25% after reports suggested that Iran and the United States had reached a potential agreement. The upbeat geopolitical sentiment weakened demand for the safe-haven US Dollar, allowing the Australian currency to recover sharply from daily lows near 0.7097. At the time of writing, the exchange rate was trading at 0.7158 as investors increased risk-sensitive trading positions.

Additional pressure on the USD emerged after fresh US economic data showed the economy expanded at a slower pace than markets had anticipated. The weaker GDP figures fueled speculation that the Federal Reserve could maintain a cautious policy stance, limiting further upside for the greenback against major currencies including the EUR and AUD. Currency traders reacted by reducing long USD exposure, supporting broader gains across risk-linked forex markets.

From a technical perspective, the rebound from 0.7097 reinforced near-term support for the AUD/USD pair, while the move above 0.7150 signaled improving bullish momentum. Market participants are now watching whether the pair can sustain gains toward higher resistance levels if risk appetite remains firm and US macroeconomic indicators continue to disappoint. The combination of softer US data and easing geopolitical tensions has shifted short-term sentiment in favor of the Australian Dollar.
