Gold prices (XAU/USD) fell sharply below $4,800, dipping to around $4,775 during the early Asian session on Monday. The drop comes amid escalating tensions between the United States and Iran over the critical Strait of Hormuz, a vital shipping route for global oil supplies. This geopolitical uncertainty is weighing on investor sentiment, prompting a sell-off in gold.

As markets react to the heightened risk, traders are closely monitoring the potential implications for the USD. A stronger dollar often puts pressure on commodity prices, including gold. The USD's performance in the wake of these developments could dictate further movement in the precious metals market, potentially pushing XAU/USD toward key support levels as traders adjust positions.
