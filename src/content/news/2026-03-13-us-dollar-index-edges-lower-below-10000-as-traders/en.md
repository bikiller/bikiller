The US Dollar Index (DXY) slipped below the 100.00 mark, trading at 99.70 during Friday's Asian session. This decline comes as traders prepare for the release of the US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) inflation data later today, a key indicator closely watched by the Federal Reserve. A weaker USD could signal that inflationary pressures are easing, potentially influencing future monetary policy.

The DXY's retreat reflects a broader pullback in the greenback across major currency pairs. EUR/USD is seeing slight upward momentum as traders position themselves ahead of the PCE report. A lower-than-expected inflation reading could dampen expectations of aggressive rate hikes from the Fed, leading to further USD weakness and potentially driving the EUR/USD exchange rate higher in the coming days.
