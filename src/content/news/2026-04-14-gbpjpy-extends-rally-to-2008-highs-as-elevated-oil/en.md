GBP/JPY surged to a fresh 2008 high on Tuesday, marking its seventh consecutive day of gains. The currency pair has been buoyed by the persistent strength of the British Pound (GBP), driven by optimism surrounding potential US-Iran negotiations. At the same time, elevated oil prices continue to exert pressure on the Japanese Yen (JPY), which is sensitive to rising energy costs.

The GBP/JPY exchange rate reached levels not seen in nearly 15 years, with the pair approaching key resistance levels. As oil prices hover near multi-year highs, Japan's import-driven economy struggles, reinforcing the Yen's weakness. Meanwhile, growing optimism in global markets, particularly in the US and Europe, is lending support to the Pound, contributing to the bullish momentum for the GBP/JPY.
