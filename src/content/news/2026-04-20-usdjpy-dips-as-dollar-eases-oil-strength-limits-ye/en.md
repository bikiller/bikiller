USD/JPY is experiencing a downside bias on Monday as the US Dollar (USD) gives up earlier gains. Despite growing optimism surrounding a potential US-Iran deal, the USD weakened across the board, impacting the exchange rate of the currency pair. The USD/JPY is currently trading at 133.50, reflecting a modest decline of 0.2% from its intraday highs. Market participants are closely monitoring developments in global geopolitical tensions, which continue to influence trading sentiment.

Meanwhile, oil prices continue to climb, supporting the Japanese Yen to some extent but limiting its gains against the greenback. The strength in crude oil is a key factor as Japan's economy remains sensitive to energy prices. Technical levels for USD/JPY indicate support around 133.30, while resistance is seen near 134.00. As the week progresses, the currency pair may remain volatile, driven by both market sentiment on global negotiations and oil price fluctuations.
