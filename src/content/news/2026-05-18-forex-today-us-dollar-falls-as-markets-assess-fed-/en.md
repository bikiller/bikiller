The US Dollar edged lower on Monday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering near 99.10 as traders digested fresh geopolitical developments and the upcoming Federal Reserve leadership transition. Currency pairs involving the USD, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, reflected cautious trading, with investors weighing the implications of US-Iran negotiations on risk sentiment and dollar demand.

Market participants are closely monitoring the Fed’s upcoming policy shift, anticipating potential changes in interest rate guidance. The softer tone in USD exchange rates has pushed EUR/USD higher, testing key technical levels, while USD/JPY remains under pressure amid a slightly more dovish market outlook. Traders are recalibrating positions ahead of central bank communications and geopolitical updates.

Technically, the DXY’s slide toward 99.10 suggests limited immediate upside, though broader trading sentiment hinges on both Fed signals and developments in Middle East diplomacy. Currency markets are showing heightened sensitivity, with short-term fluctuations driven by risk appetite, safe-haven flows, and expectations of future USD strength or weakness across major pairs. Analysts note that sustained moves will depend on confirmed policy direction and geopolitical stability.
