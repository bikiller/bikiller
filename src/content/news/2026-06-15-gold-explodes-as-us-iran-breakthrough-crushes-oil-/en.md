Gold surged more than 3% on Monday after the United States and Iran reached a breakthrough agreement to end their conflict, triggering a sharp decline in crude oil prices and easing inflationary expectations across global markets. The move supported risk rebalancing in forex trading, as investors reassessed USD positioning amid improving geopolitical sentiment.

At the time of writing, the XAU/USD currency pair trades at $4,351 after rebounding strongly from daily lows near $4,218, highlighting heightened volatility in the gold exchange rate. The precious metal’s recovery reflects shifting capital flows, with traders adjusting USD exposure as lower energy prices reduce inflation pressure and alter expectations for central bank policy trajectories.

Improved risk sentiment following the US-Iran deal is likely to weigh on safe-haven demand for gold in the near term, while boosting appetite for risk assets and potentially supporting currencies such as EUR against the USD. However, traders will closely monitor whether the breakdown in oil prices sustains, as it remains a key driver for inflation-linked forex positioning and broader market direction.
