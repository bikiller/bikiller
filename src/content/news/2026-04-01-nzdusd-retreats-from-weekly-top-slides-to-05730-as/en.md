The NZD/USD currency pair faced selling pressure after a brief rise during the Asian session, peaking at 0.5760, marking the weekly high. This pullback saw the exchange rate slide back to 0.5730, as traders adjusted their positions following the previous day's recovery from a four-month low. 

The dip in the Kiwi was largely driven by renewed speculation over a potential Fed rate hike, which bolstered the USD across the board. As the market continues to factor in U.S. monetary policy tightening, the NZD/USD exchange rate may remain vulnerable to further declines unless there’s a shift in sentiment. The focus now shifts to upcoming U.S. economic data and its potential impact on the Fed's decisions.
