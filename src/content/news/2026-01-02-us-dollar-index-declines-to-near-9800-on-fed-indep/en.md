The US Dollar Index (DXY) has slipped to around 98.15 during Asian trading hours, reflecting growing concerns over the independence of the Federal Reserve and increasing bets on potential rate cuts. As traders await key US economic data this month, the sentiment is shifting, influencing the exchange rates of the USD against major currencies like the EUR and JPY.

Market participants are closely monitoring developments that could signal the Fed's future monetary policy direction. The decline in the DXY highlights the impact of these uncertainties on trading strategies, with fluctuations in USD value prompting adjustments in currency pairs as investors position themselves ahead of crucial economic releases.
