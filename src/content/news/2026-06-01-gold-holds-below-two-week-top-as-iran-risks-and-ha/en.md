Gold opened the week under pressure, trading just below the two-week high around $1,960 per ounce, following last Friday’s peak near $1,965. The precious metal is weighed down by a firm US dollar, underpinned by hawkish bets on the Federal Reserve, while geopolitical tensions involving Iran add a cautious backdrop for traders monitoring the XAU/USD currency pair. Market participants are assessing whether recent safe-haven flows can sustain momentum.

The USD remains broadly supported across major currency pairs, with the EUR/USD exchange rate hovering near 1.0900 amid mixed economic signals from Europe. Investors are positioning for potential Fed rate guidance, keeping gold on the defensive. Trading volumes suggest a careful approach, with gold struggling to reclaim levels above $1,970, highlighting sensitivity to both USD strength and risk-off sentiment in broader markets.

Technically, gold faces resistance near last week’s peak, while support holds around $1,950. A sustained move below this level could expose further downside for XAU/USD, whereas a rebound may trigger renewed buying against weaker USD crosses. Traders continue balancing geopolitical risks with interest rate expectations, making gold a barometer for USD strength and global uncertainty in the forex market.
