The Indian Rupee (INR) continued its downward slide against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, extending losses from the previous day. The USD/INR currency pair climbed higher as the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) rolled back its INR-supportive measures, which had provided some support to the domestic currency in recent weeks. As of early Tuesday, the pair was trading at 83.20, marking a 0.2% increase.

The market is now focused on the upcoming testimony of former Federal Reserve Governor Kevin Warsh, which could influence USD sentiment and provide additional volatility. Warsh’s comments on US economic policy may offer insight into future Fed actions, further impacting the USD/INR exchange rate. Traders are eyeing technical levels, with the 83.50 resistance in sight as the pair continues to track broader USD strength.
