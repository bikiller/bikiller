The NZD/USD surged on Wednesday, crossing above the key 0.5800 level as a result of two major factors. Firstly, a de-escalation in the Middle East conflict eased global market tensions, contributing to risk-on sentiment. Secondly, the Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ) Governor, Anna Breman, delivered hawkish comments following the central bank's latest monetary policy meeting, which further supported the Kiwi's strength.

Traders are now focusing on the 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) as a potential resistance level. If NZD/USD continues to gain momentum, breaking through this level could signal further upside, with traders eyeing the next targets for the currency pair. Market sentiment remains influenced by global risk appetite and central bank signals, particularly from the RBNZ, as the NZD continues to show resilience against the USD.
