West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil has surged to approximately $74.50 per barrel during early Asian trading hours, marking its highest level since June 2025. This significant increase is largely attributed to escalating tensions in the Middle East, particularly the ongoing conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran, which poses serious risks to global oil supply chains. 

The closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical passage for oil shipments, further exacerbates concerns in the market. As traders react to these geopolitical developments, the rising prices of WTI may impact currency pairs involving the USD, influencing exchange rates against the EUR and other major currencies as traders adjust their positions in anticipation of potential supply disruptions.
