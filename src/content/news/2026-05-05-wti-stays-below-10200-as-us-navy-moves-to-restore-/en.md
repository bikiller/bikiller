WTI crude oil edged slightly lower on Tuesday, hovering around $101.80 per barrel during Asian trading, after posting modest gains the previous session. Market attention remains on geopolitical developments in the Strait of Hormuz, where the US Navy has moved to secure shipping lanes, easing some of the recent supply concerns. Analysts note that energy markets are closely watching these moves as risk sentiment fluctuates.  

The softer oil price weighed subtly on commodity-linked currencies, with the AUD/USD pair trading lower near 0.6640, reflecting sensitivity to global crude trends. Meanwhile, the USD held steady against major counterparts like the EUR, as traders balanced safe-haven demand with broader risk appetite. Exchange rates in Asia showed limited volatility, suggesting a cautious stance ahead of upcoming US economic data.  

Investors are also monitoring technical levels for WTI, noting resistance near $102.50 and support around $101.00. Market participants expect continued range-bound trading unless further geopolitical developments emerge, which could sway both energy prices and currency pairs. Traders are positioning carefully, weighing crude movements against USD strength and global risk sentiment, with implications for forex and commodity-linked assets alike.
