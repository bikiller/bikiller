EUR/USD extended its subdued trading for the second consecutive day, hovering near 1.1540 during Asian hours on Friday. The currency pair remains under pressure, staying below the nine-day exponential moving average (EMA) around 1.1550, reflecting cautious sentiment among traders. Market participants appear hesitant, balancing mixed economic data from the eurozone and the US while monitoring broader risk appetite in global forex markets.  

Technical analysis on the daily chart points to a potential bullish reversal as EUR/USD tests the upper boundary of its descending channel. A sustained break above this level could signal short-term gains, attracting speculative buying and increasing trading activity. Conversely, failure to breach resistance may reinforce the downward trend, keeping the exchange rate confined to the channel.  

Investors are watching closely for momentum shifts as the euro navigates its current range against the USD. The pair’s behavior near key technical levels could influence positioning and hedging strategies, with implications for both short-term traders and longer-term investors. Currency markets remain sensitive to US economic data and eurozone developments, leaving EUR/USD trading under moderate volatility.
