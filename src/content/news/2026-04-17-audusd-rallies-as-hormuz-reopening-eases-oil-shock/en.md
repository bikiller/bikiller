The AUD/USD climbed sharply toward the 0.7200 level on Friday, supported by easing tensions in the Middle East. Reports of the Strait of Hormuz reopening alleviated earlier oil supply fears, prompting a retreat in the US Dollar. The rebound in risk appetite bolstered the Australian Dollar, a currency closely tied to global trade and commodity flows, while the greenback faced selling pressure across major pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD.  

Traders noted that the exchange rate's push above key technical levels signals renewed buying interest, with momentum favoring risk-linked currencies. AUD/USD’s move reflects both the short-term reprieve in geopolitical risk and broader market sentiment improving toward higher-yielding assets. Analysts suggest that continued stability in oil markets could sustain trading above 0.7200, while any fresh Middle East developments may trigger renewed USD demand.  

Currency markets reacted to the news with increased liquidity, as investors repositioned ahead of the weekend. The USD’s decline against the AUD highlights its vulnerability amid risk-on trading conditions, while EUR/USD and other major pairs showed muted but supportive moves. With risk sentiment driving flows, traders are monitoring both geopolitical headlines and technical resistance levels to gauge the sustainability of the AUD/USD rally.
