AUD/USD exchange rate extends gains for a second straight trading session as the Australian Dollar stays on the front foot against a broadly weaker US Dollar following China Services PMI data. Improved sentiment linked to Chinese economic activity has supported risk-sensitive currencies, with forex traders adjusting expectations for USD demand across major currency pairs amid shifting macro signals.

After a modest Asian session downtick toward the 0.6910 region, the AUD/USD currency pair reversed higher as fresh US Dollar selling emerged in early trading. The dip to 0.6910 attracted buyers back into the market, with spot trading stabilising above key short-term support levels. The move highlights continued volatility in intraday forex trading conditions and liquidity-driven price swings across the session.

Market implications suggest that ongoing USD softness is providing near-term tailwinds for the Australian Dollar, while traders continue to monitor China-linked data for directional cues. In the broader foreign exchange market, AUD/USD may remain sensitive to global risk sentiment and USD index movements, with participants eyeing whether the exchange rate can sustain momentum above recent support zones.
