Brent crude has eased to around $111, while WTI is trading at $102, despite a recent attack on a Kuwaiti tanker. This price dip comes amid tightening oil markets in Europe and Africa as Asia steps in to fill supply gaps. The volatility continues to stem from geopolitical tensions, with traders closely monitoring disruptions in key shipping routes.

Bloomberg warns that oil could surge to $140 if further attacks on Red Sea shipping lanes occur, exacerbating global supply concerns. The fluctuations are impacting major currency pairs, including USD/EUR, as oil price shifts influence inflation and central bank policy expectations. Traders are adjusting exchange rates based on oil market reactions, anticipating increased market volatility in the short term.
