The Canadian Dollar strengthened against the US Dollar for a second consecutive session on Wednesday, pushing the USD/CAD currency pair lower toward the 1.3600 level during Asian trading hours. The decline in the exchange rate reflected softer demand for the USD, as improving geopolitical sentiment reduced the appeal of traditional safe-haven assets across forex markets.

Investor confidence improved after reports pointed to growing optimism surrounding a potential agreement between Washington and Tehran. The easing of global risk concerns encouraged traders to rotate away from defensive positions tied to the US Dollar, while supporting commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian Dollar. The shift in sentiment also influenced broader currency trading activity, with major pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD holding firm against the greenback.

Market participants are now closely monitoring whether USD/CAD can maintain momentum below the 1.3600 psychological threshold, a level viewed by traders as an important near-term technical marker. Continued weakness in the USD could place additional pressure on the pair, particularly if risk appetite remains strong and crude oil prices continue to support the Canadian Dollar.
