Gold (XAU/USD) edged slightly higher on Thursday, maintaining its position within a multi-week range as investors held back from committing to strong directional trades. The precious metal’s limited movement reflects cautious sentiment ahead of key developments in US-Iran negotiations, with traders weighing potential geopolitical impacts on safe-haven demand. Technical support around $1,970 and resistance near $2,010 continue to define the current trading corridor.  

Meanwhile, broader market dynamics kept the USD moderately firm against major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Oil-driven inflation pressures capped gold’s upside, as surging crude prices support expectations of higher commodity-linked costs, prompting traders to balance currency hedging with inflation protection. The exchange rate fluctuations in forex markets remain sensitive to these intersecting factors, influencing both short-term trading strategies and longer-term positioning in metals and currencies.  

Traders are closely monitoring developments from Washington and Tehran, as any breakthrough could trigger significant moves in risk-sensitive assets, including gold and currency pairs tied to the USD. While XAU/USD continues to hover near its recent range, volatility could accelerate if geopolitical clarity emerges. For now, market participants favor measured trading, focusing on technical levels and macroeconomic signals to guide forex positions and manage exposure amid lingering uncertainty.
