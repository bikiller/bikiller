The EUR/USD pair slipped to around 1.1715 during the early Asian session on Wednesday, remaining in negative territory. The decline comes amid growing uncertainty over a potential ceasefire in the Middle East, which has prompted investors to seek safety in the US Dollar (USD). This shift towards the greenback has put pressure on the Euro (EUR), pushing the exchange rate lower.

As traders await the Federal Reserve's upcoming rate decision, market sentiment remains cautious. The US Dollar's safe-haven appeal has intensified due to geopolitical risks, overshadowing the Euro's performance. The EUR/USD exchange rate faces resistance around the 1.1700 level, with further declines possible if the USD continues to gain momentum following any Fed policy signals.
