The EUR/USD currency pair continued its downward momentum on Monday, sliding below the 1.1500 mark as geopolitical tensions weighed on market sentiment. In early Asian trading, the pair fell 0.15% to hover around 1.1490, marking its fifth consecutive day of losses. Traders cited caution over heightened risk factors, particularly warnings from Iran about potential US ground attacks, which fueled demand for the safe-haven USD.  

Market participants noted that the exchange rate’s decline reflects broader risk aversion, with investors moving away from the euro amid escalating Middle East tensions. Technical analysts pointed to 1.1500 as a key psychological support level, suggesting that further trading below this threshold could signal additional pressure on the euro. The USD, benefiting from safe-haven flows, remained supported across major currency pairs.  

The sustained weakness in EUR/USD highlights the interplay between geopolitical uncertainty and currency markets. Traders are likely to monitor developments closely, with any escalation in US-Iran tensions potentially prompting further euro depreciation. For the short term, the exchange rate may continue to test support levels near 1.1480–1.1500, influencing trading strategies for both speculative and hedging positions in global forex markets.
