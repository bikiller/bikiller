The USD/CAD exchange rate rose sharply on Monday, with the Canadian Dollar under pressure as geopolitical tensions surrounding the US-Iran conflict fueled a global risk-off sentiment. The war prompted increased demand for the safe-haven US Dollar, pushing the currency pair higher. The CAD weakened by 0.4%, trading near 1.3740 against the USD, as oil prices surged, further complicating the outlook for the Loonie, which is heavily influenced by crude price movements.

Market traders are bracing for further volatility, with oil prices jumping over 3% amid the uncertainty. The CAD, typically sensitive to fluctuations in crude oil, faces a tough battle as the safe-haven demand for USD strengthens. Analysts suggest the pair could test 1.3800 if risk sentiment continues to favor the Greenback, while oil price dynamics will remain a key factor influencing the CAD’s direction.
