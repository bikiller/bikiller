Brent crude extended its decline as markets reacted to renewed optimism over the possible reopening of the Strait of Hormuz following a US–Iran agreement, triggering a broad easing in energy risk premiums. The move reflected a shift in trading sentiment toward improved supply security, with investors unwinding previous geopolitical hedges. In forex markets, the adjustment supported a firmer USD, while commodity-linked currency pairs adjusted lower alongside weaker crude-linked pricing expectations.  

OCBC FX strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong caution that the current Brent pullback may be overstating stability gains, arguing that the Strait of Hormuz risk premium remains fragile despite easing tensions. They note markets could be underpricing geopolitical risks, leaving oil and related currency pairs exposed to sudden repricing if conditions deteriorate, with implications for trading volatility across USD, EUR, and commodity FX.  

Looking ahead, traders are closely watching whether the US–Iran deal holds and whether flows through the Strait of Hormuz normalize, as any renewed disruption could quickly lift Brent and reintroduce a geopolitical premium. In FX markets, such a shift would likely strengthen safe-haven demand for USD and JPY, while pressuring risk-sensitive currency pairs and boosting short-term volatility in energy-linked trading strategies.
