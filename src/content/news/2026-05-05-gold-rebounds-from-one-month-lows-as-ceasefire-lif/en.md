Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded sharply on Tuesday, climbing nearly 1% as investors returned to the market following a fragile ceasefire between the US and Iran. The currency pair bounced off one-month lows around $4,500, with the exchange rate rising to $4,560. Improved risk sentiment lifted Wall Street trading, prompting traders to rotate back into safe-haven and commodity-linked positions, while the USD showed mixed performance against major currencies.  

The rebound comes amid broader market dynamics, with equity markets benefiting from easing geopolitical tensions and lifting overall risk appetite. Analysts note that gold’s technical levels near $4,500 acted as a strong support, encouraging buyers to step in. Meanwhile, the USD remains influential in shaping XAU/USD movements, as currency pair volatility often mirrors shifts in investor sentiment toward safe-haven assets.  

Traders are closely monitoring the implications for both gold and other markets, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, as geopolitical developments continue to impact liquidity and exchange rates. With the ceasefire still tentative, volatility may persist, offering short-term trading opportunities. Investors are balancing gold’s appeal against the backdrop of rising equities and fluctuating USD strength, signaling cautious optimism in commodity and forex markets alike.
