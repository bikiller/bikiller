European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member Gabriel Makhlouf expressed concern over the potential for prolonged high energy prices. Speaking on Friday, he warned that the ongoing uncertainty surrounding the Middle East conflict could lead to a "higher-for-longer" energy price scenario. Such a scenario could keep inflationary pressures elevated, adding to market volatility. The EUR/USD exchange rate is under pressure as investors closely monitor geopolitical developments, with energy prices remaining a key factor in currency fluctuations.

Makhlouf’s comments come as the ECB faces growing challenges in its efforts to curb inflation. Energy price hikes, if sustained, could force the ECB to maintain its hawkish stance longer than previously anticipated. This uncertainty has caused further fluctuations in EUR/USD trading, where the euro remains vulnerable against the USD. Traders are now weighing the implications for ECB policy, with market expectations for future rate hikes continuing to shift amid concerns about global economic stability.
