Singapore’s Non-oil Domestic Exports (NODX) are expected to maintain a K-shaped recovery in the coming months, according to UOB economist , as electronics and pharmaceutical shipments continue to drive external demand. The rebound highlights improving regional trade conditions and could lend near-term support to the Singapore Dollar against the USD and EUR in forex trading, particularly as investors monitor Asia’s export momentum and broader exchange rate trends.

Electronics exports remained the strongest contributor to the latest NODX upswing, reflecting resilient semiconductor demand and stabilizing global supply chains. Pharmaceutical shipments also posted firm gains, offsetting softer petrochemical performance amid weaker energy-related demand. The uneven recovery reinforces the “K-shaped” pattern identified by UOB, where high-value manufacturing sectors outperform more cyclical industries. Currency pair traders are increasingly watching Singapore’s trade data as a leading indicator for regional growth and USD/SGD direction.

The stronger export backdrop may provide the Monetary Authority of Singapore with greater flexibility on policy settings as inflation and external demand stabilize. Analysts expect the Singapore Dollar exchange rate to remain relatively supported if export conditions continue improving, although slower petrochemical activity and uncertain global demand could still limit upside momentum. In broader forex markets, traders are also assessing how Singapore’s trade resilience compares with export trends across China, the EUR area, and other Asian economies.
