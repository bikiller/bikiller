The Indian Rupee continued its recovery against the US Dollar on Thursday, extending gains for the second consecutive session. The USD/INR currency pair retreated to around 93.65, reflecting growing investor confidence in the local currency. Traders noted increased buying interest in the rupee following the Reserve Bank of India’s announcement of additional measures aimed at curbing speculative trading in the forex market.  

Market participants highlighted that the RBI’s intervention, designed to tighten liquidity and reduce volatility, provided crucial support to the rupee. The central bank’s steps are expected to discourage short-term speculative flows while encouraging more stable trading patterns in USD/INR. Analysts suggested that the move could also influence other major currency pairs, including EUR/INR, as the rupee strengthens against the greenback.  

Technically, USD/INR now faces potential resistance near 94.00, with the current level of 93.65 acting as a key support. Traders are watching whether the pair can sustain its decline amid broader market volatility. The RBI’s measures, coupled with sustained domestic demand, could maintain downward pressure on the dollar, signaling a more favorable environment for importers and stabilizing forex trading conditions.
