Silver prices remained under pressure on Wednesday, with XAG/USD extending losses for a second straight session and falling to its lowest level since March 23 during Asian trading. Persistent selling interest kept bears firmly in control, dragging the precious metal toward the March lows below the $64.50 mark. The move reflects a cautious trading environment as investors continue to reassess risk exposure across global markets.

The decline in silver comes amid broader strength in the USD, which has weighed on precious metals and commodity-linked assets. A firmer US dollar tends to reduce the appeal of non-yielding assets such as silver, while shifts in exchange rate expectations and monetary policy outlooks continue to influence market sentiment. Traders are also monitoring movements in major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, for additional clues on capital flows and risk appetite.

From a technical perspective, the inability of XAG/USD to hold above key support levels has reinforced the bearish outlook. The break toward levels below $64.50 suggests sellers remain dominant, increasing the risk of further downside if demand fails to recover. Market participants will watch upcoming economic data and USD-driven trading dynamics closely, as any change in sentiment could determine whether silver stabilizes near current levels or extends its decline further.
