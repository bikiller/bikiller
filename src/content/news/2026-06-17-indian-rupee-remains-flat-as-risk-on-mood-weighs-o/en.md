Indian Rupee (INR) holds steady on Wednesday after two consecutive sessions of gains against the US Dollar (USD). Risk-on sentiment across global markets weighs on the greenback, keeping demand for safe-haven assets limited. In forex trading, the USD/INR currency pair remains subdued as investors reassess risk appetite and monetary policy expectations.

However, the upside potential for the USD/INR exchange rate appears capped in the near term as downward pressure on the Indian Rupee eases. Declining global crude oil prices provide support to the INR, reducing import cost concerns for India. Market participants remain focused on inflation trends and Fed policy signals, which continue to influence USD strength.

Overall, the currency pair is expected to trade in a narrow range as mixed macro signals dominate sentiment. While risk-on flows limit USD upside, supportive fundamentals for INR from energy prices help stabilize the exchange rate. Traders are likely to watch upcoming US economic data and RBI commentary for directional cues in USD/INR trading.
