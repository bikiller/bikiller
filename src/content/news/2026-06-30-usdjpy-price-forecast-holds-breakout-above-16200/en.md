The USD/JPY currency pair traded 0.16% higher at around 161.25 during Tuesday’s European trading session, marking price action near its lowest level seen in over four decades. The exchange rate remains under pressure from volatile forex trading conditions, with participants monitoring broader USD strength and yen weakness across global currency markets.

The pair continues to hold a technical breakout above the 162.00 resistance level, even as spot trading hovers near 161.25. This divergence highlights heightened volatility in USD/JPY, with forex traders weighing momentum signals against potential corrective pullbacks. The exchange rate structure suggests lingering bullish pressure on the USD versus the Japanese yen despite intraday softness.

Market participants will continue to watch USD/JPY trading dynamics for confirmation of whether the currency pair can sustain momentum above the 162.00 threshold or slip further toward recent multi-decade lows. Broader USD trends, along with shifts in EUR and other major currencies, may also influence short-term exchange rate direction in global forex markets.
