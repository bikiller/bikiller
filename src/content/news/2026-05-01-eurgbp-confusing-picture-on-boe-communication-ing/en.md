The recent Bank of England communication has left the market confused, according to ING's Chris Turner. While the BoE appears to be preparing for a rate hike in June, the mixed message from officials has led to divergent reactions. Some traders interpreted the guidance as dovish, dampening expectations of aggressive tightening. This uncertainty has caused significant volatility in the GBP, as it remains heavily influenced by global factors, including fluctuations in oil prices.

The EUR/GBP exchange rate has experienced sharp fluctuations, reflecting the market's struggle to interpret the BoE's stance. Sterling has faced downward pressure as a result of weaker UK rates, largely driven by a combination of softer oil prices and shifting expectations regarding the USD. As the June decision approaches, traders will likely remain focused on the evolving outlook for UK monetary policy, which could add further volatility to the currency pair in the coming weeks.
