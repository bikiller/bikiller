The Indian Rupee (INR) gained strength against the US Dollar (USD) during afternoon trading on Friday, as the USD/INR currency pair saw a slight decline ahead of the anticipated US Non-Farm Payroll (NFP) data release. This movement in the exchange rate reflects market sentiment as investors brace for potential shifts in monetary policy based on the upcoming employment figures.

Traders are closely monitoring the USD/INR pair, which has been influenced by fluctuations in global economic indicators. As the NFP data is expected to provide insights into the US labor market, its impact on the USD could further shape the exchange rate dynamics. A robust report could bolster the dollar, while weaker-than-expected results may benefit the INR as risk appetite shifts.
