The Australian Dollar extended its sharp decline on Friday, with the AUD/USD currency pair falling to around 0.7155, down 0.91% on the day. This marked its lowest level in over a week, as the exchange rate came under sustained selling pressure. Traders are reacting to broad-based US Dollar strength, driven by a repricing of expectations around upcoming Federal Reserve interest rate hikes.  

Market participants have been adjusting their positions amid heightened USD demand, with safe-haven flows supporting the greenback against a range of major currencies, including the AUD and EUR. The persistent appreciation of the USD has put additional downward pressure on the AUD/USD pair, prompting technical selling as it tested key support levels near 0.7160.  

Analysts note that continued USD strength could weigh further on the Australian Dollar, influencing trading strategies in the forex market. Investors are watching central bank signals closely, as any change in Fed policy expectations may trigger renewed volatility in exchange rates. The AUD/USD’s movement reflects broader market sentiment, highlighting the interplay between interest rate differentials and currency pair dynamics.
