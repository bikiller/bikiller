The Indian Rupee (INR) edged higher against the US Dollar (USD) in Wednesday’s early trading session, prompting the USD/INR currency pair to retreat toward 94.30 after touching a record high of 94.75 in the previous session. The pullback in the exchange rate reflects a modest recovery in risk appetite, with traders reassessing recent bullish momentum in the USD.

Market sentiment improved on growing expectations of a potential ceasefire in the Middle East, which has encouraged a broader risk-on mood across global financial markets. This shift has reduced safe-haven demand for the US Dollar, allowing emerging market currencies like the INR to stabilize. In forex trading, such geopolitical developments often influence short-term price action and capital flows.

Despite the INR’s gains, underlying pressure remains as elevated USD strength and external uncertainties continue to weigh on the currency pair. Traders are closely monitoring key technical levels near 94.30 and 94.75, with further movement likely to depend on geopolitical headlines and global risk sentiment. The near-term outlook suggests continued volatility in the USD/INR exchange rate.
