West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil prices surged to $100 per barrel in early Monday trading, marking a continuation of its winning streak for the fourth consecutive session. The rise in prices is largely driven by ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, adding to supply concerns and bolstering demand for the commodity. This uptick comes amidst a broader market rally, with oil prices benefiting from risk-off sentiment as traders weigh the implications of potential disruptions to global oil supply chains.

The move above the $100 threshold has been supported by a weakened US dollar, which continues to affect the USD/EUR exchange rate. As oil prices climb, the demand for commodities denominated in USD strengthens, adding pressure to the greenback. Market participants are closely monitoring the evolving situation in the Middle East, with expectations that further geopolitical risks could push prices higher. As WTI remains above key technical levels, traders will be watching for any breakout or retracement signals that could influence further moves in oil prices and related forex pairs.
