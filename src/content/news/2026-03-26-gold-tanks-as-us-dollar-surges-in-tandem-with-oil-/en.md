Gold (XAU/USD) dropped sharply on Thursday, falling nearly 2.50% as rising oil prices exerted upward pressure on inflation and fueled strength in the US Dollar. This movement comes amid growing uncertainty about a potential agreement between the US and Iran, which has added volatility to the markets. As of writing, XAU/USD is trading at $4,394, significantly down from its daily high of $4,544.

The surge in oil prices has reinforced expectations of higher inflation, prompting a stronger USD. This has had a direct impact on the XAU/USD currency pair, making gold less attractive to investors. With oil continuing to climb and inflation concerns mounting, the Greenback is gaining strength, pushing the exchange rate lower. Market participants are now closely watching geopolitical developments for further volatility.
