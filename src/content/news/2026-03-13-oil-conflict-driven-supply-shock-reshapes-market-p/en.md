Oil markets are set to be shaped by factors like tanker availability, refining capacity, and the duration of ongoing conflicts, according to Bob Savage of BNY. With crude prices hovering above $80 per barrel, inflation concerns rise, threatening to slow demand. The volatile supply dynamics could lead to prolonged price instability, impacting both the energy sector and global markets.

As oil prices climb, the risk of demand destruction intensifies, particularly for major currencies like the USD and EUR. The uncertainty around crude supply adds pressure to the forex market, potentially driving further shifts in currency pairs. Traders will likely focus on these oil-related factors when assessing the USD’s strength and the euro’s vulnerability, especially in the context of broader geopolitical risks.
