The US Dollar Index (DXY) slipped during Thursday’s Asian session, trading around the psychologically important 100.00 level. Despite recent spikes in safe-haven demand, the dollar’s strength against major currencies including the euro, yen, and pound showed signs of waning after modest gains the previous day. Traders noted that the USD’s trading range remains tight, reflecting cautious sentiment amid mixed global risk appetite.

Currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY mirrored the DXY’s subdued performance. The euro found slight support, while the yen edged higher against the dollar, signaling that investors are weighing geopolitical tensions and macroeconomic indicators. Technical analysts highlighted that sustaining the 100.00 level is key, as a break below could trigger broader weakness in the USD across major pairs.

Market participants are closely monitoring developments in US Treasury yields and global equity markets, which continue to influence exchange rate movements. The dollar’s muted response to safe-haven flows suggests that traders are balancing risk-off positioning with underlying concerns over inflation, interest rates, and potential economic slowdown, keeping the USD trading dynamic and highly sensitive to market news.
