The escalating conflict in the Middle East, triggered by the assassination of Iran’s Supreme Leader by the US and Israel, has led to a surge in oil prices. As tensions rise, oil prices have spiked by 4%, pushing Brent Crude above $90 per barrel. This geopolitical turmoil has also caused significant volatility in the currency markets, with the USD facing pressure as a safe-haven currency. 

The US Dollar weakened following a disappointing Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report, which showed job growth below expectations, dropping the USD/EUR exchange rate to 1.08. Traders reacted to the softer economic data by pushing the EUR/USD up by 0.5%. The NFP miss heightened concerns over the Federal Reserve's tightening cycle, leading to further declines in USD pairs. Market focus will now shift to potential geopolitical developments and their impact on global risk sentiment.
