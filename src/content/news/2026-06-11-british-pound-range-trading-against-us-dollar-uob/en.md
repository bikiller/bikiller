The British pound remained locked in a consolidation phase against the USD, with the GBP/USD currency pair failing to sustain momentum after a brief move above the 1.3420 level. Analysts at UOB noted that the attempted breakout lacked follow-through buying interest, suggesting that near-term trading conditions remain broadly range-bound rather than signaling the start of a stronger upward trend in the exchange rate.

In the short term, market participants are expected to focus on technical levels as GBP/USD fluctuates within a relatively narrow band. UOB sees any pullback remaining contained between 1.3330 and 1.3395, with no clear break below 1.3330 anticipated for now. The inability to hold gains above 1.3420 highlights a cautious market environment, where traders continue to balance demand for the pound against ongoing support for the USD.

The outlook points to continued range trading as investors assess broader currency market drivers, including economic data and central bank expectations. While GBP/USD has shown resilience above key support, the lack of bullish momentum suggests limited upside in the immediate term. Currency traders are also monitoring movements across major pairs involving the USD and EUR, as broader foreign exchange sentiment could influence the next directional move in sterling’s exchange rate.
