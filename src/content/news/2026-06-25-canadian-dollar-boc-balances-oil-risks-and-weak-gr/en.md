TD Securities strategists note that the Bank of Canada’s June Summary of Deliberations continued to reflect a careful balancing act between rising oil price spillover risks and ongoing domestic economic softness. For forex markets, the tone kept the Canadian dollar closely tied to energy dynamics, while reinforcing a cautious policy stance that matters for USD/CAD trading sentiment.

The assessment suggests the Canadian dollar remains sensitive to crude oil fluctuations, with higher energy prices supporting CAD upside risk, while weak domestic growth tempers sustained gains. In forex trading, this keeps USD/CAD range-bound, as USD strength and risk sentiment offset commodity support. EUR/CAD cross rates also remain reactive to shifts in European Central Bank expectations versus BoC caution.

Looking ahead, traders will watch incoming Canadian data and oil price trends for clearer direction on the exchange rate outlook. The BoC’s data-dependent stance implies limited immediate policy catalysts, leaving CAD direction driven largely by external USD movements, global risk appetite, and commodity-linked flows across major currency pairs.
