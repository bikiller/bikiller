Gold (XAU/USD) dropped to a four-week low as the US Dollar (USD) strengthened amid rising concerns over oil-driven inflation. The precious metal traded at $1,927, down 1.3% from the previous session, as the USD maintained momentum following hawkish remarks from Federal Reserve officials. The strong dollar, coupled with the uptick in crude oil prices, added pressure on gold, which traditionally faces headwinds in such an environment.

Market focus remains on geopolitical tensions, particularly stalled diplomatic talks between the United States and Iran over the ongoing conflict. Investors are closely monitoring these developments, as any shift could have broader implications for the USD and commodities. The EUR/USD exchange rate remained subdued, as traders refrained from making significant moves ahead of potential volatility linked to global energy prices and foreign policy shifts.
