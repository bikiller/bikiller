San Francisco Federal Reserve President Mary Daly signaled uncertainty over the Fed’s next move on interest rates, noting that there is no single “most likely” path for monetary policy. Speaking amid elevated oil prices driven by the Iran conflict, Daly emphasized that unless the situation stabilizes quickly, the central bank may not be able to simply “look through” temporary price spikes. This leaves USD trading volatile as markets weigh future rate decisions.  

The prospect of further Fed action has impacted currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY, with traders adjusting positions in response to potential shifts in policy. Rising oil costs have added another layer of complexity, pressuring risk-sensitive currencies and amplifying exchange rate fluctuations. Market participants are closely monitoring central bank signals to anticipate whether the Fed will tighten, pause, or cut rates, keeping USD trading active and reactive.  

Analysts suggest that forex markets could remain choppy until clarity emerges on both geopolitical tensions and Fed guidance. Investors are focusing on technical levels in major pairs, as short-term volatility could create trading opportunities while the broader direction of the USD remains uncertain. Oil-driven inflationary pressures combined with a cautious Fed outlook are expected to influence currency flows and shape exchange rate dynamics in the weeks ahead.
