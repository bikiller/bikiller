The Australian Dollar held its ground against the U.S. Dollar during Thursday’s Asian trading session, with the AUD/USD currency pair hovering around 0.6950. After two consecutive days of declines, the pair found stability as market participants digested comments from RBA Deputy Governor Michele Kent, who offered a cautious outlook on the economic and monetary landscape. Traders appeared reluctant to push the exchange rate significantly in either direction.  

Market reaction suggests that the AUD’s recent weakness may be pausing, with technical levels around 0.6950 providing a temporary floor. The cautious tone from the Reserve Bank of Australia tempered any aggressive bullish bets, while the USD maintained broad strength amid global risk sentiment. Analysts note that short-term trading may remain range-bound as investors weigh domestic economic signals against the performance of major currencies like the EUR and USD.  

Looking ahead, the Australian Dollar could face continued pressure if global data points to slower growth or if the USD gains further momentum. Traders are watching key support and resistance levels, with 0.6950 acting as a critical pivot for intraday strategies. The interplay between domestic RBA guidance and international currency flows will likely define the next phase of AUD/USD trading, as market participants balance caution with opportunistic positioning.
