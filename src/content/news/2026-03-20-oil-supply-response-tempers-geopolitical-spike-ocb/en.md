Crude oil prices briefly surged to $120 per barrel following attacks in Iran, prompting concerns over supply disruptions. The geopolitical spike was short-lived, however, as US officials assured the market of potential supply support. Meanwhile, Israel hinted at a quicker de-escalation in the region, which helped ease the upward pressure on prices. 

The immediate market reaction was tempered by these signals, as oil retreated from its highs. Traders closely monitored these developments, aware that the volatility could influence broader market dynamics. With oil trading in a range, the impact on the USD/EUR exchange rate and other currency pairs will depend on the ongoing balance between supply fears and geopolitical stability.
