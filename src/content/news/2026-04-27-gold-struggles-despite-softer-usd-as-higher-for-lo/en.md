Gold (XAU/USD) is trading slightly lower on Monday, showing a mild downside bias despite a softer US Dollar. The currency pair is hovering around $4,669, down approximately 0.84% on the day, after peaking intraday at $4,730. Traders are closely monitoring exchange rate fluctuations as macroeconomic concerns weigh on the market, limiting bullish momentum for the safe-haven metal.  

Market sentiment remains cautious as expectations of higher-for-longer US interest rates temper gold’s appeal. The weaker USD has provided limited support, as geopolitical tensions, including the ongoing US-Iran war, continue to influence trading decisions. Investors are balancing the metal’s traditional hedge against currency fluctuations with pressure from rising yields, keeping XAU/USD swings within a narrow range.  

Technical levels are under scrutiny as traders assess short-term trends. While the intraday high of $4,730 indicates potential resistance, the current price near $4,669 suggests consolidation around key support. Movements in other major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, are also impacting sentiment, as shifts in exchange rates influence cross-market flows and risk appetite.
