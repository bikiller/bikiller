The EUR/JPY currency pair trades in positive territory near 184.20 during the early European session on Monday, holding gains above the 184.00 level. The exchange rate shows steady trading momentum in the EUR/JPY cross, as the euro attempts to strengthen against the Japanese yen amid cautious USD sentiment influencing broader forex markets.

However, upside potential for the currency pair remains constrained as traders remain nervous about a fragile US-Iran ceasefire, which dampens global risk appetite. This risk-off tone limits demand for higher-yielding assets, keeping gains in check despite the euro’s resilience against the yen while technical indicators show a bearish bias below the 100-day SMA, suggesting limited bullish continuation in the EUR/JPY exchange rate amid USD volatility.

Looking ahead, traders will closely monitor whether EUR/JPY can sustain levels above 184.00 or face renewed selling pressure. A break below short-term support could reinforce downside momentum in the exchange rate, particularly if USD-driven volatility increases. For now, forex markets remain range-bound, with EUR and JPY reacting to geopolitical headlines and shifting risk sentiment.
