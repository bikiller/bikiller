The Indian Rupee (INR) fell sharply against the US Dollar (USD) in early trading this week, with the USD/INR exchange rate rising by 0.60% to reach 83.06. The Rupee's decline follows the failed US-Iran negotiations, which led to a surge in oil prices. As concerns over global supply disruptions grow, crude prices spiked, intensifying inflation fears in India and pressuring the INR further.

The rising oil prices have also weighed on the Indian economy, which is heavily reliant on crude imports. Traders are now closely monitoring the 83.10 resistance level for USD/INR, with potential to breach this threshold if geopolitical tensions continue to escalate. The strengthening of the USD has added to the volatility, as the Dollar remains supported by robust US economic data and tightening monetary policy, while the INR remains vulnerable amid global uncertainties.
