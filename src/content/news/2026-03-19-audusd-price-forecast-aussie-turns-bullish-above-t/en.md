The AUD/USD currency pair rose by 0.47% on Thursday, trading around 0.7050 as the US Dollar weakened. This movement comes despite traders pushing back expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts. The decline in US Crude Oil prices, down 4.21%, added further pressure on the greenback, contributing to the Australian Dollar’s gains. 

Technically, the Aussie is turning bullish, supported by its positioning above the 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA). With strong momentum, the pair eyes the 0.7100 mark as its next potential target. The combination of USD weakness and favorable market conditions has set the stage for further gains in the near term.
