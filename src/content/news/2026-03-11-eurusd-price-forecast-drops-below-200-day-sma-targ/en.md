The EUR/USD currency pair extended its decline for a second consecutive session on Tuesday, slipping below the crucial 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at 1.1672. The drop reflects persistent US Dollar strength, which has pressured the exchange rate despite the euro’s earlier attempts to regain momentum. Traders noted that the pair’s inability to hold above the SMA signals growing bearish sentiment in the market.  

Market participants are now eyeing the 1.1500 level as the next potential target for the EUR/USD. The technical breakdown below the long-term moving average suggests further downside risk, prompting traders to reassess positions and hedging strategies. Momentum indicators indicate that selling pressure could intensify if the USD continues to benefit from safe-haven flows and expectations of stronger US economic data.  

Analysts highlight that the euro’s vulnerability against the dollar is being compounded by uneven economic signals across the eurozone, while the USD remains underpinned by solid domestic fundamentals. This environment has kept trading activity focused on short-term swings and key technical levels, reinforcing the 1.1500 mark as a critical support zone for the currency pair in the near term. Investors are closely monitoring any shifts in risk sentiment that could influence the trajectory of the exchange rate.
