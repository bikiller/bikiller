The euro slipped against the Japanese yen on Tuesday, with the EUR/JPY currency pair dropping around 0.18% to trade at 184.93. Earlier in the session, the pair had peaked near 185.46, but renewed demand for the Japanese yen pushed the exchange rate lower. Traders noted that the JPY’s strength coincided with remarks from US Treasury officials, which triggered short-term volatility in forex markets.  

US Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent highlighted that excessive fluctuations in currency markets are undesirable, sending ripples across major pairs. His comments appeared to boost safe-haven flows into the yen, leading traders to reassess positions in EUR/JPY and other cross-pairs. The shift reflects growing market sensitivity to policy signals and global liquidity dynamics, impacting euro trading against both USD and JPY.  

Technically, the EUR/JPY pair now hovers below recent intraday highs, with analysts watching for potential support around the 184.50 level. Continued yen strength could pressure the euro further, while any market reassurance on FX stability might allow the pair to recover. Forex traders are closely monitoring these developments, as shifts in sentiment may influence broader exchange rate trends across USD, EUR, and other major currencies.
