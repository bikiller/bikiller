Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded on Friday, recovering from earlier losses as traders reacted to fresh geopolitical developments in the Middle East. At the time of writing, the currency pair was trading around $1,655, following a sharp dip to a one-month low of $1,510 earlier in the week. The market's sensitivity to the ongoing conflict has sparked safe-haven demand, temporarily lifting the price of gold.

However, gains were capped by persistent concerns over the Federal Reserve's "higher-for-longer" interest rate stance. The expectation that the USD will remain strong due to prolonged rate hikes kept upward pressure on the precious metal in check. Gold traders are balancing geopolitical risks with the ongoing implications of U.S. monetary policy, creating a tense environment for future price movements.
