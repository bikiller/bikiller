Brent crude oil experienced sharp swings this week, reflecting the unwinding of a war premium tied to Iran. Prices surged from $90 to $120 per barrel after President Trump hinted that the conflict might be nearing an end, before retracing as traders reassessed risks. MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst Michael Wan noted that this volatility has injected renewed caution across global markets, influencing both commodity-linked currencies and risk sentiment.

The gyrations in Brent have had ripple effects on major currency pairs, particularly USD-linked trading. The US dollar strengthened against the euro and yen as investors rotated into safe-haven positions, while commodity-sensitive currencies such as the Australian and Canadian dollars felt pressure. Wan emphasized that short-term exchange rate movements are increasingly tied to geopolitical headlines, highlighting the sensitivity of forex trading to rapid shifts in oil markets.

Analysts caution that Brent’s technical levels remain closely watched, with support near $95 and resistance around $125 shaping trading strategies. Currency traders are adjusting positions in EUR/USD and USD/JPY accordingly, while cross-asset correlations are under scrutiny. The episode underscores how geopolitical signals can trigger swift price action in both energy and currency markets, leaving traders to balance risk exposure with potential gains.
