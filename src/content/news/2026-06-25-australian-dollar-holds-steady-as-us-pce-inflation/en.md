The Australian Dollar remained broadly steady against the USD on Thursday, with the AUD/USD currency pair hovering near the 0.6900 level as traders weighed fresh US inflation data and rising geopolitical tensions around the Strait of Hormuz. Despite a modest pullback in the US Dollar, the Aussie struggled to gain momentum, reflecting cautious sentiment across foreign exchange markets.

Market attention centered on the latest US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) inflation figures, a key gauge closely watched by the Federal Reserve. The data reinforced expectations that the Fed will maintain a cautious policy stance, limiting the likelihood of near-term interest-rate cuts. As a result, USD demand remained relatively supported, preventing stronger upside movement in the AUD/USD exchange rate.

The muted trading pattern highlights the balance between softer Dollar pressure and broader risk concerns. Escalating geopolitical risks in the Middle East added another layer of uncertainty for investors, encouraging a defensive approach across currency markets. For traders, the 0.6900 area remains an important near-term technical level, with future direction likely to depend on incoming US economic data, Fed policy signals, and shifts in global risk appetite.
