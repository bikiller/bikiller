The US Dollar remained steady on Tuesday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering near the 99.90 level as traders adopted a cautious stance ahead of Wednesday’s closely watched US Consumer Price Index (CPI) release. Market participants expect inflation to edge higher in May, prompting a wait-and-see approach across major currency pairs and limiting broader USD volatility during the session.

Currency markets were also influenced by escalating tensions in the Middle East, which supported demand for safe-haven assets and helped underpin the Greenback. While risk sentiment remained fragile, investors refrained from making aggressive trading decisions before the inflation data, which could shape expectations for future Federal Reserve policy and drive near-term exchange rate movements.

The upcoming CPI report is expected to be a key catalyst for forex trading, particularly for major pairs such as EUR/USD. A stronger-than-expected inflation reading could reinforce the USD by reducing expectations for imminent rate cuts, while a softer outcome may weigh on the currency. As a result, traders are closely monitoring both geopolitical developments and economic data for direction in the foreign exchange market.
