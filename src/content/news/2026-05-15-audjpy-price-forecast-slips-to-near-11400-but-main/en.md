The AUD/JPY currency pair slipped to around 114.00 in early European trading on Friday, reflecting a softening in the Australian Dollar (AUD) against the Japanese Yen (JPY). Traders cite the stalled progress on reopening the Strait of Hormuz as a key factor weighing on risk sentiment, prompting demand for safe-haven assets like the JPY. Despite the pullback, the pair maintains a bullish structure above its 100-day moving average, hinting at underlying support.

Market participants remain cautious as global trade and geopolitical tensions continue to influence AUD flows, especially against the backdrop of USD and EUR trading. The AUD, often viewed as a China proxy, tends to respond to broader Asia-Pacific developments, and concerns over energy and shipping routes are keeping investors wary. Meanwhile, the JPY benefits from safe-haven inflows, reinforcing its resilience in turbulent conditions.

Technically, the 114.00 level acts as a short-term pivot, with the 100-day average offering a crucial floor for bulls. Traders eye potential rebounds if risk appetite returns, with AUD/JPY possibly resuming upward trading toward previous highs. Exchange rate movements remain sensitive to global headlines, USD strength, and broader market sentiment, underscoring the pair’s role in risk-on versus risk-off dynamics.
