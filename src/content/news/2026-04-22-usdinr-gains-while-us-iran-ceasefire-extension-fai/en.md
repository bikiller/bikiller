The Indian Rupee (INR) continued its downward trend against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, marking the third consecutive day of losses. The USD/INR currency pair traded at higher levels, reflecting a prevailing strength in the dollar as the exchange rate struggled against various global economic pressures. 

Despite the recent extension of the US-Iran ceasefire, investor sentiment remained cautious, failing to provide the anticipated support for the INR. As traders reacted to geopolitical developments, the INR lost ground, with the currency pair edging closer to significant resistance levels. This sustained weakness in the rupee could have broader implications for trade dynamics and inflation in India.
