Gold (XAU/USD) is trading within a narrow range on Friday, showing little movement as traders await key US inflation data. Currently hovering around $1,980, the yellow metal has been largely directionless, held back by cautious sentiment in the market. The focus remains on the evolving geopolitical situation in the Middle East, adding an element of uncertainty to global markets.

With the US Consumer Price Index (CPI) report scheduled for release later today, investors are adopting a wait-and-see approach. A stronger-than-expected inflation reading could boost the USD, putting downward pressure on gold, while a softer report may support the metal’s position. The upcoming data will be crucial for determining future Fed policy, with markets already pricing in the possibility of further tightening, which could impact both gold and major currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY.
