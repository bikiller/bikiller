The British Pound recovered against the US Dollar on Friday, with GBP/USD rebounding from intraday lows as improving market sentiment weighed on the greenback. The currency pair traded around 1.3460 at the time of writing, supported by renewed optimism surrounding a potential US-Iran peace agreement. The softer USD encouraged buying interest in risk-sensitive assets, helping Sterling regain lost ground after earlier weakness.

Broader foreign exchange trading was influenced by a shift in risk appetite, as investors reacted to reports suggesting progress toward a possible diplomatic breakthrough between Washington and Tehran. Expectations that easing geopolitical tensions could reduce demand for safe-haven assets pressured the US Dollar, allowing major currencies, including the Pound and EUR, to stabilize against the greenback. Despite the intraday recovery, GBP/USD remained on track to finish the week largely unchanged.

The latest price action highlights how geopolitical developments continue to shape currency market dynamics and exchange rate movements. While the Pound’s rebound reflects a temporary improvement in sentiment, traders remain cautious ahead of further developments that could influence USD direction and broader market positioning. For now, GBP/USD’s ability to hold near 1.3460 suggests balanced trading conditions as investors assess both geopolitical risks and macroeconomic drivers.
