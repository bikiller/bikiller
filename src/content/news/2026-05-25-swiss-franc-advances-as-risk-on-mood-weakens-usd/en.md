The Swiss franc gained ground as risk appetite softened, pushing the USD/CHF currency pair lower for a fourth consecutive session. During Asian trading on Monday, the pair hovered around 0.7820, reflecting sustained dollar weakness amid cautious sentiment. Market participants noted that the franc's safe-haven appeal was bolstered as investors trimmed exposure to riskier assets.

The decline in USD/CHF coincided with broad dollar weakness against major currencies, including the euro, as traders reassessed prospects for U.S. economic growth and monetary policy. Technical charts suggest that support near 0.7800 could be tested if the current trend continues, while resistance levels around 0.7850 may cap near-term recovery attempts. Analysts highlighted that shifts in sentiment, rather than major economic data, were driving the trading pattern.

Looking ahead, currency traders are eyeing the interplay between risk sentiment and central bank guidance. A sustained risk-off mood could favor further franc strength, potentially pressuring the USD/CHF exchange rate toward multi-week lows. Conversely, a revival in risk appetite could see the greenback regain ground, making short-term trading opportunities in the pair more volatile. Market watchers remain attentive to both forex flows and geopolitical cues influencing demand for safe-haven currencies.
