Gold (XAU/USD) saw a brief surge to $5,400 during the Asian session on Monday, marking its highest point since late January. However, the precious metal retreated, now trading just above the key $5,300 level. Despite this pullback, gold is still up over 1.0% on the day, fueled by ongoing Middle East tensions that continue to support demand for safe-haven assets.

The retreat comes as traders recalibrate after the sharp rally, with the price stabilizing below the mid-$5,300s. The volatility reflects broader market concerns, with the USD and other currencies experiencing mixed reactions to the geopolitical uncertainty. With gold holding its ground above $5,300, further fluctuations are likely as the market responds to any developments in the region.
