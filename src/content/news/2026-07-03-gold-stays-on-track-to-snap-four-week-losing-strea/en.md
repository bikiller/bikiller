Gold (XAU/USD) extended its upward momentum for a third consecutive trading session on Friday, maintaining a bullish bias during the first half of the European session. The precious metal hovered near a one-and-a-half-week high and is on track to snap a four-week losing streak, supported by softer USD conditions in forex trading.

The upside in XAU/USD is largely driven by fading expectations of further Federal Reserve interest rate hikes, which has pressured the US Dollar and lowered US Treasury yields. In forex markets, a weaker USD typically boosts gold’s appeal, improving exchange rate dynamics for bullion priced in dollars, while traders reassess monetary policy outlooks.

Traders are now watching whether XAU/USD can sustain its bullish momentum above recent highs as the market assesses incoming US economic data and USD positioning. Continued weakness in the dollar could extend the recovery in gold, while any rebound in rate hike expectations may cap further gains in the currency pair in near-term trading.
