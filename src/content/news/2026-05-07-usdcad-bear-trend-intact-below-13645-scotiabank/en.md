The USD/CAD currency pair remains under pressure below the 1.3645 level, maintaining a bearish trend, according to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret. Trading near 1.36, the pair has shown limited movement as markets await developments on US-Iran tensions and anticipate Friday’s Canadian employment report. Despite recent weakness, the spot rate still sits above Scotiabank’s estimated fair value of 1.3514.  

Market participants are monitoring broader forex sentiment, with the USD showing mixed performance against major peers such as the EUR and JPY. Risk appetite and geopolitical headlines continue to influence trading flows, keeping the Canadian Dollar relatively steady. Analysts suggest that any sharp moves in the US dollar could prompt further adjustments in USD/CAD, with technical levels like 1.3645 serving as key resistance for traders.  

Looking ahead, the Canadian employment data will be closely watched for its potential impact on the exchange rate. A stronger-than-expected print could support the CAD, pushing USD/CAD lower, while disappointing figures may allow the dollar to regain ground. For now, the currency pair remains range-bound, reflecting cautious trading amid geopolitical and economic uncertainty.
