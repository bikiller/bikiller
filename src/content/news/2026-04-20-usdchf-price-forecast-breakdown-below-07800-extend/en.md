The USD/CHF currency pair extended its recent downtrend on Monday, testing a critical support level at the trendline following a decisive move below the 50-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at 0.7828. The breach of the 0.7800 mark intensified selling pressure, pushing the exchange rate toward last week’s lows around 0.7775. Traders noted increased bearish momentum as the pair struggled to regain footing above key technical levels.  

Market sentiment is weighed down by a stronger USD amid risk-averse trading, as investors shift focus to U.S. economic data and global geopolitical developments. The inability of USD/CHF to hold above 0.7800 signals continued vulnerability, potentially attracting short positions targeting further declines. Meanwhile, the EUR/CHF pair shows relative stability, highlighting divergent strength between the euro and the franc versus the U.S. dollar.  

Analysts suggest that a sustained drop below 0.7775 could open the door for deeper losses, with traders eyeing support near 0.7750. Conversely, a rebound above 0.7828 might indicate a temporary recovery in the exchange rate. The ongoing price action emphasizes caution in trading USD/CHF, as technical indicators signal persistent downside risk while broader currency markets remain sensitive to global economic cues.
