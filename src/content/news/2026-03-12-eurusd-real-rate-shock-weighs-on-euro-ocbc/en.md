The EUR/USD currency pair is feeling the pressure as a rise in nominal interest rate expectations from the European Central Bank (ECB) fails to bolster the Euro. According to OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, the impact of oil-driven stagflation is eroding real returns in the euro area, exacerbating the region's external balance issues. This economic backdrop has led to increased selling pressure on the Euro, impacting overall exchange rates.

As market participants navigate this challenging landscape, the external balance concerns are becoming more pronounced. The exchange rate between EUR and USD reflects these dynamics, with traders closely monitoring shifts in economic indicators that could signal further volatility. In light of these developments, the Euro's ability to recover will be contingent upon stabilizing inflation and improving the region's economic outlook.
