GBP/USD slipped 0.21% on Wednesday, trading at approximately 1.3320, after the release of a stronger-than-expected U.S. inflation report. The U.S. Producer Price Index (PPI) data showed hotter-than-anticipated price pressures, reigniting fears of persistent inflation in the U.S. This prompted a shift in market expectations, with investors scaling back their dovish bets on the Federal Reserve.

The PPI report has caused traders to reassess their outlook on Fed policy, leading to a hawkish repricing of U.S. interest rate expectations. As a result, the USD strengthened, adding downward pressure on GBP/USD. With the pair now hovering around key technical levels, market participants will closely monitor upcoming U.S. economic data and Fed commentary for further clues on the central bank's monetary tightening path.
