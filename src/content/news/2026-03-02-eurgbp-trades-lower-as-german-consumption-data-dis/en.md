EUR/GBP is trading lower around 0.8750 on Monday, down 0.23% for the day following two consecutive days of gains. Profit-taking has pushed the currency pair lower as the Euro (EUR) faces pressure from disappointing German consumption data. This economic setback is weighing on the outlook for the single currency, with traders reassessing the strength of the Eurozone's recovery.

The decline in EUR/GBP comes amid expectations of a potential rate cut by the Bank of England (BoE). With the UK facing its own economic challenges, any dovish signals from the BoE could drive further movement in the exchange rate. At the same time, market participants are watching closely for further developments as the cross looks set to consolidate at these levels in the short term.
