Emerging markets are showing resilience in 2026 as a softer US Dollar underpins broader gains across currencies and equities. HSBC economists highlight that a weakening USD is boosting the appeal of EM assets, with investors shifting focus from US mega-cap tech to diversified markets. Currency pairs like USD/BRL and USD/INR are reflecting this trend, trading at more favorable exchange rates that support higher returns.

Global policy easing is further bolstering EM performance, as central banks in Asia and Latin America maintain accommodative stances. This environment encourages trading in emerging market currencies against the EUR and USD, creating opportunities for carry trades and portfolio diversification. Analysts note that EM equities are gaining momentum, while FX volatility remains manageable, signaling sustained investor confidence.

For traders, these dynamics suggest that monitoring technical levels in major currency pairs such as USD/TRY and USD/ZAR will be crucial. With broadening market participation, exchange rates are reacting to both monetary policy signals and USD weakness, implying that 2026 could be a favorable year for capturing gains beyond traditional US tech exposure. Strategic positioning in EM currencies could therefore offer compelling risk-adjusted returns.
