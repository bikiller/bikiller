Gold prices extended their decline in early Asian trading on Tuesday, with XAU/USD slipping to around $4,485 as investors reacted to renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. While escalating concerns surrounding Iran typically support safe-haven demand, markets have increasingly focused on the inflationary risks associated with rising regional instability. Higher energy costs and supply-chain uncertainties have reinforced expectations that inflation could remain elevated for longer, weighing on bullion prices.

The precious metal came under pressure as traders reassessed the outlook for global monetary policy, particularly in the United States. Persistent inflation concerns have strengthened speculation that the Federal Reserve may keep interest rates higher for an extended period or even consider additional tightening if price pressures intensify. This backdrop has supported the USD, making non-yielding assets such as gold less attractive relative to interest-bearing investments.

Currency markets also reflected the shift in sentiment, with investors closely monitoring major currency pair movements and exchange rate fluctuations for clues on broader risk appetite. A firmer US Dollar against counterparts including the EUR has added further pressure on gold, as a stronger USD typically raises the cost of the metal for holders of other currencies. Market participants now remain focused on incoming economic data and central bank signals for direction in both precious metals and forex trading.
