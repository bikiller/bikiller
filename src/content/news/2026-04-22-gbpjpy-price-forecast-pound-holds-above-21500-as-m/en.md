GBP/JPY is trading sideways on Wednesday, holding above the 215.00 level after posting three consecutive days of gains. Despite the recent advance, upside momentum is beginning to fade as traders reassess risk appetite. Market sentiment remains broadly neutral, with a slight optimistic tilt, as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East continue to shape overall forex positioning across major currency pairs.

From a technical perspective, the currency pair’s ability to stay above 215.00 keeps buyers in control in the short term, though resistance above recent highs is limiting further upside. Traders are also watching broader USD and EUR movements for cues, as shifts in dollar strength and euro volatility often spill over into cross pairs like GBP/JPY. The lack of strong catalysts has contributed to subdued trading activity.

Looking ahead, GBP/JPY may remain in a consolidation phase unless fresh macroeconomic drivers emerge, including risk sentiment changes tied to global geopolitical developments or central bank expectations. Volatility could return if USD demand strengthens or if safe-haven flows intensify, potentially pushing the pair out of its current range-bound structure around the 215.00 handle.
