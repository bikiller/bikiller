WTI Crude Oil prices faced resistance near the $100.00 mark, retreating after reaching a one-week high during the Asian session on Monday. Despite briefly touching this psychological level, the commodity couldn't sustain the momentum as concerns over the Strait of Hormuz reopening weighed on market sentiment. This development comes amid ongoing efforts to ease tensions in the region, which had supported previous oil price gains.

As WTI struggles below $100, traders are watching key technical levels for signs of further movement. The $98.50 level remains critical, with a break below potentially signaling further declines. With the USD continuing to influence oil pricing, fluctuations in the exchange rate between the greenback and other major currencies, including EUR and GBP, will also play a crucial role in shaping the oil market's direction in the coming days.
