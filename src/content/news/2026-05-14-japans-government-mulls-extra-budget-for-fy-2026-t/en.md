Japan is considering an additional budget for FY 2026 to offset rising oil costs, aiming to relieve households facing higher energy bills. The move comes amid surging crude prices, which have put upward pressure on the yen, influencing USD/JPY and EUR/JPY trading. Forex traders are watching closely as policy signals could sway short-term exchange rate movements and market sentiment.  

Analysts suggest the extra spending may stabilize domestic consumption, potentially supporting the yen against the dollar. Technical levels around 150.50 in USD/JPY and 160.20 in EUR/JPY could see increased trading activity as investors react to policy expectations. Higher oil prices have already impacted inflation metrics, making fiscal intervention a key factor for currency markets.  

The market is weighing the broader implications: a larger budget could temper economic slowdown but may add pressure to government debt. Traders are adjusting positions in USD, EUR, and JPY, monitoring for volatility spikes. The move underscores the link between commodity prices and forex flows, highlighting how macroeconomic policy choices ripple through currency pair dynamics.
