WTI crude oil edged up to its highest level in nearly two weeks on Wednesday, driven by renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East that cast doubt over a near-term US-Iran deal. Concerns over disruptions to shipments through the Strait of Hormuz pushed Brent and WTI prices higher, influencing forex markets as the USD strengthened slightly against the EUR and JPY amid risk-off sentiment. Traders are watching oil-related currency pairs closely for volatility signals.

The Energy Information Administration (EIA) reported a draw in US crude stockpiles, further supporting bullish momentum. WTI futures briefly touched near $85 a barrel, while Brent hovered around $87. This combination of geopolitical risk and domestic supply tightening has prompted traders to adjust positions in commodities-linked currency pairs, with the AUD/USD and CAD/USD seeing increased trading activity as market participants hedge against oil price swings.

Market analysts suggest that continued uncertainty in the Strait of Hormuz could sustain elevated crude levels, keeping pressure on oil-importing nations and influencing central bank considerations. Currency exchange rates may remain volatile, with the USD likely benefiting from safe-haven flows, while the EUR/USD pair faces downward pressure. Traders are advised to monitor geopolitical developments and inventory data closely to navigate potential spikes in trading volatility.
