Pound Sterling extended its recovery on Wednesday, with the GBP/USD currency pair drawing renewed buying interest for the second consecutive session. The pair rebounded from its weekly low near the 1.3515-1.3510 range touched on Tuesday, benefiting from a softer US dollar amid growing optimism over a potential US-Iran peace agreement. Traders noted that this support helped the exchange rate regain upward momentum.  

Market participants highlighted the USD’s weakness as a key driver behind Sterling’s advance. As investors assessed the geopolitical developments, risk sentiment improved, prompting a shift from safe-haven holdings into more volatile currencies such as the GBP. Technical analysts observed that the 1.3510-1.3515 level provided a critical floor, encouraging short-term traders to increase long positions in the currency pair.  

The broader trading landscape also reflected spillover effects, with EUR/USD and other major pairs reacting to shifts in US dollar demand. Analysts suggested that sustained optimism over diplomatic progress could reinforce GBP gains while keeping the USD under pressure. For now, market watchers are closely monitoring both the technical levels and geopolitical headlines, as any further escalation or disappointment could quickly reshape exchange rate dynamics across major forex markets.
