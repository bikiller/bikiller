The US Dollar maintains a firm stance near the 98.40 level on the US Dollar Index (DXY), underpinned by safe-haven buying as tensions escalate in the Middle East. Reports suggest Iran may have targeted a US military vessel, although Washington denies the claim. This geopolitical uncertainty is keeping investors cautious, boosting demand for the USD against risk-sensitive currencies such as the AUD and NZD.  

Currency pairs like EUR/USD are trading lower, reflecting the greenback’s resilience, while USD/JPY edges higher as traders weigh potential safe-haven flows. Market participants are also eyeing upcoming US economic data, which could influence short-term exchange rate movements. Technical analysts note that sustaining the 98.40 support could signal continued USD strength, while any breach might trigger a corrective pullback.  

Ahead of the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) policy meeting, the AUD faces pressure against the USD, with traders wary of both global risk sentiment and central bank guidance. The confluence of geopolitical risk and data-driven expectations is shaping forex trading dynamics, keeping the US Dollar in focus as a benchmark currency. Investors are positioning cautiously, balancing potential gains from USD strength with volatility risks across major pairs.
