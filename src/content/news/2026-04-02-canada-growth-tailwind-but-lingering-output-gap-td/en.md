Higher oil prices are expected to provide a positive boost to Canada's GDP in 2026, according to TD Securities' Robert Both. With crude oil prices on the rise, Canada’s economy is likely to benefit from stronger export revenues. However, persistent bottlenecks in exports could limit the overall growth potential, creating a drag on economic performance.

The Canadian dollar (CAD) could see some support from this growth trajectory, but the export challenges may cap significant gains in the currency pair. Traders will likely keep an eye on the USD/CAD exchange rate, which could face resistance as global oil dynamics influence the broader market. For EUR/CAD, the near-term outlook remains cautious, with export constraints putting a damper on a more substantial rally. The market’s focus will remain on navigating these challenges while balancing the benefits of rising oil prices.
