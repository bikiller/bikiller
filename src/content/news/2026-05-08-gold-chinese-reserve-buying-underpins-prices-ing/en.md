Gold prices are nudging higher as persistent central bank purchases, particularly from China, continue to provide support. ING strategists Ewa Manthey and Warren Patterson highlight that this steady demand is cushioning the yellow metal against recent market volatility. Geopolitical tensions are adding an extra layer of upward pressure, while the USD remains under moderate strength, keeping traders cautious but engaged in gold-related positions.  

In currency markets, the USD’s exchange rate against the EUR and other major pairs shows a mixed picture, influencing safe-haven flows into gold. The XAU/USD trading range has been tight, with technical levels around $2,020–$2,040 per ounce acting as short-term support and resistance. Traders are watching how central bank buying, especially in Asia, interacts with broader forex trends, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY moves, to gauge potential breakouts.  

Looking ahead, sustained reserve accumulation by China and ongoing geopolitical uncertainty could keep gold trading resilient, even if the USD edges higher. Currency pair movements, particularly against emerging market currencies, may further influence positioning in precious metals. Market participants are weighing these factors carefully, as shifts in exchange rates could either amplify safe-haven demand or create short-term retracements in gold prices.
