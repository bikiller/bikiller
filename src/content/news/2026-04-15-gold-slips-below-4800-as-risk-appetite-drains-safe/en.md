Gold retreated below $4,800 as investor risk appetite strengthened, eroding demand for the traditional safe-haven asset. The yellow metal fell nearly 1% during the session, reflecting a rotation of flows toward riskier assets. Rising US equities, particularly the S&P 500, which climbed past 7,000 and is eyeing the all-time high of 7,014, weighed on bullion’s appeal amid broad USD strength in currency markets.  

Traders noted that the stronger USD is influencing commodity pricing, with currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD showing modest depreciation, reinforcing dollar-denominated gold’s relative cost. Market participants highlighted that as equities attract capital, gold’s inverse relationship with risk-on sentiment becomes more pronounced. The current technical setup suggests near-term support levels around $4,780, while a rebound could face resistance at $4,820, keeping traders cautious in positioning.  

FX and commodity traders are watching closely as the dynamics between USD exchange rates and gold prices play out. Continued rotation into US equities could keep the metal under pressure, while shifts in the EUR/USD or USD/JPY may create short-term trading opportunities. Analysts suggest that momentum in the S&P 500, combined with global currency moves, will likely dictate gold’s trajectory and influence hedging strategies in the forex and commodities markets.
