Gold prices slid over 2% as US President Donald Trump extended military operations in Iran, dampening expectations for a quick resolution in the Middle East. The announcement that the conflict would last another two to three weeks bolstered the US Dollar, prompting a sell-off in safe-haven assets. Spot gold fell to $1,841 per ounce, while the US Dollar gained across the board.

The strengthening USD impacted currency pairs such as EUR/USD, which traded lower at 1.0840, showing a bearish trend. Traders reacted to the geopolitical uncertainty by favoring the greenback, which gained momentum amid rising risk aversion. With Trump’s remarks signaling a prolonged conflict, investors are reassessing their positions, fueling further demand for USD-denominated assets. The outlook for gold remains cautious as geopolitical tensions persist.
