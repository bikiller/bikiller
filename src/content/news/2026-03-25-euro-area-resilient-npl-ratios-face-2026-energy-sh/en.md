BNP Paribas reports that EU manufacturing firms are entering the anticipated 2026 energy shock with historically low non-performing loan (NPL) ratios, signaling improved financial resilience compared to 2022. Despite potential energy disruptions from Iran, these firms' strong balance sheets may help stabilize the EUR/USD exchange rate. This development is significant for traders, as the Euro may remain relatively strong against the USD in the near term, reflecting the EU's financial stability.

With NPL ratios lower than in previous years, market participants could see less pressure on the euro amid external shocks. The 2026 energy crisis is likely to have a muted impact on the EUR/USD trading range if firms can maintain their current financial health. Traders should monitor this trend closely, as it could reduce volatility in the forex market and offer further opportunities for EUR gains against other major currencies.
