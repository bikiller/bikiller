Silver (XAG/USD) is struggling below key simple moving averages as the currency pair pauses its four-day winning streak on Monday. After a strong advance last week, when trading activity drove a 5.55% rally, buyers are now stepping back to reassess momentum in the exchange rate amid broader forex caution and softer risk appetite in global markets.  

Firmer US Dollar (USD) strength is limiting upside momentum for XAG/USD, even as easing Federal Reserve rate hike expectations provide underlying support for precious metals. The exchange rate remains technically capped below major simple moving averages, keeping trading conditions uneven across forex markets and reinforcing short-term consolidation pressure in the spot market.  

Market participants now watch whether XAG/USD can reclaim key resistance zones after last week's 5.55% gain, with USD trends and broader forex sentiment, including EUR/USD direction, likely to influence near-term trading. Continued dollar resilience could keep silver range-bound, while renewed risk appetite may trigger another upside attempt in the sessions ahead.
