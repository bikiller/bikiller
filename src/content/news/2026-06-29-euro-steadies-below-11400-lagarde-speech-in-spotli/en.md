The EUR/USD currency pair is holding steady in early European trading on Monday, hovering near 1.1385, keeping the exchange rate below the 1.1400 mark. The euro shows limited momentum against the USD as forex trading remains cautious, with investors awaiting fresh catalysts to break the narrow range and define near-term direction.

Punctuated by assessment of developments surrounding talks aimed at ending the US war with Iran, market sentiment remains fragile. The potential for de-escalation has supported risk appetite intermittently, yet uncertainty continues to influence USD demand and broader currency pair movements. Traders are closely monitoring geopolitical headlines, as shifts in risk perception can quickly alter EUR/USD trading dynamics and volatility across forex markets.

Attention turns to European Central Bank President Christine Lagarde, whose upcoming speech is in focus for clues on future monetary policy direction. Any hawkish or dovish signals could impact euro valuation and EUR/USD exchange rate movements. With liquidity thin and traders cautious, the speech may trigger short-term volatility, especially around the 1.1400 resistance zone in the currency pair.
