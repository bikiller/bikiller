The Pound Sterling slipped against the US Dollar in early trading on Monday, with the GBP/USD currency pair hovering around 1.3240, marking its lowest level in nearly two weeks. Investors showed caution as reports of a potential US ground invasion heightened global risk aversion, prompting traders to favor safe-haven assets like the USD. The move reflects a broad risk-off sentiment across major currency pairs.  

Market participants noted that the pound struggled to gain traction against the greenback, with the EUR/USD also showing muted momentum amid heightened geopolitical tensions. Analysts suggested that the sterling’s recent decline is technically supported by resistance near 1.3350, leaving the 1.3200 level as a key short-term floor. Trading activity indicated that risk-sensitive currencies could remain under pressure if uncertainty persists.  

Traders are monitoring developments closely, as a sustained move below 1.3240 could open the door for further declines toward 1.3200. Conversely, any easing of geopolitical concerns could support a rebound in the GBP/USD exchange rate. With the dollar benefiting from safe-haven demand, currency markets remain sensitive to news flows, and positions in major pairs like GBP/USD, EUR/USD, and USD/JPY are likely to remain volatile in the near term.
