The South Korean won (KRW) has remained volatile, primarily due to rising oil prices and the country's heavy reliance on Middle Eastern crude. This has kept the USD/KRW exchange rate fluctuating between 1,420 and 1,500, with the won showing a decline year-to-date. The surge in oil prices continues to exacerbate pressure on the KRW, creating a challenging environment for traders.

As the Bank of Korea (BoK) steps in to defend the won, concerns over further weakness persist. The oil shock, combined with South Korea's exposure to higher energy costs, has added significant stress to the won's performance. Market participants remain cautious, with the USD/KRW trading near the upper end of its recent range, reflecting ongoing uncertainty in the currency pair's outlook.
