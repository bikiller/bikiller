Brent crude has experienced sharp volatility in recent weeks as traders react to evolving prospects of a US-Iran deal. According to Deutsche Bank analysts, Brent fell 19.3% in May, marking its steepest monthly decline since March 2020. Last week alone, it dropped 11.1% amid growing optimism over a potential ceasefire, prompting swings across energy-linked currency pairs and weighing on USD/EUR trading sentiment.

The market reaction reflects heightened sensitivity to geopolitical developments, with oil-linked currencies such as the Norwegian krone and Canadian dollar showing notable intraday movements. Exchange rates in key pairs, including USD/JPY and EUR/USD, mirrored shifts in risk appetite, as investors recalibrated trading positions around energy sector exposure. Analysts noted that technical support levels for Brent are being tested, influencing hedging strategies and speculative flows in forex markets.

Looking ahead, any formal agreement between the US and Iran could further reshape crude valuations, impacting commodity-linked currencies and broader forex trading dynamics. Traders are closely monitoring spot and futures prices for signs of stabilization, while the USD continues to respond to oil-driven volatility. Market participants are expected to adjust positions proactively, keeping volatility high and trading volumes elevated as news headlines unfold.
