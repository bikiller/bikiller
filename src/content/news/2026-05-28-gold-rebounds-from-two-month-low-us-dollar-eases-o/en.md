Gold (XAU/USD) bounced back from a two-month low on Thursday as the US Dollar (USD) softened amid renewed optimism over a potential US-Iran truce. The exchange rate saw the precious metal retrace gains after recent declines, with traders reacting to headlines suggesting progress in diplomacy and easing inflation pressures in the US. Gold trading reflected heightened demand as investors sought safe-haven assets.  

The USD index slipped against major currencies including the euro (EUR) and yen, contributing to gold’s rebound. Traders noted that the softer US inflation data dampened expectations for aggressive Federal Reserve tightening, pressuring the USD lower and supporting commodities priced in dollars. Currency pair movements, particularly EUR/USD and USD/JPY, mirrored the broader market sentiment as investors recalibrated positions on risk and geopolitical developments.  

Market analysts highlighted that gold’s rebound could extend toward key technical levels if USD weakness continues. Forex trading volumes showed increased activity in gold-related pairs, with investors eyeing both short-term rallies and longer-term implications of a potential diplomatic breakthrough. Traders remain cautious, balancing geopolitical optimism with underlying US economic data that could influence exchange rates and precious metal prices in coming sessions.
