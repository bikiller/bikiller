Gold (XAU/USD) held a cautious tone through Monday’s Asian session, trading just above the $4,600 mark amid persistent inflation concerns. Investors are weighing the potential for more hawkish moves from major central banks, which has kept safe-haven demand for gold under pressure. Despite the cautious footing, the metal has found some support, with limited follow-through preventing a sharper decline in exchange rates against the USD.  

The currency markets reflected similar caution, with the USD strengthening broadly against major counterparts like the EUR and JPY. Traders have been pricing in higher interest rate expectations, which has pressured gold and other non-yielding assets. The exchange rate dynamics highlight how central bank policy signals continue to influence both forex trading and commodity flows, as investors adjust positions across multiple asset classes.  

Technically, XAU/USD remains above the $4,600 support level, but momentum appears subdued, signaling a potential consolidation phase unless fresh economic data triggers renewed volatility. With central bank guidance and inflation prints in focus this week, traders are closely monitoring the interplay between USD strength, gold’s exchange rate, and broader market sentiment, which could set the tone for both forex and precious metals trading in the near term.
