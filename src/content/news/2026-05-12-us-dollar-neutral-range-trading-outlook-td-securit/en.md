TD Securities has adopted a neutral short-term outlook for the US Dollar (USD) following the release of April's US Consumer Price Index (CPI). The data showed a slight uptick in inflation, with core CPI rising by 0.4%, indicating potential for modest US interest rate adjustments. This has kept the USD in a consolidated range, with the EUR/USD exchange rate fluctuating near 1.0900.

Market reactions have been muted, as the CPI figures did not drastically alter expectations for the Federal Reserve's next moves. Despite some volatility, USD remains resilient, staying within a neutral range, unable to break decisively above key resistance or fall below critical support levels. Traders are likely to maintain cautious stances, awaiting more significant economic signals.
