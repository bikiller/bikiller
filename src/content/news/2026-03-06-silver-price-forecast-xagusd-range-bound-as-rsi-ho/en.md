Silver (XAG/USD) edged slightly higher on Friday as traders digested weaker-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data, which eased pressure on the US Dollar and Treasury yields. The currency pair hovered around $25.10, with the RSI lingering near 50 and the MACD flattening, signaling a lack of strong directional momentum. Despite the intraday gains, silver remains poised for its first weekly decline in three weeks.  

Market participants noted that the subdued US employment figures weighed on USD strength, encouraging short-term buying in XAG/USD. The exchange rate’s range-bound behavior reflects cautious trading, with investors balancing softer economic data against broader risk sentiment. Analysts suggest that while silver benefits from temporary USD weakness, any sustained rally may be limited without follow-through from macroeconomic indicators.  

Technically, XAG/USD continues to consolidate, as flattening MACD and neutral RSI hint at indecision among traders. The precious metal’s modest rebound is unlikely to reverse the weekly downtrend unless momentum picks up, potentially influenced by upcoming US inflation and retail sales reports. Traders remain attentive to shifts in currency pair dynamics, particularly the interplay between USD, EUR, and safe-haven assets.
