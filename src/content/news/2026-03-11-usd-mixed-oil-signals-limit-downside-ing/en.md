The USD is closely tracking oil volatility, with recent movements in Brent crude adding pressure to the currency. Oil prices have been fluctuating amid mixed reports on Middle East tensions and the potential for a record IEA reserve release. This uncertainty has kept the USD from falling significantly, as traders remain wary of any negative impacts on the global economy. 

EUR/USD has been consolidating around the 1.0600 mark, reflecting the broader risk sentiment in the market. The ongoing oil market turbulence is limiting any substantial downside for the USD, despite a slightly weaker tone in global growth forecasts. Traders are keeping a close eye on any new developments in oil that might further impact the exchange rate, while the EUR remains sensitive to shifts in the broader economic outlook.
