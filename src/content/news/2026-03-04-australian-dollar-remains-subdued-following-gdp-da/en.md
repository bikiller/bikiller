The Australian Dollar weakened further on Wednesday, with the AUD/USD currency pair extending losses for a second consecutive session and trading near 0.7010 during Asian trading hours. The exchange rate remained under pressure after the release of Australia’s latest Gross Domestic Product (GDP) data, which dampened sentiment toward the AUD and reinforced cautious positioning among forex traders.

Market participants interpreted the GDP figures as a signal of softer economic momentum, prompting renewed demand for the USD and pushing the AUD/USD pair lower in early trading. The reaction reflects broader currency market dynamics, where macroeconomic indicators often influence capital flows and short-term trading strategies. As a result, the Australian Dollar struggled to attract buyers despite relatively stable global risk conditions.

The subdued performance of the AUD also highlights the sensitivity of commodity-linked currencies to domestic growth signals and shifts in investor expectations. With the pair hovering around the 0.7010 level, traders are closely monitoring whether the exchange rate can stabilize or if further downside pressure emerges. Upcoming economic indicators and broader USD strength could continue shaping the near-term direction of the AUD/USD currency pair in forex markets.
