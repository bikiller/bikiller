Oil prices surged sharply on Monday as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East escalated. Western Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude jumped over 3%, driven by Iran’s attacks on the United Arab Emirates and rising concerns over potential US and Israeli responses. Traders are closely watching the Strait of Hormuz, a critical oil chokepoint, as any disruption could tighten global supply, pushing crude and energy-linked currencies higher in trading.  

The spike in oil has reverberated across forex markets, with the USD strengthening against commodity-linked currencies. The AUD/USD and USD/CAD pairs saw increased volatility as traders priced in higher crude-driven risk premiums. Meanwhile, the EUR/USD exchange rate held near key technical support, reflecting cautious sentiment amid safe-haven flows into the US dollar as geopolitical uncertainties mount.  

Market participants are now weighing both short-term disruptions and longer-term implications for global trade. Brent futures are likely to mirror WTI’s move, influencing FX pairs tied to energy exports. Analysts warn that further escalation could amplify volatility across currency pairs, with emerging markets particularly exposed. Trading desks are adjusting hedges and positioning strategies to navigate a fast-shifting environment where oil shocks and geopolitical risk are intertwined.
