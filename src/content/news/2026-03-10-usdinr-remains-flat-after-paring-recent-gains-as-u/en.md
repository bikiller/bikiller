USD/INR held steady in early trading, paring recent gains after a modest rebound following slight losses in the previous session. The currency pair briefly surged to a record high of 92.81 on Monday, reflecting persistent selling pressure on the Indian Rupee amid a backdrop of rising global oil prices and heightened risk aversion. Traders cited the weakening US Dollar as a factor limiting further upside.  

Market participants noted that the INR has struggled against the USD as foreign inflows slowed and crude costs climbed, adding to inflation concerns. The exchange rate’s recent volatility underscores the sensitivity of the rupee to external factors, including shifts in risk sentiment and movements in major currencies such as the EUR and JPY. Analysts suggest that while the pair remains near all-time highs, short-term trading may see consolidation around current levels.  

Investors continue to monitor global cues, with the USD index and benchmark oil futures influencing intraday moves in the USD/INR. The pair’s trajectory could have broader implications for imports, exports, and monetary policy expectations. Traders are watching technical levels closely, noting that any breach above 92.81 may trigger further intervention or speculative activity, while a pullback could provide a temporary reprieve for the Rupee.
