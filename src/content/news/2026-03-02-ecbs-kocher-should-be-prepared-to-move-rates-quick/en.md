European Central Bank policymaker Martin Kocher signaled that the ECB must remain ready to adjust interest rates swiftly in either direction, reinforcing expectations of a flexible and data-dependent approach. In an interview on Monday, the Austrian central bank governor stressed that policymakers should not hesitate to respond quickly if inflation or growth dynamics shift, a message closely watched by EUR traders.

The remarks come as the EUR/USD currency pair continues to trade in a narrow range, with investors weighing the timing of potential policy adjustments against a resilient USD. Kocher’s comments underline that rate cuts are not on a preset path and that tightening cannot be ruled out if price pressures re-emerge, adding two-way risk to the euro exchange rate outlook.

For forex markets, the signal of agility from Frankfurt injects fresh volatility potential into euro trading. A readiness to move rates quickly could amplify short-term swings in EUR crosses, particularly against the USD, as traders recalibrate expectations around yield differentials and future ECB policy decisions.
