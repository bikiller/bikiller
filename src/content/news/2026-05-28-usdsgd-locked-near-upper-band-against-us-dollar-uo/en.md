USD/SGD remained pinned near the upper end of its recent range, with the currency pair hovering around 1.2770 during subdued holiday trading conditions, according to UOB Global Economics & Markets Research. Limited market participation kept volatility contained, while the Singapore Dollar continued to show resilience against the USD despite broader caution across Asian foreign exchange markets.

UOB noted that the Singapore Dollar Nominal Effective Exchange Rate (S$NEER) was trading close to the 2% upper bound of its estimated policy band, reinforcing expectations that the Monetary Authority of Singapore will maintain a firm stance on exchange rate management. The positioning of the S$NEER suggests underlying SGD strength even as the US Dollar holds steady against regional peers.

The relatively stable exchange rate reflects a balance between persistent USD demand and Singapore’s supportive currency framework. Analysts said the tight trading pattern in USD/SGD could persist in the near term unless stronger macroeconomic catalysts emerge from the United States or Asia. Broader forex sentiment also remains sensitive to interest rate expectations, global risk appetite, and movements in major currency pairs including EUR/USD and USD/JPY.
