The USD/CAD currency pair edged cautiously around Thursday’s low near 1.3580 during late Asian trading on Friday, reflecting a softer tone for the US Dollar. Market participants noted that the Loonie maintained its weakness, even as the broader USD came under pressure following intervention in the Japanese yen markets. Traders are closely watching technical levels, with the descending 20-day EMA acting as a key support point for potential further downside.  

Recent trading activity highlights a cautious environment, as the USD’s retreat against major currencies like the EUR and JPY has influenced the CAD exchange rate. The intervention in Japan weighed on the greenback, curbing its momentum and prompting a softer tone for USD-linked currency pairs. Analysts suggest that USD/CAD may test lower levels if the current trend persists, with market sentiment increasingly sensitive to global central bank actions.  

Looking ahead, the USD/CAD exchange rate remains under technical scrutiny, with the 20-day EMA serving as a short-term guide for traders. Weakness in the USD against other currencies continues to shape intraday movements, while the Loonie’s performance remains tied to North American economic data and broader market flows. Investors and forex traders are expected to monitor intervention signals and market reactions closely, as these factors may drive further volatility in the pair.
