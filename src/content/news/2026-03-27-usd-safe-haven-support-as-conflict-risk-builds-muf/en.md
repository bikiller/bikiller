The US Dollar is showing resilience despite early weakness following President Trump’s decision to extend the pause on attacks targeting Iran’s energy infrastructure. MUFG’s Derek Halpenny observes that the DXY index dipped only slightly before stabilizing, reflecting continued safe-haven demand. Currency traders are closely watching the USD as geopolitical tensions influence market sentiment, with exchange rates remaining sensitive to news flow from the Middle East.  

The USD’s modest retreat was mirrored in major currency pairs, with EUR/USD hovering near key technical levels. Trading volumes suggest cautious positioning as investors weigh the risk of escalation against ongoing macroeconomic fundamentals. Analysts highlight that even minor shifts in the DXY can ripple across forex markets, impacting emerging market currencies and commodity-linked pairs.  

Market participants are eyeing potential support levels for the USD, which could strengthen further if geopolitical risks intensify. A sustained rise in safe-haven flows might reinforce USD/JPY and USD/CHF, while EUR/USD and GBP/USD could face pressure. Traders are advised to monitor headlines closely, as any sudden escalation could trigger sharp movements across major currency pairs and influence broader risk sentiment.
