The Japanese Yen opened the week with notable strength as the USD/JPY currency pair dipped approximately 0.67%, reflecting improved risk sentiment in global markets. Traders reacted to the White House’s announcement delaying military action against Iran by five days, coupled with reports of “very productive” discussions with Tehran. This positive geopolitical development has supported demand for riskier assets, while the safe-haven yen gained ground against the US dollar.  

Technically, USD/JPY faces a key support level at 159.00, which bears are defending aggressively. A decisive close below 158.00 could signal further downside momentum, attracting short-term trading interest. Market participants are closely watching this level, as a breach may prompt a wave of stop-loss orders, potentially accelerating the decline.  

The broader trading landscape sees the dollar holding steady against other major currencies, while the euro and yen exhibit sensitivity to geopolitical headlines. Currency traders are weighing both fundamental drivers and technical signals, positioning for potential shifts in exchange rates. The interplay between risk appetite and safe-haven flows is likely to continue shaping USD/JPY trading in the near term.
