AUD/USD traded little changed on Friday as hawkish policy signals from both the Federal Reserve (Fed) and the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) offset each other, leaving the currency pair stuck in a narrow range. A modest pullback in the US Dollar (USD) provided limited support but failed to establish a clear directional bias in exchange rate trading.

Market sentiment remained balanced as expectations of prolonged restrictive policy from the Fed continued to underpin USD demand, while the RBA’s similarly hawkish tone helped sustain the Australian Dollar. The clash between the two central banks prevented a breakout, keeping forex trading activity confined and volatility subdued across AUD-related pairs.

For traders, the lack of momentum suggests continued range-bound conditions in AUD/USD unless new macroeconomic catalysts emerge. Attention now turns to upcoming data releases and central bank commentary that could shift expectations. Until then, the currency pair is likely to remain sensitive to USD fluctuations, with exchange rate movements driven mainly by interest rate outlook differentials.
