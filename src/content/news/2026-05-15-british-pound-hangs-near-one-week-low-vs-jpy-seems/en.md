The GBP/JPY currency pair edged lower for a second consecutive day on Friday, slipping beneath the 212.00 level during the Asian session. This marks a one-and-a-half-week low, as traders weigh the pound’s performance against the yen amid ongoing market uncertainty. The pair’s decline also continues a broader three-day negative trend, highlighting pressure near the 100-day simple moving average.  

Market participants cite cautious sentiment in Asian trading hours, with the pound appearing vulnerable if it fails to reclaim key technical support. Analysts note that the exchange rate may face further downward momentum if it closes consistently below 212.00, potentially prompting short-term trading adjustments. The yen’s relative strength, coupled with ongoing USD and EUR fluctuations, adds another layer of complexity to the cross’s movements.  

Traders are closely monitoring this technical juncture, as a sustained break below the 100-day SMA could attract additional selling interest. With the GBP/JPY hovering near critical support, volatility is expected to remain elevated, influencing risk management strategies across forex markets. The currency pair’s trajectory in the coming sessions will likely hinge on broader USD and euro zone developments, as well as shifts in market sentiment.
