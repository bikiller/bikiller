The USD/KRW currency pair saw two-way trading amid rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, according to OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong. The Korean Won came under pressure, reflecting its higher-beta profile and status as a net oil importer, while the US Dollar strengthened alongside gains in crude oil. Investors weighed safe-haven demand for USD against regional risk factors affecting the won.  

Trading activity indicated that USD/KRW was reactive to both oil price movements and geopolitical headlines, with market participants eyeing potential volatility. The pair’s exchange rate fluctuated as speculative flows and hedging activities influenced short-term dynamics. Analysts suggest that heightened global risk sentiment could continue to drive USD strength versus the won, while broader EM currencies may see mixed performance depending on energy price trends.  

Looking ahead, the trajectory of USD/KRW could hinge on developments in Middle Eastern tensions and oil markets. Traders are advised to monitor technical levels and intraday ranges, as sudden shifts in risk appetite could trigger sharp moves. With the US Dollar maintaining a firm tone against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, the Korean Won may remain vulnerable, reinforcing the importance of cautious trading strategies in the near term.
