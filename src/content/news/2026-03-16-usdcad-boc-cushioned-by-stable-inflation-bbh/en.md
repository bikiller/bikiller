The USD/CAD currency pair edged lower, slipping back below the 1.3700 mark as traders positioned ahead of Canada’s February CPI data. Analysts at Brown Brothers Harriman, led by Elias Haddad, noted that the market appears to be weighing the likely impact of stable inflation on the Bank of Canada’s policy outlook. Headline inflation is expected to soften due to base effects, while core inflation remains near the mid-2% range.  

Trading activity in USD/CAD reflected cautious sentiment, with investors factoring in the central bank’s potential response to steady price pressures. A softer CPI print could reduce expectations for further rate hikes, putting downward pressure on the Canadian dollar against the USD. Meanwhile, broader forex markets, including EUR/USD, showed muted volatility as market participants awaited fresh economic cues.  

Traders continue to monitor the exchange rate closely, as any surprises in inflation data could trigger sharper movements in USD/CAD. Technical levels around 1.3700 serve as key support, with a breach likely influencing short-term trading strategies. The outlook suggests that market participants will remain sensitive to core inflation trends and the Bank of Canada’s commentary, with ripple effects across major currency pairs.
