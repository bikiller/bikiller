ABN AMRO research highlights that EUR/USD is being driven by a mix of growth convergence between the Eurozone and the US alongside ongoing repricing of Federal Reserve policy expectations. The analysis suggests the currency pair is entering a phase where macro data differences and central bank outlooks increasingly shape trading direction, with investors reassessing USD strength versus EUR resilience in the exchange rate outlook.

Shifts in Federal Reserve policy expectations continue to influence USD dynamics, as markets adjust pricing for the timing and scale of potential rate changes. This repricing process is contributing to heightened volatility in EUR/USD trading, with the US dollar reacting to incoming data and forward guidance while investors reassess interest rate differentials that underpin currency pair movements across major FX markets.

At the same time, improving Eurozone fundamentals and signs of growth convergence are providing underlying support for the euro, helping to stabilise sentiment around EUR/USD. For traders, this creates a more balanced exchange rate environment, where shifts in macroeconomic data releases and central bank communication from both sides of the Atlantic are likely to remain key drivers of short-term trading decisions.
