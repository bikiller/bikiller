United Overseas Bank (UOB) analysts Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann reported sharp intraday swings in USD/JPY, with the currency pair spiking to 161.92 before sliding to 161.06 and eventually closing at 161.54. The exchange rate action highlights elevated volatility in recent trading sessions as the US dollar maintained firm positioning against the Japanese yen.

According to the bank, the broader outlook suggests the Japanese yen retains scope to revisit its 2024 low against the US dollar, reflecting continued weakness in the currency pair. The USD/JPY exchange rate remains supported by dollar strength, while yen sentiment stays fragile amid ongoing trading pressure and limited recovery signals in spot markets.

For forex trading participants, the USD/JPY pair continues to hover near elevated levels, with the exchange rate closely watched around the 161 handle. Market participants remain alert to further directional moves, as sustained US dollar demand could keep pressure on the Japanese yen and reinforce the prevailing bullish USD sentiment in currency markets.
