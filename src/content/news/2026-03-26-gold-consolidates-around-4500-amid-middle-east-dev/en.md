Gold (XAU/USD) steadied below the key $4,500 level during Thursday’s Asian trading session, showing resilience despite lingering downside pressure. The precious metal paused its pullback after failing to sustain gains above the 100-day Simple Moving Average (SMA), a technical barrier that prompted selling interest. Price action reflects cautious positioning as traders assess broader currency pair dynamics and near-term exchange rate trends.

The upside in gold remains constrained by a firmer US Dollar (USD), which continues to draw support from safe-haven demand and shifting expectations around global risk sentiment. As the USD strengthens across major currency pairs such as EUR/USD, it reduces the appeal of non-yielding assets like gold. This inverse relationship is keeping bullion capped despite underlying geopolitical uncertainty.

Market participants are closely monitoring developments in the Middle East, which are providing some support to gold prices but not enough to trigger a sustained breakout. In the near term, trading is likely to remain range-bound, with the $4,500 psychological level acting as resistance while the 100-day SMA defines technical direction. Currency movements and USD strength will remain key drivers.
