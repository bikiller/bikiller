USD/SGD has slipped in recent trading, tracking broader US dollar weakness as risk sentiment tentatively stabilised across financial markets, according to OCBC’s Christopher Wong. The currency pair’s exchange rate reflects a modest pullback in USD strength, with trading activity showing softer demand for the greenback against Asian currencies amid improving risk appetite.

OCBC notes that bullish momentum on the daily chart has faded, with the Relative Strength Index (RSI) slipping lower, signalling weakening upside pressure for USD/SGD. Technical indicators suggest that the prior uptrend in the forex currency pair is losing traction, shifting short-term trading bias toward a more cautious stance in line with softer USD trend.

Overall, USD/SGD exchange rate outlook points to upside risks fading and a gradual tilt toward downside potential, as stabilising risk sentiment and weaker USD trading conditions weigh on the pair. In forex markets, further declines may emerge if RSI weakness persists, keeping traders cautious on near-term positioning, according to OCBC analysis.
