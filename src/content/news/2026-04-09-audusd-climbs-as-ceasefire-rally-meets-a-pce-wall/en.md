AUD/USD surged by 0.56% on Thursday, marking a four-session winning streak as a ceasefire rally continued to support the Australian Dollar. The rally was fueled by risk-on sentiment, with investors responding positively to peace developments. The currency pair reached a high of 0.6710, benefiting from increased demand for riskier assets and a weaker USD.

Despite the positive momentum, AUD/USD faced resistance at the PCE data release, which pressured the pair’s gains. The latest inflation report in the US showed stronger-than-expected numbers, boosting the USD and capping further upside for the Aussie. As the exchange rate hovered around 0.6700, traders remained cautious, awaiting additional market catalysts to drive the next move.
