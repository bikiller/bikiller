NZD/USD extended its decline for the fourth consecutive day, trading at 0.5820, marking a 0.58% drop on Friday. The currency pair remains under pressure as the US Dollar gains strength, bolstered by rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and heightened inflation concerns. 

The ongoing conflict in the region has driven demand for the safe-haven USD, pushing the NZD lower. This renewed volatility in global markets has overshadowed the New Zealand Dollar, which has struggled to maintain its footing against the greenback. As market sentiment remains sensitive to these developments, the NZD/USD exchange rate could face further downside pressure in the short term.
