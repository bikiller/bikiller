The Pound Sterling remained firm against the US Dollar on Friday, with the GBP/USD currency pair holding near 1.3450 during Asian trading hours after posting modest gains in the previous session. The exchange rate was supported by a softer demand for traditional safe-haven assets, as improving market sentiment reduced the appeal of the USD and encouraged investors to seek higher-yielding currencies.

Market participants continued to monitor broader risk conditions, which helped underpin Sterling and kept the GBP/USD pair on stable footing. The decline in safe-haven flows weighed on the Greenback, allowing the British currency to maintain its recent upward momentum. Currency trading activity also reflected a more balanced risk environment, with traders showing greater confidence in risk-sensitive assets across major markets.

The latest price action suggests that the Pound may remain supported in the near term if demand for the USD continues to ease. Investors are also watching developments in other major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, for additional clues on market direction. While GBP/USD remains around 1.3450, sustained weakness in safe-haven demand could provide further support for the pair and reinforce the positive tone seen in recent trading sessions.
