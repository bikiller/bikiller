West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures on NYMEX recovered sharply in European trading on Wednesday, clawing back roughly half of their early losses to hover near $96.00 per barrel. The rebound comes despite oil prices still trading 1.5% lower from the previous session’s highs, as lingering concerns over supply disruptions continue to support crude amid mixed demand signals from major economies. Traders are eyeing whether WTI can sustain above key technical levels.  

The USD has remained a focal point alongside oil, with the greenback showing modest strength against the euro and other major currencies. This dynamic has influenced commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian dollar and Norwegian krone, which are closely tied to energy prices. Market participants are monitoring exchange rate movements, as shifts in the USD/EUR pair can amplify volatility in oil-linked forex trading.  

Analysts suggest that persistent geopolitical tensions and production constraints are likely to keep WTI volatile near the $95–$97 range. Forex traders are adjusting positions in USD, EUR, and commodity currencies to hedge against price swings, while watching technical indicators for potential breakout or retracement levels. The interplay between crude prices and currency pairs underscores the growing interconnection of energy markets and forex trading strategies.
