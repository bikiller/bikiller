Gold prices are heading for a weekly loss, down by around 0.70% on Friday. As the U.S. Dollar (USD) continues to rise, the precious metal is poised to finish the week with a decline of more than 2%. This weakness is largely attributed to the Dollar's dominance in the market, driven by heightened investor concerns over inflation and the ongoing Middle East conflict, which is amplifying demand for safe-haven assets.

The DXY, a key gauge of the USD's performance, has surged above the 100.00 level, supporting the Greenback's strength across major currency pairs. As a result, gold's appeal as a safe-haven investment has waned, with traders shifting focus toward the USD and away from commodities like gold. The broader market sentiment remains cautious, with the possibility of further inflationary pressures weighing heavily on risk sentiment, creating headwinds for gold prices in the short term.
