The USD/CAD currency pair dipped roughly 0.4% on Monday, reaching session lows near 1.3790 as traders responded to a combination of a softer US Dollar and a strengthening Canadian Loonie. The exchange rate's decline below the 1.3800 threshold highlighted increased selling interest in the greenback amid broad risk-on sentiment and favorable commodity dynamics.  

Crude Oil prices surged sharply, providing a tailwind for the Canadian Dollar given Canada’s status as a major oil exporter. Traders noted that the uptick in oil added pressure on the USD/CAD pair, reinforcing technical support levels around 1.3790. Market participants closely watched energy-driven flows, which continue to exert influence on North American currency trading.  

Analysts suggest that the Loonie’s recent strength could persist if US Dollar softness continues alongside commodity gains. The USD’s retreat against other major currencies, including the EUR and JPY, adds further context to the move. For traders, key levels near 1.3800 now act as short-term resistance, with potential implications for positioning in both spot and derivative markets in the coming sessions.
