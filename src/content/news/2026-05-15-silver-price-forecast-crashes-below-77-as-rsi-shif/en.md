Silver prices plunged sharply on Friday, with the XAG/USD currency pair dropping 7.90% to trade at $76.88 after peaking at $83.87. The decline was fueled by a surge in US Treasury yields, which heightened investor concerns over a potential second wave of inflation. Traders are reacting to the possibility that central banks may respond with tighter monetary policies, influencing both USD and global forex markets.

Technical indicators show the relative strength index (RSI) turning bearish, suggesting further downside pressure. Market watchers are now eyeing the $75 support level as a potential target, with short-term trading likely to remain volatile. The sudden drop underscores how sensitive precious metals are to shifts in US interest rates and broader economic expectations.

Currency traders are keeping close tabs on related pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, as volatility in silver often coincides with wider shifts in USD demand. The interplay between rising yields, inflation expectations, and commodity trading is creating a dynamic environment, pushing investors to reassess risk positions across metals, forex, and equities alike. Momentum indicators and exchange rate movements suggest cautious positioning in the near term.
