The Canadian Dollar (CAD) regained some ground against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday as the USD/CAD currency pair saw earlier gains reversed. Traders reacted to a softer Greenback while factoring in weaker Crude Oil prices, which often support CAD strength. Technical indicators suggest the pair remains in an uptrend, although momentum may be slowing as the Relative Strength Index (RSI) approaches overbought territory.

In early trading, USD/CAD hovered near key resistance levels, reflecting cautious buying in the USD amid broader market uncertainty. The pair’s movements highlight the interplay between commodity prices and exchange rates, with oil-sensitive currencies like CAD closely tracking energy market trends. Analysts note that sustained USD weakness could keep the pair elevated, even as CAD gains modest support from commodity-driven inflows.

Looking ahead, traders are monitoring whether USD/CAD can break past current resistance or retrace toward support around recent lows. The technical outlook suggests potential consolidation before a decisive move, with RSI levels hinting at limited upside momentum. Forex participants continue to weigh US economic signals, crude market shifts, and global risk sentiment to gauge the pair’s next directional push.
