Silver (XAG/USD) traded virtually unchanged on Tuesday, hovering near $70.00 as the precious metal held above its 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA). The currency pair showed limited volatility, with traders in the forex market consolidating positions after recent swings. The rebound above this key technical level has helped stabilize short-term trading sentiment, suggesting a pause in momentum as participants reassess USD-driven direction.

Market participants are focused on the Federal Reserve’s upcoming monetary policy decision, keeping USD trading cautious across major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Expectations around interest rates have tempered aggressive positioning, leaving silver and other dollar-denominated assets in a narrow range as investors await clearer guidance from the Fed. This wait-and-see approach has reduced intraday volatility in broader forex trading.

The outlook for XAG/USD hinges on whether the metal can sustain support above the 200-day SMA. A dovish surprise could weaken the USD exchange rate and lift silver, while a hawkish stance may trigger renewed selling pressure. Volatility in forex trading is expected to rise following the announcement, shaping the next directional move for the currency pair.
