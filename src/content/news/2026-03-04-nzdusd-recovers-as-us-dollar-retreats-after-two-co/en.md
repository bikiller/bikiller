NZD/USD saw a notable rebound on Wednesday, rising by 0.45% to trade around 0.5920. The currency pair’s recovery comes as the US Dollar retreats after two consecutive days of solid gains. This shift marks a reversal in momentum, with the USD losing ground across the board following its recent strength.

The pullback in the USD has provided the New Zealand Dollar (NZD) an opportunity to regain some lost ground. Traders are closely watching this price action, with the 0.5900 level now a key technical support point for NZD/USD. A continued decline in USD strength could see the exchange rate test higher levels in the coming days, especially with upcoming economic data releases likely to influence market sentiment.
