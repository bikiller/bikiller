NZD/USD trades around 0.5640 as the currency pair extends losses for a seventh consecutive trading session, hovering near its weakest level since November 2025. The exchange rate remains under sustained pressure as a broadly stronger US Dollar dominates global forex markets, limiting any recovery attempts for the New Zealand Dollar.  

Drivers stem from a resilient US Dollar and a broader risk-off tone across financial markets, as investors scale back appetite for risk-sensitive currencies. Attention now turns to upcoming US PCE inflation data, a key gauge for Federal Reserve policy expectations, which continues to shape USD strength and forex trading sentiment.  

With NZD/USD holding near its November 2025 lows, downside pressure remains intact as traders monitor whether the 0.5600 handle could come into focus. Market participants stay cautious, with ongoing USD dominance limiting recovery in the currency pair, while short-term trading sentiment reflects heightened sensitivity to US macroeconomic releases and data-driven volatility ahead of the PCE release.
