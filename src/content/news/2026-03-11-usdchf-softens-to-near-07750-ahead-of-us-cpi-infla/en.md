The USD/CHF currency pair is weakening, dropping to around 0.7765 during early European trading on Wednesday. The ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East have pushed investors towards safe-haven assets, with the Swiss Franc (CHF) benefitting from this flight to safety. As risk sentiment shifts, the US Dollar (USD) faces pressure, resulting in a subdued exchange rate.

This market movement comes ahead of the highly anticipated US CPI inflation data release, which could further influence the USD/CHF exchange rate. A higher-than-expected inflation print may offer support to the USD, while a weaker reading could allow the CHF to maintain its recent strength. As traders await the release, the focus remains on potential shifts in inflation dynamics and their impact on US monetary policy.
