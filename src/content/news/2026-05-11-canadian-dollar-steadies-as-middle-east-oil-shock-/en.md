The Canadian Dollar held steady against the USD on Monday as rising oil prices linked to escalating Middle East tensions limited broader US Dollar gains. The USD/CAD currency pair traded near 1.3670, edging down around 0.05% during the session as energy markets supported the commodity-linked CAD. Crude oil’s sharp rebound helped stabilize the Canadian exchange rate, even as cautious market sentiment kept demand for the US Dollar relatively firm.

Currency trading remained subdued with investors balancing geopolitical risks against expectations for US monetary policy. The Greenback initially found support from safe-haven flows, but higher oil prices capped the USD rebound against the Canadian Dollar. Market participants also monitored broader forex movements across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, as traders assessed whether inflationary pressure from energy markets could influence future central bank decisions.

From a technical perspective, USD/CAD continued to hover within a narrow range around 1.3670, reflecting indecision in near-term trading conditions. Analysts note that sustained strength in crude oil prices could provide additional support for the Canadian Dollar, while renewed demand for safe-haven assets may keep the USD resilient. The balance between geopolitical uncertainty and commodity market dynamics is likely to remain a key driver for the currency pair in the sessions ahead.
