Gold (XAU/USD) recorded a modest gain of 0.30% on Monday, trading at $4,726. The uptick in price follows heightened tensions in the Iran-US conflict, as a proposal from Tehran was rejected outright by US President Donald Trump, who described it as “totally unacceptable.” The ongoing geopolitical uncertainty is driving investors toward safe-haven assets, reflecting a growing demand for gold amid rising war risks.

As the market reacts to these developments, the exchange rate for gold remains sensitive to further political changes. With the dollar (USD) showing fluctuations against other currencies like the euro (EUR), traders are closely monitoring the situation for potential impacts on the broader forex market. The combination of stalled negotiations and increasing tensions suggests that gold may continue to attract attention as a hedge against volatility in currency markets.
