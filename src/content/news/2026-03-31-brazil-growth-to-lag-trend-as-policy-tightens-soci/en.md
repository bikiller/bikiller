Brazil's economic growth is expected to fall below trend in 2026, with tighter policy and a challenging external environment limiting activity, according to Societe Generale’s Dev Ashish. The central bank's tighter stance is likely to suppress domestic demand, while higher oil prices are expected to pressure inflation. Despite this, soft demand may cushion the impact, preventing runaway price increases.

The implications for the Brazilian Real (BRL) are clear, as weak growth and inflationary pressures could push the USD/BRL exchange rate higher. Traders should keep an eye on external factors, such as oil price movements and global economic conditions, which will play a key role in shaping Brazil's economic outlook. The EUR/BRL pair may also feel the effects of the soft demand and external headwinds.
