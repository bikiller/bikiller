The forex market is experiencing notable fluctuations, primarily driven by developments regarding Iran, according to OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong. Recent headlines have significantly influenced oil prices and bond yields, which in turn impact the USD's exchange rate. The ongoing situation has led to increased volatility in currency pairs, particularly against the EUR, as traders react to the evolving context.

As the market adjusts to these geopolitical dynamics, analysts suggest that any depreciation of the USD may be shallow. The focus remains on how ceasefire negotiations will unfold, with traders closely monitoring these developments for further cues. Current trading patterns reflect heightened sensitivity to news from Iran, indicating that currency exchange rates could continue to shift as the situation evolves.
