Gold prices surged over 1% on Monday, driven by heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. As the conflict escalated, a joint US-Israel strike on Iran prompted investors to seek safe-haven assets, propelling the XAU/USD pair to trade at $5,341. Earlier, the precious metal had reached a one-month peak of $5,419, reflecting growing market uncertainty.

The rally in gold also came amid an oil price spike, further fueling inflation fears and risk aversion in the financial markets. With investors focusing on geopolitical risks, gold became a preferred store of value. The ongoing tensions have left traders closely monitoring developments in the region, which may continue to impact the broader forex market, including the USD and EUR pairs.
