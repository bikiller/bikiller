The USD/JPY currency pair steadied near the 157.00 level on Monday, finding support after dipping to around 156.60 during the Asian trading session. Modest buying interest emerged at lower levels, helping the exchange rate recover as traders assessed competing market forces. The pair’s resilience highlights continued demand for the USD despite underlying volatility in global markets.

Geopolitical tensions linked to Iran provided some support for the Japanese yen, traditionally viewed as a safe-haven currency. However, this defensive demand was offset by lingering speculation of potential intervention by Japanese authorities, which has kept traders cautious. The balance between risk sentiment and policy expectations has limited sharper downside movement in USD/JPY.

From a trading perspective, the currency pair appears to be consolidating within a narrow range, with 156.60 acting as near-term support and 157.00 serving as a psychological resistance level. Market participants are closely monitoring developments in both geopolitical risk and central bank signals, as these factors are likely to influence near-term direction in the USD/JPY exchange rate and broader forex market sentiment.
