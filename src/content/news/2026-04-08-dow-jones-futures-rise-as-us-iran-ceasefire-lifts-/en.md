Dow Jones futures climbed 2.32% to hover near 47,900 during European trading hours on Wednesday, reflecting a surge in market sentiment following reports of a US-Iran ceasefire. Investors showed renewed appetite for risk, with S&P 500 futures up 2.49% near 6,820 and Nasdaq 100 futures rising 3.19% to around 25,150. Equity markets opened higher, boosting the USD across major currency pairs.  

In forex trading, the dollar gained modestly against the EUR and JPY as investors balanced equities optimism with safe-haven demand. The EUR/USD exchange rate edged lower amid the rally, while USD/JPY saw increased trading volumes near technical resistance levels. Market participants are watching if this momentum sustains into the US session, as geopolitical easing often triggers short-term volatility across both equities and FX markets.  

Analysts note that the jump in futures could signal broader appetite for risk assets, potentially influencing commodity-linked currencies like AUD and NZD. Traders are closely monitoring exchange rate fluctuations and technical patterns for entry points, while liquidity conditions may amplify USD moves. With the ceasefire easing geopolitical fears, the interplay between equity indices and major currency pairs could set the tone for trading in the days ahead.
