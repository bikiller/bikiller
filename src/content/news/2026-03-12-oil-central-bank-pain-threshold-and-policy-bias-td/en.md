TD Securities' James Rossiter highlights a shift in central bank policies, with major institutions now prioritizing inflation and expectations over growth, following recent supply shocks. This change in strategy comes as inflation concerns rise, especially with oil prices influencing the global economy. Trading in currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY has been affected by this policy adjustment, leading to market volatility as investors recalibrate their expectations on central bank responses.

As oil prices continue to climb, central banks are adjusting their monetary policies to focus on inflation control, which may impact exchange rates in the coming months. The USD has strengthened against the EUR, pushing the EUR/USD below key technical levels, while commodity-linked currencies like CAD are also seeing shifts. The new emphasis on inflation management signals further challenges for forex traders, as interest rate expectations become more uncertain.
