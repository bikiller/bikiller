The USD/JPY currency pair held firm around the 158.90 level on April 9, with the exchange rate supported by strong US economic data. The latest Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) report reinforced expectations that the Federal Reserve will maintain a higher-for-longer policy stance, underpinning USD demand. In forex trading, this backdrop continues to favor the dollar against lower-yielding currencies like the Japanese yen.

Resilient US data has strengthened market confidence in the Fed’s cautious approach to rate cuts, keeping Treasury yields elevated and boosting the USD. The USD/JPY pair remains sensitive to policy divergence, as the Bank of Japan maintains accommodative settings. This contrast sustains upward pressure on the pair, with traders closely monitoring any signals of intervention near current levels.

Looking ahead, the currency pair’s trajectory will depend on incoming US inflation and labor data, alongside broader risk sentiment. If the higher-for-longer narrative persists, USD/JPY could continue testing upside levels beyond 158.90. However, volatility may increase should Japanese authorities step in to stabilize the yen, adding uncertainty to near-term trading conditions.
