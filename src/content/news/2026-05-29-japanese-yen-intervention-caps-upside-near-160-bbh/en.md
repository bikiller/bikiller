USD/JPY remained directionless above 159.00, with the Japanese Yen showing limited gains after Japanese authorities stepped into the foreign exchange market. According to Brown Brothers Harriman, Japan’s Ministry of Finance carried out record-sized FX intervention to prevent the currency pair from breaking sustainably above the 160.00 level, a threshold that has become a key focus for traders.

The intervention helped stabilize the exchange rate and reinforced expectations that Japanese officials are prepared to act again if excessive volatility returns. While the move temporarily curbed USD strength against the Yen, broader market drivers continue to favor the US currency. Interest rate differentials and resilient US economic data remain supportive of USD demand, limiting the scope for a deeper decline in USD/JPY.

For now, the 160.00 area appears to be a significant technical and psychological resistance level for the currency pair. Traders across forex markets are closely monitoring whether renewed intervention risks can offset underlying bullish momentum in the USD. Developments in other major currencies, including the EUR, could also influence broader trading sentiment, but official action from Tokyo is likely to keep USD/JPY movements under heightened scrutiny in the near term.
