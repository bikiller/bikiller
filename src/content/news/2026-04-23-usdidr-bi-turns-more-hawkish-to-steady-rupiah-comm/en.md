Bank Indonesia (BI) held its benchmark BI Rate at 4.75% for a seventh consecutive meeting, according to analysts at Commerzbank led by Charlie Lay. However, the central bank adopted a more hawkish policy stance, signalling increased focus on defending the Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) amid global currency volatility and shifting USD exchange rate dynamics in forex trading.

Analysts say the unchanged policy rate suggests stability, but the hawkish tilt could provide underlying support for the IDR in the USD/IDR currency pair. In forex markets, traders are watching whether Bank Indonesia’s stance will curb rupiah weakness against the US dollar, especially as broader exchange rate pressures and USD strength continue to influence emerging market assets.

Looking ahead, sustained hawkish rhetoric from BI could help anchor the rupiah and limit volatility in USD/IDR trading sessions. However, global risk sentiment and potential US Federal Reserve policy shifts remain key drivers for the currency pair. Investors will also compare regional currencies, including EUR crosses, for relative yield and positioning opportunities in forex markets.
