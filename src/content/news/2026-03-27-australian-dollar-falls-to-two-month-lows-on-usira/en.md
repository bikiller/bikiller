The Australian Dollar weakened to two-month lows as AUD/USD extended its losing streak for a fourth straight session, trading near 0.6880 during Friday’s Asian session. The currency pair remains under pressure amid persistent demand for the USD, with investors gravitating toward safer assets as geopolitical uncertainty surrounding US–Iran peace prospects continues to weigh on risk sentiment.

The deterioration in market confidence has driven a stronger Greenback, pushing the exchange rate lower despite relatively stable domestic data from Australia. Traders are closely monitoring technical levels, with 0.6850 emerging as near-term support, while resistance is seen around 0.6920. The bearish momentum reflects broader caution across forex markets, where risk-sensitive currencies are underperforming.

Looking ahead, continued volatility in geopolitical developments is likely to dictate short-term trading direction for AUD/USD and other major currency pairs. A sustained USD rally could deepen losses for the Australian Dollar, while any signs of easing tensions may trigger a corrective rebound. Market participants remain attentive to shifts in sentiment as global uncertainties shape exchange rate dynamics.
