The USD saw limited movement as geopolitical concerns from Iran and comments from former President Trump took center stage, creating uncertainty in the markets. US Treasury yields flattened, indicating a pause in investor confidence, while economic data proved more resilient. Strong ISM Manufacturing and Retail Sales figures surprised the market, indicating economic strength despite global tensions. This provided some support for the USD, particularly against the EUR.

TD Securities analysts predict a weaker-than-expected US payroll print, forecasting only 30k jobs added compared to consensus expectations. Despite this, they expect the unemployment rate to remain unchanged, providing little new direction for the USD. As a result, currency pairs like EUR/USD saw muted reactions, as the market digests mixed economic data and ongoing geopolitical developments. The exchange rate of the USD is likely to remain sensitive to upcoming reports.
