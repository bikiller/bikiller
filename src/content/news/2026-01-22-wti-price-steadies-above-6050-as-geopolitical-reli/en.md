West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices have stabilized above the $60.50 mark, currently trading around $60.60 during early European hours on Thursday. This steady performance comes as easing geopolitical tensions, particularly concerning Greenland, help to mitigate concerns regarding oversupply in the market. 

As traders navigate the fluctuating landscape, the stability in WTI prices may influence currency pairs, particularly those involving the USD and EUR. With WTI maintaining these levels, market participants will be keenly observing the implications for inflation and broader economic indicators, as oil prices often serve as a barometer for overall market health.
