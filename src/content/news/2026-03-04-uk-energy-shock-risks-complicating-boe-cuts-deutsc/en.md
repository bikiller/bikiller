Renewed energy price shocks are raising concerns about the UK inflation outlook, according to Deutsche Bank analysts Sanjay Raja and Shreyas Gopal. With oil and gas prices surging, approximately 50% of the UK Consumer Price Index (CPI) basket is now heavily influenced by energy costs, particularly in the services sector. This shift could complicate the Bank of England's (BoE) plans for interest rate cuts and impact exchange rates.

As traders navigate these developments, the GBP/USD currency pair remains sensitive to fluctuations in energy prices. The outlook for the British pound is further complicated by the rising inflationary pressures, potentially leading to a more cautious approach from the BoE. Market participants will be closely monitoring these dynamics, as any significant shifts could influence trading strategies and the broader forex market, particularly against the euro (EUR) and the US dollar (USD).
