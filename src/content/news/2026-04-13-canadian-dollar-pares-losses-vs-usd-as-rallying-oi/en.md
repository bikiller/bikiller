The Canadian dollar pared earlier losses against the US dollar on Monday as surging oil prices helped offset expectations of further Fed tightening. The USD/CAD currency pair pulled back slightly from its Asian session peak, finding support near the 1.3860-1.3855 range. Traders noted that the exchange rate remains up roughly 0.15% for the day, reflecting continued volatility amid mixed economic signals.  

Market participants highlighted the impact of rising crude prices on the Canadian dollar, providing temporary relief against the backdrop of hawkish sentiment from the Federal Reserve. While the USD retains strength against other major currencies like the EUR, the rebound in CAD shows how commodity-linked currencies can respond to global energy dynamics. Trading activity in the pair suggests short-term corrections could continue if oil maintains its momentum.  

Technical observers pointed to the 1.3860-1.3855 area as a key intraday support level, with resistance near the Asian session highs. The exchange rate’s modest retracement indicates that while USD strength remains intact, the CAD’s sensitivity to commodities could influence near-term trading patterns. Investors will be watching whether oil-driven gains can sustain the loonie or if broader US monetary expectations will resume dominance in shaping the currency pair’s trajectory.
