The British Pound extended its advance against the USD on Monday, with the GBP/USD currency pair approaching the key 1.3500 exchange rate level as improving sentiment surrounding a potential US-Iran agreement reduced demand for safe-haven assets. The softer US Dollar reflected broader optimism across financial markets, with investors shifting toward risk-sensitive currencies as geopolitical tensions appeared to ease.

In forex trading, the Greenback remained under pressure as expectations of diplomatic progress between Washington and Tehran encouraged traders to reduce defensive USD positions. The Pound also benefited from steady market confidence in the UK economy, allowing GBP/USD to maintain bullish momentum during the European session. Analysts noted that sustained trading above near-term support levels could reinforce upward pressure on the pair.

Meanwhile, broader currency markets showed mixed movement, with EUR/USD also holding firm as declining demand for the Dollar supported major European currencies. Traders are now closely watching upcoming economic data and geopolitical developments for further direction. A confirmed break above the 1.3500 technical resistance zone could open the door for additional gains in GBP/USD, while renewed uncertainty may trigger corrective USD buying.
