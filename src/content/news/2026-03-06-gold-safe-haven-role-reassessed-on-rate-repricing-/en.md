Gold is on track for its first weekly loss in five weeks as market expectations shift. BNY’s Bob Savage highlights the impact of rate hikes in Europe and fewer anticipated cuts from the FOMC. This shift, alongside a stronger USD, has pressured gold prices. The dollar’s rise makes gold less attractive as a safe haven, while traders also use it as a liquidity source, further weighing on its value.

Gold’s decline is evident across the markets, with the metal facing significant resistance. The EUR/USD exchange rate has come under pressure, reflecting broader market adjustments as traders recalibrate their positions in anticipation of tightening policies. The shift in rate expectations is shifting market sentiment, and gold’s role as a hedge against inflation is being reassessed. As these dynamics unfold, the metal is set to face more volatility in the short term.
