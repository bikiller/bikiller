The Japanese Yen has shown resilience in recent trading, holding firm against both the US Dollar and the Euro despite the ongoing energy price shock. MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst Lee Hardman notes that market participants are increasingly pricing in a Bank of Japan rate hike, potentially as soon as April, which has provided support for the JPY amid global volatility. The USD/JPY exchange rate has seen limited upside, reflecting cautious bullishness for the yen.  

Technical levels are also influencing trading flows, with the yen maintaining key support zones that have underpinned short-term strength. Traders are closely watching BoJ communications for signals on the timing and magnitude of potential policy tightening, while the EUR/JPY pair has mirrored the yen’s steadiness, showing muted swings despite broader risk-off sentiment in equity markets. Market liquidity remains moderate, with JPY trading volumes reflecting cautious positioning ahead of upcoming economic data releases.  

Looking ahead, the yen’s trajectory will likely remain sensitive to both domestic monetary policy cues and international energy price trends. A confirmed BoJ rate increase could trigger further appreciation against major currencies, while persistent volatility in energy markets may weigh on investor sentiment. Currency traders are weighing these dynamics carefully, balancing near-term technical levels with fundamental drivers, as the yen navigates a complex mix of inflation, policy expectations, and global market risk.
