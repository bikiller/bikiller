The Euro (EUR) edged lower against the British Pound (GBP) on Friday, with the EUR/GBP currency pair trading around 0.8642. The pullback came after Eurozone GDP data was revised downward, signaling weaker-than-expected economic growth across the region. Traders reacted cautiously, trimming long positions in EUR while seeking refuge in the relative stability of the GBP amid heightened uncertainty over the Eurozone outlook.  

Market participants noted that the revised growth figures have renewed concerns over inflation pressures and potential monetary policy shifts by the European Central Bank. The EUR/GBP exchange rate’s retreat follows two days of gains, suggesting that recent bullish momentum may be faltering. Analysts highlighted that technical levels around 0.8650 could act as near-term resistance, while support may be tested closer to 0.8620 if selling intensifies.  

The broader forex market reflected similar caution, with the USD showing mixed strength against major currencies. Investors are weighing Eurozone economic fragility against stronger UK data, influencing trading flows in the EUR, GBP, and other key currency pairs. This environment underscores how macroeconomic revisions can quickly reshape exchange rate dynamics, prompting traders to reassess positioning in both spot and derivative markets.
