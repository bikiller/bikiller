European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member Gediminas Simkus, also head of Lithuania’s central bank, urged markets to remain calm ahead of the ECB’s next policy meeting, stressing that investors should avoid overreacting to geopolitical tensions stemming from the Middle East conflict. He noted that while the situation could influence risk sentiment, the bank’s monetary policy trajectory remains guided by inflation dynamics rather than short-term shocks.  

The euro edged lower against the dollar, with the EUR/USD currency pair hovering near 1.0735 amid cautious trading. Analysts pointed to increased volatility as traders digested both geopolitical developments and recent ECB signals, with the exchange rate testing short-term support around 1.0720. Market participants are closely monitoring liquidity flows, with USD strength benefiting from safe-haven demand, while EUR sentiment remains sensitive to ECB communications.  

Simkus highlighted that maintaining a measured approach is essential to prevent exaggerated market swings. Traders are advised to watch for technical levels, including 1.0750 resistance and 1.0700 support, as these could guide short-term trading decisions. The euro’s movement reflects a balancing act between fundamental pressures, policy expectations, and global risk factors, suggesting that currency pair dynamics may remain choppy until clearer guidance emerges from the ECB.
