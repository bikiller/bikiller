The NZD/USD currency pair opened the Asian session on Monday in the red, hovering near 0.5950. Despite China's hotter-than-expected CPI inflation reading, the New Zealand Dollar continues to face downward pressure against the US Dollar. Traders cite ongoing USD strength and cautious risk sentiment as key factors keeping the exchange rate under pressure, while technical support levels around 0.5950 are being closely monitored.

Market participants are assessing the implications of China’s inflation data, which typically influences commodity-linked currencies like the NZD. However, the NZD’s weakness suggests that investors are favoring USD liquidity, limiting any upside reaction from the Asian CPI report. The pair’s intraday trading shows subdued momentum, with volatility remaining contained amid mixed regional economic signals.

Looking ahead, the focus shifts to the US Existing Home Sales data for April, which could spark further movements in the NZD/USD and broader forex markets. Any stronger-than-expected figures may reinforce the US Dollar rally, potentially pushing the currency pair below key technical thresholds. Traders are also keeping an eye on cross-currency flows, including EUR/USD and AUD/USD, for broader risk sentiment cues that may impact New Zealand Dollar trading.
