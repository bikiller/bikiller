Chinese yuan weakness extended in forex trading after a breakout against the US Dollar, with USD/CNH surging to 6.8195, according to United Overseas Bank analysts Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann. The currency pair move reflects continued USD strength and sustained pressure on the offshore yuan as traders reassess near-term exchange rate dynamics amid firm dollar demand.

Intraday price action shows consolidation after an overdone rise, with USD/CNH now oscillating between 6.8030 and 6.8185. The pullback suggests short-term profit-taking in forex trading, while technical indicators point to stabilization following the sharp spike. Support near 6.8030 and resistance around 6.8185 are now key levels for currency pair direction.

Market implications remain focused on sustained US dollar resilience, with traders monitoring whether the yuan can stabilize after the recent breakout. Continued USD strength could keep USD/CNH elevated, while broader forex sentiment across EUR/USD and other majors remains sensitive to shifts in rate expectations and risk appetite in global trading sessions and near-term outlook.
