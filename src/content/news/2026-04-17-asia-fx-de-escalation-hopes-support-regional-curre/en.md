Improved diplomatic signals in the Middle East have eased tensions, boosting risk sentiment and supporting Asian currencies in forex trading. The US Dollar (USD) softened against its regional counterparts, with currency pairs such as USD/JPY and USD/CNH showing notable declines. The positive shift in sentiment follows hopes for de-escalation, yet USD remains underpinned by high US front-end yields, which continue to keep bond markets cautious.

While the softer USD benefits regional currencies, the bond market’s hesitancy limits broader moves. USD's resilience stems from expectations of further rate hikes by the Federal Reserve, which keeps pressure on exchange rates like EUR/USD and GBP/USD. The mixed signals in the market suggest volatility ahead, with traders balancing geopolitical risk with the Fed's stance on interest rates.
