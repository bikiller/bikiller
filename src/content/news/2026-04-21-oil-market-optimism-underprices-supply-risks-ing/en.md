Oil prices are currently driven by optimism surrounding potential progress in US–Iran peace talks. However, ING analysts Warren Patterson and Ewa Manthey caution that ongoing disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz are keeping supply risks elevated. This has created a volatile environment, where market sentiment appears to be underpricing the potential risks to supply chains, which could disrupt price stability.

As oil trading continues to react to geopolitical tensions, the EUR/USD pair is showing notable fluctuations, with the exchange rate responding to shifts in investor sentiment and oil market developments. The USD remains influenced by broader global economic conditions, making oil prices a key factor for traders when forecasting future currency moves. The price of crude oil, along with supply concerns, could prompt more volatility in both energy and forex markets in the coming weeks.
