British Pound (GBP) strengthened against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, with the GBP/USD currency pair benefiting from broader USD weakness and improved risk sentiment driven by renewed peace hopes. The exchange rate climbed to a fresh 10-day high at 1.3460, as traders reacted to softer demand for the greenback across forex markets.

Despite the move, the currency pair remains confined within a four-week trading range, reflecting ongoing consolidation in the forex market as investors shift attention toward monetary policy expectations and central bank signals that continue to drive USD and EUR positioning. Market participants remain cautious, awaiting clearer guidance from upcoming policy meetings that could determine near-term direction.

Looking ahead, the GBP/USD currency pair is likely to stay sensitive to shifts in risk appetite and central bank commentary, with the 1.3460 level acting as a short-term technical reference after marking the recent 10-day high. Sustained USD volatility could keep trading conditions range-bound across major forex pairs. Investors will watch upcoming central bank decisions closely for fresh directional cues.
