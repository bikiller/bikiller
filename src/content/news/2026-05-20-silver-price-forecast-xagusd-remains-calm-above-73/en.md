Silver (XAG/USD) traded quietly around $73.70 per troy ounce during Asian hours on Wednesday, holding above the $73.50 support level. The precious metal remains under pressure after a steep 5.18% drop in the previous session, reflecting persistent USD strength and a cautious trading environment. Analysts note that inflationary concerns continue to influence safe-haven demand, affecting market sentiment across currency pairs.  

Investors are monitoring key economic data that could sway USD exchange rates, including U.S. inflation figures and Eurozone monetary signals. The XAG/USD pair’s subdued movement suggests traders are weighing potential shifts in interest rates against ongoing global inflationary risks. Technical indicators highlight a consolidation phase, with volatility likely to rise if the pair tests major resistance levels above $74.  

Market participants remain cautious, with trading activity in silver closely linked to broader currency pair fluctuations, especially the USD’s performance against the EUR and other major currencies. Any uptick in inflation expectations could spur renewed buying, while a strong dollar may cap gains. Traders are advised to watch for breakout patterns as XAG/USD navigates this narrow range amid ongoing economic uncertainty.
