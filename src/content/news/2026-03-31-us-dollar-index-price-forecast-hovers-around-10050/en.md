The US Dollar Index (DXY) held firm near the 100.50 level during Tuesday’s Asian trading session, consolidating just below fresh 10-month highs after a five-day winning streak. The steady USD performance reflects sustained demand across major currency pairs, with the greenback maintaining strength against the EUR, JPY, and GBP as traders continue to favor the dollar in current market conditions.

Momentum in the exchange rate has been supported by resilient US economic signals and expectations that the Federal Reserve may keep interest rates elevated for longer. This outlook has underpinned USD buying activity, pressuring counterparts like the EUR/USD pair, which remains sensitive to diverging monetary policy paths between the European Central Bank and the Fed.

From a technical perspective, the DXY hovering around 100.50 suggests a consolidation phase near key resistance levels, with traders closely watching for a potential breakout above recent highs. Continued strength could reinforce bullish sentiment in USD trading, while any pullback may invite profit-taking and short-term corrections across major currency pairs.
