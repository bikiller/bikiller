The US Dollar (USD) enters the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) decision on solid ground, according to Bob Savage, Head of Markets Macro Strategy at BNY. Safe-haven flows have supported the greenback, reversing earlier February Dollar hedging sales. The EUR/USD exchange rate remains sensitive to the Federal Reserve's stance, with USD gaining traction ahead of any policy changes.

In the lead-up to the FOMC meeting, the USD is holding its ground against major currencies. The reversal of hedging activity and investor demand for the USD has strengthened its position, particularly in the EUR/USD pair, where the greenback’s dominance remains intact. Traders will be watching closely for any signals of tightening or dovish moves, which could shift the exchange rate dynamics.
