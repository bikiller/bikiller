The USD/INR currency pair edged higher on Friday as the Indian Rupee opened weaker against the US Dollar following a market holiday for Ram Navami. The softer start reflects cautious trading conditions, with investors reacting to persistent geopolitical uncertainty. Tensions surrounding the US-Iran divide over Middle East negotiations have dampened risk appetite, lending support to the USD and pressuring emerging market currencies.

Market participants remain sensitive to global cues, particularly safe-haven demand for the US Dollar amid fragile diplomatic developments. The INR’s decline highlights broader concerns over capital flows and external stability, as traders weigh geopolitical risks alongside domestic factors. Meanwhile, subdued activity in other major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, suggests a defensive tone across forex markets.

In the near term, the USD/INR exchange rate is likely to remain driven by headlines surrounding Middle East diplomacy and shifts in global risk sentiment. Traders will also monitor US economic data and Treasury yields for further direction. Any escalation in geopolitical tensions could reinforce USD strength, keeping the Rupee under pressure and influencing broader emerging market trading dynamics.
