Gold (XAU/USD) held steady above $4,700 on Thursday, showing resilience amid renewed geopolitical concerns in the Middle East. The currency pair climbed to a daily high of $4,764 during North American trading before settling near $4,705. Investors are reacting to reports that the US may revive Project Freedom, prompting a defensive demand for both gold and the USD as safe-haven assets.  

The surge in XAU/USD coincided with broader market movements, where the greenback strengthened against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY. Traders cited heightened risk sentiment as driving USD buying, which in turn provided support for gold prices. Market participants are closely watching technical levels around $4,700 and $4,760, with momentum indicators suggesting a cautious trading environment ahead.  

Analysts note that the revival of tensions in the Strait of Hormuz could maintain volatility in commodity-linked currencies and precious metals. A firm gold price above $4,700 may signal continued interest from investors seeking protection against geopolitical uncertainty and currency fluctuations. For forex traders, shifts in the USD exchange rate remain a key factor influencing XAU/USD and correlated assets in the near term.
