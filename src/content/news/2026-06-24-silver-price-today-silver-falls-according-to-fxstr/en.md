Silver (XAG/USD) declined on Wednesday per FXStreet data, reflecting a softer tone in precious metals trading. The exchange rate slipped to $61.15 per troy ounce, marking a 0.72% drop from Tuesday’s $61.60 close. The move signals mild profit-taking after recent strength, with traders reassessing short-term positioning in the silver market as volatility persists across USD-denominated commodities.

Broader forex market dynamics contributed to the downside pressure, with USD fluctuations influencing demand for dollar-priced metals. A steadier US dollar can weigh on silver by making it more expensive for non-USD buyers, including EUR-based investors watching the XAG/EUR cross. Traders also monitored shifting expectations in interest rates, which often impact non-yielding assets like silver, alongside broader commodity sentiment in global currency pair trading environments.

Technically, silver remains sensitive around the $61.60 prior level, now acting as near-term resistance after Wednesday’s pullback. The $61.15 exchange rate keeps XAG/USD within a tight trading range, suggesting consolidation rather than a clear trend reversal. Market participants will watch USD momentum and macroeconomic signals for direction in upcoming sessions.
