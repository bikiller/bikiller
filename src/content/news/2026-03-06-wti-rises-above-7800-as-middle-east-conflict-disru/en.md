West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil surged above $78.00, trading at approximately $78.10 during the early Asian session on Friday. This increase follows a remarkable 8.5% gain in a single day, marking WTI's largest rise since 2020. The sharp climb in prices is primarily driven by escalating conflicts in the Middle East, which have raised concerns over potential supply disruptions.

As traders react to the geopolitical tensions, the implications extend beyond the oil market, influencing related currency pairs. The rising crude prices could impact the USD/EUR exchange rate, as oil is a crucial commodity affecting inflation and economic stability. Market participants will be closely monitoring further developments in the region, as any sustained disruptions could lead to continued volatility in both the oil market and the forex arena.
