The New Zealand Dollar (NZD) extended losses during the European session on Tuesday, with the NZD/USD currency pair trading 0.4% lower at around 0.5690. The pair remains under pressure as hawkish Federal Reserve (Fed) bets continue to dampen appetite for risk-sensitive currencies. Broader USD strength has also reinforced the downside bias across major forex trading pairs.

Expectations that the Federal Reserve will maintain a restrictive policy stance have lifted the US dollar, weighing on higher-yielding assets such as the New Zealand Dollar. In forex markets, diverging central bank outlooks are keeping USD firm against peers like the euro and yen, while commodity-linked currencies continue to face sustained selling pressure.

Attention now turns to upcoming US PMI data, which could set the next direction for the currency pair and broader forex sentiment. Stronger-than-expected figures may further boost USD strength and extend losses in NZD/USD, while softer readings could offer limited relief. Traders remain cautious amid shifting rate expectations and ongoing volatility in major exchange rates.
