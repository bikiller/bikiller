Standard Chartered economists have revised their outlook for Indonesia's monetary policy, maintaining expectations that Bank Indonesia will cut its policy rate by 25 basis points in Q2 2026. However, the economists note that rising oil prices and persistent inflationary pressures now introduce risks of a prolonged hold on the rate, potentially affecting the IDR/USD exchange rate.

The delay in easing could influence market sentiment, especially for the Indonesian rupiah (IDR) against the USD. Higher oil prices generally impact trade balances, adding inflationary pressure and challenging Bank Indonesia's efforts to stimulate economic growth. As traders keep an eye on inflation trends and oil price fluctuations, the risk of continued policy tightening could dampen IDR strength in the near term.
