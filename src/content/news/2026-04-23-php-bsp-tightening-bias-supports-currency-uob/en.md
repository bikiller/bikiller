The Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) has initiated a new tightening cycle, raising the Target Reverse Repurchase (RRP) rate to 4.50%. This move signals a commitment to combat inflation and hints at further interest rate hikes in the near future. As a result, the Philippine peso (PHP) has demonstrated increased resilience against the US dollar (USD), with the exchange rate reflecting the market's response to the BSP's actions.

UOB economists Julia Goh and Loke Siew Ting noted that the BSP's tightening bias is likely to support the PHP in the forex market. As traders adjust to the changing interest rate landscape, the PHP could strengthen against major currency pairs, including the euro (EUR) and USD. This tightening cycle may also influence broader market sentiment and liquidity, impacting trading strategies in the coming weeks.
