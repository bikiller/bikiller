Eurozone inflation is expected to surge in March, with Societe Generale economists predicting a significant rise in headline inflation due to climbing energy prices. This uptick is likely to keep the EUR/USD exchange rate volatile, as traders react to mounting inflationary pressures. Despite this, core inflation is projected to ease slightly, signaling some relief in underlying price growth. 

The European Central Bank's hawkish stance remains intact as inflationary concerns persist. The ECB is likely to continue its aggressive tightening strategy, which could support the euro in the medium term. Market participants are closely monitoring the EUR/USD for any breakout or breakdown around key levels, with 1.05 as a critical support zone. As the dollar reacts to the Fed’s decisions, the interplay between these two currencies could see further fluctuations in the coming weeks.
