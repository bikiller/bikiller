ECB Governing Council member Joachim Nagel, who also heads the Deutsche Bundesbank, said on Monday during European trading hours that oil supply conditions are unlikely to normalize quickly, warning that it could take months before markets see meaningful stabilization. He stressed that inflationary pressure across the eurozone remains stubbornly elevated, even as geopolitical tensions linked to the US–Iran situation appear to ease.

In currency markets, traders reacted cautiously as energy-driven inflation expectations kept sentiment fragile. The EUR/USD currency pair struggled to build upside momentum, while the US dollar held relatively firm against major peers amid ongoing risk aversion. Higher oil prices continued to cloud the disinflation outlook, prompting bond yields to reflect expectations that monetary policy may stay restrictive for longer than previously assumed.

For forex trading, the outlook points to continued volatility across major currency pairs, especially EUR crosses, as ECB policy expectations remain highly data dependent. If inflation proves sticky due to prolonged energy pressure, bets on near-term rate cuts could be pushed further out, offering additional support to the USD. Traders are likely to stay focused on oil dynamics as a key driver of exchange rate direction in the sessions ahead.
