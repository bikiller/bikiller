The European Central Bank (ECB) held interest rates steady, signaling no immediate move for hikes despite rising oil prices and ongoing tensions in the Middle East. This decision comes as market participants had been weighing the impact of global instability on the ECB's policy stance. EUR/USD reacted mildly to the news, maintaining a narrow trading range, as traders adjusted expectations for future rate movements.

Carsten Brzeski, ING's Global Head of Macro, highlighted that the ECB remains cautious, with the Eurozone's economic outlook preventing any aggressive tightening. Despite concerns over inflation and energy prices, the ECB’s wait-and-see approach continues to dominate, with EUR/USD hovering just below the 1.0600 mark. As market sentiment remains mixed, traders will look to upcoming data for further direction, with attention focused on any potential changes in inflationary pressures or geopolitical developments.
