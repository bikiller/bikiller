The USD/CHF pair experienced a slight pullback to around 0.7985 during the Asian trading session on Tuesday, unable to extend its five-day winning streak. The US Dollar (USD) faced modest selling pressure as market sentiment shifted following reports that President Donald Trump is open to negotiating peace with Iran without demanding significant concessions. This development has eased some geopolitical concerns, leading to reduced demand for the safe-haven USD.

Despite the minor dip, the USD/CHF exchange rate remains supported above the 0.7980 level. Traders are closely monitoring any further news from the US administration regarding its stance on the Middle East. A sustained peace initiative could limit the upside potential of the USD, while ongoing uncertainty may keep the franc (CHF) in favor among risk-averse investors. The pair’s movement is likely to stay influenced by these developments, alongside broader trends in USD sentiment.
