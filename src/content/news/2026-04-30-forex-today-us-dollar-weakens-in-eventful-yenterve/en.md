The US Dollar Index (DXY) saw a sharp decline to around 98.10 on Thursday after disappointing economic data revealed that US GDP growth in Q1 came in below expectations. The weaker-than-forecast performance raised concerns about the pace of recovery, sending the greenback lower against major currencies. EUR/USD surged as a result, pushing towards the 1.1000 level.

Adding pressure on the USD, Japanese authorities intervened in the forex market for the first time in nearly two years. This "Yentervention" aimed to curb the yen's depreciation, adding volatility to the currency markets. USD/JPY dropped significantly, testing support near the 128.50 level, as traders adjusted positions amid the intervention news.
