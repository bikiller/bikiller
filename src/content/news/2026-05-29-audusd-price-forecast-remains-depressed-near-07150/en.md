AUD/USD came under renewed selling pressure on Friday, trimming the previous session’s rebound from below the 0.7100 mark and hovering near 0.7150 during European trading. The currency pair struggled to extend gains as the USD remained broadly supported by rising US Treasury yields and cautious market sentiment. Traders also scaled back expectations for aggressive Reserve Bank of Australia tightening, weighing on the Australian Dollar exchange rate.

The stronger Greenback continued to dominate forex trading flows, with investors favoring the USD amid persistent concerns over global growth and inflation risks. Reduced bets on further RBA rate hikes weakened demand for the Aussie, even as commodity-linked currencies attempted to stabilize. Meanwhile, the EUR/USD pair and other major currencies also traded defensively against the US Dollar, reflecting broader demand for safe-haven assets.

From a technical perspective, AUD/USD remains vulnerable after failing to sustain momentum above the 0.7150 region. The pair’s retreat from Thursday’s recovery reinforces bearish sentiment, while support is seen near the sub-0.7100 zone touched earlier this week. A decisive break below that level could expose additional downside pressure, whereas any recovery may face resistance as firm USD demand continues to limit upside potential across major currency pairs.
