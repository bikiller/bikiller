The British Pound (GBP) has recently outperformed other G10 currencies, bolstered by reduced expectations for an interest rate cut by the Bank of England. According to Rabobank's Senior FX Strategist Jane Foley, the GBP has benefited from market reassessments, leading to more optimism in the currency's outlook. This shift has seen GBP/USD trading at higher levels, currently testing key resistance at 1.2250.

Foley also highlights that inflation risks in the UK remain a concern, which could limit further GBP gains. However, the Pound’s strength against the Euro (EUR) has persisted, with EUR/GBP consolidating below 0.8700. While the market expects the BoE to stay cautious, any future policy shifts could prompt significant volatility in GBP pairs, with traders closely monitoring inflation data for further direction.
