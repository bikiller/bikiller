The US Dollar (USD) extended its losses for the second consecutive day, driven by growing concerns over escalating tensions in the Middle East and the upcoming Federal Reserve (Fed) policy decision. As of the latest trading session, the USD weakened against major currencies, with the EUR/USD rising to 1.0900, up 0.45%. Traders are opting for caution, leading to a shift in market sentiment ahead of the Fed’s anticipated rate decision on Wednesday.

The broader market remains on edge, with the USD facing pressure as geopolitical uncertainty compounds the wait for the Fed’s next move. The USD/JPY dropped below the 133.00 mark, down 0.3%, as investors sought safer assets. As the Fed’s decision looms, market participants are watching for any signals regarding future monetary tightening or dovish hints that could shift the currency dynamics, further influencing USD exchange rates.
