EUR/USD holds modest gains on Friday, but the currency pair struggles to extend its recent advance as the US dollar stabilizes after Thursday’s weaker-than-expected US jobs report. Traders are reassessing the Federal Reserve (Fed) and European Central Bank (ECB) outlook, while overall trading activity remains subdued with US financial markets closed for the Independence Day holiday.

Despite the modest upside, the exchange rate lacks momentum as investors weigh diverging monetary policy expectations between the Fed and ECB. The US dollar’s stabilization limits further EUR/USD gains, keeping price action range-bound. Market liquidity is thin due to the holiday, amplifying muted volatility across major forex pairs, with traders awaiting fresh US data for directional cues.

Looking ahead, the EUR/USD exchange rate is likely to remain sensitive to shifting Fed and ECB rate expectations, with the US dollar’s near-term stability capping further euro upside. In the absence of US market participation, trading conditions remain thin, suggesting continued consolidation until liquidity normalizes and new macroeconomic catalysts emerge.
