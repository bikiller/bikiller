Gold (XAU/USD) is trading under pressure below the $4,700 mark as the US Dollar (USD) continues its upward momentum. The currency pair faces resistance amid uncertainty over US-Iran diplomatic talks and expectations of a hawkish Federal Reserve stance, which are keeping investors cautious. Traders are monitoring the USD’s strength against other major currencies like the EUR, impacting gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset.

The exchange rate movement reflects a broader shift in forex markets, with USD pairs attracting flows amid risk-off sentiment. Gold’s struggle to hold support near $4,700 underscores the influence of central bank policies, while technical levels suggest a consolidation phase. Any reversal in USD strength or shifts in geopolitical tensions could trigger renewed trading activity and volatility for XAU/USD.

Analysts note that while gold remains a hedge against uncertainty, its short-term performance is tethered to currency dynamics. A sustained USD rally could pressure gold further, whereas easing Fed expectations or progress in international negotiations may boost trading interest. Investors are watching key support and resistance levels closely as market participants balance risk between commodities and major forex pairs.
