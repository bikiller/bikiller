The US Dollar Index (DXY) edged around the 98.00 mark during Thursday’s Asian session, stabilizing after a sharp retreat of nearly 0.5% in the previous day. Traders noted a decline in safe-haven demand for the USD as optimism surrounding potential US-Iran diplomatic developments tempered risk aversion. The index’s movements reflect a cautious sentiment, with the greenback holding steady against a basket of major currencies including the EUR, JPY, GBP, CAD, SEK, and CHF.  

Currency pairs mirrored the DXY’s moderate shift, with EUR/USD testing higher levels as the euro found some breathing room. Traders pointed to a softer USD exchange rate, allowing paired currencies to recover after the dollar’s earlier losses. Momentum indicators suggest that while the USD remains supported near 98.00, any escalation in geopolitical risk could reignite demand for safe-haven assets, potentially reversing recent gains in major pairs like USD/JPY and GBP/USD.  

Market participants remain focused on the interplay between geopolitical developments and the Fed’s policy outlook. The current stabilization around technical support levels indicates traders are weighing the impact of easing tensions against the USD’s broader performance. Short-term trading strategies are likely to hinge on news flow from Washington and the Middle East, with the DXY acting as a barometer for broader risk sentiment and influencing exchange rates across key currency pairs in the global forex market.
