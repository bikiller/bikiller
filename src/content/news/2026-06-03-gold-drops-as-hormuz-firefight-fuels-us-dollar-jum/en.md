Gold prices (XAU/USD) dropped more than 1% on Wednesday, extending losses for a second consecutive session as tensions between the US and Iran escalated near the Strait of Hormuz. The flare-up in hostilities spurred a sharp rally in the US Dollar, boosting the USD against major currency pairs like EUR/USD, while oil prices climbed on fears of supply disruptions. Traders reacted swiftly, pushing gold below key technical support levels.  

The exchange rate movements reflected safe-haven flows into the USD, with investors seeking liquidity amid geopolitical uncertainty. EUR/USD slid as the euro came under pressure, while commodity-linked currencies showed mixed performance. Market analysts noted that gold’s decline, despite heightened geopolitical risk, highlights the current dominance of USD strength in forex trading and its influence on precious metals markets.  

Looking ahead, the trajectory of XAU/USD will hinge on both geopolitical developments and dollar dynamics. Should tensions intensify, energy-linked currencies and metals could see heightened volatility. Forex traders are closely monitoring U.S. Treasury yields and currency pair correlations, as any further spike in the USD could pressure gold, while EUR and other major currencies may face additional headwinds in the coming sessions.
