Oil prices surged on Monday as a stalemate in US-Iran peace talks heightened concerns over global energy supply. ING analysts Warren Patterson and Ewa Manthey highlight that flows through the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil transit route, remain constrained, fueling bullish sentiment. Traders noted that Brent crude approached $88 per barrel, while WTI rose to near $84, pushing the USD higher against several major currency pairs amid safe-haven demand.  

The tighter oil market has rippled through forex trading, with energy-linked currencies like the CAD gaining against the USD and EUR. Technical levels suggest that the USD/CAD pair may test resistance around 1.3550, while the EUR/USD remains under pressure below 1.0850. Traders are closely watching how rising energy costs influence inflation expectations and central bank policy, which could drive further volatility in exchange rates.  

Market participants expect trading to remain sensitive to geopolitical headlines, particularly around the Middle East. Any disruption to oil supply could spur another wave of risk-off flows, strengthening the USD and weighing on commodity-linked currencies. Analysts warn that short-term market swings may present trading opportunities, but broader trends will hinge on the pace of diplomatic progress between Washington and Tehran.
