The AUD/USD currency pair edged higher following the Reserve Bank of Australia’s (RBA) widely anticipated 25-basis-point rate hike, lifting the cash rate to 4.35%. Traders initially pushed the exchange rate upward, reflecting confidence in ongoing policy support. However, the tone from Governor Bullock leaned dovish, signaling a potential pause in tightening, which tempered further upside and kept market participants cautious.  

TD Securities strategists Prashant Newnaha and Alex Loo highlight that the RBA’s cautious stance suggests that while inflation remains a consideration, the central bank is not rushing to continue hikes. This dovish messaging has pressured AUD/USD trading, preventing sharp gains against the USD, even as broader market sentiment remains mildly supportive of risk currencies.  

Technically, AUD/USD is hovering near short-term resistance levels, with upside limited unless follow-through buying emerges. Traders are watching closely for cues from future RBA commentary, while global USD strength and EUR movements continue to influence trading flows. The current environment favors measured positions, as the market weighs the balance between higher rates and the central bank’s preference for stability.
