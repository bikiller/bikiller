The EUR/USD currency pair is facing increasing pressure as strong support levels just below 1.1500 come under threat. Chris Turner from ING notes that elevated oil prices are adversely affecting Europe’s terms of trade, which may contribute to the weakening of the euro against the dollar. As market participants monitor these developments, traders should be aware of how this could impact the exchange rate.

Despite narrowing interest rate differentials between the U.S. and the eurozone, Turner suggests that any potential relief rally driven by the International Energy Agency (IEA) may offer limited upside for the euro. This scenario underscores the complexities of trading in the current environment, as ongoing geopolitical tensions and economic factors continue to influence the markets. Investors should stay vigilant as they navigate potential volatility in the EUR/USD pair.
