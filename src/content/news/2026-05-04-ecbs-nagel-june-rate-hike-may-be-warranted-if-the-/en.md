European Central Bank board member and Bundesbank President Joachim Nagel signaled that a June interest rate hike could be on the table if inflation pressures persist. Speaking in Frankfurt on Monday, Nagel warned that ongoing tensions in the Middle East risk keeping consumer prices elevated, suggesting that the ECB may need to act to stabilize the euro. Traders are closely watching EUR/USD and other major currency pairs for signs of market reaction.  

The euro dipped slightly against the dollar in early trading, reflecting investor caution amid uncertainty over the ECB’s next move. Analysts note that the exchange rate could face increased volatility if the inflation outlook fails to improve, with technical levels around 1.0850 and 1.0900 in focus. Market participants are weighing the potential policy response against broader economic risks, including energy price shocks linked to geopolitical developments.  

Forex desks report heightened activity in EUR/USD trading, with speculative positions adjusting to the possibility of tighter monetary policy. A sustained rise in inflation may prompt a more aggressive stance from the ECB, potentially supporting the euro in the medium term. Conversely, a deterioration in risk sentiment could weigh on the currency, while USD strength continues to influence cross-market dynamics.
