The US Dollar extended its recent gains as traders reacted to heightened geopolitical tensions. The US Dollar Index (DXY) climbed to the 100.50 area, buoyed by safe-haven demand after President Donald Trump signaled the US might adopt a firmer stance against Iran if regional hostilities intensify. The uptick in the USD has pushed several major currency pairs lower, with EUR/USD testing key support levels near 1.0800.  

Market participants are also positioning ahead of the upcoming Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report, which could further influence trading dynamics. The combination of geopolitical uncertainty and anticipation of strong US labor data has reinforced demand for the greenback, keeping USD/JPY above 134.50 and supporting the overall exchange rate of the dollar against a basket of major currencies.  

Analysts note that the USD rally may face resistance near 101.00 on the DXY, while softer European data could extend EUR weakness. Traders remain cautious, monitoring developments in the Middle East and key economic releases, as these factors could drive volatility across major currency pairs. Short-term trading strategies are likely to favor USD strength while keeping an eye on potential market corrections.
