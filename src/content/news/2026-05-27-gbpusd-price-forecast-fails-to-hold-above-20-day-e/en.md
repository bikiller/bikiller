The GBP/USD currency pair remained under pressure in early European trading on Wednesday, hovering close to Tuesday’s low near 1.3450 after failing to sustain momentum above the 20-day EMA. The exchange rate traded cautiously as market participants reassessed risk exposure amid growing uncertainty surrounding the durability of the ceasefire between the United States and Iran following Washington’s military strikes on southern Iran.

Demand for the USD strengthened across major currency markets as investors shifted toward safer assets, weighing on Sterling and limiting upside attempts in the Cable. Traders also monitored broader forex sentiment, with geopolitical tensions reviving concerns about global market stability and slowing appetite for risk-sensitive currencies. The inability of GBP/USD to hold above the 20-day EMA reinforced a weaker short-term technical outlook for the currency pair.

In wider trading, the stronger US Dollar pressured several major exchange rates, including EUR/USD, as investors favored defensive positioning ahead of further geopolitical developments. Analysts noted that if GBP/USD remains below the 20-day EMA and continues to trade near the 1.3450 region, bearish momentum could intensify in the near term. Market attention now turns to upcoming US economic data and geopolitical headlines for fresh direction in forex markets.
