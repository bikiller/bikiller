The correlation between the USD, oil, and equities has shifted recently, according to Bob Savage, Head of Markets Macro Strategy at BNY. Traditionally, these markets have moved in tandem, but recent events, such as tensions surrounding Iran and upcoming central bank meetings, have caused a break in these patterns. The US Dollar's exchange rate is now reacting more independently to these factors, as investors adjust their strategies amid geopolitical risks and monetary policy decisions.

In the forex market, this shift has impacted currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY. While oil prices typically influence the USD, the current dynamics suggest that traders are focusing more on geopolitical developments and central bank actions. As central banks prepare for key meetings, volatility in both oil and equity markets could continue to drive fluctuations in the USD, creating a more complex trading environment.
