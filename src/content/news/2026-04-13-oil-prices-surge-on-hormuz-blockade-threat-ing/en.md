Oil prices surged sharply on renewed geopolitical tensions as US-Iran talks collapsed, sending ICE Brent soaring over 9% and NYMEX WTI climbing past $105 a barrel. ING analysts Ewa Manthey and Warren Patterson highlight that the US plans a maritime blockade around Iranian ports, intensifying supply concerns and prompting broad risk-off sentiment in global markets. Energy-linked currencies and commodity-sensitive pairs reacted quickly.  

In forex markets, the USD strengthened against the EUR and other major currencies as investors sought safe-haven assets. The EUR/USD exchange rate fell, while commodity-linked currencies like the AUD and CAD faced selling pressure amid rising crude prices. Traders cited heightened volatility in both oil and forex markets, with market participants closely watching geopolitical developments for signs of escalation or resolution.  

Technically, oil’s breakout above $105 per barrel for WTI has triggered momentum buying, while Brent’s 9% jump added further upward pressure. Forex traders are monitoring correlations, noting that surging oil often supports USD strength but weighs on oil-importing nations’ currencies. Analysts suggest that continued geopolitical risks could sustain volatility in both energy and FX markets, with exchange rates and trading flows remaining sensitive to news from the Persian Gulf.
