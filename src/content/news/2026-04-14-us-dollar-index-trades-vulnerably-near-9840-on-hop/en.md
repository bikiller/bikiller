The US Dollar Index is trading vulnerably near 98.40 as market sentiment reflects cautious optimism over a potential US-Iran ceasefire. Investors weighed the outcome of the first round of talks in Pakistan, which ended without a concrete breakthrough, against signs that both Washington and Tehran remain committed to a second round of negotiations. The softer tone in USD trading has extended Monday’s losses, pressuring the currency across key pairs.  

The EUR/USD exchange rate has benefited from the USD’s vulnerability, edging higher as traders reallocate positions amid hopes of geopolitical easing. Technical analysts note that the USD’s failure to hold above near-term resistance around 98.50 could signal further retracement toward support levels in the 98.20–98.30 zone. Meanwhile, other major currency pairs, including USD/JPY and GBP/USD, are showing muted gains, reflecting cautious risk appetite and sensitivity to geopolitical developments.  

Market participants are positioning ahead of the anticipated second round of US-Iran talks, factoring in the potential for volatility in USD trading. A decisive move in either direction could influence short-term currency flows and shape broader market expectations for risk-on versus safe-haven assets. Traders are closely monitoring exchange rate trends, technical triggers, and news flows for guidance on USD strength and its impact across the forex market.
