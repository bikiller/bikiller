Gold (XAU/USD) remained largely flat around $4,700 on Thursday, holding near a one-week high as the USD showed signs of weakness. The precious metal has struggled to build on a two-day rebound from the $4,500 mark, a level that marked its lowest point in over a month. Traders noted the lack of clear directional momentum as the currency pair oscillated within a narrow range during early Asian session trading.  

The softer USD supported gold’s stability, with investors watching broader forex market flows for guidance. Moves in major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, influenced sentiment, as risk appetite and dollar index trends shaped demand for safe-haven assets. Analysts highlighted that gold’s resistance near $4,720 could face testing if the USD continues to retreat, while immediate support remains around $4,680.  

Market participants are weighing potential implications of weaker dollar trends on commodity-linked trading strategies. With global economic data and central bank cues in focus, XAU/USD’s price action reflects cautious positioning ahead of key announcements. A sustained weakening of the USD could encourage further upside in gold, while any rebound in the greenback may cap gains, keeping traders alert to short-term exchange rate swings.
