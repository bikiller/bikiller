The Australian Dollar extended its decline on Tuesday, with the AUD/USD currency pair slipping around 0.25% against the USD as risk sentiment deteriorated across global financial markets. The exchange rate traded at 0.7027 after briefly touching a six-week low of 0.7005, reflecting increased demand for the US Dollar as investors sought safer assets amid rising geopolitical tensions.

Market pressure intensified after former US President Donald Trump threatened retaliation against Iran following reports that a helicopter had been downed in the Strait of Hormuz. The development unsettled trading conditions and weighed on risk-sensitive currencies, including the Australian Dollar, while supporting the Greenback. Broader market caution also influenced major currency pairs, with traders closely monitoring movements in the USD and EUR as sentiment weakened.

The test of the 0.7005 level highlights a key technical area for AUD/USD, with investors assessing whether further downside could emerge if geopolitical risks escalate. For now, the currency pair remains under pressure as safe-haven flows continue to favor the US Dollar. Market participants are expected to watch upcoming developments closely, as shifts in risk appetite could play a decisive role in determining the pair’s near-term direction.
