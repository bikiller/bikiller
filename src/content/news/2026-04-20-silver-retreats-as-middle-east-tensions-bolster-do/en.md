Silver (XAG/USD) is seeing a decline in early trading on Monday, falling by 1.30% to $79.75. The precious metal's retreat comes amid rising tensions in the Middle East, which have prompted increased demand for the US Dollar as a safe-haven asset. This shift in investor sentiment is also fueling speculation that the Federal Reserve's interest rate hikes may persist for a longer period.

The stronger USD is putting pressure on silver, which is typically inversely correlated with the Dollar. As the market adjusts to these geopolitical developments and the outlook for higher-for-longer rates, silver's downside remains in focus. Traders are closely watching the $79.50 level for further signs of weakness in the currency pair.
