Silver (XAG/USD) clawed back from early Asian-session losses that pushed the exchange rate below $84.00, yet the rebound shows limited momentum. The currency pair continues to face resistance around the 100-hour Exponential Moving Average (EMA) near $86.15, a level that could cap further upside unless USD weakness or broader risk sentiment supports buying interest. Traders are watching this technical barrier closely for signs of follow-through.  

Market participants note that USD strength across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, has pressured silver earlier in the session. Despite the bounce, XAG/USD remains vulnerable, with short-term trading likely confined between $84.00 support and the EMA hurdle. Any decisive move above $86.15 could reignite bullish sentiment, while failure to breach it may see the metal retest sub-$84.00 levels.  

Analysts emphasize that momentum in the precious metals sector hinges on both technical triggers and macro drivers. Shifts in USD trends, equity market volatility, and interest rate expectations remain key for trading strategies in XAG/USD. With the 100-hour EMA acting as a pivotal resistance, traders may adjust positions cautiously, awaiting clear signals before committing to long or short positions in silver.
