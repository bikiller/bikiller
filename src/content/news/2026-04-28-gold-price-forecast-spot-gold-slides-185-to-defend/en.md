Spot gold (XAU/USD) experienced a significant decline of 1.85% on Tuesday, settling at $4,596 after hitting a low of $4,555 during the spot session. The drop came as the market digested growing expectations surrounding the Federal Reserve’s upcoming decision, which has added volatility to gold prices. Despite the pullback, the precious metal managed to recover slightly by the New York close.

The price action left gold trading within a narrow range, with investors eyeing the $4,600 level as a critical point of defense. This technical support may hold as traders await further cues from the Fed, especially as the USD remains a key driver of fluctuations in the gold market. As the Federal Reserve’s decisions unfold, market participants are bracing for potential shifts in the gold-to-USD exchange rate, influencing broader trading sentiment.
