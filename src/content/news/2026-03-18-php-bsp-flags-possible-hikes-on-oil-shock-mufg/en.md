The Philippine Peso (PHP) could face pressure to strengthen as the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) considers potential rate hikes due to rising oil prices. MUFG's Senior Currency Analyst, Michael Wan, highlighted that if oil prices continue to climb, driven by disruptions from the ongoing Iran conflict, the BSP may act to curb inflationary pressures. This development could influence the USD/PHP exchange rate, which has been volatile amid global oil market instability.

Traders will closely monitor oil price movements and geopolitical risks, particularly in the Middle East, as they assess the likelihood of BSP tightening. Any signs of tightening could provide support for the Peso, impacting currency pairs like EUR/PHP and USD/PHP. The market reaction to these signals will be crucial for future exchange rate trends and overall market sentiment toward the Philippines' economic outlook.
