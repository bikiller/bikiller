Indian Rupee traded largely unchanged against US Dollar at the start of the week following a long weekend, as the USD/INR currency pair opened on a muted note. The exchange rate showed minimal movement, with trading activity relatively subdued as global forex markets positioned cautiously ahead of key US Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) data scheduled later in the week.  

Sentiment across currency markets stayed restrained, with traders refraining from aggressive positioning before the NFP release, a report that often drives sharp volatility in the USD and broader forex space. The lack of strong domestic catalysts kept the Rupee confined to a narrow range, while major pairs such as EUR/USD and other G10 currencies also reflected consolidation and hesitant risk appetite.  

In the near term, USD/INR is likely to see stronger directional moves as liquidity improves and macroeconomic data flow intensifies. Volatility expectations are building across emerging market currencies, with traders closely watching US labor market signals for cues on USD strength. The outcome is expected to set the tone for forex trading momentum throughout the week.
