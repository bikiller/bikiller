Pound Sterling continued its downward trajectory against the US Dollar on Monday, with the GBP/USD currency pair slipping 0.6% to hover around 1.3260 during European trading. Traders pointed to heightened tensions in the Middle East, particularly around the Strait of Hormuz, as a key factor eroding risk appetite and driving investors toward safer-haven assets, including the USD. The decline reflects growing caution across forex markets amid geopolitical uncertainty.  

The exchange rate movement comes as investors reassess global trade and energy supply risks. The pound’s retreat against the dollar coincides with broader market volatility, with EUR/USD and other major pairs also feeling pressure from risk-off sentiment. Analysts note that if tensions persist, sterling could face additional downward pressure, testing technical support levels near 1.3200, while the dollar continues to benefit from its safe-haven appeal.  

Traders remain alert to upcoming developments in the Middle East, which could further influence currency flows. Market participants are balancing risk exposure across major pairs, including GBP/USD, EUR/USD, and USD/JPY, as geopolitical concerns weigh on sentiment. The pound’s current weakness underscores the sensitivity of forex markets to external shocks, emphasizing cautious trading and heightened attention to key technical levels in the near term.
