Vietnam’s inflationary pressures intensified in May, adding to concerns over the outlook for the Vietnamese dong as consumer prices accelerated to 5.6% year-on-year, the highest level since January 2020, according to Commerzbank. Rising food and energy costs were the main drivers behind the increase, highlighting persistent price pressures that could influence monetary policy expectations and trading sentiment in the FX market.

At the same time, Vietnam’s external position weakened as the country recorded a record trade deficit of USD 5.2 billion. The deterioration was largely driven by robust import growth, reflecting strong domestic demand but also increasing pressure on the balance of payments. For currency traders, the widening deficit may raise concerns about the sustainability of the dong’s exchange rate against major counterparts such as the USD and EUR.

The combination of elevated inflation and a sharply wider trade gap presents growing headwinds for the Vietnamese currency. While stronger imports can signal economic resilience, rising price pressures and external imbalances could weigh on investor confidence and increase volatility in currency pair trading involving the dong. Market participants will closely monitor upcoming inflation and trade data for clues on the currency’s near-term direction.
