The AUD/USD currency pair is showing resilience ahead of Australia’s March employment report, as traders weigh potential downside risks against the Reserve Bank of Australia’s (RBA) policy trajectory. ING’s Francesco Pesole notes that while a slight uptick in the unemployment rate could pressure the Aussie, market expectations largely anticipate that the RBA will proceed with a May rate hike, keeping the exchange rate supported.  

Recent trading has seen AUD/USD maintain its footing above key technical levels, with investors eyeing shifts in USD strength and broader risk sentiment. The possibility of weaker employment data has prompted cautious positioning, though the RBA’s hawkish stance underpins upside potential, suggesting the currency pair could retain momentum against the greenback even if data disappoint.  

Market participants are also monitoring correlations with other major pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY, as fluctuations in global yields and USD performance influence trading flows. Pesole’s view reinforces the idea that near-term volatility may emerge around the jobs report, but structural support from RBA policy expectations is likely to keep AUD/USD elevated, offering traders both risk and opportunity in the current environment.
