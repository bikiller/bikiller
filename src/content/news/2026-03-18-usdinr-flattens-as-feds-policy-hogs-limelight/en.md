The Indian Rupee (INR) held steady against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, with the USD/INR currency pair hovering near 92.80 during India’s afternoon trading session. Market participants remained cautious, keeping the pair largely range-bound as trading volumes thinned ahead of the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy decision scheduled for 23:30 IST (18:00 GMT). The lack of domestic drivers contributed to muted intraday movement.  

Investors appear hesitant to take directional bets, with the spotlight on the Fed’s policy stance and its potential impact on global liquidity. Any shift in US interest rates or forward guidance could influence USD sentiment broadly, potentially spilling over into emerging market currencies such as the INR. Analysts note that while technical levels near 92.75-92.85 currently cap fluctuations, volatility may spike once the Fed announcement is released.  

Meanwhile, other major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, have shown measured trading as traders await central bank cues. The cautious tone reflects a broader strategy of sidelining risk in favor of waiting for clarity from the Fed, highlighting the interplay between global monetary policy and local exchange rate movements. Market watchers suggest that any unexpected signals could trigger rapid adjustments across both developed and emerging market forex markets.
