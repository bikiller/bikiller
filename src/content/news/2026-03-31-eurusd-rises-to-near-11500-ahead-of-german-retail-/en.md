EUR/USD rebounded slightly after five consecutive days of losses, trading near 1.1480 during Tuesday's Asian session. The currency pair saw a modest increase as the market awaited key data from Germany and the Eurozone. The upcoming German Retail Sales and the Eurozone HICP inflation report could drive further price action, influencing traders' expectations for the ECB's monetary policy stance.

The euro has been under pressure recently due to mixed economic data and concerns over the global growth outlook. Despite this, the slight recovery in EUR/USD highlights a potential shift, with the pair nearing the 1.1500 level. The exchange rate's movement could be highly sensitive to upcoming reports, which may offer clarity on inflation trends and consumer spending in the region, impacting both the euro and the broader USD outlook.
