GBP/USD edged higher on Monday, climbing roughly 0.3% as the currency pair reclaimed the 1.3400 level. The rebound followed an early-session dip to around 1.3280, with the pair approaching, but not quite reaching, the 1.3450 mark. Traders noted that the pound’s modest gains came amid a broader consolidation in the forex market, reflecting mixed sentiment and cautious positioning ahead of key economic data.  

Market participants highlighted that the exchange rate movement was influenced by ongoing USD strength and lingering concerns over UK economic momentum. Technical analysts pointed to the consolidation trap between 1.3280 and 1.3450 as a critical range, suggesting that sustained trading above 1.3400 could attract renewed interest in the pound against the greenback. Meanwhile, EUR/USD and other major pairs remained under pressure, indicating that traders were weighing both domestic and international factors.  

Looking ahead, the pound’s trajectory against the USD may hinge on upcoming UK data releases and any shifts in risk appetite. A decisive break above 1.3450 could open the door for further upside, while failure to hold 1.3400 might keep the pair trapped within the current range. Traders remain alert to technical cues and macroeconomic headlines, as forex flows continue to react to volatility in global markets.
