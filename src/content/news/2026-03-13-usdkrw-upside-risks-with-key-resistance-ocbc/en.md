USD/KRW has edged closer to the 1,495 level, highlighting the South Korean won’s sensitivity amid heightened geopolitical tensions and surging energy prices. According to OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, this move underscores KRW’s high-beta behavior, with the currency pair responding sharply to risk-off sentiment in regional and global markets. Traders are closely monitoring the exchange rate for signs of sustained momentum or a potential pullback.  

The current advance in USD/KRW comes amid a backdrop of broader USD strength, with investors favoring the greenback over risk-sensitive Asian currencies. Volatility in oil and gas markets has further amplified KRW’s swings, reinforcing the currency pair’s role as a barometer for regional risk sentiment. Market participants are weighing whether the exchange rate will breach the key resistance around 1,495 or face corrective pressure in the short term.  

Looking ahead, the interplay of energy costs, geopolitical developments, and U.S. dollar movements will likely shape USD/KRW trading dynamics. A sustained push above the 1,495 mark could signal further upside risks, prompting active positioning by forex traders. Meanwhile, movements in other major pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, may influence investor sentiment and cross-currency flows, adding another layer of complexity for those tracking the Korean won.
