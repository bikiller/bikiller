Gold (XAU/USD) retreated as the Federal Reserve kept interest rates steady at 3.50%–3.75% on Wednesday, while the Summary of Economic Projections signaled policymakers still expect inflation above the 3% threshold. The currency pair showed heightened volatility as traders reassessed USD positioning, with the exchange rate oscillating within the $4,330–$4,280 range amid shifting risk sentiment and rate expectations.

Hawkish elements within the SEP reinforced expectations of persistent price pressures, supporting the US dollar and keeping yields elevated. In forex trading, this backdrop weighed on non-yielding gold as investors rotated into USD-linked assets. The XAU/USD pair remained under pressure, with $4,280 acting as immediate support and $4,330 marking the near-term resistance zone for the exchange rate.

Looking ahead, traders are likely to monitor further Fed signals as USD strength and interest rate expectations continue to drive direction in the currency pair. For XAU/USD, sustained trading within the $4,280–$4,330 band suggests a consolidation phase, with breakout moves dependent on shifting macro data and evolving US monetary policy outlook.
