The Malaysian Ringgit (MYR) remains under pressure against the US Dollar (USD), trading cautiously amid a backdrop of hawkish sentiment from the Federal Reserve. Analysts from OCBC, Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, note that despite supportive domestic economic indicators, the MYR continues to weaken as US Treasury yields firm up, reinforcing the strength of the USD.

Global oil prices have softened, providing only a limited buffer for the MYR rather than serving as a significant catalyst for recovery. As the currency pair of MYR/USD fluctuates, traders are closely monitoring these developments, which could influence exchange rates and broader market dynamics in the coming days. The outlook suggests continued volatility as external factors play a crucial role in shaping the MYR's trajectory against the USD.
