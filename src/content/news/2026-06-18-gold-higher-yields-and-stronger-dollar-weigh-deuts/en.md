Gold came under pressure as hawkish Federal Reserve repricing strengthened the US Dollar, Deutsche Bank said. The move followed the FOMC meeting, where markets adjusted expectations for policy easing. A firmer USD currency basket weighed on bullion and broader risk sentiment, with trading flows shifting toward higher-yielding US assets.

Deutsche Bank highlighted that risk assets sold off after FOMC, with both gold and Bitcoin declining. The catalyst was a sharp rise in 2-year Treasury yields, signaling tighter financial conditions. In FX markets, the stronger USD pressured major currency pairs and reduced demand for non-yielding assets like gold.

Implications for trading are that elevated yields continue to support USD strength across currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD, keeping gold’s upside limited in the near term. Traders are watching the exchange rate dynamics and Treasury curve movements closely, as further repricing in rate expectations could extend pressure on bullion and other risk-sensitive assets.
