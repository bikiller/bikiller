EUR/USD extends gains for the second consecutive session, trading around 1.1620 during Friday Asian hours. The currency pair shows continued euro strength as expectations of additional European Central Bank rate hikes gain traction. Meanwhile, the USD remains subdued ahead of key US Nonfarm Payrolls data, keeping exchange rate pressure tilted toward the upside.

Rising ECB rate hike odds are underpinning the euro, as markets price in a more hawkish policy outlook compared to the Federal Reserve's cautious stance. Yield differentials are supporting EUR demand, while USD sentiment weakens ahead of US labor data. Traders remain focused on positioning flows, with volatility compressing but bias still leaning euro-positive in the short term.

Looking ahead, attention turns to US Nonfarm Payrolls, which is expected to be the next major catalyst for EUR/USD trading direction. A stronger-than-expected print could revive USD buying and cap further gains above 1.1650, while a softer outcome may extend euro upside momentum and reinforce support near the 1.1600 level.
