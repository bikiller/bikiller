The USD/CAD currency pair extended its decline for a second consecutive session, trading near 1.3590 in early European hours on Wednesday. Momentum remains skewed to the downside as the pair struggles below the nine-day exponential moving average (EMA), reinforcing the bearish sentiment. Traders are watching closely as the exchange rate continues to test support within the broader descending channel observed on the daily chart.  

Technical indicators suggest that the bearish trend could persist unless USD buyers regain control above the 1.3600 level. Market participants cite a combination of softer U.S. dollar sentiment and recovering oil prices, which often support the Canadian dollar, as key drivers behind the recent moves. Trading volumes appear steady, with a cautious approach dominating ahead of upcoming economic data releases that may influence USD/CAD direction.  

Analysts note that a sustained break below the channel’s lower boundary could accelerate selling pressure, potentially pushing the exchange rate toward the next support near 1.3550. Conversely, a rebound above the nine-day EMA may signal a short-term recovery, drawing USD traders back into the market. For now, the technical setup points to a continued bearish bias, leaving investors attentive to both U.S. and Canadian economic indicators that could reshape the pair’s trajectory.
