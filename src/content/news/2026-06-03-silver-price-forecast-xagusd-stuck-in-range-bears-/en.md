Silver (XAG/USD) reversed its earlier gains on Wednesday, dropping over 2% as geopolitical tensions flared. The overnight exchange of fire between the US and Iran has intensified market concerns, sending safe-haven flows into the USD and pressuring the non-yielding metal. Traders now watch the $73.00 level closely, as a breach could signal further downside in the currency pair’s exchange rate.  

The sell-off reflects growing speculation that central banks may tighten monetary policy in response to geopolitical instability. With the USD strengthening against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, silver’s trading momentum faces headwinds. Analysts note that XAG/USD remains range-bound, struggling to sustain gains amid higher yields, which historically weigh on precious metals.  

Market participants are navigating a delicate balance between risk-off sentiment and technical pressures. While silver briefly tested resistance zones earlier in the week, the renewed USD strength and geopolitical uncertainty have tilted the outlook bearish. Traders are positioning for potential further declines, closely monitoring the exchange rate against key support and resistance levels for clues on the next directional move.
