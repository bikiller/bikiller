The Indian Rupee extended its losses against the US Dollar on Tuesday, resuming its downward trajectory after a brief pause in early trading. The USD/INR currency pair climbed toward the 94.46 level, reflecting persistent pressure on the domestic exchange rate. Market participants noted continued weakness in the Rupee as global factors overshadowed local support.

Elevated crude oil prices remain a key driver behind the INR’s decline, given India’s heavy reliance on oil imports. Higher energy costs tend to widen the trade deficit and increase demand for USD, pushing the currency pair higher. This dynamic has kept traders cautious, with sentiment leaning bearish on the Rupee in the near term.

In the broader forex market, the strengthening USD has also played a role, supported by firm demand and global risk trends. The USD/INR rally highlights ongoing vulnerabilities in emerging market currencies, with traders closely monitoring oil price movements and capital flows for further direction.
