Gold (XAU/USD) is showing limited momentum despite heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East and a softer US dollar. The precious metal struggles to build on modest intraday gains, remaining below the one-week high reached yesterday. Traders are closely watching the exchange rate as it hovers near $2,025 per ounce, with market participants cautious ahead of the upcoming US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data, which could drive USD volatility.  

In forex trading, the USD’s weakness has generally supported commodity-linked currencies, including gold. Yet, the lack of decisive buying pressure suggests market participants are weighing risks carefully, considering both safe-haven demand and broader risk sentiment. EUR/USD and other major currency pairs are also reflecting the cautious stance, with traders balancing geopolitical concerns against expectations for US inflation figures later this week.  

Technically, XAU/USD faces resistance near the recent one-week top, while intraday support around $2,015 holds key significance for short-term trading. A strong CPI reading could bolster the dollar, pressuring gold, whereas softer inflation may reignite bullish moves. Market watchers note that the interplay between currency pair dynamics and macroeconomic data will likely dictate trading direction in the near term.
