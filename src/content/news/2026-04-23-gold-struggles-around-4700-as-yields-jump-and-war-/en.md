Gold (XAU/USD) faced pressure on Thursday, hovering around $4,700 as rising yields and ongoing geopolitical tensions weighed on market sentiment. The exchange rate for gold has struggled amid fears of escalating conflict, particularly between the US and Iran. The upcoming talks involving Israeli and Lebanese ambassadors at the White House, with President Trump present, add to the uncertainty, impacting traders’ perception of safe-haven assets.

As yields increase, the allure of gold diminishes, impacting its position against other currencies. The fluctuations in the USD have contributed to the gold price's volatility, with investors closely monitoring developments in global relations that could further influence trading strategies. With geopolitical concerns persistent, traders are likely to remain cautious, weighing their positions in gold against potential market shifts tied to the USD and EUR dynamics.
