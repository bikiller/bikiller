Recent resilience in the USD and a renewed rise in US Treasury yields have created a more challenging environment for forex markets, according to MUFG analysts Lee Hardman and Abdul-Ahad Lockhart. Higher US yields have reduced the appeal of traditional FX carry trades, while commodity-linked currencies have underperformed against major peers. As a result, trading conditions have become less supportive for higher-yielding currencies, with investors increasingly focused on relative interest-rate dynamics.

The Japanese Yen remains particularly sensitive to upcoming inflation data, with domestic CPI figures expected to play a key role in shaping expectations for future policy moves from the Bank of Japan. Any signs of stronger inflation could strengthen the JPY by reinforcing speculation that Japanese policymakers may continue moving away from ultra-loose monetary settings. This has kept the USD/JPY currency pair in focus as traders assess the outlook for the exchange rate.

At the same time, broader USD strength continues to influence major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY. Elevated US yields have supported the US Dollar despite shifting global growth expectations, limiting demand for alternative currencies. Market participants are now closely monitoring inflation trends and central-bank signals, as these factors are likely to determine near-term trading direction across the forex market and influence exchange-rate volatility in the sessions ahead.
