West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil edged higher on Wednesday, stabilizing near $88.00 per barrel after two consecutive days of declines. Early European trading saw modest gains as investors digested ongoing US-Iran peace talks, which are adding uncertainty to energy markets. The recent dip had weighed on risk-sensitive currencies, while the USD saw mild support as traders monitored safe-haven flows amid geopolitical developments.  

Currency markets reflected the cautious mood, with the EUR/USD exchange rate hovering around 1.0980, struggling to gain traction against the USD. Commodity-linked currencies such as the AUD and CAD showed slight volatility, tracking WTI movements and broader risk sentiment. Traders highlighted that any breakthrough in the US-Iran talks could spur stronger dollar moves or lift commodity-linked currencies as oil prices react.  

Analysts noted that technical levels around $87.50 acted as support, while resistance near $88.50 could shape short-term trading. Forex participants remain alert to headlines, balancing energy market shifts with central bank policy expectations. With the WTI recovery modest but steady, the interplay between oil and major currency pairs continues to influence trading strategies across global markets.
