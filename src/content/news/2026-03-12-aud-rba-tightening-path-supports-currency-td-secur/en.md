The Australian dollar is showing resilience as expectations of further Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) tightening support the currency. TD Securities’ Prashant Newnaha projects a 25 basis point rate hike in both March and May, fully reversing last year’s cuts and lifting the cash rate back to 4.35%. Traders are pricing in these moves, with AUD/USD holding firm near key technical levels as markets anticipate continued hawkish guidance.  

The tightening outlook has boosted confidence in the AUD against major peers, including the USD and EUR. Exchange rates are responding to expectations of higher yields, with the currency pair seeing increased trading volumes as investors adjust positions ahead of the RBA meetings. Analysts note that sustained rate increases could reinforce the AUD’s appeal in forex markets, especially versus low-yielding currencies.  

Market participants are also monitoring broader implications for cross-currency dynamics. A rising Australian dollar could pressure commodity-linked currencies and influence USD/JPY and EUR/AUD flows, while traders weigh RBA commentary against global risk sentiment. The combination of policy-driven momentum and technical support suggests the AUD may continue to find strength in the near term, shaping trading strategies across major FX pairs.
