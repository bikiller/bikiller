The Australian Dollar showed little movement on Friday, hovering around 0.6910 against the US Dollar during Asian trading hours. After posting modest losses in the previous session, the AUD/USD currency pair regained some ground as the USD softened. Traders appeared cautious, weighing the impact of China’s latest Services PMI, which suggested steady but unspectacular activity, leaving the Aussie’s near-term outlook mixed.  

Market participants also reacted to broader risk sentiment, with the USD benefiting from safe-haven flows amid escalating tensions in the Middle East. Despite this, the Aussie held firm, indicating resilience against short-term geopolitical pressures. The exchange rate movements suggest that traders are balancing domestic economic signals from Australia and China with global risk trends, keeping volatility contained.  

Technically, AUD/USD faces resistance near 0.6930, while immediate support lies around 0.6890. The pair’s muted trading range reflects market indecision, though any sharp shifts in USD sentiment or regional geopolitical developments could trigger more pronounced moves. Analysts highlight that a breakout above key levels could attract fresh momentum, while downside risks remain limited unless broader risk aversion intensifies.
