Gold briefly slipped below USD 4,000 per troy ounce during intraday trading as Fed repricing pressure weighed on precious metals, Commerzbank analyst Carsten Fritsch noted. Bargain buying emerged quickly, stabilising the metal. The move reflected broader USD strength dynamics influencing currency pairs such as EUR/USD and risk sentiment across trading desks.

The market remains under pressure, with gold on track for its largest quarterly decline in 13 years alongside a steep monthly drop as expectations for US Federal Reserve policy shift. Traders reassess interest rate trajectories, impacting USD valuation and related forex trading flows between EUR and USD currency pair positioning.

Looking ahead, volatility in gold is expected to remain elevated as macro data and Fed signals continue to drive USD exchange rates. Sustained dollar strength could cap recovery attempts, while traders in forex markets watch EUR/USD spreads and broader risk appetite for confirmation of trend continuation across global trading sessions and liquidity conditions.
