The European Central Bank is widely expected to raise its deposit rate by 25 basis points to 2.25% in June, according to a Reuters poll. Out of 70 economists surveyed, 59 anticipate the hike, up from 44 of 85 in April, signaling a more aggressive approach to tightening monetary policy. This move is likely to influence EUR trading against major currencies, particularly USD, as investors adjust expectations for the eurozone’s borrowing costs.  

The potential rate increase comes amid persistent inflation pressures across the eurozone, prompting traders to reassess EUR/USD exchange rate trajectories. Higher interest rates typically bolster the euro, attracting yield-seeking flows and possibly pushing the EUR above technical resistance levels. Forex markets are already pricing in the likelihood of a June hike, which could lead to short-term volatility in currency pairs, especially against the dollar.  

Market participants are watching ECB communications closely for hints on the pace of future tightening. If the central bank signals further increases beyond 2.25%, EUR/USD could see a sustained uptrend, while USD positions may face downward pressure. For forex traders, positioning ahead of the ECB’s June decision will be critical, as shifts in expectations can trigger rapid trading activity across global markets.
