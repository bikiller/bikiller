The Federal Reserve is expected to maintain its current interest rates during the March FOMC meeting, according to ING’s Benjamin Schroeder. Elevated oil prices and persistent inflation expectations are limiting the central bank's ability to cut rates in the near term. As a result, markets are not anticipating any rate reductions today, with the first potential cut not fully priced in until the end of the year.

This outlook is impacting major currency pairs, particularly the USD. Traders are adjusting their positions as the EUR/USD exchange rate reflects a cautious sentiment. The US dollar remains supported by the Fed’s hawkish stance, while the euro faces downward pressure due to concerns over inflation and economic growth within the Eurozone. With no immediate rate cuts expected, the trading focus will likely shift to inflation data and oil price movements in the coming months.
