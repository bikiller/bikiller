The USD/JPY exchange rate remains steady just below the 160.00 mark on Wednesday, maintaining its position within a one-month trading range despite a broadly weaker US Dollar. The pair has been largely unaffected by the USD's overall decline, as it continues to hover around key resistance levels. Market sentiment remains cautious, with traders eyeing potential intervention from Japan to curb excessive volatility.

Oil prices, which have risen amid ongoing tensions in the Middle East, are adding pressure on the Japanese Yen. The elevated cost of oil is likely to keep inflation concerns high for Japan, further influencing the currency pair’s movement. As USD/JPY stabilizes below the 160.00 threshold, traders will continue to monitor geopolitical developments and central bank actions for signs of future intervention or policy shifts.
