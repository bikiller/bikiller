Oil prices fell as market expectations diverge from underlying data, according to Commerzbank Commodity Research led by Norman Liebke and colleagues. The team notes growing optimism over US–Iran diplomatic talks, which has reduced geopolitical risk premiums. Traders in global commodity and forex markets reacted with increased selling pressure, affecting risk sentiment across currency pairs and influencing broader trading activity in USD and EUR-denominated assets.

Commerzbank analysts highlight that expectations of a potential reopening of the Strait of Hormuz have further weighed on crude oil benchmarks. The strategic waterway is critical for global supply flows, and easing tensions has encouraged bearish positioning. In forex markets, weaker oil prices tend to support import-sensitive currencies while influencing USD strength dynamics and EUR/USD exchange rate volatility through shifting risk appetite.

Looking ahead, market participants will closely monitor US–Iran negotiations and any signals regarding maritime security in the Strait of Hormuz. Sustained easing of tensions could keep crude oil under pressure, while stabilizing global risk sentiment. Forex traders may adjust positions in USD crosses and EUR pairs, as shifting energy prices continue to influence inflation expectations and central bank rate outlooks.
