The GBP/JPY currency pair rebounded from a four-week low on Wednesday, showing renewed strength below the 100-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) for the second consecutive session. Traders lifted the exchange rate as hopes of de-escalation in Iran weighed on risk sentiment, encouraging demand for higher-yielding assets. The pair is now eyeing the 210.50 level, signaling potential upside if positive geopolitical news sustains market confidence.  

Market participants noted that the pound’s resilience came despite broader USD strength, which has pressured many major currencies including EUR and JPY. GBP/JPY trading saw cautious optimism as investors balanced risk appetite with technical constraints around the SMA. Analysts suggest that any sustained move above the 100-day SMA could attract further speculative flows, pushing the cross toward near-term resistance levels.  

Technical traders highlighted that support near recent lows helped limit losses, while the 210.50 target now serves as a focal point for short-term positioning. Meanwhile, volatility in EUR/USD and USD/JPY continues to influence cross-market flows, underscoring how macro events and central bank expectations drive currency dynamics. With geopolitical developments in focus, the market remains sensitive to headlines, leaving GBP/JPY poised for potential swings in either direction.
