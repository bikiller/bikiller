The US Consumer Price Index (CPI) data for March, set for release on Friday, is anticipated to reveal a sharp rise in inflation, reversing a two-year trend of gradual decline. The surge is expected to be primarily driven by a spike in crude oil prices following a joint US-Israel military strike on Iran. As oil prices climb, the impact on energy costs will likely ripple through the broader economy, affecting consumer goods.

This inflationary shift is expected to influence the USD’s performance against other currencies, with the EUR/USD pair potentially facing volatility. Traders will be closely monitoring the CPI report as it could signal further tightening by the Federal Reserve, altering market sentiment and shaping expectations for future monetary policy. The exchange rate for USD may see significant movement, reflecting investor anticipation of rising inflationary pressures.
