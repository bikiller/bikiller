The US Dollar extended its recent decline, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) sliding toward the 98.10 area, marking multi-week lows. Traders reacted to softer-than-expected US inflation data, which fueled speculation that the Federal Reserve may adopt a more cautious approach to tightening. The greenback's weakness was compounded by easing US Treasury yields and a dip in oil prices, pressuring USD across major currency pairs.  

EUR/USD led the gains among key forex pairs, climbing steadily as risk appetite returned to global markets. Optimism over Iran's potential economic developments supported broader market sentiment, encouraging traders to shift capital into higher-yielding or less defensive currencies. Meanwhile, USD/JPY and other dollar-denominated pairs faced persistent selling pressure, reflecting the broader trend of a softer dollar against both G10 and emerging market currencies.  

Analysts note that the current trading environment highlights the interplay between economic data, geopolitical factors, and commodity markets in shaping exchange rates. Should the dollar remain under pressure, technical levels around 98.10 on the DXY could attract buyers, but a sustained move lower may open room for further upside in EUR, GBP, and commodity-linked currencies, signaling a potential rotation in forex flows.
