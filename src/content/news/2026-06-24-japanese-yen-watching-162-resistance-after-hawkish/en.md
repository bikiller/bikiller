Japanese yen was only fractionally weaker against the US dollar in recent trading, but it continued to outperform across G10 currency crosses, suggesting underlying support in broader forex trading. Market participants attributed the resilience to growing caution over potential official intervention and expectations following a more hawkish tone from the Bank of Japan.

USD/JPY traders are now focusing on the 162 resistance level, a key technical barrier that could determine near-term direction in the currency pair. Trading conditions remain sensitive, with exchange rate moves reflecting thin liquidity and intervention risk, as investors assess whether the Japanese authorities may step in to curb further yen weakness against the US dollar.

According to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret, the yen’s relative strength versus other G10 currencies highlights persistent caution in FX markets. While USD demand remains supportive, the risk of policy action from Japan keeps trading ranges contained, with EUR and other major currency pairs also watching broader dollar dynamics.
