USD/JPY continues to trade in a tight range around 161.60 during Wednesday’s European trading session, reflecting subdued volatility in the currency pair. The exchange rate remains largely confined as traders avoid aggressive positioning, with spot price action showing limited directional momentum across intraday trading sessions in the forex market as participants await further cues from central banks and US data releases.

Market sentiment is being shaped by growing speculation that the Bank of Japan (BoJ) may maintain a more hawkish policy stance, which is providing underlying support for the Japanese Yen. This contrasts with continued US Dollar strength, as the USD maintains broad outperformance across major currency pairs, keeping USD/JPY anchored despite limited downside pressure in the broader forex trading environment.

Technically, the USD/JPY exchange rate shows signs of further range tightening near the 161.60 level, suggesting consolidation before a potential breakout move. Traders are closely watching whether sustained BoJ hawkish expectations or renewed USD demand will define the next directional phase in this major currency pair. Market participants are also monitoring risk sentiment and upcoming macroeconomic releases for fresh catalysts.
