The Japanese Yen (JPY) initially surged to a one-week high against the US Dollar (USD) due to a combination of intervention fears and hawkish signals from the Bank of Japan (BoJ). However, the JPY has since retraced a significant portion of its intraday gains and is now trading near the daily low as the European session approaches.

Market participants are closely monitoring the exchange rate dynamics, with the JPY's earlier strength attributed to safe-haven demand amidst global economic uncertainties. As traders assess the implications of potential policy shifts from the BoJ, the currency pair's volatility reflects the ongoing tug-of-war between the Yen's bullish momentum and a broadly weaker USD.
