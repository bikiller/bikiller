The South African Rand (ZAR) is facing an unwind of previously favored long positions against the USD. ING’s Chris Turner attributes this shift to rising market volatility, mounting pressure on low inflation, and a decline in precious metal momentum. As a result, the USD/ZAR exchange rate has seen significant moves, with traders reassessing their positions.

The unwinding of Rand positions has pushed the currency pair lower, highlighting a reversal in sentiment. Precious metals, which had been a key support for the Rand, are losing their appeal, while increasing volatility dampens investor confidence. As the market adjusts, traders are closely monitoring the USD/ZAR for any further shifts in momentum and the broader implications for emerging market currencies.
