Mexican Peso weakened against US Dollar on Wednesday, with USD/MXN trading at 17.55, up 0.37%, as the currency pair extended gains amid renewed demand for USD. Broader forex markets saw the Greenback firm across major pairs, pressuring emerging market currencies and pushing the exchange rate higher during midweek trading sessions.  

Sentiment was driven by growing speculation around a potential cancellation of the United States-Mexico-Canada Agreement (USMCA) signed in 2020, which weighed heavily on the Mexican Peso. At the same time, sustained US Dollar strength supported downside pressure on MXN, with traders repositioning across forex markets amid risk-off flows in emerging market currency trading.  

Looking ahead, the currency pair remains sensitive to trade policy headlines and USD momentum, with volatility likely to persist in short-term trading sessions. Market participants will continue monitoring USMCA developments, as further uncertainty could keep the Mexican Peso under pressure against the USD and influence broader emerging market FX performance.
