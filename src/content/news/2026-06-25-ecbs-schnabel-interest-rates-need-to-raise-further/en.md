European Central Bank policymaker Isabel Schnabel reiterated that interest rates still need to rise further, stressing that monetary policy is not yet sufficiently restrictive to bring inflation down to the 2% target. According to remarks released by Econostream during Thursday’s European trading session, the ECB maintains a cautious stance as price pressures persist across the euro area.

Foreign exchange markets reacted cautiously, with traders reassessing euro (EUR) strength against the US dollar (USD) and other major currency pairs. The prospect of additional ECB tightening supported the euro exchange rate, although sentiment remained sensitive to incoming inflation data. In trading, EUR/USD fluctuations reflected shifting expectations for future interest rate paths and central bank divergence.

A more hawkish ECB tone reinforces expectations of prolonged higher interest rates in the euro area, potentially supporting EUR-denominated assets while influencing global USD flows. For forex trading, this keeps attention on EUR/USD direction, yield differentials, and policy divergence between the ECB and Federal Reserve, shaping near-term volatility across major currency markets.
