OPEC+ is preparing to discuss a potential increase in its oil output quota at the upcoming meeting on April 5, aiming to cushion any disruptions should Strait of Hormuz export constraints ease. Traders are watching closely, as expectations of higher supply could pressure crude prices, impacting commodity-linked currencies like the CAD and NOK. The USD/CAD exchange rate may face downward pressure if oil weakens, while risk-sensitive pairs such as AUD/USD could see modest gains on easing energy cost concerns.  

Analysts suggest that even a modest hike could shift market sentiment, influencing both short-term trading and longer-term positioning. Brent crude has recently hovered near $85 per barrel, and WTI around $81, levels that are sensitive to any supply announcements. The euro and yen could see volatility if risk sentiment changes, as investors weigh potential USD strength against energy-driven currencies. Technical levels in USD/JPY near 133.50 and EUR/USD around 1.1000 may be tested depending on market reaction.  

Market participants are positioning ahead of the meeting, with speculative flows in oil futures likely to ripple across forex markets. If OPEC+ confirms a hike, traders may interpret it as a signal of easing tightness, prompting a rebound in commodity-linked currencies and a softening of the USD. Conversely, a more cautious approach could reinforce oil’s recent stability, supporting the greenback and prompting traders to reassess technical setups across key pairs. The outcome is expected to set the tone for both energy and forex markets in early April.
