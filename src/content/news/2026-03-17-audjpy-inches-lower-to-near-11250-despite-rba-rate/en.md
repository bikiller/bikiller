AUD/JPY slipped back toward the 112.50 level in Asian trading on Tuesday, erasing earlier gains seen after the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) delivered its latest policy update. The currency pair struggled as the Australian Dollar faced headwinds, leaving traders to reassess positions in the wake of the central bank’s rate hike. Market participants noted that despite the RBA’s decision, AUD demand remained muted against the Japanese Yen.  

Investors are closely watching technical levels, with 112.50 acting as a key short-term support. The exchange rate’s retreat underscores cautious sentiment in broader forex markets, where risk appetite has been tepid. Analysts point out that AUD/JPY dynamics are being influenced not only by domestic monetary policy but also by USD strength, global risk sentiment, and movements in EUR and other major currencies.  

Traders remain wary of near-term volatility, with AUD/JPY likely to continue reacting to RBA signals and broader market cues. The pair’s performance could offer insight into risk trends, particularly as the USD consolidates and other currencies like EUR show mixed momentum. For now, the focus remains on how traders navigate technical floors and adjust exposure amid shifting monetary conditions.
