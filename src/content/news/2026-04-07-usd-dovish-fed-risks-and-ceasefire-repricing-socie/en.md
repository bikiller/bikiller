The USD faces significant risks amid dovish Federal Reserve expectations and potential market shifts following a ceasefire. According to Societe Generale’s Kit Juckes, the dollar appears overvalued against G10 currencies, particularly when accounting for rate differentials. If the geopolitical situation stabilizes, the USD could see a sharp decline as oil prices tumble and safe-haven flows reverse, impacting pairs like EUR/USD, which is testing critical support levels.

A ceasefire could trigger a repricing of market expectations, particularly in the commodity space. With oil prices likely to fall, the USD’s appeal as a safe-haven would diminish, driving selling pressure across major pairs. In turn, this could weaken the dollar’s dominance, pushing EUR/USD above key resistance levels. Traders will closely monitor Fed policy signals and global risk sentiment for further indications on the USD's direction in the coming weeks.
