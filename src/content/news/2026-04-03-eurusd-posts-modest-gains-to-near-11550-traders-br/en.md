The EUR/USD currency pair edged higher to around 1.1540 during early Friday Asian trading, reflecting modest gains as traders prepared for key US economic data. Market activity is subdued ahead of the Good Friday holiday, keeping volumes relatively light. The euro’s slight advance comes amid steady demand for the USD, with investors closely watching the exchange rate for signs of short-term direction.  

Focus now shifts to the March US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report, which is expected to influence trading sentiment. Strong NFP figures could reinforce USD strength, potentially pressuring the EUR/USD lower, while weaker-than-expected employment data may support further euro gains. Market participants remain cautious, balancing positions ahead of the report that often triggers significant volatility in currency pairs.  

Technically, the EUR/USD is hovering near the 1.1550 mark, a level that could act as near-term resistance if buying interest continues. Traders are watching for breakout or reversal signals, mindful that thin liquidity could amplify price swings. The exchange rate’s movement today will likely set the tone for the pair’s behavior in the post-NFP session, shaping both short-term trading strategies and broader forex market sentiment.
