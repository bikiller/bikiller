The USD/CAD currency pair gained traction on Tuesday, trading around 1.3690 during Asian hours after a nearly flat session the previous day. The Canadian Dollar continues to face headwinds as safe-haven demand boosts the US Dollar, pushing the exchange rate higher. Market participants remain cautious amid rising geopolitical tensions, which are supporting broad USD strength across major pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD.  

Traders are watching technical levels closely, with 1.3700 acting as near-term resistance for USD/CAD. Momentum appears to favor the greenback, reflecting a flight to safety as investors seek USD liquidity amid global uncertainties. The Canadian Dollar’s struggles are compounded by lackluster domestic data, leaving it vulnerable to continued USD appreciation and potential short-term reversals.  

The dynamics of the forex market suggest USD/CAD could test higher levels if risk-off sentiment persists. Market watchers emphasize that broader USD movements against other major currencies, including EUR and JPY, are influencing Canadian Dollar positioning. Trading strategies may focus on the interplay between geopolitical developments and safe-haven flows, with key support and resistance levels guiding entry and exit points.
