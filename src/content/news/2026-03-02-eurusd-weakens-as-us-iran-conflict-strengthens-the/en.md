The Euro (EUR) weakened against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, with the EUR/USD exchange rate falling by more than 1%. Investors flocked to the safe-haven US Dollar as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East heightened, particularly the intensifying US-Iran conflict. This shift towards USD-based assets led to a significant dip in the Euro’s value, pushing the currency pair lower.

The strength of the US Dollar was further supported by its role as a global reserve currency, with market sentiment favoring the Greenback amidst growing uncertainty. As the EUR/USD continued to slide, traders watched for potential support levels around 1.0500, a critical threshold. The ongoing geopolitical risks suggest further volatility ahead, with EUR/USD likely to remain sensitive to developments in the Middle East.
