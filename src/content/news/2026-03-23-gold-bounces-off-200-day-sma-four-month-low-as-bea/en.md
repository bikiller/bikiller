Gold (XAU/USD) staged a modest rebound on Monday, bouncing off its 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) as traders reacted to technically oversold conditions. The yellow metal trimmed part of its earlier losses after dipping to a four-month low, with the USD’s recent strength keeping exchange rates under pressure. Early European session trading saw cautious buying emerge, limiting further downside momentum.  

The currency pair’s retreat from the SMA highlights a pause in bearish sentiment, as investors weigh broader market dynamics. Risk sentiment and the greenback’s performance against major currencies like the EUR continue to influence gold’s trajectory. Analysts note that oversold readings on daily charts are attracting short-term technical buying, even as macroeconomic factors, including US data and interest rate expectations, underpin a firm USD.  

Looking ahead, XAU/USD faces resistance near the 200-day SMA, with intraday swings likely dictated by shifts in USD liquidity and trading flows in key European and US sessions. Market participants are closely watching if the bounce can extend or if renewed selling resumes, potentially challenging support levels set during the four-month low. The interplay between safe-haven demand and currency pair dynamics will remain central to gold’s near-term outlook.
