Gold (XAU/USD) extended its recovery on Wednesday, building on the previous day's rebound from a low just below the $1,800 level, its weakest point in over a week. The precious metal found support amid rising safe-haven demand driven by escalating geopolitical tensions. As a result, the price gained positive traction during the Asian trading session, with traders flocking to gold as a hedge against uncertainty.

However, the upside was capped by the strength of the US dollar, which remains bolstered by a robust economic outlook. The USD’s firm hold continues to limit gold's potential for significant gains, as the exchange rate for the greenback remains a key factor in the broader market dynamics. With EUR/USD also impacted by the dollar’s dominance, gold's gains remain somewhat muted as the market waits for further geopolitical developments.
