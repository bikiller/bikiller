USD/CNH is extending its decline, with Societe Generale highlighting downside targets in the 6.77–6.69 range. The currency pair has struggled to sustain rebounds after repeated failures at the 50-day moving average, which has acted as a ceiling since last year. Traders note that this technical resistance has kept USD gains in check despite broader market volatility.  

The recent trading activity reflects a cautious USD outlook against the CNH, as market participants react to both technical cues and macroeconomic signals. With the 50-DMA consistently capping upward moves, USD/CNH is increasingly pressured toward lower support levels. This trend may influence hedging strategies for corporations and forex investors managing exposure between USD and emerging market currencies.  

Broader implications could ripple through related FX markets, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY, as USD strength softens. Analysts suggest monitoring intraday trading ranges closely, as further declines toward 6.77–6.69 could trigger technical selling. For now, the exchange rate dynamics underscore the interplay of technical resistance and investor sentiment shaping near-term currency trading strategies.
