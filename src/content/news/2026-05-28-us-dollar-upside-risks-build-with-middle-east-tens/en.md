The US dollar is showing signs of strength as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East push investors toward safe-haven currencies. Analysts from MUFG, Derek Halpenny and Abdul-Ahad Lockhart, highlight that rising oil prices are amplifying upside risks for the USD. Higher energy costs are fueling inflation concerns, prompting traders to anticipate a more hawkish stance from the Federal Reserve, which in turn supports demand for the greenback in major currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY.

Forex markets are reacting to the broader implications of energy-driven inflation. As crude prices climb, the dollar is benefiting from safe-haven flows, while the euro and other risk-sensitive currencies face pressure. Technical levels suggest that USD trading could test key resistance points, with market participants closely watching whether the escalation in Middle East conflict sustains upward momentum or triggers broader volatility across currency pairs.

Traders are adjusting positions in light of these developments, with the USD exhibiting resilience against a backdrop of global uncertainty. MUFG emphasizes that continued geopolitical friction and sustained higher oil prices could keep the exchange rate elevated, influencing forex strategies and shaping central bank expectations. Market watchers are particularly focused on how these dynamics might affect short-term trading opportunities and longer-term USD trends.
