Gold (XAU/USD) dipped on Thursday, ending a two-day rally as market sentiment soured amid ongoing uncertainty around US-Iran negotiations. The heightened geopolitical tensions kept traders cautious, with the yellow metal trading under pressure. At the time of writing, XAU/USD was down 0.4%, hovering just above the $1,930 mark.

The pullback in gold prices was primarily driven by rising global interest rate expectations. Hawkish central bank stances, particularly in response to oil-driven inflation, have bolstered the USD, further weighing on gold. As the USD continues to appreciate, the EUR/USD exchange rate remains under pressure, trading near 1.0700. Traders are closely monitoring upcoming economic data for further indications on central bank policy direction.
