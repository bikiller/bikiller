The AUD/USD currency pair held steady near the mid-0.7000s on Thursday, drawing dip-buyers around the 0.7020 mark during the Asian session. This buying interest partly reversed the previous day’s retracement from last week’s highs, reflecting cautious optimism in the market. Traders are closely watching the 200-EMA on the H4 chart, which remains a key technical level influencing short-term trading decisions.  

Market sentiment around the Australian dollar was supported by broader USD softness, while risk appetite in global markets contributed to a mild rebound. The exchange rate’s recovery suggests traders are balancing profit-taking from recent rallies with renewed buying on dips. Technical indicators point to potential stabilization above 0.7000, though the pair’s direction will depend on both USD moves and broader currency market flows, including EUR and other major currencies.  

Short-term trading strategies may focus on testing resistance levels while monitoring the 200-EMA for breakout or rejection signals. With AUD/USD hovering around 0.7020, intraday volatility could persist, offering opportunities for scalpers and swing traders alike. Analysts note that any decisive move above recent highs could attract additional momentum, while a drop below the key EMA could invite further retracement. Overall, market participants remain alert to both technical cues and macroeconomic drivers shaping the currency pair.
