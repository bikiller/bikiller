The Euro (EUR) continued its retreat against the British Pound (GBP) on Tuesday, pushing the EUR/GBP currency pair toward its near-term support at 0.8700. This follows Monday’s recovery to 0.8735, marking a second consecutive day of losses. Traders pointed to mixed Eurozone and UK services data as the immediate catalyst, with the exchange rate reflecting caution in European markets amid lingering economic uncertainties.  

Market participants noted that the downward momentum in EUR/GBP aligns with broader trading trends, where the Euro has struggled against the Pound despite the US Dollar (USD) maintaining relative strength in global forex markets. Analysts highlighted that investor focus remains on service sector performance and inflation indicators, which continue to sway short-term exchange rate fluctuations across EUR, GBP, and USD crosses.  

Technically, the 0.8700 level now acts as a critical threshold. A break below could signal further depreciation for EUR in forex trading, while a rebound from this support may attract short-term buyers targeting the 0.8735 highs. As traders weigh Eurozone growth against UK economic resilience, the currency pair remains under close observation for signs of volatility and potential USD-linked cross-market impacts.
