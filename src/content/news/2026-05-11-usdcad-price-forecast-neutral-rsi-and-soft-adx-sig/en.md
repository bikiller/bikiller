The USD/CAD currency pair is showing little directional momentum on Monday, trading near 1.3672 as the market reacts to a broadly steady US Dollar (USD) alongside elevated crude oil prices. Technical indicators, including a neutral Relative Strength Index (RSI) and a soft Average Directional Index (ADX), suggest that neither bulls nor bears are firmly in control, keeping exchange rates within a tight range. Traders are monitoring both macroeconomic cues and commodity trends for guidance.  

Momentum in the forex market remains subdued, with the USD/CAD pairing experiencing minor gains and losses throughout the session. The stability of the USD is counterbalanced by higher oil prices, which traditionally support the Canadian Dollar (CAD), creating a tug-of-war effect. This balance has limited breakout opportunities, leaving the exchange rate relatively flat and challenging traders seeking short-term volatility.  

Looking ahead, analysts indicate that without significant shifts in USD strength or oil market dynamics, the USD/CAD may continue to consolidate around current levels. Key levels near 1.3672 are likely to act as reference points, while traders watch for external catalysts from US economic data or shifts in risk sentiment across global currency markets, including EUR and other major pairs. This suggests a cautious trading environment, with potential for gradual directional shifts rather than sharp moves.
