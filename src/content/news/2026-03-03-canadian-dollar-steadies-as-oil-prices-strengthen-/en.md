The Canadian Dollar (CAD) has found stability as oil prices rise following the closure of the Strait of Hormuz. During Asian trading hours on Tuesday, the USD/CAD exchange rate remained steady around 1.3670, maintaining the modest gains from the previous session. This resilience reflects the CAD’s correlation with commodity prices, particularly oil.

The uptick in oil prices has bolstered the Canadian Dollar, allowing it to withstand pressures from the USD. The ongoing geopolitical tensions in the region have heightened market sensitivity to oil supply, which could further impact the CAD’s performance against the USD. Traders are closely monitoring these developments, as fluctuations in oil prices may lead to more significant shifts in the USD/CAD currency pair in the coming days.
