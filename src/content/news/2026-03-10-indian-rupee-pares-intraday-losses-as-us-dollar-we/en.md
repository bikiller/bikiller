The Indian Rupee (INR) trimmed its intraday losses as the US Dollar weakened, with the USD/INR pair recovering slightly after modest declines in the previous session. The exchange rate had reached a historic high of 92.81 on Monday, driven by heightened risk aversion in global markets and rising oil prices, which increased pressure on the INR.

Despite the pullback, the Indian Rupee continues to face significant selling pressure, fueled by concerns over the domestic economic outlook and elevated commodity costs. With the USD strengthening amid broader market volatility, traders are closely monitoring these key technical levels in the USD/INR pair, as further fluctuations are expected depending on global risk sentiment and oil price movements.
