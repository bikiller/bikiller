West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil surged almost 28% early on Monday, reaching a fresh three-and-a-half-year high near $113.00 per barrel during the European trading session. The rally signals strong bullish momentum in the commodity, driven by tightening global supply and rising geopolitical concerns. As the WTI price flirted with multi-year highs, it left traders anticipating a potential breakout above $126 in the coming months.

Despite the early gains, WTI futures saw a slight pullback as the session progressed. Traders are weighing the potential impact of the stronger US Dollar (USD) and global economic data. With market volatility expected to persist, oil prices could experience further fluctuations, but the focus remains on critical levels such as $113.00 for immediate support and $126.00 for longer-term resistance. The broader currency market, including the EUR/USD exchange rate, may also be influenced by oil price trends in the near future.
