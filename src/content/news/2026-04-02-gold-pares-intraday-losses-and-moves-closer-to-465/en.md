Gold (XAU/USD) has partially recovered from earlier losses, moving back toward the $4,650 level during the early part of the European trading session. The yellow metal faced significant downward pressure, weighed down by a stronger US Dollar (USD), which has seen a notable increase in demand. As a result, gold's upside potential remains limited, despite the brief bounce.

The strengthening USD has been the primary driver behind gold's struggle to maintain momentum. With the USD gaining traction across various currency pairs, including EUR/USD, investor sentiment remains cautious. Until there is a significant shift in the dollar's demand or a catalyst for a bullish reversal, gold's price movement may remain subdued near current levels.
