WTI oil prices experienced a sharp decline on Friday, falling to around $81.50 after dropping 9.12% during the day. The drop came after crude briefly spiked above $90 earlier, but market sentiment quickly shifted as sellers took control. The dramatic price movement highlights volatility in the energy market, driven by global supply concerns.

The trigger for the downturn was Iran's full reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, alleviating fears of major supply disruptions. This move has led to a sense of relief among traders, easing the pressure on oil prices and impacting related currency pairs, particularly the USD. With the geopolitical tension subsiding, traders have shifted focus to other economic factors, driving the reversal in crude prices.
