Gold (XAU/USD) faced selling pressure during the early Asian session on Friday, dropping below the $5,100 mark. The precious metal continued its decline as the US Dollar (USD) strengthened, alongside rising Treasury yields. This movement came amid growing inflation concerns fueled by higher oil prices, which prompted traders to adjust their positions, driving gold lower.

As oil prices surged, investors turned cautious, reflecting fears that escalating energy costs could further tighten global inflationary pressures. With the USD in an upward trajectory, traders favored the dollar for its safe-haven appeal, putting additional downward pressure on gold. The precious metal’s inability to hold above key technical levels, including $5,090, suggests a continuation of the bearish trend unless the market shifts dynamics in the coming sessions.
