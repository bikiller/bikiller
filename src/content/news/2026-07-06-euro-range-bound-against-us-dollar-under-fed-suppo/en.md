The EUR/USD currency pair remained confined to a narrow trading range on Monday, holding above the key 1.1400 level as investors reassessed the outlook for both the European Central Bank and the Federal Reserve. A firmer USD, supported by expectations that the Fed will maintain a relatively restrictive policy stance, continued to limit upward momentum in the exchange rate despite the Euro’s underlying resilience in recent sessions.

Market participants also adjusted expectations for the ECB’s policy path, with the probability of a September rate hike now priced at below 50%. That shift has reduced bullish enthusiasm for the EUR, encouraging cautious trading rather than aggressive positioning. According to ING’s Chris Turner, the balance between Fed support for the US Dollar and a more measured ECB outlook is keeping EUR/USD locked in consolidation instead of developing a stronger directional trend.

For forex traders, the 1.1400 level remains an important technical reference, as sustained trading above this support could help stabilize sentiment even if fresh gains prove difficult. Until clearer guidance emerges from incoming economic data and central bank communication, the EUR/USD exchange rate is likely to remain range-bound, with USD strength and evolving interest-rate expectations continuing to shape short-term price action.
