EUR/USD edged higher as traders recalibrated expectations for European Central Bank policy. ING’s Francesco Pesole noted that, despite the ECB’s outwardly cautious stance, discussions within the bank suggest a potential April rate hike if inflation remains above target. The shift has prompted markets to price in earlier tightening, boosting the euro against the dollar in early trading sessions.  

The currency pair gained on speculation of back-to-back ECB moves, reflecting increased demand for EUR amid softer USD sentiment. Analysts highlight that the exchange rate’s upward momentum is underpinned by market anticipation of higher rates, with traders closely watching eurozone inflation data for confirmation. Technical levels around key resistance points are now in focus as positions adjust.  

Market participants are weighing the implications for broader forex trading strategies. A hawkish tilt from the ECB could strengthen the euro further, pressuring USD pairs and influencing cross-currency flows. Short-term traders may target volatility around upcoming ECB commentary, while long-term investors assess the sustainability of EUR gains in the context of global monetary tightening trends.
