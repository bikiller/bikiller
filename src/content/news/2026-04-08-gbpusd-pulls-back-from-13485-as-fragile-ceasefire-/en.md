GBP/USD retreated from session highs near 1.3485 on Thursday after rallying more than 1% on Wednesday, driven by news of a Pakistan-brokered two-week ceasefire between the US and Iran. Traders had initially pushed Cable sharply higher as geopolitical tensions eased, boosting risk sentiment and fueling demand for the British pound against the US dollar. The exchange rate’s rebound highlighted the market’s sensitivity to political developments.  

The sudden pullback reflects caution, with investors weighing the fragility of the ceasefire against broader economic fundamentals. While the pound gained on risk-on flows, the USD maintained underlying support amid ongoing concerns over US inflation and interest rate expectations. Technical traders noted resistance around 1.3485, while key support levels near 1.3400 could shape the next leg of trading for the currency pair.  

Market watchers suggest that Sterling’s short-term momentum could remain volatile as geopolitical headlines continue to influence sentiment. EUR/USD and other major pairs also felt spillover effects, with investors balancing risk appetite against safe-haven demand. Analysts emphasize that while the ceasefire offers temporary relief, sustained gains in GBP/USD will depend on broader economic indicators and central bank signals, making this week’s trading closely watched for directional clues.
