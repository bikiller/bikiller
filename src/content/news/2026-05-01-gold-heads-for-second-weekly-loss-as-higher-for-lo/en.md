Gold (XAU/USD) is set for its second consecutive weekly loss, with the price slipping further on Friday. The precious metal's decline comes as expectations for higher interest rates for a prolonged period continue to dominate the market. These expectations are largely fueled by persistent inflation concerns, amplified by rising oil prices. The current price action sees XAU/USD trading lower, reflecting market sentiment that favors stronger USD.

The continued strength of the USD, supported by the Federal Reserve's stance on interest rates, has put downward pressure on gold. As inflation remains a key concern, higher oil prices further intensify market fears of prolonged price instability. Traders are closely monitoring the situation, with gold’s recent performance highlighting a strong inverse correlation with the US dollar, as the market adjusts to expectations for tighter monetary policy.
