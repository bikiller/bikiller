Emerging market currencies are under pressure as investor appetite shifts toward safety. According to BNY’s iFlow data, heightened risk aversion is driving strong inflows into G10 and Eurozone government bonds, while EM sovereign debt sees sustained selling. In FX markets, the Indian rupee and euro face notable outflows, reflecting caution among traders, whereas the Chinese yuan and South African rand are attracting demand amid perceived stability.  

Trading activity shows the USD strengthening against INR and EUR, with the USD/INR pair edging higher on heavy selling. The CNY benefits from both defensive positioning and supportive capital inflows, while the ZAR gains as investors rotate into energy-linked and commodity-sensitive currencies. Market participants are closely watching how these flows influence exchange rate dynamics in the near term, particularly against a backdrop of global uncertainty.  

The rotation from EM to G10 assets underlines a broader risk-off sentiment, suggesting potential volatility in currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/ZAR. Traders are positioning for continued demand for safe-haven assets, with bond yields in Europe and the U.S. shaping FX trading strategies. Analysts highlight that these shifts could impact cross-currency hedging and portfolio allocations, emphasizing careful monitoring of both sovereign debt flows and currency pair movements in the weeks ahead.
