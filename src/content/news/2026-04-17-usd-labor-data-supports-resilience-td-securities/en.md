The US labor market showed unexpected resilience, according to TD Securities, with initial jobless claims falling below forecasts. At 240,000, the figure was lower than the anticipated 245,000, signaling continued strength in the economy. Continuing claims were also close to consensus at 1.8 million, suggesting stable employment conditions. This data supports a positive outlook for the USD as traders eye economic stability.

In the forex market, the US dollar gained ground against major currencies, with the EUR/USD exchange rate dipping below 1.0900. The upbeat labor data further solidifies expectations of sustained growth, keeping the Federal Reserve's tightening cycle on track. As the dollar strengthens, traders are likely to adjust positions, with a focus on potential rate hikes in upcoming Federal Open Market Committee meetings. This resilience in the labor market is key to the dollar’s ongoing upward momentum.
