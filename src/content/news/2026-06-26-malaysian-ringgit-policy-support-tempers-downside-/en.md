Malaysian ringgit outperformed in FX trading, rising 0.4% against the US dollar (USD) as the USD/MYR currency pair weakened. According to MUFG strategist Lloyd Chan, the move followed Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) reintroducing a 2024-style foreign exchange support measure aimed at encouraging the repatriation and conversion of offshore earnings into the domestic currency.

MUFG noted that the policy has helped stabilise FX flows by incentivising exporters and corporates to bring overseas proceeds back onshore, improving liquidity in the ringgit market. While this policy support tempers downside pressure on the currency pair, it also caps upside momentum during broader USD strength and shifting EUR and Asian FX sentiment.

Looking ahead, traders will continue to monitor BNM policy signals alongside US dollar direction for the USD/MYR exchange rate. Persistent policy intervention may reduce volatility but could limit sustained appreciation, keeping the ringgit broadly range-bound in forex trading as global rate expectations and risk sentiment evolve with investors weighing capital flows and regional FX performance.
