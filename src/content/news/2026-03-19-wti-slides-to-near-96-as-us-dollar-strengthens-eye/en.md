West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude edged lower to around $96.00 in early European trading on Thursday, pressured by a firming US Dollar. Investors are keeping a close watch on currency moves, with the USD gaining against major peers such as the EUR and JPY. The strengthening greenback has heightened the cost of dollar-denominated commodities, adding downward pressure on oil prices despite lingering Middle East tensions.  

The USD index, reflecting the dollar’s strength against a basket of currencies, has climbed in recent sessions, influencing trading across forex pairs and commodity markets. WTI’s retreat highlights how currency fluctuations can directly affect oil pricing, as traders weigh geopolitical risks against the relative appeal of dollar-denominated assets. The EUR/USD pair remains under pressure, reflecting broader market sentiment favoring the USD.  

Market participants are now assessing whether oil’s pullback is temporary or signals a more sustained trend amid global currency dynamics. The interplay between exchange rates and crude pricing underscores the sensitivity of commodities to forex movements. Analysts suggest that any shift in the USD’s trajectory could quickly impact WTI trading, influencing both hedging strategies and speculative flows in the oil and forex markets.
