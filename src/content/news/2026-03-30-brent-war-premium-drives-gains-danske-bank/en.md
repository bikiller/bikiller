Brent crude prices extended gains in early Asian trading, hovering near 115 USD per barrel, as heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East injected a fresh war premium into energy markets. Analysts at  note that supply risk concerns are dominating sentiment, driving aggressive positioning across commodities and influencing broader financial market trading dynamics.

The move in oil has fed directly into forex markets, where the USD has shown mixed performance against major currency pairs. Elevated energy costs are supporting oil-linked currencies while weighing on import-heavy economies, impacting exchange rate fluctuations in pairs such as EUR/USD. Traders are increasingly factoring geopolitical risk into pricing, alongside shifting expectations for US policy signals.

Looking ahead, sustained strength in Brent could reinforce inflationary pressures globally, complicating central bank outlooks and FX trading strategies. A prolonged war premium may keep volatility elevated across both commodity and currency markets, with traders closely monitoring key technical levels and macro signals. The interplay between oil prices and USD demand remains a central theme shaping near-term exchange rate movements.
