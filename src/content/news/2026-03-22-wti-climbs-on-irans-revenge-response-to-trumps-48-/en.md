West Texas Intermediate (WTI) surged Monday, building on Friday’s 3.5% gain to briefly reclaim the $100 per barrel mark. The rally comes after Iran signaled retaliatory action in response to former President Trump’s 48-hour ultimatum, stoking geopolitical tension and driving safe-haven flows. Crude futures trading saw increased volatility as investors priced in potential supply disruptions, with market participants closely watching USD strength amid rising oil prices.  

Currency markets reflected the oil-driven sentiment, with the USD showing modest gains against major pairs. The EUR/USD exchange rate dipped below key technical support at 1.0950, while USD/JPY trading held near 135.00, reflecting a combination of risk aversion and anticipation of central bank commentary. Traders noted that energy-linked currencies, including the CAD, benefited from the move, with USD/CAD easing to 1.3400 as crude climbed.  

Analysts suggest the rally could influence broader forex and commodities trading this week. WTI’s push past $100 may heighten inflation expectations, impacting central bank outlooks and USD positioning. Market participants are eyeing technical levels in both oil and currency pairs, balancing geopolitical risk with economic data, as volatility could persist until clarity emerges on Iran’s next moves and their effect on global energy supply.
