BNP Paribas analysts project a gradual decline in the USD as U.S. economic growth slows in 2026. They forecast GDP growth at 2.9% and inflation at 3.0%, suggesting a stable economic environment. With three rate cuts anticipated in 2025, the Federal Reserve is expected to maintain a target range of 3.5%-3.75% through 2026, which may exert downward pressure on the USD.

As a result, currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY could face moderate declines in the coming years. With inflationary pressures easing and the Fed's dovish stance, traders may look for opportunities to capitalize on the USD's weakening, while the EUR and other currencies may see improved exchange rates. Market participants will closely monitor U.S. economic data for further indications of slower growth and its effects on monetary policy.
