Brent oil prices have dropped to approximately $85 per barrel, as the market reacts to the U.S. administration's consideration of various policy measures to manage the rising costs of oil and gasoline amidst the ongoing tensions in Iran. This shift comes after a recent surge in crude prices, fueled by supply concerns and geopolitical instability. 

The potential policy interventions are aimed at tempering further inflationary pressure, which has already affected global markets. As traders continue to assess the U.S. dollar’s strength, especially against the EUR and other major currencies, the market remains sensitive to any news that may influence oil prices. Investors are closely watching exchange rates, with fluctuations in the USD likely to impact commodity-based assets.
