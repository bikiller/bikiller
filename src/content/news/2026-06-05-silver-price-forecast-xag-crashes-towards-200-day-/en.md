Silver prices plunged sharply this week, testing the 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) near $67.79 as the white metal fell by nearly 8%. The sell-off accelerated after a stronger-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls report, which bolstered the US dollar and pressured commodities. XAG/USD traders are now closely watching the $61.00 level as a potential next support amid heightened volatility in currency pairs involving the USD and EUR.  

The decline reflects broader market dynamics, with the US dollar strengthening across major currency pairs, prompting a shift in trading flows away from risk-sensitive assets like silver. EUR/USD and other dollar-linked pairs also reacted to the robust employment data, highlighting the interconnection between forex markets and precious metals. Investors appear cautious, balancing inflation concerns with higher yields in the USD.  

Technical analysts note that if XAG breaks below the 200-day SMA convincingly, a further slide toward $61.00 could unfold, intensifying bearish momentum. Traders are factoring in both macroeconomic indicators and intraday exchange rate movements, adjusting positions in silver futures and forex portfolios. This week’s 10% weekly loss underscores the sensitivity of silver to USD strength and macroeconomic surprises.
