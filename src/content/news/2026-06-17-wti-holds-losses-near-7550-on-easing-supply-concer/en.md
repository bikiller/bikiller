West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude continues to trade under pressure, holding losses for a fifth consecutive session as it hovers near $75.60 per barrel during Wednesday’s European trading hours. The currency pair dynamics in commodity-linked markets reflect broader USD strength, which has added weight to dollar-denominated assets such as crude oil.

The weakness in oil prices is largely driven by easing supply concerns, as investors reassess the potential impact of a developing US–Iran peace agreement. Improved geopolitical sentiment has reduced fears of supply disruptions, prompting traders to unwind previous risk premiums embedded in the exchange rate of energy commodities.

Market participants are closely monitoring whether WTI can stabilize around the $75.50 support zone, as further downside could accelerate selling pressure. In forex and commodity-linked trading, fluctuations in USD demand continue to influence pricing momentum, with oil-sensitive currencies such as CAD also likely to react to sustained movements in crude benchmarks.
