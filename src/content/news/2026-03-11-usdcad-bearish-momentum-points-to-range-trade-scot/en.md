The USD/CAD exchange rate remains under bearish pressure, with the Canadian Dollar continuing its consolidation phase against the U.S. Dollar. Scotiabank strategists, Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret, point to narrowing interest rate differentials and rising oil prices as key drivers behind the CAD's resilience. As these factors persist, the Canadian Dollar is likely to hold its ground.

With this backdrop, traders can expect the USD/CAD pair to trade within a defined range. The ongoing strength of oil and the narrowing gap between U.S. and Canadian interest rates could prevent significant movement in either direction. For now, the outlook suggests further range-bound trading, with the pair remaining susceptible to shifts in commodity prices and any adjustments to central bank policies.
