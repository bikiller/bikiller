The Indian Rupee (INR) continued its downward slide against the US Dollar (USD), marking the fifth consecutive session of losses. The USD/INR pair surged to a record high of 92.58 during early trading on Wednesday, driven by rising oil prices and heightened risk aversion in global markets. The rupee’s weakness comes amid concerns over inflationary pressures and potential tightening in the US.

Oil price hikes have further strained the INR, as India relies heavily on imports for its energy needs, making the currency more vulnerable to global fluctuations. Additionally, growing risk aversion among investors, triggered by uncertainties in global markets, has boosted demand for the USD as a safe-haven currency. As the USD/INR pair hits new highs, traders will closely monitor any policy shifts from the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) and the Federal Reserve, which could influence the future direction of the exchange rate.
