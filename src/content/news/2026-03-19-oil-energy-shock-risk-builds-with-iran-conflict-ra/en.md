Brent crude and European natural gas (TTF) surged sharply after escalating tensions in the Middle East. Rabobank’s Senior Global Strategist Michael Every noted that Israel, in coordination with the US, struck Iran’s largest gas field, prompting Iran to retaliate against GCC energy assets. The geopolitical flare-up has heightened the risk of a broader energy shock, sending oil prices up and intensifying volatility across commodity-linked currency pairs.  

The energy-driven market jitters are spilling over into forex trading, with the USD showing relative strength as investors seek safe-haven assets. EUR/USD has faced pressure, dipping below key support levels, while commodity-linked currencies such as NOK and CAD are tracking oil and gas price swings closely. Traders are adjusting positions amid rising Brent and TTF benchmarks, anticipating further volatility in exchange rates tied to global energy markets.  

Analysts warn that continued conflict could sustain elevated energy prices, fueling inflationary concerns and impacting central bank policy expectations. Currency pairs sensitive to commodities and geopolitical risk may see increased trading ranges, while USD demand remains resilient. Market participants are advised to monitor developments closely, as any escalation could reverberate through both oil markets and the broader forex landscape, shaping short-term and medium-term trading strategies.
