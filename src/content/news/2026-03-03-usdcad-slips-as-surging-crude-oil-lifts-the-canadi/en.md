The USD/CAD currency pair saw a slight decline of about 0.1% on Tuesday, dropping to around 1.3660. This followed Monday's US Dollar strength, which failed to sustain momentum. The pair has been on a downward trend since reaching the January highs near 1.3930, with the exchange rate consistently posting lower highs and lower lows.

A key driver behind this movement is the surge in crude oil prices, which is bolstering the Canadian Dollar. As oil prices rise, the Canadian economy benefits due to its strong ties to energy exports, pushing the CAD higher against the USD. Traders are watching closely for further oil price developments, which could dictate the future direction of USD/CAD.
