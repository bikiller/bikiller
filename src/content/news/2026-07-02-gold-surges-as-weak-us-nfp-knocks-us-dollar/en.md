Gold (XAU/USD) surged more than 2% on Thursday after the latest US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report came in weaker than expected, reducing expectations of further Federal Reserve rate hikes. The softer labor data pressured the US Dollar (USD) and US Treasury yields, boosting demand for safe-haven assets. The currency pair trades at $4,111 after rebounding from daily lows near $4,032.

Market sentiment shifted firmly in favor of bullion as traders reassessed interest rate expectations and recalibrated USD positioning. The drop in US yields weakened the US Dollar further, supporting broader forex flows into risk-sensitive currencies and commodities. In the short term, XAU/USD may remain volatile, with traders watching key resistance around recent highs and support near the $4,032 level.

Investors will continue monitoring upcoming US data releases and Federal Reserve commentary for fresh direction, as expectations around policy easing or tightening remain highly sensitive to labor market signals. Any further softness in economic indicators could extend the US Dollar weakness and provide additional upside momentum for gold in the forex market.
