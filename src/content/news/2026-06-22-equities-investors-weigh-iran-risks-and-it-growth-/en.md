Global equities declined on Friday, with European markets leading losses as renewed geopolitical concerns over Iran and stalled diplomatic talks dampened risk appetite. According to Danske Research Team, the shift triggered a defensive rotation across sectors, while currency markets saw cautious trading in major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and USD strengthening as safe-haven demand increased.

Technology-related equities showing relative resilience from IT growth trends, but broader indices still under pressure as investors rotate toward defensive assets. The forex market reflected mixed sentiment, with USD firming against the EUR and other majors, while exchange rates remained volatile amid shifting risk appetite and uncertainty around Middle East tensions and global growth outlook.

Outlook suggests continued caution in trading activity, with forex participants closely monitoring USD strength and EUR exchange rate movements as risk sentiment remains fragile. Equity markets may stay defensive if geopolitical tensions persist, influencing capital flows and supporting safe-haven demand. Investors are likely to maintain hedged positions across currency pairs while awaiting clearer signals on Iran-related developments.
