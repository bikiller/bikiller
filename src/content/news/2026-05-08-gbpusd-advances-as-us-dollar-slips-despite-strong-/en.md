The GBP/USD currency pair edged higher on Friday, trading around 1.3630 at the time of writing, marking a daily gain of 0.54%. The Pound showed resilience as the US Dollar softened, despite the release of a strong US nonfarm payrolls (NFP) report that pointed to continued US labor market strength. Traders appeared to favor the British currency amid broader USD weakness across major pairs.  

Market participants noted that the US Dollar’s retreat pressured key exchange rates, with GBP/USD climbing as investors reassessed short-term US economic momentum. The robust NFP figures, while signaling healthy employment growth, were overshadowed by a softer-than-expected performance in other USD-sensitive sectors, prompting traders to take profits on long-dollar positions. This dynamic supported a rebound in the Pound against the backdrop of mixed global risk sentiment.  

Technically, GBP/USD’s advance toward 1.3630 reflects ongoing demand for the British currency, with resistance levels near 1.3650 likely under scrutiny. Traders are closely monitoring upcoming US economic data, as further signs of USD vulnerability could fuel additional gains for GBP, while shifts in risk appetite may also influence EUR and other major currency pairs. The pair’s current trading momentum suggests heightened short-term activity for forex participants.
