USD/JPY edged slightly lower on Monday, trading around 159.60 during Asian hours after four consecutive days of losses. Despite the pullback, the currency pair maintains a firm footing above the 159.50 support level, signaling ongoing interest from traders. The modest retreat comes amid mixed risk sentiment in global markets, with investors weighing U.S. economic data against broader equity trends.  

Technical analysis highlights a persistent bullish bias, as USD/JPY continues to track within a well-defined ascending channel on the daily chart. Momentum indicators suggest that buyers remain in control, keeping the exchange rate poised for potential upside. Any breach below 159.50 could invite short-term corrective pressure, but the overall trend favors continued gains in the medium term.  

Traders are closely monitoring factors influencing USD strength, including U.S. inflation expectations and Treasury yields, while EUR and other major currencies provide context for comparative moves. With the pair holding above key support, market participants anticipate further testing of resistance levels, making USD/JPY a focal point for both carry trades and short-term trading strategies in the global forex landscape.
