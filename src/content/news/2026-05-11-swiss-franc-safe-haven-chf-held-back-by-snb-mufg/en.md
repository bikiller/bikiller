The Swiss Franc (CHF) has shown restrained performance this week, underperforming against both the USD and EUR. Traders note that the currency pair USD/CHF has hovered near 0.9300, while EUR/CHF remains around 0.9850. MUFG analysts highlight that the Swiss National Bank (SNB) continues to lean against CHF appreciation, signaling a willingness to act if the franc strengthens too rapidly. This stance dampens CHF demand despite its traditional safe-haven appeal.

Market participants point to the SNB's subdued reaction to recent inflation trends as another factor limiting CHF gains. With inflation figures running below market expectations, the central bank is emphasizing stability over aggressive tightening. As a result, CHF trading has been marked by muted volatility, with positions in USD/CHF and EUR/CHF showing limited directional momentum. Investors appear cautious, weighing safe-haven flows against central bank signals.

Looking ahead, MUFG economists suggest that CHF movements will remain sensitive to SNB guidance and broader market risk sentiment. Should global uncertainty spike, CHF could attract renewed buying, but persistent SNB interventions may cap gains. Currency traders are closely monitoring key technical levels, including 0.9300 in USD/CHF and 0.9850 in EUR/CHF, as potential pivot points for short-term trading strategies. The interplay between safe-haven demand and policy restraint continues to shape CHF dynamics.
