The USD/JPY currency pair inched lower for a second consecutive day on Tuesday, slipping less than 0.1% to close near 158.90. Trading was muted, with investors adopting a cautious stance ahead of key central bank decisions from the Federal Reserve and the Bank of Japan. The exchange rate showed little directional momentum, reflecting a wait-and-see mood as market participants weighed potential policy shifts.  

Traders noted that despite the minor decline, USD liquidity remained steady against the yen, while broader forex markets exhibited limited volatility. The slight pullback came amid thin trading conditions, as investors considered interest rate expectations and their impact on the USD and other major currencies such as the EUR. Technical levels around 158.90 are being closely monitored, as a break could signal more pronounced moves in either direction.  

Market strategists suggested the subdued session underscores how traders are positioning ahead of the Fed and BoJ meetings. Any surprises in policy statements or economic guidance could trigger sharper swings in the USD/JPY exchange rate. For now, the currency pair continues to trade in a tight range, with cautious USD buyers and sellers awaiting clarity on monetary policy direction.
