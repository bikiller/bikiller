The Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) struggled to hold gains on Tuesday, hovering near 50,750 in European afternoon trading after an overnight rebound from a low around 50,500. The index briefly tested the 51,000 level before reversing, weighed down by mixed tech sector performance despite a chip stock rally. Traders are closely watching U.S. CPI data, which could disrupt sentiment and influence USD exchange rates across major currency pairs.  

In forex markets, the dollar showed slight strength against the euro, with the EUR/USD pair testing 1.0940 amid cautious risk appetite. Investors weighed the potential for tighter monetary policy against lingering inflation pressures, while stock market indecision kept trading volumes subdued. Technical levels in both equities and currency pairs are being closely monitored, as any surprises from CPI could trigger sharp moves in USD, JPY, and other liquid currencies.  

Market participants highlighted the correlation between equities and forex, noting that chip stocks’ volatility and broader index fluctuations directly impact USD sentiment. A softer CPI could ease pressure on the greenback, while stronger readings may reinforce demand for safe-haven currencies. Traders remain positioned ahead of the data release, with short-term strategies reflecting a mix of risk-on and risk-off sentiment across both FX and equity markets.
