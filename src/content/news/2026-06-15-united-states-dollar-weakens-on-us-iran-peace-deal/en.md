US Dollar trades weaker against major currencies on Monday, as improved global risk sentiment reduces demand for safe-haven assets. The move follows news that the United States and Iran have agreed on a framework peace deal aimed at reopening the Strait of Hormuz, a key shipping route. In FX trading, currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD climbed modestly as the USD softened across exchange rates.

However, downside pressure on the US Dollar remains contained as expectations for a hawkish Federal Reserve continue to support yield differentials. Traders are trimming short-term positions but still price in relatively high interest rates for longer, which keeps USD demand resilient. The exchange rate reaction across majors remains measured, with forex trading activity focused on rate expectations rather than geopolitical headlines.

Looking ahead, market participants expect the US Dollar to remain sensitive to both geopolitical developments and central bank guidance. If risk appetite continues to improve, major currency pairs could see further USD weakness in the near term. However, any shift in Federal Reserve rhetoric could quickly stabilize the currency, keeping trading ranges relatively tight across EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and other FX markets.
