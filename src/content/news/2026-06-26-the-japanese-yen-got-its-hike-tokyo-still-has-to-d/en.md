USD/JPY ended the week pressing the 162.00 handle, sitting just beneath its multi-decade high as the exchange rate kept the currency pair firmly bid. Trading activity reflected continued USD strength against the Japanese Yen, even after expectations that a Bank of Japan rate hike would provide meaningful relief to the yen. Broader forex sentiment also kept EUR pairs relatively stable, underscoring divergent central bank paths.

Although the BoJ rate hike was positioned as a turning point for policy normalization, its immediate impact on the USD/JPY exchange rate remained limited, with traders viewing it as largely priced in. Carry-driven trading strategies and persistent USD demand kept the currency pair elevated near resistance, while volatility in broader forex trading stayed contained despite shifting expectations.

Looking ahead, Tokyo still has to do the rest if it wants to meaningfully alter the currency trajectory, as USD/JPY hovers close to the 162.00 level that remains a key technical barrier. Market participants continue to monitor potential intervention risks, while EUR and USD dynamics across major currency pairs suggest a still-differentiated global rate environment.
