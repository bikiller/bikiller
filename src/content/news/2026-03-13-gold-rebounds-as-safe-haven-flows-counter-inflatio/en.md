Gold (XAU/USD) staged a modest recovery in early Asian trading on Friday, clawing back part of the losses it suffered over the previous two sessions. The safe-haven metal benefited from renewed risk aversion, as investors weighed lingering inflation concerns against expectations of further Federal Reserve rate hikes. At the time of writing, gold was trading around $1,985 per ounce, reflecting a rebound of roughly 0.6% from its recent low near $1,973.  

The uptick in gold came amid a slightly softer USD, which eased pressure on the XAU/USD currency pair. Traders noted that the EUR/USD exchange rate showed mild gains, hovering near 1.1025, as European markets digested mixed economic data. Market participants suggested that a cautious stance in equity markets and geopolitical uncertainty continued to drive flows into precious metals, highlighting gold’s role as a hedge against both currency volatility and inflation risks.  

Technically, analysts pointed to resistance near $1,995 and support around $1,975 as key levels for short-term trading. A sustained move above $2,000 could signal renewed bullish momentum, while a drop below $1,975 might attract further selling. Overall, the interplay between USD strength, central bank policy expectations, and global market sentiment remains central to gold’s near-term direction, keeping traders alert to rapid shifts in exchange rates and cross-asset flows.
