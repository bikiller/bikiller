West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil is trading at $86.30, reflecting a 1.20% increase on Wednesday. The gains come amid rising tensions in the Middle East, where geopolitical instability has raised concerns about global oil supply. Traders are closely monitoring the situation as it influences energy markets, especially as central banks and governments explore measures to ensure stability.

As WTI prices climb, market participants are also looking toward potential interventions from the G7 and the International Energy Agency (IEA). Both are considering actions such as tapping into strategic reserves to mitigate the impact on global energy prices. These developments could influence the USD, as fluctuations in oil prices often impact broader market sentiment, including the exchange rates of key currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY.
