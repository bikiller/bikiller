The USD/CHF currency pair slipped further on Wednesday, dipping below the critical 0.7800 support level as selling pressure intensified. At the time of writing, the exchange rate trades near 0.7789, marking a decline of over 0.50%. Traders have closely watched this breach, signaling that the downtrend may continue toward the March 10 swing low at 0.7748. Market participants are assessing broader USD sentiment as risk appetite influences safe-haven flows into the Swiss franc.  

Momentum in the forex market reflects ongoing USD weakness against major currencies, including the EUR and JPY, amid mixed economic data. Technical analysts highlight the broken trendline as a key indicator, suggesting bears are gaining control. Short-term traders are recalibrating their positions, eyeing potential support levels and considering how global interest rate expectations and geopolitical developments may impact further USD/CHF movements.  

If the pair holds below 0.7800, downside targets remain firmly at 0.7750 and potentially the March low at 0.7748. Conversely, any rebound above the breached trendline could prompt temporary USD strength, though the prevailing sentiment favors continued selling. The current exchange rate dynamics underscore the importance of monitoring both technical signals and macroeconomic drivers that influence USD performance across global forex markets.
