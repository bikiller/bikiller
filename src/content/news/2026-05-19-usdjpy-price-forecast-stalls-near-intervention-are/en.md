USD/JPY extended its rally for a seventh consecutive trading day, with the currency pair rising 0.10% to a 12-day high of 159.25 before easing back toward 159.00. The move keeps the exchange rate close to the psychologically important 160.00 level, a zone closely watched by forex traders because of previous concerns over Japanese intervention.

The USD remains supported by bullish momentum, while the yen continues to struggle as markets assess the policy gap between the Federal Reserve and the Bank of Japan. However, the pair’s advance near the 159.00–160.00 area may become more cautious, as Japanese authorities have repeatedly signaled discomfort with excessive FX volatility and rapid yen weakness.

From a technical perspective, buyers remain focused on a potential test of 160.00, while immediate trading support is seen around 159.00 after the pullback from 159.25. A sustained break above 160.00 could reinforce bullish sentiment in USD/JPY, but failure to clear that level may trigger profit-taking, especially if intervention risks intensify.
