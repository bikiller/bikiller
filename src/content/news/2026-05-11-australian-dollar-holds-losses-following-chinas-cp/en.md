The Australian Dollar held onto earlier losses against the US Dollar on Monday, with the AUD/USD currency pair trading around 0.7240 during Asian session hours. The pair had opened with a bearish gap but managed to recover slightly, showing limited upside despite fresh economic data from China. Traders remained cautious as the exchange rate struggled to gain momentum, reflecting a mix of risk sentiment and regional inflation trends.  

China’s Consumer Price Index (CPI) for April came in stronger than expected, raising concerns about potential tightening measures from Beijing. While such data typically supports risk-sensitive currencies like the AUD, the impact on the AUD/USD remained muted. Market participants noted that the exchange rate continues to face resistance near 0.7250, suggesting that short-term gains may be capped unless global risk appetite improves.  

Investors are closely watching how US Dollar strength interacts with regional developments. With EUR/USD and other major pairs also trading in tight ranges, the Australian Dollar’s movement underscores a cautious trading environment. Analysts suggest that any significant shift in China’s inflation trajectory or USD dynamics could quickly influence the AUD/USD, highlighting the sensitivity of currency pairs to both macroeconomic indicators and broader market sentiment.
