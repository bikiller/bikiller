The EUR/JPY currency pair edged higher on Tuesday, propelled by a weaker Japanese Yen. Trading at 184.47 at the time of writing, the pair extended gains for a second consecutive session. Market participants are eyeing March’s highs around 184.65-184.75, as the Euro benefits from broad USD strength and persistent Yen softness. Traders cite technical momentum and risk sentiment as key drivers behind the steady uptrend.  

Analysts note that the Euro’s resilience against the Yen reflects both domestic stability in the Eurozone and ongoing pressure on Japan’s currency from low interest rates. With the USD also influencing flows, EUR/JPY traders are closely watching whether the pair can breach the March peak. Breakouts above 184.75 could signal further upside, while any retracement might attract short-term corrections around the 184.20-184.30 support range.  

Market implications remain significant for cross-currency trading, as a sustained rally in EUR/JPY could reshape positioning across Asian and European sessions. Investors and forex traders alike are monitoring both technical levels and macro cues, including USD performance, Eurozone economic indicators, and Bank of Japan policy signals. A move above the March high would reinforce bullish sentiment, potentially driving further capital into Euro-denominated assets.
