The Indian Rupee (INR) continues its downward trajectory against the US Dollar (USD), marking the fifth consecutive session of losses. Amid heightened safe-haven demand, the USD/INR currency pair soared to a new record high of 92.67 during Asian trading hours on Wednesday. This surge reflects ongoing market concerns that favor the stability of the US currency.

Traders are monitoring the implications of this exchange rate movement closely, as the sustained strength of the USD may pressure the RBI to implement measures to support the INR. The prevailing sentiment across the forex market indicates that investors are gravitating towards safer assets, which could further influence the dynamics of the USD/INR pair in the coming days.
