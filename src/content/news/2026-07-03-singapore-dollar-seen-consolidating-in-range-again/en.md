The Singapore Dollar is expected to consolidate within a range against the USD, according to Commerzbank, as supportive domestic data offsets external pressures. Strong manufacturing and electronics PMIs continue to point to resilient economic momentum, reinforcing a constructive growth outlook. Market participants anticipate Q2 GDP growth could surpass Q1’s robust 6% expansion, underpinning confidence in the SGD’s medium-term trajectory.

In forex trading, the USD/SGD currency pair has edged slightly lower in recent sessions, although the exchange rate remains close to its highs for the year. This suggests that while the USD retains underlying strength, the Singapore Dollar is finding support from improving macroeconomic fundamentals. Investors are balancing expectations of US monetary policy with Asia’s growth dynamics.

Looking ahead, range-bound trading conditions may persist as traders assess incoming data and central bank signals. The interplay between USD strength and Singapore’s growth resilience will likely keep USD/SGD within a defined band, with limited directional conviction in the near term.
