The Canadian Dollar (CAD) saw strong gains against most major currencies during Thursday's European trading session, with the exception of the US Dollar (USD). The CAD benefitted from rising oil prices, which surged following a speech by former President Donald Trump. The USD/CAD pair remained relatively stable, trading at 1.3650, while other crosses like EUR/CAD and GBP/CAD saw the Canadian Dollar push higher.  

The rally in oil prices, which is crucial to Canada's economy, boosted investor sentiment in the loonie, with oil futures reaching $72 per barrel. This upward momentum provided CAD with support across the board, despite the broader market concerns over global inflation. The CAD's strength against the euro, currently trading at 1.49, suggests that investors remain confident in its performance amid global uncertainties. However, the USD/CAD pair's resistance around the 1.37 level suggests that the US Dollar's dominance still plays a key role in dictating market sentiment.
