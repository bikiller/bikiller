EUR/USD trading has seen limited upside this week as surveys and the European Central Bank’s cautious stance weigh on the currency pair. ING’s Chris Turner notes that a busy schedule of ECB speakers ahead of the blackout period underscored policymakers’ readiness to hike rates if necessary, while signalling a preference to wait for more economic clarity. Investors have pulled forward expectations for a 30 April move, leaving the market with roughly 50% odds of a June rate hike.  

The exchange rate remains under pressure from mixed eurozone data and persistent USD strength. Market participants are carefully watching ECB commentary, interpreting measured hawkish signals as a signal that the central bank may hold steady in the near term. Trading activity reflects a balance between speculation on upcoming policy decisions and underlying eurozone economic indicators, keeping EUR/USD confined to a tight range despite intermittent rallies.  

Technical levels are increasingly relevant as traders weigh positions ahead of June. The EUR/USD pair is consolidating around key support and resistance zones, with momentum indicators showing subdued volatility. Analysts at ING expect that if inflation and growth data support the ECB’s hawkish bias, the currency pair could test higher levels later in the quarter, though near-term trading may remain restrained, reflecting both the uncertainty of policy timing and broader market caution.
