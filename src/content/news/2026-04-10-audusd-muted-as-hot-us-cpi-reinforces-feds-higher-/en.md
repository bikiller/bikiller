The AUD/USD currency pair held steady on Friday, edging slightly lower after a four-day rally, as traders weighed a hot US CPI report that reinforced expectations of a “higher-for-longer” Federal Reserve policy. The pair traded around 0.6765, showing caution after surging earlier in the week amid stronger risk appetite and modest gains in commodity-linked currencies. Market participants remained alert to shifts in US inflation data, which continue to support the dollar.  

Traders noted that the robust CPI reading, highlighting persistent inflation pressures, fueled demand for USD assets and kept the exchange rate for AUD/USD under pressure. The Australian dollar struggled to reclaim earlier gains despite upbeat commodity prices, with traders monitoring support near 0.6750 and resistance around 0.6780. The broader forex market showed mixed movements, with EUR/USD hovering near 1.0950 as European data and Fed policy expectations influenced trading sentiment.  

Geopolitical tensions added further caution, with investors reluctant to push the AUD/USD sharply higher. Market watchers emphasized that any deviation in US economic data could quickly shift sentiment, making technical levels crucial for intraday trading. Analysts suggest that traders maintain a balanced approach, keeping an eye on both US dollar strength and Australian economic developments, as the currency pair remains sensitive to inflation signals and central bank guidance.
