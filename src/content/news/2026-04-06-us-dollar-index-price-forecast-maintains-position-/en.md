The US Dollar Index (DXY) is holding firm above the 100.00 mark, currently trading around 100.10 in early Asian session activity on Monday. After two consecutive days of gains, the index has edged slightly lower, hovering near its nine-day exponential moving average (EMA). Traders remain focused on the USD's strength against major currencies, including the EUR, JPY, GBP, CAD, SEK, and CHF, as market participants weigh upcoming economic data and central bank signals.  

Currency pairs involving the USD have shown mixed performance amid this cautious consolidation. The EUR/USD is testing nearby support levels, while USD/JPY and GBP/USD maintain moderate upside momentum. Analysts note that the DXY's proximity to key technical levels, such as the 100.00 psychological threshold and short-term EMA, could influence intraday trading decisions and guide forex market sentiment in the near term.  

Market participants are assessing the broader implications for exchange rates as volatility remains subdued. Continued resilience in the USD may pressure commodity-linked currencies, while a pullback could offer relief for the EUR and other majors. Traders are watching global risk sentiment, economic releases, and monetary policy cues closely, as these factors are expected to shape USD trading ranges and inform strategic positions in major currency pairs.
