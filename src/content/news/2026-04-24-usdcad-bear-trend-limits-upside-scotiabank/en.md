USD/CAD is little changed as the Canadian dollar holds steady, with the currency pair retreating from repeated tests of the low 1.37 area, according to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret. The exchange rate shows limited follow-through after failing to break lower, reflecting cautious trading conditions in the broader forex market.

According to the bank, the prevailing bear trend continues to cap upside momentum for USD/CAD, limiting sustained advances despite periodic rebounds. Resistance around the 1.37 handle has repeatedly held, while downside attempts have been contained. Overall, price action suggests a market struggling to establish clear direction amid balanced USD and CAD fundamentals.

For traders, the pair remains in a narrow trading range, with forex participants closely watching whether USD/CAD can break decisively beyond the 1.37 zone. Until then, short-term strategies may favor range trading rather than trend-following approaches, as macro drivers for USD and Canadian dollar remain largely offsetting in current conditions.
