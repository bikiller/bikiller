The ongoing conflict in Iran is driving an oil shock, adding complexity to the Federal Reserve's monetary policy decisions, according to TD Securities strategists Oscar Munoz and Eli Nir. The surge in oil prices complicates the central bank’s rate path, with inflationary pressures rising due to higher energy costs. This could force the Fed to take a more cautious stance in the coming months, as market uncertainty intensifies.

In forex markets, the US Dollar (USD) is seeing mixed reactions. The USD/EUR exchange rate remains volatile, with the euro trading around 1.0900, down 0.3% on the day. Meanwhile, oil's rally pushes the USD higher against commodity-linked currencies like the Canadian Dollar (CAD), with USD/CAD testing key resistance near 1.3800. Traders are closely monitoring the Fed’s next move, as oil price fluctuations continue to shape market sentiment.
