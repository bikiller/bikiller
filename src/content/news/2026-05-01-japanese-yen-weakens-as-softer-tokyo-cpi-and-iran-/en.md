The Japanese Yen (JPY) weakened as the USD/JPY pair extended its rebound from the previous day’s low, around the mid-155.00s, a level not seen in over two months. During the Asian session on Friday, the pair saw positive movement, driven by a combination of factors. Softening Tokyo Consumer Price Index (CPI) data and rising tensions surrounding Iran contributed to the Yen's decline, overshadowing earlier warnings of potential intervention from Japanese authorities.

The currency pair gained traction as market participants weighed the potential implications of the softer CPI, which dampened expectations of aggressive tightening from the Bank of Japan. Meanwhile, concerns over geopolitical risks, especially related to Iran, added pressure on the Yen. As the USD/JPY pair held above the critical mid-155.00s level, traders remained focused on potential volatility ahead, with the exchange rate’s next moves likely influenced by upcoming economic data and global developments.
