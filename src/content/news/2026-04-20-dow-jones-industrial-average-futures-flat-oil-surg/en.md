Dow Jones Industrial Average (DJIA) futures opened lower on Sunday, dipping toward the 49,100 level amid cautious trading sentiment, but steadily recovered through the US session to close just shy of Friday’s 49,400 mark. Investors weighed geopolitical tensions and earnings reports, keeping trading volumes moderate as major indices tested recent support levels. The USD broadly held steady, while safe-haven flows bolstered demand for the Japanese yen and Swiss franc against the dollar.  

Crude oil prices surged roughly 5% following renewed escalation between the US and Iran, fueling concerns over Middle East supply disruptions. Brent and WTI futures climbed sharply, sending energy-linked currencies like the Canadian dollar higher. Forex traders reacted swiftly, adjusting EUR/USD and GBP/USD positions, with the euro easing slightly against the USD as risk sentiment wavered. Commodity-linked currencies reflected heightened volatility amid the geopolitical backdrop.  

Market participants remain alert to further developments, as oil-driven inflation pressures could influence Federal Reserve policy expectations. USD/JPY and USD/CHF exchange rates showed modest gains on safe-haven demand, while EUR/USD hovered near key technical levels. Analysts note that sustained geopolitical tensions could drive short-term currency swings, offering active trading opportunities across major pairs while equity indices balance risk and reward.
