Spot gold has surged above $5,000, continuing its parabolic uptrend since late January. After initially rising from the $4,600 range, the precious metal reached a record high of $5,598.25. This aggressive rally marked a notable shift in market sentiment, driven by safe-haven demand and concerns over global economic stability. 

However, gold faced a sharp reversal in late January, erasing nearly $1,000 in value in the final days of the month. The pullback highlights the volatility in precious metals markets and raises questions about the sustainability of the recent rally. Traders are now watching key technical levels closely, with $5,000 serving as a psychological support level.
