The Indian Rupee (INR) is expected to remain under pressure as the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is anticipated to hold the repo rate at 5.25% in its upcoming monetary policy meeting. Kunal Kundu of Societe Generale suggests that the RBI will maintain a neutral stance, aiming for stability following recent shocks in oil prices and foreign exchange markets. The central bank’s decision reflects its cautious approach amid ongoing inflationary risks and external uncertainties.

In the forex markets, the INR remains sensitive to global market dynamics, including fluctuations in oil prices and the US dollar's movements. With the RBI focused on maintaining economic stability, the currency pair USD/INR could see limited movement around current levels, while traders closely monitor global risk sentiment. Any significant shifts in oil or FX markets may trigger volatility in the INR, with potential ripple effects on other emerging market currencies.
