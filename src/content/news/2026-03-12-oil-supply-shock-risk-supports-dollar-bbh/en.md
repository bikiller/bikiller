Brent crude briefly reclaimed the $100 per barrel mark following recent tanker attacks near Iraq and Dubai, sparking renewed stagflation fears. Brown Brothers Harriman’s Elias Haddad noted that this supply shock is bolstering the US Dollar, putting pressure on global bond markets and equities. Traders are closely watching energy-related risks, as rising oil costs often drive safe-haven demand for the USD, influencing currency pair dynamics across G10 and emerging markets.  

In forex trading, the surge in oil prices has supported the USD against major counterparts like the EUR and JPY. The EUR/USD exchange rate has been weighed down by higher energy costs, while USD/JPY continues to reflect a safe-haven preference amid geopolitical tension. Market participants are analyzing whether Brent’s rebound will sustain USD strength or if risk assets may recover once immediate supply concerns ease.  

Analysts highlight that technical levels in currency pairs are increasingly sensitive to commodity-driven shocks. With oil above $100, traders are monitoring whether the momentum can push USD index readings higher, while equities may see further pressure from elevated input costs. The interplay between energy markets and forex trading underscores the growing influence of geopolitical events on exchange rates and broader financial market flows.
