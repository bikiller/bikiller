Gold (XAU/USD) extended losses in forex trading, reversing sharply as the currency pair slid to two-day lows near $4,219. The move came as the US dollar strengthened broadly, pressuring precious metals and triggering heavy selling in spot gold markets. The exchange rate shift reflected renewed risk recalibration across global trading desks.

Sentiment turned hawkish after the US Federal Reserve, under new Chair Kevin Warsh, signaled expectations for tighter monetary policy as inflation remains above the 2% target. Traders adjusted US dollar (USD) positioning higher across forex markets, weighing on risk-sensitive assets and reinforcing bullish momentum in the greenback against majors like EUR/USD.

Looking ahead, forex trading sentiment remains tilted toward USD strength, keeping gold vulnerable to further downside pressure as investors reassess rate expectations. With the dollar dominating major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, gold’s role as a safe-haven asset is under strain, though volatility may persist around key macroeconomic data releases.
