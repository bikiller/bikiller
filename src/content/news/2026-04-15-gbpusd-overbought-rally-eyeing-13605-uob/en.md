The GBP/USD currency pair extended its rally, climbing to 1.3590 as traders pushed the exchange rate higher amid a sustained short-term overbought condition. According to United Overseas Bank strategists Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann, momentum suggests potential for further gains toward the 1.3605 level, reflecting continued buying interest in sterling against the USD. Market sentiment remains cautious, with traders eyeing near-term technical thresholds for guidance.  

Despite the positive momentum, UOB analysts note that the 1.3640 resistance level appears challenging to breach in the current trading environment. The pair’s advance has been supported by broad USD weakness and selective risk-on flows across global markets. With short-term indicators pointing to overextended conditions, traders may see limited room for aggressive moves, prompting some to consider partial profit-taking near key levels.  

Looking ahead, the GBP/USD outlook suggests a measured approach, balancing the possibility of a minor push higher against the likelihood of retracement from overbought territory. Market participants are closely monitoring related EUR/USD and USD index trends for additional context, as shifts in broader dollar sentiment could influence sterling’s trajectory. Trading strategies may hinge on technical levels, with 1.3605 a near-term target and 1.3640 serving as a more distant barrier.
