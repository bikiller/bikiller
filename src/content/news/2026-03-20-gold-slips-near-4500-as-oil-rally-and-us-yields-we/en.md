Gold (XAU/USD) continued its downward trajectory on Friday, marking an eighth consecutive day of losses. The precious metal fell sharply, now trading near $4,500, with an expected weekly decline exceeding 8.50%. The rally in oil prices added to the pressure on gold, strengthening the appeal of the US Dollar as a safe haven in a volatile market. 

The rise in oil prices and higher US yields are the primary drivers of this decline, pulling investors away from gold and pushing the USD to dominate the trading landscape. As a result, gold’s safe-haven demand waned, further weakening its exchange rate against the USD. With the week’s losses, XAU/USD is set to finish well below previous support levels, reflecting broader market shifts.
