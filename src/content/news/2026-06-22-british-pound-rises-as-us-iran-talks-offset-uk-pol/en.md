Sterling advanced 0.14% on Monday as easing geopolitical tensions supported risk appetite in the foreign exchange market. The Pound Sterling gained after US-Iran negotiations were described as laying a “good foundation” by US Vice President JD Vance, following the first round of talks held in Switzerland. The development reduced immediate risk aversion across USD-denominated assets and lifted sentiment toward the British currency.

Currency pair GBP/USD saw modest buying interest as forex traders responded to improving global risk sentiment, with exchange rate dynamics favoring sterling against the US dollar. Trading activity reflected cautious repositioning in major FX markets, while demand for the pound also improved versus other majors, including the euro, amid reduced geopolitical stress and softer USD defensive flows.

Looking ahead, forex trading conditions for GBP remain sensitive to further developments in US-Iran diplomacy, with the USD likely to fluctuate as risk sentiment shifts. Market participants expect exchange rate volatility to persist across major currency pairs, including GBP/USD and EUR/USD, as geopolitical headlines continue to influence safe-haven demand and broader risk appetite in global markets.
