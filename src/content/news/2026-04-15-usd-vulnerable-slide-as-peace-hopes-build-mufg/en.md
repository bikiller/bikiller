The US Dollar is facing renewed selling pressure, with risk sentiment improving after President Trump’s remarks about resuming talks and the potential end of the ongoing war. The comments have spurred optimism, helping lift US equities to levels above pre-conflict figures. As a result, the USD is struggling, particularly against safe-haven currencies, with EUR/USD climbing to its highest levels in weeks, testing key resistance at 1.2300.

The renewed risk appetite has led to a weakening of the USD, particularly in the forex markets. As US Treasury yields remain subdued and global investors shift towards higher-risk assets, the currency’s upside momentum is stalling. Analysts are eyeing the 1.2300 level on EUR/USD, which could set the tone for further USD weakness if broken, signaling that peace hopes could continue to weigh on the greenback’s performance.
