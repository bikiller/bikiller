Gold (XAU/USD) is facing downward pressure, trading below the $4,700 mark during the Asian session on Friday, hovering near a two-week low established the previous day. The current market sentiment suggests that gold is on track to experience weekly losses for the first time in five weeks, as inflation fears and the ongoing US-Iran standoff bolster the strength of the USD.

The strength of the US dollar is contributing significantly to the decline in gold prices, as investors shift their focus to currency pairs like USD and EUR. The rising exchange rates are making gold less attractive as a safe-haven asset, highlighting the intricate relationship between geopolitical tensions and currency movements in the forex market. With these factors at play, traders should closely monitor upcoming developments that could impact the gold market and influence broader trading strategies.
