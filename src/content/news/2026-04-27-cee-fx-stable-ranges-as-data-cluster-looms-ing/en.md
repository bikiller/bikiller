Central and Eastern European currencies are expected to trade in relatively stable ranges this week as markets await a cluster of key economic data. ING strategist Frantisek Taborsky highlighted that despite recent swings in global rates and headline-driven volatility, CEE FX is likely to see limited directional movement. The EUR/PLN has been hovering around 4.65, while the USD/HUF remains near 365, reflecting cautious trading sentiment.  

Investors are closely watching upcoming releases, including inflation figures and central bank commentary, which could influence the exchange rate dynamics. EUR/CZK continues to hold just below 25.50, with technical support around 25.45. Traders are balancing global risk sentiment against domestic economic signals, keeping positions light and ranges tight. The interplay between USD strength and local monetary policy is expected to shape short-term moves across the region.  

Market participants are positioning ahead of potential shocks, with EUR/HUF trading near 400 and USD/PLN steady at 4.65. Any surprise in inflation or rate guidance could trigger sharper moves, though current expectations suggest subdued volatility. Liquidity may remain thin, creating opportunities for tactical trading, while broader CEE FX trends continue to mirror global USD and EUR movements, emphasizing the importance of monitoring both regional and international drivers.
