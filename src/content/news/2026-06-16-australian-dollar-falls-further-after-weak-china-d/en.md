The Australian Dollar (AUD) extended its decline during the Asian trading session on Tuesday, coming under renewed selling pressure against major currencies. The AUD/USD currency pair slipped 0.16% to trade near 0.7060, reflecting cautious risk sentiment across forex markets as traders reassessed regional growth prospects and broader USD strength environment.

The downside move was largely driven by weak economic data from China, Australia’s key trading partner, which dampened demand expectations for commodity-linked currencies. Softer Chinese indicators weighed on sentiment in the Asia-Pacific FX space, leaving the Australian Dollar vulnerable against the US Dollar and other majors such as EUR/USD cross movements.

The pair’s weakness highlights persistent sensitivity of AUD/USD to Chinese macroeconomic releases, with traders closely watching upcoming data for directional cues. In broader forex trading, sustained USD strength continues to cap upside attempts, potentially keeping the currency pair biased lower in the near term unless risk sentiment improves across global equity markets.
