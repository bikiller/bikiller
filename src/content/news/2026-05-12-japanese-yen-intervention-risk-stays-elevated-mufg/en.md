MUFG analyst Lee Hardman notes that the US Dollar has extended its rebound, supported by higher oil prices, pushing USD/JPY back toward 158.00. The pair is now trading close to levels seen before suspected Japanese intervention, highlighting renewed pressure on the Japanese yen in the forex market as USD strength dominates currency pair flows.

Broader currency trading dynamics show the USD benefiting from resilient US yields and risk-sensitive positioning, while rising oil prices add inflationary concerns that support the dollar. Against this backdrop, the yen remains under pressure across major currency pairs, with USD/JPY maintaining upward momentum as market participants reassess interest rate differentials and safe-haven demand.

Market participants continue to monitor the risk of further Japanese intervention should USD/JPY sustain moves above 158.00, a level previously associated with official concern. Persistent yen weakness could prompt heightened volatility in forex markets, with traders closely watching policy signals from Tokyo and the Bank of Japan amid ongoing divergence with US monetary policy.
