USD/JPY is showing a mild downside bias on Friday, trading lower despite a stronger-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report. The exchange rate remains capped, with the Japanese Yen (JPY) supported by persistent fears of potential intervention. As the USD retains strength across major currency pairs, the Yen’s resilience highlights the market's caution surrounding possible government action to curb the Yen's decline.

The USD continues to trade solidly against its major counterparts following the surprise in the US labor market data. However, with USD/JPY holding around 133.50, intervention risks are keeping upward momentum in check. Traders remain vigilant, balancing the robust US economic data with concerns about Japan's authorities taking measures to prevent further Yen depreciation, adding a layer of uncertainty to the pair’s future movements.
