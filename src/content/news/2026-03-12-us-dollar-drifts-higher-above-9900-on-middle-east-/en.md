The US Dollar Index (DXY) is trading at 99.35 during Thursday's Asian session, reflecting a steady increase in the greenback’s value. The rise follows heightened tensions in the Middle East, which have driven global market uncertainty. The DXY’s movement signals growing demand for safe-haven assets like the USD as geopolitical risks rise.

The escalation in the Middle East has put pressure on global markets, pushing traders to seek the relative stability of the US Dollar. This shift is visible across major currency pairs, with the EUR/USD dipping below key support levels, reflecting broader strength in USD trading. As the situation develops, the DXY’s rise could continue, potentially testing the 100.00 level in the coming sessions.
