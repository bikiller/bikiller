USD/JPY slipped on Friday as the Japanese Yen gained ground against a softer US Dollar, with the currency pair trading near 158.18, marking a 0.61% decline for the day. Traders cited a combination of easing oil prices and broad USD weakness as key drivers, with Japan’s reliance on imported energy amplifying the impact. Market participants monitored the exchange rate closely amid heightened risk sentiment.  

The fall in WTI crude added pressure on the USD/JPY, as lower oil costs reduce Japan’s import bill, supporting the Yen. Meanwhile, geopolitical developments, including Iran reopening the Strait of Hormuz, contributed to shifts in market positioning, prompting traders to adjust their currency exposure. Technical levels around 158.50 offered initial resistance, with momentum indicators suggesting further downside potential if the USD continues to lag.  

Investor attention also remained on broader forex dynamics, including USD performance against other major currencies like EUR and GBP. With the dollar showing signs of vulnerability, speculative flows favored safe-haven and commodity-linked currencies, driving the Yen higher. Analysts noted that continued volatility in energy markets and the USD could shape trading strategies for the USD/JPY pair in the coming sessions, highlighting the interplay between geopolitical news and macroeconomic sensitivities in forex markets.
