Gold extended its rally on Thursday, edging closer to the $4,800 level as the US Dollar softened against major currencies. The XAU/USD pair found support from broad USD weakness, with traders reacting to easing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The yellow metal’s advance coincided with a slight pullback in US Treasury yields, boosting gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset amid mixed risk sentiment in currency markets.  

Investor attention shifted after Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu signaled willingness to engage in talks with Lebanon, tempering regional risk and supporting risk-on trading in forex markets. The softer USD and subdued yields lifted demand for alternative stores of value, prompting higher gold prices and influencing related currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, as traders reassessed hedging strategies.  

Technically, XAU/USD is testing resistance near the $4,800 milestone, with momentum supported by the dollar’s exchange rate pressure and cautious positioning ahead of US economic data. Market watchers note that further dollar weakness or easing geopolitical tensions could reinforce gold’s rally, while a rebound in yields or stronger USD could cap upside. Traders remain focused on these dynamics for potential shifts in trading flows across commodities and currency pairs.
