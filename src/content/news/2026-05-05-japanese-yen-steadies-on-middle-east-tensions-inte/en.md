The USD/JPY currency pair held firm around 157.25 during Tuesday’s Asian trading session, as investors weighed geopolitical risks against potential central bank intervention. Recent tensions in the Middle East have pushed oil prices higher, raising concerns over global market stability and prompting cautious trading. The Japanese yen showed limited movement, reflecting a delicate balance between safe-haven demand and intervention expectations from the Bank of Japan.  

Market participants noted that the yen’s steadiness came despite heightened volatility in risk assets. Oil markets surged on reports of supply disruptions, indirectly affecting currency flows as traders reassessed USD exposure. The exchange rate remained anchored near key technical levels, with the 157.25 mark serving as a temporary pivot amid subdued liquidity in early trading hours.  

Analysts suggest that continued Middle East unrest could sustain elevated market caution, influencing both USD and EUR trading dynamics. While the yen has seen sporadic safe-haven inflows, intervention fears appear to cap sharp moves, keeping the currency pair in a narrow range. Traders are closely monitoring geopolitical headlines, anticipating that any escalation could trigger renewed volatility in Asian and global forex markets.
