The Japanese Yen weakened to a two-week low against the US Dollar on Friday as the USD/JPY currency pair climbed during the Asian session, extending its weekly uptrend. Traders appeared unconcerned by intervention risks, with the exchange rate touching fresh highs as a broadly stronger USD continued to dominate global forex markets. The pair’s momentum reflects ongoing demand for safe-haven USD amid mixed risk sentiment.

Market participants noted that the Yen’s softness comes despite persistent speculation about potential Japanese government or Bank of Japan action to curb sharp declines. Technical traders highlighted the pair’s ability to hold above key support levels, signaling that bearish pressure remains intact. Meanwhile, USD strength against major currencies like the EUR and GBP reinforced the momentum in USD/JPY trading.

Looking ahead, analysts suggest that continued USD appreciation could push the currency pair higher, testing recent resistance points. Forex traders are watching macroeconomic indicators, interest rate differentials, and geopolitical developments closely, as these factors could dictate whether the Yen stabilizes or faces further depreciation against the USD in the near term. Volatility in other major pairs could also influence trading flows and the Yen’s trajectory.
