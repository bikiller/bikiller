AUD/USD exchanged hands around 0.6700 on Monday, gaining 0.10% for the day after overcoming earlier intraday losses. The uptick followed the release of disappointing US Purchasing Managers Index (PMI) data, which raised concerns about the economic outlook in the United States and led to a softer USD across the board.

The Australian Dollar has demonstrated resilience amid a challenging macroeconomic environment, supported by expectations surrounding the Reserve Bank of Australia's policy direction. As traders assess the implications of the US economic data, the AUD's movement against the USD highlights the currency pair's sensitivity to global economic indicators and central bank policies.
