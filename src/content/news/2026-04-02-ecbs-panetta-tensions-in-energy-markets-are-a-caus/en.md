ECB executive board member Fabio Panetta highlighted concerns over the potential impact of the global energy crisis on financial stability. Speaking during European trading hours on Thursday, Panetta emphasized that ongoing tensions in energy markets could disrupt the broader economy, leading to increased volatility in currency exchange rates. As markets react, the EUR/USD pair saw slight pressure, trading around 1.0920, reflecting investor caution.

The energy market uncertainty has left traders wary of broader economic repercussions. The euro's vulnerability to external shocks, including energy price fluctuations, has added stress to EUR/USD trading, with the pair struggling to break through resistance at 1.0950. The USD's safe-haven appeal remains robust, with markets awaiting further ECB commentary and U.S. economic data to gauge future forex movements.
