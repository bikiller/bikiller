The US Dollar Index (DXY) rebounded toward the 100.00 level as President Donald Trump announced plans to escalate military action against Iran over the next two to three weeks. This development dampened expectations of a de-escalation in tensions, sparking renewed demand for the Greenback as a safe haven. The USD saw a notable uptick across major currency pairs, with the EUR/USD slipping below the 1.1200 mark.

Meanwhile, oil prices surged, driven by the escalating geopolitical risks, with crude climbing above $70 per barrel. This boost in oil prices further supported the USD, which often benefits from higher commodity prices. In contrast, gold prices sank as investors shifted away from the precious metal, driven by the strength of the US dollar. The negative correlation between gold and USD was evident as the yellow metal traded lower, hovering around $1,850.
