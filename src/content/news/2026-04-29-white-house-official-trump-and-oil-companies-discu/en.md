A White House source told Reuters that President Donald Trump held discussions with top executives from Chevron and other leading energy firms regarding the potential extension of oil supply restrictions amid ongoing Middle East tensions. Traders reacted cautiously, with Brent crude edging higher by 1.2% to $84.50 a barrel, while WTI climbed 1.0% to $79.30, reflecting heightened geopolitical risk that could tighten global supply and influence commodity-linked currency pairs.  

In forex markets, the USD showed mild strength against the EUR and JPY, with the EUR/USD sliding to 1.0980 as investors priced in potential inflationary pressures from higher oil prices. USD/JPY traded near 134.50, testing resistance from recent trading ranges, while commodity-sensitive currencies such as the CAD saw gains, moving to 1.3550 versus the USD amid expectations of elevated Canadian oil exports supporting the currency.  

Market participants are weighing the likelihood of extended blockades on crude flows and their implications for energy prices and broader financial markets. Analysts note that sustained disruptions could trigger volatility across oil-linked assets and currency pairs, prompting traders to adjust positions in futures, options, and spot FX. The potential for continued supply constraints keeps both commodities and USD-centric forex pairs under close watch.
