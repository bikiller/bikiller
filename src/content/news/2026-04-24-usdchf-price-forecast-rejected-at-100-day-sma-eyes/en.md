USD/CHF traded under pressure on Friday after being rejected at the 100-day simple moving average (SMA), with sellers regaining short-term control. The currency pair hovered around 0.7841 during the session, reflecting choppy trading conditions as technical resistance capped upside momentum in the USD vs CHF exchange rate near a key resistance zone.

Despite Friday weakness, USD/CHF ended the week with gains of over 0.35%, supported by shifting risk sentiment in forex markets. Traders reacted to growing confidence that US-Iran talks could resume over the weekend, potentially easing geopolitical tensions and influencing demand for safe-haven currencies like the Swiss franc versus the US dollar.

Looking ahead, the currency pair remains focused on the downside, with market participants eyeing the 0.7800 level as a key support area. A sustained break below could extend USD/CHF losses, while renewed USD strength or improved risk appetite may stabilise trading and limit further downside against the Swiss franc in the near term.
