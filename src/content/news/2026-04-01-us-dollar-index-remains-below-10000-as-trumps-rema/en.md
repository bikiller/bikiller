The US Dollar Index (DXY), which tracks the USD against a basket of six major currencies, continues to retreat, trading around 99.80 during the Asian session on Wednesday. This marks the second consecutive day of losses for the index, as investor sentiment shifts towards risk-on assets. The greenback's weakness comes amid remarks from former President Donald Trump, which have fueled optimism in broader markets.

The pullback in the DXY reflects a broader shift in risk appetite, with traders moving away from safe-haven USD towards more risk-sensitive currencies. Key currency pairs such as EUR/USD are benefiting from the USD's softness, with the euro gaining ground. This movement highlights the impact of political statements on market sentiment and suggests further volatility ahead as global traders assess the evolving geopolitical landscape.
