UK retail sales are expected to drop by 0.6% month-on-month in February, slightly outperforming market expectations of a 0.7% decline, following a 1.8% rise in January. TD Securities attributes this slowdown to the fading effects of previous factors supporting sales, combined with adverse weather conditions that reduced foot traffic to physical stores. 

The data could weigh on GBP, particularly against USD and EUR, as it signals a cooling in consumer activity. Despite the setback, UK economic momentum remains intact for now, with traders keeping an eye on upcoming data to gauge whether the softening retail performance is part of a broader trend.
