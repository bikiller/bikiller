EUR/USD regained some ground on Thursday, trading around 1.1490 during Asian hours after recovering losses from the previous session. The currency pair continues to face downward pressure, constrained within a descending channel that highlights a persistent bearish trend. Traders are closely watching the nine-day EMA, which aligns near the 1.1500 barrier, a level that could influence short-term trading sentiment in the euro-dollar exchange rate.  

Technical indicators on the daily chart suggest that despite the rebound, the overall momentum favors the USD. Bears remain in control, and any rallies toward 1.1500 may encounter strong resistance. Market participants are monitoring global economic cues and risk sentiment, which could further impact EUR/USD fluctuations, especially as the dollar shows resilience amid ongoing geopolitical and economic uncertainties.  

The outlook for traders remains cautious. Short-term strategies may revolve around the descending channel, with the 1.1500 threshold serving as a key reference point for potential reversals or continuation of the downtrend. As EUR/USD navigates these technical levels, the interplay between EUR weakness and USD strength will likely dictate the next moves in the currency market.
