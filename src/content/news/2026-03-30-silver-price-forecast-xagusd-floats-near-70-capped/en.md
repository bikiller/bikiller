Silver (XAG/USD) remains range-bound near $70, hovering between $67.50 and $71.50 for the third consecutive trading day. Market participants have failed to break the price out of this zone, with buyers and sellers locked in a tug-of-war. The 100-day simple moving average (SMA) has been acting as a barrier, capping any potential upside for the precious metal.

The XAG/USD exchange rate is closely monitoring the strength of the USD and broader market sentiment. With silver trading within this narrow range, any significant price action will likely hinge on upcoming economic data and potential fluctuations in the U.S. dollar. Until then, the market remains in a cautious wait-and-see mode, with investors looking for a catalyst to drive the next move.
