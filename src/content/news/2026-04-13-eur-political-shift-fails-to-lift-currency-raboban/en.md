The Euro remains under pressure in early Asian trading despite a clear win for Peter Magyar’s pro-EU Tisza party over Viktor Orban’s Fidesz in Hungary’s elections. EUR/USD continues to trade lower, maintaining its weak tone as the pair hovers near the 1.0900 level. Political shifts in Hungary failed to inject significant optimism into the currency, with broader market sentiment still dominated by global risk factors.

The market reaction suggests that traders are focused on the ongoing strength of the USD, driven by higher U.S. yields and expectations of a continued tightening cycle from the Federal Reserve. The EUR/USD exchange rate struggles to regain momentum, even as European political developments create an initial buzz. With the USD retaining its dominance, the Euro is likely to face continued headwinds in the near term, particularly if U.S. economic data shows further signs of strength.
