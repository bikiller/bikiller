West Texas Intermediate (WTI) holds gains above $75.00, trading near $75.10 per barrel during Asian hours on Thursday, rebounding after a five-day losing streak. The recovery comes as crude stabilizes in early trading, with broader currency market moves influencing sentiment, including USD exchange rate fluctuations that typically affect commodity-linked pricing across global trading desks.

Oil prices edge higher despite easing Middle East tensions and softer supply concerns, which would normally weigh on crude. However, expectations around potential Federal Reserve rate hikes in 2026 continue to support the US dollar and shape risk appetite, keeping traders cautious across USD and EUR currency pair positioning in broader forex markets.

Market participants are watching whether WTI can sustain levels above $75.00, a key short-term support zone, as USD strength and EUR/USD volatility influence commodity demand expectations. Any sustained shift in exchange rate dynamics could affect crude pricing momentum, with traders balancing macro signals against improving risk sentiment in global trading conditions.
