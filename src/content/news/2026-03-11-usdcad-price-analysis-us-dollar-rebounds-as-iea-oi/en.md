The USD/CAD currency pair is showing a rebound, trading around the 1.3580 mark in Wednesday's American session. The rebound follows the International Energy Agency’s decision to release 400 million barrels of oil to ease supply pressures stemming from the conflict in Iran. Traders reacted quickly, pushing the US dollar higher against the Canadian dollar as markets weighed the potential impact on crude-linked currencies.  

Technical indicators suggest that the exchange rate may encounter resistance near 1.3600, with immediate support around 1.3550. Market participants are closely monitoring the interplay between USD strength and CAD vulnerability amid oil volatility. Momentum in the pair reflects cautious optimism for the US dollar, even as global energy dynamics continue to influence risk sentiment.  

The move underscores the USD’s appeal as a safe-haven currency, with broader implications for forex trading. A stronger USD/CAD could pressure cross-rates like EUR/CAD and GBP/CAD, prompting traders to adjust positions. As crude markets respond to the IEA release, the Canadian dollar remains sensitive, keeping the currency pair’s near-term direction heavily linked to both energy developments and broader macroeconomic signals.
