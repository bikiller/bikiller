During Thursday’s European trading session, European Central Bank (ECB) official Olli Rehn cautioned that traders should not be overly optimistic about the duration of the ongoing conflict involving the US, Iran, and Israel. Rehn, who also serves as the Governor of the Finnish Central Bank, highlighted the conflict's potential to fuel inflationary pressures in the near term. 

This geopolitical tension is likely to have ripple effects on currency markets, particularly for the EUR/USD pair. Inflationary concerns could lead to further volatility in exchange rates, with the USD potentially benefiting from safe-haven demand. As traders closely monitor the conflict’s development, any signs of escalation may prompt a shift in market sentiment, impacting trading volumes and price action across major currency pairs.
