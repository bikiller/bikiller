Silver (XAG/USD) edged slightly higher on Monday, rebounding after a sharp 8% drop on Friday. The retreat came in response to a stronger-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report, which reinforced expectations that the Federal Reserve will maintain higher interest rates for an extended period. Traders noted that XAG/USD continues to hover near its 200-day simple moving average, signaling cautious sentiment in precious metals markets.  

The USD’s strength following the NFP data weighed on silver and other commodities, with investors closely watching the exchange rate dynamics against major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Market participants highlighted that the current trading range reflects uncertainty over the pace of Fed tightening, with the 200-day SMA serving as a critical technical barrier for further gains.  

Looking ahead, XAG/USD faces downside risks if the USD retains momentum and equity markets remain volatile. Analysts suggest that a sustained breach below the 200-day SMA could open the door for further declines, while any softening in US economic indicators might offer silver a chance to recover. Traders are positioning carefully, balancing risk against potential shifts in global monetary policy and currency pair fluctuations.
