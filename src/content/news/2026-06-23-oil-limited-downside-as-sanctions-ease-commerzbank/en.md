Brent crude extended its decline, slipping back below the USD 80 per barrel mark and briefly touching USD 76.5, according to Commerzbank analyst Carsten Fritsch. The move followed a US decision allowing Iran to continue exporting oil and refined products until at least 21 August, easing immediate supply concerns and weighing on energy markets.

Lower crude prices have spillover effects across currency markets, with the USD supported through risk-off flows while commodity-linked currencies such as CAD and AUD face pressure. In FX trading, EUR/USD and other major currency pairs often react to shifts in oil-driven inflation expectations, as easing energy costs can temper central bank tightening bets globally.

Despite the drop, analysts suggest downside in oil may remain limited if geopolitical risks re-emerge or if demand stabilises, keeping Brent within a volatile range. For traders, the interaction between oil and forex markets continues to shape USD valuation trends, influencing exchange rate movements and short-term positioning across major FX pairs.
