European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member François Villeroy de Galhau reassured markets that the central bank is actively monitoring energy prices amidst the ongoing Middle East conflict. Speaking during European trading hours, Villeroy highlighted that the duration of the war in Iran will be a key factor in determining its impact on global energy prices. These developments continue to weigh heavily on the EUR/USD exchange rate, which has remained volatile in recent sessions.

The ECB's vigilant stance comes as oil prices and energy costs fluctuate, directly influencing the trading environment. The EUR/USD pair has shown significant fluctuations, with the Euro struggling to maintain upward momentum, influenced by both geopolitical risks and ECB monetary policy. As energy markets react to the conflict, traders are closely watching the central bank's next moves, while the dollar’s strength, particularly against the Euro, is expected to persist in the short term.
