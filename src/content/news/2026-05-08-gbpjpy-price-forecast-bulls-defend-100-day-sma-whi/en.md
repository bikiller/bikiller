GBP/JPY crept higher on Friday, with the currency pair holding above its 100-day simple moving average (SMA) as bullish traders defended key technical support. Momentum remains muted, suggesting caution among investors despite the slight upward drift. The exchange rate’s resilience indicates that traders are still weighing broader risk sentiment against the backdrop of recent Japanese intervention attempts in the yen.

The Japanese Yen continues to face selling pressure, pressured by sustained USD strength and mixed data from the Eurozone that have shifted flows toward higher-yielding assets. Market participants are closely monitoring Tokyo’s central bank activity after earlier intervention reports, as any renewed action could quickly influence trading dynamics across major pairs including GBP/USD and EUR/JPY.

Analysts note that while short-term gains in GBP/JPY are limited, the pair’s ability to maintain the 100-day SMA provides a base for further upside if risk appetite persists. Traders are balancing yen weakness with global interest rate expectations, making movements in the pair sensitive to both macroeconomic news and technical levels, highlighting the intricate interplay between currency fundamentals and market positioning.
