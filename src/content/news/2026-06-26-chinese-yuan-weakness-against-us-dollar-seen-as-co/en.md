Chinese yuan weakness against the US dollar is being viewed as largely corrective, according to OCBC FX strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong. The USD/CNH currency pair recently stalled after its advance, with the exchange rate hovering near 6.8020. Despite the pause, broader USD demand continues to influence trading sentiment across Asian FX markets.

Technical signals show the run-up in USD/CNH has stalled, though daily bullish momentum remains intact. However, RSI has turned lower from overbought territory, suggesting near-term consolidation in the currency pair rather than a decisive reversal in trading direction for the US dollar versus the yuan. Traders may watch support levels around recent lows and broader USD trends.

Market participants are likely to treat the yuan’s weakness as a corrective phase within broader forex trading conditions, with USD strength still guiding the USD/CNH currency pair direction. Volatility in Asian FX may also influence cross rates such as EUR/USD, as investors reassess dollar demand and risk sentiment across global exchange rate markets. Overall, trading activity is expected to remain sensitive to US dollar moves and evolving technical indicators.
