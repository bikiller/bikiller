WTI Oil extended losses on Thursday, sliding to around $73.20 per barrel at the time of writing, marking a 2.35% daily decline. The benchmark has now fallen to its lowest level in more than three months as bearish sentiment dominates energy trading. The move reflects shifting risk appetite across global markets, with USD-linked flows influencing broader forex currency pair dynamics.

Geopolitical easing around a potential Strait of Hormuz deal has weighed heavily on crude, with optimism over improved supply security offsetting a larger-than-expected US inventory drawdown. Traders in energy and forex markets have shifted positioning, as reduced oil risk premiums alter USD and commodity-linked currency pair correlations across global exchange rate movements.

The decline in WTI is expected to ripple through forex markets, strengthening the US dollar versus commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian dollar, while influencing EUR/USD trading ranges amid shifting risk sentiment. Lower energy prices also reshape inflation expectations and interest rate outlooks, adding volatility to major currency pair exchange rate movements heading into upcoming sessions.
