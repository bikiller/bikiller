Crude oil prices have seen muted recovery following initial losses on reports of a ceasefire in Iran, with markets reacting cautiously to the fragility of the truce. Rabobank strategist Molly Schwartz highlighted that while oil’s sharp early drop triggered some buying, renewed tensions and the re-closure of the Strait of Hormuz have limited a full rebound. Traders remain wary, keeping volatility elevated across energy-linked currency pairs like USD/CAD and NOK/USD.  

The USD has shown relative strength as investors weigh geopolitical risks against oil market stability. The exchange rate for EUR/USD edged lower, reflecting haven demand for the dollar amid uncertainty. Oil-linked currencies, including the Australian dollar, saw mixed trading, with AUD/USD hovering near key support levels as markets absorbed the potential for supply disruptions. Technical analysts note that a decisive move above $90 per barrel could reignite risk-on positioning, while a slip below $84 keeps traders cautious.  

For forex markets, crude movements are feeding through to broader trading strategies. Carry trades and commodity-linked pairs are particularly sensitive, as the fragile ceasefire introduces uncertainty in market liquidity. Investors are monitoring not only headline developments but also intraday price swings, as energy risk continues to influence USD strength, cross-currency volatility, and positioning in major currency pairs ahead of the next geopolitical update.
