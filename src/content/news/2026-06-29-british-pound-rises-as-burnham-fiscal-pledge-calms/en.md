The British Pound Sterling strengthened in Monday trading, rising 0.40% as demand improved across major currency pairs, including GBP/USD and GBP/EUR. The move reflected firmer risk appetite in the foreign exchange market, with the exchange rate benefiting from easing concerns over UK fiscal direction. Traders noted steady buying interest in sterling during early European sessions, supporting intraday momentum.

Sterling's advance followed comments from Andy Burnham, widely expected to become the next UK Prime Minister, who pledged to adhere to fiscal rules set by Chancellor Rachel Reeves. The assurance helped stabilise sentiment around UK fiscal policy, reducing perceived risk in the currency pair valuation. As a result, traders reassessed short-term positioning in the pound across spot forex markets.

Looking ahead, GBP trading may remain sensitive to fiscal headlines and broader USD and EUR moves, as investors evaluate relative economic resilience and central bank expectations. The pound’s exchange rate outlook will likely depend on upcoming policy signals and macro data, with volatility persisting in major currency pairs as positioning adjusts in response to shifting market sentiment.
