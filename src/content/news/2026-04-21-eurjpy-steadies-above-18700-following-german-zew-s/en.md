EUR/JPY held steady above the 187.00 level on Monday, trading around 187.20 during Asian sessions. The currency pair showed limited volatility after posting modest gains the previous day, reflecting a cautious tone among investors. The Euro’s movement was subdued as markets digested the latest German ZEW Survey data, which offered mixed signals on economic sentiment, keeping traders in a wait-and-see mode.  

The exchange rate remained resilient against the backdrop of broad USD weakness, with market participants weighing the impact of European economic indicators on EUR positioning. Technical support near 186.80 continues to underpin the pair, while resistance around 187.50 may limit upside in the short term. Analysts note that trading flows are largely dictated by sentiment shifts and geopolitical developments affecting risk appetite.  

Looking ahead, EUR/JPY could see further consolidation as investors monitor incoming Eurozone and U.S. data for directional cues. Volatility may rise if the Euro responds more decisively to economic releases, influencing carry trades and cross-currency flows. For now, the pair’s steady performance signals a market balancing between risk-on and risk-off pressures, with traders adjusting positions ahead of potential policy or macroeconomic updates.
