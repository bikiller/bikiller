US Dollar strengthened following a hawkish Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) stance, with DBS Group Research’s Philip Wee noting renewed upside momentum across major currency pairs. The US Dollar Index (DXY) climbed to its highest level since May 2025, reflecting stronger demand for USD in forex trading as investors reassess interest rate expectations.

Fed guidance, perceived as more restrictive, continues to underpin USD strength, pushing the currency higher against peers such as the EUR/USD and other major currency pairs. Traders are pricing in a slower pace of policy easing, which supports the greenback and weighs on risk-sensitive FX crosses amid shifting yield differentials and safe-haven flows.

According to DBS, the USD outlook remains sensitive to upcoming data releases, particularly inflation-linked indicators such as the PCE. Any upside surprise could reinforce further gains in the USD Index, while softer readings may trigger consolidation. In forex trading, investors are closely monitoring exchange rate volatility across G10 currency pairs for directional cues.
