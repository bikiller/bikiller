The demand for safe-haven assets remains strong amid the escalating crisis in the Middle East. As tensions rise, the Japanese yen (JPY) and Swiss franc (CHF) continue to benefit, with the USD/JPY hovering around 134.00, down 0.3%. The EUR/USD is trading at 1.0600, showing modest declines as investors flock to more stable currencies. These movements reflect heightened risk aversion in global markets.

In contrast, commodity currencies like the Australian dollar (AUD) and Canadian dollar (CAD) are under pressure. The AUD/USD is trading just above 0.6600, slipping 0.2%, while the USD/CAD remains near 1.3700. These declines reflect broader concerns over global growth and oil prices, with risk-sensitive assets facing headwinds in the wake of geopolitical instability. Traders are closely watching developments in the Middle East, as they may further influence currency market trends.
