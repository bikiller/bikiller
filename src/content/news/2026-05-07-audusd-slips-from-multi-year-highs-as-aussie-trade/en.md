AUD/USD traded 0.2% lower on Thursday, slipping to around 0.7205 after reaching a multi-year high near 0.7280 earlier in the session. The pullback marked a pause in the Australian dollar's recent rally, as traders reacted to weaker Australian trade data, which pressured the currency. 

Meanwhile, the U.S. dollar showed signs of recovery, as the greenback gained ground across major currency pairs. The strengthening USD was driven by a combination of solid economic expectations and a rebound in risk sentiment, pushing the AUD/USD exchange rate lower. Traders are now eyeing key technical levels as the currency pair consolidates.
