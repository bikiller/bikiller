The USD/CAD currency pair slipped on Friday, trading around 1.3670, marking a 0.26% decline on the day. The US Dollar came under pressure as geopolitical tensions eased with the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, easing safe-haven demand. Despite a sharp drop in oil prices, the Canadian Dollar gained traction, supporting the exchange rate and keeping USD/CAD under pressure near key technical levels.  

Traders are eyeing the Canadian CPI report, which could influence further movements in USD/CAD. The pair’s decline highlights the interplay between commodity-driven currencies and broader USD sentiment, with market participants closely monitoring both macroeconomic data and geopolitical developments. Momentum indicators suggest that the recent downward move could face short-term support around the 1.3650 level, while resistance may emerge near 1.3700.  

Market watchers note that USD strength elsewhere, such as against the EUR or JPY, remains a counterbalance, but CAD resilience continues to weigh on the pair. With the Canadian CPI and US economic indicators looming, trading could see increased volatility. Investors remain cautious, adjusting positions in the currency pair in response to shifting risk sentiment and macroeconomic signals from North America and global oil markets.
