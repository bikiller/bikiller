Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded on Wednesday, recovering some of Tuesday’s losses as markets responded to US President Donald Trump’s announcement extending the ceasefire with Iran just hours before its expiration. The safe-haven metal gained traction against the USD, reflecting investor caution amid stalled peace talks. Trading volumes picked up in early European sessions, with XAU/USD hovering near key technical support around $1,820 per ounce.  

The extension of the ceasefire eased immediate geopolitical tensions, prompting traders to adjust positions in gold and other major currency pairs. The USD edged lower against the euro and yen, with EUR/USD testing 1.0900 amid the shift in risk sentiment. Analysts noted that short-term trading strategies might favor gold accumulation, while USD-based assets could face mild downward pressure as safe-haven demand temporarily softens.  

Market watchers emphasized that gold’s rebound underscores its role as a hedge in periods of uncertainty. Technical indicators show resistance near $1,835, suggesting XAU/USD could face consolidation before any decisive breakout. Forex traders remain alert to developments in Middle East diplomacy, as any renewed tensions could quickly reverse current trends and influence broader currency exchange rates, including USD/JPY and EUR/USD.
