The USD/CAD currency pair pared earlier losses on Friday, with the exchange rate rebounding amid heightened geopolitical uncertainty. Traders remain cautious ahead of critical US-Iran negotiations, which are shaping risk sentiment across global markets. Despite fresh US inflation and Canadian employment data, the pair’s movements were dominated by these geopolitical concerns, keeping trading ranges unpredictable and volatility elevated.  

US CPI figures showed modest inflationary pressure, while Canadian jobs data indicated steady but unspectacular employment trends. These economic releases offered limited directional cues, as investors weighed broader market risks. Technical levels around key support near 1.3450 and resistance at 1.3550 are being closely watched, with momentum indicators signaling potential swings in either direction.  

The broader forex landscape reflects a risk-off tone, with USD strength observed against commodity-linked currencies, including the CAD. Traders are monitoring how developments in the Middle East might influence safe-haven flows, while the euro and other major currencies exhibit restrained movement. For USD/CAD, the combination of geopolitical uncertainty and technical positioning suggests continued choppy trading, highlighting the importance of cautious position sizing and careful timing.
