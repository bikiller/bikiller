The GBP/USD currency pair extended its decline on Friday, marking a fourth consecutive daily loss as the British Pound remained under heavy pressure from mounting political uncertainty in the UK and renewed demand for the USD. The exchange rate is now on track to end the week more than 2% lower, reflecting growing investor caution toward UK assets and broader risk-sensitive trading conditions across the forex market.

Sterling sentiment deteriorated after speculation intensified that a potential successor to Prime Minister Keir Starmer could pursue more expansionary fiscal policies, potentially widening budget deficits and increasing borrowing needs. The political backdrop has added to concerns over the UK’s long-term fiscal outlook, prompting traders to rotate into safer currencies, particularly the USD, while the EUR also gained relative strength against the Pound in cross-market trading.

At the same time, escalating geopolitical tensions linked to Iran boosted safe-haven flows into the US Dollar, further pressuring GBP/USD. Currency traders continued to monitor risk appetite, Treasury yields, and central bank expectations for direction in major exchange rates. The pair’s sustained weakness also raised concerns about deeper downside momentum should political instability persist and broader market sentiment remain tilted toward defensive USD positioning.
