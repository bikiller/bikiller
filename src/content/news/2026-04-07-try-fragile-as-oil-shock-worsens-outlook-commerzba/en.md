The Turkish lira (TRY) faces increasing pressure as rising oil prices and external shocks worsen the country's economic outlook. Commerzbank's Tatha Ghose notes that Turkey's brief period of disinflation has already been overshadowed by these global developments. While the headline Consumer Price Index (CPI) slowed in March, core inflation remains robust, and concerns about the credibility of the central bank persist. 

As a result, the TRY is trading under heightened volatility, with the USD/TRY exchange rate moving in response to both domestic inflationary trends and the broader oil market. The lira's vulnerability is compounded by market doubts about Turkey's monetary policy credibility. The EUR/TRY pair remains under pressure, as investors continue to monitor external factors influencing Turkey's economy. The outlook for the currency is fragile, with further oil price increases likely to keep the TRY on the defensive.
