AUD/USD currency pair trades in a muted range near the 0.7030 exchange rate on Thursday, with overall trading activity lacking strong momentum. The Australian Dollar struggles to extend recent gains, while the US Dollar remains supported after the latest US jobless claims data pointed to a steady and resilient labor market backdrop.

The firmer USD tone continues to reflect confidence in underlying US economic conditions, limiting downside pressure on the greenback across forex markets. This dynamic leaves risk-sensitive currencies, including the Australian Dollar, on the defensive as investors hesitate to build fresh long positions against the USD in current trading conditions.

Looking ahead, AUD/USD is likely to remain confined within a relatively narrow range as traders weigh incoming macroeconomic releases from both economies. A sustained move above or below the 0.7030 level could help define short-term direction, with exchange rate action driven by US labor data expectations and broader shifts in global risk sentiment.
