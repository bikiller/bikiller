The European Commission’s new Industrial Accelerator Act is set to reshape the Eurozone’s industrial landscape, according to Rabobank’s Head of Macro Strategy, Elwin de Groot. The initiative aims to strengthen the region’s manufacturing base, speed up decarbonisation efforts, and reduce reliance on external suppliers. Analysts suggest these structural reforms could underpin the euro, supporting the EUR against the USD and other major currency pairs in the medium term.  

Traders have reacted cautiously, with the EUR/USD pair trading near key technical levels around 1.0850, reflecting mixed sentiment. Market participants are weighing the Act’s potential to boost long-term growth against short-term fiscal and regulatory costs. Any sustained improvement in industrial output and energy transition could gradually increase investor confidence, influencing exchange rates across EUR-USD, EUR-GBP, and EUR-JPY pairs.  

Currency strategists note that stronger eurozone fundamentals may shift trading flows, as the EUR could benefit from increased capital inflows while the USD faces pressure from dovish Fed signals. Volatility is expected in the near term, with intraday swings around the 1.08–1.09 range. Investors remain focused on industrial investment data and policy updates, which could drive the euro’s trading momentum and shape broader forex market dynamics.
