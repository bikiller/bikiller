GBP/USD advanced sharply on Thursday, with the currency pair rallying nearly 1.3% to reclaim the 1.3400 level and settle near 1.3430. The move was driven by broad USD weakness and a surprise shift in tone from the Bank of England, which delivered a unanimous hawkish pivot that caught forex markets off guard and fueled aggressive buying in sterling during active trading.

The BoE’s stance signaled increased confidence in the UK outlook, prompting traders to reassess interest rate expectations and push the exchange rate higher against both the USD and EUR. The sudden alignment among policymakers amplified the impact, triggering momentum-driven flows and reinforcing bullish sentiment around GBP across major currency pairs.

From a technical perspective, the break above 1.3400 marks a significant shift in near-term positioning, opening the door for further upside if momentum holds. Traders will now watch whether GBP/USD can sustain gains above this level, as continued USD softness and evolving central bank expectations remain key drivers influencing forex market direction.
