The British Pound came under sustained pressure on Wednesday as softer-than-expected UK CPI data weighed on sentiment across major currency pairs. The weaker inflation reading reinforced expectations that the Bank of England may remain cautious on future tightening, dragging sterling lower against both the US dollar and euro. Early trading saw the exchange rate lose momentum as sellers dominated short-term flows in forex markets.

Later in the session, the currency pair faced additional pressure after Kevin Warsh delivered a hawkish first Federal Reserve decision, boosting US dollar demand across forex trading desks. Expectations for higher-longer interest rates strengthened the greenback, intensifying selling pressure on GBP/USD and broadening losses in sterling against major currencies in the global exchange rate market.

Overall, the British Pound was effectively double-teamed by softer domestic inflation and a more aggressive US monetary stance. The policy divergence between the UK and United States is expected to keep volatility elevated in currency trading, with traders closely monitoring whether GBP can stabilise or extend declines if USD strength persists across forex markets in coming sessions.
