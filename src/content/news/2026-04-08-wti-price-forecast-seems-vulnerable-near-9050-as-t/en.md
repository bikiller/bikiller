West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices fell sharply to a nearly two-week low during the Asian session on Wednesday. The decline was triggered by news that the US and Iran reached a two-week ceasefire agreement, easing concerns over potential supply disruptions. As a result, WTI prices tested support near $90.50, highlighting growing vulnerability in the market.

This technical breakdown raises concerns that WTI could face further downside if the $90.50 level fails to hold. Traders are closely watching for any confirmation of this shift, with the broader market now reacting to the easing geopolitical tensions. In the forex market, the USD and other commodity-linked currencies may experience fluctuations as oil prices continue to weigh on sentiment.
