Gold (XAU/USD) held steady on Friday as investors weighed mixed US employment data, which showed modest job growth but a slight rise in the unemployment rate. The precious metal traded near $2,010 per ounce, reflecting cautious sentiment in currency markets, with the USD index remaining supported by expectations of steady Federal Reserve policy. Traders also monitored technical levels, noting support around $2,000 and resistance near $2,025.  

Currency markets showed muted reactions, with the EUR/USD pair hovering around 1.095, reflecting a balance between Eurozone recovery optimism and dollar strength. USD/JPY trading remained range-bound near 145, as traders focused on macroeconomic signals and geopolitical tensions that could sway safe-haven flows. The mixed data prompted a reassessment of risk sentiment, keeping gold attractive as a hedge against market uncertainty.  

Meanwhile, geopolitical developments in the Middle East continued to influence trading behavior. Heightened tensions supported gold demand, countering the USD’s underlying strength. Analysts noted that sustained conflict risks could push gold above technical resistance, while stable conditions might favor USD appreciation and weigh on the XAU/USD exchange rate. Traders remain alert to both economic indicators and geopolitical headlines in shaping short-term moves.
