India’s exposure to global energy volatility is coming into sharp focus as the ongoing Iran conflict threatens supply routes and fuels imported energy dependence. Societe Generale’s Kunal Kundu notes that rising crude and natural gas prices are pressuring India’s consumption basket, pushing up inflationary risks. For traders, the INR/USD currency pair has seen heightened volatility, with the rupee slipping amid widening external balances and growing demand for dollars to cover energy imports.  

Kundu emphasizes that sustained oil price surges could spill over into trade deficits, complicating the Reserve Bank of India’s policy stance. The INR/EUR exchange rate also reflects these tensions, showing weaker domestic currency dynamics against the euro. Market participants are adjusting trading strategies, with forex flows responding to both higher energy costs and geopolitical uncertainties that could influence near-term monetary policy signals.  

Analysts expect policymakers to balance growth with inflation containment, potentially deploying a mix of targeted fiscal measures and currency interventions. Short-term trading in USD/INR and EUR/INR may remain choppy, while hedging activity in energy-linked derivatives could increase. Overall, the combination of external energy shocks and policy maneuvering underlines a cautious outlook for the rupee and broader forex market positioning.
