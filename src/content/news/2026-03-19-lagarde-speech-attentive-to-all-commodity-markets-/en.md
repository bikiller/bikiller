EUR held relatively steady against the USD following remarks from ECB President Christine Lagarde, who confirmed that policymakers left key interest rates unchanged at the March meeting. The EUR/USD currency pair saw limited volatility as traders digested her emphasis on monitoring commodity markets and persistent supply bottlenecks, factors that continue to influence inflation dynamics and the broader exchange rate outlook.

Lagarde highlighted that while price pressures are easing in some sectors, risks remain tied to energy and raw material markets, reinforcing a cautious stance on monetary policy. This balanced tone kept EUR trading within a narrow range, as investors refrained from aggressive positioning. The ECB’s data-dependent approach signals that future rate adjustments will hinge on how supply constraints and global demand evolve.

In forex markets, the lack of immediate policy shifts reduced momentum for EUR bulls, while USD strength remained supported by comparatively resilient economic indicators. Traders are now watching incoming inflation data and commodity price trends for clearer direction. Near-term movement in the EUR/USD pair is likely to stay range-bound, with sentiment driven by macroeconomic signals rather than central bank surprises.
