Thursday trading was dominated by US Dollar strength, leaving the Australian Dollar on the defensive. AUD/USD briefly spiked toward 0.6950 after a softer-than-expected US payrolls print triggered knee-jerk USD selling. However, the move faded quickly, with the currency pair retreating from its highs and closing well below the intraday peak as buyers failed to sustain momentum.

The exchange rate structure kept the Aussie above its broader growth line, but price action showed it acting more like a passenger than a driver in global forex trading. USD strength remained the key theme, supported by shifting expectations around US economic data, while EUR and other majors also reflected cautious positioning across currency markets.

For traders, the AUD/USD exchange rate remains sensitive to US releases, with short-term flows still dictated by USD demand rather than domestic Australian drivers. The pair’s rejection from 0.6950 highlights resistance pressure, suggesting range-bound trading conditions unless momentum improves. Broader forex markets continue to watch US data for direction, with spillovers into EUR/USD and risk-linked currencies.
