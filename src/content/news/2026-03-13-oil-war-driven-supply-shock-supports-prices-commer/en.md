The ongoing conflict in Iran has led to unprecedented oil supply disruptions, with the International Energy Agency (IEA) estimating daily losses of at least 8 million barrels. This supply shock has significantly impacted oil prices, pushing them higher and contributing to volatility in global markets. The effects of this supply squeeze are being felt across currency markets, especially with the USD showing strength against the EUR, as traders seek safe-haven assets.

As oil prices surge, market participants are closely monitoring currency pairs like USD/JPY and EUR/USD, with the former benefiting from increased demand for the greenback amid global uncertainty. The disruptions have also led to shifts in exchange rates, with commodities such as oil influencing broader market sentiment. Traders are adjusting their positions, as the heightened risks associated with the Iran crisis continue to create ripples in both the forex and commodities markets.
