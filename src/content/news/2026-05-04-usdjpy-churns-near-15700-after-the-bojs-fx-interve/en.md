The USD/JPY currency pair traded largely sideways on Monday, hovering near 157.20 following a volatile session. Early-Asia trading saw the pair dip sharply toward 156.20, reflecting lingering uncertainty after the Bank of Japan’s surprise FX intervention. Market participants noted the initial gap lower triggered stop-losses, but the pair gradually recovered through European and US trading hours as investor appetite for the dollar reasserted itself.  

Traders cited technical levels around 157.00 as a key pivot, with buying support emerging near intraday lows. The exchange rate’s rebound was underpinned by broader USD strength against major currencies, including the EUR, amid expectations of continued divergence in monetary policy between the US Federal Reserve and the BoJ. Momentum indicators suggest the pair may continue to oscillate around current levels while markets digest the intervention’s implications.  

Analysts highlighted that the BoJ’s move introduces heightened volatility in USD/JPY trading, prompting cautious positioning from both speculative and institutional players. While the pair’s retreat toward 156.20 tested downside resilience, the steady recovery above 157.00 signals potential stabilization. Investors will likely monitor the FX market closely this week for follow-through flows, with USD/JPY remaining a focal point for currency traders amid shifting global risk sentiment.
