The Pound Sterling (GBP) gained over 0.20% against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, with the GBP/USD exchange rate climbing amidst speculation surrounding a potential ceasefire in the Middle East. However, reports soon dampened optimism, revealing the slim chances of a deal, which shifted market sentiment. Traders are now bracing for increased geopolitical tensions, particularly as the deadline set by former President Donald Trump approaches.

The likelihood of a US military response has heightened concerns in the forex market, with analysts predicting volatility in the currency pair. The 0.20% rise in GBP/USD suggests that traders are cautiously optimistic, but with global risks still in play, the pair could face additional fluctuations. As the situation in the Middle East evolves, the direction of the exchange rate remains uncertain, with the USD expected to remain sensitive to any developments.
