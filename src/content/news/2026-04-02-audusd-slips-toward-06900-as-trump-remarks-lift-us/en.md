The Australian Dollar slipped toward the 0.6900 level on Thursday, retreating 0.36% against the US Dollar. The AUD/USD currency pair came under pressure after US President Donald Trump issued stern remarks on the ongoing Middle East tensions, warning that the conflict would persist for two to three weeks and could inflict a heavier toll on Iran. Traders reacted swiftly, boosting demand for the safe-haven USD.  

Market participants noted that the surge in the US Dollar pushed the AUD/USD exchange rate lower, reflecting heightened risk aversion. Technical analysts highlighted that 0.6900 now acts as a short-term support, with further declines possible if geopolitical uncertainty intensifies. Meanwhile, EUR/USD and other major currency pairs showed muted reactions, as the focus remained on USD strength driven by political developments.  

The trading environment suggests cautious positioning among forex investors, with the Australian Dollar particularly sensitive to external shocks. A prolonged conflict could further strengthen the USD, potentially weighing on commodity-linked currencies like AUD. Traders will be closely watching subsequent statements and any escalation, as these could determine near-term volatility and influence the broader forex market dynamics.
