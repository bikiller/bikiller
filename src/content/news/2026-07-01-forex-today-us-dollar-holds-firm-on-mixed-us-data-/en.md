US Dollar Index (DXY) held near 101.40 on Thursday, as the US dollar remained firm in forex trading amid mixed US economic data and steady Treasury yields. Elevated long-term US government bond yields and cautious central bank remarks provided support, keeping USD broadly resilient against major currency pairs, while traders assessed shifting interest rate expectations.

US ISM Manufacturing PMI slipped to 53.3 in June from 54.0 in May, missing expectations but staying above the 50.0 expansion line, signaling continued growth in the manufacturing sector that still underpins USD strength in currency pair trading against peers like EUR and GBP. The data reinforces a steady but not overheating US economy, limiting aggressive Fed easing bets.

Looking ahead, forex markets remain cautious ahead of the upcoming Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report, which could reshape USD direction and short-term volatility across major currency pairs. Traders are likely to monitor EUR/USD and other majors for breakout signals as expectations for central bank policy divergence continue to drive dollar demand in the near term.
