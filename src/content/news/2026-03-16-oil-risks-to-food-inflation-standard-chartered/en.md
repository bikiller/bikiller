Higher oil prices pose a significant risk to global food inflation, according to Standard Chartered analysts Ethan Lester and Madhur Jha. They emphasize that the surge in oil costs could quickly drive up fertiliser prices, leading to increased food production expenses. Additionally, trade bottlenecks are likely to exacerbate these pressures, affecting supply chains across the globe.

Countries' food inflation outcomes will vary depending on food's share in their CPI baskets, import intensity, and dietary patterns. For example, nations heavily reliant on food imports, particularly those with large oil-dependent agricultural sectors, could see sharper inflationary pressures. As oil prices rise, currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY may also be influenced by these broader economic shifts, as the USD strengthens with higher oil prices.
