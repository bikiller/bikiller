Crude prices saw a slight dip during Monday's European session, with West Texas Intermediate (WTI) trading at $86.85 per barrel, down from the day’s high of $88.50. Despite the pullback, the price remains well above last Friday’s low of $78.90, reflecting ongoing volatility in the market. 

The fluctuating prices come amid concerns over the US-Iran peace process, which has added uncertainty to global oil supply expectations. As talks between the two nations stall, traders are reacting to the potential for geopolitical risks impacting oil availability. This has left WTI prices hovering near key technical levels, with $88.50 acting as resistance and $78.90 as support.
