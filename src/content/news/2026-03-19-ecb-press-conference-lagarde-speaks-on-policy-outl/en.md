At the March ECB press conference, President Christine Lagarde confirmed that the European Central Bank would maintain its key interest rates, keeping the benchmark at 3.00% for the main refinancing operations. This decision was in line with market expectations, as the ECB continues to monitor inflationary pressures. Lagarde highlighted that the bank's stance remains data-dependent, with future moves hinging on economic developments.

In forex markets, the EUR/USD exchange rate saw little immediate reaction following the announcement, trading around 1.0700. However, the euro remains under pressure as investors remain cautious over the ECB's ability to combat persistent inflation while navigating slower economic growth. The lack of a rate hike keeps the door open for further volatility in the euro, particularly as traders continue to digest the outlook for global central banks.
