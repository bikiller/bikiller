EUR/JPY saw a modest decline on Monday, trading at 186.65, down 0.08% amid growing risk aversion. The currency pair’s movement comes in response to the collapse of US-Iran talks, which have heightened geopolitical tensions and led to cautious sentiment in the markets. As traders react to this uncertainty, the EUR/JPY exchange rate has struggled to maintain upward momentum.

Meanwhile, rising energy prices have further fueled speculation that the Bank of Japan (BoJ) may accelerate its tightening cycle. Expectations of higher rates from the BoJ have added additional pressure on the EUR/JPY as market participants adjust their positions in response to this changing outlook. With these dual factors influencing sentiment, the currency pair remains vulnerable to further volatility.
