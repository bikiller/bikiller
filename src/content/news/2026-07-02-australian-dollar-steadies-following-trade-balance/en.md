AUD/USD edged higher during Thursday Asian session, recovering from modest losses in the previous day, trading around 0.6900. The currency pair's exchange rate steadied as the Australian Dollar found support following domestic data, while US dollar movements remain mixed across broader forex markets amid cautious risk sentiment ahead of key economic releases later in the week.

The Australian Dollar drew support from the latest domestic Trade Balance data release, which helped stabilize sentiment toward the currency. Improved external accounts provided a tailwind for the AUD, reinforcing its resilience against the US dollar in the forex market. As a result, traders saw reduced downside pressure on the currency pair, contributing to firmer short-term exchange rate action.

The pair may continue to consolidate around the 0.6900 level as traders assess upcoming US economic data and broader USD direction. Any sustained break above near-term resistance could open the door for further upside in the currency pair, while renewed US dollar strength may cap gains in AUD/USD in the near term.
