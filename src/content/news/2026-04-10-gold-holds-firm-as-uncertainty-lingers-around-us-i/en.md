Gold (XAU/USD) remained relatively steady on Friday, showing limited upward momentum as investors weighed ongoing uncertainty surrounding US-Iran negotiations. The precious metal’s price hovered near recent levels, with traders cautious amid mixed signals from the Middle East. Market participants are closely monitoring geopolitical developments, recognizing their potential to influence safe-haven demand and the broader trading dynamics of major currency pairs like USD/EUR.  

Meanwhile, the latest US inflation data added a nuanced layer to market sentiment. A softer-than-expected reading prompted some traders to reconsider the pace of Federal Reserve tightening, supporting gold’s appeal against the US dollar. The exchange rate fluctuations of USD against major currencies have also contributed to cautious trading, with investors balancing risk exposure while awaiting clearer directional cues.  

Technically, XAU/USD faces resistance near $2,050, while immediate support holds around $2,030. Analysts note that sustained uncertainty in the Middle East could maintain this consolidation, as gold remains a preferred hedge against geopolitical and inflationary risks. Traders are likely to watch both currency pair movements and geopolitical headlines closely, positioning for potential volatility in USD, EUR, and other key forex markets.
