The USD remained resilient on Tuesday, March 3, despite increasing tensions in the Middle East. The dollar held steady against major currencies, with the EUR/USD trading at 1.1835, reflecting a 0.10% dip. Investors remained cautious as geopolitical risks in the region fueled safe-haven demand for the greenback, limiting substantial movement across the forex market. 

The ongoing conflict added volatility to risk-sensitive pairs, with USD/JPY climbing to 106.45, gaining 0.25% on the day. The chaos in the Middle East kept traders focused on potential shifts in global sentiment, weighing heavily on commodities and currencies linked to global trade. As investors eye the impact of this instability, the USD’s strength could continue to provide support for the currency pair, while the EUR/USD struggles to gain momentum.
