The Turkish Lira (TRY) has found a more stable footing against the US dollar, with the USD/TRY currency pair showing limited volatility in recent trading sessions. Analysts from ING, including Muhammet Mercan, Frantisek Taborsky, and James Wilson, attribute this calm to easing US-Iran tensions, which have relieved geopolitical pressure on the lira. The Central Bank of Turkey’s (CBT) consistent policy stance continues to support market confidence, keeping the exchange rate on a steady path.

Trading activity suggests that renewed carry interest is attracting investors back to TRY positions, as the high-yield environment remains appealing amid global uncertainty. The USD/TRY exchange rate has held firm, showing resilience despite occasional fluctuations in broader forex markets. EUR/TRY trading has mirrored this pattern, with the euro following a relatively stable trajectory against the lira, signaling market expectations of continued central bank guidance.

Market watchers emphasize that while short-term stability is evident, broader macro factors—including US dollar movements, global risk sentiment, and Turkish inflation dynamics—will continue to influence trading. The current USD/TRY levels offer insight into investor appetite for emerging market currencies, highlighting how geopolitical easing and central bank credibility can reinforce a stable trading range and support sustained carry-driven flows.
