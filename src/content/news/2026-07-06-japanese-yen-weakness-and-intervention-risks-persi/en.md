Japanese yen weakness continued as the USD/JPY currency pair trades near its highest level in roughly four decades. The exchange rate remains elevated in the forex market, reflecting persistent divergence between US dollar strength and Japan’s softer monetary stance. Trading activity shows the pair consolidating near these extremes, keeping the yen under sustained pressure across major currency pairs.

HSBC strategists note that USD/JPY may have shifted into a new, higher trading range, with the currency pair now operating closer to multi-decade extremes. The assessment suggests a potential structural adjustment in the exchange rate, where sustained US dollar demand and Japan’s low-yield environment reinforce upward pressure. This raises the likelihood of increased volatility and keeps intervention risks in focus.

For forex traders, the elevated USD/JPY exchange rate underscores continued US dollar dominance and a fragile outlook for the Japanese yen. Market participants remain alert to potential intervention by Japanese authorities should volatility accelerate or the currency weaken further. Broader forex trading sentiment continues to favor USD strength, though risks of sharp corrective moves remain present.
