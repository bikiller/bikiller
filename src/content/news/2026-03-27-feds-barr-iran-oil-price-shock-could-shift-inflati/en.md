Federal Reserve Governor Michael Barr warned on Thursday that rising oil prices, particularly from an Iran oil shock, could disrupt inflation expectations and delay potential rate cuts. While the economy has remained resilient, Barr noted that these external pressures are complicating the Fed’s ability to achieve its 2% inflation target. This uncertainty could impact the USD’s strength, potentially influencing trading across key pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.

The Fed's struggle to balance inflation and economic resilience could keep the central bank on hold for longer than expected, putting downward pressure on USD in the near term. Market participants will closely monitor how these global shocks influence the USD's exchange rate, with the EUR/USD and GBP/USD pairs particularly sensitive to any changes in interest rate expectations. As the situation evolves, forex traders will watch for signs of a shift in the Fed’s outlook and any resultant impact on global trading sentiment.
