Gold (XAU/USD) holds gains above $4,300, trading around $4,335 during early Asian session on Wednesday as the currency pair extends recovery momentum. The exchange rate remains supported after bouncing from its lowest level of the year last week, while USD dynamics and broader forex trading sentiment keep precious metals bid. Investors watch positioning in dollar-denominated assets.  

Gold rebounded from its lowest levels of the year recorded last week, after the United States and Iran agreed on a framework deal aimed at ending the war. The development reduced geopolitical risk premiums, though traders continued to reassess risk appetite across forex markets and adjust USD exposure amid shifting sentiment.  

Market focus now shifts to the upcoming Federal Reserve rate decision, which could influence USD direction and bond yields, with spillover effects on XAU/USD trading. Expectations for interest rate policy remain a key driver for gold exchange rate volatility, as investors balance inflation signals against safe-haven demand in broader currency markets.
