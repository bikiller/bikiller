The Canadian Dollar (CAD) has shown resilience since the onset of the conflict, largely supported by its strong Oil-linked terms of trade. Despite ongoing market uncertainty, the USD/CAD currency pair has remained within its recent range, trading around 1.3700. MUFG’s Derek Halpenny suggests that the Bank of Canada (BoC) is likely to keep rates unchanged while delivering a cautious outlook, emphasizing concerns over inflation driven by energy prices.

Halpenny further notes that the BoC's stance reflects the broader uncertainty in the global economy. With energy prices remaining volatile, the central bank's caution is expected to prevent any significant shifts in the CAD's trajectory. This cautious approach should maintain the current trading range of USD/CAD, with traders focusing on upcoming economic data for further direction.
