Euro extends losses for a third consecutive session, with EUR/USD trading around 1.1340 on Wednesday, down 0.39% on the day. The currency pair remains under sustained selling pressure as the US Dollar strengthens across forex markets. Trading sentiment continues to favor the USD, with investors increasingly pricing in the possibility of further monetary tightening in the United States, reinforcing downside momentum in the exchange rate.

The main driver behind the move is the repricing of Federal Reserve rate expectations, which has outweighed a modest improvement in Germany’s IFO business climate index. Despite the slightly stronger German data, euro demand remains muted as interest rate differentials continue to support the USD. The stronger dollar environment dominates currency trading flows, limiting any meaningful recovery attempts in EUR/USD during both European and US sessions.

From a broader market perspective, the EUR/USD exchange rate remains highly sensitive to shifts in US Treasury yields and Federal Reserve commentary. Persistent USD strength suggests traders are hesitant to build long euro positions, with momentum still pointing to a bearish bias. Unless eurozone fundamentals show clearer improvement, the currency pair is likely to struggle regaining higher levels in the near term, keeping overall trading sentiment fragile.
