USD/JPY currency pair traded without clear direction near 162.50 on Wednesday, as traders reacted to mixed United States economic data and ongoing pressure on the Japanese Yen. The exchange rate remained elevated, reflecting persistent USD strength and limited intervention signals from Japanese authorities, keeping overall forex trading sentiment cautious in early trade.

Market sentiment remains driven by mixed US data releases, which have tempered expectations for near-term Federal Reserve rate cuts, while Japan's ultra-loose monetary policy continues to weigh on the yen. The widening yield differential between USD and JPY supports carry trades, keeping the currency pair near multi-decade lows around a 40-year range.

Traders remain cautious as USD/JPY hovers near key technical levels around 162.50, with risk of intervention from Japanese policymakers limiting further upside in the exchange rate. Market participants are also watching EUR and other major currency pairs for spillover effects, as volatility in USD trading conditions could intensify ahead of upcoming macroeconomic releases.
