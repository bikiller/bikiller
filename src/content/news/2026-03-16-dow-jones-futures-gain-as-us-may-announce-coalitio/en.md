Dow Jones futures rose 0.33%, reaching 46,750 during European trading hours, ahead of Monday's regular US market open. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures also gained, climbing 0.49% and 0.51%, respectively, to hover around 6,670 and 24,520. These gains come as speculation grows that the US may announce a coalition to secure the Strait of Hormuz, impacting global oil supply and market sentiment.

The potential move has given a boost to the USD, with market traders closely watching developments for any shifts in the EUR/USD exchange rate. While oil prices may be directly affected, broader implications for risk sentiment could further influence currency trading. Investors are cautious, awaiting any official announcements to gauge how geopolitical tensions could drive market movements and influence the upcoming week's forex trends.
