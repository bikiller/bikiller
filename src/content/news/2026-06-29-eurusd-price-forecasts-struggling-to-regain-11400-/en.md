EUR/USD edged higher against the US Dollar on Monday as the currency pair attempted a mild recovery in early trading. However, the exchange rate struggled to establish a sustained move above the 1.1400 level, with ongoing geopolitical uncertainty keeping USD demand relatively supported. Broader forex markets remained cautious, limiting Euro gains at the start of the week in thin liquidity conditions.

Price action remained constrained below the key resistance at 1.1400, while the 13-month low around 1.1320 continues to act as a critical downside reference for traders. Market participants are closely watching this trading range, as repeated rejections near resistance highlight persistent hesitation in EUR/USD momentum amid mixed risk sentiment across global forex markets during Monday’s session as volatility stays contained.

Looking ahead, EUR/USD traders are weighing whether geopolitical tensions will continue to support the US Dollar or allow the Euro to regain upward momentum. A decisive break above 1.1400 could signal renewed bullish trading, while failure to hold gains may expose the pair to another test of the 1.1320 support zone in the near-term outlook as investors remain cautious overall conditions remain uncertain.
