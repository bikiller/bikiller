The USD/CHF pair fell on Friday, with the Swiss Franc gaining strength against the US Dollar. The exchange rate continued its downward trend, marking a second consecutive weekly decline for the Greenback. USD/CHF dropped as market sentiment improved on growing expectations of a potential US-Iran deal. As a result, the US Dollar remained under pressure, while the Swiss Franc benefitted from risk-on sentiment in the markets.

The market's shift in sentiment was influenced by Iran's declaration that the Strait of Hormuz remains open, easing concerns over oil supply disruptions. This development led to a boost in global risk appetite, benefiting currencies like the Swiss Franc. As the USD struggles with ongoing geopolitical uncertainties, traders will closely watch further developments, particularly any news regarding US-Iran negotiations, which could continue to affect the USD/CHF currency pair.
