The US Dollar Index (DXY) eased on Monday, slipping slightly after recent gains and trading near 101.10 while still holding close to its latest highs. The move reflected mild profit-taking among traders following a strong run in the US dollar across major currency pairs. Despite the pullback, the overall tone in USD trading remained broadly supported, suggesting limited bearish pressure in the exchange rate for now.

Attention turned to upcoming US labor market data later in the week, which is expected to be a key driver for USD volatility and direction. Traders also monitored global events including the ECB Forum and China PMIs, which could influence risk sentiment and FX flows. Caution dominated trading activity across major currency pairs as investors adjusted positions ahead of these catalysts.

Forex traders are now watching how USD performance shapes key currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD, with the dollar index near 101.10 acting as a reference for broader market sentiment. Any stronger-than-expected US data could reinforce USD strength, while softer figures may support a correction. Overall, exchange rate movements are expected to remain range-bound until fresh catalysts emerge.
