Silver (XAG/USD) rebounded sharply on Wednesday, climbing past the $76.00 mark as the US Dollar weakened across the board. The currency pair recovered losses from the previous two sessions, with traders noting that USD softness against major rivals like the EUR and JPY supported silver's upward momentum. At the time of writing, XAG/USD reached session highs, signaling renewed buying interest among forex and commodity investors.  

Market participants attributed the rally to a combination of technical and fundamental factors. The greenback’s retreat pushed the exchange rate lower, making dollar-denominated silver more attractive to overseas buyers. Trading volumes picked up as momentum traders sought to capitalize on the bounce, while analysts highlighted that resistance around $76.50 could test bullish sentiment if the USD continues to underperform.  

The rebound in silver suggests potential near-term upside, though market watchers remain cautious amid ongoing currency fluctuations. A softer USD may continue to lift XAG/USD, with the next key levels eyed at $77.00 and $78.00. Traders are closely monitoring macroeconomic indicators and central bank cues, which could drive further volatility in both the currency pair and broader forex markets.
