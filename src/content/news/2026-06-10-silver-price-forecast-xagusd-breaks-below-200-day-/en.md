Silver extended its sharp decline in Friday trading, with XAG/USD falling below the $65.00 level and deepening its week-long losses to more than 6%. The precious metal was down nearly 2.50% on the day as selling pressure intensified across commodity markets. The move pushed the currency pair decisively beneath the 200-day Simple Moving Average (SMA) at $67.25, a key technical support level closely monitored by traders.

The break below the 200-day SMA signals weakening bullish momentum and reflects growing caution among investors amid shifting sentiment toward USD-denominated assets. As the US Dollar remains firm against major currencies such as the EUR, market participants have continued to reduce exposure to risk-sensitive instruments, weighing on silver prices and broader precious metals trading activity.

From a technical perspective, the loss of support at $67.25 increases the likelihood of further downside in XAG/USD. Traders are now turning their attention to the year-to-date low, which has emerged as the next significant target for sellers.
