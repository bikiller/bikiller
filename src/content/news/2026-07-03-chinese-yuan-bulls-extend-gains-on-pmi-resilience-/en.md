Societe Generale notes that stronger-than-expected China PMI data point to slow but steady economic growth, easing expectations for aggressive policy support. In the foreign exchange market, this reduces the urgency for the PBoC to cut rates or loosen liquidity further, supporting sentiment toward the Chinese yuan versus major currency pairs, including USD and other G10 currencies.  

USD/CNY has slipped back below its 50-day moving average, a key technical level closely watched by forex traders. The move signals improving yuan momentum in short-term trading, with exchange rate dynamics shifting in favor of CNY bulls. Market participants see this break as reinforcing a more constructive near-term outlook for the currency pair amid softer dollar positioning.  

Underlying yuan strength is also being supported by resilient Chinese exports, buoyed by global demand linked to the artificial intelligence boom. This external driver helps stabilize trade flows and enhance FX inflows, improving sentiment across currency markets. As a result, the yuan’s performance may influence broader USD and EUR trading patterns across emerging Asia.
