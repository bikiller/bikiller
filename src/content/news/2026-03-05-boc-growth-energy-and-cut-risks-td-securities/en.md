TD Securities analysts believe the Bank of Canada (BoC) faces a challenging balancing act between trade uncertainty, rising oil prices, and domestic demand. As the Canadian economy grapples with these factors, the BoC must ensure GDP growth remains above potential levels to avoid disinflationary risks and potential rate cuts. The impact of oil prices on the Canadian dollar (CAD) remains a critical factor in market sentiment, particularly as the USD/CAD exchange rate fluctuates in response to global oil trends.

Looking ahead, Q1 data will be pivotal in determining the BoC's next move. Should domestic demand and oil prices continue to support growth, the central bank may hold off on cutting rates. However, any signs of a slowdown could trigger a shift in policy, leading to increased volatility in currency pairs such as EUR/CAD and USD/CAD. Market participants will closely monitor these developments, as they could have significant implications for trading strategies in the coming months.
