Gold (XAU/USD) surged on Friday, testing the $4,600 mark as traders digest news surrounding a potential US-Iran deal. After slipping to a two-month low of $4,366 on Thursday, the precious metal rebounded sharply, currently trading around $4,583. The uptick in gold has weighed on the US Dollar, prompting broader movements in currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD as market participants reassess risk sentiment.

Oil prices faced downward pressure amid the same geopolitical optimism, reflecting expectations of increased supply if a deal is finalized. This dynamic has fueled speculative trading in both commodities and FX markets, with investors shifting funds from USD into gold and other safe-haven assets. Exchange rates have fluctuated in tandem, highlighting the interconnectedness of energy, geopolitics, and forex trading flows.

Technically, XAU/USD has shown resilience above $4,500, signaling potential for further gains if the US Dollar continues to soften. Traders are closely watching momentum indicators and support levels, while positioning in correlated currencies like USD/JPY and AUD/USD reflects the evolving market sentiment. With both geopolitical catalysts and macro data shaping trading decisions, the gold and FX markets remain highly sensitive to the next headlines.
