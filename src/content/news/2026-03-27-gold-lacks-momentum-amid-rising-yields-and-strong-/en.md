Gold (XAU/USD) faces difficulty in gaining momentum as rising US Treasury yields and a strong US Dollar exert pressure on the precious metal. Despite geopolitical tensions surrounding the US-Israel conflict and potential escalation with Iran, gold's appeal as a safe haven has been limited. The yellow metal is trading near $1,860, unable to capitalize on market uncertainty due to the dominance of the USD in the forex market.

The US Dollar remains robust as economic data supports expectations of higher interest rates, further weighing on gold. With yields on the 10-year US Treasury note pushing higher, demand for non-yielding assets like gold weakens. Meanwhile, the EUR/USD exchange rate is also reflecting USD strength, trading around 1.0600, as traders remain cautious in the face of global market volatility. The combination of stronger yields and a resilient USD may keep gold under pressure in the short term.
