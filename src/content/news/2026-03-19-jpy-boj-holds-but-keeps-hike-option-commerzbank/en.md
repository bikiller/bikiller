The Bank of Japan (BoJ) held interest rates steady with an 8–1 majority, as anticipated, signaling a continued cautious approach to monetary policy. Despite maintaining the current rates, the BoJ emphasized its readiness to raise them if the economy develops as expected. Market participants are pricing in a 60% chance of a rate hike by April, with the potential for further volatility in the JPY.

As trading progresses, the USD/JPY pair remains sensitive to BoJ's decisions, with traders keeping a close eye on any indications of policy tightening. While the current exchange rate trends continue to reflect a more dovish stance from the BoJ, any shift towards higher rates could influence the yen's performance against major currencies like the EUR. Investors will likely adjust positions in anticipation of the upcoming policy shifts.
