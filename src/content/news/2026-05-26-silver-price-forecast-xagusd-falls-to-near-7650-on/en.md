Silver prices retreated during Tuesday’s Asian trading session, with XAG/USD slipping toward $76.40 per troy ounce after posting gains in the previous session. The precious metal faced renewed pressure as investors reassessed geopolitical risks surrounding possible US-Iran peace developments, prompting a cautious shift in market sentiment. The softer demand for safe-haven assets weighed on silver trading, while the USD exchange rate remained relatively stable against major currency pairs including EUR/USD.

Market participants also monitored broader forex and commodity flows as traders balanced geopolitical uncertainty with expectations for US monetary policy. A firmer US Dollar limited upside momentum in silver prices, even as lingering concerns over global growth and inflation continued to support demand for defensive assets. Analysts noted that XAG/USD remains vulnerable to further downside if the greenback strengthens across the currency market.

From a technical perspective, silver is hovering near the key $76.50 support zone, with traders watching whether the metal can stabilize above that level. A sustained move below support could trigger additional selling pressure in the commodity and forex markets, while any renewed geopolitical tensions may help revive bullish sentiment. Investors are also tracking upcoming US economic data and Federal Reserve signals for clues on future USD direction and broader trading conditions.
