The US Dollar (USD) is gaining traction ahead of the highly anticipated Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report, with its strength further bolstered by rising oil prices. Crude surged to its highest point since July 2024, spiking on concerns of disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz and attacks on vessels in the region. This uptick in oil prices has supported the USD, particularly against major pairs like EUR/USD, which continues to struggle below the 1.0600 level.

The ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East have put additional pressure on global markets, pushing up the price of oil and, by extension, benefiting the USD. The USD/JPY pair is holding steady above 146.00, reflecting the dollar's strength, while EUR/USD trades near 1.0550. Traders are keenly awaiting the NFP data, which could provide further momentum for the USD if it shows stronger-than-expected job growth.
