The USD/JPY currency pair traded largely flat on Tuesday as the Japanese Yen (JPY) stabilized against the US Dollar (USD). After posting modest early gains during the Asian session, the exchange rate surrendered momentum as the Greenback drifted slightly lower. Cautious trading conditions dominated the forex market, with investors closely monitoring geopolitical developments and adjusting positions across major currency pairs.

Market sentiment remained fragile as traders assessed the potential impact of escalating tensions between the United States and Iran, particularly the risks such conflict could pose to global energy supply. Concerns about oil disruptions tend to influence safe-haven demand, often supporting the Yen in periods of uncertainty. As a result, the USD/JPY exchange rate struggled to maintain upward traction despite earlier USD strength.

The muted movement in the currency pair reflects a broader wait-and-see approach across global FX trading. Investors are balancing geopolitical risk with expectations for US economic signals and broader currency market trends involving the USD and other majors such as the EUR. Until clearer catalysts emerge, the USD/JPY exchange rate may remain range-bound as traders manage risk exposure and monitor developments in energy markets and global politics.
