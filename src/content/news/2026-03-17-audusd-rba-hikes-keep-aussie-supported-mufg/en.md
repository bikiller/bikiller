The Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) raised interest rates by 25 basis points for the second consecutive meeting, pushing the cash rate to 4.10%. This makes the Australian Dollar (AUD) the beneficiary of the highest policy rate among G10 central banks. The AUD/USD exchange rate surged initially, as traders responded positively to the hawkish move, but gains later started to fade.

Despite the RBA's action, the strength of the Aussie Dollar against the US Dollar diminished as market sentiment adjusted. The AUD/USD remains sensitive to shifts in global risk appetite and the ongoing monetary policies of the Federal Reserve. As the RBA tightens, it maintains support for the AUD, but any sustained strength will depend on broader market dynamics, including US interest rate expectations.
