The USD/JPY currency pair pulls back from a one-month high on Wednesday as the US Dollar (USD) loses momentum after two days of strong gains. The exchange rate has seen a modest decline, with the Yen (JPY) regaining some ground. At 1:00 PM GMT, USD/JPY trades at 131.25, down 0.3% from its recent peak. Market participants are closely watching developments amid heightened geopolitical tensions, particularly the ongoing US-Iran conflict.

While the US Dollar had enjoyed a rally, buoyed by solid economic data and expectations of continued Federal Reserve tightening, investor sentiment is now shifting. The recent pullback in USD/JPY reflects a market pause as traders weigh the risks of global instability. The Yen's rebound may be limited, however, as traders remain cautious in the face of looming uncertainties, with the 130.50 level acting as a key support for the pair.
