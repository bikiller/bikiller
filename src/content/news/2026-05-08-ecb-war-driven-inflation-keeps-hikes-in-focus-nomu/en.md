Even with a potential swift resolution to the US-Iran conflict and a reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, the European Central Bank (ECB) is expected to maintain its hawkish stance, Nomura analysts led by George Buckley suggest. Persistent inflationary pressures, driven largely by the war, keep interest rate hikes in focus, signaling continued strength for the euro against major currencies. Traders are watching the EUR/USD pair closely for signs of ECB action.  

Market participants have priced in a June rate increase, keeping EUR trading resilient near key technical levels. The USD remains under pressure amid expectations of slower Federal Reserve tightening, supporting EUR/USD exchange rates around the 1.1000–1.1050 range. Nomura highlights that even easing geopolitical tensions may not offset core inflation concerns, leaving trading flows skewed toward euro strength.  

Analysts note that a sustained hawkish ECB could prompt further capital inflows into the euro, boosting trading volumes across EUR/USD and EUR/GBP pairs. FX markets are positioning for a volatile period, with short-term traders eyeing breakout levels while long-term investors focus on inflation-driven monetary policy divergence between the eurozone and the US. The interplay of geopolitics and central bank decisions remains central to currency pair dynamics.
