WTI oil prices surged on Monday, briefly hitting a peak above $110 per barrel during the Asian session, the highest since mid-2022. At the time of writing, West Texas Intermediate was trading around $100.70, marking a 13.70% jump on the day. The spike was driven by escalating tensions in the Middle East, which have spooked global markets and raised concerns about oil supply disruptions.

However, WTI's upward momentum faded as the US government initiated discussions on tapping into emergency oil reserves to stabilize prices. As these talks progressed, oil prices retreated, signaling that traders are now focused on potential interventions from US authorities to counteract supply shocks. The fluctuations in oil prices are closely watched by forex traders, particularly those trading USD/EUR and other related pairs, as energy prices have a significant impact on inflation and broader economic conditions.
