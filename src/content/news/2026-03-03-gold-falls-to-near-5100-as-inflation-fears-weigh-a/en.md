Gold dipped toward $5,100 in early Asian trading on Wednesday, pressured by renewed strength in the US Dollar (USD) and fading expectations of imminent Federal Reserve rate cuts. The XAU/USD currency pair traded lower as investors weighed persistent inflation concerns against ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Market participants noted that safe-haven demand for gold was being tempered by rising USD exchange rates.  

The shift in sentiment came as the US Dollar gained against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, lifting the cost of gold for holders of other currencies. Traders highlighted that the combination of a firmer USD and subdued expectations for monetary easing was curbing buying interest in bullion. Technical analysts pointed to resistance around $5,120 and support near $5,090 as key levels for intraday trading.  

Market watchers said that while geopolitical risks continue to underpin some safe-haven flows, broader macroeconomic forces are dictating near-term movements in gold. The precious metal’s retreat underscores the inverse relationship with the USD and reflects traders’ sensitivity to inflation data, interest rate projections, and cross-currency exchange rate fluctuations. Positions in XAU/USD remain closely monitored for signs of a rebound or further downside.
