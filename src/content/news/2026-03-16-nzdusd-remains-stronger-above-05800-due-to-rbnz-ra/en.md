NZD/USD maintained its upward momentum above the 0.5800 mark, trading near 0.5810 in Asian session hours on Monday. After four consecutive days of losses, the currency pair has found support as market participants weigh the potential for another Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ) rate hike. The New Zealand Dollar’s resilience reflects growing optimism over the central bank’s hawkish stance amid stable domestic economic indicators.  

The pair’s gains were further bolstered by recent Chinese economic data, which influenced sentiment across the Asia-Pacific currency markets. Traders noted that stronger-than-expected manufacturing and trade figures from China provided indirect support for the NZD, encouraging speculative buying against the USD. Meanwhile, broader market dynamics saw the US Dollar maintain mixed performance, keeping the exchange rate movements in focus for forex traders.  

Technical analysts highlight that sustaining above 0.5800 could pave the way for NZD/USD to test higher resistance levels around 0.5850. The combination of RBNZ expectations and external economic factors suggests continued volatility in the currency pair. Traders are monitoring closely for shifts in USD sentiment and risk appetite, with potential implications for EUR, JPY, and other major forex pairs in the coming sessions.
