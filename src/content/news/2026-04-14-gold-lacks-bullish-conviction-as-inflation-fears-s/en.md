Gold (XAU/USD) is maintaining a mild upward bias as it approaches the European trading session on Tuesday, with the exchange rate hovering around $4,765, reflecting a 0.50% gain for the day. Despite the advance, the metal shows limited bullish conviction, as investors remain cautious amid rising concerns over inflation and its potential impact on the U.S. dollar. The dollar’s resilience continues to influence gold’s short-term trajectory.  

Market participants are weighing a mix of factors, including hopes for progress in Iran diplomacy, which is adding complexity to risk sentiment across currency pairs. While gold benefits from safe-haven demand, the broader forex market shows the USD retaining strength against major counterparts like the EUR, limiting room for a sharper rally in precious metals. Traders are closely watching technical levels for clues on possible breakout or pullback points.  

Analysts suggest that unless inflation signals weaken or geopolitical developments provide fresh impetus, gold’s advance may remain capped. Exchange rate dynamics, especially in USD pairs, are expected to continue dictating gold’s performance. Traders are monitoring whether XAU/USD can sustain above current levels or retreat in response to renewed dollar strength, highlighting the delicate balance between macroeconomic pressures and geopolitical optimism.
