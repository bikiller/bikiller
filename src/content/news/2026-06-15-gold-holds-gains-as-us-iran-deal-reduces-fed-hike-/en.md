Gold (XAU/USD) trades with mild gains during the early Asian session on Tuesday, extending its recent rally after the United States and Iran reached a comprehensive framework deal aimed at ending hostilities. The development has eased geopolitical risk and inflation concerns, prompting traders to reassess the outlook for the currency pair and broader USD-denominated asset flows.

At the same time, reduced expectations for further Federal Reserve rate hikes have supported gold, as lower yields improve demand for non-yielding assets. In forex trading, a softer USD exchange rate typically boosts XAU/USD pricing, making the metal more attractive to investors seeking diversification amid shifting monetary policy signals and risk appetite in global markets.

Looking ahead, traders will monitor USD movement, Fed commentary, and geopolitical developments for further direction in the currency pair. The XAU/USD exchange rate may remain sensitive to shifts in risk sentiment and interest rate expectations, with volatility likely to persist in short-term trading conditions across major forex markets, especially as USD liquidity and macro data remain in focus.
