EUR/USD trades in positive territory around 1.1370 during early European trading on Thursday, extending a modest rebound above the 1.1350 level. The currency pair shows cautious buying interest after recent pressure, with the exchange rate stabilizing as traders reassess short-term positioning in the forex market amid mixed USD demand conditions.

Hawkish signals from newly appointed Federal Reserve chair Kevin Warsh continue to drive USD sentiment, with markets increasingly pricing in a potential US interest rate hike as soon as September. The shift in monetary policy expectations has supported the dollar and kept pressure on the EUR/USD exchange rate in global markets.

Despite the intraday rebound, the broader outlook for the currency pair remains bearish while EUR/USD stays below a key resistance zone. Sustained USD strength could limit further upside, keeping trading sentiment fragile and exposing the exchange rate to renewed downside risks if momentum fades. Outlook remains cautious.
