Asian stock markets saw significant declines on Tuesday, as concerns over persistently high oil prices continue to weigh on investor sentiment. Despite rising optimism over the potential end of the Middle East conflict, fears that elevated energy costs could disrupt global growth kept risk appetite subdued. The Nikkei 225 fell 1.5%, while the Hang Seng dropped 2.2%, reflecting broader regional market losses.

In forex markets, the USD remained resilient, supported by concerns about higher oil prices, with the USD/JPY edging higher to 133.45. Meanwhile, the EUR/USD traded at 1.0850, marking a slight drop as the euro struggled to gain traction amidst global risk-off sentiment. Oil’s persistent upward pressure adds a layer of complexity to global trade, influencing both equity and currency markets, as traders brace for further volatility in the coming days.
