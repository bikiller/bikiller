The USD/JPY currency pair held steady around the 159.60 mark during the Asian session on Friday, with little movement observed amid ongoing geopolitical concerns in the Middle East. The lack of significant volatility is partly attributed to reduced trading volumes, as many market participants are away for the Good Friday holiday. As a result, market activity remains subdued.

Traders are now focusing on the release of the U.S. March Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report later today, which is expected to provide insights into the health of the U.S. labor market and could influence the future direction of the USD. Any major deviation from expectations could trigger increased volatility in the USD/JPY exchange rate, with potential implications for broader market sentiment and monetary policy outlook.
