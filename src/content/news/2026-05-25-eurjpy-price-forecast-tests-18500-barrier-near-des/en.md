EUR/JPY climbed for a second consecutive session on Monday, with the currency pair trading near 184.90 during Asian market hours as buyers continued to test the key 185.00 psychological barrier. The exchange rate advanced toward the upper boundary of a descending channel, signaling that bullish momentum is strengthening despite the broader corrective structure that has capped gains in recent weeks.

The euro found support from improving risk sentiment and steady demand for EUR crosses, while the Japanese yen remained under pressure amid subdued safe-haven flows and expectations that the Bank of Japan will maintain accommodative monetary settings. The weaker JPY also reflected ongoing divergence between regional interest-rate outlooks and broader USD positioning across global forex trading markets.

Technically, EUR/JPY is approaching a critical resistance zone around 185.00, closely aligned with the descending channel top. A sustained break above this level could open the door toward further upside targets and reinforce bullish sentiment for the currency pair. However, failure to clear resistance may trigger renewed selling pressure, potentially pushing the exchange rate back toward near-term support levels as traders monitor USD trends, bond yields, and overall market volatility.
