The EUR/JPY currency pair remains within a narrow range, trading around the 184.00 mark, despite some intraday retracements. Risk sentiment stays elevated, with traders maintaining optimism in broader markets. However, the Japanese Yen has strengthened against the Euro, reflecting growing expectations of policy action from the Bank of Japan (BoJ). 

The BoJ's monetary outlook and intervention risks are key factors driving the Yen's recent gains. As market participants price in potential BoJ moves, the Euro's weakness is becoming more apparent, keeping the EUR/JPY exchange rate capped below critical levels. With high volatility in global markets, any shifts in BoJ policy could trigger further movement in the pair.
