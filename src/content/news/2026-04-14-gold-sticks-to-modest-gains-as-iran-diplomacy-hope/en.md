Gold (XAU/USD) maintained a firm footing during the Asian session on Tuesday, trading near $4,775 and marking a gain of over 0.65% for the day. The safe-haven metal’s modest rally comes as optimism surrounding renewed diplomacy with Iran bolsters risk sentiment, while lingering doubts over the Federal Reserve’s next move continue to weigh on the US dollar. Currency traders noted that a softer USD tends to support bullion’s appeal against major currencies like the EUR and JPY.  

Market participants highlighted that gold’s exchange rate remains sensitive to shifts in both geopolitics and monetary policy expectations. Technical charts suggest that sustaining the current level could open the door for further upside, with $4,780 and $4,790 identified as short-term resistance levels. Meanwhile, spot trading in other currency pairs, including USD/JPY and EUR/USD, reflected the same cautious sentiment, with investors monitoring central bank signals for clues on future rate adjustments.  

Analysts suggest that if the USD continues to struggle against a basket of currencies, gold may see continued inflows from traders seeking a hedge. However, any concrete developments in Iran negotiations or unexpected Fed commentary could quickly reshape the trading environment. For now, XAU/USD’s steady advance highlights the interplay between geopolitical optimism and currency market dynamics, leaving traders alert to both macroeconomic data and headline risks.
