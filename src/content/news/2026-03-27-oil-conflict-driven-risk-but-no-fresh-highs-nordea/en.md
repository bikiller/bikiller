Despite recent geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, oil prices have failed to reach new highs, according to Nordea's Jan von Gerich. While global markets saw volatility, with large swings, oil remains steady without breaking key resistance levels. This stagnation comes amidst a surge in German yields and a decline in equities, keeping the commodity in a range-bound pattern.

As oil stabilizes, the implications for forex markets are significant. The lack of fresh highs in oil may prevent the USD from gaining additional strength against the EUR. Oil-related risk-off sentiment could support the USD, but the steady price of crude limits further upside in the greenback's trading. Traders should keep an eye on market reactions, as any breakout or renewed geopolitical risks could shift the dynamics for oil and major currency pairs.
