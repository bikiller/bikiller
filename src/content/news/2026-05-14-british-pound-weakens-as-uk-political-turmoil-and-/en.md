The British Pound is under pressure against the US Dollar, with the GBP/USD currency pair trading around 1.3482, down nearly 0.30% on Thursday. Market participants are responding to growing political uncertainty in the United Kingdom, which has contributed to the pound’s decline. This marks the third consecutive day of losses for the pair, reflecting cautious sentiment among forex traders navigating current UK developments.  

The stronger USD continues to weigh on the exchange rate, with investors favoring dollar-denominated assets amid global risk aversion. Technical analysts note that GBP/USD remains below key resistance levels, keeping the currency pair on the defensive. Meanwhile, broader market dynamics, including upcoming economic data from both the UK and US, are likely to influence short-term trading momentum.  

Traders are closely watching how ongoing political instability could affect monetary policy expectations and investor confidence. A sustained decline in the pound could impact cross-currency flows, including EUR/GBP movements, and might trigger wider adjustments in the forex market. For now, GBP/USD is trading cautiously, with market participants assessing both domestic and international factors shaping the currency’s near-term trajectory.
