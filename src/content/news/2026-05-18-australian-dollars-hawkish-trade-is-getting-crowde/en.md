The Australian Dollar edged higher on Monday, finding support at the 0.7120 level during early European trading. As the session progressed, the currency pair climbed toward 0.7180, benefiting from a softer US Dollar and an improving risk sentiment. Traders appeared drawn to the AUD’s hawkish outlook, adding momentum to its recovery against major peers including the USD and EUR.  

Market participants noted that the exchange rate dynamics reflected both technical resilience and broader risk appetite. The rebound from 0.7120 suggested buyers were active near key support, while the move toward 0.7180 highlighted the influence of a modestly weaker USD. These movements underscore the interplay between central bank expectations and risk-driven flows in the currency markets.  

Analysts suggest that if the AUD maintains above 0.7150, it could continue to attract speculative interest, potentially challenging near-term resistance levels. Trading activity is likely to remain sensitive to shifts in the USD and global sentiment, with the currency pair offering insight into broader forex trends as investors weigh economic data and market positioning.
