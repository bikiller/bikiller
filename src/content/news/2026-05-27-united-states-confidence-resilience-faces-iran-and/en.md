US consumer sentiment showed resilience in May as the Conference Board's confidence index dipped only slightly, suggesting that household optimism remains relatively firm despite broader economic uncertainties. TD Securities notes that this measure continues to outperform other sentiment indicators, supporting steady demand and offering a subtle boost to USD trading amid cautious market conditions.

Geopolitical tensions with Iran and fluctuating oil prices are adding complexity to currency movements. The USD has seen mixed trading against major pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY, with investors weighing safe-haven flows against risk-on appetite. Oil price volatility is keeping commodity-linked currencies such as CAD and NOK under pressure, influencing broader exchange rate dynamics in forex markets.

For traders, these developments imply a cautiously bullish outlook for the USD, though risk events could trigger sharp intraday swings. Technical levels around key support and resistance points in EUR/USD and GBP/USD are likely to guide short-term positioning. Market participants remain alert to economic data and geopolitical headlines, as these factors continue to shape forex trading strategies and exchange rate expectations.
