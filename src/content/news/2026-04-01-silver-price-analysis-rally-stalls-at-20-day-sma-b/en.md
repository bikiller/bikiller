Silver struggled to extend its recent gains on Wednesday, with XAG/USD hovering around $75.00 as momentum from earlier rallies cooled. The white metal failed to break above its 20-day simple moving average (SMA) at $76.90, capping upside potential. Traders remain cautious as US Treasury yields pared losses, weighing on risk-sensitive assets and limiting bullish enthusiasm in the silver market. At the time of writing, XAG/USD trades at $75.07, essentially flat.  

The USD maintained relative strength against other major currencies, influencing the exchange rate for silver and broader commodities. Market participants highlighted the difficulty of sustaining rallies when key technical resistance aligns with supportive USD dynamics. The consolidation near $75.00 suggests investors are awaiting fresh catalysts, including economic data and Treasury movements, before committing to directional trades.  

Analysts note that failure to reclaim the 20-day SMA could signal further sideways trading or short-term retracement. For traders, monitoring the XAG/USD currency pair in relation to USD performance and Treasury yields remains critical. The current trading range underscores the delicate balance between bullish positioning and caution amid fluctuating market conditions, with implications for both short-term momentum and medium-term trend development.
