EUR/USD continued its upward trajectory on Monday, gaining momentum as the US Dollar weakened to fresh six-week lows. The US Dollar Index (DXY) dipped to around 98.36, reflecting a broad-based retreat in USD demand. At the time of writing, the euro-dollar currency pair trades at 1.1757, marking a 0.32% rise as traders responded to improved market sentiment and softer dollar positioning.  

The rise in EUR/USD comes amid a backdrop of heightened risk appetite, with investors shifting away from safe-haven USD holdings. The euro has benefited from this environment, as the exchange rate advances on expectations of stabilizing European economic data and potential easing of global financial concerns. Market participants note that the currency pair’s climb is reinforced by technical buying near key support levels in the USD.  

Looking ahead, analysts suggest that the euro-dollar trading could maintain its momentum if the dollar continues to lose ground, particularly against a basket of major currencies. With the DXY hovering near 98.36, further declines could accelerate the pair’s gains, while EUR/USD levels around 1.1750–1.1760 may act as short-term resistance. Traders remain attentive to macroeconomic indicators and market sentiment shifts that could influence the next leg of this move.
