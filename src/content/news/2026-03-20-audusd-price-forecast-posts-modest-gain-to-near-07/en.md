The AUD/USD currency pair edged higher during early European trading on Friday, hovering near the 0.7100 level after posting modest gains around 0.7090. Traders reacted to the Reserve Bank of Australia’s hawkish comments, which reinforced expectations for continued policy support for the Australian Dollar. The pair’s exchange rate remains underpinned by a combination of broad USD weakness and optimism surrounding the local economy.  

Technical indicators suggest a period of consolidation, with the Relative Strength Index (RSI) sitting in neutral territory, signaling limited momentum in either direction. Market participants are closely watching key support and resistance levels near 0.7050 and 0.7120, respectively, as intraday trading unfolds. The AUD’s modest appreciation against the USD reflects steady investor confidence, even as the broader forex market remains sensitive to US data and eurozone developments.  

Looking ahead, traders anticipate that the currency pair could remain range-bound unless significant macroeconomic data shifts sentiment. With the USD broadly under pressure and the RBA maintaining a hawkish tone, the AUD may continue to test levels around 0.7100. Investors are balancing technical signals with fundamental drivers, including global risk appetite and cross-currency flows involving EUR, USD, and other major currencies.
