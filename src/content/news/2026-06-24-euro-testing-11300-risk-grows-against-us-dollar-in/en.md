EUR/USD currency pair remains under pressure as spot exchange rate tests the 1.1300 level, with traders reacting to a broader equity sell-off and disappointing German PMI data. The weaker manufacturing readings have reinforced concerns about slowing Eurozone momentum, while US resilience continues to support a US–EU growth divergence narrative that weighs on the euro in forex trading.

According to ING’s Francesco Pesole, widening EUR:USD two-year swap differentials are adding further downward pressure on the euro, as rate expectations continue to favor the US dollar. The renewed Dollar risk premium has strengthened USD demand across major currency pairs, contributing to more defensive positioning in EUR/USD trading and limiting recovery attempts above recent lows.

Market participants are now closely watching the 1.1300 support level, with a sustained break potentially exposing further downside in EUR/USD as macro and rate differentials continue to favor the US dollar. Near-term trading is expected to remain volatile, with sentiment driven by data surprises and shifts in global risk appetite.
