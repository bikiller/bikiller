Silver (XAG/USD) climbed sharply on Wednesday, trading around $76.00, marking a 3.11% gain as the precious metal regained appeal among investors. The rebound followed a retreat in US Treasury yields, which reduced the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like silver. The move came amid a broader risk-off sentiment, with traders keeping a close eye on USD exchange rate fluctuations versus major currencies such as EUR and JPY.  

Geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran also boosted safe-haven demand, supporting silver prices. The currency pair dynamics, including softer USD momentum, encouraged traders to rotate into precious metals, while technical levels near $75 acted as a floor for the bullish move. Market participants noted increased trading volumes in both spot and futures contracts, signaling strong investor interest.  

Analysts suggest that sustained low yields and continued geopolitical uncertainty could keep silver elevated, potentially testing resistance around $78–$79. Currency pair movements, especially USD strength or weakness, remain key drivers for traders seeking hedges. With broader forex market volatility, silver’s performance is increasingly intertwined with cross-currency flows, positioning it as a sensitive barometer for risk sentiment.
