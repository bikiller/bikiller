Australia’s services sector showed a slightly slower pace in May, with the final S&P Global Services PMI reading at 48.7, down from 50.7 in the previous month but above forecasts of 47.7. While still below the 50 mark that signals expansion, the stronger-than-expected result highlights resilience in domestic services activity, which could support the Australian dollar in short-term trading against the USD and other major currencies.

The AUD/USD exchange rate may see increased volatility as traders digest this data alongside broader economic signals. The PMI suggests that despite headwinds, demand in services remains relatively robust, which could influence market positioning and speculative flows in forex markets. Technical levels around 0.6700 may act as immediate support, while 0.6800 could serve as a resistance target if risk sentiment improves.

Investors will be watching how this PMI interacts with other indicators, including employment and inflation metrics, to gauge the Reserve Bank of Australia’s next moves. Strength in services may offer the AUD some lift versus the USD, EUR, and JPY, especially in light of ongoing global monetary tightening. Traders should monitor intraday trading volumes and currency pair correlations to anticipate potential swings in the coming sessions.
