The Canadian Dollar is under pressure as USD/CAD extended gains for a third consecutive day on Thursday. The currency pair climbed amid renewed demand for the US Dollar, with traders weighing recent US economic data that bolstered expectations of a more hawkish Federal Reserve stance. Despite oil prices remaining elevated, the loonie struggled to gain traction, reflecting broader USD strength across major currency pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD.

Market participants are citing geopolitical developments and persistent inflation signals in the US as key drivers behind the USD’s resilience. Investors are actively trading the USD/CAD pair, adjusting positions ahead of upcoming Fed communications, while monitoring technical levels that could influence short-term exchange rate movements. The loonie’s resistance appears capped near 1.3550, with support levels around 1.3450 drawing attention from technical traders.

Analysts suggest the current environment favors the USD in the near term, with the Canadian Dollar likely to remain sensitive to shifts in US policy expectations and global risk sentiment. Traders are also eyeing broader forex market implications, as strength in USD pairs such as USD/EUR and USD/JPY could affect cross-currency flows and hedging strategies, reinforcing the dominant trend in North American and global trading desks.
