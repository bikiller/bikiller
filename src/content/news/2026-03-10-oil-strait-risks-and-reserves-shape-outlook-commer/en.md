Oil markets have cooled after an initial surge triggered by the Iran conflict, with Brent and WTI retreating from war-driven highs. Commerzbank’s Carsten Fritsch points out that risks around the Strait of Hormuz remain the dominant factor shaping crude prices. While G7 reserve releases and sizable OECD inventories offer temporary relief, they are unlikely to fully offset potential supply disruptions from the Gulf, keeping traders attentive to geopolitical tensions.

The recent volatility has also influenced currency markets, particularly the USD and commodity-linked pairs. Stronger oil prices tend to support commodity currencies like the CAD and NOK, while risk-sensitive flows have pressured the EUR/USD exchange rate, which has seen increased intraday swings. Traders are watching key technical levels as geopolitical developments continue to dictate short-term trading behavior across forex and commodity markets.

Looking ahead, market participants are weighing temporary supply buffers against persistent Middle East risks. Any escalation in the Iran situation or a blockade at the Strait of Hormuz could trigger renewed spikes in Brent and WTI, prompting broader USD buying and shifts in currency pairs tied to oil exports. Analysts stress that while short-term corrections are possible, the underlying geopolitical supply threat keeps oil and related forex markets on edge.
