The US Dollar weakened on Thursday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering near the 99.10 level as improving global risk sentiment reduced demand for traditional safe-haven assets. Optimism surrounding potential progress in US-Iran negotiations encouraged investors to shift toward higher-yielding currencies, pressuring the Greenback during early trading. The softer USD tone also supported gains across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD.

Market participants remained cautious ahead of further guidance from the Federal Reserve, as policymakers continued to emphasize a data-dependent approach to interest rates. The combination of easing geopolitical tensions and restrained Fed rhetoric weighed on the Dollar’s exchange rate performance, while Treasury yields also struggled to extend recent gains. Analysts noted that a sustained move below the 99.00 region on the DXY could expose additional downside pressure in the near term.

In the broader forex market, traders closely monitored risk-sensitive currencies and commodity-linked pairs as sentiment improved. EUR/USD attracted fresh buying interest, while USD/JPY traded with limited upside amid declining safe-haven flows. Investors now await upcoming US economic data and Fed commentary for clearer direction on USD trading momentum and the next move across major foreign exchange markets.
