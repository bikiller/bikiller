The USD/INR currency pair saw a notable decline on Friday, with the Indian Rupee strengthening against the US Dollar following the Reserve Bank of India’s (RBI) latest interventions. The central bank’s introduction of special credit lines for state-run oil companies has provided additional liquidity for foreign exchange requirements, easing pressure on the rupee. Traders responded quickly, pushing the USD/INR lower in early trading sessions.  

Market participants highlighted that the RBI’s measures aim to curb aggressive dollar purchases, supporting domestic currency stability amid persistent inflows and volatile global markets. The Indian Rupee gained ground against other major currencies as well, with trading activity showing heightened demand for INR amid the easing of external financing concerns. Technical analysts noted key support levels around 82.50 and potential resistance near 83.20, signaling a cautiously bullish environment for the rupee in the short term.  

The broader forex market reflected cautious optimism, as USD selling intensified and emerging market currencies, including the INR, benefited from policy support. Investors monitoring the USD/INR exchange rate observed that central bank interventions could influence capital flows, oil import financing, and trade balances. Analysts anticipate that if the RBI maintains targeted measures, trading in the pair may continue to favor the rupee, while fluctuations in global USD demand will remain a critical driver.
