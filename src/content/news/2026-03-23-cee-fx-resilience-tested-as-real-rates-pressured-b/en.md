Central and Eastern European (CEE) currencies, including the Hungarian Forint (HUF) and Polish Złoty (PLN), have shown notable resilience despite ongoing geopolitical tensions. According to BNY’s Bob Savage, these currencies have maintained stability into the fourth week of the conflict, primarily supported by strong real interest rates and ample liquidity. As a result, both the HUF and PLN have fared better than many other emerging market currencies under pressure.

However, the outlook remains uncertain as real rates face increasing pressure. The strength of the USD, combined with shifting risk sentiment, is likely to test the endurance of these currencies moving forward. With the EUR/HUF and PLN/USD exchange rates holding steady for now, traders are closely monitoring any signs of volatility. A potential tightening in liquidity or further escalation of the conflict could prompt shifts in CEE currency dynamics.
