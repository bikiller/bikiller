Silver (XAG/USD) rebounded from its weekly low of $72.46 on Thursday, climbing to trade near $74.00 and posting gains of more than 1.70% on the day. The precious metal recovered as investors adjusted positions across financial markets, with trading activity reflecting a mixed risk environment. Despite the advance, the currency pair remained constrained below its 50-day simple moving average (SMA), signaling that bearish pressure has not fully eased.

Market sentiment was shaped by a rotation away from technology stocks and into other sectors, a shift that helped support demand for defensive assets such as silver. At the same time, ongoing tensions linked to the Middle East conflict continued to influence risk appetite. While broader moves in USD- and EUR-related assets contributed to fluctuating exchange rate dynamics, traders remained cautious about extending bullish positions.

From a technical perspective, the failure to break above the 50-day SMA suggests sellers still retain control of the near-term trend. Although the bounce from $72.46 highlights underlying buying interest, sustained gains may require a decisive move beyond key resistance levels. Until then, market participants are likely to monitor both geopolitical developments and USD movements for clues on the next direction in XAG/USD trading.
