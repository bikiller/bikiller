Gold (XAU/USD) struggled to maintain upward momentum during the Asian session on Friday, edging lower after yesterday's gains. The precious metal held above the critical $4,600 level, but the market remains cautious amid a modest bounce in the US Dollar. Despite the pullback, gold is poised for a second consecutive weekly decline, pressured by fluctuations in the broader forex market.

The USD’s rebound, particularly against the EUR and other major currencies, has weighed on demand for gold, traditionally seen as a safe-haven asset. As investors adjust to the shifting dynamics in currency pairs, the precious metal’s outlook remains uncertain. Gold’s ability to sustain its position above $4,600 will be crucial in determining its near-term direction, with mixed market signals continuing to add volatility.
