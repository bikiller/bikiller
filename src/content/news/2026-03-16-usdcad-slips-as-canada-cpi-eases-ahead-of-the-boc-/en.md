USD/CAD traded lower on Monday as the Canadian Dollar (CAD) found support from a weaker US Dollar (USD), with the pair slipping in response. The exchange rate hovered near 1.3670, a level marked by recent fluctuations. While Canadian inflation data showed a slight easing, it failed to trigger a significant market reaction as traders remained focused on global uncertainties, particularly the escalating tensions in the US-Iran conflict.

Despite the inflation data showing a slowdown in Canada’s consumer price index (CPI), the market's attention was dominated by geopolitical risks, leaving little room for CAD gains. The softer US Dollar contributed to the overall strength of the Canadian Dollar, but USD/CAD traders were reluctant to make major moves ahead of the Bank of Canada’s (BoC) upcoming interest rate decision. The broader market sentiment remains cautious as tensions in the Middle East continue to weigh on global markets.
