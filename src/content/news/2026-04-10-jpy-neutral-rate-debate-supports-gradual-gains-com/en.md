The Japanese Yen (JPY) is poised for gradual appreciation in the second half of 2026, driven by expectations of two further interest rate hikes by the Bank of Japan (BoJ). Commerzbank's Volkmar Baur anticipates these rate increases will push Japan's monetary policy closer to a neutral stance, benefiting the yen against the USD and EUR. As the BoJ adjusts its rates, the JPY could see a steady rise in currency pair trading, particularly versus the Dollar and Euro.

The market outlook suggests moderate gains for JPY, with the potential for a tighter exchange rate between USD/JPY and EUR/JPY. As Japan moves toward a more neutral policy, investors are likely to shift their focus to the yen, potentially driving stronger demand. This shift in the BoJ's stance could trigger further volatility in the forex market, with traders closely monitoring upcoming policy announcements.
