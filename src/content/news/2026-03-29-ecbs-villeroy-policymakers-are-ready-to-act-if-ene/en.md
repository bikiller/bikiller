European Central Bank Governing Council member and Bank of France Governor François Villeroy de Galhau signaled readiness to intervene if inflation pressures from rising energy costs intensify. Speaking Monday, he noted that energy-driven price surges could influence broader consumer inflation, prompting the ECB to adjust its monetary stance. Traders reacted cautiously, with the EUR/USD hovering near 1.0870, reflecting market sensitivity to policy signals amid global energy volatility.  

Villeroy emphasized that the ECB is closely monitoring inflation dynamics and exchange rate movements. Analysts suggest that persistent energy shocks could push the euro higher against the dollar if the central bank signals tighter policy sooner than expected. EUR/USD trading remains choppy, testing technical support around 1.0850, while resistance sits near 1.0900, as market participants weigh potential rate adjustments.  

The comments underscore growing uncertainty in forex markets, where energy-linked inflation is shaping investor expectations. USD positions have seen increased hedging, reflecting concerns over ECB action. As traders digest Villeroy’s remarks, eurozone economic indicators and energy price trends will likely guide short-term EUR/USD swings, influencing both carry trades and speculative flows in the currency pair.
