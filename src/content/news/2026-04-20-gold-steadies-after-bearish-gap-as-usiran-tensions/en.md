Gold (XAU/USD) held steady on Monday, following a bearish gap at the week's open. The precious metal’s price action was influenced by rising geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran, which continued to keep markets on edge. As of the latest data, gold's price traded around $1,830 per ounce, maintaining a cautious upward momentum despite initial losses. This price action reflects broader risk aversion, with traders reacting to global uncertainties.

The escalation of US-Iran tensions has sparked volatile moves across financial markets, impacting currency pairs like the EUR/USD and USD/JPY. Investors remain focused on how these geopolitical developments might shape future Fed policy decisions and their potential effects on the USD. Gold’s relatively stable performance amid such volatility underscores its role as a safe-haven asset, with traders monitoring technical levels closely for further signs of price consolidation or potential breakout.
