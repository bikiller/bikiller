The Japanese Yen (JPY) is trading slightly firmer against its G10 peers, outperforming despite continued broad US Dollar (USD) strength, according to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret. The USD/JPY currency pair shows signs of losing upside momentum as stronger PMI data signals improving growth conditions, supporting risk sentiment and stabilizing currency flows.

Market participants note that stabilising yield spreads between the US and Japan are reducing support for the greenback, easing the interest rate differential that previously fueled USD/JPY upside. As a result, trading conditions suggest growing signs of technical exhaustion in the recent USD/JPY advance, with momentum indicators flattening and positioning becoming more cautious across short-term forex flows overall outlook suggests.

Looking ahead, USD/JPY may remain range-bound as traders assess whether the recent rally has fully exhausted. A firmer JPY could emerge if yield stability persists and US macro data underwhelms expectations. However, persistent USD strength may cap downside, keeping the exchange rate sensitive to shifting rate expectations and broader risk sentiment across global forex trading sessions in the near term.
