US equity futures opened the week largely flat as investors balanced heightened geopolitical tensions with an eye on this week’s critical inflation data. The Dow Jones Industrial Average showed little change, reflecting caution after Iran rebuffed US overtures, which sent crude oil prices higher. Brent crude climbed above $88 per barrel, while WTI settled near $83, prompting traders to watch energy-linked sectors closely and assess potential inflation pressures.  

In currency markets, the USD held firm against major peers, with the EUR/USD pair hovering around 1.0970. Safe-haven flows supported the USD/JPY near 145.60, while commodity-linked currencies like the AUD and CAD tracked oil and metal prices higher. Traders noted that central bank guidance and CPI readings could drive further volatility in the coming sessions, shaping exchange rate trends and influencing risk sentiment across global forex markets.  

Technical levels remained key focus points: EUR/USD support sits at 1.0940, resistance near 1.1020, while USD/JPY faces resistance at 146.00. With Wall Street indecisive, forex traders are eyeing short-term trading ranges, liquidity shifts, and momentum signals, anticipating that CPI releases later this week could trigger decisive moves in both equities and major currency pairs. Market participants remain positioned for sudden swings amid geopolitical headlines and inflation surprises.
