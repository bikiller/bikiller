The EUR/USD currency pair is trading around 1.1730 late in the US session on Friday, pulling back from earlier intraday gains. This shift came after US President Donald Trump escalated trade tensions, threatening to hike tariffs on European Union (EU) cars and trucks from 15% to 25%. Trump's comments fueled concerns over the impact on EU exports, boosting demand for the US dollar.

The market reacted to these fresh tariff threats as well as ongoing geopolitical concerns surrounding Iran, pushing the USD higher. Despite initial optimism for the euro, the currency pair's upward momentum was quickly capped as risk sentiment soured. As a result, the EUR/USD exchange rate saw significant volatility, with traders digesting the potential implications of Trump's remarks on both the currency markets and global trade dynamics.
