French President Emmanuel Macron stated on Thursday that reopening the Strait of Hormuz is a complex issue, requiring cooperation with Iran and potentially military involvement. His comments came during the European trading session, sparking a shift in global market sentiment. As tensions in the region continue to rise, oil prices saw a spike, with Brent crude rising over 2% to $75.50 per barrel, which in turn supported the USD, pushing the EUR/USD exchange rate lower to 1.0915.

Market traders are closely monitoring geopolitical risks as they impact energy prices and broader financial markets. The uncertainty surrounding the Strait of Hormuz is a critical factor influencing global supply chains, with potential repercussions for the EUR/USD and oil-linked currency pairs such as USD/JPY. In particular, any further escalation could trigger a flight to safety, strengthening the USD further and weighing on risk-sensitive assets like the EUR. As tensions mount, the market is bracing for volatility in both oil and forex markets.
