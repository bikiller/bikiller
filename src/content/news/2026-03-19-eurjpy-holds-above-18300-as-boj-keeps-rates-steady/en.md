EUR/JPY rebounded in early Asian trading on Thursday, climbing back above the 183.00 level and hovering around 183.30. The currency pair recovered from losses seen in the previous session as investors digested the Bank of Japan’s decision to maintain its short-term interest rate at 0.75%, in line with market expectations. The move kept the Japanese Yen softer against the euro, supporting a higher exchange rate.  

The BoJ’s steady stance contrasts with rising rates in other major economies, leaving the yen vulnerable against currencies like the USD and EUR. Traders note that the lack of tightening signals continues to weigh on JPY demand, prompting flows into the euro and other stronger currencies. Technical support around 183.00 is holding, while resistance near 184.00 could define near-term trading ranges.  

Market participants are now weighing the broader implications for forex trading, particularly against USD pairs where rate differentials are widening. With the euro maintaining strength and the yen subdued, momentum could favor continued gains for EUR/JPY if global risk sentiment remains stable. Analysts suggest monitoring BoJ guidance and broader USD movements for potential triggers that might drive the next leg of the exchange rate action.
