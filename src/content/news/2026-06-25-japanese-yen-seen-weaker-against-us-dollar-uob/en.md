Japanese yen continued to show weakness against the US dollar, with the USD/JPY currency pair holding near recent highs in a consolidative phase. Analysts at United Overseas Bank, Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann, noted that the exchange rate remains confined to a narrow trading structure, reflecting limited directional momentum in the forex market.

Intraday trading is expected to remain contained between 161.40 and 161.90, following a previous session range of 161.46 to 161.83. The tight band suggests subdued volatility in USD/JPY, with traders closely watching short-term resistance near the upper boundary and support levels emerging around the mid-161.40 area in spot forex activity.

Persistent yen weakness underscores ongoing US dollar strength in the forex market, with USD/JPY trading dynamics influenced by yield differentials and carry trade demand. Unless a breakout above 161.90 or below 161.40 occurs, the currency pair may continue range-bound action, while broader USD trends also shape sentiment across EUR/USD and other majors.
