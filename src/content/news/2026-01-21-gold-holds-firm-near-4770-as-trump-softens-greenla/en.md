Gold (XAU/USD) demonstrated resilience during the North American trading session on Wednesday, climbing 0.25% to hold firm near $4,770. This uptick comes after prices briefly touched a record high of $4,888 earlier in the day, driven by shifts in market sentiment.

The movement in gold prices was influenced by US President Donald Trump's softened rhetoric regarding Greenland, which alleviated some geopolitical tensions. As investors reacted to these developments, the exchange rate of gold remained attractive, highlighting its role as a safe haven amid ongoing uncertainties in the forex markets.
