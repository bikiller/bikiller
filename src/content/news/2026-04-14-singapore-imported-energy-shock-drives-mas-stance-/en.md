Singapore’s Monetary Authority (MAS) has adjusted its 2026 inflation forecasts higher, with core and headline ranges now set at 1.5–2.5%, reflecting a sharp rise in imported energy costs, according to UOB strategist Jester Koh. Surging oil and gas prices are expected to feed through into consumer prices via electricity, transportation, and goods, adding renewed pressure on the Singapore dollar and influencing market expectations for monetary policy tightening.  

The SGD has shown increased volatility against major currencies such as the USD and EUR, as traders weigh MAS’s updated outlook. The exchange rate is particularly sensitive to energy-driven inflation, with USD/SGD and EUR/SGD trading reflecting anticipatory positioning. Market participants are monitoring short-term technical levels for potential breakouts as higher commodity prices persist.  

Analysts note that prolonged energy inflation could maintain upward pressure on interest rates, impacting forex flows and trading strategies. Investors are closely watching how MAS’s stance may affect capital movements, with risk appetite and currency pairs adjusting in response to expected adjustments in domestic liquidity and external shocks. The evolving scenario underscores the tight link between commodity prices, inflation, and exchange rate dynamics.
