BNY’s Bob Savage pointed out that the European Central Bank (ECB) is now leaning toward a rate hike in June, signaling a shift from its previous stance of "policy in a good place." This contrasts with the Bank of England (BoE), which remains cautious and awaits further confirmation before adjusting rates. As a result, the EUR/USD exchange rate could see significant volatility in the coming weeks, with traders pricing in expectations for tighter ECB policy.

The shift in tone from the ECB is likely to influence euro trading, particularly against the USD. If the ECB acts in June, it could strengthen the euro, pushing EUR/USD toward key resistance levels. Conversely, a delay in action could result in a pullback, with traders reassessing the outlook for the euro amid global economic uncertainties. Investors will be closely monitoring ECB communications and economic data for any signs of a rate change.
