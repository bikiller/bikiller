Gold prices (XAU/USD) held steady on Thursday as traders remained cautious amid growing uncertainty over the US-Iran ceasefire. The fragile ceasefire in the Middle East kept markets on edge, with investors awaiting key US economic data later in the day. Gold's price hovered around $1,950 per ounce, maintaining its position as a safe haven while global risk sentiment fluctuated.

The ongoing tension in the region, combined with mixed expectations for US data, weighed on broader market movements. As traders monitor the situation, gold's ability to maintain above the $1,940 level remains a focal point for technical analysts. Meanwhile, the USD continued to dominate against major currencies, with the EUR/USD trading at 1.0800, reflecting cautious optimism in the forex markets.
