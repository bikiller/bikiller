Silver (XAG/USD) extended its sharp decline on Tuesday, falling more than 3.5% as investors reacted to rapidly shifting developments surrounding the Middle East conflict. The precious metal remained highly sensitive to geopolitical headlines, with volatility driving heavy selling across commodities and currency markets. At the time of writing, the XAG/USD currency pair was trading near $65.50, marking its lowest level since March 23.

The latest move highlights a significant deterioration in market sentiment toward silver, as traders reduced exposure amid heightened uncertainty and broad risk repricing. While the USD attracted demand as a defensive asset, pressure intensified on silver despite ongoing geopolitical tensions that often support precious metals. The decline in the exchange rate reflects a stronger bearish bias and increasing momentum on the downside.

From a technical perspective, sustained trading below recent support zones has reinforced negative sentiment, encouraging additional selling activity. Market participants are closely monitoring whether XAG/USD can stabilize around current levels or extend losses further. With bearish momentum building, traders are increasingly focusing on the $60 level as a key downside target, while developments in global risk sentiment, USD performance, and major currency pairs such as EUR/USD are expected to remain important drivers of price action.
