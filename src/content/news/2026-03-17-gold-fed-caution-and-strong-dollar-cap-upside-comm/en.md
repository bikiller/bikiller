Gold has retreated roughly 5% since the outbreak of the Iran conflict, struggling to live up to its traditional safe-haven role. Commerzbank’s Commodity Analyst Carsten Fritsch highlights that a firmer USD and shifting expectations around the Fed’s policy stance have capped upside momentum. Investors have favored the USD over bullion, pushing key currency pairs like EUR/USD lower as traders reassess risk.

The precious metal faces technical pressure, with resistance levels remaining stubborn amid elevated Treasury yields. Despite geopolitical tensions, gold’s appeal has been muted, reflecting a market more focused on exchange rate dynamics and central bank signals. Traders navigating USD-denominated assets are weighing potential Fed moves against broader market sentiment, which continues to favor the dollar’s strength over alternative stores of value.

Looking ahead, gold may struggle to regain footing unless USD weakness emerges or risk premiums rise significantly. Currency pair fluctuations, particularly in EUR/USD and USD/JPY, will continue influencing trading strategies, as hedging flows and speculative positioning react to Fed guidance. Analysts suggest that gold’s short-term trajectory remains constrained, even amid ongoing geopolitical uncertainties, underscoring the interplay between monetary policy and commodity markets.
