The US Dollar (USD) remains firm but rangebound, driven by higher oil prices and mixed US-Iran headlines that are influencing risk sentiment. Scotiabank strategists note that the USD's strength persists despite a neutral stance from the Federal Reserve, with the greenback trading within a narrow range against major peers. EUR/USD hovers around 1.0915, while USD/JPY is steady at 134.50, showing limited movement in recent sessions.

Market traders are closely monitoring geopolitical tensions and their impact on oil prices, as these factors continue to fuel risk-off sentiment. This has kept the USD resilient, but it also faces limited upside as global uncertainty keeps trading conditions volatile. The range-bound trading is expected to persist unless there is a clear directional shift in oil dynamics or a significant policy change from the Fed.
