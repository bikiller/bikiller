The US Dollar (USD) continues to find support amid ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, according to ING’s Chris Turner. Rising conflict risk has bolstered safe-haven demand, while several US trading partners have taken steps to defend their own currencies through intervention, tighter monetary policy, or regulatory measures. This dynamic has kept key currency pairs like USD/EUR and USD/JPY under close watch by traders, with exchange rates showing persistent strength for the greenback.

Market participants note that the USD’s resilience is further reinforced by mixed economic data from major economies. While the US shows steady growth, other regions are grappling with slowing activity, prompting central banks to consider defensive measures. As a result, trading volumes in pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD have seen increased volatility, reflecting both the underlying geopolitical uncertainties and market reactions to policy signals abroad.

Analysts suggest that unless tensions ease or external shocks emerge, the USD is likely to maintain its support levels. Investors are keeping a close eye on technical levels in key currency pairs, as sustained strength in the greenback could pressure export-dependent economies and influence global capital flows. For traders, understanding these factors is crucial for navigating exchange rate fluctuations and adjusting positions in a volatile forex environment.
