West Texas Intermediate (WTI) rebounded to around $86.40 per barrel in early Asian trading on Tuesday, recovering from the extreme volatility seen during Monday’s session, where prices surged close to $120. The rally follows disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, keeping supply tight and fueling risk sentiment across global markets. Traders remain cautious as the spike underscores the sensitivity of energy markets to geopolitical tensions.  

The uptick in crude has rippled through forex markets, with the USD gaining against major currency pairs amid safe-haven demand. The EUR/USD exchange rate fell slightly, testing support near 1.0920, while USD/JPY climbed toward 149.50 as investors repositioned for higher energy-driven inflation expectations. Commodity-linked currencies such as the AUD and CAD also showed mixed trading, reflecting sensitivity to WTI price swings.  

Market participants are eyeing the technical levels closely, with WTI support around $85 and resistance near $88 shaping short-term strategies. Analysts warn that extended Strait of Hormuz disruptions could trigger further USD strength and volatility in currency pairs, potentially impacting cross-border flows and emerging market FX. Momentum in oil and forex markets will likely remain intertwined as geopolitical developments evolve.
