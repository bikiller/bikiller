The US Dollar has staged a notable rebound this week as investors reassess geopolitical risks in the Middle East. Hopes for a rapid US‑Iran agreement have dimmed amid fresh military incidents in the Strait of Hormuz, prompting a restart of US naval escorts. The resulting risk-off sentiment has lifted the USD against major peers, with the USD/EUR exchange rate climbing from recent lows and trading firm near key technical levels.  

Traders are closely watching the currency pair dynamics as the market responds to heightened uncertainty. The resurgence in USD demand has pressured the EUR, while safe-haven flows have supported the greenback across other major currencies. Market participants are weighing the potential for further volatility, particularly if regional tensions escalate, influencing trading strategies and short-term positioning in the forex market.  

Technically, the USD shows resilience around key support levels, suggesting the recovery may have room to extend if risk sentiment remains cautious. Analysts highlight that continued geopolitical strain could keep the USD elevated, while the EUR and other risk-sensitive currencies may struggle to reclaim ground. For currency traders, monitoring these developments is critical, as exchange rate shifts could affect cross‑pair correlations and trading opportunities in the coming sessions.
