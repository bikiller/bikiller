The British Pound (GBP) edged higher against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, with the GBP/USD currency pair approaching the 1.3200 level during trading. The exchange rate firmed as investors trimmed USD long positions ahead of key US economic data, leading to mild dollar softness across major forex markets. The move reflects cautious sentiment rather than a decisive shift in broader trading direction.

Market participants are focused on the upcoming US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) Price Index, a crucial inflation gauge for the Federal Reserve. Expectations surrounding potential policy implications have driven defensive positioning, with traders adjusting exposure across USD and EUR currency pairs ahead of potential volatility in the forex market. Liquidity remains thin as investors await clearer inflation signals.

Technically, the 1.3200 handle remains a key psychological level for GBP/USD trading, with near-term direction hinging on the PCE release. A softer US inflation reading could extend Pound gains and weaken the USD further, while stronger data may revive dollar demand and cap upside momentum in the currency pair, reinforcing resistance near current exchange rate levels.
