The Australian dollar retreated from four-year highs on Thursday, with AUD/USD easing 0.5% as trading momentum softened through the afternoon. The currency pair had tested the 0.7280 level, its strongest since June 2022, but gains stalled amid cautious positioning ahead of the Reserve Bank of Australia’s minutes and upcoming Chinese economic data.

Market participants cited the pullback as a natural correction after the recent surge in the Australian dollar against the USD and other major currencies like the EUR. Traders are weighing the implications of central bank guidance and external demand signals, with the exchange rate now navigating a delicate balance between bullish momentum and potential resistance at multi-year highs.

Analysts suggest that while the short-term retracement may provide opportunities for USD buyers, broader trends still favor AUD strength if the RBA signals tighter policy or Chinese data shows robust activity. For currency traders, the session highlighted the sensitivity of AUD/USD to macroeconomic cues and the importance of technical levels like 0.7280 in shaping trading strategies.
