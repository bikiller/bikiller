The Australian dollar is trading in a narrow range against the US dollar, hovering near the 0.7250 mark, as investors digest an unexpectedly strong US Producer Price Index (PPI) for April. The hotter-than-forecast reading has added upward pressure on the USD, keeping the AUD/USD currency pair on a cautious footing. Traders are weighing the impact of potential tightening from the Federal Reserve on global exchange rates.  

Market participants noted that despite the PPI surprise, the Australian dollar showed limited movement, reflecting mixed sentiment across forex markets. Technical levels around 0.7250 are acting as a short-term equilibrium for the AUD/USD pair, while broader USD strength against major currencies like the EUR is influencing flows. Traders remain attentive to inflation metrics and central bank commentary for guidance on future currency trends.  

Analysts suggest that continued USD gains could temper AUD rebounds, with the Australian dollar trading closely alongside commodity-linked sentiment. As the forex market responds to economic data from both sides of the Pacific, the AUD/USD exchange rate may stay range-bound until fresh signals on inflation or interest rates emerge. Monitoring developments in USD, EUR, and other major currencies will be key for trading strategies in the near term.
