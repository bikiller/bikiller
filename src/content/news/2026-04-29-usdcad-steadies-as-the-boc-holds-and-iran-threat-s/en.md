The USD/CAD currency pair held steady on Wednesday, hovering near 1.3680 after a brief spike toward 1.3710 was quickly reversed. Traders reacted to the Bank of Canada’s decision to leave interest rates unchanged, which offered little new direction for the loonie. Early-session volatility was exacerbated by geopolitical tensions in Iran, which briefly pushed the exchange rate higher before profit-taking and technical resistance brought it back down.  

Market participants noted that the BoC’s neutral stance maintained pressure on USD/CAD within a narrow range. While the U.S. dollar showed resilience against major currencies such as the EUR and GBP, the loonie remained sensitive to oil price fluctuations, which influence Canada’s trade-driven economy. Analysts highlighted that levels around 1.3680 could act as support, with resistance near the 1.3710 mark limiting upside potential amid a cautious trading environment.  

Traders are monitoring how broader risk sentiment and geopolitical developments might shape North American currency movements. With the exchange rate consolidating, short-term strategies focus on scalps and range trades rather than directional bets. Any renewed tensions in the Middle East or shifts in central bank guidance could trigger fresh volatility, keeping the USD/CAD pair under close observation as markets digest both domestic policy and international risk factors.
