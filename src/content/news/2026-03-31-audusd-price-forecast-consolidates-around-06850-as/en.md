The AUD/USD pair is consolidating around the 0.6850 mark, with bears eyeing a potential break below the 100-day Simple Moving Average (SMA). After reaching its lowest level in over two months on Monday, the currency pair has been oscillating between small gains and slight losses during the Asian session on Tuesday. The recent weakness comes after a sustained period of selling, with the Aussie facing mounting pressure against the US dollar.

Despite the minor fluctuations, the outlook remains bearish, with the 100-day SMA acting as a critical support level for AUD/USD. A clear break below this technical level could further extend losses, dragging the exchange rate lower. The USD continues to benefit from strong economic data and investor risk sentiment, while the Australian dollar struggles with weak domestic growth and global market uncertainties. Traders will closely monitor the next moves for a clearer direction.
