The US Dollar Index (DXY) continues its upward trajectory, rising above 99.00 to 99.20 during the Asian session on Wednesday. This marks a third consecutive day of gains as market participants react to escalating tensions in the Middle East. The DXY's strength reflects concerns over global inflation, with investors flocking to the safety of the USD as a hedge against potential economic instability.

As the DXY strengthens, the USD sees broad-based gains against major currencies, including the EUR/USD pair, which is facing downward pressure. The heightened geopolitical risks contribute to concerns over rising inflation, further driving demand for the US Dollar in the global forex market. Traders will be closely watching further developments in the region, with any additional volatility likely to impact the USD’s exchange rate dynamics.
