Bank Indonesia has stepped up measures to stabilize the Indonesian Rupiah amid heightened FX volatility, according to DBS Group Research’s Radhika Rao. The central bank tightened documentation thresholds for USD purchases, aiming to curb speculative demand, while maintaining its bond-buying program. This combination has supported the IDR, helping the currency recover against the USD after recent pressure, with traders noting improved liquidity in onshore markets.  

Selected dealers have also been granted access to offshore non-deliverable forwards (NDFs), providing additional channels for hedging and smoothing exchange rate fluctuations. The move comes as the IDR faced upward pressure on the USD/IDR currency pair, with volatility spilling over into EUR/IDR and other cross rates. Analysts suggest that these measures could help maintain technical levels near recent support zones, limiting sharp intraday swings.  

Market participants are now watching how these interventions affect trading sentiment, particularly with USD strength persisting globally. While bond purchases offer a backstop for yields and capital flows, tighter FX controls may influence corporate hedging strategies and foreign investor participation. For now, the IDR’s stabilization signals cautious optimism, though sustained pressure from broader dollar trends will likely dictate the next directional moves for the currency pair.
