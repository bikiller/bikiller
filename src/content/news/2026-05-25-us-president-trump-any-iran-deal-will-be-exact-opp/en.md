US President Donald Trump weighed in on a potential Iran deal, emphasizing it would be “either a great and meaningful one, or there will be no deal,” contrasting sharply with the JCPOA framework. The statement, posted on Truth Social during the European trading session Monday, triggered cautious movement in USD currency pairs as traders assessed geopolitical risk and potential impacts on oil markets and safe-haven flows.

In forex trading, the USD gained modestly against the EUR and JPY as investors digested the comments. EUR/USD hovered near 1.0920, while USD/JPY edged toward 144.50, reflecting renewed demand for the greenback amid uncertainty over Iran sanctions and broader Middle East tensions. Market sentiment suggests that any firm deal could weaken the USD if oil prices drop, whereas a collapse in talks could reinforce dollar strength.

Analysts highlight that exchange rate volatility may persist, with trading volumes likely to spike around news on negotiation outcomes. Technical levels around EUR/USD 1.0900 support and 1.0950 resistance, along with USD/JPY 144.00 support, are being closely watched. Forex traders are positioning for swings as developments in U.S.-Iran diplomacy remain a key driver for market sentiment and cross-currency flows.
