Gold (XAU/USD) slipped toward $4,880 on Wednesday, pressured by the Federal Reserve’s hawkish stance. The central bank opted to maintain rates, signaling only one potential cut in 2026, sharply contrasting early-year market expectations that had priced in nearly 60 basis points of easing by mid-February. The USD gained across major currency pairs, adding to the headwinds for gold trading.  

Traders saw the Fed’s messaging as a clear signal that interest rates will stay elevated for longer, supporting the dollar against the euro and other currencies. XAU/USD remains under technical pressure, with momentum indicators pointing toward further declines if it breaches immediate support levels near $4,880. The hawkish tone has also impacted broader commodity and FX markets, as investors reassess risk and yield-sensitive assets.  

Market participants now weigh the Fed outlook against global economic signals, adjusting positions in gold, USD/JPY, and EUR/USD. With rate-cut expectations deferred, speculative flows are favoring the greenback, tightening spreads in currency markets. Analysts suggest gold may test lower support levels before settling, while FX traders monitor upcoming economic data for clues on Fed policy shifts and potential volatility in USD-linked pairs.
