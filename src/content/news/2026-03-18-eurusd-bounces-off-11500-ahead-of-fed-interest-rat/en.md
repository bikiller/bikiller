EUR/USD edged higher on Wednesday, reclaiming some of its earlier losses as the US Dollar eased slightly from its intraday peaks. The currency pair found support near the 1.1500 level, with traders closely eyeing the Federal Reserve’s interest rate decision scheduled for 18:00 GMT. Market participants remain cautious, weighing how Fed guidance could shape the USD’s short-term trajectory and influence overall trading sentiment.  

The exchange rate’s rebound comes amid subdued USD strength, offering the Euro modest breathing room after a period of pressure. Analysts note that the 1.1500 mark serves as a key technical support, with a sustained break below potentially triggering further downside for EUR/USD. Trading volumes have reflected this cautious stance, with investors hesitant to commit ahead of the central bank’s announcement.  

Looking ahead, market watchers anticipate volatility around the Fed’s policy update, as any shift in tone or interest rate expectations could prompt swift moves in the EUR/USD pair. A firmer USD may cap the Euro’s gains, while dovish signals could see the pair testing higher levels. Traders are positioning strategically, balancing technical levels with fundamental drivers to navigate the pre-decision environment.
