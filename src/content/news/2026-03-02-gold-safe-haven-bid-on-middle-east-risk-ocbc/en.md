Gold prices are climbing as tensions in the Middle East fuel a safe-haven bid. OCBC strategists, Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, highlight the geopolitical risks that have pushed gold's appeal higher. With weekend strikes escalating concerns, they see a potential for upward movement, with resistance levels at 5,440 and 5,500. 

As market volatility intensifies, the precious metal is drawing support from traders seeking refuge. OCBC sets key support levels at 5,149 and 5,013, signaling potential price floors. This shift in market sentiment could further influence the USD and EUR pairs, as traders adjust their portfolios amidst rising uncertainty. Gold’s resilience reflects broader risk-off sentiment in currency markets.
