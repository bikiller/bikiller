The Bank of Canada is expected to maintain its current interest rate through 2026, according to RBC Economics. Analysts Claire Fan and Nathan Janzen highlight that recent oil price spikes, driven by geopolitical supply disruptions, are unlikely to prompt a shift in policy. Unlike the 2015 rate cuts, which reflected a structural downturn in oil markets, today’s energy-driven price moves are seen as temporary and less threatening to Canada’s broader economic outlook.  

In forex markets, the CAD has shown mixed trading against the USD and EUR amid these developments. The USD/CAD exchange rate has hovered near recent technical support levels, while EUR/CAD continues to respond to eurozone inflation data and broader risk sentiment. Traders are weighing oil’s influence on the Canadian dollar alongside interest rate expectations, leaving currency pair movements sensitive to both commodity news and central bank guidance.  

RBC emphasizes that the Bank of Canada’s steady stance should limit sharp volatility in the CAD, keeping trading ranges relatively contained. Market participants are focusing on upcoming economic indicators, including CPI and employment data, for cues on any potential policy reassessment. For forex traders, this suggests CAD positioning may be more reactive to short-term oil swings than long-term rate shifts, reinforcing cautious trading strategies in USD/CAD and EUR/CAD pairs.
