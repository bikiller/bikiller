Silver (XAG/USD) posted a rare green session on Thursday in forex trading, though the move looked more like a reactive bounce than a confirmed bottom. The currency pair-linked metal found support after sliding earlier, but overall sentiment toward USD-driven assets kept traders cautious. Market participants largely avoided calling a reversal despite the brief recovery in the exchange rate dynamics.

Intraday volatility intensified after US data releases, with XAG/USD dipping to a session low near 56.35 before attracting buying interest. The metal quickly surged, briefly spiking close to 59.00 as traders reacted to shifting USD expectations. The move highlighted how sensitive silver remains to currency pair flows and macroeconomic surprises in forex markets.

However, the rally faded into the close, with silver settling back around 58.00, still up roughly 0.8% on the day. The exchange rate between XAG/USD suggests buyers lack conviction at higher levels, and resistance continues to cap gains. Traders now watch whether support holds or if this rebound proves temporary rather than a durable shift.
