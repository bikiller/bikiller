Pound Sterling struggled against the US dollar on Tuesday as traders reacted to hotter-than-expected US CPI data, with PPI figures still awaited. The GBP/USD currency pair slipped roughly 0.7%, retreating from session highs near 1.3650 and testing the psychologically significant 1.3500 level. A modest rebound later in the session offered only limited relief for sterling traders.

The sharp pullback in the exchange rate reflects concerns that persistent US inflation could reinforce expectations for further Federal Reserve tightening, making USD assets more attractive. Market participants noted that the upcoming producer price index could add additional volatility, influencing short-term trading strategies across major forex pairs, including GBP/USD and EUR/USD.

Technical watchers highlighted the 1.3500 round number as a key support, with resistance around 1.3650. Analysts suggest that while sterling’s recent weakness is primarily USD-driven, broader market sentiment and economic indicators will dictate whether the pound can stabilize or continue its decline in coming sessions. Traders remain closely positioned ahead of potential data-driven swings in currency markets.
