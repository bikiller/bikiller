Gold (XAU/USD) dipped toward a weekly low in early European trading on Friday, as investors weighed the lack of progress on a US‑Iran ceasefire. The precious metal slipped below key support levels, with trading volumes picking up amid heightened market caution. Persistent geopolitical tensions are keeping the yellow metal volatile, even as the USD strengthened across major currency pairs, putting additional pressure on XAU/USD.  

Traders are eyeing the upcoming US jobs data, which could influence broader market sentiment and currency exchange rates. The USD’s resilience against the EUR and other currencies is limiting gold’s upside, while technical indicators suggest that sellers remain in control near the $1,980–$1,990 range. Volatility in XAU/USD has also coincided with cautious trading in other commodities, highlighting investor sensitivity to both geopolitical and macroeconomic developments.  

Market participants note that any fresh signals on a ceasefire could spark sharp moves in gold and forex markets. Currency traders are monitoring USD/EUR and USD/JPY closely, as swings in the greenback could directly affect gold pricing. Overall, the combination of geopolitical uncertainty and looming US employment data continues to shape trading strategies, with gold poised to react quickly to shifts in risk sentiment.
