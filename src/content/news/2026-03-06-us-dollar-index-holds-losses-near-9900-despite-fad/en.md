The US Dollar Index (DXY) is holding steady near the 99.00 mark during Thursday’s Asian trading session, following a slight decline after modest gains earlier in the week. Despite fading expectations for a rate cut by the Federal Reserve, the greenback remains under pressure, with traders adjusting their positions amid mixed economic data and concerns over global growth.

In currency pair movements, the USD is trading weaker against major rivals, including the EUR/USD, which is edging higher toward the 1.0800 handle. The subdued demand for the dollar reflects a shift in market sentiment, with the DXY hovering just above its 99.00 level as investors remain cautious, eyeing upcoming economic indicators and any signs of changes in US monetary policy.
