The Australian Dollar (AUD) weakened against the US Dollar (USD) during Thursday's Asian session, with the AUD/USD pair falling to around 0.7065. This decline comes after Australia reported a surprising narrowing of its trade surplus in January. The data caught market participants off guard, triggering some selling pressure on the AUD as traders reassessed the outlook for the currency.

The unexpected shift in Australia's trade balance has added a bearish sentiment to the AUD, which had previously been underpinned by stronger commodity prices. As the trade surplus contracted, the Australian Dollar's appeal diminished, pushing the AUD/USD exchange rate lower. At the moment, the currency pair is closely monitoring any further economic updates, as they could influence the next directional move in this key currency pair.
