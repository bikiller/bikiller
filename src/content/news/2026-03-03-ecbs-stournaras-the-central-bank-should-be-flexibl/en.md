European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member Yannis Stournaras emphasized the importance of flexibility in the ECB's monetary policy in light of the ongoing tensions between the U.S., Israel, and Iran. Stournaras highlighted that such geopolitical instability could impact global markets and the euro’s exchange rate. In this context, the ECB may need to adjust its policies to maintain economic stability.

The war’s potential disruption of oil prices and energy markets could lead to higher inflationary pressures, affecting the EUR/USD trading pair. With the U.S. dollar’s role in global oil trade, any price surges could bolster the USD, creating additional challenges for the ECB. As markets remain volatile, traders should watch for potential shifts in ECB policy signals that could influence euro strength against the USD in the coming weeks.
