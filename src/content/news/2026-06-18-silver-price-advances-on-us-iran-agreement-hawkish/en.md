Silver (XAG/USD) extended gains on Thursday, climbing toward $68.10 as easing geopolitical tensions following a US-Iran agreement supported broader risk appetite. The white metal advanced 1.05% on the day at the time of writing, with trading activity reflecting renewed demand in the precious metals segment of the forex market across global sessions.

Hawkish Federal Reserve expectations, however, continue to cap further upside in the silver exchange rate, as stronger USD demand keeps pressure on precious metals priced in dollars. Broader currency pair dynamics, including EUR/USD weakness, have also supported the dollar, limiting speculative trading momentum in XAG/USD despite improved sentiment globally overall.

From a technical perspective, XAG/USD trades near the $68.10 resistance zone, with traders watching whether momentum can extend gains or face profit-taking. Near-term outlook remains tied to USD liquidity conditions and broader forex trading flows, with volatility expected as markets reassess exchange rate expectations across major sessions ahead this week.
