The Reserve Bank of Australia delivered its second rate hike of the year, yet the AUD/USD currency pair continues to underperform, remaining below the 0.71 level. According to Commerzbank’s Volkmar Baur, the Australian dollar is trading choppily, reflecting a cautious market response despite tighter monetary policy. Traders are weighing the RBA’s move against broader USD strength and global risk sentiment.  

Market participants note that the exchange rate’s muted reaction suggests the hike may already be priced in. The USD’s ongoing resilience, bolstered by firm economic data, has limited AUD gains, keeping the pair within a narrow range. Analysts highlight that AUD/USD volatility could persist as investors monitor upcoming US and Eurozone data, along with commodity-driven flows that often influence the Australian currency.  

Looking ahead, Baur points to the potential for further RBA tightening, but emphasizes that AUD trading remains sensitive to external factors. A sustained move above 0.71 could signal renewed bullish momentum, while any dips may attract short-term selling pressure. For traders, balancing rate expectations with USD trends and global market cues remains critical in navigating this choppy currency pair.
