Copper on the London Metal Exchange is extending losses as global macroeconomic headwinds continue to weigh on industrial metals. Analysts from ING, including Warren Patterson and Ewa Manthey, note that a firming US Dollar (USD) and rising inflation concerns tied to the Iran conflict are pressuring the market. Weaker Chinese economic data further dampens demand expectations, adding to downward momentum in copper prices.

The USD’s strength against major currency pairs, including the EUR/USD and GBP/USD, has made dollar-denominated commodities less attractive to foreign buyers. Traders are watching exchange rates closely, as a stronger USD can increase costs for importers, potentially curbing industrial consumption. Copper futures are showing consistent selling pressure, reflecting caution among market participants amid global uncertainty.

Market implications suggest that if inflation pressures persist and Chinese demand remains soft, copper may test lower technical support levels on the LME. Forex traders are factoring these developments into broader currency trading strategies, while industrial hedgers may adjust exposure to USD-linked commodities. Overall, copper’s trajectory highlights the intertwined nature of metals markets, global growth indicators, and currency fluctuations, signaling continued volatility for both traders and industrial users.
