The Philippine Peso (PHP) remains under pressure as the Iran and Middle East tensions continue to impact global markets. MUFG Senior Currency Analyst, Michael Wan, highlights that risks around the closure of the Strait of Hormuz and rising oil prices, now above $100 per barrel, are contributing to the Peso's vulnerability. The USD/PHP exchange rate is feeling the effects, with traders wary of escalating geopolitical risks.

As oil prices climb, the Peso faces increased inflationary pressures and a deteriorating trade balance. The potential for sustained oil prices above $100 per barrel could limit the Peso’s recovery in the near term. With geopolitical tensions and higher commodity costs weighing on the market, the outlook for the USD/PHP remains bearish, with the pair potentially testing key technical levels if the situation worsens.
