West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude surged past $100.50, hitting $103.85 in early Asian trading on Monday, as escalating conflict in the Middle East rattled global fuel supplies. The sudden supply disruption sent energy markets into overdrive, prompting investors to reassess risk positions. Rising oil prices are boosting USD demand against commodity-linked currencies, with the USD/JPY and USD/CAD pairs seeing increased trading activity amid heightened volatility.  

Traders are eyeing technical levels closely, with WTI’s break above $100 signaling potential further gains toward $105 if tensions persist. The oil spike is pressuring EUR/USD, which dipped as the euro struggled against a strengthening dollar. Markets are pricing in a risk premium, driving speculative flows in both FX and commodities, while safe-haven currencies like the JPY and CHF are attracting inflows amid uncertainty.  

Analysts warn that prolonged conflict could stoke inflationary pressures globally, reinforcing USD strength across major currency pairs. FX traders are adjusting positions as the energy-driven surge affects emerging market currencies and commodity-linked exchange rates. Market participants are preparing for continued volatility, with WTI movements shaping both short-term trading strategies and broader currency market sentiment.
