The Euro (EUR) showed a slight uptick against the US Dollar (USD) on Thursday, trading around 1.1550. Despite this modest gain, the currency pair remains confined within the range established over the past two sessions, hovering just above two-month lows near 1.1500. Traders are closely watching the European Central Bank (ECB) for cues on future monetary policy.

Market participants are factoring in the potential impact of a dovish ECB rate hike, which could keep the EUR under pressure. Technical analysts note that failure to hold above 1.1550 may open the path toward lower support levels, intensifying selling pressure in EUR/USD trading. Exchange rate volatility remains heightened ahead of the central bank’s announcement.

The current trading environment reflects cautious sentiment, with investors weighing economic data and policy signals. Short-term traders are positioning around key levels, while the broader forex market remains sensitive to any dovish guidance. Should the ECB adopt a softer tone, the Euro could see further downside against the USD, reinforcing the recent trend toward two-month lows.
