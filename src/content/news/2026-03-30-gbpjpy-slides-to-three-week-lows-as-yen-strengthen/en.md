GBP/JPY fell to a three-week low on Monday as the Japanese Yen strengthened following increased verbal intervention from Japanese authorities. The move came after USD/JPY approached the 160.00 level, a key threshold that has historically prompted official action to prevent further yen depreciation. The exchange rate for GBP/JPY declined as traders reacted to the risk of potential intervention in the currency market.

The Yen's broad rally across the forex market led to a tightening of the GBP/JPY exchange rate, reflecting a shift in market sentiment. As the pair moved lower, the focus remained on Japan's proactive stance regarding USD/JPY’s movements. Traders are now cautious about further intervention, with the potential for increased volatility in the coming days. The situation underscores the Japanese government's efforts to curb excessive yen weakness amid global inflation concerns.
