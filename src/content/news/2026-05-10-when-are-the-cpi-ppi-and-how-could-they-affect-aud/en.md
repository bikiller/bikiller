Traders are eyeing China’s upcoming inflation data as the National Bureau of Statistics (NBS) prepares to release April figures at 01:30 GMT. The Consumer Price Index (CPI) is forecast to rise 0.8% year-on-year, down from March’s 1.0%, signaling a modest slowdown in consumer price growth. Analysts note that weaker-than-expected CPI could weigh on risk appetite, putting pressure on the AUD/USD currency pair and other commodity-linked currencies.  

Alongside CPI, the Producer Price Index (PPI) will offer insight into upstream inflation trends, potentially affecting broader forex sentiment. A softer PPI reading may indicate easing cost pressures for manufacturers, which could support the AUD if global trade flows remain stable. Conversely, stronger-than-expected inflation could prompt a rally in the USD as traders anticipate tighter monetary conditions or a shift in risk allocation.  

The AUD/USD exchange rate has been sensitive to Chinese inflation data given Australia’s trade links. A decline in CPI or PPI may drive the pair toward technical support levels, while a surprise uptick could see a short-term rebound. Forex traders will also monitor EUR/USD and other major pairs, as shifts in risk sentiment and commodity prices ripple across global markets, influencing trading strategies and hedging positions.
