The Canadian Dollar (CAD) is trading higher against its major peers, benefitting from a surge in oil prices. However, the CAD is slightly down against the US Dollar (USD), with the exchange rate approaching 1.3735 during the European trading session on Monday. The price movement reflects ongoing volatility in energy markets, which directly impacts Canada's oil-dependent economy.

The Canadian Dollar's outperformance is largely attributed to the recent rally in crude oil, which has strengthened demand for CAD. Despite this, the USD remains resilient, and the CAD/USD currency pair faces resistance near 1.3750. Traders are closely watching the market's reaction to potential shifts in global oil prices and economic data that could influence future CAD strength against the USD and other major currencies like EUR.
