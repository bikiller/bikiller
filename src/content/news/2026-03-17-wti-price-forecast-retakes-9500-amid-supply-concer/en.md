WTI crude oil reclaimed the $95.00 level on Tuesday, reversing part of Monday’s pullback from the $100.00 psychological threshold. Gains were driven by renewed concerns over tightening global supply, supporting bullish sentiment across energy markets. The move coincided with a firmer USD, which has weighed on commodity-linked currencies, including the AUD and CAD, as traders adjusted exchange rate expectations in light of shifting crude dynamics.  

Technically, WTI’s recovery aligns with a robust support base near $93.50, reinforcing a short-term bullish setup. Momentum indicators suggest the market could test $97.00 if supply worries persist, prompting cautious trading among currency pairs sensitive to oil prices, such as USD/CAD. Market participants are closely monitoring geopolitical signals and OPEC+ output forecasts, which may continue to influence trading decisions and exchange rate movements.  

The rebound in crude has broader implications for forex markets, with oil-importing economies like Japan and the eurozone facing potential inflationary pressures that could affect the USD/JPY and EUR/USD pairs. Traders are weighing the interplay between commodity price trends and central bank guidance, with oil volatility driving speculative positions across currencies. As WTI navigates key technical levels, the market remains attentive to both supply-side developments and macroeconomic signals.
