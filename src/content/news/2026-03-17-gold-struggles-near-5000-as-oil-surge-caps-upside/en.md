Gold prices remained under pressure on Tuesday, hovering near the $5,000 mark, with a slight decline of 0.11%. The price action occurred amidst a broad weakening of the US Dollar and falling US Treasury yields, providing some support to precious metals. However, the surge in oil prices capped the upside potential for gold, limiting any significant movement above this critical level.

In the forex market, the movement of the EUR/USD currency pair reflected this broader market sentiment, as the US Dollar weakened against most major currencies. Despite the softer USD, traders are cautious about gold’s upside due to the influence of rising oil prices, which have driven inflation expectations. The outlook for gold remains uncertain, with technical resistance holding firm just below the $5,000 threshold.
