The British Pound slipped against the US Dollar on Tuesday, with the GBP/USD currency pair trading near 1.3525, down 0.62% on the day. Market participants reacted to stronger-than-expected US inflation data, which bolstered the USD, while ongoing political uncertainty in the UK added downward pressure on the pound. Trading volumes reflected cautious positioning ahead of key economic releases.  

The US inflation surprise reinforced demand for the greenback, pushing exchange rates in favor of the USD across major pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Analysts noted that higher inflation readings increase expectations for further US Federal Reserve tightening, prompting traders to reassess positions and hedge against potential volatility in forex markets.  

For the GBP, the combination of domestic political turmoil and a firmer USD weighed heavily on market sentiment. Technical levels around 1.3500 are being watched closely, with potential support near that zone. Traders are positioning for further swings, with currency pair dynamics now influenced both by macroeconomic data and geopolitical developments, leaving the pound vulnerable to additional pressure in upcoming sessions.
