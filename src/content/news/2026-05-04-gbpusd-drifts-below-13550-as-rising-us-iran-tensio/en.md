The GBP/USD currency pair slipped below the 1.3550 mark on Monday, reversing early gains after climbing above 1.3650 in the previous session. Traders saw the British Pound retreat as mounting geopolitical tensions between the US and Iran weighed on risk sentiment, prompting a cautious approach to trading. The Pound’s correction underscores market sensitivity to external shocks, with the exchange rate retracing key levels from Friday’s highs.  

Analysts note that the GBP/USD’s move comes amid broader USD strength, with investors favoring the greenback amid uncertainty. The pair’s intraday swings highlight the ongoing tug-of-war between technical resistance near 1.3650 and support around 1.3550, as traders monitor geopolitical headlines closely. Currency markets are digesting the implications for global risk appetite, with potential spillover effects for EUR/USD and other major pairs.  

Looking ahead, traders are keeping a close eye on both fundamental and technical drivers. A sustained dip below 1.3550 could open the door for further downside toward mid-1.3500 levels, while a rebound above Friday’s highs may signal a resumption of the Pound’s recovery. Exchange rate volatility is expected to remain elevated, reflecting the interplay of market sentiment, geopolitical developments, and USD movements.
