The Indonesian Rupiah remained under pressure against the USD as persistent macroeconomic headwinds continued to weigh on the currency pair, according to MUFG strategist Lloyd Chan. Rising US Treasury yields, elevated oil prices, and narrowing interest-rate differentials have reduced the appeal of Indonesian assets, keeping the USD/IDR exchange rate biased higher in recent trading. Market participants also noted that stronger Dollar demand and cautious risk sentiment have added pressure to emerging Asian currencies.

Higher crude oil prices have further complicated the outlook for Indonesia’s external balance, increasing concerns over imported inflation and the country’s trade position. At the same time, expectations that the Federal Reserve could keep US rates elevated for longer have supported broader USD trading momentum, limiting the Rupiah’s ability to recover despite occasional inflows into regional markets. Investors continue to monitor whether Bank Indonesia can maintain currency stability without aggressively tightening policy.

Even so, MUFG warned that reversal risks remain in play for the USD/IDR currency pair if global market conditions shift. Any moderation in US yields, softer Dollar sentiment, or renewed appetite for emerging-market assets could help the Rupiah regain ground against the USD and EUR crosses. Traders are therefore watching upcoming US inflation data and Federal Reserve signals closely for direction on the next move in the exchange rate.
