The ongoing tensions in Iran and the disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz are significantly tightening the global oil market, according to Commerzbank commodity analysts Barbara Lambrecht and Carsten Fritsch. This instability is causing a widening of the Brent-WTI spread and boosting time spreads, which has fueled market volatility. As the situation escalates, crude oil prices are experiencing upward pressure, with Brent Crude hovering near $90 per barrel. 

The growing risks in the Middle East are contributing to the tightening of supply and putting additional strain on oil prices. This has led to increased trading activity across major currency pairs, especially between the USD and EUR, as the oil market is closely linked to exchange rate fluctuations. Investors are cautious about the potential for further disruptions, which could push oil prices even higher and impact global inflation rates, leading to more uncertainty in foreign exchange markets.
