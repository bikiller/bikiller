Societe Generale notes that South Korean authorities have rolled out a multi-pronged foreign exchange stabilisation package aimed at easing short-term USD funding stress and curbing speculative pressure on the Korean won. The move comes as trading conditions in the USD/KRW currency pair show heightened sensitivity to liquidity shifts, with exchange rate volatility drawing increased attention from market participants.

According to SocGen, the pressure reflects tighter USD liquidity in regional funding markets and increased speculative positioning against the won. Volatility in the forex market has been amplified by shifting expectations for US monetary policy, driving uneven price action across Asian currency pairs and reinforcing defensive positioning in short-term trading flows.

Authorities’ response is expected to support near-term stability in KRW trading by improving USD funding conditions and dampening excess volatility. However, market participants remain focused on whether these measures can sustain momentum against broader USD strength, with implications potentially extending to other major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and broader G10 FX sentiment.
