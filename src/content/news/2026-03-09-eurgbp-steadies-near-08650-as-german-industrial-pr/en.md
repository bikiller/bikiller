EUR/GBP has shown modest gains, trading around 0.8670 during Monday's European session, following two consecutive days of losses. The currency pair managed to edge higher after the release of disappointing German Industrial Production data for January, which showed a decline in output. The news weighed on the euro, but the pair found some support near the 0.8650 level.

Market attention now shifts to the Eurozone’s Sentix Investor Confidence index, due later today. This data could offer further insight into investor sentiment and impact the EUR/GBP exchange rate. While the pair remains relatively strong, the ongoing pressure from weaker economic data suggests potential volatility in the near term. Traders will likely monitor these developments closely, especially given the broader global market uncertainty.
