The euro rebounded on Thursday, recovering from an intraday low of 1.1586 against the US Dollar (USD) as traders digested fresh US economic data. The EUR/USD currency pair is currently trading around 1.1655, reflecting easing demand for the greenback. Analysts note that despite ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, market sentiment is favoring the euro amid a softer USD outlook.  

US data, particularly the latest Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) figures, contributed to the dollar's loss of momentum. Investors interpreted the readings as a signal that inflationary pressures might be moderating, prompting a recalibration of trading strategies across major currency pairs. The euro’s recovery highlights the sensitive interplay between macroeconomic data and exchange rate movements in the forex market.  

Technically, the EUR/USD is navigating a range where earlier support at 1.1586 has held, encouraging short-term buyers. Traders are now closely watching whether the pair can sustain gains above 1.1655, which could indicate further upside potential. Market participants are adjusting positions accordingly, balancing geopolitical risks against the influence of US monetary and economic indicators on USD strength.
