Gold prices came under heavy selling pressure on Tuesday, with Gold (XAU/USD) sliding more than 1.3% as broad USD strength dominated market trading. The precious metal fell to $4,506 at the time of writing after previously reaching an intraday high of $4,589. The sharp reversal in the XAU/USD currency pair reflected a clear shift in investor sentiment as traders rotated away from safe-haven assets and toward the US Dollar.

The move was largely driven by an oil shock that sparked a surge in US Treasury yields, strengthening the Greenback across foreign exchange markets. The benchmark US 10-year Treasury yield climbed close to a 16-month peak, increasing the appeal of yield-bearing assets relative to non-interest-paying gold. Rising yields also boosted the USD exchange rate against major counterparts, including the EUR and other leading currencies.

For forex and commodity traders, the latest price action highlights the growing influence of bond markets on currency pair movements and broader risk appetite. Higher US yields may continue supporting the USD in the near term, while Gold trading could remain vulnerable if Treasury rates stay elevated. Market participants are now watching whether this momentum creates further downside pressure across precious metals and FX markets.
