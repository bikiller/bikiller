The Swiss Franc weakened against the US Dollar on Wednesday, with the USD/CHF currency pair extending gains for a second consecutive session to trade near 0.7890 during Asian market hours. The move reflects renewed safe-haven demand for the USD, supporting the exchange rate as investors adopt a more cautious stance amid broader market uncertainty and defensive trading sentiment.

The stronger US Dollar has continued to pressure the Swiss Franc, traditionally viewed as a defensive currency alongside the USD, though current flows appear to favor the greenback. In forex trading, this shift suggests market participants are prioritizing dollar liquidity, helping the USD/CHF pair maintain upward momentum. The move may also influence related currency pairs, including EUR/USD, as broader USD strength reshapes positioning.

If the current trend holds, traders will watch whether USD/CHF can sustain gains above the 0.7890 level, which may reinforce near-term bullish sentiment for the pair. A prolonged rise in safe-haven demand could further lift the exchange rate, while any easing in risk aversion may allow the Swiss Franc to recover. Near-term forex trading is likely to remain highly sensitive to sentiment-driven USD flows.
