The USD/JPY currency pair edged lower toward the 156.60 level on Friday as traders weighed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. The Japanese Yen benefited from safe-haven demand, briefly bolstering its exchange rate against the US Dollar. Despite this, the USD found underlying support from resilient US labor-market data, which tempered more significant declines in the currency pair during early trading sessions.  

Market participants noted that the safe-haven flows into JPY reflected growing risk aversion, prompting cautious positioning across major forex pairs. The USD/JPY slide toward 156.60 contrasted with broader trading in other currency pairs, including EUR/USD, which remained relatively steady amid mixed global risk sentiment. Analysts suggested that traders were balancing geopolitical concerns with domestic US economic resilience, keeping volatility contained.  

Technically, the 156.60 region is a key support level, with the USD/JPY hovering just above it as investors assess potential spillover effects from geopolitical developments. Continued safe-haven buying could test this level, while further strong US economic data may limit any deeper USD depreciation. Traders are watching closely for shifts in sentiment, which could influence short-term trading strategies across USD, JPY, and other major currencies.
