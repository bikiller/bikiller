Gold (XAU/USD) edged lower during early Friday Asian trading, slipping to around $5,085 as the US Dollar strengthened. The bullion’s retreat comes amid broad USD gains against major currency pairs, including the euro and yen, as traders positioned ahead of key economic data. Market participants cited stronger demand for the greenback, weighing on gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset.  

The USD’s upward momentum lifted exchange rates across forex markets, prompting a cautious tone in commodities trading. Analysts noted that the metal’s decline below $5,100 could trigger further short-term technical pressure if the currency pair fails to stabilize. Investors are closely monitoring shifts in the USD index and EUR/USD trading patterns for signals of broader market sentiment.  

Attention now turns to the US employment report for February, which could drive volatility in both forex and commodity markets. Any surprise in payrolls or unemployment figures may reinforce the dollar’s trajectory, influencing trading strategies in gold, silver, and related currency pairs. Traders are bracing for potential swings in exchange rates and positioning accordingly ahead of the data release.
