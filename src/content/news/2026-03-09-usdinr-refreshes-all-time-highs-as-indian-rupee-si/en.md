The Indian Rupee (INR) tumbled to a fresh all-time low against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, with the USD/INR currency pair surging to nearly 92.80. This decline came amid rising oil prices, which weighed heavily on the INR, while the USD strengthened due to a risk-off sentiment across global markets. 

As oil prices continue to climb, the Indian economy faces increased pressure, exacerbating the Rupee's vulnerability. Traders are closely monitoring this technical level, as the sharp rise in the USD/INR exchange rate reflects broader market concerns over inflation and potential supply chain disruptions. The ongoing volatility in global markets could lead to further instability for the INR in the short term.
