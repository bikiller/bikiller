South Korea’s won (KRW) is showing renewed bullish potential as the global technology cycle strengthens, according to MUFG. Senior Currency Analyst Michael Wan highlights that despite the currency’s sensitivity to prolonged geopolitical tensions and rising oil prices, KRW could outperform in a de-escalation scenario. Traders are watching the USD/KRW and EUR/KRW currency pairs closely, with the exchange rate likely to reflect optimism around the strong AI and semiconductor sectors.  

Market participants are increasingly factoring in NPS hedging flows, which are tilting sentiment back toward KRW appreciation. MUFG points to sustained demand in technology and AI-driven exports as key drivers, supporting trading positions that favor KRW against the USD and other major currencies. Any easing of conflict risks could accelerate inflows, reinforcing technical levels that have held as support in recent sessions.  

For forex traders, the combination of sectoral strength and hedging dynamics presents a window for KRW strategies. While elevated oil prices and regional uncertainty remain potential headwinds, MUFG’s outlook suggests that careful positioning in USD/KRW and EUR/KRW could capture upside moves. Monitoring global tech indices alongside won liquidity flows will be critical for gauging short- to medium-term trading opportunities.
