The GBP/USD currency pair faces potential turbulence as geopolitical tensions in Iran raise fresh risks for the UK economy. According to BNY’s Bob Savage, citing National Institute of Economic and Social Research (NIESR) analysis, an escalation in the Iran conflict combined with a prolonged Strait of Hormuz closure could disrupt oil supply, driving volatility in exchange rates and impacting sterling trading against the US dollar. Investors are eyeing key technical levels as risk sentiment shifts.  

Market participants are increasingly weighing the economic fallout on the UK, where energy-import reliance could pressure growth and inflation metrics. Sterling has already shown sensitivity to Middle East developments, with traders monitoring GBP/USD moves for signals of broader risk aversion. USD strength in recent sessions further complicates trading, as safe-haven flows push the exchange rate lower, while EUR/GBP correlations hint at cross-market contagion.  

Technical analysts highlight support near 1.2400 and resistance around 1.2650 for GBP/USD, noting that a breach could trigger sharper market swings. With NIESR emphasizing the macroeconomic consequences of a blocked Strait of Hormuz, the outlook suggests heightened volatility for both short-term trading and longer-term positioning in forex markets, underlining sterling’s vulnerability amid geopolitical shocks and global USD dynamics.
