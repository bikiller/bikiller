The EUR/GBP exchange rate has been creeping higher, with recent moves driven by fading expectations of a March Bank of England (BoE) rate cut. Since late February, the British Pound has outperformed the Euro, leading to a divergence in their performance. The Pound's strength has been tempered by diminished market expectations for a rate cut, while the Euro has lagged behind in comparison.

Rabobank’s Senior FX Strategist, Jane Foley, highlights that the recent EUR/GBP gains are primarily due to shifts in market positioning. As traders adjust their outlooks, the EUR/GBP currency pair has seen incremental gains, though the risk of further upward movement remains low. The impact of BoE policy expectations continues to weigh on the exchange rate, creating uncertainty for short-term trading.
