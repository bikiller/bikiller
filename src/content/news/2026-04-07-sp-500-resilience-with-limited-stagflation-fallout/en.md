The S&P 500 has shown remarkable resilience in the face of recent oil price volatility, with only a modest pullback compared to previous oil shocks. Deutsche Bank's Henry Allen attributes this to market expectations of a short-lived conflict, coupled with strong macroeconomic data and a continued dovish stance from central banks. This reaction has kept major currency pairs like the USD/EUR relatively stable, despite fluctuations in broader market sentiment.

While concerns over stagflation persist, the impact on risk assets remains limited, as central banks like the Federal Reserve maintain accommodative policies. The market's cautious optimism is reflected in the S&P 500's ability to recover, while the USD continues to hover around key support levels against the EUR. Traders are closely monitoring any changes in monetary policy or further escalation in geopolitical tensions that could shift exchange rates and market sentiment.
