AUD/USD trades steadily near 0.7070 on Wednesday, with the Australian Dollar holding a firm tone against the US Dollar as risk appetite improves across global currency markets. The exchange rate remains supported after remarks from Donald Trump regarding Iran eased immediate geopolitical concerns, encouraging a mild recovery in risk-sensitive assets.

Improved sentiment in broader trading conditions has helped the Australian Dollar outperform several major peers, as investors rotate back into higher-yielding currencies. The USD shows a softer undertone in response, while demand for safe-haven flows has eased. Cross-currency moves also reflect cautious positioning in EUR/USD, though the main focus remains on AUD/USD stability.

From a technical perspective, the currency pair continues to consolidate around the 0.7070 level, suggesting a neutral-to-slightly bullish bias in the short term. Traders are watching whether sustained risk-on momentum can push the exchange rate toward higher resistance zones, or if renewed USD strength could cap gains in upcoming trading sessions.
