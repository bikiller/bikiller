The Indian Rupee (INR) is trading near historic lows against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, pressured by rising US Treasury yields and surging energy costs. The USD/INR currency pair has breached critical support levels, prompting traders to reassess short-term positions as the exchange rate hovers close to record highs. Market participants cite widening interest rate differentials and higher crude prices as key drivers.  

The rupee’s weakness is mirrored across other Asian currencies, with capital outflows amplifying pressure on the INR. Forex traders are watching the pair alongside the EUR/INR, where the rupee’s depreciation is influencing import costs and inflation expectations. Analysts note that elevated US yields continue to attract portfolio inflows, reinforcing USD strength while weighing on emerging-market currencies.  

Looking ahead, the INR may remain volatile as India navigates energy price shocks and external financing demands. Short-term resistance levels are being closely monitored, while traders remain cautious amid global uncertainty. Currency markets are pricing in sustained USD momentum, suggesting that the INR could face continued pressure in the weeks ahead, affecting trade and investment flows.
