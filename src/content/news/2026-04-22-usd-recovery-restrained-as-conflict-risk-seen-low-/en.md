DBS Group Research economist Chang Wei Liang highlights a restrained recovery for the USD, driven by the recent surge in oil prices approaching $100 due to stalled negotiations between Iran and the U.S. This rise in oil prices has provided some support for the U.S. Dollar, particularly against other currencies. However, the potential for further gains appears limited by a low risk of conflict in the region.

As the USD experiences fluctuations, traders should closely monitor the USD/EUR exchange rate, which could reflect the underlying stability in geopolitical tensions. While the dollar's strength has impacted currency pairs, the broader market dynamics suggest that the anticipated recovery may not be robust without heightened conflict risks. Thus, forex market participants remain cautious amid these developments, balancing their trading strategies accordingly.
