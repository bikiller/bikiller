China's General Administration of Customs will release its February trade balance data on Tuesday at 03:00 GMT. The trade balance is forecast to widen significantly to $179.60 billion, up from the $114.10 billion reported in January. This sharp increase could have a notable impact on market sentiment, especially for the AUD/USD currency pair.

The widening trade surplus is likely to support the Chinese yuan, while potentially boosting the Australian dollar, given its close trade ties with China. Traders will be watching closely for any significant movement in the AUD/USD exchange rate. A stronger trade balance may also have a ripple effect on broader market sentiment, influencing the USD's strength against other major currencies like the EUR and GBP.
