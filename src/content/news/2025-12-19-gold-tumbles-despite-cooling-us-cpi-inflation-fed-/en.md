Gold prices (XAU/USD) fell below $4,350 during early Asian trading on Friday, driven by a mix of profit-taking and weak long liquidation from short-term futures traders. Despite the recent cooling in US CPI inflation and rising expectations for a Federal Reserve rate cut, the precious metal struggled to maintain its ground as market sentiment shifted.

The decline highlights investor caution in the face of potential shifts in monetary policy. As traders reassess their positions amidst fluctuating economic data, the exchange rate for gold continues to be influenced by broader market dynamics, including movements in the USD and other currency pairs. The market's response suggests that even favorable inflation figures may not be enough to bolster gold prices in the immediate term.
