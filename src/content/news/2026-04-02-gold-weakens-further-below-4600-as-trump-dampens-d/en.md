Gold (XAU/USD) continued its steep decline on Thursday, slipping below $4,600 after hitting a two-week peak near $4,800 earlier in the session. The precious metal faced strong selling pressure as the US Dollar (USD) surged broadly, reflecting heightened risk sentiment and disappointing de-escalation signals from former President Trump. The intraday slide pushed XAU/USD into the mid-$4,500s, weighing on trading across major forex markets.  

Traders noted the USD’s strength against major currencies, with the euro (EUR/USD) and other key pairs showing renewed support for the greenback. The sharp reversal in gold prices highlighted market sensitivity to geopolitical rhetoric and the broader USD exchange rate trends, suggesting that safe-haven flows were temporarily overshadowed by dollar strength. Technical analysts pointed to the rejection from $4,800 as a key resistance level that triggered further selling.  

Market participants are now closely monitoring XAU/USD around $4,550–$4,600, eyeing whether the pair can stabilize or if the USD rally will continue to pressure metals and related currency pairs. The combination of political uncertainty and currency market dynamics underscores the importance of tracking both macro headlines and intraday trading flows in shaping precious metals and forex markets.
