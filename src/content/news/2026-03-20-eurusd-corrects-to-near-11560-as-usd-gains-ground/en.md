The EUR/USD currency pair slipped to around 1.1560 during Friday’s Asian session, retreating from Thursday’s weekly high of 1.1616. The 0.2% pullback reflects a rebound in the US Dollar, which is attempting to recover after a recent sharp sell-off. Traders are watching how the exchange rate responds near key technical levels, with the USD’s resilience weighing on the euro’s gains.  

Market participants attribute the correction to a combination of profit-taking and renewed demand for the dollar amid risk sentiment shifts. With the USD showing signs of strength, the EUR/USD faces pressure to consolidate below the weekly peak. Analysts suggest that short-term trading could see the pair test support around the 1.1550–1.1560 zone before any sustained euro recovery.  

The recent volatility highlights the tug-of-war between euro bullishness and dollar recovery, influencing forex trading strategies across major pairs. Investors remain cautious, monitoring economic data and technical indicators that could dictate the next directional move. With the exchange rate hovering near 1.1560, momentum traders are likely to adjust positions based on USD strength, potentially shaping market flows into the end of the week.
