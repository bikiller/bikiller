Silver extended its gains on Thursday, with the XAG/USD currency pair climbing to $79.65 per troy ounce, marking a 0.83% increase from Wednesday’s close at $78.99, according to FXStreet data. The uptick in silver trading reflects renewed investor interest in safe-haven assets amid fluctuating USD strength and ongoing concerns over global economic stability. Traders noted that support around $78.50 helped sustain buying momentum.  

The exchange rate movement comes as the USD faced mild pressure against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, contributing to higher appeal for precious metals. Analysts suggested that weaker greenback sentiment can boost demand for silver as an alternative store of value, while rising inflation expectations continue to underpin trading activity. Short-term technical levels indicate resistance near $80, a key psychological barrier for the XAG/USD pair.  

Market participants are closely monitoring shifts in forex markets, especially ahead of U.S. economic data releases, which could influence USD direction and silver pricing. With silver now trading near multi-week highs, trading volumes have picked up, highlighting the metal’s sensitivity to both currency pair dynamics and broader macroeconomic indicators. Investors remain cautious yet opportunistic as volatility in exchange rates continues to drive active positioning in the precious metals sector.
