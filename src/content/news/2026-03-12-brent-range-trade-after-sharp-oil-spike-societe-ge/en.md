Brent crude oil has seen significant volatility recently, with prices breaking out of a large base and surging toward $120 before facing a sharp pullback. Currently holding above $81, the contract remains in a range-bound trade. A notable upside gap near $93.80 adds a potential target for traders, while $120 remains a crucial resistance level. 

This market action follows a surge in global oil demand and geopolitical tensions, both contributing to upward pressure on prices. As Brent continues its volatile movement, investors are eyeing key levels for potential breakouts or reversals. If the price manages to clear the $120 mark, further bullish momentum could follow, but the immediate outlook is contingent on sustaining support above the $81 region.
