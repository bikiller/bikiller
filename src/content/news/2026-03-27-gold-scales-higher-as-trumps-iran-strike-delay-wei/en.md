Gold (XAU/USD) gained traction during the Asian session on Friday, rebounding from Thursday’s decline to the $4,350 mark. The precious metal benefited from a weaker USD, as tensions surrounding President Trump’s delay in military action against Iran led to market uncertainty. This prompted a shift in risk sentiment, supporting demand for safe-haven assets like gold.

Despite the upward movement, the gains were capped by the hawkish stance of the Federal Reserve. With expectations for further rate hikes, the USD remained supported, limiting gold's rally. As the trading session progressed, the exchange rate for XAU/USD stayed near its session highs, reflecting the delicate balance between geopolitical factors and monetary policy expectations.
