West Texas Intermediate (WTI) extended its gains to a two-week high on Monday, climbing toward the $102.50 mark amid escalating tensions in Iran that stoke fears of supply disruptions. Traders were active across energy-linked currency pairs, with USD exchange rates reacting to the bullish oil momentum, pushing the greenback slightly higher against major peers including the EUR and JPY.

The oil rally, marking the third consecutive day of buying, reflects market concerns over potential Iranian supply constraints, reinforcing WTI’s support near recent technical levels. Forex traders noted the impact on commodity-linked currencies, with the CAD and NOK showing resilience as risk sentiment favored energy assets, while EUR/USD hovered near key psychological support points.

Analysts suggest that sustained geopolitical risks could continue to underpin crude prices, influencing USD liquidity and trading strategies in global forex markets. Investors are closely monitoring technical resistance around $102.50, with implications for cross-currency pairs and the broader commodities-linked forex trades, as traders adjust positions in anticipation of further volatility.
