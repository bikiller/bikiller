The USD/JPY currency pair is trading on a flat note near 160.20 during Asian trading hours on Monday, as the exchange rate stabilizes after recent volatility. The US Dollar shows mild softness against the Japanese Yen following reports of a US-Iran peace agreement, which has slightly shifted forex sentiment across major currency pairs.

Market participants remain focused on evolving geopolitical sentiment and Bank of Japan (BoJ) rate hike expectations, which continue to underpin the Japanese Yen. While risk-on flows from easing Middle East tensions tend to weigh on safe-haven demand, the USD/JPY exchange rate remains anchored near the 160.20 psychological level, reflecting balanced trading pressures between USD strength and JPY support.

In the near term, traders are likely to monitor whether USD/JPY can sustain moves above the 160.00 handle, with volatility expected as macro headlines drive intraday trading. Broader forex markets, including EUR/USD, may also react to shifts in USD sentiment and global risk appetite, keeping currency pair movements sensitive to central bank signals and geopolitical developments.
