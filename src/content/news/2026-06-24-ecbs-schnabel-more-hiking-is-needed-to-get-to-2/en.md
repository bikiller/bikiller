ECB Executive Board member Isabel Schnabel said on Wednesday that further interest rate increases are needed from the current perspective to bring inflation back to the European Central Bank’s 2% target. Her comments reinforce the ECB’s commitment to maintaining restrictive monetary policy until price pressures are sustainably contained across the eurozone.

Financial markets reacted by maintaining a cautious stance on the euro, with EUR/USD trading sentiment supported by expectations that higher rates could widen yield differentials against the US dollar. Traders continue to assess the impact of prolonged ECB tightening on European growth, while the USD remains sensitive to Federal Reserve policy expectations, contributing to short-term volatility in major currency pairs.

Looking ahead, markets will focus on upcoming ECB communications and eurozone inflation data for confirmation of the policy path. Any further hawkish signals could provide near-term support for the euro, while weaker data may cap gains. Overall, traders remain data-dependent, balancing EUR/USD direction against shifting USD interest rate expectations in the near term.
