The USD/CAD currency pair is holding below key resistance levels, maintaining a bearish bias as market participants monitor shifts in the US Dollar and risk sentiment. Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret highlight that the Canadian Dollar has gained slight strength, with USD/CAD trading near previous ranges. A softer USD and improved risk appetite are providing modest support for the CAD, influencing short-term trading dynamics.  

Analysts note that fair value for USD/CAD has edged lower, now hovering around 1.3424, signaling potential room for further Canadian Dollar appreciation if current market conditions persist. Traders are weighing the interplay between a weaker US Dollar, firm global equities, and ongoing demand for safe-haven and commodity-linked currencies, which together shape the intraday and medium-term exchange rate outlook.  

Technical trends suggest that USD/CAD could continue to face downward pressure unless a significant rebound in the USD occurs. Forex traders are watching key levels closely, as small shifts in risk sentiment or US economic data could tilt momentum. The interplay of these factors underscores the cautious trading environment, with the CAD’s modest strength reflecting broader market confidence and influencing cross-currency strategies against both the USD and the EUR.
