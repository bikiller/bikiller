The EUR/USD currency pair saw a slight uptick on Thursday as the US Dollar softened across the board, following speculation of intervention from Tokyo. Authorities were reportedly selling USD to stabilize the Japanese Yen, which provided some relief for the Euro. As a result, EUR/USD edged higher, maintaining its position below a key Fibonacci resistance level.

The Euro also received additional support from the European Central Bank's recent monetary policy decision, which has bolstered market sentiment. Despite this, the pair continues to consolidate beneath the 1.1050 level, with bulls defending the 200-day simple moving average (SMA). The current trading range reflects cautious optimism, with traders awaiting further cues from both sides of the Atlantic for direction.
