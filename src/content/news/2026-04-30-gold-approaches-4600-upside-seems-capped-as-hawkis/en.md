Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher in early European trading on Thursday, recovering from the monthly low hit the previous day. The precious metal approached the $4,600 level, buoyed by ongoing geopolitical tensions in Iran and persistent hawkish signals from the Federal Reserve. Despite this rebound, the upside for gold appears limited as a stronger USD continues to weigh on risk sentiment and precious metals demand.  

The currency pair’s recent moves highlight the tug-of-war between safe-haven flows into gold and dollar strength. Traders noted that while gold’s technical support near the monthly low provided a floor, the exchange rate faces resistance just below $4,600. EUR/USD and other major pairs are also reacting to the Fed’s stance, which keeps the greenback firm and constrains broad commodity-based gains.  

Market participants are closely watching developments in the Middle East and upcoming U.S. economic data, which could influence both USD direction and gold trading. Any escalation in geopolitical risks might lift gold, while further hawkish comments from the Fed could cap gains. For now, XAU/USD remains range-bound, with short-term traders balancing momentum against the broader dollar-driven macro backdrop.
