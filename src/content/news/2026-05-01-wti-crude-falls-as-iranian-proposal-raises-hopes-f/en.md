WTI crude oil prices dipped on Friday, retreating from earlier gains as the market reacted to new diplomatic developments. The Iranian proposal to resume negotiations with the US over the ongoing conflict generated optimism, boosting market sentiment. At the time of writing, WTI was trading around $71.50 per barrel, reflecting a modest decline after a week of strong upward momentum. The oil market remains sensitive to geopolitical events, with the US-Iran talks offering potential for easing tensions and altering supply dynamics.

The fresh diplomatic efforts also impacted currency markets, influencing the USD. The exchange rate for USD/EUR saw fluctuations, with the greenback showing signs of volatility in response to oil price movements. Oil traders continue to monitor geopolitical developments closely, aware of their potential to sway both energy prices and broader market sentiment. A successful resolution of the US-Iran situation could provide some relief to the dollar, but uncertainties surrounding the negotiations keep traders cautious.
