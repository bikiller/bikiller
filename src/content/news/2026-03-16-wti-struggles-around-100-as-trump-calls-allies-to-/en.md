West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude edged slightly lower, hovering near $98.00 per barrel during Monday’s European session on NYMEX, reflecting cautious sentiment in energy markets. The decline comes amid heightened geopolitical focus, as former U.S. President Donald Trump urged allies to secure the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil chokepoint. Traders weighed the potential for supply disruptions against broader demand concerns, keeping volatility in check.  

The slight pullback in WTI also influenced currency markets, with the USD showing resilience against major pairs. The EUR/USD exchange rate traded near 1.0850, as traders balanced safe-haven flows from oil uncertainty against expectations for U.S. interest rate stability. Commodity-linked currencies, including the CAD and NOK, experienced minor fluctuations, mirroring the modest shift in oil prices and reflecting trading activity linked to energy sector exposure.  

Analysts note that while WTI remains close to the psychologically important $100 level, the market’s response to geopolitical developments will likely dictate near-term direction. Continued tension in the Middle East could push oil prices higher, supporting USD strength through energy-related capital flows, while a stabilizing outlook may limit further gains. Traders are watching key technical levels and currency pair correlations closely to gauge risk sentiment and potential trading opportunities.
