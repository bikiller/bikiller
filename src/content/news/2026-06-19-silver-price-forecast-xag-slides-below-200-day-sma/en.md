Silver (XAG/USD) extended its decline for a third straight trading session, sliding more than 3.32% on Friday. The precious metal also broke below its 200-day simple moving average, a key technical level watched in forex and commodities trading. The move signals weakening momentum in the exchange rate as sellers maintain control across global currency and metals markets.

Broader USD strength has driven the sell-off, supported by rising US Treasury yields that have climbed since Wednesday. The shift follows a more hawkish Federal Reserve tone, tightening expectations across interest rate markets. In FX trading, this has pressured non-yielding assets and weighed on pairs such as EUR/USD and other major currency pairs linked to dollar liquidity conditions.

Looking ahead, bears retain control as XAG/USD risks extending losses toward the $61 level if momentum persists below the 200-day SMA. Market participants are closely monitoring USD direction and broader risk sentiment, which could influence volatility across forex markets and commodity-linked currency pairs in the near term with traders eyeing upcoming US data releases for fresh direction cues.
