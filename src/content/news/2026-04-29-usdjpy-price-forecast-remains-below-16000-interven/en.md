The USD/JPY pair remains below the critical 160.00 threshold as the market awaits further cues from the Federal Reserve. After bouncing back from sub-159.00 levels, triggered by the Bank of Japan’s hawkish pause, the exchange rate has been moving sideways, oscillating in a narrow range during the Asian session on Wednesday. This price action suggests that traders are cautious ahead of upcoming US economic data.

The dollar is struggling to extend gains against the yen, despite a brief recovery. The BoJ’s decision to pause its tightening cycle has provided some support for the yen, limiting the USD's ability to break through the 160.00 resistance level. With the Fed’s stance on interest rates in focus, market participants are likely to remain hesitant in making significant moves, keeping USD/JPY within its recent range.
