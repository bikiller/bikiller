Societe Generale strategist Kit Juckes notes that recent G10 monetary policy adjustments, including a Bank of Japan (BOJ) rate hike, have not triggered dramatic foreign exchange moves. Despite shifts across major currency pairs, including USD/JPY, overall FX volatility has remained contained, with the Japanese yen showing limited follow-through in trading sessions across G10 markets.

Juckes argues that the key driver ahead is the Federal Reserve outlook, with a dovish shift likely to weigh on the US dollar and support short USD/JPY positioning. In currency trading, weaker US yields typically pressure USD against the yen, improving the risk-reward for yen bulls. Market participants are closely watching Fed policy signals.

Overall, the market narrative suggests limited yen reaction to domestic policy changes, while global rate differentials and Fed expectations dominate USD/JPY direction. If US monetary policy turns more accommodative, investors may increase yen long exposure and extend USD selling. This could reinforce downward pressure on USD/JPY exchange rate in broader G10 currency trading.
