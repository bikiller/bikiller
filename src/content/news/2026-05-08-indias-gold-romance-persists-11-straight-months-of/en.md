Indian investors are maintaining a strong appetite for gold, marking the 11th consecutive month of inflows into Exchange Traded Funds (ETFs). This persistent demand is helping support spot gold prices, which stabilized after a sharp pullback in March. Analysts note that despite the dollar index (DXY) trading near resistance levels around 104.50, domestic appetite for gold remains robust, reflecting hedging strategies against currency fluctuations.  

The sustained ETF inflows come amid a volatile currency environment, with the USD/INR exchange rate hovering near 82.50 and EUR/INR holding around 89.30. Traders cite the combination of a firm rupee and global gold consolidation as fueling local buying. Technical indicators suggest gold’s support around $1,980 per ounce remains intact, while resistance near $2,050 could cap near-term gains.  

Market watchers see these trends as a signal of continued investor caution. Rising geopolitical tensions and the prospect of interest rate adjustments in the U.S. are prompting traders to maintain exposure to gold as a safe haven. As forex markets remain reactive, gold ETFs offer Indian investors a currency-hedged avenue to benefit from potential USD weakness while diversifying portfolios in a time of heightened volatility.
