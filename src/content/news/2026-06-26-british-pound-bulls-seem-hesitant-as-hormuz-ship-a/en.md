GBP/USD maintains a mild positive bias for a second straight session, but British Pound bulls appear hesitant as the currency pair struggles to extend gains. The exchange rate remains below the previous day’s swing high and is trading just under the 1.3200 level during Friday’s Asian session, reflecting limited upward momentum in trading.

Hormuz ship attack supports safe-haven USD demand, lifting the US dollar and weighing on risk-sensitive currencies such as the British Pound. Improved safe-haven flows into USD strengthen the currency in forex markets, adding pressure on GBP/USD trading and keeping bulls cautious amid heightened geopolitical risk. This dynamic also influences broader FX sentiment, including EUR and other majors.

Pound-dollar pair outlook remains tied to geopolitical developments and US dollar strength, with the 1.3200 level acting as an immediate resistance in GBP/USD trading. Market participants are closely watching risk appetite and potential spillovers into EUR/USD and other major currency pairs, which could shape near-term forex exchange rate direction in Friday trading.
