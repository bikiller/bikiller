The EUR/USD currency pair opened the week with a sharp bearish gap, quickly sliding to the 1.1520-1.1515 zone during the Asian session, marking its lowest level since November 2025. Traders noted the firming of the USD as the primary driver, with the exchange rate under pressure amid broad dollar strength across global markets. Market participants are monitoring whether this momentum will persist through European and U.S. sessions.  

Technical analysts point to the 1.1500 neighborhood as a key psychological support, with the spot price struggling to stabilize above it. The pair’s recent decline reflects heightened selling interest in EUR amid safe-haven flows into USD, and traders are weighing macroeconomic data, central bank signals, and geopolitical developments that could extend the downtrend. Short-term trading strategies are increasingly favoring downside positions.  

Fundamental factors continue to reinforce the bearish outlook, as the USD gains traction against major currencies while the euro faces headwinds from economic uncertainties and policy divergence. Investors are watching closely for potential rebounds, but the prevailing sentiment suggests the path of least resistance remains lower, keeping EUR/USD traders alert for further testing of support levels in coming sessions.
