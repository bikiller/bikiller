Gold (XAU/USD) stabilized around $4,700 on Friday, managing to recover from earlier losses. However, despite this slight rebound, the precious metal is set to post a weekly decline, primarily driven by a persistent outlook for higher interest rates. Traders remain cautious as macroeconomic uncertainties, particularly tensions in the Middle East, continue to overshadow market sentiment.

The ongoing geopolitical instability has kept buyers on the sidelines, limiting gold's potential for a stronger performance. As the market navigates these challenges, the exchange rate dynamics between the USD and other currencies, such as the EUR, will play a crucial role in shaping gold's trading trajectory in the coming weeks. Investors will closely monitor these developments to gauge future movements in this critical currency pair.
