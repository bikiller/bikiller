Gold (XAU/USD) maintains its footing above the $4,800 level as traders weigh geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, which continue to underpin safe-haven demand. The metal has trimmed some of its intraday gains but retains a positive bias, reflecting persistent USD softness against major currencies, including the EUR and JPY. Market participants remain alert to potential supply disruptions that could further influence exchange rates.  

Trading activity in precious metals shows cautious optimism, with XAU/USD bouncing off key technical support near $4,800. Analysts note that any escalation around Hormuz could reinforce safe-haven flows, supporting gold against a backdrop of a broadly weaker USD. Currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD also exhibit volatility, reflecting shifting risk sentiment and central bank positioning.  

Investors are closely monitoring geopolitical developments alongside macroeconomic indicators to gauge gold’s trajectory. Sustained trading above $4,800 could signal further upside potential, while any breakdown may attract short-term profit-taking. Forex traders are advised to watch correlations with USD strength and broader market risk appetite, as movements in gold often ripple across major currency pairs, influencing hedging strategies and trading flows.
