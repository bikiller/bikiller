Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded on Thursday, recovering from earlier losses as sellers failed to maintain momentum below the $4,600 level. The precious metal’s intraday pullback reversed amid a softer US Dollar, which eased pressure on the currency pair, allowing gold to regain footing. Traders noted that the exchange rate’s resilience near key technical support encouraged renewed buying interest in the market.  

Market participants also cited falling US Treasury yields as a supportive factor, reducing the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like gold. The combination of a weaker USD and lower yields contributed to the rebound, highlighting the sensitivity of XAU/USD trading to macroeconomic indicators. Analysts suggested that short-term trading could remain choppy, with $4,600 acting as a crucial support level for further moves.  

Investors continue monitoring broader forex markets, with the USD’s trajectory influencing major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD. A softer dollar tends to boost gold demand, while shifts in Treasury yields can impact currency flows and risk sentiment. As gold stabilizes above $4,600, traders are weighing the implications for upcoming sessions, considering both technical levels and macro drivers in their positions.
