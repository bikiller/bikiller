The Euro (EUR) has retraced its early gains against the British Pound (GBP) on Wednesday, driven by the latest inflation data from the UK and the Eurozone. Currently, the EUR/GBP currency pair is trading at approximately 0.8817, down from an intraday peak of 0.8839. Market participants are assessing the implications of this data on future monetary policy.

As traders digest the steady inflation figures, attention is shifting towards the upcoming UK Budget. With the current exchange rate reflecting a cautious sentiment, analysts are closely monitoring any policy signals that could impact the GBP. The interplay between these economic indicators and the upcoming fiscal announcements could influence trading strategies in the days ahead.
