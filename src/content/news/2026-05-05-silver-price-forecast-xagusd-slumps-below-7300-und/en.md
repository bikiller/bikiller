Silver edged lower on Tuesday, with the XAG/USD currency pair slipping below the $73.00 mark to trade near $72.85 during Asian hours. The metal remained under sustained selling pressure, retreating beneath its 100-day exponential moving average (EMA), a key technical level watched by traders. The downturn reflects growing caution in the markets as investors weigh broader economic risks.  

Heightened tensions in the Middle East contributed to the slide, with reports of Iranian attacks on vessels in the Strait of Hormuz pushing crude oil prices higher. Rising energy costs have sparked renewed inflation concerns, prompting forex traders to favor the USD amid its safe-haven appeal. The silver exchange rate mirrored this risk-off sentiment, underperforming other commodities and paired currencies like the EUR.  

Market participants noted that the technical breakdown below the 100-day EMA could open the door for further downside momentum if geopolitical uncertainty persists. Traders are closely monitoring both energy markets and USD dynamics, as continued strength in the dollar may further pressure precious metals. Short-term trading strategies may focus on key support around $72.50 and resistance near $73.50, with volatility expected to remain elevated.
