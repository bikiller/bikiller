Market sentiment turned cautious following reports from Israel N12 News that Iranian Parliament speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf had resigned from the nuclear negotiating team. This uncertainty contributed to a spike in crude oil prices, which rose sharply during the American trading session. As a result, the price of West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude surged to $85.50, reflecting a 3% increase.

The heightened risk aversion also influenced currency markets, with the USD gaining traction against the EUR, pushing the exchange rate to 1.0680. Traders are now closely monitoring developments in Iran, as any escalation in tensions can further impact oil prices and currency dynamics. The ongoing geopolitical instability underscores the volatility in the forex market, prompting traders to reassess their positions amid fluctuating oil prices and uncertain diplomatic outcomes.
