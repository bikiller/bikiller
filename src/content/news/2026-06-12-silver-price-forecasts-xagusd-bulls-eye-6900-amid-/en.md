Silver (XAG/USD) regained traction during Friday’s European trading session, reversing part of the losses recorded earlier in the week and climbing back toward the mid-$67.00s. The rebound was supported by an improvement in overall market sentiment, encouraging investors to return to risk-sensitive assets. The precious metal’s recovery also reflected renewed trading interest as the USD softened against several major currencies, helping support commodity-linked exchange rate movements.

Market participants continue to monitor the currency pair alongside broader developments in the USD and EUR markets, as shifts in risk appetite remain a key driver of price action. A more constructive market mood has reduced demand for defensive positions, while expectations surrounding monetary policy and global growth prospects have contributed to increased volatility across financial markets. These factors have helped silver stabilize after recent weakness and regain upward momentum.

From a technical perspective, XAG/USD remains focused on higher resistance zones, with bulls targeting the $69.00 level if buying pressure continues to strengthen. Sustained trading above the mid-$67.00 region could reinforce the bullish outlook and attract additional momentum-driven flows. However, traders remain cautious as fluctuations in the US Dollar and broader market sentiment could influence near-term direction and determine whether the metal can extend its advance toward key upside objectives.
