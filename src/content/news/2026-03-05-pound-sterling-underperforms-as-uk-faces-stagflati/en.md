The Pound Sterling is underperforming against its major peers, with the exchange rate slipping 0.22% to 1.3340 against the US Dollar (USD) during Thursday's Asian session. This decline comes amid rising concerns over the UK's economic outlook, with stagflation risks putting pressure on the currency. The combination of stagnant growth and rising inflation has spooked investors, weighing on market sentiment.

The ongoing geopolitical tensions in the Middle East further exacerbate the uncertainty surrounding global markets, adding volatility to forex trading. With the USD holding steady, the Pound faces additional challenges as traders remain cautious amid broader economic and geopolitical risks. The current exchange rate levels suggest further potential for downside movement if UK economic data continues to disappoint.
