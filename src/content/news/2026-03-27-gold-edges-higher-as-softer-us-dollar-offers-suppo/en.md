Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher on Friday, recovering modestly after a sharp 2.75% decline the previous day, as a softer US Dollar provided support. The exchange rate showed renewed stability amid heightened market jitters over escalating tensions between the US, Israel, and Iran. Traders appeared cautious, balancing safe-haven demand for gold with broader risk sentiment across global currency pairs.  

The USD weakened against major currencies, including the EUR and JPY, fueling a rebound in commodity-linked trading. Analysts noted that gold’s technical levels, particularly near $1,920 per ounce, acted as a floor for buyers, while resistance around $1,960 could cap further gains. Market participants are closely monitoring geopolitical developments, which continue to drive volatility across forex and commodity markets.  

Investors are weighing the potential implications for both currencies and metals, as escalating conflict risks push traders toward hedges in gold and other safe-haven assets. Trading volumes remained elevated, reflecting heightened uncertainty in the USD and related currency pairs. The interplay between a softer Dollar and geopolitical concerns underscores gold’s ongoing role as a barometer for risk appetite and currency market dynamics.
