The Euro strengthened sharply during Monday trading, with the EUR gaining ground against the USD as improving geopolitical sentiment weighed on the US Dollar. The EUR/USD currency pair climbed 0.37%, reaching 1.1645 at the time of writing. Traders reacted to renewed optimism surrounding diplomatic progress between the United States and Iran, prompting a shift away from traditional safe-haven demand for the greenback.

Market sentiment improved after reports suggested hopes for an agreement between Washington and Tehran to extend the current ceasefire by 60 days. Discussions linked to Iran’s uranium enrichment program further encouraged risk appetite across currency trading markets. As geopolitical tensions appeared to ease, investors reduced defensive USD positions and moved toward higher-yielding and risk-sensitive assets, supporting the Euro exchange rate.

The latest move in the EUR/USD exchange rate highlights how geopolitical developments continue to drive short-term forex trading direction. A sustained decline in demand for the US Dollar could keep the currency pair supported in the near term, while traders monitor whether negotiations evolve into a broader agreement capable of influencing global risk sentiment and future USD flows.
