NZD/USD edges higher, hovering near a one-week peak against the USD after a choppy previous session marked by two-way price action. The currency pair is trading around the 0.5685 level during the Asian session on Friday, reflecting mild buying interest as traders reassess short-term exchange rate direction, supporting near-term sentiment in spot trading.

Market participants remain cautious as the USD lacks clear momentum ahead of the upcoming US Nonfarm Payrolls report. The forex trading environment is subdued, with investors reluctant to take large positions across major currency pairs. The NZD benefits slightly from this hesitation in the greenback, while broader FX markets, including EUR crosses, remain range-bound.

Attention now turns to US NFP data, which could provide fresh impetus for NZD/USD trading and determine whether the currency pair can extend gains beyond its current one-week highs. A stronger USD outcome could pressure the exchange rate lower, while softer data may support further upside momentum in risk-sensitive currencies like the New Zealand dollar.
