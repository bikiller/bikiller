The AUD/USD currency pair edged higher on Wednesday, pushing toward four-year highs amid renewed optimism over a potential Iran nuclear deal, which weighed on the US Dollar. The pair gained around 0.8%, testing an intraday peak of 0.7280 before failing to hold above the 0.7250 level. By the close, AUD/USD settled near 0.7240, reflecting broad USD weakness and improved risk sentiment across global markets.  

Traders cited a combination of geopolitical developments and technical factors driving the exchange rate higher. The Australian Dollar found support from commodity-linked flows, while the US Dollar came under pressure as investors assessed potential easing in geopolitical tensions. Market participants noted that resistance around 0.7280 could limit further upside in the short term, while 0.7250 remains a key handle for intraday trading decisions.  

The move in AUD/USD highlights how macro events and market positioning continue to shape forex trading. A sustained break above the recent intraday highs could attract further buying interest versus USD, while broader USD trends against major currencies like EUR and JPY may influence near-term volatility. For now, traders are monitoring geopolitical headlines and technical levels closely as the exchange rate hovers near multi-year peaks.
