The US dollar lost ground on Monday as global markets reacted to heightened Oil volatility. Reports that several nations have cut Oil output, combined with disruptions in exports through the Strait of Hormuz due to threats from Iran, triggered risk-off sentiment. The USD index slipped 0.4%, pulling major currency pairs lower, while EUR/USD pushed above 1.0950, reflecting growing appetite for the Euro amid easing dollar demand.  

Crude futures surged more than 3%, intensifying concerns about supply and fueling volatility across commodity-linked currencies. The GBP/USD edged higher to 1.2450, supported by risk-on flows, while the USD/JPY dipped below 135.50, testing key technical support. Traders focused on these movements as geopolitical tensions intersected with monetary dynamics, prompting a cautious trading session for the greenback.  

Analysts note that extended oil supply disruptions could pressure the USD further, potentially shifting capital toward currencies with perceived stability or higher yields. Market participants are watching technical levels closely: EUR/USD aims for 1.1000, USD/JPY must hold 135.30, and commodity currencies like AUD and CAD track crude prices for direction. The interplay of geopolitical risk and central bank expectations continues to define near-term currency exchange trends.
