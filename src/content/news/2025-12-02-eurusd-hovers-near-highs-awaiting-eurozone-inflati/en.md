EUR/USD is currently trading at 1.1615, showing marginal gains as traders anticipate the upcoming Eurozone inflation data. After reaching a two-week high above 1.1650 on Monday, the currency pair has experienced a slight pullback, reflecting a cautious sentiment in the market. 

Market participants are closely monitoring this inflation report, as it could significantly influence the exchange rate between the euro and the US dollar. Any surprises in the data may lead to increased volatility, affecting future trading strategies for both currencies. As the market awaits further developments, the focus remains firmly on the economic indicators that could shape the trajectory of the EUR/USD pair.
