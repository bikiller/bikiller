Brent crude edged lower to around $96 per barrel on Thursday during the European session, retreating from Wednesday's $98 highs. The easing comes after Israel and Lebanon announced a ceasefire agreement, reducing geopolitical risk and dampening recent supply concerns. Crude’s pullback has prompted modest USD strength, with the EUR/USD exchange rate hovering near 1.0960 as traders digest the news.

The oil market’s correction reflects a cautious shift in risk sentiment, affecting broader commodity-linked currencies. The USD/JPY pair showed slight gains, reflecting demand for the safe-haven dollar, while commodity currencies like the AUD and CAD experienced mild volatility in early trading. Analysts suggest Brent may face resistance near $98, with support around $95, keeping traders attentive to further geopolitical developments.

For forex traders, the linkage between energy prices and currency movements remains significant. A sustained decline in Brent could ease inflation pressures, influencing USD exchange rates against major pairs. Meanwhile, oil-driven currencies may see short-term reversals, with EUR/USD and GBP/USD sensitive to shifts in crude pricing. Market participants continue monitoring both geopolitical headlines and technical levels to guide trading strategies.
