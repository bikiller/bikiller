The US Dollar (USD) continued its downward momentum on Wednesday, marking a second consecutive day of losses. The greenback retreated from recent multi-month highs as risk sentiment improved across markets. Hopes for a potential resolution to the Middle East conflict have boosted global risk appetite, weighing on the USD. As a result, the EUR/USD pair saw a modest rebound, rising 0.3% to 1.0900.

The softer USD also impacted other major currency pairs, with USD/JPY trading lower at 131.20, down 0.4% from its recent peak. The ongoing volatility in the US labour market remains a key focus for traders, with Friday's non-farm payrolls report set to add further clarity on the health of the US economy. Any surprises in the data could provide fresh direction for the greenback in the near term.
