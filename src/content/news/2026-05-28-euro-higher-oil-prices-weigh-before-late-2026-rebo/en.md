The EUR/USD currency pair remained volatile as investors reacted to changing expectations surrounding the possible reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, according to ABN AMRO analysts. Higher oil prices continued to pressure the euro in the near term, with rising energy costs expected to weigh on growth across the Eurozone. The exchange rate traded within a broad range as market sentiment shifted between geopolitical risks and improving global trade conditions.

ABN AMRO expects the EUR to remain under pressure against the USD through much of 2026, largely due to elevated oil prices and cautious European economic prospects. However, the bank anticipates a stronger rebound in the euro later in 2026 as energy markets stabilize and investor confidence improves. Trading activity in the currency pair is also being influenced by diverging monetary policy expectations between the European Central Bank and the Federal Reserve.

Analysts noted that the USD has continued to benefit from safe-haven demand during periods of geopolitical uncertainty, limiting upside potential for the EUR/USD exchange rate in the short term. Still, a gradual recovery in European fundamentals and easing energy pressures could support a more constructive outlook for the euro over the longer horizon.
