West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude held steady near $95.30 on Friday, reflecting a delicate balance between easing supply pressures and mounting geopolitical concerns in the Middle East. The release of strategic reserves by major producers has tempered immediate supply risks, keeping oil prices range-bound. Traders are weighing these developments against persistent tensions in the region, which could disrupt flows and trigger volatility in energy-linked currency pairs such as USD/CAD and EUR/USD.  

Forex markets showed measured reactions, with the US dollar maintaining strength against the euro as investors priced in potential risk premiums from oil market uncertainties. Commodity-linked currencies, including the Canadian dollar and Norwegian krone, saw subdued trading, mirroring the muted shifts in crude. Analysts note that sustained WTI levels near $95 may continue to influence USD-denominated exchange rates, affecting cross-border capital flows and broader forex trading strategies.  

Technical indicators suggest WTI is consolidating after recent swings, with $94.80 acting as near-term support and $96.00 as resistance. Market participants are closely monitoring both Middle East developments and central bank commentary, anticipating that any escalation could drive sharp moves in oil and correlated currency pairs. This cautious environment underscores the interplay between energy markets and forex trading, highlighting the importance of strategic positioning in USD, EUR, and other major currencies.
