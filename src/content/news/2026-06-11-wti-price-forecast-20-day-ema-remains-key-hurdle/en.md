West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures on NYMEX fell sharply on Thursday, dipping nearly 3% to trade around $87.60 during European hours. The decline highlights the 20-day exponential moving average (EMA) as a key resistance level, with traders closely monitoring whether WTI can rebound or continue sliding. Market participants are factoring in broader USD strength and fluctuating energy demand in shaping their positions.

The sell-off in WTI comes amid heightened volatility in currency markets, where the EUR/USD exchange rate has experienced minor retracements as the USD maintains a firmer stance. Traders cite technical levels and shifting economic data as drivers, linking oil price movement with dollar-denominated commodities. A weaker oil market could influence USD liquidity and trading flows, affecting both currency pairs and commodities portfolios.

Looking ahead, analysts suggest that sustaining momentum below the 20-day EMA may encourage further bearish sentiment in WTI, potentially impacting energy-linked currencies. Market watchers are also tracking geopolitical developments and macroeconomic indicators that could sway both exchange rates and crude benchmarks. For forex traders, this interplay between oil prices and USD performance underscores the need for vigilant risk management in short-term trading strategies.
