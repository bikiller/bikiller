The Korean Won and Indonesian Rupiah remained under pressure in Asian foreign-exchange trading, despite softer oil prices offering some support to regional currencies. According to OCBC FX Strategist Sim Moh Siong, the improvement in energy costs has provided only limited relief, as broader market forces continue to weigh on sentiment. The focus remains on capital flows, exchange-rate stability, and demand for USD across Asia.

The KRW has faced ongoing headwinds from equity outflows, with investors continuing to reduce exposure to local assets. These flows have added pressure to the currency pair dynamics against the USD, even as external conditions become marginally more favorable. Market participants are closely monitoring whether sustained foreign selling could keep the Won vulnerable in near-term trading sessions.

Meanwhile, the Indonesian Rupiah has remained under strain amid concerns surrounding the policy outlook and investor confidence. While lower oil prices may help ease some inflation-related pressures, traders appear reluctant to adopt a more constructive view on the currency. The broader Asia FX landscape continues to reflect cautious positioning, with USD demand staying firm and limiting the ability of regional currencies to strengthen against major counterparts such as the EUR and the US dollar.
