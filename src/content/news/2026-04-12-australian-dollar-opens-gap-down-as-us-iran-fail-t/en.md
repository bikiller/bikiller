The Australian dollar opened sharply lower against the US dollar on Monday, reflecting heightened geopolitical tension after the US and Iran failed to reach a peace deal. The AUD/USD currency pair initially gapped down, testing early support near 0.7000, before finding some footing. During Asian trading hours, the pair was hovering around 0.7010, signaling modest recovery but remaining firmly in negative territory. Traders cited risk-off sentiment as a key driver behind the dip.  

Technical analysts note that the AUD/USD exchange rate faces immediate resistance around 0.7035, with the next major support seen near 0.6990. Momentum indicators suggest limited upside in the short term, as investors remain cautious amid unresolved international developments. The market reaction underscores the sensitivity of commodity-linked currencies like the Australian dollar to global geopolitical shocks, especially in periods of heightened uncertainty.  

Market participants are closely watching broader forex movements, with the USD gaining strength against several major currencies including the EUR, amid a flight to safe-haven assets. Trading volumes in the Asia-Pacific session reflected selective buying of the US dollar, while the Australian dollar struggled to regain lost ground. Analysts caution that sustained negative sentiment could weigh on the AUD further, keeping traders focused on geopolitical headlines and technical levels around 0.7000–0.7050.
