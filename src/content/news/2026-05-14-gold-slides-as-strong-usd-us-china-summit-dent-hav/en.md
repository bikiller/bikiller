Gold extended losses on Thursday as the US dollar strengthened, with XAU/USD slipping about 0.25% during the North American session. Despite ongoing tensions in the Middle East, investors appeared less inclined toward safe-haven assets, with the spot gold price trading near $4,678. The stronger USD exerted additional pressure on commodities, impacting gold’s appeal versus major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.

Market focus also shifted to the high-profile US-China summit, where President Donald Trump met Chinese President Xi Jinping. Traders monitored developments closely, with expectations that any positive trade signals could dampen haven demand further. The exchange rate dynamics saw currency pairs sensitive to global risk sentiment, with the USD maintaining gains across forex markets while risk-linked assets underperformed.

Technically, gold’s retreat below key support levels reflects a combination of fundamental and geopolitical factors. Analysts suggest that unless fresh market shocks emerge, XAU/USD may continue to consolidate around current levels. Forex traders are advised to watch USD movements and geopolitical headlines, as these will likely dictate near-term trading momentum in gold and correlated currencies.
