Gold (XAU/USD) rebounded on Friday, climbing to a two-day high of $4,096 as softer US Treasury yields weighed on the US Dollar. The precious metal extended its recovery in the forex market, with the XAU/USD exchange rate trading around $4,076, marking a 1.24% daily gain amid renewed demand for safe-haven assets.

The move comes as investors scale back hawkish expectations for the Federal Reserve, triggering a pullback in US yields and weakening the USD across currency pairs. Lower yields reduce the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like gold, supporting buying interest in the XAU/USD trading pair and reinforcing its upward momentum in broader forex markets.

From a trading perspective, the XAU/USD pair remains supported while US yields stay subdued, though volatility may persist depending on upcoming Fed commentary and USD direction. Traders continue to monitor key resistance near $4,096 and support around recent consolidation levels as the gold price attempts to stabilize above the $4,070 zone in the forex session.
