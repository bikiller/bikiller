ECB Governing Council member and Malta’s central bank governor, Alexander Demarco, signaled on Friday that the European Central Bank is likely to lift interest rates at its June meeting. Traders reacted cautiously, with the EUR/USD exchange rate hovering around 1.0970, as markets priced in the potential policy shift. The remarks underscore persistent inflationary pressures in the eurozone and the ECB’s readiness to act to maintain price stability.

The prospect of a June hike has sparked renewed trading activity across major currency pairs. The EUR gained modestly against the USD on European sessions, while safe-haven flows kept the USD slightly firm against emerging market currencies. Technical levels suggest resistance near 1.1000 for EUR/USD, with support around 1.0920, indicating a narrow range as investors await further ECB guidance.

Market participants are weighing the implications for global forex flows, particularly in euro-denominated assets. A rate increase could strengthen the EUR, affecting cross-currency trading strategies and influencing commodity-linked currencies. Analysts note that while the timing of the hike is anticipated, the magnitude and follow-up moves will be closely monitored, keeping trading sentiment sensitive to any ECB commentary in the coming weeks.
