AUD/USD rebounded sharply on Monday, recovering from a gap lower at the start of the week as traders digested developments surrounding a potential US-Iran deal. The currency pair climbed back toward the 0.6800 level, supported by a softer US Dollar amid improved risk sentiment. Investors appeared more confident in risk assets, with the Australian Dollar benefiting from the broader market optimism.  

The exchange rate movement reflects a shift in sentiment after heightened geopolitical concerns earlier weighed on the pair. Traders cited easing tensions in the Middle East and optimism over renewed US-Iran negotiations as key drivers behind the reversal. Technical analysts noted that sustaining above the 0.6780 support could open the way for a test of the 0.6820 resistance, signaling potential near-term strength in AUD/USD.  

Market participants also monitored USD performance against other major currencies, including EUR and JPY, as the greenback struggled amid the changing risk backdrop. FX trading volumes remained steady, with investors balancing geopolitical headlines against broader macroeconomic data. The rebound in AUD/USD highlights how sensitive currency pairs remain to geopolitical catalysts and sentiment-driven shifts in the forex market.
