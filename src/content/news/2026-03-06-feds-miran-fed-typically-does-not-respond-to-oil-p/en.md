Federal Reserve Governor Stephen Miran expressed cautious optimism about recent economic data, noting that a single month's job report is insufficient to make sweeping policy decisions. In an interview with CNBC on Friday, Miran emphasized that such reports generally do not prompt the Fed to alter its stance drastically, further hinting at a continued dovish approach for U.S. monetary policy. 

The remarks have implications for the forex market, particularly for USD-based pairs. With the Fed maintaining a cautious outlook, the USD could face downward pressure, especially against the EUR. EUR/USD has been consolidating around the 1.0700 mark, with traders awaiting more signals from the Fed before making significant moves. Miran's dovish tone could keep the market cautious, potentially slowing the U.S. dollar's recovery in the near term.
