GBP/USD edged lower on Tuesday, trading near 1.3310 during Asian hours, following a strong rally in the previous session that saw the currency pair gain nearly 0.75%. The pound faced renewed selling pressure as the market reassessed risks, testing the 1.3300 support level. Traders remain cautious amid a prevailing bearish bias, with the exchange rate struggling to sustain the recent upside momentum against the USD.  

The USD regained some strength against major currencies, including EUR and GBP, reflecting a shift in risk sentiment and expectations for upcoming economic data. Investors are monitoring UK inflation signals and U.S. Treasury yields, which continue to influence the pound-dollar trading dynamics. A breach below 1.3300 could open the door to further downside, while a rebound above 1.3350 may be needed to restore bullish confidence.  

Market participants are positioning for short-term volatility, with the GBP/USD closely tracking broader forex trends. Technical analysts highlight that momentum favors the downside, but key support and resistance levels will dictate near-term moves. Traders are balancing risk from macroeconomic releases, central bank signals, and global market sentiment, keeping the currency pair under the microscope for potential shifts in the exchange rate trajectory.
