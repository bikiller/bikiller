The US Dollar extended its gains, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) climbing to around 99.90, supported by heightened safe-haven demand as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East persist. Traders have been cautious, seeking the relative security of USD amid uncertainty over Iran, while interest rate differentials continue to favor the Greenback against major currencies such as the EUR, GBP, and JPY.  

Currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY have reflected this dynamic, with EUR/USD retreating toward key support levels as USD strength dominates trading sessions. Analysts note that the firming USD exchange rate is partly driven by expectations of sustained US monetary policy, keeping investors attentive to technical levels that could influence near-term price movements.  

Market participants are weighing the broader implications, as a stronger USD impacts global trade and commodity pricing. While short-term trading favors risk aversion, movements in USD/CHF and AUD/USD suggest volatility could persist, with potential breakouts hinging on geopolitical developments and central bank signals. Investors are monitoring these currency pairs closely, balancing risk and opportunity in a cautious trading environment.
