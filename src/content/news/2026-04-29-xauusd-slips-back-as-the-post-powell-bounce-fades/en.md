XAU/USD retreated Wednesday, losing roughly 1.6% as the post-Powell rally faded. The currency pair surged to a session high near 4,610 earlier in the day, buoyed by cautious optimism ahead of the FOMC decision, but slipped to a session low around 4,510 as traders reassessed the market outlook. The decline reflected USD strength and profit-taking in precious metals amid lingering rate hike concerns.  

During Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell’s press conference, XAU/USD briefly recovered to roughly 4,565, as investors digested his comments on monetary policy and economic growth. Market participants weighed signals from the Fed against broader USD movements, with exchange rates in other major currencies like EUR/USD also influencing gold’s trading dynamics. Volatility remained elevated as traders balanced inflation risks and potential shifts in central bank guidance.  

Following the rebound, the currency pair slipped again, testing support near 4,520 before stabilizing around 4,534. Analysts noted that technical levels and market sentiment could continue to drive short-term fluctuations, with XAU/USD sensitive to USD strength and global economic indicators. Trading activity suggested cautious positioning ahead of upcoming economic data, as investors monitor potential implications for forex markets and gold prices.
