The GBP/USD currency pair slipped below the 1.3350 mark on Friday, retreating from a daily peak of 1.3442. The pair shed 0.84% as traders grew cautious amid rising tensions in the Middle East and an uptick in oil prices, which fueled risk-off sentiment. Market participants weighed the impact on global currencies, with the USD benefiting from its safe-haven status in the wake of geopolitical uncertainty.  

Investor focus also centered on the Federal Reserve’s outlook, as expectations of no rate cuts in 2026 bolstered the greenback. This shift in sentiment pressured the British pound, with traders recalibrating positions in the currency pair and broader forex markets. The exchange rate movement underscores the sensitivity of GBP/USD to both geopolitical shocks and central bank guidance.  

Technical analysts noted that the retreat below 1.3350 could signal further downside if risk aversion persists, while a rebound above 1.3442 may offer temporary relief for the pound. Forex traders are closely monitoring oil price trends and Fed communications, as these factors continue to shape USD strength and GBP performance across major currency pairs, influencing trading strategies in both short- and medium-term horizons.
