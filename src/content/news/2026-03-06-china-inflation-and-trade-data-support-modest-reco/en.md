China's inflation and trade data are signaling a modest recovery, with February's CPI inflation expected to rise to 1.0% year-on-year, according to ING economists Lynn Song and Min Joo Kang. The increase is largely attributed to the Lunar New Year, which traditionally boosts spending. Additionally, the impact of rising oil prices is anticipated to show up later, potentially adding upward pressure on prices in the coming months.

The inflation outlook is likely to affect the USD/CNY exchange rate, as traders factor in these developments when positioning for the yuan. A modest inflationary uptick could reinforce expectations of a steady recovery in the Chinese economy, providing support for the yuan against the USD. However, market participants will be closely monitoring how these inflationary trends interact with broader global economic forces and oil price movements, which could influence the EUR/CNY and other key pairs in the near term.
