West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil steadied near $92 per barrel Thursday, reclaiming some ground after two days of declines. During Asian trading, WTI hovered around $91.90, underpinned by ongoing constraints in the Strait of Hormuz, a key chokepoint for global oil flows. Analysts note that the tight supply outlook continues to support crude, while investors watch the USD for potential spillover effects on energy-linked currency pairs like USD/CAD and EUR/USD.  

The exchange rate dynamics have also been influenced by oil movements, with currencies of energy exporters reacting to price swings. A firmer WTI tends to boost commodity-linked currencies, driving USD/CAD lower, while risk-sensitive pairs such as EUR/USD can reflect broader market sentiment shifts. Traders remain focused on whether the supply bottleneck persists, as any escalation could tighten global liquidity, impacting both crude and forex markets simultaneously.  

Technical charts show WTI holding above short-term support near $91.50, suggesting a consolidation phase before potential further gains. Market participants are evaluating trading strategies that factor in crude’s influence on USD strength, with attention on resistance around $93.00. The interplay between oil prices and currency pairs highlights the interconnectedness of commodity markets and forex, underscoring the need for careful positioning amid geopolitical uncertainty in the Gulf.
