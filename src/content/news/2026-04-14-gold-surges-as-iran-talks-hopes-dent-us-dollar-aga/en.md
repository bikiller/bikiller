Gold (XAU/USD) surged nearly 2% on Tuesday, driven by renewed optimism over the resumption of US-Iran talks. The currency pair rebounded from daily lows of $4,742, reaching $4,835, reflecting a notable weakening of the US Dollar amid geopolitical uncertainty. Traders are weighing the impact of ongoing tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, where the US military recently seized Iran-linked ships, adding a risk premium to safe-haven assets like gold.  

The uptick in gold comes as the USD faces broad-based pressure, with exchange rates against major currencies such as the EUR showing muted strength. Market participants are increasingly factoring in geopolitical developments as a driver for forex trading strategies, influencing both spot and futures markets. Technical levels around $4,742 provided a base for the rebound, highlighting support zones traders are monitoring for XAU/USD positions.  

Analysts suggest the bullish momentum could persist if diplomatic progress continues, keeping the Dollar under strain. This scenario may also affect correlated markets, including oil and emerging market currencies, as investors reassess risk exposure. Traders are closely watching subsequent US-Iran developments, with any escalation or breakthrough likely to trigger further swings in gold prices and forex exchange rates globally.
