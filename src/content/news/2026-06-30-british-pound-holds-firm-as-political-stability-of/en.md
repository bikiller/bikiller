The British Pound Sterling held firm in Tuesday trading, edging up 0.11% even as UK economic data signaled a growth miss and signs of a slowing economy. Political stability helped offset weaker fundamentals, supporting demand for the currency. The GBP/USD currency pair traded around 1.3270, reflecting cautious but steady positioning in the foreign exchange market.

On the US side, the latest Bureau of Labour Statistics report showed job vacancies rose in May, reinforcing resilience in the US labour market and providing support for the USD. This kept pressure balanced across major currency pairs, with traders reassessing near-term Federal Reserve expectations amid mixed macro signals.

For forex traders, GBP/USD remains range-bound near 1.3270 as the currency pair navigates contrasting UK weakness and US labour strength. Short-term trading sentiment hinges on upcoming macro data and interest rate expectations. Against the euro, sterling stability also reflects relative political calm, keeping volatility contained across major FX markets.
