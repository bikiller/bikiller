The Indian Rupee (INR) opened Tuesday’s session largely unchanged against the US Dollar (USD), with the USD/INR currency pair hovering near the 95.00 level. Trading has been subdued as market participants digest mixed signals, while the broader forex market keeps an eye on external pressures, including global crude oil movements and geopolitical developments.

Oil prices have rebounded recently amid renewed concerns over a potential US-Iran deal, adding pressure to the Indian Rupee. The recovery in crude has dampened the outlook for the INR, given India’s reliance on imported energy. Analysts suggest that elevated oil costs could weigh on the exchange rate, making it difficult for the currency to gain traction against the USD.

Forex traders are monitoring the USD/INR pair for technical levels around 95.00, while also tracking USD and EUR movements in global markets for broader sentiment cues. Any further oil-driven volatility could influence trading ranges and prompt short-term fluctuations, highlighting the interplay between energy prices, geopolitical uncertainty, and currency performance in the Indian market.
