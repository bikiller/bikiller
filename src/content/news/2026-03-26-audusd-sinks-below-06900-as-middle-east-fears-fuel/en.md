The Australian Dollar fell sharply for the third consecutive session, with the AUD/USD currency pair slipping below the 0.6900 mark. The exchange rate dropped 0.76% as traders moved away from riskier assets amid mounting concerns over escalating tensions in the Middle East. Market participants are closely monitoring the situation between the US and Iran, with doubts over a potential ceasefire prompting a broad-based demand for the safe-haven USD.  

Trading activity showed the USD gaining strength not only against the AUD but also across other major currencies, including the EUR, as investors sought stability in uncertain geopolitical conditions. The surge in USD demand reflects heightened risk aversion, putting pressure on commodity-linked currencies like the Australian Dollar. Analysts suggest that further escalation could keep the AUD under pressure, with traders watching key technical levels and market sentiment for guidance.  

The current environment underscores the sensitivity of the AUD/USD pair to global geopolitical developments. With the exchange rate hovering below 0.6900, short-term trading strategies may favor USD strength, while risk assets face headwinds. Investors are expected to remain cautious, as shifts in Middle East diplomacy could trigger sharp swings across forex markets, influencing both intraday and longer-term positions.
