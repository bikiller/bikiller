Asian currencies are set to open weaker as geopolitical tensions drive crude oil prices and the US Dollar higher. OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong anticipate pressure on high-beta oil importers like the South Korean Won (KRW), Thai Baht (THB), Philippine Peso (PHP), and Indian Rupee (INR). The renewed uncertainty surrounding oil markets could dampen sentiment for these currencies, leading to a potential downturn in their exchange rates against the USD.

While crude prices climb amid geopolitical risks, the resumption of Hormuz Strait transit offers some relief, easing concerns of further escalation. Despite this, the Dollar's strength remains a key influence on Asian FX. As trading intensifies, investors will closely monitor key levels, particularly for USD/INR, USD/THB, and USD/KRW, where recent trends suggest further pressure. The market's focus remains on balancing oil market stability with the broader impact of the US Dollar.
