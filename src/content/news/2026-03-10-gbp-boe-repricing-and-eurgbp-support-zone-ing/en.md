UK rate markets have undergone one of the most significant repricings among major economies following the recent energy shock, a shift that continues to influence the GBP exchange rate across major currency pairs. According to ING strategist Chris Turner, persistent UK inflation and expectations of a relatively hawkish Bank of England have pushed traders to adjust interest-rate forecasts aggressively. This repricing has helped underpin GBP in FX trading, particularly against the USD and EUR.

The shift in expectations around Bank of England policy reflects growing concern that inflation pressures in the UK will remain elevated for longer than in other developed markets. As energy costs feed through the economy, traders in the currency market increasingly anticipate tighter monetary policy, which has supported the GBP exchange rate in recent sessions. This dynamic has been visible across multiple currency pair moves, including GBP/USD and EUR/GBP.

In the EUR/GBP currency pair specifically, market participants are watching a key support zone that could determine the next phase of trading. Turner notes that if this support level holds, the euro may struggle to extend gains against sterling despite broader EUR movements versus the USD. For FX traders, the combination of UK inflation risks, BoE rate expectations, and technical levels in EUR/GBP remains central to near-term exchange rate direction.
