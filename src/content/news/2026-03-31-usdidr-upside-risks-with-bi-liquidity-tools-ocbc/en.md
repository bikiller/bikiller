The USD/IDR exchange rate is rising towards the 17,000 level, driven by a stronger US Dollar and global risk-off sentiment. OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong point to several factors, including oil-related terms-of-trade pressures that are weighing on the Indonesian Rupiah. As a result, traders are cautious, and the Indonesian currency faces increasing downward pressure in the face of a firmer Dollar.

Market sentiment remains fragile, and this downside risk for the IDR could be exacerbated by Bank Indonesia's (BI) liquidity management tools. The central bank's actions to stabilize the domestic market may temporarily cushion the Rupiah, but the overall outlook for USD/IDR remains tilted towards the upside. Traders will be watching key technical levels as the exchange rate approaches 17,000, signaling potential volatility ahead.
