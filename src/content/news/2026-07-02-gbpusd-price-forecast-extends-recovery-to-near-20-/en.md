The GBP/USD currency pair extended its recovery during Thursday’s European trading session, with the British Pound gaining 0.1% against the USD to trade near 1.3290. The move reflects improving investor confidence in the Pound as traders responded to renewed expectations that the UK government will remain committed to its fiscal rules, helping support the exchange rate after recent volatility.

Market sentiment toward Sterling strengthened as hopes of disciplined fiscal policy outweighed lingering concerns about the broader economic outlook. The currency pair also found technical support by climbing toward its 20-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA), a closely watched indicator that often signals improving short-term momentum. Currency trading remained cautious, however, with investors continuing to monitor developments affecting both the GBP and USD.

The recovery in GBP/USD suggests that buyers are regaining control, although sustained gains may depend on confirmation of fiscal commitments and upcoming macroeconomic data. Traders are also watching broader moves in the USD and EUR, as shifts in global risk appetite and central bank expectations continue to influence major currency pairs. A decisive break above the 20-day EMA could reinforce the current bullish tone, while failure to hold above the 1.3290 area may limit further upside in the near term.
