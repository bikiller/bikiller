AUD/JPY currency pair is trading in negative territory around 112.20 during early European trading on Tuesday, extending losses after slipping below the 112.50 level. The exchange rate reflects renewed selling pressure on the Australian Dollar against the Japanese Yen, as forex trading activity remains cautious across major pairs including USD and EUR crosses.

The Japanese Yen strengthened broadly as traders increased positioning amid speculation of possible intervention from Japanese authorities, keeping volatility elevated in the forex market. Risk-sensitive currencies like the Australian Dollar continue to underperform, while USD-linked flows remain steady and EUR pairs show mixed momentum as investors reassess interest rate expectations across major central banks.

Technically, the break below 112.50 has shifted the bias for AUD/JPY to mildly bearish, with traders eyeing further downside if the exchange rate fails to reclaim that threshold. Market participants will monitor upcoming economic data and risk sentiment, as sustained Yen strength could pressure the pair further in short-term forex trading sessions.
