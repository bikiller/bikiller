Rising energy costs are putting upward pressure on European building materials, according to ING’s Maurice van Sante. Surging oil and gas prices, fueled by escalating tensions in the Middle East, are expected to push input costs higher, with cement, steel, and lumber facing significant price pressure. Traders watching EUR/USD are factoring in these inflationary risks, potentially influencing the euro’s exchange rate against the dollar.  

The impact is already rippling through forex markets, as higher commodity-linked costs can affect economic growth expectations. EUR/USD trading shows increased volatility as investors weigh the likelihood of central banks responding to inflationary pressures. Rising energy bills for manufacturers may translate into tighter margins, affecting corporate earnings and investor sentiment.  

For forex participants, these developments underline the importance of monitoring commodity-driven inflation alongside traditional macro indicators. A stronger USD could emerge if investors seek a haven amid market uncertainty, while EUR may see temporary weakness. Exchange rate movements in other currency pairs tied to energy exporters are also likely to respond, offering trading opportunities for those positioned to navigate these shifts.
