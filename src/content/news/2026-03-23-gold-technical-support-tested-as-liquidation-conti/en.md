Gold remains under pressure as liquidation continues, with prices dipping below key technical support levels. According to MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst, Lee Hardman, gold’s decline persists despite rising geopolitical tensions. At the time of writing, gold is trading around $1,970 per ounce, marking a significant drop of 1.5% on the day. The precious metal faces mounting headwinds as traders remain cautious, influenced by a stronger USD.

Other precious metals, including silver and platinum, are also struggling, mirroring gold’s weakness. The USD continues to dominate across currency pairs, with the EUR/USD exchange rate testing lower levels around 1.0850. Rising bond yields and expectations of further tightening by the Federal Reserve have created additional pressure on metals, pushing investors toward the safety of the greenback. This ongoing trend is likely to keep the precious metals market subdued in the short term.
