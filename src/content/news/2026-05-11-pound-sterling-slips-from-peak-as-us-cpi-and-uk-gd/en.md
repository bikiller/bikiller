Sterling retreated from a recent high near 1.3650, trading around 1.3610 in European markets on Monday. The GBP/USD currency pair had surged during the Asian session, briefly touching new local highs, but profit-taking and cautious positioning ahead of key economic data prompted a pullback. Traders are weighing the impact of the upcoming US CPI and UK GDP releases on exchange rates.  

The broader forex market saw the Pound’s gains tempered against the USD as investors eyed potential volatility from inflation metrics in the United States. Analysts note that a stronger-than-expected US CPI could support the dollar, pressuring the GBP/USD pair, while weaker UK GDP figures might further weigh on Sterling. Meanwhile, EUR/GBP movements remained subdued, reflecting the Pound’s cautious retracement.  

Technical levels around 1.3610 are now under scrutiny, as sustained trading above this mark could signal renewed upside momentum. Market participants are monitoring liquidity flows and interbank activity, balancing Sterling exposure with the uncertainty surrounding upcoming macroeconomic releases. Short-term traders are particularly focused on how the Pound reacts in the immediate lead-up to the key US and UK data, which could drive notable swings in both USD and EUR currency pairs.
