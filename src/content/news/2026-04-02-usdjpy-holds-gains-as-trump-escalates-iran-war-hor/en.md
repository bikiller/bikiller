The USD/JPY currency pair maintained its gains on Thursday, hovering around the 159.40 level after a sharp rally earlier in the session. Trading was marked by heightened volatility during the American hours as geopolitical tensions intensified. Headlines reporting U.S. actions against Iran, particularly near the Strait of Hormuz, briefly bolstered risk appetite, fueling a stronger dollar against the yen and pushing the exchange rate higher.  

Traders noted that the sudden spike tested technical resistance zones, prompting both short-term profit-taking and renewed buying interest. The USD’s resilience contrasted with mixed performances in other major pairs, including EUR/USD, which faced pressure amid cautious risk sentiment. Market participants remained attentive to potential escalations, weighing the impact of political developments on currency flows and broader forex trading dynamics.  

Analysts suggest that if tensions persist, the USD/JPY could see further upside, with 160.00 eyed as a psychological target. Conversely, any de-escalation may allow the yen to recover. For now, liquidity and headline-driven swings dominate trading, with traders closely monitoring geopolitical developments and their implications on dollar strength and global currency markets.
