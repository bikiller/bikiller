Gold (XAU/USD) continues its recovery on Tuesday, holding firmly above the $4,300 level as the exchange rate trades near $4,340, up 0.70% on the day. The currency pair remains supported as forex markets digest expectations around the Federal Reserve’s upcoming decision while traders monitor developments in the US-Iran peace framework, which is shaping risk sentiment.

Safe-haven demand continues to underpin the precious metal as geopolitical uncertainty surrounding the US-Iran deal offsets broader USD strength in forex trading. Expectations around the Fed decision are keeping currency markets cautious, with lower US Treasury yields limiting downside pressure on gold and supporting demand for the non-yielding asset against major currency pairs.

Market participants now look to fresh catalysts from the Fed statement and any updates on the US-Iran negotiations, with gold’s technical structure showing resilience above the $4,300 support zone. Sustained buying could reinforce bullish momentum in XAU/USD, while a stronger USD could cap gains, keeping trading conditions volatile across forex and commodities markets.
