The Indian Rupee (INR) remains under pressure against the US Dollar (USD), holding onto weekly losses as it opens Monday’s trading session. The USD/INR exchange rate is trading near 83.20, with oil prices adding to the pressure. Higher crude prices are likely to exacerbate India’s current account deficit, dampening sentiment towards the Rupee in the short term.

Market participants are also awaiting the Federal Reserve's next move on interest rates, with expectations that any signals from the Fed could affect USD dynamics. As the Fed keeps its tightening stance amid inflation concerns, the USD is likely to maintain its strength against emerging market currencies like the INR. Traders will be closely monitoring key technical levels around 83.50 for potential support or resistance as market conditions unfold.
