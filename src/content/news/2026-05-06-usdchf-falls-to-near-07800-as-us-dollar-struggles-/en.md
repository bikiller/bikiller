The USD/CHF currency pair extended its losses for a second consecutive session, trading near the 0.7800 level during Wednesday’s Asian hours. The US Dollar came under pressure as investor sentiment favored riskier assets, fueled by growing optimism that Washington may reach a diplomatic breakthrough with Iran. Traders noted that the exchange rate has now tested key support levels around 0.7800, reflecting broad USD weakness across global markets.  

EUR/USD and other major pairs also benefited from the risk-on environment, with the Euro and other currencies gaining against the greenback. Market participants cited improving risk appetite, which pressured safe-haven currencies like the Swiss franc in relative terms, despite the USD/CHF decline. Technical analysts highlighted that a sustained move below 0.7800 could signal further USD downside, while intraday resistance remains near 0.7835.  

The trading landscape suggests that the US Dollar may continue to face headwinds if geopolitical optimism persists, impacting its exchange rates against both the Swiss franc and other key currencies. Currency traders are watching economic data releases and political developments closely, as these factors could further shape the trajectory of USD/CHF and broader forex markets in the coming sessions.
