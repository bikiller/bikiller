EUR/JPY currency pair declined on Wednesday, with the exchange rate slipping 0.08% as the Euro retreated against the Japanese Yen. Price action broke below the 100-day SMA, reinforcing a short-term bearish trading bias in the forex market. Traders are increasingly focused on the 183 level as a key downside target, with momentum suggesting that selling pressure may persist if intraday support continues to weaken.

Market sentiment toward the yen strengthened amid rising speculation that Japanese authorities could intervene in foreign exchange markets to curb excessive volatility. At the same time, Japan’s producer-side inflation came in above expectations, holding above the 3% threshold. The combination of potential policy action and persistent inflation has provided underlying support for the yen, pressuring the EUR/USD and broader EUR crosses.

For traders, the break below the 100-day moving average signals a technically significant shift in the EUR/JPY exchange rate outlook. Short-term trading conditions now favor downside momentum, although volatility remains elevated across major currency pairs. Any confirmed intervention or changes in Bank of Japan and European Central Bank expectations could rapidly alter sentiment and trigger sharp reversals in price action.
