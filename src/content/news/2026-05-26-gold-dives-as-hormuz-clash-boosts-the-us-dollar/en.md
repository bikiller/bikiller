Gold (XAU/USD) tumbled more than 1.60% on Tuesday as renewed geopolitical tensions in the Strait of Hormuz lifted demand for the US Dollar. Following US strikes in southern Iran, safe-haven flows shifted into the USD, pressuring the precious metal. The exchange rate slipped back below the $4,500 mark after touching a daily high of $4,580 earlier in the session.

Broader forex trading saw the US Dollar strengthen across major currency pairs as risk-off sentiment dominated markets. The USD’s rebound weighed on non-yielding assets like gold, while pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD retreated. Traders reassessed exposure amid escalating Middle East tensions, with haven demand concentrating in the dollar rather than traditional defensive commodities.

Looking ahead, traders will monitor further developments around the Strait of Hormuz and US-Iran tensions, which could sustain volatility in both gold and forex markets. Key technical levels for XAU/USD remain in focus, with $4,500 acting as immediate support and $4,580 as resistance. Continued USD strength may cap upside momentum unless risk sentiment shifts decisively.
