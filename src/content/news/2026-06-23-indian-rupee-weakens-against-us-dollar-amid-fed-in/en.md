The Indian Rupee (INR) extended losses against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, with the USD/INR currency pair trading on the higher side of its recent range. The exchange rate reflected renewed dollar strength as traders adjusted positions amid changing interest rate expectations in the United States. Broader forex trading sentiment remained cautious across major currency pairs.

Federal Reserve rate hike bets continued to support the US Dollar, keeping pressure on the INR in Asian trading hours. Investors remained focused on US bond yields and upcoming macroeconomic data, which influenced demand for safe-haven currency flows. The EUR and other major currencies also showed mixed performance against the dollar amid shifting risk appetite.

Market participants expect continued volatility in USD/INR as Federal Reserve policy signals remain a key driver for global forex trading. On the domestic side, the Reserve Bank of India is closely monitoring currency movements to curb excessive volatility. In the near term, import costs and capital flows may remain sensitive to USD strength, keeping the rupee under pressure.
