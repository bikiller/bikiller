Japan's exports saw their largest increase since 2022, yet the Japanese Yen remained relatively stable against the US Dollar. Despite the positive export data, the USD/JPY exchange rate slipped slightly, reflecting a muted market response. The pair traded lower, with USD/JPY hovering just below the 132.50 mark, showing limited movement.

Market attention is now shifting towards the Bank of Japan's (BOJ) policies, with traders anticipating potential adjustments. The Yen's reaction to the export surge underscores the ongoing balance between economic data and the BOJ's accommodative stance, leaving the currency pair vulnerable to further shifts depending on future central bank actions.
