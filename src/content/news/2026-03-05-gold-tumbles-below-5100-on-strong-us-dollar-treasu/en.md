Gold prices dropped below $5,100 on Thursday, retreating from previous gains as a surge in US Treasury yields and a robust US Dollar pressured the precious metal. The strong performance of the USD, fueled by solid US jobs data, weighed on gold's appeal as a safe-haven asset. XAU/USD was trading at $5,069, down over 1.35% at the time of writing.

The increase in US Treasury yields intensified selling pressure on gold, making it less attractive for investors compared to higher-yielding assets. As the USD strengthened, the exchange rate for the EUR/USD and other currency pairs reflected the broader market shift, signaling that investor sentiment remains heavily influenced by the US economic outlook. Gold’s technical levels are now under scrutiny as traders adjust to these changing conditions.
