Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher during the Asian session on Thursday, regaining a significant portion of Wednesday’s pullback from a near four-week peak. The yellow metal attracted dip-buyers as hopes for progress in Iran diplomacy supported risk-sensitive flows, while speculation over a potential Federal Reserve rate cut added momentum. Traders watched gold hover near the key technical zone around $2,040, signaling strong underlying support.  

The broader trading environment saw the USD weaken against major currencies, including the EUR and JPY, amplifying gold’s appeal as a safe-haven asset. Currency pairs like EUR/USD climbed modestly, reflecting dollar softness, while USD/JPY edged lower, feeding further interest in gold for portfolio diversification. Market participants noted that sentiment-driven buying could continue if geopolitical optimism sustains, despite technical resistance levels near $2,045–$2,050.  

Analysts highlighted that renewed Fed rate cut expectations could influence both gold and the currency market in the coming sessions. Spot gold’s exchange rate movements remain sensitive to shifts in USD liquidity, Treasury yields, and currency pair fluctuations. Traders are weighing potential catalysts, from central bank signals to diplomatic developments, as gold consolidates near multi-week highs while testing short-term technical ceilings.
