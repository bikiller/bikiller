The Indian Rupee continued its slide against the US dollar, with the USD/INR currency pair extending gains for a third consecutive session. During Tuesday’s Asian trading hours, the exchange rate hovered around 95.40, reflecting persistent demand for the greenback amid market caution. Traders are closely monitoring the rupee’s momentum as global and domestic factors keep currency markets on edge.

Market participants are now turning their attention to India’s upcoming HSBC Composite and Services Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) data, scheduled for release later in the day. The PMI readings are expected to provide insight into the health of the country’s service sector and broader economic activity, potentially influencing short-term trading positions and the rupee’s trajectory. Analysts suggest that stronger-than-expected figures could ease some selling pressure on the INR, while weaker data may reinforce the current bullish trend in USD/INR.

The rupee’s performance also aligns with broader forex dynamics, where USD strength against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY continues to shape emerging market flows. For traders, levels around 95.40 could act as a near-term pivot, with any sustained breach likely to attract further speculative interest. As the market braces for the PMI release, currency traders are positioning for potential volatility, balancing risk exposure with expectations for India’s economic signals.
