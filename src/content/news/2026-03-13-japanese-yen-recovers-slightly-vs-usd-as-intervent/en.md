The Japanese Yen made a slight recovery against the U.S. Dollar during the Asian trading session on Friday, as the USD/JPY pair saw a pullback after a three-day rally. The exchange rate retreated to the 159.40-159.45 region, coming off its year-to-date high. This shift comes amid growing speculation about potential market intervention as traders brace for the release of key U.S. PCE data.

With the Yen's recovery, market participants remain cautious, considering the risk of intervention from Japanese authorities if the USD/JPY continues its upward momentum. Traders are closely monitoring the PCE data, which could influence the Federal Reserve's stance on interest rates, adding volatility to the pair. The current technical levels around 159.40-159.45 will be crucial in determining the next move for the currency pair in the near term.
