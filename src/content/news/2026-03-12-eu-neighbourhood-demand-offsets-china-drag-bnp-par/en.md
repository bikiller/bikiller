In 2025, European Union exports to China saw a sharp decline, affecting the EUR/USD currency pair. Despite this, nearby markets such as Türkiye, Morocco, Ukraine, and the Balkans provided a boost, compensating for the drop in Chinese demand. The EU’s trading activity with these neighboring regions has led to a moderate strengthening of the EUR against other currencies in the region. This shift has influenced the exchange rate dynamics, with EUR traders closely monitoring the EUR/TRY and EUR/UAH pairs.

The change in EU export patterns has had a notable impact on the forex market, as traders adjust their positions in response to shifting demand. The increased demand from neighboring partners is expected to help maintain the EUR’s relative stability, despite weaker ties with China. Market participants are keeping an eye on future data releases, particularly those related to the EUR/GBP and EUR/USD, which could provide further insight into how these geopolitical shifts will shape broader market trends.
