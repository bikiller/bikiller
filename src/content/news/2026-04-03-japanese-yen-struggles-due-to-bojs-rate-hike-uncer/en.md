The Japanese yen remains under pressure as uncertainty over the Bank of Japan’s next policy move keeps traders cautious. The USD/JPY currency pair extended gains for a third consecutive session, rising more than 0.5% on Thursday. During Asian trading hours on Friday, the exchange rate hovers around 159.60, reflecting steady momentum but limited volatility amid the Good Friday holiday.  

Trading volumes are thin, curbing sharper moves despite the pair’s upward trend. Investors continue to weigh the BoJ’s potential response to persistent inflationary pressures and recent global USD strength. The yen’s performance against the dollar contrasts with broader market sentiment, where risk-sensitive currencies such as the euro have seen relatively muted shifts.  

Analysts suggest that USD/JPY could maintain its elevated levels near 160 if market participants interpret central bank signals as dovish for Japan. Short-term traders remain alert to any BoJ statements that could trigger renewed volatility. The interplay between USD strength, BoJ policy ambiguity, and subdued holiday liquidity is likely to dominate trading in the near term.
