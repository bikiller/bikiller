The AUD/JPY currency pair extended its bullish momentum for the fourth straight session, hitting fresh all-time highs above the mid-113.00s during Wednesday’s Asian trading. The spot exchange rate surged 0.90% on the day, driven by renewed market speculation that the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) may tighten policy further. Traders highlighted the weakness in the Japanese yen as another key factor supporting the cross.  

Market participants noted that the rally reflects broader risk sentiment, with the Australian dollar benefiting from expectations of stronger domestic growth and potential rate hikes. Meanwhile, the yen’s ongoing softness against major currencies like the USD and EUR has amplified AUD/JPY gains. Analysts suggest that continued appetite for higher-yielding assets could sustain the current uptrend, with technical levels around 113.50 now under focus.  

Looking ahead, traders are monitoring RBA commentary and global risk cues to gauge the exchange rate’s next move. The robust upward trajectory positions AUD/JPY as one of the top performers among Asia-Pacific currency pairs this week, influencing broader forex flows and prompting investors to reassess exposure to USD and EUR-linked trades. Momentum indicators imply that further gains could be on the horizon if the RBA maintains its hawkish stance.
