Euro climbed to a nine-day high against the US Dollar on Thursday, with the EUR/USD currency pair strengthening as traders reacted to softer-than-expected US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data. The move in the exchange rate reflected renewed USD weakness, as forex market sentiment shifted toward expectations that the Federal Reserve may delay further rate hikes.

Weaker US employment figures signaled cooling labor market conditions, putting pressure on the US Dollar and reinforcing downside momentum in USD-denominated assets. In forex trading, investors adjusted positions rapidly, with EUR demand increasing across major currency pair flows. Lower bond yields further weighed on USD, supporting continued euro buying interest during the session.

Looking ahead, market participants will closely watch upcoming US data releases for confirmation of the Federal Reserve’s policy path, with the euro’s short-term outlook remaining supported while USD sentiment stays fragile. Increased forex volatility is expected as traders reassess interest rate differentials, keeping EUR/USD trading activity elevated across global sessions.
