The Pound Sterling weakened at the start of the week, with the GBP/USD currency pair snapping a five-day winning streak and opening lower near the 1.3390 level during Monday’s Asian trading session. The shift in the exchange rate reflects a broader risk-off tone across financial markets, as investors moved away from risk-sensitive assets and into the USD amid rising geopolitical uncertainty.

Market sentiment deteriorated following the collapse of United States–Iran peace talks, fueling fresh concerns over a potential Hormuz Strait blockade. Such a development could disrupt global oil flows and intensify volatility, prompting traders to reassess exposure to higher-yielding currencies like the British Pound. The USD benefited from safe-haven demand, adding pressure on the GBP/USD pair.

From a trading perspective, the pullback near 1.3390 signals a pause in bullish momentum, with participants closely watching nearby technical levels for direction. If risk aversion persists, the currency pair could face further downside pressure, while any easing in geopolitical tensions may help stabilize the Pound and support a rebound in the exchange rate.
