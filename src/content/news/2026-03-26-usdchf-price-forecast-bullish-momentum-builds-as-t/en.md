USD/CHF continued its upward trajectory on Thursday, building on broad-based USD strength amid escalating tensions in the Middle East. The currency pair is testing the 200-day simple moving average, signaling growing bullish momentum. Traders note that the Swiss Franc has struggled to gain ground, with the exchange rate facing resistance as market participants weigh the potential for Swiss National Bank intervention to temper excessive CHF appreciation.  

The US Dollar’s resilience is supported by safe-haven demand and robust trading flows, while the CHF remains under pressure despite traditional haven status. Investors are closely watching cross-currency dynamics, with EUR/CHF movements reflecting similar caution. Technical analysts highlight that a sustained break above the 200-day SMA could open the door for further gains toward key resistance levels, shaping trading strategies across major currency pairs.  

Market participants remain attentive to geopolitical developments, central bank signals, and USD liquidity conditions. With USD/CHF hovering near critical technical thresholds, short-term traders may capitalize on momentum, while longer-term investors monitor potential corrections. The interplay between US Dollar strength and CHF stability continues to define exchange rate dynamics, influencing trading patterns in EUR, GBP, and other major currencies.
