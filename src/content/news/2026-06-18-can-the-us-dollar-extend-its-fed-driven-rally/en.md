The US Dollar (USD) extended its Fed-driven rally after the Dollar Index (DXY) climbed back above the 100.00 level, following a hawkish policy update from the Federal Reserve under new Chair Kevin Warsh. The move reinforced bullish sentiment across the currency market, as traders reassessed interest rate expectations and repositioned USD exposure across major currency pairs.

Risk appetite in FX trading remained mixed, with the greenback outperforming most majors as EUR/USD and GBP/USD softened in response to the stronger USD bid. Exchange rate volatility increased as investors adjusted positioning around the Fed outlook, while short-term trading flows favored dollar demand over carry-driven strategies in emerging and developed market currencies.

Looking ahead, the sustainability of the USD rally will depend on whether the Fed maintains its hawkish tone and if upcoming data continues to support higher-for-longer interest rates. In the near term, traders will closely watch key inflation prints and Fed commentary, which could drive further volatility across USD crosses and broader forex markets.
