The EUR/USD pair dropped below the 1.1700 level early Thursday, dipping to 1.1680 during the European session. This move comes as the US Dollar (USD) gained strength against the Euro (EUR), following the Federal Reserve’s decision to keep interest rates unchanged. The Fed’s cautious stance on tightening monetary policy has provided support to the USD, further weighing on the EUR.

With the Fed holding rates steady, market participants are now looking ahead to upcoming economic data and potential signs of tightening in future policy meetings. The EUR/USD’s recent pullback highlights the ongoing market sensitivity to US monetary policy, with the 1.1680 level now serving as a key support point. If the USD continues to strengthen, the pair may test further lower levels, while a shift in the Fed’s outlook could see a rebound in the Euro.
