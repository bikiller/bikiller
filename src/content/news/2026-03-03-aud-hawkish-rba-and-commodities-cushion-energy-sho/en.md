The Australian Dollar (AUD) has been resilient against the US Dollar (USD), benefiting from a hawkish stance by the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) and strong demand for commodities. AUD/USD is trading near 0.6750, holding steady as global commodity prices offer support amid rising energy costs. This dynamic cushions the currency against broader USD strength driven by market expectations of further Federal Reserve tightening.

In contrast to weaker European currencies, the Australian Dollar, along with the Canadian Dollar (CAD), remains relatively firm. CAD/USD trades around 1.36, aided by higher oil prices, while the Euro (EUR) struggles below 1.06 against the greenback. The divergence in performance highlights the contrasting economic environments, with commodity-linked currencies benefiting from global supply chain pressures and robust resource demand.
