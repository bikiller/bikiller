Gold trading sentiment remained closely watched as Scotiabank’s Global FX Strategy team highlighted renewed strength across non-energy commodities, particularly copper, while cautioning that attention continues to center on a technically significant $4500/oz level in Gold. The move comes as broader commodity activity intersects with USD-driven market dynamics, with traders also monitoring shifts in currency pair positioning and exchange rate trends that may influence capital flows across global markets.

The renewed bid in industrial commodities has added another layer to cross-asset trading activity, reinforcing expectations that demand conditions outside the energy sector may be improving. Even so, Gold continues to occupy a different role within market strategy, with participants balancing its traditional safe-haven appeal against broader movements in the USD and EUR. Currency pair volatility, particularly in major USD-linked markets, remains an important factor in determining investor behavior.

Scotiabank noted that the $4500/oz threshold remains a key technical level under scrutiny, with traders viewing it as an important anchor point for near-term direction. A sustained move around this area could affect trading sentiment not only in precious metals but also in related exchange rate activity, potentially shaping broader market expectations as investors assess risk appetite and USD positioning.
