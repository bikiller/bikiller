The USD/KRW exchange rate has shown signs of stabilization after a recent decline. According to OCBC’s Christopher Wong, the downward momentum for the currency pair has slowed, with bearish pressure easing as the Relative Strength Index (RSI) recovers from oversold conditions. This suggests that the market could be primed for a short-term rebound before any further downside momentum.

Despite the brief recovery potential, Wong notes that a "proxy sell" trade could still emerge, keeping downward risks in play for USD/KRW. Traders will be closely watching for any shifts in market sentiment that could drive the exchange rate higher or trigger renewed selling pressure. The price action in the coming sessions will likely set the tone for short-term positioning.
