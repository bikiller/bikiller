The EUR/USD currency pair opened the week with a bullish gap, trading near 1.1650 as traders look to extend gains above the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level. Positive sentiment around a potential US-Iran peace deal has pressured the US Dollar, prompting investors to favor the Euro amid a softer risk-off environment. This shift is driving early-week momentum in the foreign exchange market.

Technical charts suggest that if EUR/USD maintains support above 1.1650, further upside toward the next resistance levels could unfold. Traders are closely watching price action for confirmation of bullish continuation, as breaking above the 23.6% Fibonacci could signal renewed appetite for long positions in the Euro. Exchange rate volatility remains moderate, influenced by both geopolitical developments and USD performance.

Market participants are weighing the implications for broader forex trading, as the weakening safe-haven USD opens opportunities for currency pairs sensitive to risk sentiment. The Euro’s strength is a key driver for cross pairs, and momentum above current technical levels may attract short-term traders looking to capitalize on bullish trends. Overall, the focus remains on geopolitical catalysts and technical thresholds shaping EUR/USD movements.
