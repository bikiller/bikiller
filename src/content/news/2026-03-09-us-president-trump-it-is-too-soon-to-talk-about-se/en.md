US President Donald Trump said it was “too soon” to discuss the possibility of the United States seizing Iranian oil, speaking in an interview with NBC News on Monday. The remarks signaled caution on a highly sensitive geopolitical issue, leaving currency markets watching closely for further policy clarity. In forex trading, geopolitical uncertainty often influences the USD exchange rate, particularly against major pairs such as EUR/USD and USD/JPY.

The measured tone from Washington limited immediate volatility in major currency pairs, though traders continued to monitor developments tied to Middle East energy flows and potential sanctions. Oil-related geopolitical risk can affect inflation expectations and risk sentiment, factors that frequently drive movements in the US Dollar and broader foreign exchange markets. As a result, the USD remained a focal point for short-term positioning in global FX trading.

Market participants noted that any escalation involving Iranian oil exports could quickly ripple through commodities and currency markets. Higher energy prices tend to strengthen inflation-linked expectations, which can influence Federal Reserve policy outlook and, in turn, the USD exchange rate. Until clearer policy signals emerge, forex traders are likely to remain cautious, keeping close watch on headlines that could shift sentiment across major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD.
