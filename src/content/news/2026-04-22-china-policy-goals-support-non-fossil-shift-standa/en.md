China’s economic resilience is helping cushion currency markets amid global energy volatility. Standard Chartered economists Hunter Chan and Shuang Ding note that the recent Middle East oil shock had limited impact on China, thanks to its diversified energy mix and sustained investment in non-fossil fuels. The USD/CNY exchange rate remained relatively stable, with traders citing the nation’s policy-driven shift away from fossil dependence as a stabilizing factor for the yuan.  

Market participants observing EUR/CNY trading noted that the euro has seen modest gains against the yuan, reflecting both global risk sentiment and China’s steady energy strategy. Analysts suggest that ongoing non-fossil energy initiatives could reduce China’s exposure to external oil price swings, indirectly supporting its currency and easing volatility in key currency pairs.  

For forex traders, China’s policy direction signals potential stability in RMB trading against major currencies, including USD and EUR. While global oil prices surged briefly, technical support around 6.90 USD/CNY held firm, reflecting confidence in China’s energy diversification. Moving forward, the combination of strategic energy planning and stable policy execution may underpin calmer trading conditions, giving currency markets room to adjust gradually rather than react sharply to external shocks.
