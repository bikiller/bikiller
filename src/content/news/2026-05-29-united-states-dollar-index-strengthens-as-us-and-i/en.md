The US Dollar Index (DXY), which tracks the USD against a basket of six major currencies, edged higher toward the 99.00 mark during Friday’s Asian session as investors responded to reports that the United States and Iran had agreed to a 60-day truce. While the development supported risk sentiment and boosted demand for the greenback, market participants remained cautious as former President Donald Trump had not yet approved the agreement.

The USD’s advance reflected expectations that a temporary easing of geopolitical tensions could reduce immediate market uncertainty and stabilize global financial conditions. Currency traders closely monitored the latest headlines, with the DXY finding support near current levels as investors reassessed safe-haven flows and broader risk appetite. The move also influenced major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, as exchange rate dynamics shifted in favor of the US Dollar.

Looking ahead, the sustainability of the DXY’s gains will likely depend on further confirmation of the truce and any official response from Trump. Market participants are expected to remain sensitive to geopolitical developments, which could drive volatility across forex trading. A confirmed agreement may reinforce USD strength, while renewed uncertainty could trigger fresh fluctuations in the Dollar and other major currencies.
