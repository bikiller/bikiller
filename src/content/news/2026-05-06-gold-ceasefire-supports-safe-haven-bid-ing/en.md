Gold edged higher as markets responded to the ongoing US-Iran ceasefire, easing concerns over a wider geopolitical conflict. ING analysts Warren Patterson and Ewa Manthey note that while safe-haven demand remains, it has moderated slightly, supporting trading in gold without triggering sharp rallies. The metal’s move comes amid a mixed forex environment, with the USD seeing steady gains against the EUR and JPY in early European trading.  

Spot gold rose toward $2,025 an ounce, reflecting cautious optimism. Currency traders monitored the USD’s exchange rate across major pairs, with EUR/USD holding around 1.0960 and USD/JPY near 134.15, as risk sentiment improved. Technical charts show gold maintaining support near $2,010, while resistance at $2,035 could limit further gains in the near term. Traders are balancing safe-haven flows with broader market risk appetite.  

The ceasefire’s stability could continue to weigh on volatility, influencing both metals and forex markets. A stronger USD may temper gold’s upside, while shifts in currency pairs like GBP/USD and AUD/USD will be closely watched. Investors remain alert for geopolitical developments that could swiftly revive demand for haven assets, impacting trading strategies across commodities and major currency pairs alike.
