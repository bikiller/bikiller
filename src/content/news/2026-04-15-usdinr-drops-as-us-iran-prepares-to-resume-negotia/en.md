The Indian Rupee (INR) strengthened against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, with the USD/INR exchange rate slipping following a public holiday on Tuesday for Dr. Baba Saheb Ambedkar Jayanti. Market participants saw a modest rebound in the rupee, which traded near 82.70 against the dollar. This positive movement comes as global sentiment shifted due to growing expectations of US-Iran nuclear talks resuming.

The potential for US-Iran negotiations to restart provided some relief to the markets, boosting emerging market currencies, including the INR. The development could ease tensions surrounding global oil supply, influencing the broader forex market. However, traders remain cautious, with the INR facing resistance at the 82.50 level, a key technical point, as investors continue to monitor the global geopolitical landscape for further updates.
