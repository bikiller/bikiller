West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil surged roughly 5% on Monday, though the headline gain masked a highly volatile trading session driven by escalating geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Energy markets reacted swiftly to the renewed uncertainty, pushing oil prices sharply higher during intraday trading before partial pullbacks emerged. The move spilled over into broader financial markets, influencing USD sentiment and shifting risk appetite across major currency pair trading.

The spike in crude prices reinforced demand for the US Dollar as traders reassessed inflation risks and potential monetary policy implications. Rising energy costs often translate into higher inflation expectations, which can support the USD exchange rate against counterparts like the EUR. In forex markets, this dynamic pressured EUR/USD positioning as traders monitored commodity-linked volatility alongside ongoing geopolitical developments.

Currency markets remain sensitive to energy-driven macro signals, particularly when oil’s sharp moves coincide with geopolitical instability. If Middle East tensions continue to escalate, sustained upside pressure in crude could reshape short-term trading flows across major currency pairs. Forex participants are watching whether the latest oil rally strengthens USD demand further or triggers broader shifts in exchange rate momentum tied to global risk sentiment.
