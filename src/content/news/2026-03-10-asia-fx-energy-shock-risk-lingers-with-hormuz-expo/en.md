Asian currencies remain under pressure as rising tensions in the Middle East continue to impact regional markets. The heavy dependence of Asia on energy supplies from the region, particularly through the Strait of Hormuz, has heightened risks for currencies like the JPY, CNY, and INR. Iran-related headlines have driven volatility, with trading in the USD/JPY and EUR/USD currency pairs reflecting the market's concerns.

MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst, Michael Wan, points to the heightened risk of an energy shock, particularly in the context of supply disruptions. The ongoing exposure of Asian economies to potential conflicts in the Strait of Hormuz has added layers of uncertainty. As a result, the Japanese yen, Chinese yuan, and Indian rupee have faced swings, with investors closely monitoring any further escalation in the region's geopolitical tensions.
