The EUR/USD currency pair managed to recover some of its earlier losses during Monday's late Asian trading session. Despite the rebound, the pair remains 0.25% lower, trading near the 1.1780 level. The initial decline was fueled by surging energy prices, driven by ongoing tensions surrounding the US-Iran conflict, which supported the USD.

The rise in energy prices has added pressure on the euro, as higher oil costs could slow down the economic recovery in the Eurozone. Meanwhile, the US dollar found strength amidst geopolitical uncertainty, with investors seeking refuge in the greenback. As the trading session progresses, all eyes will be on the developments in energy markets and geopolitical tensions, which could further impact the EUR/USD exchange rate.
