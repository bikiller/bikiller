The US Dollar Index (DXY) is holding steady near the 100.00 mark in early European trading on Friday, reflecting cautious sentiment among currency traders. Investors are weighing the impact of upcoming US jobs data alongside escalating tensions in the Middle East, particularly the ongoing Iran conflict. The greenback’s resilience has kept key currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, under pressure, with the euro trading below 1.08 and the pound hovering near 1.23.  

Market participants are closely monitoring the DXY’s ability to maintain support at the 100.00 level, which could signal continued USD strength. Traders note that risk aversion driven by geopolitical uncertainty often boosts the dollar as a safe-haven currency. This dynamic has pushed USD/JPY higher, testing the 145.50 resistance, while commodity-linked currencies like AUD and NZD are seeing modest declines in exchange rates amid flight-to-quality flows.  

The upcoming US employment report is expected to provide further direction for the dollar, with stronger-than-expected figures likely reinforcing the greenback’s bullish trend. Conversely, softer data could trigger short-term corrections across major currency pairs. Investors remain cautious, balancing macroeconomic indicators against the backdrop of Middle East instability, which continues to influence trading strategies and market positioning in global forex markets.
