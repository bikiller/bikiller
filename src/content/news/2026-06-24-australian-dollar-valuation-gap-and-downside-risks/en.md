Societe Generale analysts report that the Australian dollar remains under persistent pressure, with the AUD/USD currency pair gradually converging toward the 0.69 handle. Trading dynamics show the exchange rate also eyeing the March–April trough near 0.6833, highlighting a continued downside bias despite shifting interest rate expectations. The broader forex market reflects lingering USD resilience and limited support for the commodity-linked Australian dollar.

Even as the 2-year yield spread has tightened significantly to 39 basis points, the improvement has not translated into sustained AUD strength. Analysts note that valuation gaps remain a key driver, with investors reluctant to re-enter long positions amid uneven global risk sentiment. In FX trading, this divergence between rate differentials and spot performance underscores weakening conviction in near-term recovery for the AUD/USD pair.

Looking ahead, the exchange rate faces a fragile technical outlook, with 0.69 acting as an immediate magnet and 0.6833 forming a critical support zone. Unless USD softness emerges or risk appetite improves, downside pressure may persist across the forex market. Traders continue to monitor macro signals and cross-asset flows for direction, as the Australian dollar remains vulnerable to broader USD-led movements.
