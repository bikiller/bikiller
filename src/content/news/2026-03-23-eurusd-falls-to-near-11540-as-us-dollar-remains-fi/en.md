The EUR/USD currency pair edged lower during Monday’s late Asian session, sliding about 0.2% to hover near 1.1545. The euro is facing downward pressure as the US Dollar maintains strength, supported by heightened demand for safe-haven assets amid rising tensions in the Middle East. Traders are closely watching how geopolitical developments are influencing short-term exchange rate movements.  

Market participants note that the firming USD is attracting flows from risk-sensitive assets, weighing on the EUR/USD trading range. Technical levels around 1.1540 are drawing attention, with potential for further downside if geopolitical risks persist. Analysts suggest that volatility may remain elevated as investors adjust positions in response to global uncertainties.  

The broader implications for forex trading highlight a defensive stance among currency markets, with the USD benefiting from its safe-haven status. Euro weakness against the dollar may influence cross-currency pairs and impact hedging strategies for institutions exposed to European assets. Traders are likely to monitor ongoing Middle East developments and US economic indicators for cues on the next directional move in the EUR/USD exchange rate.
