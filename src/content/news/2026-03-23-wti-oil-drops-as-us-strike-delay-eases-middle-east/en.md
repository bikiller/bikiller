West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude slid sharply on Monday, trading around **$92.20**, down **5.45%** on the day after sinking from about **$100** to a **daily low of $83.99**, its weakest in over a week as traders digested easing geopolitical risk. The move came after a delay in a planned US strike that had initially stoked worries about Middle East supply disruption, pushing markets to reassess risk premia in energy and forex trading, particularly against the **USD**. The pullback has rippled through **currency pairs** sensitive to commodity prices, with commodity‑linked currencies such as the **AUD** and **CAD** softening against the greenback amid the relief rally.  

In the market’s view, the diminished prospect of imminent conflict has alleviated some crude supply concerns, reducing volatility across commodities and associated **exchange rates**. WTI’s drop reverberated through broader trading as risk appetite returned, tempering earlier safe‑haven demand for the **USD** and impacting crosses like **EUR/USD** and **GBP/USD** as investors recalibrate positions. Technical traders noted that the slide through key levels around the $95‑$90 area could signal further short‑term pressure if geopolitical headlines remain muted, though renewed tensions might quickly reverse the move and support risk assets again.

> On March 23, 2026, oil prices dropped significantly after President Trump announced a five-day postponement of planned military strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure. This decision followed what Trump described as "productive talks" with Iran. Brent crude, the global oil benchmark, fell by 11% to just under $100 per barrel, while WTI crude, the U.S. benchmark, dropped by 12% to approximately $87 per barrel. Earlier in the day, oil prices had been rising amid heightened tensions and potential military action, particularly due to Trump’s warning that the U.S. would target Iranian infrastructure if Iran did not open the Strait of Hormuz. The delay in military action eased investor concerns, prompting the sharp price decline.
