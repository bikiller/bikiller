Gold (XAU/USD) opened the week lower, slipping towards $4,803, marking a 0.70% decline. The drop follows increased risk aversion, spurred by heightened tensions in the US-Iran conflict over the weekend. As geopolitical concerns weigh on market sentiment, gold’s appeal as a safe haven has been tempered by other factors, including rising US Treasury yields and oil price gains.

Meanwhile, the rally in oil prices further pressured gold, with the commodity's advance limited despite its traditional role as a hedge against uncertainty. Strong US Treasury yields continue to support the USD, reinforcing the bearish outlook for gold. Traders are closely monitoring the evolving geopolitical situation, which could impact the broader currency markets, including the USD's exchange rate movements against other major currencies like EUR and JPY.
