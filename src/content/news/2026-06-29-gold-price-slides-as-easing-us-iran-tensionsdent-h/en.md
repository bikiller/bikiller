Gold (XAU/USD) fell about 1.50% on Monday as easing US-Iran tensions reduced safe-haven demand in global markets. Risk appetite improved after a weekend halt in hostilities between the United States and Iran, which had previously raised concerns over stalled negotiations and potential disruption to oil flows through the Strait of Hormuz.

In forex trading, the XAU/USD currency pair reflected renewed selling pressure as investors shifted toward risk assets, supporting higher-yielding currencies. The improved sentiment also influenced broader exchange rate movements, with USD positioning fluctuating against peers such as EUR/USD. A firmer risk tone typically weighs on gold as traders unwind defensive positions.

Looking ahead, traders will monitor geopolitical developments and their impact on oil flows and broader forex markets. The outlook for gold remains sensitive to USD strength and shifts in risk sentiment across major currency pairs. In the near term, XAU/USD trading may remain volatile as markets reassess safe-haven demand and global growth expectations.
