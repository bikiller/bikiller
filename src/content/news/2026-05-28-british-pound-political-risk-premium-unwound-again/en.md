The British Pound has rebounded against the Euro as recent political uncertainties in the UK appear to have been fully priced out of the market. According to ING’s Francesco Pesole, the EUR/GBP political risk premium, which was estimated at around 1% in mid-May, has now reverted to zero, signaling that traders are increasingly confident in the pound’s stability amid prior political turbulence.

Currency traders have responded by adjusting their positions, pushing the exchange rate lower for EUR/GBP. This unwinding of the political risk premium reflects a broader recalibration in forex markets, with investors re-evaluating exposure to UK-related risks. The pound’s performance against other major currencies, including the USD, also hints at a normalization of market sentiment following weeks of uncertainty.

The shift carries implications for trading strategies and portfolio management, as the removal of the risk premium may alter hedging and speculative activity. For forex markets, a zero political risk premium could enhance liquidity in the GBP-related currency pairs and reduce volatility, encouraging investors to revisit positions in both the Euro and US Dollar against the pound. Analysts suggest that market participants will closely watch macroeconomic indicators and central bank guidance to gauge the pound’s next trajectory.
