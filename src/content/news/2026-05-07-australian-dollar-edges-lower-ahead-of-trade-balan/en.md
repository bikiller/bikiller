The Australian Dollar (AUD) slipped against the US Dollar (USD) during Asian trading hours on Thursday, with the AUD/USD currency pair dipping to around 0.7230 after two days of gains. This movement comes as traders await the release of the latest Trade Balance data, which could influence the AUD's trajectory in the near term.

Despite the current pullback, there are factors that may bolster the Australian Dollar. Market sentiment suggests a potential breakthrough in negotiations between the US and Iran, which could lead to enhanced stability in the global economy. As developments unfold, traders will closely monitor the AUD's performance against the USD and other major currencies like the Euro (EUR), assessing the implications of geopolitical tensions on exchange rates.
