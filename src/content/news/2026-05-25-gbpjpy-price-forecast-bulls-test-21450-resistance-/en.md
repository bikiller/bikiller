GBP/JPY extended its advance on Monday, with the currency pair climbing to 214.52 and posting a 0.30% daily gain as improving global risk appetite boosted demand for the British Pound against the Japanese Yen. Traders reacted positively to renewed optimism surrounding a possible US-Iran agreement, which supported broader market sentiment and reduced safe-haven flows into the JPY and USD.

The exchange rate pushed toward the key 214.50 resistance zone, with bullish momentum strengthening during European trading hours. Market participants noted that the Pound continued to outperform major peers, including the EUR and USD, as investors favored higher-yielding currencies amid stabilizing geopolitical conditions. The Yen, meanwhile, remained under pressure as easing risk aversion weakened defensive positioning across forex markets.

From a technical perspective, GBP/JPY’s move above recent consolidation levels signals improving upside potential, with traders closely monitoring whether the pair can sustain gains above 214.50 in the near term. A confirmed breakout could encourage additional buying interest and open the door toward higher resistance targets, while failure to hold current levels may trigger short-term profit-taking in the cross.
