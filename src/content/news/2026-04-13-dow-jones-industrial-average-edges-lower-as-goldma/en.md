The Dow Jones Industrial Average slipped around 50 points on Monday, hovering near 47,900 after bouncing back from an early steep decline. Investors reacted cautiously as Goldman Sachs shares weighed on the index, while the S&P 500 edged up 0.2% to trade above 6,800 and the Nasdaq Composite advanced 0.6%, crossing the 23,000 mark. Market participants monitored US dollar sentiment closely, noting steady USD strength against the EUR and JPY in early trading.  

Crude oil prices climbed back over $100 per barrel, supporting energy stocks and adding volatility to commodity-linked currency pairs such as USD/CAD. Traders noted that rising oil could influence inflation expectations and Fed policy considerations, potentially affecting USD exchange rates across the board. The combination of equities volatility and commodity gains drove cautious positioning in major forex markets.  

Analysts highlighted that the modest Dow retreat alongside gains in tech and broader indices may signal selective risk-taking. Currency traders focused on how equity fluctuations and commodity trends could shift USD/EUR and USD/JPY trading ranges. With the market digesting mixed signals from earnings and macroeconomic data, the outlook remains sensitive to shifts in equities, oil, and dollar momentum.
