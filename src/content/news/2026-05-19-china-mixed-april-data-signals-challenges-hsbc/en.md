China’s April economic data painted a mixed picture for forex markets, as weak consumer demand and slowing investment reinforced concerns over the country’s recovery momentum. HSBC noted that Retail Sales missed expectations while Fixed Asset Investment fell sharply, pressuring sentiment toward Asian currencies and keeping traders focused on the USD/CNY exchange rate. The softer domestic backdrop also limited broader risk appetite across EUR and commodity-linked currency pairs during trading.

At the same time, Industrial Production remained relatively firm, supported by resilient manufacturing activity and continued expansion in the services sector. HSBC highlighted that technology-related demand, particularly linked to artificial intelligence infrastructure, helped stabilize parts of the economy despite broader weakness. The stronger production figures prevented a deeper market reaction, with investors balancing signs of resilience against concerns over slowing household consumption and investment spending.

Inflation data added another layer for currency markets. China’s CPI stayed broadly stable in April, suggesting limited immediate inflationary pressure, while Producer Price Index growth accelerated on higher oil prices and stronger AI-driven industrial demand. The combination could influence expectations for further policy easing from Beijing and shape USD and EUR trading flows against regional currencies. Traders are now watching whether additional stimulus measures can stabilize growth and support the yuan in coming sessions.
