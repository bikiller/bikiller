USD/CAD continued its upward momentum on Wednesday, climbing above the 1.3700-1.3750 resistance zone as the US Dollar maintained broad strength across major currency pairs. Traders noted that the dollar’s resilience, fueled by solid economic data and a firm Treasury yield backdrop, kept the Canadian Dollar under pressure, supporting the exchange rate’s advance. This marked the second consecutive session of gains for the USD/CAD currency pair.  

Market participants highlighted that the pair’s break through key technical levels could attract additional buying interest, with short-term traders eyeing a potential extension toward 1.3800. The broader USD strength against the Euro and other majors added to the momentum, reinforcing the narrative of a bullish bias. Momentum indicators suggest the market remains tilted in favor of buyers, though occasional profit-taking may temper intraday moves.  

Analysts expect that upcoming Canadian economic data and any shifts in US monetary policy could further influence USD/CAD trading. For now, the pair’s upward trajectory reflects a combination of technical breakout and macro-driven USD appreciation, underscoring the sensitivity of the Canadian Dollar to global market sentiment and interest rate differentials. Traders remain focused on both price action and broader risk factors that could drive near-term volatility.
