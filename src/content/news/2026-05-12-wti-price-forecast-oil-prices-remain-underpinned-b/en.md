WTI crude oil surged over 3% on Tuesday as lingering tensions around the Strait of Hormuz continue to pressure markets. Traders are increasingly concerned that any escalation between the US and Iran could further disrupt oil flows, keeping supply tight. The rally coincides with a firm USD, which has influenced currency pairs like EUR/USD, as risk sentiment drives a cautious trading mood across forex markets.

The tight supply outlook has also boosted market appetite for commodities, with WTI testing key technical levels that suggest further upside potential. Analysts note that a combination of geopolitical uncertainty and limited near-term production increases could sustain elevated prices. Currency traders are watching USD/JPY and GBP/USD closely, as movements in oil prices often ripple through global exchange rates, impacting commodity-linked currencies.

For forex participants, the interplay between crude prices and exchange rates underscores the broader macro picture. Stronger oil can support currencies tied to energy exports, while safe-haven demand keeps the USD robust. Traders are advised to monitor upcoming economic data alongside geopolitical developments, as any sudden news out of the Gulf could trigger volatility in both oil and currency markets, reinforcing the connection between commodities and forex flows.
