The Australian dollar remained resilient near the 0.7000 mark, with the AUD/USD currency pair trading around 0.6990 during Monday’s early Asian session. Traders are keeping a close watch on geopolitical developments in the Middle East, particularly escalating tensions between the United States, Israel, and Iran, which continue to influence risk sentiment and demand for safe-haven assets like the USD and JPY.  

Market participants noted that the exchange rate has found tentative support above 0.6950, suggesting a cautious optimism among traders despite heightened uncertainty. The Aussie’s ability to hold ground reflects underlying strength in domestic fundamentals, while volatility is likely to remain elevated as news flow from the conflict drives intraday trading dynamics.  

Analysts expect that if tensions intensify, the USD could gain further, potentially weighing on the AUD/USD pair and other commodity-linked currencies. Conversely, any de-escalation may support a rebound toward 0.7050. Traders are also monitoring broader forex flows, including EUR and GBP pairs, as risk sentiment and central bank outlooks interplay with geopolitical developments, shaping short-term trading strategies.
