The Australian Dollar (AUD) opened the week on a softer note, slipping 0.27% to trade near 0.6850 against the US Dollar (USD) in early session activity. Market participants reacted cautiously to heightened geopolitical tensions after Iran issued strong warnings against potential US ground military action, prompting investors to seek safe-haven assets. The AUD/USD currency pair faced immediate selling pressure as risk appetite in the forex market waned.  

Elsewhere, the broader forex landscape showed mixed movements. The AUD also edged lower against the Euro (EUR) and the British Pound (GBP), reflecting a cautious tone across commodity-linked currencies. Traders cited growing uncertainty around Middle East developments and the potential impact on global trade and energy markets as key drivers behind the softening exchange rate. Technical levels around 0.6860 had initially offered minor support, but momentum favored the downside.  

Analysts suggest that if geopolitical tensions escalate, the AUD could continue to face downward pressure against major peers. Investors remain watchful of US Dollar strength, which could accelerate trading flows into the USD as a safe haven. Market watchers are also monitoring support near 0.6840, with any breach potentially triggering further selling in the AUD/USD currency pair, while broader risk sentiment will shape short-term movements across global forex markets.
