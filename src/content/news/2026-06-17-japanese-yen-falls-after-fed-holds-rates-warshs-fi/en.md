Japanese yen weakened across major currency pairs as the USD/JPY exchange rate climbed toward the 160.40 level in trading, reflecting renewed dollar strength after the Federal Reserve kept policy unchanged. The move signaled continued pressure on the yen, with traders reacting quickly to shifting rate expectations and broader risk sentiment across FX markets.

The Federal Reserve left interest rates unchanged in the 3.50%-3.75% range during Kevin Warsh’s first policy meeting as Fed Chair, meeting expectations across markets. The updated dot plot leaned cautious, reinforcing a data-dependent stance that kept volatility contained while traders reassessed USD direction and relative yield differentials versus the yen.

Looking ahead, USD/JPY trading near 160.40 highlights persistent yen weakness as interest rate divergence supports the US dollar. Market participants remain attentive to further signals from the Fed, with the cautious dot plot suggesting limited urgency for policy easing. This keeps upside risks for the dollar supported while Japanese authorities watch exchange rate volatility closely.
