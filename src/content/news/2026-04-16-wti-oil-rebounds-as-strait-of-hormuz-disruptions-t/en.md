WTI crude oil saw a strong rebound on Thursday, rising 2.50% to $90.45, following three days of consecutive losses. The surge was driven by disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, which have raised concerns over global oil supply. These disruptions come at a time when market optimism surrounding US-Iran peace talks had started to build, adding volatility to oil prices.

The move in WTI has impacted broader market sentiment, especially against the backdrop of a fluctuating USD. As oil prices climb, it puts pressure on currencies tied to energy exports, such as the Canadian Dollar (CAD), while strengthening the USD due to increased demand for the greenback. Traders will be closely monitoring geopolitical tensions in the region, as any escalation could further fuel volatility in both crude oil and forex markets.
