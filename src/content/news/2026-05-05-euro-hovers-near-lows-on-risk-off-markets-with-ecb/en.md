The Euro (EUR) edged lower for a third consecutive session against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, reflecting ongoing risk-off sentiment in global markets. The EUR/USD currency pair hovered near multi-week lows, pressured by safe-haven demand for the USD as investors digested lingering concerns over slowing economic growth and geopolitical tensions. Traders noted that the exchange rate struggled to hold key technical support levels, signaling caution in Euro trading.  

Market participants are also eyeing comments from European Central Bank President Christine Lagarde later in the day, which could influence short-term EUR/USD dynamics. Analysts suggest that any indication of further monetary tightening or dovish signals may sway the currency pair, with the Euro vulnerable to continued depreciation if investor sentiment remains cautious. The USD’s strength, underpinned by higher Treasury yields, continues to exert upward pressure on the exchange rate, keeping EUR traders on alert.  

Technically, the EUR/USD remains under pressure near its recent lows, with immediate support observed around key Fibonacci levels. Resistance is likely to emerge only if risk sentiment improves or Lagarde signals a more hawkish policy stance. As trading unfolds, the interplay between global risk appetite, central bank commentary, and USD momentum will remain the dominant driver of the Euro’s trajectory against the greenback.
