USD/JPY tumbled more than 300 pips on Friday, hitting its lowest point in several weeks following a suspected "rate check" by Japan's Ministry of Finance. The pair fell sharply to 156.18, down almost 1.40% on the day, as traders speculated that Japan's authorities may be preparing to intervene in the forex market amid concerns over excessive Yen weakness.

This dramatic drop has stoked fears of government action to curb the Yen's ongoing decline. The pair's decline to this multi-week low comes amid growing volatility, with the exchange rate dipping below key technical levels not seen since late December. As the USD continues to benefit from strong momentum, market participants are closely monitoring any signs of intervention in the Yen, which remains under pressure.
