West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil eased slightly on Tuesday, surrendering part of its earlier gains as traders struggled to maintain momentum at elevated levels. Prices remained sensitive to ongoing geopolitical tensions, particularly the US-Iran conflict, and supply concerns in the Strait of Hormuz. Market participants noted that heightened volatility in oil has ripple effects on currency markets, influencing the USD and commodity-linked pairs such as USD/CAD and AUD/USD.  

The trading environment saw exchange rates respond to oil fluctuations, with the USD strengthening against several major currencies amid safe-haven demand. Analysts highlighted that WTI’s inability to break decisively above recent resistance levels could weigh on risk appetite, prompting cautious positioning in EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Technical indicators showed support near $83.50 per barrel, while resistance levels around $87 kept investors alert to potential swings.  

Investors continue to monitor supply disruptions and geopolitical developments closely, as any escalation could trigger sharp moves in both commodities and forex markets. Traders are balancing oil-related gains with broader macroeconomic trends, affecting currency pair trading strategies and USD liquidity. With uncertainty high, volatility in WTI is expected to remain a key driver for short-term trading decisions across global FX markets.
