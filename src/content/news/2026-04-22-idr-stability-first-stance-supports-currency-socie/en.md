Bank Indonesia (BI) has maintained its policy rate at 4.75%, sticking to its inflation target of 2.5% ±1%, signaling a continued focus on stability. Analysts Kunal Kundu and Galvin Chia of Societe Generale highlighted this decision as part of BI's stability-first approach, which aims to support the Indonesian rupiah (IDR) in the face of global market pressures.

This cautious stance on monetary policy comes as Indonesia seeks to navigate fluctuating global economic conditions. While the USD/IDR exchange rate remains influenced by external factors, BI’s emphasis on stability aims to avoid excessive volatility. The central bank’s consistent messaging could help keep the IDR’s movements more predictable, despite risks tied to global inflationary trends and shifting commodity prices.
