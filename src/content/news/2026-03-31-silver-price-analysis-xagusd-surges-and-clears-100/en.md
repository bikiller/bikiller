Silver prices surged more than 7% in March, reaching $75.00 as oil prices slumped, exerting downward pressure on the U.S. dollar (USD). This shift was driven by the close inverse correlation between oil and the Greenback. As the USD weakened, silver (XAG/USD) gained traction, benefiting from falling U.S. Treasury yields which further supported the white metal’s rally.

The move above the 100-day simple moving average (SMA) marked a key technical milestone, reinforcing silver's bullish momentum. As investors continue to react to the shifting macroeconomic environment, silver’s advance signals growing demand for safe-haven assets. With the USD facing headwinds from lower Treasury yields, XAG/USD could maintain its upward trajectory, with market participants closely monitoring the next moves in U.S. economic data and interest rates.
