The EUR/USD exchange rate saw a significant drop of 4% in early March, reaching 1.1415. This decline was driven by heightened geopolitical tensions, particularly surrounding Iran, which boosted demand for the safe-haven USD. As investors flocked to the dollar, concerns over global stability contributed to the downward pressure on the Euro.

Market expectations are currently pricing in two interest rate hikes by the European Central Bank (ECB), set for June and September. If the ECB does not counter this anticipation, EUR/USD could find technical support around the 1.1390 level. Traders are closely monitoring ECB signals, as their stance could determine whether the Euro gains traction or continues to face challenges against the stronger USD.
