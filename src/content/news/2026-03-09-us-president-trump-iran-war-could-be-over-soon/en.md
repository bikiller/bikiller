US President Donald Trump told NBC News on Monday that the conflict with Iran could be resolved sooner than expected, signaling cautious optimism. He refrained from commenting on the potential seizure of Iranian oil, noting it is premature to speculate. The remarks sparked immediate reactions in the forex market, with the USD strengthening against risk-sensitive currencies as traders weighed geopolitical risk against safe-haven flows.  

The USD/JPY pair traded higher, testing resistance near 145.20, while the EUR/USD edged lower toward 1.0900 as investors recalibrated positions. Oil-linked currencies such as the CAD and NOK saw mixed movements, reflecting uncertainty over future crude supply. Traders noted that any escalation or resolution in the Middle East would directly influence commodity-linked exchange rates and overall market volatility.  

Analysts caution that while Trump's comments may ease short-term market jitters, underlying tensions remain. Spot trading activity picked up across EUR/JPY and GBP/USD, with technical levels around 152.50 and 1.2400 attracting interest. Market participants remain focused on geopolitical developments, USD liquidity, and central bank signals, highlighting the interplay between political headlines and currency dynamics in shaping trading strategies.
