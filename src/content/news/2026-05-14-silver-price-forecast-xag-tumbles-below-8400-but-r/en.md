Silver (XAG/USD) slipped below the $84.00 mark on Thursday, retreating toward a key support level as the US Dollar (USD) staged a modest recovery. The currency pair fell to $83.53, marking a decline of over 4.40%, as traders opted to take profits after failing to push silver past the $90.00 resistance. Market participants reacted cautiously following robust US Retail Sales data, which bolstered the USD against major currencies like the EUR.  

Trading activity reflected a shift in sentiment, with the stronger USD putting pressure on precious metals. Investors who had driven XAG/USD higher in recent sessions began unwinding positions, contributing to the pullback. Technical analysts note that while the decline tests the $84.00 support, the broader trend remains bullish, suggesting potential for renewed upward momentum if the currency pair finds stability.  

Looking ahead, traders will watch for further cues from US economic indicators and global currency movements. A sustained USD strength could prolong pressure on silver, but any weakening in the USD or geopolitical tensions may revive buying interest. The exchange rate dynamics between XAG/USD, USD, and EUR remain key for gauging short-term trading strategies and positioning in the forex markets.
