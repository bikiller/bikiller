Gold prices have recently dropped despite ongoing geopolitical tensions, as a surge in oil prices has shifted market focus toward inflationary pressures and real interest rates. The rise in oil prices has prompted traders to reassess the Federal Reserve’s monetary policy stance, dampening hopes for any immediate easing. As a result, gold is struggling to gain traction, with the precious metal trading lower in tandem with the fluctuating USD.

The strong upward movement in oil is raising concerns over sustained inflation, which could influence the Fed to maintain its hawkish approach. This shift has impacted currency markets, pushing the EUR/USD exchange rate lower as the USD strengthens. As the gold market faces downward pressure, traders are keeping an eye on key technical levels, with gold struggling to hold above the $1,950 mark. The renewed focus on inflation could extend the USD's dominance, complicating gold’s path to recovery.
