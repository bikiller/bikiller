AUD/USD edged higher on Friday, trading near 0.7020, marking a 0.11% gain as traders reacted to a notably weaker-than-expected US employment report. The slowdown in US job growth sparked a reassessment of the Federal Reserve’s near-term monetary policy, fueling demand for the Australian dollar against the greenback. Market participants saw the softer data as a signal that USD momentum could ease in the coming weeks.  

The currency pair’s move also reflects lingering optimism around the Reserve Bank of Australia’s stance. Expectations of a potential rate hike have lent support to AUD, encouraging traders to increase long positions. Analysts note that the exchange rate may test nearby technical resistance if USD sentiment continues to falter, while broader forex markets weigh shifts in global risk appetite and cross-currency flows, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD dynamics.  

Trading in AUD/USD is being closely monitored for further signs of trend consolidation, as investors balance US economic data with Australian monetary signals. The pair’s uptick underscores the interplay between macroeconomic indicators and central bank outlooks, highlighting how subtle shifts in employment figures or policy expectations can influence short-term exchange rate movements. Volatility may persist as traders react to incoming US and Australian economic releases.
