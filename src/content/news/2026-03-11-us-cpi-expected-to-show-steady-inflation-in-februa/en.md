The US Consumer Price Index (CPI) report for February, set for release on Wednesday, is expected to reveal inflation holding steady, remaining above the Federal Reserve's 2% target. Analysts predict a slight easing in price pressures, though concerns over rising oil prices may cloud the Fed's outlook. Oil's surge has the potential to maintain upward pressure on inflation, influencing the Fed's future policy stance.

The market is closely watching how these inflation trends affect the USD. With inflation still elevated, the US Dollar remains sensitive to shifts in CPI data. As the EUR/USD exchange rate continues to test key technical levels, traders are adjusting their positions in anticipation of the Fed's next move. Stronger-than-expected CPI data could further support the USD, while a softer print might prompt cautious moves from the central bank.
