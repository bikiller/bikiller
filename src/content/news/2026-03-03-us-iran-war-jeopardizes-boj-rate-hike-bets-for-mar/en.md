Heightened volatility in global markets, fueled by rising oil prices amid the ongoing US-Iran conflict, has cast uncertainty on the Bank of Japan’s (BoJ) expected interest rate hike in March. The geopolitical tensions have caused significant fluctuations in energy prices, affecting investor sentiment across major currency pairs. As markets brace for the BoJ’s monetary policy decision on March 19, traders are reevaluating their expectations for a rate increase, with the USD/JPY exchange rate under pressure.

Oil prices have surged sharply, pushing inflation concerns to the forefront, and dampening the chances of a rate hike by the BoJ. The uncertainty has influenced the yen, which weakened against the US dollar, trading near ¥135. The EUR/USD pair saw muted movement, as the European Central Bank (ECB) remains on its own tightening path. If the conflict escalates further, it could complicate trading strategies for forex investors, impacting both the Japanese yen and broader market dynamics.
