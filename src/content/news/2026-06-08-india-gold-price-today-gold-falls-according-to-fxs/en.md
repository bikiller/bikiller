Gold prices in India dipped on Monday, reflecting renewed USD strength and cautious trading sentiment. According to FXStreet data, investors reacted to the firming dollar index, which influenced the INR/USD currency pair, keeping domestic gold slightly under pressure. Technical levels indicate support around ₹61,500 per 10 grams, while resistance remains near ₹62,200, as traders adjust positions amid mixed global cues.

The decline comes amid broader market movements, where the USD strengthened against major currencies including the EUR and JPY. Traders cited rising U.S. Treasury yields and anticipation of upcoming economic data as key drivers behind the shift. Gold, often a hedge against currency volatility, saw reduced demand in local trading, reflecting investors’ preference for USD assets amid global uncertainty.

Analysts suggest that the near-term outlook for gold remains range-bound, influenced by the INR/USD exchange rate and global risk sentiment. Spot trading indicates cautious buying at lower levels, with market participants closely monitoring forex fluctuations and international commodity trends. Any further strengthening of the USD could pressure gold further, while a pullback in the dollar may provide an opportunity for renewed buying interest in the Indian market.
