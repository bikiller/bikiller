GBP/USD traded around 1.3465 during Friday Asian session, holding just above a nearly two-week low from the previous day. The currency pair remains in a bearish consolidation phase as traders reassess direction in foreign exchange markets, with pound sterling struggling to build momentum against the US dollar amid cautious risk appetite.

Market sentiment remains fragile as rising geopolitical tensions involving Iran weigh on risk appetite, limiting bullish attempts in the GBP/USD currency pair. Traders appear reluctant to extend positions in sterling against the USD, which continues to benefit from safe-haven demand. The exchange rate reflects cautious trading conditions, with volatility contained as participants await clearer macroeconomic cues.

In the near term, GBP/USD may continue to oscillate within a narrow range as bearish consolidation persists around the 1.3465 area, which now acts as a key reference level after the recent two-week low. Further downside risks remain if USD strength persists, while sterling bulls will need stronger catalysts to regain control of the forex trading trend.
