Silver prices dropped over 1% on Monday, as XAG/USD slid to $79.78 after gapping down over the weekend. The bearish sentiment follows a volatile Friday session, where optimism reversed after tensions escalated in the Middle East. The closure of the Strait of Hormuz and the seizure of an Iran-flagged vessel by the U.S. contributed to the risk-off mood, pressuring the precious metal.

The silver market is now encountering resistance at $80, with a doji candlestick pattern hinting at potential downside. A break below $79 could signal further declines, as traders react to shifting geopolitical risks and the USD's relative strength. As market uncertainty grows, silver traders are closely monitoring developments, especially those impacting global risk sentiment and the broader commodity complex.
