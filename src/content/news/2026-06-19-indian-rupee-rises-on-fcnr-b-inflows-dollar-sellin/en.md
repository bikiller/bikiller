The Indian rupee extended its upward momentum for a second straight session on Friday, strengthening against the US dollar as the USD/INR currency pair maintained a firm downward bias. The exchange rate drifted lower amid improving domestic inflows, signaling renewed confidence in the local currency in forex trading markets during Asian trading hours across spot markets.

Support for the rupee was largely driven by robust FCNR-B inflows and sustained dollar selling from exporters, which together shifted daily foreign exchange flows in favor of the currency. Market participants noted that this rebalancing of supply and demand in the USD/INR pair reduced pressure on the rupee and reinforced its intraday gains across Asian trading sessions.

From a trading perspective, the improved sentiment suggests near-term stability for the rupee, though volatility in global USD trends and broader risk appetite could still influence direction. Traders are closely watching further foreign inflows and exporter behavior, as continued support may keep the exchange rate under pressure and sustain the rupee’s recent recovery momentum.
