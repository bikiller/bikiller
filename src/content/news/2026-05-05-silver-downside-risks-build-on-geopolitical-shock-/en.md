Silver markets are showing renewed downside pressure as heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East push risk sentiment lower. OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong point to rising oil prices as a key driver, fueling demand for safe-haven assets like the USD while weighing on metals. Traders noted Silver recently tested support near $24.50 per ounce, signaling potential for further declines if risk aversion persists.  

The broader FX market reflects this risk-off mood, with currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD seeing increased volatility. The strengthening USD against major currencies is amplifying the downward pressure on Silver, as commodities priced in dollars become more expensive for non-US investors. Analysts suggest traders watch the $24.00 technical level, which could act as a key floor in the near term.  

Looking ahead, market participants remain cautious, weighing both geopolitical developments and energy price trends. Higher oil costs are expected to continue influencing trading flows, reinforcing the USD’s role as a safe haven. Momentum in Silver could remain weak until clarity emerges on regional tensions, with potential spillover into related currency pairs and broader commodity-linked exchange rates.
