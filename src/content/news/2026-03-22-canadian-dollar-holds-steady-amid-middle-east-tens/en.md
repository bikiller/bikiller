The USD/CAD currency pair remained largely unchanged at 1.3725 during early Asian trading on Monday, reflecting a cautious market stance amid ongoing Middle East tensions. Traders are closely watching the geopolitical situation, which has the potential to influence oil prices. Crude oil's steady rise is supporting the Canadian Dollar, traditionally sensitive to commodity price fluctuations.

Market participants are also balancing expectations of central bank policies. While the Federal Reserve signals a hawkish hold, maintaining a firm stance on interest rates, the Bank of Canada appears to favor a dovish approach. This divergence continues to influence the exchange rate, with the Canadian Dollar holding steady against the U.S. Dollar as investors assess the economic implications of each central bank’s stance.
