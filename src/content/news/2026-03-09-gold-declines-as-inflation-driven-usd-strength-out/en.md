Gold (XAU/USD) slipped to a four-day low in early Asian trading as the US dollar strengthened on persistent inflation concerns. The safe-haven metal faced heavy selling pressure, with traders reacting to stronger-than-expected US economic data that boosted the USD. Despite the pullback, gold found some support near the $5,000 psychological level, reflecting underlying demand amid lingering geopolitical tensions.  

The currency pair’s movement underscores the influence of USD strength on precious metals, as higher yields and expectations of tighter monetary policy weigh on gold. Traders monitoring the exchange rate between USD and other major currencies, including EUR and JPY, note that risk sentiment and central bank signals remain key drivers. Short-term technical indicators suggest that further declines could test lower support levels if the dollar continues its upward momentum.  

Market participants are weighing inflation dynamics against broader macroeconomic risks. While geopolitical uncertainties typically support gold as a hedge, the prevailing USD strength has dominated trading sentiment. Investors remain cautious, tracking currency flows, interest rate expectations, and safe-haven demand, which could shape XAU/USD movements in the coming sessions. A rebound may depend on shifts in the US dollar index or renewed market fears that could reignite buying interest in gold.
