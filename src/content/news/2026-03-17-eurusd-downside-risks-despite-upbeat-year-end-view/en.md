EUR/USD remains vulnerable to downside risks despite an optimistic year-end outlook, according to ING’s Francesco Pesole. Even if a prolonged Gulf conflict were to unfold, European gas prices are unlikely to return to 2022’s extreme highs. This stability in energy costs could help support the EUR against the USD in the medium term, but the currency pair still faces significant volatility in the short run.

Pesole highlights that while the Eurozone’s economic resilience is improving, EUR/USD remains susceptible to external factors, including U.S. monetary policy and global risk sentiment. The exchange rate continues to trade within a volatile range, with the 1.05 level acting as a key support zone. A break below this could trigger further downside, while a recovery above 1.10 would be needed to shift the medium-term outlook more positively for the euro.
