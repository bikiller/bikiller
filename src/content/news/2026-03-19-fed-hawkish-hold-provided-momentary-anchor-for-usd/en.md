The Federal Reserve's decision to keep the Fed Funds Rate at 3.50-3.75% has temporarily supported the USD, providing what DBS economist Philip Wee refers to as a "hawkish hold." Despite this, the US Dollar's strength has been short-lived, as the broader market remains highly volatile. This stance has created brief stability in the exchange rates, but traders are cautious as uncertainty lingers.

The EUR/USD currency pair has been particularly sensitive to these shifts, with the Dollar experiencing a short-term boost before market forces led to a return to volatility. While the Fed's policy has offered some anchor for the USD, the underlying uncertainty in global markets suggests the Dollar's gains may not hold. Forex traders are closely monitoring upcoming economic data to gauge the next potential direction for USD trading.
