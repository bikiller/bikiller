USD/JPY declined to 158.40 on Thursday as traders weighed recent central bank moves from both the U.S. and Japan. Despite the Federal Reserve's decision to keep interest rates unchanged, market sentiment was affected by Chair Jerome Powell's comments. Powell warned that rising energy prices could contribute to higher inflation, which may prompt further tightening in the future. 

The currency pair’s drop reflects a cautious market reaction to mixed signals. While the Fed maintains a hawkish stance, the Japanese yen remains pressured by the Bank of Japan's ongoing dovish policies. The current exchange rate for USD/JPY reflects the tug-of-war between global inflationary pressures and diverging monetary policies. Traders are keeping a close eye on the next moves by both central banks, particularly as inflation concerns continue to dominate the global economic landscape.
