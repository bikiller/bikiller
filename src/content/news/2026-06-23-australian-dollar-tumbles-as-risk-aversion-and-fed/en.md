The Australian Dollar extended losses on Tuesday, sliding more than 1.20% as risk aversion dominated global markets and drove capital flows into the US Dollar. Expectations that the Federal Reserve may keep interest rates higher for longer further strengthened the USD, pressuring risk-sensitive currencies. In forex trading, the AUD/USD currency pair weakened sharply as sentiment deteriorated across equities and commodities.  

During the session, AUD/USD dropped over 1.20%, retreating from an intraday high near 0.7005 before settling around 0.6915. The breakdown below the 0.7000 psychological level signaled weakening bullish momentum, with USD demand accelerating across major forex pairs. Traders noted that higher US yields continued to support the greenback, keeping pressure on the Australian Dollar throughout trading.  

Market participants expect the Australian Dollar to remain vulnerable in the near term if risk-off sentiment persists and US rate expectations stay elevated. Continued strength in the USD could limit recovery attempts in the AUD/USD exchange rate, especially if commodity-linked currencies remain under pressure. Overall, trading conditions favour USD outperformance across major currency pairs.
