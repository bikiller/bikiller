The EUR/USD currency pair traded in a narrow range around the 1.1550 level on Thursday, with the Euro showing limited movement against the USD despite growing expectations that the European Central Bank will deliver an interest rate increase at its upcoming policy meeting. Market participants appeared cautious ahead of the decision, keeping the exchange rate largely stable during early trading.

Investors continue to assess how a potential ECB rate hike could influence monetary policy expectations across the Eurozone. While higher interest rates typically support a currency by improving yield prospects, much of the anticipated move appears to have already been priced into the EUR. As a result, the currency pair remained muted, with traders reluctant to take aggressive positions before receiving clearer guidance from policymakers.

The subdued price action highlights a broader wait-and-see attitude in the foreign exchange market. Traders are closely monitoring ECB communications for signals on future policy tightening, which could shape the next direction for EUR/USD.
