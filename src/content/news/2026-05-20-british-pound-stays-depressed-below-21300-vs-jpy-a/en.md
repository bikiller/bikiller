The British Pound remained under pressure against the Japanese Yen on Wednesday, with the GBP/JPY currency pair staying below the 213.00 threshold for a second straight session. Despite the subdued tone, the exchange rate avoided deeper losses, holding above Tuesday’s swing low as traders refrained from aggressive selling ahead of key UK inflation data. Political uncertainty in the UK continued to weigh on Sterling sentiment.

Market participants appear reluctant to take strong directional positions before the release of UK CPI figures, which could shape expectations for Bank of England policy and near-term GBP trading. The lack of follow-through selling suggests some technical support remains intact, though broader risk sentiment and safe-haven demand for the Yen continue to cap upside. Cross-currency flows involving USD and EUR also remain relevant to positioning.

If UK inflation surprises to the upside, Sterling could find relief as traders reassess rate expectations, potentially lifting GBP/JPY off current depressed levels. Conversely, softer CPI data may reinforce bearish momentum, exposing the currency pair to renewed downside pressure. For now, the market remains in a holding pattern, with technical traders watching whether support above the prior session’s low can continue to contain losses.
