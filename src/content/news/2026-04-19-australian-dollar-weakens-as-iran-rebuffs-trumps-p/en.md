The Australian Dollar (AUD) is facing downward pressure as it weakens against the US Dollar (USD) amid escalating tensions between the US and Iran. During the early Asian session on Monday, the AUD/USD pair fell to around 0.7140, with traders reacting to Iran’s rejection of President Trump’s proposal for a new round of peace talks. This geopolitical uncertainty continues to impact market sentiment, pushing the AUD lower.

Traders are now turning their attention to upcoming economic data, particularly the US March Retail Sales report, which is set for release on Tuesday. A stronger-than-expected report could further bolster the USD, potentially sending the AUD/USD pair lower. Market participants are closely monitoring these developments, with key technical levels at 0.7100 and 0.7200 in focus for any further price action.
