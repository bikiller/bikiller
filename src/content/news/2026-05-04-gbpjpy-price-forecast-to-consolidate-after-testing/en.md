The GBP/JPY currency pair edged lower on Monday, declining roughly 0.23% as traders gravitated toward the safe-haven appeal of the Japanese Yen. The pair continues to absorb volatility following recent intervention by Japanese authorities in the FX markets, which has added an extra layer of caution among investors. Market participants noted that the exchange rate is testing its 100-day simple moving average (SMA), signaling a potential period of consolidation.  

Technical analysts point out that the pair’s movement around the 100-day SMA could set the tone for short-term trading, as GBP/JPY may pause after the recent push. Broader market sentiment remains influenced by USD and EUR trends, with risk appetite and cross-currency flows shaping intraday action. Traders are closely monitoring developments in Japanese monetary policy and global currency shifts, which could impact the balance between safe-haven and higher-yielding assets.  

Looking ahead, the GBP/JPY exchange rate may stabilize near the 100-day SMA, with market participants weighing the impact of geopolitical events, central bank interventions, and economic data. A sustained move above or below this technical level could trigger further directional trading, while consolidation would suggest a temporary pause in momentum. Investors remain alert to USD and EUR moves, which continue to ripple through FX markets, influencing positioning in major pairs like GBP/JPY.
