AUD/USD extended losses on Thursday during Asian trading hours, slipping toward the 0.7100 mark after briefly posting gains of more than 0.5% in the previous session. The currency pair last traded around 0.7120, reflecting renewed pressure on the Australian Dollar against the US Dollar. The exchange rate pullback signals a shift in short-term sentiment following recent volatility in forex trading.

Softer Australian labor market data weighed on the currency after the unemployment rate climbed in April, reinforcing expectations of a cooling domestic economy. The weaker print prompted traders to reassess interest rate outlooks, limiting demand for the Australian Dollar in forex markets. Meanwhile, USD strength against major currency pairs contributed to the downward pressure on AUD/USD exchange rate.

Market focus now turns to the key 0.7100 support level, with a sustained break potentially opening the door to further downside in AUD/USD trading. Conversely, stabilization above this threshold could trigger short-covering as forex traders adjust positions across major pairs, including EUR/USD. Overall sentiment remains cautious as investors weigh US Dollar resilience against Australian economic signals.
