The UK inflation picture remains subdued, complicating the Bank of England’s policy trajectory, according to TD Securities. The firm’s Global Strategy Team forecasts headline CPI at 3.0% year-on-year for February, matching both BoE expectations and market consensus. Core inflation is expected steady at 3.1%, with services costs moderating while core goods see slight upward pressure. This uneven trend could influence interest rate guidance and market sentiment around GBP trading.  

Currency markets are reflecting the cautious outlook, with the GBP/USD and GBP/EUR pairs showing limited directional momentum. Traders are weighing the implications of stalled inflation against recent BoE rhetoric, which suggests a careful, data-driven approach to further tightening. Any shift in CPI trajectory could trigger swings in exchange rates, as positioning in sterling versus the dollar and euro adjusts to updated expectations.  

Analysts note that while headline numbers are holding, the mix of easing services and firmer goods suggests a nuanced inflation story. For forex traders, this may translate into short-term consolidation in GBP pairs, with volatility likely around key technical levels. Market participants are monitoring whether the BoE will maintain its cautious stance or adjust policy, a decision that could ripple across USD, EUR, and other major currency pairs.
