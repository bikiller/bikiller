Societe Generale strategist Kit Juckes highlighted that the recent strength in oil prices could provide continued support for the USD, putting downward pressure on the EUR/USD currency pair. He notes that higher energy costs typically bolster the dollar by widening the trade imbalance, a dynamic that traders should monitor closely as they assess the euro’s resilience against a firming greenback.  

Juckes points to history for context: when Brent crude hovered near current levels in early 2022, the EUR/USD exchange rate tumbled sharply from roughly 1.13 to 0.95. This precedent suggests that sustained oil strength could trigger similar volatility in the currency markets, potentially leading to accelerated USD buying and increased selling pressure on the euro.  

For market participants, this scenario underscores the importance of watching both energy prices and central bank signals. Traders in the forex market may adjust positions anticipating further declines in EUR/USD, while technical analysts eye key levels established during past sell-offs. With oil acting as a catalyst, the outlook favors USD strength and heightened exchange rate sensitivity for EUR/USD.
