USD/SGD saw a strong rebound following a setback in the Strait of Hormuz, reversing its Friday decline to 1.2667. OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong highlighted the surge in the currency pair as a direct response to Iran's decision to reclose the strategic waterway. This geopolitical development prompted renewed demand for the USD, pushing the exchange rate higher.

The rebound in USD/SGD underscores the market's sensitivity to geopolitical risks, particularly in key global shipping routes like the Strait of Hormuz. Traders are closely monitoring these tensions, as the movement could influence broader market sentiment and future trading decisions. The recent price action could set the stage for further fluctuations, especially if geopolitical concerns persist in the coming sessions.
