The Australian Dollar’s rally against the USD is beginning to fade as investors reassess the outlook for the AUD/USD currency pair, according to MUFG analysts. After benefiting from improved global risk sentiment and broad US Dollar weakness, the exchange rate now faces mounting resistance as positioning in the Australian Dollar becomes increasingly stretched. Traders are also turning more cautious as momentum in China’s economy softens, reducing support for commodity-linked currencies.

MUFG noted that the Reserve Bank of Australia is no longer the primary driver of AUD trading, with rising US Treasury yields regaining influence over forex markets. Higher US yields have supported the USD across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and AUD/USD, limiting further upside for the Australian currency. The bank believes expectations for additional gains are becoming harder to justify without a renewed improvement in global growth sentiment.

The shift in market dynamics suggests AUD/USD could struggle to extend recent advances in the near term, particularly if risk appetite deteriorates further. Analysts said investors are increasingly focused on external drivers, including US economic resilience and China’s slowdown, rather than domestic Australian policy signals. That backdrop may keep the Australian Dollar vulnerable to renewed USD strength as forex trading conditions become more defensive.
