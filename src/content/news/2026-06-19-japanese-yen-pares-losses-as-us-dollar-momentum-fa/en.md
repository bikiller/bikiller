USD/JPY currency pair traded near 161.00 on Friday, easing slightly after touching a two-year high of 161.81 on Thursday, as the US Dollar’s five-day winning streak came to an end. The exchange rate pulled back as USD momentum faded despite a persistently hawkish Federal Reserve stance, with trading activity reflecting cautious profit-taking at elevated levels.

Japanese Yen remains fragile as concerns over potential intervention by Japanese authorities to strengthen the currency intensify. The JPY struggles to sustain gains against the US Dollar, with traders wary of official action amid elevated exchange rate levels around the 161 handle. Broader forex market sentiment highlights vulnerability in the yen despite periodic corrective moves.

Market outlook for USD/JPY remains sensitive to diverging monetary policy expectations between the Federal Reserve and the Bank of Japan. Traders monitor whether intervention risk can cap further upside beyond 161.00–161.81 resistance zone, while USD demand may remain supported by higher US yields even as momentum cools. Volatility is expected to persist as traders reassess positioning around key technical levels.
