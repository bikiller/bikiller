The Canadian Dollar (CAD) extended its decline early Monday, sliding from a two-week peak of 1.3844 against the US Dollar (USD). The USD/CAD currency pair gained traction as investors sought the Greenback as a safe-haven amid market jitters, offsetting a rally in WTI crude that typically supports the loonie. Trading volumes picked up near the 1.3860 mark, highlighting sensitivity to global risk sentiment.  

Market participants noted that the CAD’s pullback reflects a cautious stance toward riskier assets, despite the energy-driven boost from crude prices. The USD’s broad strength against major currencies, including the EUR and GBP, added upward pressure on USD/CAD, reinforcing technical resistance around 1.3875. Analysts suggest that sustained safe-haven demand could keep the currency pair elevated near recent highs, while any fresh crude rally may provide only limited relief for the loonie.  

Traders are watching key levels closely as the exchange rate navigates this tug-of-war between commodity support and USD resilience. A break above 1.3875 could signal further upside for USD/CAD, while a retreat toward 1.3820 might offer short-term opportunities for CAD buying. Overall, the market remains reactive to geopolitical uncertainty and crude price fluctuations, underlining the CAD’s sensitivity to global risk dynamics.
