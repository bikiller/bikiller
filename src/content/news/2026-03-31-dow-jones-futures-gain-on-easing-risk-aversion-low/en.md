Dow Jones futures climbed by 0.81%, surpassing 45,800 during European trading hours, as easing risk aversion boosted sentiment ahead of the US market open on Tuesday. The positive movement in global equities was also reflected in S&P 500 and Nasdaq 100 futures, which advanced by 0.78% and 0.54%, nearing 6,440 and 23,260, respectively. The improved outlook was driven in part by a drop in oil prices, which helped alleviate some concerns around inflation.

The market's recovery coincided with a strengthening of the USD, supported by the more stable risk environment. Traders shifted their focus to upcoming US economic data, which could further influence the EUR/USD exchange rate. With oil prices retreating, there was a reduction in inflationary fears, aiding the risk-on sentiment in both equity and forex markets. As the US session approaches, investors will be closely monitoring any developments that could affect the broader market momentum.
