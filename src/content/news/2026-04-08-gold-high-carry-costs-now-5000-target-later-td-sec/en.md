TD Securities strategists caution that gold's high carry costs remain a challenge in the near term, driven by persistent energy-linked inflation and the delay in anticipated Federal Reserve interest rate cuts. The ongoing elevated opportunity cost of holding gold is particularly felt against the backdrop of a stronger USD, with traders closely watching the USD/EUR exchange rate as a barometer of market sentiment.

Looking ahead, TD Securities maintains a bullish long-term outlook for gold, projecting a $5,000 target, driven by broader economic pressures. However, they highlight potential risks, including a lack of capital from the Middle East, which could weigh on gold's momentum. As the Fed's rate decisions continue to play a pivotal role in forex market movements, traders will need to monitor any changes to monetary policy that may impact gold's appeal in the coming months.
