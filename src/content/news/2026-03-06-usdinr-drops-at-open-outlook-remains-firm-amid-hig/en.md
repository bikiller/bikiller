The Indian Rupee opened stronger against the US Dollar on Friday, reversing part of the previous session’s losses. The USD/INR currency pair edged lower in early trade, reflecting a mix of domestic and external pressures. Traders noted that sustained foreign institutional investor (FII) selling continued to weigh on the dollar, while rising crude oil prices added pressure on India’s import-driven currency dynamics.  

At the open, USD/INR hovered near key technical levels, with the exchange rate testing support zones around 82.45–82.50. Market participants cited higher global oil benchmarks, which elevate import bills, as a primary driver keeping the rupee under watch. Meanwhile, broader risk sentiment saw other major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD exhibiting relative stability, suggesting selective dollar weakness rather than a broad-based move.  

Analysts expect the rupee to remain sensitive to FII flows and crude price volatility in the coming sessions. Short-term trading may see USD/INR consolidating between 82.40–82.80, while any fresh inflows from foreign investors could provide temporary relief. The currency pair’s trajectory will likely influence hedging strategies, import-export planning, and broader market positioning in the forex segment over the near term.
