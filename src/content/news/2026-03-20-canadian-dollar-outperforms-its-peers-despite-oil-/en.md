The Canadian Dollar (CAD) showed strength against its major peers on Friday, outperforming most currencies despite a cooling in oil prices. Trading flat against the US Dollar (USD), the CAD held steady at 1.3740 during the European session, reflecting resilience in the currency amid mixed global market sentiment. The CAD's performance has been largely driven by its close ties to oil, yet recent declines in crude prices have not significantly hindered its momentum.

In contrast, the broader forex market saw limited movement, with the EUR/USD exchange rate remaining relatively stable. The US Dollar continues to show mixed results against its counterparts, while CAD's strength highlights investor confidence in Canada’s economic outlook. As oil markets stabilize, the Canadian Dollar may face more pressure, but its current standing at key levels suggests continued resilience in the short term.
