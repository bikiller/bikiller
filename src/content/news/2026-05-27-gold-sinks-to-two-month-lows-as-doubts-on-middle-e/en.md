Gold prices tumbled to their lowest level in nearly two months on Wednesday, with XAU/USD falling more than 1% as renewed demand for the US Dollar pushed the precious metal sharply lower. The currency pair dropped to $4,443, marking its weakest trading level since March 30, while the USD exchange rate strengthened broadly against major peers including the EUR and JPY. Market sentiment shifted toward caution as investors reassessed geopolitical risks and safe-haven flows.

The Greenback regained momentum after earlier losses, supported by speculation that negotiations between the United States and Iran could face delays or even collapse. The prospect of stalled diplomatic progress revived demand for the USD in forex trading, limiting appetite for non-yielding assets such as gold. Traders also rotated back into the Dollar as broader risk appetite turned neutral across global markets, reducing demand for defensive positions tied to bullion and related commodity currency pairs.

Meanwhile, forex participants monitored technical levels closely as XAU/USD extended its downside break below recent support zones. Analysts noted that continued USD strength and higher Treasury yields could keep pressure on gold prices in the near term, particularly if upcoming US economic data reinforces expectations for tighter monetary policy. The latest move also highlighted how geopolitical uncertainty continues to influence exchange rate volatility and cross-market trading sentiment.
