The Pound Sterling edged lower against the US dollar on Monday, with the GBP/USD currency pair hovering around 1.3520 during Asian trading hours. The exchange rate pared some of its earlier losses but remained in negative territory as investor sentiment soured amid stalled US–Iran peace negotiations. Market participants cited heightened geopolitical uncertainty, which often pressures risk-sensitive currencies like the Pound.  

Traders observed that the GBP’s weakness was mirrored in broader European currency markets, with the EUR/USD pair showing relative resilience. Analysts note that the Pound’s underperformance reflects both geopolitical concerns and ongoing economic data scrutiny in the UK. Technical levels around 1.3500 are being closely watched, with a breach potentially signaling further downside for the GBP against the USD.  

The stalled talks between Washington and Tehran continue to influence global risk appetite, reinforcing demand for safe-haven assets such as the US dollar. Market strategists warn that sustained uncertainty could keep the GBP under pressure in coming sessions, prompting traders to monitor both diplomatic developments and upcoming UK economic indicators. For forex investors, the movement highlights the interplay between geopolitical events and currency pair volatility in the short term.
