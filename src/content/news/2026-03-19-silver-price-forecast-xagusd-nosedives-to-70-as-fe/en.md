Silver (XAG/USD) tumbled sharply on Thursday, dropping nearly 6.5% to hover around the $70 mark during European trading. The sudden decline reflected heightened selling pressure, as investors recalibrated positions amid expectations that the Federal Reserve is unlikely to cut interest rates this year. The exchange rate movement intensified as the USD strengthened, putting additional strain on the precious metal.  

Traders reacted to the growing consensus of an extended Fed pause, which undercut silver’s appeal as an alternative asset. The pullback in XAG/USD highlights how sensitive the currency pair is to shifts in U.S. monetary policy, with technical support levels near $70 now under pressure. Market participants also noted that the stronger USD discouraged speculative inflows, amplifying the downward momentum in trading sessions.  

The broader implications for forex and commodity markets are significant. Continued weakness in silver could influence EUR/USD and other currency pairs as investors reassess risk sentiment and safe-haven demand. Analysts suggest that unless the Fed signals a future easing, XAG/USD may remain constrained, while trading dynamics could see further volatility around key psychological and technical thresholds.
