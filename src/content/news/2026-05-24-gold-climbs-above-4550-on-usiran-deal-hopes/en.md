Gold price (XAU/USD) advanced to near $4,570 during early Asian trading on Monday as the US Dollar weakened across major currency pairs. The move followed reports suggesting the United States and Iran are nearing an agreement that could reopen the Strait of Hormuz, easing concerns over supply disruptions and improving broader market sentiment. Softer USD trading conditions helped boost demand for the precious metal despite a more stable risk backdrop.

The weaker Greenback pressured the USD exchange rate against several major currencies, while traders monitored developments in Middle East diplomacy for fresh direction. Market participants also adjusted positions ahead of upcoming US economic data, with investors closely watching Treasury yields and Federal Reserve expectations. Gold remained supported as lower US Dollar demand increased the metal’s attractiveness for holders of foreign currencies, including the EUR and JPY.

In the broader commodities and forex trading landscape, analysts noted that geopolitical headlines continue to drive short-term volatility across safe-haven assets. XAU/USD holding above the $4,550 level reinforced bullish momentum, with traders now focusing on whether gold can sustain gains near $4,570. Any confirmation of a US-Iran agreement could influence risk appetite, currency market flows, and overall trading sentiment in the sessions ahead.
