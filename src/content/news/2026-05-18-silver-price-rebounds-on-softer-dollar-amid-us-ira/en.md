Silver (XAG/USD) recovered early Monday, trading around $76.55, marking a 0.80% gain as the metal steadies following last week’s sharp declines. The softer USD amid ongoing US-Iran diplomatic talks has provided some support, prompting traders to re-enter positions in the white metal. Exchange rate fluctuations between the USD and other major currencies like the EUR are influencing sentiment in precious metals markets.

Despite the rebound, elevated US Treasury yields continue to cap upside potential, keeping traders cautious. Technical levels suggest resistance near $77, with support around $75.50, indicating a narrow range of near-term trading. Investors are closely watching shifts in the USD index and currency pair movements, as any strengthening dollar could weigh on silver prices.

Market participants are also factoring in broader macro conditions, including inflation expectations and central bank policy signals. The interplay between currency pairs, particularly USD/EUR and USD/JPY, will likely shape trading dynamics in the coming sessions. For now, silver appears poised for a tentative recovery, with momentum limited by high yields and ongoing geopolitical uncertainties.
