GBP/USD softened toward the 1.3340 area in recent trading, reflecting a modest pullback in the currency pair as USD demand edges higher. Despite the dip, analysts at UOB note that downside momentum remains limited, suggesting the exchange rate lacks the conviction for a sustained bearish move. Market participants appear cautious, balancing near-term USD strength against broader macro uncertainty.

Technically, the intraday bias still leans lower, but declines are expected to remain contained. UOB highlights 1.3305 as a key support level, with further weakness likely to stall near this zone rather than trigger a deeper slide. On the topside, resistance is seen at 1.3355 and 1.3375, levels that could cap any recovery attempts during current trading sessions.

The muted movement in GBP/USD reflects a market waiting for stronger catalysts, including economic data and central bank signals from both the UK and the US. For forex traders, the narrow range between 1.3305 and 1.3375 suggests short-term consolidation, with limited volatility unless new drivers emerge to shift sentiment in the currency pair.
