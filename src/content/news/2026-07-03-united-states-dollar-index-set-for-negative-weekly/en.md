The US Dollar Index (DXY) is heading for a negative weekly close as the currency weakens across the board following disappointing US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) data for June released on Thursday. The report prompted traders to scale back expectations of a hawkish Federal Reserve (Fed) stance, triggering renewed selling pressure on the USD in the foreign exchange market and shifting short-term trading sentiment.

Major currency pairs are reflecting the shift, with the US dollar underperforming against peers such as the euro and British pound in EUR/USD and GBP/USD trading, while USD/JPY also softens as yield differentials narrow. Exchange rates move in response to reduced rate-cut skepticism, as investors reassess interest rate trajectories and reposition portfolios ahead of upcoming US macroeconomic releases.

The outlook for the USD remains fragile as traders weigh the implications of weaker labor market data on Federal Reserve policy. Sustained pressure on the Dollar Index could extend volatility across forex trading desks, with risk-sensitive currencies gaining traction. Market participants now focus on whether additional data will reinforce expectations of a less hawkish Fed, shaping near-term exchange rate direction.
