The USD held firm against major currencies on Wednesday, reflecting ongoing uncertainty over a potential US-Iran agreement. The EUR/USD pair hovered around 1.0930, showing little directional bias, while USD/JPY edged slightly higher to 134.20 amid safe-haven flows. Traders remained cautious, weighing geopolitical risks alongside the upcoming Federal Reserve rate decision, which could set the tone for the dollar’s trajectory in the near term.  

Commodity-linked currencies also felt pressure as oil prices stayed elevated on Middle East tensions. AUD/USD dipped to 0.6425, and USD/CAD climbed to 1.3640, as investors balanced risk sentiment with central bank expectations. Technical levels are under close watch, with EUR/USD support near 1.0910 and resistance at 1.0965, shaping short-term trading strategies.  

Market participants are positioning ahead of the Fed announcement, anticipating either a pause or further tightening. Volatility is expected to rise, particularly in pairs like GBP/USD, which traded at 1.2560, and NZD/USD, which retreated to 0.5920. The combination of geopolitical uncertainty and monetary policy outlook continues to drive exchange rate fluctuations, keeping traders alert for rapid moves across major currency pairs.
