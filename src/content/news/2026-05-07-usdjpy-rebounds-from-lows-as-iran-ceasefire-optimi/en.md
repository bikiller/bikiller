The USD/JPY currency pair rebounded on Thursday, climbing to around 156.90, marking a roughly 0.4% gain from Wednesday’s sharp decline to 155.04, the lowest exchange rate since early February. Traders have been digesting mixed signals from global markets, as optimism over a potential ceasefire in Iran began to waver, prompting renewed demand for the dollar amid risk-off sentiment. Technical levels near 156.50–157.00 are now attracting attention as potential resistance zones.  

Market participants are also eyeing the U.S. economic calendar, with the upcoming Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report expected to influence USD trading. The rebound in USD/JPY reflects cautious positioning ahead of the data, as investors weigh the prospects of stronger employment figures potentially supporting the greenback. Meanwhile, volatility in other major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD underscores the broader uncertainty in forex markets.  

Analysts suggest that if the NFP report confirms robust job growth, the USD could maintain its upward momentum, testing higher technical levels against the yen. Conversely, softer-than-expected data may trigger further consolidation around the 155–157 range. For now, trading remains sensitive to geopolitical developments and U.S. economic indicators, keeping the USD/JPY exchange rate under close scrutiny.
