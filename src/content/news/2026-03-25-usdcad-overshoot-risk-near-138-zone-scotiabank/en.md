The Canadian Dollar is under pressure as the USD/CAD currency pair edges higher toward the 1.38 zone, according to Scotiabank strategists Shaun Osborne and Eric Theoret. Analysts highlight that the US Dollar’s sustained positive momentum is driving the exchange rate away from their fair value estimate, signaling potential overshoot risks. Traders are closely watching technical levels around 1.38 as the pair tests short-term resistance.  

Market participants attribute the CAD’s softness to broad USD strength, supported by resilient US economic data and higher yields that attract capital flows. The USD/CAD trading dynamic reflects a combination of domestic currency weakness and external factors favoring the greenback, with implications for cross-currency hedging and import-export cost calculations.  

Looking ahead, the strategists caution that while USD/CAD could continue its upward drift, the overshoot near 1.38 may present a window for profit-taking or short-term corrections. Investors and forex traders are monitoring momentum indicators and market positioning, balancing the USD’s gains against potential Canadian economic support, which could influence the pair’s trajectory over the coming sessions.
