Gold (XAU/USD) is experiencing downward pressure as it trades lower amid a robust US dollar and growing uncertainty surrounding peace talks between the US and Iran. Renewed tensions in the Strait of Hormuz over the weekend have dampened optimism, leading to a negative bias in the market. As traders adjust their positions, the precious metal's appeal is weakened, reflecting a shift in sentiment driven by geopolitical factors.

The exchange rate for gold has been impacted significantly by these developments, alongside fluctuations in the broader market. The strong performance of the USD continues to influence trading dynamics, as the EUR/USD pair also reflects the dollar's strength, creating a challenging environment for gold investors. With ongoing geopolitical concerns and a resilient dollar, the outlook for gold remains cautious as traders navigate these complexities.
