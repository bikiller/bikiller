Gold prices (XAU/USD) are poised to register their third consecutive weekly loss, continuing the downward trend following a sharp two-day drop. The precious metal's price has reached its lowest point since early February, hovering just above the $4,500 mark. This decline is driven by the ongoing "higher-for-longer" interest rate outlook, which continues to pressure gold.

The strong performance of the USD, bolstered by expectations of prolonged interest rate hikes from the Federal Reserve, has weighed heavily on gold’s appeal. As the U.S. dollar remains robust, trading in key currency pairs like EUR/USD and USD/JPY shows sustained strength, making gold less attractive to traders seeking better returns in other assets. With the price action nearing critical technical levels, market participants will be watching closely for further movement.
