Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher on Tuesday yet remained confined within a familiar two-week range, reflecting cautious trading in the currency pair. The exchange rate hovered near $4,530, marking a gain of almost 1% on the day as USD dynamics and broader forex sentiment kept moves contained despite intraday buying interest.

Geopolitical headlines dominated sentiment after comments from US President Donald Trump indicated that US-Iran talks are continuing at a rapid pace, keeping safe-haven demand for gold supported. Traders in the forex market reacted to shifting risk appetite, with USD movements and Middle East tensions influencing flows across major currency pairs.

From a technical perspective, XAU/USD continues to consolidate within its established two-week trading range, suggesting indecision among forex participants. While the USD index remains a key driver for direction, spillovers into EUR/USD and other major currency pairs highlight broader market caution. A decisive break beyond current boundaries would likely set the next trading bias for gold.
