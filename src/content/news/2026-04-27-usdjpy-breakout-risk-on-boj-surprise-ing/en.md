USD/JPY has been trading with unusually muted volatility, despite a backdrop of heightened global risk and several major central bank meetings. ING’s Chris Turner notes that the currency pair remains largely range-bound, suggesting that traders may be underestimating potential shocks. The exchange rate has held steady even as geopolitical tensions and market uncertainty continue to influence broader USD and EUR trading dynamics.  

Analysts at ING caution that the Bank of Japan could surprise markets with a rate hike or upward revisions to inflation forecasts. Such a move would likely trigger a sharp breakout in USD/JPY, disrupting current trading patterns and impacting carry trades. Investors are increasingly weighing the possibility that the yen could strengthen if BoJ signals a shift away from its long-standing ultra-loose policy stance.  

Market participants are advised to watch technical levels closely. A breakout above key resistance could accelerate USD gains, while a move below support would favor yen appreciation. The subdued volatility suggests positioning may be compressed, raising the stakes for sudden, high-impact moves. Traders in USD, EUR, and other major currencies are monitoring the situation for broader implications across global FX markets.
