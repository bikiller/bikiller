GBP/USD edged below the 1.3300 mark after swinging through a broad intraday range, reflecting heightened volatility in the currency pair. The exchange rate hit a session high near 1.3375 before sliding to a low around 1.3283, as traders reacted to comments from Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell. His warnings on persistent inflation reinforced expectations that the USD could remain resilient against major currencies, including the pound.  

Trading activity showed cautious positioning, with the British pound under pressure amid speculation over continued US rate support. Investors weighed the implications for cross-currency pairs, particularly GBP/EUR and EUR/USD, as market participants assessed whether the pound’s weakness could extend further. Technical levels near 1.3280 may act as short-term support, while resistance around 1.3375 sets the ceiling for any retracement.  

The market reaction suggests a delicate balance between inflation-driven USD strength and broader risk sentiment affecting the pound. Traders remain focused on upcoming US economic data, with any signs of persistent inflation likely to influence the currency pair’s trajectory. Short-term trading strategies may emphasize range-bound moves, but a sustained breach below 1.3280 could signal further GBP depreciation against the dollar.
