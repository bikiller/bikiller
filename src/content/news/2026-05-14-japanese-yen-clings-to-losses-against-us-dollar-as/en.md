The Japanese Yen remained under pressure against the US Dollar during Thursday's European trading session, with the USD/JPY currency pair hovering near a two-week high around 158.00. The exchange rate continues to benefit from the sustained strength of the US Dollar, supported by expectations of a hawkish Federal Reserve stance. Traders noted that Yen weakness reflects both domestic stagnation and overseas demand for USD liquidity.

Market participants are closely watching the technical level at 158.00, which has acted as a firm resistance point in recent sessions. The persistent outperformance of the US Dollar versus major currencies like the Euro (EUR) underscores a broader trend of USD dominance in global forex markets. Analysts suggest that any pullback in USD/JPY could be limited as long as hawkish Fed bets remain intact and the Yen struggles to attract safe-haven flows.

The outlook for the currency pair hinges on upcoming economic data from both the US and Japan. Strong US inflation or employment reports could further bolster USD gains, while any signs of economic recovery in Japan may provide temporary relief to the Yen. For forex traders, the current exchange rate dynamics emphasize the interplay between central bank expectations, technical thresholds, and market sentiment in driving USD/JPY movements.
