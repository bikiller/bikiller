British pound trading came under pressure after the Bank of England kept interest rates unchanged at 3.75%, triggering a sharp fall in GBP/USD before losses were partially recovered. According to UOB Global Economics & Markets Research, the currency pair dropped on the policy surprise but later stabilised, last seen around 1.3236 against the US dollar amid cautious risk sentiment.

Market attention shifted to the Bank of England decision, where policymakers voted 7–2 to hold rates at 3.75%, with two members preferring a hike to 4.00%. The split highlighted ongoing inflation concerns and limited room for easing, reinforcing expectations that UK monetary policy will remain restrictive compared with other major central banks.

Looking ahead, sterling may remain sensitive to USD dynamics and broader currency trading flows, with GBP/USD direction hinging on upcoming UK data and US economic signals. The cautious BoE tone suggests limited upside for the pound in the near term, while traders watch whether exchange rate support near 1.32 holds against renewed dollar strength.
