Gold (XAU/USD) is holding onto modest intraday gains for a third consecutive day, trading in the $4,560-$4,565 range as European markets open. Earlier this Tuesday, the precious metal touched a one-and-a-half-week high, but bullish momentum appears subdued. Traders are weighing the potential impact of Federal Reserve rate hike expectations on USD strength, which continues to influence gold’s exchange rate dynamics.  

Market participants note that while gold has shown resilience against a firmer USD, uncertainty around upcoming U.S. economic data is keeping investors cautious. The currency pair remains sensitive to shifts in Treasury yields and broader dollar index movements, with short-term traders closely monitoring support around $4,550 and resistance near recent highs. EUR/USD and other major pairs are also reflecting the market’s cautious stance ahead of key central bank commentary.  

Analysts suggest that unless the USD softens or risk sentiment turns, gold may struggle to extend gains beyond its current trading band. The interplay between dollar strength and safe-haven demand is likely to dictate near-term price action, with market watchers keeping a close eye on Fed communications, macroeconomic releases, and technical levels to gauge potential breakout or retracement scenarios in XAU/USD trading.
