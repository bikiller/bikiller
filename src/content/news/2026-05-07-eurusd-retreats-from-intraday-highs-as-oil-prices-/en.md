EUR/USD eased from intraday highs on Thursday as traders reacted to renewed geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, which bolstered the US Dollar and lifted crude oil prices. The currency pair retreated from earlier gains, reflecting market caution amid uncertainty around the Strait of Hormuz, a critical oil transit route. The shift highlights how geopolitical events continue to influence currency trading and global risk sentiment.  

Oil prices rebounded sharply, offsetting recent losses, while the USD gained traction against major peers. Traders noted that safe-haven demand for the greenback intensified amid the evolving situation, weighing on the EUR/USD exchange rate. Technical levels around key intraday resistance and support points guided short-term trading decisions, with investors closely monitoring price reactions to newsflow for direction.  

Market participants expect that continued volatility in energy markets and geopolitical headlines could sustain swings in the EUR/USD pair. Analysts caution that if tensions escalate, USD strength may persist, potentially pushing the exchange rate lower. Conversely, any easing in regional risks could restore confidence in the euro, prompting a rebound in trading activity as investors reassess risk exposures.
