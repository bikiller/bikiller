GBP/USD edged lower on Tuesday, slipping 0.15% to hover around the 1.3500 mark after a choppy trading session. The currency pair saw a roughly 60-pip range, with price action marked by sharp declines in both European and US trading hours. Each sell-off was met with buying support, allowing the exchange rate to recover modestly toward the close, highlighting persistent market volatility.  

Traders weighed upbeat risk sentiment from an Iran ceasefire against robust US data, which capped gains in the pound. Despite early rallies, the GBP struggled to hold above key technical levels as USD strength resurfaced. Analysts noted that while risk-on factors supported some recovery, broader dollar demand and economic data releases continued to dictate short-term direction in the forex market.  

Looking ahead, the GBP/USD exchange rate remains sensitive to both geopolitical developments and US economic indicators. Breaks below 1.3500 could invite further selling pressure, while sustained demand might offer upside if risk sentiment improves. Traders are keeping an eye on volatility patterns and market reaction to upcoming US releases, as well as ongoing EUR and USD movements, to gauge potential shifts in the pair’s trajectory.
