Gold prices climbed on Wednesday, reaching a peak of $4,857 earlier in the day, as easing tensions between the US and Iran sparked optimism in the markets. However, the precious metal gave up some gains and now trades at $4,735, up over 0.70%. The softer US Dollar, driven by falling oil prices, contributed to gold's upward movement. The US Dollar Index (DXY) weakened to a four-week low, offering support to the XAU/USD pair.

The US Dollar’s weakness was further fueled by the decline in oil prices, which put downward pressure on the greenback. As a result, gold, traditionally viewed as a safe-haven asset, benefited from a more favorable trading environment. The market's focus on the US-Iran ceasefire news and softening oil prices continues to influence the trading dynamics, highlighting the ongoing interplay between global geopolitical factors and forex markets.
