Rabobank strategists highlight that escalating US and Israel tensions with Iran are reshaping China’s growth outlook. Higher oil and gas prices are feeding global cost-push inflation, which could pressure commodity-linked currencies and energy-sensitive markets. Traders are watching the USD/CNY closely, as rising energy costs may widen China’s trade deficit, potentially keeping the renminbi under modest depreciation pressure in coming sessions.  

Despite global inflationary pressures, Rabobank expects China’s domestic inflation to remain contained, limiting the likelihood of a People’s Bank of China (PBOC) tightening cycle. Analysts note that softer consumer price dynamics could support stable CNY trading ranges, keeping EUR/CNY and USD/CNY within recent technical levels. Market participants are positioning cautiously, balancing geopolitical risk against China’s stimulus policies.  

FX markets reflect this nuanced environment, with the USD gaining slightly on safe-haven flows, while commodity-linked currencies like the AUD and CAD face mixed pressures from higher oil. Liquidity is thin ahead of key economic releases, with traders closely monitoring exchange rate moves across Asia. Any sudden escalation in Middle East tensions could trigger sharper USD strength, influencing cross-pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD.
