Japanese investors, particularly banks and life insurers, were significant sellers of foreign bonds in February and March, according to MUFG’s Head of Research, Derek Halpenny. These sales likely included U.S. Treasuries (USTs) and European Government Bonds (EGBs), marking a record two-month net outflow. This shift in bond appetite has impacted the yen, with the USD/JPY exchange rate remaining volatile as investors adjust their portfolios.

The sell-off is seen as part of a broader trend of Japanese investors reassessing their bond holdings in light of global economic uncertainty. With Japanese yields still low, there is growing speculation about a shift in the market dynamics, as these flows could push USD/JPY and EUR/JPY further out of range. Traders will be closely monitoring this trend, as it could signal a longer-term change in market sentiment.
