Gold held its ground during Thursday’s Asian trading session, with XAU/USD trading largely flat as investors eyed upcoming geopolitical developments. The precious metal’s stability comes ahead of the high-profile meeting between US President Donald Trump and Chinese President Xi Jinping in Beijing, which has injected caution into markets. Traders remain alert to any signals that could affect risk sentiment and currency pairs like USD/JPY and EUR/USD.  

The lack of significant movement in gold suggests that the market is digesting uncertainties surrounding trade discussions. Analysts note that while gold typically benefits from safe-haven demand, the current exchange rate environment—especially the strength of the USD—has kept XAU/USD contained near recent technical levels. Forex traders are monitoring shifts in major currency pairs, as any decisive move from the summit could trigger swings in USD, EUR, and JPY positions.  

Market participants are also keeping an eye on broader indicators, including equity and bond markets, for hints of risk appetite. Any surprise announcements from the Trump–Xi summit could prompt rapid adjustments across currencies and commodities alike, potentially influencing short-term trading strategies. For now, gold remains a steady benchmark in a cautiously poised forex landscape, with its near-term trajectory closely tied to developments in US-China trade talks.
