The USD continues its downward correction into May, as the earlier two-month rally unwinds, according to Philip Wee, economist at DBS Group Research. The Dollar's weakness in April is expected to persist, with divergence in monetary policies between the Federal Reserve and other central banks influencing the exchange rate. Market participants are adjusting positions, leading to renewed volatility in USD trading.

The EUR/USD pair has benefitted from this shift, with the euro climbing above 1.10 against the Dollar. Technical levels indicate further room for the EUR to rise if the USD remains under pressure. As the market digests the impact of diverging central bank policies, attention will turn to upcoming economic data and Fed statements for further clues on the pace of US interest rate hikes.
