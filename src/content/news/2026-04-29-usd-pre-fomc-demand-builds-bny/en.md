The US Dollar (USD) is showing renewed strength ahead of the Federal Open Market Committee (FOMC) meeting, according to BNY’s Bob Savage. Market activity points to growing demand, with the USD recording its first five-day net purchase streak in three months. Investors are increasingly turning to USD cash and short-term instruments, signaling a shift in positioning that could influence near-term exchange rate movements across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and USD/JPY.  

Trading in the USD has seen notable upticks in volume, reflecting heightened appetite for the greenback as a safe-haven and funding currency. Technical momentum appears supportive, with the currency outperforming peers amid expectations of FOMC policy guidance. Currency pairs like EUR/USD are adjusting as traders weigh the implications of potential rate decisions, with short-term instruments helping hedge against volatility.  

Analysts suggest the USD’s firming could tighten spreads and influence cross-currency flows, particularly versus the euro and yen. As the FOMC announcement approaches, market participants are positioning for possible shifts in interest rate expectations, making USD trading increasingly active. This pre-FOMC demand pattern underscores the dollar’s central role in global forex markets and hints at continued sensitivity to policy cues in the days ahead.
