The New Zealand dollar remains under pressure as the NZD/USD currency pair trades below the 0.5650 level, hovering around 0.5635 during Friday’s Asian trading hours. The exchange rate extends its losses for an eighth consecutive session, reflecting sustained bearish momentum in the forex market as sellers maintain control of the pair.

Market sentiment on the New Zealand dollar is increasingly driven by rising expectations of further Federal Reserve interest rate hikes, which continue to bolster the US dollar across major currency pairs. As USD strength persists, risk-sensitive currencies such as the NZD face renewed selling pressure in global trading sessions overall.

Looking ahead, the NZD/USD exchange rate may remain vulnerable if Fed rate hike expectations continue to support the US dollar, with immediate technical resistance seen near 0.5650 and support around 0.5635. Sustained weakness below these levels could signal further downside risks for the currency pair in upcoming trading sessions ahead.
