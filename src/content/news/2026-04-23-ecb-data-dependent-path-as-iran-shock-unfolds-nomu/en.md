Nomura analysts predict that the European Central Bank (ECB) will maintain the deposit rate at 2.00% during its meeting on April 30. They expect this steady rate to persist through the fourth quarter of 2027, as the ECB adopts a data-dependent approach in light of unfolding geopolitical tensions, particularly the situation in Iran. This decision is likely to influence the EUR/USD exchange rate, keeping it under pressure in the near term.

As traders evaluate the implications of the ECB's stance, the focus will be on how the ongoing geopolitical developments could impact inflation and economic growth in the Eurozone. A stable deposit rate could reinforce the euro's volatility against the USD, as market participants assess the balance between supporting growth and curbing inflationary pressures. The currency pair's movements will be closely watched as traders position themselves for potential shifts in monetary policy ahead.
