The GBP/USD currency pair edged higher on Friday, reflecting a boost in risk appetite ahead of the anticipated US-Iran talks in Pakistan. Traders pushed the exchange rate above the 1.2850 level, signaling renewed confidence in risk-sensitive assets. Market participants appear reassured by the prospect of easing geopolitical tensions, which has encouraged short-term buying in the pound against the dollar.  

Meanwhile, US inflation data for March came in line with expectations, with prices showing a modest uptick. Despite the increase, investors treated it as a likely one-off, leaving USD trading largely unaffected. The focus remains on broader market sentiment, with EUR/USD and other major pairs also responding to optimism over potential Middle East peace progress.  

Technical analysts note that the GBP/USD is testing resistance near 1.2875, with support around 1.2820 holding firm. Trading volumes suggest that market participants are positioning for follow-through in risk-linked currencies if talks proceed smoothly. Any surprises in the US inflation outlook or geopolitical developments could quickly shift momentum, keeping traders vigilant across the FX market.
