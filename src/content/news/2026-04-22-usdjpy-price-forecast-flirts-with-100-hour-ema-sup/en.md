The USD/JPY currency pair is facing modest intraday losses, retreating from a one-week high of 159.70 touched on the previous day. During the early European session, the exchange rate dips to around 159.00, marking a fresh daily low. Despite this pullback, the downside potential appears limited as traders watch for potential support near the 100-hour exponential moving average (EMA).

The softer USD continues to weigh on the pair, contributing to its decline, while the overall market sentiment remains cautious. The 159.00 level serves as a key psychological support, with the pair's ability to hold above it potentially signaling a consolidation phase. Traders are likely to monitor any shifts in USD sentiment and technical levels for further direction in the near term.
