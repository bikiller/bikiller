The US Dollar Index has dropped below the 98.30 mark as the greenback comes under broad selling pressure against major currencies. Traders are positioning ahead of the US preliminary Q1 Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and March Personal Consumption Expenditure (PCE) data, scheduled for release at 12:30 GMT. The weakness in USD reflects market caution, with investors anticipating how the key economic indicators might influence Federal Reserve policy and global liquidity flows.  

EUR/USD has gained traction amid the USD retreat, testing higher levels as traders adjust positions in response to the looming data. Other major pairs, including GBP/USD and USD/JPY, are also showing notable volatility, with trading activity ramping up as market participants weigh growth and inflation prospects. Analysts highlight that any surprise in GDP or PCE readings could trigger sharp shifts in exchange rates, influencing cross-currency dynamics and short-term technical trends.  

Market sentiment ahead of the US economic releases remains cautious, with the USD under pressure but not yet signaling a sustained trend reversal. Forex traders are closely watching for PCE-driven inflation signals, which may dictate USD strength in coming sessions. With the currency index hovering near critical levels, even modest surprises in GDP growth or price data could prompt significant adjustments across major currency pairs, setting the tone for early Q2 trading.
