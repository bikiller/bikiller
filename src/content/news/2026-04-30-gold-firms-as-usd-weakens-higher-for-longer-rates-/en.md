Gold prices (XAU/USD) edged higher on Thursday, recovering from a one-month low of $4,510 hit the previous day. The modest rebound comes amid a weaker US Dollar (USD), as investor concerns over potential FX interventions by Tokyo continue to build. The decline in the greenback has provided some relief to precious metals, boosting gold’s appeal as a safe haven.

However, the upside for gold remains limited as higher-for-longer interest rate expectations from the Federal Reserve continue to weigh on the market. The EUR/USD exchange rate saw some volatility as well, reacting to the USD's fluctuation, but overall, the broader sentiment in the forex market remains cautious, with traders eyeing upcoming economic data for further direction. Despite short-term gains, gold's rally faces pressure from persistent rate hike fears.
