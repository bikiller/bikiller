The EUR/USD currency pair fell below the 1.1700 mark on Tuesday, retracing its gains after a failed attempt to break the 1.1755 resistance level on Monday. The Euro's strength against the US Dollar was short-lived, as the greenback gained momentum amid cautious market sentiment and geopolitical uncertainty, particularly regarding the ongoing Middle East tensions.

The US Dollar's rise reflects broader risk aversion in the markets, with traders closely monitoring upcoming central bank decisions. As the Middle East conflict remains unresolved, the USD has benefited from its safe-haven status, overshadowing the Euro's attempts to extend its rally. This shift has put the EUR/USD pair under pressure, with further moves likely depending on market reactions to global events and monetary policy announcements in the coming days.
