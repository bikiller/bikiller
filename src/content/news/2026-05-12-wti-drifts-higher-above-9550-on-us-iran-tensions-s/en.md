West Texas Intermediate (WTI) edged above $95.50 early Tuesday, trading around $95.70 in Asian markets as geopolitical concerns in the Middle East reignited. Market participants are closely monitoring tensions between the US and Iran, with fears of a disruption to shipping through the Strait of Hormuz underpinning bullish momentum. Crude’s climb has implications for USD liquidity, influencing currency pairs sensitive to oil prices.

The uptick in oil has nudged energy-linked currencies higher, with the CAD and NOK seeing slight gains against the USD in early trading. Forex traders are weighing risk appetite, with EUR/USD hovering near key technical levels as investors reassess global economic stability. WTI’s movements are increasingly intertwined with exchange rate fluctuations, impacting trading strategies across major forex pairs.

Analysts suggest the short-term oil rally could extend if tensions escalate, potentially adding pressure to inflation expectations and central bank policies. Traders are watching for technical support and resistance around $95 and $96, respectively, while currency markets digest the broader macro implications. Volatility in both commodities and forex underscores a cautious approach to trading amid rising geopolitical uncertainty.
