Gold is facing near-term pressure as TD Securities lowers its forecasts for the next two quarters, citing rising inflation expectations from ongoing supply shocks. The firm notes that higher yields are keeping the USD firm against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, pressuring gold prices. Traders are closely watching exchange rate movements as markets increasingly price in a potential Federal Reserve rate hike by late 2026.  

Despite short-term softness, TD Securities sees longer-term strength for gold, driven by persistent inflation and safe-haven demand. The strategists suggest that while gold may trade lower against a strong USD in the near term, technical support levels around key benchmarks could attract buying interest. This dynamic keeps gold relevant for hedging strategies, especially in portfolios exposed to currency volatility.  

Forex traders are balancing gold trading against USD strength, noting that currency pair swings, particularly USD/EUR and USD/JPY, are influencing investment flows. Market participants are also factoring in expectations of Fed policy adjustments, which could reshape yields and exchange rates, ultimately affecting the interplay between gold prices and global currency markets. The outlook suggests cautious positioning in the short term while maintaining a strategic view for potential longer-term gains.
