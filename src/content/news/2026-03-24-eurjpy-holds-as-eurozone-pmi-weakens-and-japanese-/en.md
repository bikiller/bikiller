The EUR/JPY currency pair is trading around the 184.00 level, showing limited directional movement as investors digest fresh economic data. Eurozone PMI figures revealed a notable slowdown, highlighting a loss of momentum in the region’s manufacturing and services sectors. The soft reading has weighed on the euro, keeping the exchange rate contained amid cautious trading, while broader market sentiment remains influenced by USD strength in global markets.  

Meanwhile, Japanese inflation trends have eased, reducing pressure on the Bank of Japan to adjust monetary policy. Softer domestic price growth has contributed to the yen’s muted reaction, allowing EUR/JPY to hover in a neutral zone. Traders are watching key technical levels closely, noting that the cross is struggling to sustain momentum above 184.00, signaling potential consolidation in the near term.  

Market participants are weighing the combination of weak Eurozone activity and subdued Japanese inflation against broader currency dynamics. The euro faces headwinds from slowing economic indicators, while the yen’s lack of inflationary pressure limits volatility. As a result, EUR/JPY is navigating a narrow trading range, with analysts suggesting that upcoming PMI revisions and USD movements could define the next directional shift in the currency pair.
