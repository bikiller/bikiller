Gold is seeing a technical rebound after a steep decline of nearly 20% since the onset of the Iran conflict, according to OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong. The precious metal’s recovery appears largely driven by short-term technical buying rather than fundamental shifts, as rising U.S. Treasury yields continue to weigh on its appeal. Traders are watching how the yellow metal reacts near key resistance levels amid broad USD strength.  

The rebound comes amid volatile currency markets, where the USD has strengthened against major pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Higher real yields in the U.S. are keeping pressure on gold, even as geopolitical tensions support risk-averse flows. Market participants are evaluating whether this bounce can sustain itself or will be capped by the persistent attractiveness of USD-denominated assets.  

Investors are weighing the implications for trading strategies, with the near-term outlook for gold remaining cautious. While technical indicators suggest a short-term recovery, the underlying headwinds from rising yields and a firm USD may limit further gains. Currency traders are also monitoring correlations with USD/JPY and EUR/USD, as shifts in exchange rates could influence gold demand and broader commodity flows.
