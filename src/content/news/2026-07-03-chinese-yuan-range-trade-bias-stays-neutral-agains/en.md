USD/CNH exchange rate shows a slight pickup in downward momentum, but overall trading conditions remain range-bound, according to UOB strategist Quek Ser Leang. The currency pair continues to reflect a neutral bias against the US dollar, with no clear directional breakout despite recent pressure. Market participants remain cautious as price action consolidates within established levels.

UOB now sees a slightly lower intraday trading band for the USD/CNH currency pair at 6.7820–6.7940, signalling limited downside extension despite softening momentum. The US dollar remains broadly supported, but intraday flows suggest consolidation rather than trend continuation. Traders are monitoring whether price action can break below support or revert toward the upper end of the range.

On a 1–3 week horizon, the outlook for USD/CNH remains neutral, with the pair expected to oscillate between 6.7750 and 6.8080. This suggests continued range trading in the forex market as neither USD strength nor weakness dominates EUR. Investors are likely to focus on macro catalysts and US–China policy signals for a clearer directional break.
