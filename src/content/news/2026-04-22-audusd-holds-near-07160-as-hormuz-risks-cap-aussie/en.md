The AUD/USD currency pair is hovering near 0.7160 as traders weigh heightened risks in the Middle East. Market participants remain cautious following Iran’s seizure of two container ships in the Strait of Hormuz, while US President Trump has extended the ceasefire, adding temporary relief but keeping volatility elevated. The Australian dollar’s exchange rate has struggled to break out of its recent range amid these geopolitical tensions.  

Trading activity reflects a mix of safe-haven demand for the USD and selective risk appetite for AUD. Technical levels around 0.7160 have proven resilient, with intraday swings limited as investors digest developments in the Gulf. Analysts note that sustained upside for the Australian dollar may be capped until clarity emerges over shipping security and regional stability, keeping short-term trading cautious.  

Broader forex markets show USD strength against a basket of major currencies, including the EUR, as investors favor liquidity amid uncertainty. Traders are closely monitoring commodity-linked currencies like AUD, whose movements are increasingly sensitive to geopolitical shocks and risk sentiment. The current range-bound trading suggests that the exchange rate could remain constrained until further updates from the Middle East reshape market expectations.
