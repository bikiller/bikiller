AUD/USD currency pair is trading near 0.6880 on Monday, slipping from Friday’s close and hovering close to a three-month low as the Australian Dollar weakens against the US Dollar amid cautious trading conditions. The exchange rate remains under pressure after last week’s declines, reflecting broader USD strength and subdued risk appetite across forex markets.

AUD traders are awaiting the Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) Minutes for fresh direction, with market participants closely watching for signals on future interest rate policy. China-related economic cues also remain in focus, influencing the AUD/USD currency pair alongside broader USD and EUR movements in global trading sentiment as investors reassess risk exposure.

Technically, the currency pair remains near key support around 0.6880, with traders cautious ahead of potential volatility driven by USD data releases and shifts in global forex sentiment. A break below this exchange rate could extend losses, while recovery above 0.6900 may signal short-term stabilization for AUD/USD trading in coming sessions.
