Currency markets maintained an upbeat tone on Wednesday as traders digested the latest developments surrounding US-Iran nuclear talks. The USD held firm against major peers, with the EUR/USD hovering around 1.0985, while the GBP/USD traded near 1.2660. Analysts noted that lingering uncertainty over diplomatic progress kept volatility contained, even as investors balanced risk sentiment with safe-haven demand for the greenback.  

Technical indicators suggest key support for EUR/USD sits at 1.0950, with resistance around 1.1020, leaving room for range-bound trading unless fresh catalysts emerge. Meanwhile, USD/JPY remained steady near 133.50, reflecting broad USD strength, while commodity-linked currencies like AUD and NZD saw modest gains as risk appetite held. Traders continue monitoring central bank cues and geopolitical headlines for directional signals.  

Market participants highlighted that trading volumes were moderate, with the focus on how US-Iran talks could influence energy markets and, by extension, currency flows. A breakthrough could boost risk currencies, while a deadlock may reinforce demand for USD and JPY. Investors remain alert to shifts in interest rate expectations, inflation data, and technical breakouts, which could shape near-term exchange rate trajectories across major currency pairs.
