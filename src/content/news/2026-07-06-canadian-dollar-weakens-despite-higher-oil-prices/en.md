USD/CAD extended gains for a second consecutive session, trading around 1.4210 during Asian hours on Monday. The currency pair reflects continued USD strength against the Canadian Dollar, with the exchange rate edging higher as traders maintain demand for the US dollar across major forex markets in early trading activity across the region.

Despite rising oil prices, the Canadian Dollar struggled to gain traction, limiting its typical commodity-linked support. Higher crude benchmarks usually bolster CAD, but current forex trading shows muted reaction as investors weigh broader USD resilience and global risk sentiment. The divergence highlights uneven drivers influencing the currency pair in recent sessions.

Market participants will continue monitoring USD demand and oil price dynamics for direction in USD/CAD trading. While higher crude oil prices may provide a floor for the Canadian Dollar, sustained US dollar strength keeps the currency pair biased to the upside, with traders assessing whether momentum can extend beyond current exchange rate levels.
