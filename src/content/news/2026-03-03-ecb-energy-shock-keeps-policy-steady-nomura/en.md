Analysts at Nomura, including Andrzej Szczepaniak, Josie Anderson, George Buckley, and David Seif, suggest the European Central Bank is likely to maintain its current monetary stance despite recent volatility in energy markets. They argue the ECB will largely look past short-term shocks and focus on its end-horizon HICP inflation forecast, signaling a measured approach to policy. Traders in EUR/USD and other major currency pairs have reacted cautiously, weighing energy price surges against underlying eurozone inflation trends.  

The energy-driven moves have pushed spot EUR/USD trading to test technical support near 1.0860, while USD bulls have capitalized on safe-haven flows amid market jitters. Nomura emphasizes that such swings are largely transitory, with the euro exchange rate expected to stabilize as the ECB maintains forward guidance. Currency markets are closely monitoring this dynamic, as any hint of a policy shift could prompt renewed volatility across USD, EUR, and cross rates like EUR/GBP.  

Looking ahead, the focus for forex traders remains on ECB communications and eurozone inflation data. Nomura notes that while energy shocks drive short-term swings, the broader trend hinges on the ECB’s inflation trajectory. Trading strategies are increasingly oriented around end-horizon HICP expectations, with EUR/USD likely to fluctuate within a defined range unless fresh economic data prompts a rethink. The balance between energy costs and structural inflation will shape euro performance against the dollar in the near term.
