The GBP/USD currency pair has seen a modest recovery after hitting a four-month low of 1.3160-1.3155 during Tuesday's Asian session. This marked a significant low point for the British pound against the US dollar, which has been in retreat recently. The recovery halted a five-day losing streak, providing some relief to traders who had seen the pound's downward pressure intensify over the past week.

Despite the bounce, the upside potential for GBP/USD appears limited in the short term. The broader market sentiment continues to favor the US dollar, supported by its relative strength against major currencies. With the pound struggling to regain momentum, any upward movement could face resistance unless key technical levels break, particularly as investors remain cautious amid ongoing global economic concerns.
