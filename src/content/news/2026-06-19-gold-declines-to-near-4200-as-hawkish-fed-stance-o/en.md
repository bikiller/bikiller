Gold prices extended their decline in early Asian trading on Friday, with XAU/USD falling to around $4,210 after the US Federal Reserve maintained interest rates at its June policy meeting. Despite holding rates steady, the Fed delivered a hawkish message, indicating that additional rate hikes could still be possible later this year. The stronger policy outlook supported the USD and weighed on demand for the non-yielding precious metal.

The downside move came even as easing geopolitical tensions following a reported US-Iran peace agreement reduced broader market uncertainty. While the development improved risk sentiment across financial markets, traders focused more heavily on the Fed’s stance, which reinforced expectations of higher US borrowing costs. As a result, the USD exchange rate strengthened against several major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, limiting gold’s appeal.

Market participants are now assessing how future Fed decisions could influence trading conditions across currencies and commodities. Higher interest rates typically boost the US Dollar while increasing the opportunity cost of holding gold. With XAU/USD trading near $4,210, investors will closely monitor upcoming US economic data and central bank commentary for fresh direction, as shifts in rate expectations remain a key driver of both gold prices and major forex market movements.
