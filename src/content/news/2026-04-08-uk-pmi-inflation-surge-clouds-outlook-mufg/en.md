UK economic data has rattled forex markets as PMI readings signal mounting pressure on the pound. MUFG’s Head of Research, Derek Halpenny, highlighted that UK Services and Composite PMIs showed a sharper contraction than across Europe. Input prices surged to record levels, largely driven by energy costs, intensifying inflation concerns and fueling speculation over the Bank of England’s next policy moves.  

Traders responded swiftly, with GBP/USD slipping toward recent support zones, while EUR/GBP edged higher amid diverging growth trends. Analysts noted that the combination of slowing activity and soaring costs could weigh on the pound, prompting heightened volatility in short-term currency trading. Market participants are closely monitoring whether these inflationary pressures will prompt more aggressive central bank interventions.  

The broader forex outlook reflects caution. USD/GBP exchange rates are reacting to safe-haven flows into the dollar, while EUR/GBP dynamics suggest traders are pricing in stronger resilience across the Eurozone. Halpenny warned that the UK’s inflation surge clouds the economic outlook, potentially limiting upside for sterling, and technical levels around key support could see increased testing as trading patterns adapt to the data.
