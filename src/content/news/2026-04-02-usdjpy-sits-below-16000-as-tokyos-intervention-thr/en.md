USD/JPY is trading just below 159.60 as Asian markets open Friday, reflecting heightened caution ahead of the long Easter weekend. The Japanese yen remains under pressure amid persistent dollar strength, with traders balancing the risk of Tokyo intervention against market expectations. The currency pair has struggled to sustain levels above 160.00, highlighting the sensitivity of the exchange rate to both geopolitical and domestic monetary cues.  

Market participants are closely eyeing Friday’s U.S. Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP), which could further sway the USD/JPY trajectory. Strong payroll data may reinforce the dollar’s momentum, pushing the pair closer to key resistance around 160.00, while weaker-than-expected figures could give the yen some breathing room. Trading volumes remain light, yet volatility spikes are possible as investors recalibrate positions in the face of central bank signaling and intervention chatter.  

Broader currency markets reflect similar dynamics, with the USD holding firm against the EUR and other major currencies. Forex traders are navigating a delicate balance of risk appetite and safe-haven flows, as the yen’s vulnerability highlights the interplay between Japan’s intervention stance and U.S. economic indicators. For now, technical levels around 159.60–160.00 remain critical markers for short-term positioning and strategic hedging.
