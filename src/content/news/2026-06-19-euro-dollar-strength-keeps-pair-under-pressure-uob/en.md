EUR/USD extended its recent decline as US dollar strength dominated trading, with the US Dollar Index climbing to a one-year high after the Federal Reserve delivered a hawkish hold, according to UOB Global Economics & Markets Research. The move reinforced bearish pressure on the euro, keeping the currency pair under sustained downside momentum across major forex sessions.

Market participants saw renewed demand for the USD, driven by expectations that US interest rates will remain elevated for longer, weighing on EUR/USD trading dynamics. The currency pair struggled to regain upside traction, hovering closer to recent lows as euro weakness persisted amid widening monetary policy divergence between the Fed and the European Central Bank.

Looking ahead, traders are likely to focus on upcoming US data releases and Federal Reserve commentary for further clues on the dollar’s trajectory, while ECB policy signals may do little to offset current EUR/USD pressure. Persistent USD strength could keep the currency pair biased to the downside, reinforcing a cautious outlook across short-term forex trading strategies.
