The USD/CAD currency pair is showing limited upside as the Canadian dollar maintains strength against a softer U.S. dollar. Overnight, the pair bounced from the weekly low near 1.3900, but efforts to build on that momentum have faltered, with prices drifting lower during Thursday’s Asian session. Traders remain cautious amid modest market catalysts.

Market participants are closely watching the exchange rate as trading activity reflects hesitancy. The Canadian dollar’s resilience is supported by underlying fundamentals, while the U.S. dollar continues to struggle, weighing on the pair. Short-term resistance levels near 1.3950 could cap gains, signaling limited room for further upside in USD/CAD without significant economic triggers.

The broader forex market sees a mix of movements, with EUR/USD and other major pairs showing typical trading fluctuations. Analysts note that while the Canadian dollar remains on the front foot, USD weakness is not pronounced enough to drive a strong rally. Investors may favor measured positions, balancing potential opportunities against technical resistance around key levels. Overall, the USD/CAD pair is likely to trade within a defined range in the near term.
