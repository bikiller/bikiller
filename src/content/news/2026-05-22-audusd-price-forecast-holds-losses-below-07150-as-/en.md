The AUD/USD currency pair is under pressure, extending losses from the previous session and trading near 0.7140 during Friday’s Asian hours. The exchange rate remains below the key 0.7150 level, reflecting cautious sentiment among traders as a descending wedge pattern begins to take shape on the charts. Market participants are closely watching this technical formation, which could signal further downside if support levels fail to hold.

Investor focus is split between USD strength and broader risk sentiment. The greenback’s resilience against major peers such as EUR and JPY has weighed on the Australian dollar, while global market uncertainty has kept trading volumes moderate. Analysts note that any rebound above 0.7150 could face resistance near recent highs, limiting upside potential and maintaining pressure on the pair in short-term trading.

Looking ahead, the AUD/USD could continue to navigate the wedge pattern, with intraday swings likely influenced by U.S. economic data and commodity price trends that historically affect the Australian dollar. Traders are advised to monitor key technical levels closely, as a decisive break below the wedge could accelerate the decline, while a reversal could prompt a tactical rally against the USD. Volatility is expected to remain elevated amid these mixed market signals.
