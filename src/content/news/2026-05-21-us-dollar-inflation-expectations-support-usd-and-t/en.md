The U.S. dollar has found support as long-term inflation expectations rise, bolstering both USD trading and Treasury markets. Geoff Yu of BNY notes that the 5-year, 5-year forward inflation swap is increasingly aligning with European levels, reflecting market concerns over prolonged supply disruptions. This trend has reinforced the USD against major currencies, including the EUR, while lifting Treasury yields.

Currency pairs such as EUR/USD are seeing tighter ranges, with traders factoring in potential Fed-driven adjustments. The convergence in inflation expectations suggests a possible 10 basis point upside, influencing short- and medium-term exchange rate forecasts. Market participants are weighing how sustained inflation pressures could shape USD strength and Eurozone interest rate responses.

The broader implication for forex markets is a heightened sensitivity to U.S. economic signals. Stronger inflation expectations typically encourage USD buying, while impacting risk sentiment and Treasury trading. As the 5y5y measure climbs, forex traders may adjust positions across USD pairs, anticipating that continued convergence with European inflation expectations could steer the trajectory of both exchange rates and fixed income yields over the coming weeks.
