USD/JPY recovered on Tuesday, gaining over 0.25% as the currency pair extended its rebound in trading sessions. Buyers pushed the exchange rate above the 159.00 intervention zone, with momentum carrying the pair to 159.38 at the time of writing. The move reflects renewed USD strength against the yen despite caution in broader forex markets.

Despite lingering intervention fears from Japanese authorities, traders largely ignored the warning zone and continued buying USD/JPY. The exchange rate now eyes the 159.50 level, which is seen as the next short-term resistance. The trading tone suggests persistent dollar demand and yen softness, supporting bullish sentiment in the currency pair.

However, upside momentum may remain capped as intervention risk keeps markets cautious around higher levels. Volatility in USD trading could increase if authorities step in, potentially triggering sharp pullbacks. For now, USD/JPY remains elevated, but the forex outlook hinges on whether buyers can sustain pressure above key resistance or face renewed selling.
