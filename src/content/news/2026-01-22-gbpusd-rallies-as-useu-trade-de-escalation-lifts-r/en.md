GBP/USD strengthened during the North American session, rising sharply as market sentiment improved following a de-escalation of the US-EU trade tensions. The currency pair saw an uptick as traders reacted to easing trade-war fears, which boosted overall risk appetite. The exchange rate surged towards 1.2400, signaling investor confidence in the British pound amid positive developments between the US and Europe.

Despite strong US economic data, which pointed to continued strength in the economy, the dollar failed to gain traction. The data, while solid, was unable to offset the positive shift in risk sentiment that saw traders move away from the safe-haven USD. As a result, GBP/USD extended its rally, with the currency pair maintaining upward momentum, reflecting broader market optimism.
