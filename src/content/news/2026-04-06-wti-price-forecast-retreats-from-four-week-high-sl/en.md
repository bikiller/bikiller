WTI crude oil retreated below the $104.00 mark after briefly testing $106.45 earlier in the Asian session, near a four-week high. Traders cited a mix of profit-taking and cautious sentiment despite ongoing supply concerns. The dip in oil prices weighed on USD-linked commodity currencies, with the AUD/USD and CAD/USD pairs showing increased volatility as traders adjusted positions.  

The shift came amid a backdrop of mixed economic data from the US and Europe, prompting traders to reassess risk appetite. Rising WTI earlier had supported energy-linked currencies, but the pullback reinforced the US dollar’s strength across major pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Market participants cited technical levels, noting that WTI’s failure to sustain above $106.00 signaled potential short-term retracement.  

Analysts suggest that the retreat could prompt range-bound trading in both oil and currency markets. With supply risks still present, WTI could revisit $105.00 if buying interest returns, potentially impacting commodity-linked exchange rates. Forex traders are watching closely for correlations between crude movements and USD strength, as shifts in energy prices continue to influence broader trading strategies and cross-currency dynamics.
