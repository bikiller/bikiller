Gold prices saw a mild uptick on Wednesday, extending gains from the previous day as market sentiment shifted positively amidst ongoing geopolitical tensions. The XAU/USD currency pair rose, benefitting from a weaker USD despite robust economic data from the United States. Traders are increasingly optimistic that the US-Iran conflict may soon ease, adding further support to gold's rally. As of midday, gold was trading around $1,800 per ounce.

The USD's weakness was the primary driver behind gold's movement, with investors shifting focus from strong US economic figures to the geopolitical developments in the Middle East. The EUR/USD pair also showed modest gains as the USD softened. While US data remained solid, market participants remained cautious about potential long-term impacts from the US-Israel war. Technical levels for gold indicate a resistance point near $1,820, with support at $1,780. The outlook for gold remains sensitive to both geopolitical risk and USD fluctuations.
