The US Dollar is gaining ground as the DXY hovers near 99.50, bolstered by rising Treasury yields and persistent expectations of a hawkish Federal Reserve. Investors are recalibrating positions amid mixed risk sentiment, while surging oil prices amplify inflation worries, further underpinning the USD. The currency’s strength is influencing major pairs, keeping the EUR/USD under pressure and testing key technical support levels.  

In the Eurozone, disappointing PMI readings have weighed on the euro, prompting traders to favor the US Dollar in currency pair trading. Weak manufacturing and services data signal slower economic momentum, increasing market caution and reinforcing the appeal of USD-denominated assets. The EUR/USD exchange rate remains pressured, with sentiment leaning toward a continuation of recent downtrends.  

Market watchers note that elevated oil costs are contributing to broader inflationary concerns, likely impacting central bank policy expectations across major economies. The interplay of firm USD momentum, soft Eurozone data, and commodity-driven inflation suggests continued volatility in forex trading, with investors closely monitoring Treasury yields and Fed communications for clues on the next directional move in key currency pairs.
