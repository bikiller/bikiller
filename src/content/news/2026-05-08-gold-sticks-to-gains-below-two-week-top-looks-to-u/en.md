Gold (XAU/USD) remained under pressure on Friday, holding gains but staying below a two-week peak as traders awaited the US Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report for fresh direction. The currency pair edged higher in early European trading but failed to sustain momentum, with the USD showing resilience amid mixed risk sentiment. Investors are eyeing technical levels near the recent high for clues on potential breakouts.  

The precious metal's exchange rate hovered around $1,988 per ounce, reflecting cautious positioning ahead of key economic data. Market participants are weighing the impact of a stronger USD and higher Treasury yields, which could weigh on gold’s appeal. EUR/USD and other major currency pairs also traded cautiously, with volatility expected to rise post-NFP, influencing cross-asset flows and short-term trading strategies.  

Analysts suggest that a stronger-than-expected US jobs report could push the USD higher, pressuring XAU/USD and capping upside, while weaker data may reignite gold buying as a hedge against inflation. Traders continue to monitor both technical support near $1,975 and resistance around $2,000, with the upcoming NFP data likely to dictate market direction and short-term trading dynamics across currencies and commodities.
