The USD/TRY currency pair remains in focus after Commerzbank analyst Tatha Ghose signaled that the Central Bank of Turkey is likely to keep interest rates unchanged in its upcoming decision. The pause, however, is viewed as tactical rather than a shift in monetary policy direction. In the forex market, such a move could stabilize short-term trading in the Turkish lira exchange rate against the USD, even as investors remain cautious.

According to the bank’s assessment, policymakers may be opting for a temporary hold to evaluate the impact of prior tightening measures on inflation and financial conditions. While the currency pair has experienced volatile trading in recent months, maintaining current rates could offer brief support to the TRY. Still, market participants in USD and EUR trading continue to watch inflation expectations closely.

The broader implication for the USD/TRY exchange rate is that underlying risks tied to Turkey’s inflation outlook have not disappeared. If price pressures persist, the central bank may be forced to resume tightening later, potentially influencing capital flows and currency trading sentiment. For forex traders, the pause may therefore represent only a short-term stabilization phase rather than a lasting shift in policy trajectory.
