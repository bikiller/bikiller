Mexico’s economic growth is forecast to remain below potential in 2026, according to Societe Generale’s Dev Ashish. Weak manufacturing output, limited investment, and ongoing uncertainty tied to the USMCA are expected to constrain the country’s expansion. As the USD/MXN pair continues to face pressure, these factors may drag the exchange rate lower as traders adjust their expectations for the peso’s performance.

Rising oil prices could further complicate the outlook, adding inflationary risks that are likely to affect monetary policy. With the potential for higher inflation, USD/MXN could experience heightened volatility. Inflation concerns are also expected to affect the broader market sentiment, as traders look closely at the USD/EUR pair and other commodity-linked currencies for signs of further market adjustments.
