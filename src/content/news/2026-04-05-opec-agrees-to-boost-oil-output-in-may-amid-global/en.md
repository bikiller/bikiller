OPEC+ has agreed to increase oil production quotas by 206,000 barrels per day (bps) in May, a move aimed at addressing the ongoing global energy crisis. The decision, announced on Sunday, comes as oil prices continue to face upward pressure amid concerns over supply disruptions. This increase could influence key currency pairs like USD/JPY and EUR/USD, as oil price fluctuations often impact global inflation and monetary policy expectations, especially in the US.

The news sent the oil market into a volatile phase, with crude prices experiencing a slight dip in the short-term as investors digested the potential for greater supply. However, analysts expect that OPEC+’s decision could soften some of the bullish momentum for the USD, as oil-producing nations could see a boost in revenues. Traders will closely monitor the USD’s performance against emerging market currencies, as any further moves from OPEC+ could impact global inflation expectations and interest rate decisions by the Federal Reserve.
