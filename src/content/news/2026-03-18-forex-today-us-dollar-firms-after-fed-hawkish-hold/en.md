The US Dollar gained momentum after the Federal Reserve maintained a hawkish stance in its latest policy meeting, signaling continued interest rate hikes. The greenback strengthened, with the EUR/USD pair dipping to 1.0600, marking a 0.45% drop. The Fed's hold on rates bolstered investor confidence in the USD, particularly as traders priced in further tightening through the end of the year.

The USD/JPY surged to 134.25, benefiting from the Fed's hawkish outlook and rising US Treasury yields. In contrast, the GBP/USD weakened to 1.2125, down 0.40%, as market participants digested the Fed's commitment to higher rates. The broader dollar rally suggests that the greenback may continue to outperform its peers, with key technical levels for EUR/USD at 1.0600 and 1.0550.
