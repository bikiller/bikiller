The Indian Rupee (INR) reversed its four-day rally against the US Dollar (USD) on Monday, with the USD/INR currency pair opening lower near 92.80. The slight dip came as the US Dollar's momentum paused amid growing expectations of a potential reopening of the Strait of Hormuz, a key shipping route for global oil.

Market sentiment shifted, causing the USD/INR exchange rate to face downward pressure. Traders are closely monitoring the situation, as the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz could ease global oil supply concerns, influencing risk sentiment. As a result, the Indian Rupee temporarily gained ground, breaking its recent winning streak against the US Dollar.
