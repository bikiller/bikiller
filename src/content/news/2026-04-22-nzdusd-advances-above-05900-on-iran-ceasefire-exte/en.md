The NZD/USD currency pair pushed above the 0.5900 mark, reaching around 0.5915 in early European trading on Wednesday, as the New Zealand Dollar strengthened against the USD. Market sentiment improved after US President Donald Trump confirmed an extension of the Iran ceasefire, reportedly at Pakistan’s request, easing geopolitical tensions and supporting risk-sensitive currencies.

In addition to the softer US Dollar, the Kiwi gained traction following stronger-than-expected New Zealand inflation data, which reinforced expectations of tighter monetary policy. The firmer inflation outlook has encouraged bullish positioning in the exchange rate, with traders reassessing interest rate differentials between the Reserve Bank of New Zealand and the Federal Reserve.

From a trading perspective, the move above 0.5900 signals near-term upside momentum for NZD/USD, with resistance seen near the 0.5920–0.5950 region. Continued improvement in risk appetite and macroeconomic data could further support the pair, while any rebound in the USD or shifts in global sentiment may cap gains.
