The US Dollar continues to show strength, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering near 99.50. Traders responded positively to the May ISM Services PMI, which climbed to 54.5 from April’s 53.6, signaling resilient growth in the US service sector. The upbeat reading bolstered Treasury yields, providing support for USD trading and lifting sentiment across major currency pairs against the greenback.  

In forex markets, the USD is outperforming the EUR, JPY, and other currencies, as investors recalibrate positions ahead of the Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) report. The EUR/USD exchange rate remains pressured, while USD/JPY and USD/GBP are testing key technical levels, reflecting persistent demand for the dollar amid stronger economic indicators. Traders are eyeing further market reaction depending on NFP outcomes and potential Fed policy signals.  

Market participants are closely monitoring how the robust ISM data may influence the broader USD trend. Stronger services growth often translates to expectations of sustained economic activity, which can keep the dollar supported versus risk-sensitive currencies. As trading unfolds, volatility in currency pairs is expected around the NFP release, making timing and positioning critical for forex traders navigating the current USD momentum.
