Singapore Dollar trading remained range-bound as USD/SGD closed at 1.2960 following a volatile session. The currency pair saw intraday swings between 1.2938 and 1.2975, reflecting choppy but contained price action. Despite brief fluctuations, the exchange rate stayed near recent lows against the US dollar, underscoring a broadly sideways forex market environment during Asian trading hours.

United Overseas Bank analyst Quek Ser Leang noted that the near-term outlook continues to favor consolidation. He expects USD/SGD to remain confined within a tight trading band, projecting a narrow range of 1.2935–1.2975 for the coming day. This suggests limited directional momentum, with the currency pair likely maintaining a persistent range bias rather than developing a sustained trend.

For forex traders, the compressed range highlights a market leaning toward short-term range trading strategies rather than breakout positioning. Key support is seen near 1.2935–1.2940, while resistance sits around 1.2975. Overall, USD/SGD price action reflects subdued volatility, with balanced flows between the US dollar and Singapore dollar keeping trading conditions stable.
