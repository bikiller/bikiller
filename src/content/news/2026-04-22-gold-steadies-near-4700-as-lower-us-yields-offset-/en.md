Gold (XAU/USD) held steady near $4,700 on Wednesday, recovering slightly after Tuesday’s sharp 2% drop. Traders weighed geopolitical uncertainty in the Middle East, particularly around Iran, against declining US Treasury yields, which bolstered gold’s appeal as a non-yielding safe-haven asset. The precious metal’s stabilization comes amid mixed signals for the broader USD, with currency pairs like EUR/USD seeing modest swings in early trading.  

Market participants cited the softer US yields as a key driver supporting gold prices. Lower benchmark rates reduce the opportunity cost of holding non-interest-bearing assets, making XAU/USD more attractive. Analysts note that continued tension in the Middle East could add volatility, potentially pushing the exchange rate higher, while any resolution might temper demand. Forex traders remain alert to shifts in USD strength, which could influence both gold and other major currencies like GBP and JPY.  

Technically, gold is testing support around $4,700, with resistance levels eyed near $4,750. A sustained rebound could encourage short-term trading in XAU/USD, while a dip below support may trigger further USD-linked selling. Market watchers emphasize that the interplay between geopolitical developments, Treasury yields, and USD movements will continue to shape gold’s trajectory in the coming sessions.
