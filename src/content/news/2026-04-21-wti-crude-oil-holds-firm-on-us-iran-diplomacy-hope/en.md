West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil is holding steady at approximately $98.25, reflecting a 0.21% increase as of Tuesday. The market's resilience is largely attributed to ongoing diplomatic discussions between the U.S. and Iran, which have raised hopes for a more stable oil supply in the region. Traders are closely monitoring these developments as they directly impact global oil prices and, consequently, related currency pairs.

Concerns surrounding potential supply disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz are also contributing to the market's current sentiment. As the waterway is a critical passage for oil shipments, any threats to its security could lead to significant fluctuations in the exchange rates of currencies like the USD and EUR. The interplay between geopolitical events and oil prices continues to shape trading strategies, keeping investors vigilant in their assessments of market dynamics.
