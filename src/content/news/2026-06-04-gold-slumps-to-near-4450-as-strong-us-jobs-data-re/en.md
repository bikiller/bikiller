Gold prices retreated to around $4,450 per ounce during Thursday’s early Asian trading session, extending losses as stronger-than-expected US labor market data reinforced expectations that the Federal Reserve could maintain a hawkish policy stance and potentially raise interest rates later this year. The decline in XAU/USD reflects growing confidence in the US economy, which has supported the USD and pushed Treasury yields higher, reducing the appeal of non-yielding assets such as gold.

Market participants reacted to the robust employment figures by increasing bets on tighter monetary policy, a development that strengthened the US Dollar against major counterparts. The firmer USD weighed on precious metals and influenced broader forex trading activity, with investors closely monitoring key currency pair movements, including EUR/USD and USD-related crosses. A stronger exchange rate for the greenback typically makes gold more expensive for holders of other currencies, limiting demand.

The latest pullback highlights the sensitivity of gold markets to shifts in interest-rate expectations and macroeconomic data. Traders are now assessing whether upcoming US economic releases will further support the Fed’s higher-rate outlook or prompt a reassessment of policy expectations. In the near term, sustained USD strength and elevated rate forecasts could keep pressure on XAU/USD, while any signs of slowing economic momentum may help the precious metal stabilize and recover from current levels near $4,450.
