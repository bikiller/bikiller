The Australian dollar has rebounded sharply against the US dollar, testing resistance levels after recent gains. Analysts at United Overseas Bank, Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann, note that while AUD/USD showed strong upward momentum, the pace has left near-term trading direction uncertain. They suggest the currency pair is likely to oscillate between 0.7120 and 0.7175 as traders weigh broader USD movements and global risk sentiment.

This rebound comes amid mixed signals from the US dollar, with the greenback showing relative strength against other major currencies like the EUR and JPY. Market participants are closely watching economic indicators, central bank guidance, and commodity-linked flows that typically influence AUD trading. The narrowing range indicates that, although bullish sentiment remains, caution is prevailing among short-term traders.

Traders are advised to monitor technical resistance and support levels, as the exchange rate could fluctuate within the identified band before breaking decisively. Volatility in AUD/USD may continue, reflecting reactions to US macro data, global equity trends, and commodity price swings. Strategically, investors may look for breakout opportunities above 0.7175 or a pullback toward 0.7120 to guide positioning in currency and forex portfolios.
