Silver prices retreated below the $84.00 mark as traders adjusted positions following strong US retail sales data. The XAG/USD currency pair slid to $83.53, shedding over 4.40% on the day, as market participants booked profits after failed attempts to breach the $90.00 resistance. The US dollar’s rebound added further pressure, pushing silver toward a critical support level.

Market activity reflects cautious trading sentiment, with investors weighing the US economic outlook against currency fluctuations. The USD’s recovery against major currencies like the EUR has amplified selling pressure on silver, limiting bullish momentum. Technical observers note that while XAG/USD remains above key support, sustained gains will depend on the dollar’s trajectory and broader risk appetite in forex markets.

Analysts suggest that despite the pullback, silver retains its longer-term bullish potential, provided it holds above the $83.50–$84.00 range. Traders are closely monitoring exchange rate movements and macroeconomic indicators, recognizing that silver’s sensitivity to USD strength could influence short-term trading strategies. Overall, the currency pair is navigating a delicate balance between profit-taking and potential upside.
