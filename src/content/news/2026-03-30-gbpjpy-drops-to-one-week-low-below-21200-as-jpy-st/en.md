GBP/JPY fell to a one-week low, dipping below the 212.00 mark as the Japanese yen gained strength. The currency pair had shown a slight uptick to the 213.00 region during Monday's Asian session but reversed course in the final hour of trading. The decline marks the third consecutive day of losses for GBP/JPY, driven by concerns over potential intervention in the yen market.

The yen's strength comes amid ongoing warnings from Japan's authorities about possible market intervention to curb excessive currency depreciation. This intervention risk has added volatility to the forex market, particularly affecting currency pairs like GBP/JPY. Traders are now closely watching the 212.00 level for signs of further downside, while the broader market remains cautious of shifts in central bank policy and global risk sentiment.
