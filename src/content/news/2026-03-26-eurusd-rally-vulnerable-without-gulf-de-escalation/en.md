EUR/USD has retreated below the 1.16 level despite a generally positive risk environment, reflecting a partial reversal of the earlier rally sparked by the European Central Bank’s unexpected hawkish tone. According to ING’s Francesco Pesole, the currency pair is showing signs of vulnerability, with traders dialing back expectations for an April ECB rate hike. The pair’s trading remains sensitive to broader market sentiment and geopolitical developments in the Gulf.  

Oil price movements are increasingly influencing the exchange rate, as markets adjust ECB expectations in line with energy-driven inflation pressures. The initial push above 1.16 earlier this month has given way to cautious positioning, with traders weighing both central bank signals and external risk factors. This dynamic underscores the challenge for EUR/USD to sustain gains without a clear resolution to global tensions.  

Looking ahead, the currency pair’s trajectory will hinge on risk sentiment and macroeconomic signals from Europe and the U.S. A de-escalation in the Gulf could provide support for further upside, while persistent uncertainty may keep EUR/USD capped near current levels. Traders are closely monitoring technical thresholds, central bank cues, and commodity-linked inflation, all of which will shape short-term trading strategies for the euro against the dollar.
