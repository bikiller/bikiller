USD/CHF climbed modestly on Monday, advancing about 0.15% as the US Dollar regained ground from its intraday lows. The currency pair traded around 0.7854, reflecting improved risk sentiment during the North American session. Traders showed renewed interest in USD positions, pushing the pair closer to its 100-day simple moving average, a key technical level signaling potential near-term resistance.  

Market participants cited easing risk aversion and a rebound in US equity futures as drivers of the USD’s strength. The modest uptick in the exchange rate came despite a broadly mixed performance in other major currencies, including EUR and GBP, highlighting a selective USD recovery. Analysts noted that momentum around the 100-day SMA could influence short-term trading strategies, as investors weigh further upside against technical resistance.  

Looking ahead, USD/CHF may face resistance near the 100-day SMA, where profit-taking and renewed CHF buying could temper advances. Continued monitoring of risk appetite, US economic cues, and European market trends will be crucial for traders positioning in the pair. For now, the exchange rate reflects a cautious USD rebound, offering insight into broader market sentiment and the interplay between major currency pairs in a volatile trading environment.
