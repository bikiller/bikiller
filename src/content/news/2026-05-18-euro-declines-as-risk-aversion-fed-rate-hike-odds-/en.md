The euro continued to slide against the US dollar, with the EUR/USD currency pair hovering around 1.1620 during Monday's Asian trading session. This marks the sixth consecutive day of subdued trading, as investors weigh growing concerns over global risk sentiment. The US dollar strengthened amid expectations that the Federal Reserve may adopt a more aggressive approach to controlling inflation, pushing the exchange rate lower.

Market participants are closely watching the potential impact of Fed policy shifts on capital flows. The uptick in USD demand reflects a flight to safety, as traders anticipate higher interest rates could make dollar-denominated assets more attractive. Technical levels near 1.1620 are being monitored, with a breach potentially opening the door for further EUR weakness.

Analysts suggest that continued risk aversion and tightening monetary policy could weigh on the euro in the near term. Currency traders are positioning for volatility, balancing the euro’s downside against potential rebounds if risk sentiment stabilizes. The interplay between USD strength and EUR pressure underscores the sensitivity of the forex market to both macroeconomic cues and central bank signals.
