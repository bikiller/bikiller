Gold (XAU/USD) extends losses on Wednesday, slipping further as the currency pair revisits the seven-month low reached earlier this month. The exchange rate remains under pressure as traders react to growing expectations of additional Federal Reserve rate hikes, which continue to support the US Dollar across forex markets.

Hawkish Federal Reserve rhetoric has reinforced demand for the USD, lifting yields and weighing on non-yielding assets like gold. In trading sessions, stronger dollar dynamics have reduced appetite for XAU/USD, with investors increasingly positioning around the prospect of prolonged restrictive monetary policy. Broader forex conditions reflect cautious sentiment as liquidity flows favor the greenback.

Market participants note that gold bears are now eyeing the $4,000 level as a potential downside target if USD strength persists. Technically, the metal’s inability to reclaim recent highs signals continued vulnerability in the exchange rate. In forex trading terms, sustained dollar dominance may keep XAU/USD capped, while broader risk sentiment remains fragile.
