Gold (XAU/USD) maintained a modest upside bias on Tuesday, hovering just below the $4,600 level as traders balanced geopolitical developments with macroeconomic expectations. The precious metal’s exchange rate against the USD found support amid renewed optimism that tensions in the Middle East may ease, reducing immediate safe-haven demand while still keeping risk sentiment fragile in broader forex trading.

At the same time, market participants remain focused on the interest rate outlook, particularly signals from the Federal Reserve that could influence USD strength and overall currency pair dynamics. A softer rate trajectory tends to weigh on the greenback, indirectly supporting gold prices, while any hawkish shift could cap gains. This push-and-pull keeps XAU/USD within a tight trading range.

Looking ahead, traders are closely monitoring both geopolitical headlines and incoming economic data for clearer direction. Sustained movement above the $4,600 resistance could signal further bullish momentum, while failure to break higher may prompt consolidation. The interplay between USD valuation, global risk appetite, and rate expectations will continue to shape gold’s near-term trading trajectory.
