TD Securities strategists argue that political dynamics surrounding the Federal Reserve, particularly the potential trajectory of Kevin Warsh toward the Fed chair, are increasingly shaping currency market expectations. Concerns over central bank independence are being seen as a key macro driver, with analysts suggesting these factors tilt risks toward a weaker USD outlook into 2026.

Markets are increasingly pricing in policy uncertainty spilling into currency pairs, with the USD facing renewed downside pressure against major peers. In FX trading, EUR/USD and other major exchange rates could benefit from shifting rate expectations and a perceived erosion of Fed credibility. Volatility may rise as positioning adjusts to a softer dollar bias across G10 currencies.

Looking ahead to 2026, traders may increasingly position for structural USD softness if Fed governance concerns persist. Carry trade dynamics could shift as interest rate differentials compress, while safe-haven flows into the dollar lose momentum. Overall, the foreign exchange market appears tilted toward selective USD selling rather than broad-based dollar strength.
