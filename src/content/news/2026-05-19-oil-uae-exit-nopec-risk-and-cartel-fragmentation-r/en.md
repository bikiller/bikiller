Oil markets are facing renewed pressure as the United Arab Emirates signals a potential exit from OPEC, raising concerns about cartel cohesion and the risk of broader fragmentation. Rabobank warns that such a move could structurally weigh on oil prices, prompting traders to reassess their positions in USD-denominated contracts. The EUR/USD pair is seeing increased volatility as investors weigh the impact on global inflation expectations and energy-linked currency flows.

This uncertainty is fueling active trading in both spot and futures markets. Brent crude, currently hovering around $86.50 per barrel, has seen intermittent pullbacks as momentum shifts. The USD index is benefiting from haven demand, testing key technical support near 104.20, while risk-sensitive pairs like AUD/USD and NZD/USD are under pressure due to commodity market jitters.

Market participants are watching for signs of OPEC cohesion, as a splintering cartel could open the door to higher output from non-OPEC countries and further soften crude benchmarks. Traders are increasingly factoring oil-driven inflation risks into central bank expectations, with GBP/USD and USD/JPY moving in tandem with oil price swings. Analysts suggest maintaining flexible positions, keeping an eye on key support and resistance levels across energy-linked currencies and commodity derivatives.
