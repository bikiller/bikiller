The Australian Dollar (AUD) is seeing a slight retreat after two consecutive days of gains, with the AUD/USD trading around 0.7060 during the Asian session on Tuesday. The currency pair is subdued following the release of China’s trade balance data, which has added pressure to the Aussie. This pullback marks a pause in its recent recovery against the US Dollar.

The data from China, a key trading partner for Australia, highlighted weaker-than-expected export growth, leading to concerns about the broader economic outlook. As a result, market sentiment turned cautious, weighing on the Australian Dollar. The pair's movement around the 0.7060 level suggests that the USD remains dominant in the short term, with traders eyeing potential support or resistance levels in the coming sessions.
