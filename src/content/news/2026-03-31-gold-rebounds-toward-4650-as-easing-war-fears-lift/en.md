Gold prices (XAU/USD) surged nearly 3% on Tuesday, climbing back toward the $4,650 level as market sentiment improved. The rebound comes amid easing geopolitical tensions, with Iranian President Masoud Pezeshkian suggesting that the country’s regime may be ready to bring an end to the ongoing conflict. This shift in rhetoric sparked increased demand for safe-haven assets like gold.

As global uncertainties subside, the gold rally has gained momentum, reversing recent losses. Traders are now closely monitoring the potential for further gains, with $4,650 emerging as a key resistance level. The recovery in XAU/USD also reflects broader market sentiment, with traders adjusting their positions in response to easing war fears and a potential shift in the region's political landscape.
