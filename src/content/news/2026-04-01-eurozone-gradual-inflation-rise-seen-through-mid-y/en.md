Eurozone inflation showed a moderate uptick in March, rising to 2.5% year-on-year, according to ABN AMRO’s Senior Economist Bill Diviney. The increase was largely driven by higher petrol and energy costs, while core inflation, which excludes volatile items, eased slightly to 2.3%. The shift highlights mounting cost pressures within the bloc, influencing expectations for the euro’s performance against major currencies like the USD.  

The euro (EUR/USD) has seen renewed volatility as traders digest the inflation data, with the exchange rate testing key technical levels near 1.0950. Rising energy prices could sustain upward inflation pressure, prompting market participants to reconsider ECB policy forecasts and their implications for currency trading strategies. Momentum in the euro may hinge on whether inflation continues to outpace expectations through the first half of the year.  

Analysts suggest the inflation trajectory could support cautious EUR buying in the near term, particularly versus the USD, as traders anticipate potential policy recalibrations. While the euro faces headwinds from core inflation’s moderation, energy-driven price pressures maintain a dynamic backdrop for forex markets, affecting both spot and derivative trading activity. Investors are closely monitoring these developments for clues on the euro’s medium-term exchange rate direction.
