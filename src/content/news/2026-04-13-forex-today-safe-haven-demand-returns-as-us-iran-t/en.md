Safe-haven demand surged on Monday as concerns over the collapse of US-Iran talks triggered heightened market uncertainty. The USD/JPY fell to 133.28, losing 0.6% as traders sought safety in the yen. Meanwhile, the EUR/USD remained steady at 1.0900, supported by the Eurozone’s resilient economic outlook. With the geopolitical risk factor intensifying, traders flocked to traditional safe-haven assets, including the Japanese yen and Swiss franc.

The breakdown in diplomatic negotiations between the US and Iran also impacted oil prices, which dropped by 2%, causing further ripples in global markets. As the risk sentiment shifted, the USD gained ground against commodity currencies like the AUD and NZD. The AUD/USD slumped to 0.6650, down by 0.8%, while the NZD/USD also saw a decline. With heightened volatility expected, traders are keeping a close eye on the evolving geopolitical situation and its potential impact on global forex markets.
