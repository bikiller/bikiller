USD/CHF remained largely unchanged for a second consecutive session, trading near 0.8100 during Asian hours on Monday. The currency pair showed muted volatility as the Swiss Franc steadied against the US Dollar, with overall forex trading conditions remaining subdued at the start of the week, reflecting low liquidity and cautious sentiment in markets.

USD stability was driven by cautious market sentiment after recent military clashes between the United States and Iran, which were followed by a temporary truce. The US Dollar remained relatively steady, limiting directional momentum in USD/CHF exchange rate trading. Safe-haven flows into the Swiss Franc were also restrained amid easing geopolitical tensions.

Market participants are now watching for further risk catalysts and central bank cues as USD/CHF continues to consolidate around the 0.8100 handle. In the absence of fresh drivers, the currency pair is expected to remain range-bound in short-term trading, with traders focusing on US data releases and broader USD sentiment.
