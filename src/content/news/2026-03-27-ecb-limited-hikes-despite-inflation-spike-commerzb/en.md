Commerzbank’s chief economist Jörg Krämer predicts that the European Central Bank will limit its response to war-driven energy inflation to at most a single additional rate hike. Inflation in the Eurozone is expected to peak above 3% by summer before gradually easing, while growth forecasts for 2026 have been lowered to 0.6%. These dynamics are shaping investor sentiment around the EUR/USD, with the euro under pressure against the dollar.  

Traders are closely watching the exchange rate as ECB guidance signals restrained tightening, leaving the euro vulnerable to USD strength. Market participants are weighing energy-driven price pressures against slower growth, influencing positioning in short-term trading and impacting cross-currency flows. Technical levels around 1.0850 in EUR/USD are key markers for potential support in the coming weeks.  

The limited hiking outlook could sustain volatility in the forex market, particularly for EUR pairs versus the greenback. EUR weakness may accelerate if inflation proves sticky, while USD demand remains tied to broader global risk sentiment. Currency traders are balancing central bank expectations, geopolitical risks, and technical signals, navigating a trading environment where modest policy moves can still trigger significant exchange rate swings.
