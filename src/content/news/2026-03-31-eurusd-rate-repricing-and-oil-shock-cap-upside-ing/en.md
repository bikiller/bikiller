EUR/USD has faced upward pressure as short-dated Euro swap rates eased off a sharp spike. However, real rate differentials have shifted unfavorably for the pair, with the euro underperforming against the USD. ING's Chris Turner suggests this could weigh on the EUR/USD exchange rate, particularly if the European Central Bank (ECB) opts not to raise rates in April while inflation expectations remain elevated.

This dynamic highlights concerns over the Euro's strength as the ECB's stance on interest rates diverges from the Federal Reserve's aggressive tightening. With inflationary pressures in the Eurozone still high, a lack of action from the ECB could push the EUR/USD further lower, especially if market sentiment continues to favor the USD amid ongoing global economic uncertainties.
