Risk sentiment remained fragile in Tuesday trading after Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) threatened retaliation against the United States following overnight strikes on southern Iran, which Washington described as an act of self-defence. The escalation revived concerns about broader regional instability, prompting traders to closely monitor safe-haven flows into the USD. Currency pair movements were driven more by geopolitical headlines than economic data, with investors reassessing near-term exchange rate expectations across major markets.

The IRGC also stated that 25 vessels, including oil tankers, passed through the Strait of Hormuz during the last day and night, underscoring the strategic importance of one of the world’s key energy shipping routes. Any threat to maritime traffic could fuel concerns over energy supply disruptions and higher oil prices. Such developments typically influence trading conditions across foreign exchange markets, particularly for currencies sensitive to commodity prices and global risk appetite.

For forex participants, the latest tensions add another layer of uncertainty to market positioning. Demand for defensive assets may continue to support the USD against major counterparts, while EUR and other risk-linked currencies could face periods of volatility if the situation deteriorates further. Traders are likely to remain focused on geopolitical developments, as shifts in risk sentiment could quickly affect currency pair performance and broader exchange rate trends.
