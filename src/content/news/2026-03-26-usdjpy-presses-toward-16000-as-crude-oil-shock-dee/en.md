The USD/JPY exchange rate continued its upward trajectory on Thursday, rising 0.1% to around 159.70. The pair held just below the session high of 159.85, extending its strong rally from early March. Since hitting lows near 152.10 earlier this month, the currency pair has surged by approximately 770 pips, reflecting the broader trend of yen weakness.

The yen’s decline is being exacerbated by the ongoing crude oil shock, which is driving higher energy prices and weighing on Japan's economic outlook. With oil prices remaining elevated, traders are increasingly cautious about the impact on Japan's import costs, further pressuring the yen. As USD/JPY presses towards the 160.00 mark, analysts are watching for potential resistance levels and further implications for both the yen and broader markets.
