Nordea research indicates EUR/USD upside remains limited in coming months, as currency pair continues to be driven by widening interest rate differentials and divergent monetary policy between the European Central Bank and the Federal Reserve. Trading conditions still favor the US dollar, with USD strength supported by relatively higher yields, keeping euro exchange rate recovery constrained.

The ECB is viewed as closer to the end of its tightening cycle, while the Fed maintains a more restrictive stance for longer. This policy gap reinforces dollar advantage in forex markets. At the same time, Eurozone economic data has been softer compared with resilient US indicators, further weighing on EUR demand and limiting sustained euro appreciation.

For traders, this suggests EUR/USD may remain range-bound with a downward bias, especially if macro data continues to diverge. Nordea highlights that interest rate spreads and growth differentials are likely to cap rallies, making the euro vulnerable against USD in short-term trading strategies across the currency pair in coming months outlook.
