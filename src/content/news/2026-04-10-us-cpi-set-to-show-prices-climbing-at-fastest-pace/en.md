The US dollar is poised for volatility as the Bureau of Labor Statistics prepares to release March’s Consumer Price Index (CPI) on Friday, with forecasts suggesting the fastest price growth in nearly two years. Analysts point to a surge in oil prices as the main driver behind the anticipated inflation spike, adding pressure on the USD. Currency traders are closely watching the EUR/USD and USD/JPY pairs for signs of market reaction, with the dollar likely to strengthen if headline CPI beats expectations.  

Oil’s rebound has fed through energy costs, intensifying inflationary pressures and influencing interest rate expectations. Market participants anticipate that rising consumer prices could prompt the Federal Reserve to signal a more hawkish stance, boosting USD trading against major currencies. Technical levels around 1.0900 in EUR/USD and 134.50 in USD/JPY are eyed as key markers for short-term movements, with momentum indicators suggesting a possible dollar-led correction.  

Traders are positioning for potential swings, balancing energy-driven inflation concerns with broader economic signals. A stronger-than-expected CPI could push the dollar higher, testing resistance levels in global currency markets, while a softer reading may ease pressure on EUR and JPY. Volatility in USD trading is likely to increase as market participants adjust strategies, closely monitoring exchange rates, futures, and cross-currency spreads ahead of Friday’s data release.
