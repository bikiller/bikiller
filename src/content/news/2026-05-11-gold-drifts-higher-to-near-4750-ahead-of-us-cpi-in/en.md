Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher toward $4,750 in early Asian trading on Tuesday, maintaining a cautious positive tone as investors weighed developments in US-Iran diplomacy. The precious metal’s modest gains come amid a generally firm dollar, with the USD index holding steady, as traders positioned themselves ahead of a crucial US CPI inflation release scheduled later in the day. Exchange rate movements in major pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD have also reflected cautious sentiment.  

Market participants are closely monitoring the potential impact of US inflation data on both monetary policy expectations and safe-haven flows. A stronger-than-expected CPI print could support the USD and put downward pressure on gold, while softer inflation may lift the metal further as investors seek shelter from currency volatility. Traders noted that XAU/USD has found short-term support near $4,720, with resistance around $4,760 shaping intraday positioning.  

In the broader forex market, the trading session has been marked by muted risk appetite, with key currency pairs showing limited directional momentum ahead of the inflation announcement. Analysts suggest that volatility could pick up sharply post-release, influencing not only gold but also USD-related pairs such as USD/JPY and EUR/USD. For now, gold’s upward drift underscores its continued role as a hedge amid geopolitical and economic uncertainties.
