Gold (XAU/USD) found stability on Monday after opening the week with a bearish gap, as the US-Iran conflict continues to influence global markets. The precious metal's price dropped sharply at the start of the session, reflecting heightened geopolitical tensions. However, the market has since steadied, with gold trading at around $1,915 per ounce as investors weigh the potential implications of the ongoing crisis on global financial stability.

Volatility remains high across various asset classes, with traders closely monitoring developments in the US-Iran situation. The elevated risk sentiment has contributed to fluctuations in gold’s price action, as investors seek safe-haven assets amidst uncertainty. With the USD showing mixed performance against major currencies like the EUR, the market will likely continue to react to any new headlines, making gold’s short-term outlook highly sensitive to geopolitical shifts.
