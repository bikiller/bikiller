Oil prices remain volatile due to a mix of conflicting supply factors, according to Bob Savage from BNY. Initially, the U.S. escort plans in the Strait of Hormuz led to a decline in oil prices. However, this downturn was short-lived as renewed attacks in the region pushed prices higher, reflecting the ongoing geopolitical risks that continue to shape the market. The oil market’s swings are having a notable impact on forex trading, particularly for USD-based pairs.

The price fluctuations in oil also influence currency pairs like USD/EUR, where the dollar tends to gain during times of rising oil prices due to its correlation with the energy sector. As oil prices climb, traders watch for key levels around $70 and $75 per barrel, knowing these thresholds often affect global inflation expectations and central bank policies. The energy market remains one of the key drivers for USD volatility, with broader implications for global exchange rates and market sentiment.
