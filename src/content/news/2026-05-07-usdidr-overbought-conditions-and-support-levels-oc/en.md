USD/IDR has begun to ease from recent overbought levels, reflecting improved market sentiment amid growing optimism over a potential US–Iran deal. Strategists at OCBC, Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, note that this optimism has eased upward pressure on the Rupiah, while trading activity in the USD/IDR pair remains closely watched by investors seeking short-term opportunities in the currency markets.  

Bank Indonesia’s recent tightening of rules on cash FX purchases has added a stabilizing effect, complementing comments from the central bank that the Rupiah is undervalued. These measures aim to curb excessive volatility and support the IDR against sudden swings in the USD/IDR exchange rate, providing traders with clearer technical levels to monitor.  

Overall, the USD/IDR currency pair appears positioned for a period of consolidation, with support levels reinforced by BI’s policy stance and the broader geopolitical outlook. Market participants remain alert to shifts in global risk sentiment, as developments in US–Iran relations and regional economic data could influence trading momentum and exchange rate fluctuations in both USD and other major currencies like EUR.
