The USD/CAD currency pair extended its gains on Tuesday, climbing to fresh two-month highs around 1.3765. The US Dollar maintained its resilience, recovering part of the previous day’s losses, even as US manufacturing PMI data showed a softer reading than expected. Traders remain focused on the Greenback’s broad strength, which continues to weigh on the Canadian Dollar amid mixed economic signals.  

Market participants note that USD demand remains underpinned by expectations of steady monetary policy from the Federal Reserve, keeping the exchange rate elevated against major counterparts including the EUR and CAD. Technical charts show the pair holding above recent support levels, suggesting that short-term momentum favors further USD strength.  

The rise in USD/CAD reflects ongoing caution among traders around global economic growth and commodity-linked currencies. With the Canadian Dollar sensitive to oil prices and US economic indicators, any further data releases could amplify volatility. Investors are monitoring both PMI updates and central bank signals for clues on the near-term trajectory of the currency pair.
