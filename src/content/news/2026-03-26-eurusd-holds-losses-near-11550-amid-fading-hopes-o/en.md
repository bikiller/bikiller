The EUR/USD currency pair continues to hover near 1.1550, holding onto losses recorded over the past two sessions. Trading activity remains subdued as the euro struggles to regain momentum against the US dollar. Investors appear cautious amid a backdrop of fading optimism over a potential US-Iran peace agreement, which had earlier supported risk-sensitive currencies like the euro. Market participants are closely watching the exchange rate for signs of a reversal or further consolidation.  

Technical indicators suggest limited upside for EUR/USD in the short term, with immediate resistance around 1.1570 and support near the 1.1530 mark. Traders are factoring in the dollar’s firm stance, underpinned by resilient US economic data and expectations of ongoing Federal Reserve policy support. This dynamic has kept the euro under pressure, restricting its ability to mount a sustained recovery in the forex markets.  

The subdued trading in EUR/USD reflects broader market caution, with geopolitical uncertainties weighing on sentiment. Any shift in US-Iran diplomatic prospects could trigger sharp moves, while the pair’s current range indicates a consolidation phase. Forex traders are likely to remain reactive, adjusting positions as new economic data and political developments influence the balance between the EUR and USD.
