The US Dollar saw a strong rebound as the ongoing conflict between the US, Israel, and Iran sparked significant volatility in oil prices and increased demand for safe-haven assets. Iran's military raised concerns, suggesting that oil could surge to $200 per barrel, adding further pressure on energy markets. This led to a rise in the USD, with the EUR/USD dropping to a fresh low near 1.0600. Oil volatility, compounded by Iran’s closure of the Strait of Hormuz and attacks on vessels, heightened investor fears.

The US Dollar’s strength was driven by risk aversion, pushing the USD/JPY higher and supporting the Greenback against other major currencies. As the situation escalates, investors flocked to the USD, traditionally viewed as a safe-haven in times of geopolitical tension. The EUR/USD remains vulnerable to further declines, especially if oil prices continue their upward trajectory, potentially testing resistance levels around 1.0500.
