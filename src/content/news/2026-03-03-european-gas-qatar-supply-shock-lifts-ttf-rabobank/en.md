The price of Dutch TTF gas surged as QatarEnergy's halt in LNG production triggered fresh concerns over European energy security. The market reacted sharply to the news, with prices rising to €70 per MWh, a significant jump. The disruption has intensified fears about Europe's reliance on Middle Eastern LNG, especially as gas supply stability is now in question. 

As the supply shock unfolds, the EUR/USD pair saw increased volatility, reflecting market unease. The euro weakened briefly, dropping below the 1.0700 level against the USD. Traders are closely monitoring the situation, with the potential for further market turbulence should the crisis worsen. This development underscores the vulnerability of the eurozone’s energy infrastructure, influencing broader economic sentiment and currency movements in global forex trading.
