Gold (XAU/USD) remained under pressure Friday, struggling to reclaim momentum after yesterday’s unsuccessful bid to surpass the $4,800 level. Despite the slight uptick in the USD, the precious metal’s trading lacked clear bearish conviction, with prices lingering within a familiar range through the Asian session. Market participants appear cautious ahead of the upcoming US CPI report, weighing the potential impact on the currency pair and broader commodity markets.  

Investors are watching the USD closely as minor gains in the greenback have pressured gold, while safe-haven flows remain subdued. The exchange rate dynamics between gold and major currencies such as EUR and USD are influencing traders’ positioning, keeping XAU/USD anchored near recent support levels. Analysts note that without a decisive move above $4,800, gold may continue to trade sideways, reflecting a balance between cautious optimism and risk-off sentiment.  

Technical indicators suggest consolidation, with bulls hesitant to challenge resistance and bears reluctant to push prices sharply lower. The upcoming CPI release could serve as a catalyst, potentially altering trading sentiment across forex and commodity markets. For now, gold remains range-bound, with traders monitoring both USD strength and inflation expectations, as the XAU/USD pair navigates a delicate equilibrium between speculative demand and fundamental drivers.
