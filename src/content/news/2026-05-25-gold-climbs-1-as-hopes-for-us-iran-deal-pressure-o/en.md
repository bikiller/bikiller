Gold (XAU/USD) experienced a significant rebound on Monday, climbing 1.40% to trade at approximately $4,572. This surge comes amid growing optimism surrounding a potential US-Iran deal aimed at resolving the ongoing conflict in the Middle East. As hopes for stability increase, market participants have reacted by pushing down the US Dollar (USD) and oil prices, creating a more favorable environment for gold trading.

The prospect of reopening the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial shipping lane, further contributed to the decline in oil prices and the USD's strength. As traders recalibrate their strategies in response to these geopolitical developments, the gold market is seeing heightened activity. The shift in sentiment could have lasting implications for the exchange rates of major currency pairs, particularly as investors look for safe-haven assets in times of uncertainty.
