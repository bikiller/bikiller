Japanese Yen remains under sustained pressure in forex trading, hovering near multi-decade weakness and testing levels close to 40-year lows against major currencies. The USD/JPY currency pair climbed to 161.81, marking a nearly two-year low for the yen and the highest level since July 2024’s peak at 161.95, reflecting persistent dollar strength in the exchange rate market.

Sentiment in the currency market is driven by diverging monetary policy expectations, as a hawkish Federal Reserve continues to support US dollar demand. Rising US Treasury yields have further widened rate differentials, intensifying selling pressure on the Japanese yen across forex trading sessions. The exchange rate reaction highlights reduced appetite for yen-denominated assets amid higher US returns.

Market participants increasingly watch for potential intervention from Japanese authorities as the currency approaches sensitive thresholds, with policymakers wary of excessive volatility in USD/JPY. Traders remain cautious in positioning, as any sudden policy action could trigger sharp reversals in forex trading flows and increase short-term volatility across major currency pairs.
