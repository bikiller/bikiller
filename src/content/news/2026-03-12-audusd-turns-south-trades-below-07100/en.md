The AUD/USD currency pair retreated on Thursday, slipping back below the 0.7100 level after failing to sustain momentum near Wednesday’s multi-year high of 0.7186. During the Asian and early European trading sessions, the exchange rate turned lower as the US Dollar strengthened broadly across forex markets. The pullback reflects a shift in market sentiment, with traders moving away from risk-sensitive currencies such as the Australian Dollar.

Demand for the USD increased sharply as geopolitical tensions escalated in the Middle East, triggering a classic safe-haven bid for the Greenback. Rising uncertainty across global markets also lifted Oil prices, reinforcing defensive positioning among investors. The stronger USD pressured several major currency pairs, including AUD/USD, as capital flowed toward perceived safety in the US currency.

From a trading perspective, the move below 0.7100 signals fading bullish momentum after the pair’s recent rally toward 0.7186. Forex traders are now watching whether the exchange rate can stabilize above key support zones or extend the correction further. Continued geopolitical risk and shifts in commodity prices may remain key drivers for the AUD/USD currency pair and broader USD performance in the near term.
