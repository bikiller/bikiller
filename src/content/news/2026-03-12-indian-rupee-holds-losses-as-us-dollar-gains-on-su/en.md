The Indian Rupee (INR) extended its losses as the US Dollar (USD) continued its climb, supported by rising energy prices. The USD/INR currency pair marked its third consecutive session of gains on Thursday, with the exchange rate reaching 83.00. The ongoing surge in oil prices has strengthened the USD, boosting investor demand for the currency as a safe haven. This trend has placed additional pressure on the Rupee, contributing to its weakened position in the market.

As energy costs rise globally, the Indian currency faces continued challenges, especially with the INR's vulnerability to external factors like commodity prices. Traders are keeping a close eye on USD/INR for potential resistance at the 83.10 level, which could signal further momentum for the US Dollar. Market sentiment remains firmly in favor of the USD as the Federal Reserve maintains its hawkish stance, making it increasingly difficult for the INR to recover in the near term.
