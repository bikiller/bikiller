USD/MYR is approaching a critical support zone as the US Dollar softens amid rising optimism over US–Iran negotiations. According to OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, the currency pair has seen increased trading activity, with market participants recalibrating positions in response to geopolitical developments and a broader dip in USD sentiment. The ringgit is testing technical levels that could define near-term momentum.  

Traders note that the exchange rate’s movement is influenced by both external and domestic factors. While USD weakness provides relief for emerging market currencies, any reversal in sentiment could quickly shift trading dynamics. The pair’s approach to key supports has heightened focus on short-term technical setups, with stop-loss and take-profit levels being closely monitored by forex traders looking to capitalize on volatility.  

Market implications are clear: a sustained breach of these supports could signal further ringgit strength against the USD, while a rebound in the greenback may trigger renewed selling pressure. The USD/MYR’s performance will also influence regional currency trends, including the EUR/MYR and other Asia-Pacific pairs, as investors weigh both geopolitical optimism and shifts in USD trading sentiment in positioning their portfolios.
