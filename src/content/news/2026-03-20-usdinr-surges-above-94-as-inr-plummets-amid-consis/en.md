The USD/INR currency pair surged above the 94 level on Friday as the Indian Rupee (INR) continued its downward spiral. This marks a significant drop for the INR, which has faced consistent pressure from foreign capital outflows, reflecting investor concerns. The US Dollar (USD) remained strong in the market, pushing the exchange rate higher as the INR failed to recover despite some expectations of stabilization.

The INR's fall is primarily driven by persistent foreign outflows and concerns over the domestic economic outlook. The ongoing strength of the USD, bolstered by higher interest rates in the U.S., has further exacerbated the pressure on the Indian currency. As trading resumes, the market will be closely watching the 94.20 resistance level, as a breach could push the USD/INR pair even higher, creating further challenges for the INR.
