USD/TWD has shown a steady consolidation around the 32.00 level, according to OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong. The currency pair has appreciated roughly 2.5% this month, reflecting measured trading activity compared with more volatile Asian counterparts like KRW, THB, and MYR. The exchange rate’s contained movement suggests traders are cautious amid mixed macro signals and moderate USD strength.  

Market participants note that the USD/TWD’s restrained rise contrasts with broader USD trends in the region, where heightened volatility has affected other currency pairs. The consolidation near 32.00 indicates limited momentum, with technical support and resistance levels holding firm. Traders are watching the interplay between domestic economic indicators and global USD dynamics, which could influence the exchange rate in the near term.  

Looking ahead, OCBC highlights that USD/TWD trading may continue within a tight range unless stronger drivers emerge. Factors such as shifts in US interest rate expectations, regional capital flows, and risk sentiment in Asia are likely to determine whether the pair breaks out of its current consolidation. For forex traders, this environment emphasizes measured positioning and attention to both USD and regional currency movements.
