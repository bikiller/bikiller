The USD received a temporary boost as markets reacted to a brief ceasefire between the US and Iran, easing geopolitical tensions. Rabobank strategists Michael Every and Bas van Geffen highlighted that this truce sharply reduced immediate risk premia, pushing Brent crude prices lower while equities climbed. Traders saw the currency pair movements reflect this sentiment, with the USD gaining modestly against major counterparts like the EUR and JPY.  

Despite the relief, strategists caution that uncertainty remains elevated for at least the next two weeks. Exchange rates are likely to stay sensitive to headlines, as any disruption in the fragile truce could trigger sharp reversals. In forex trading, the USD may experience heightened volatility, while safe-haven flows to JPY and CHF could resurface if tensions flare.  

Market participants are weighing short-term opportunities against ongoing risks, balancing equity optimism with oil market caution. The temporary lull in Brent pricing supports a slightly stronger USD, but technical levels in key currency pairs suggest traders remain cautious. Overall, the market reprieve offers a narrow window for trading, with the focus firmly on news-driven catalysts shaping exchange rate trends.
