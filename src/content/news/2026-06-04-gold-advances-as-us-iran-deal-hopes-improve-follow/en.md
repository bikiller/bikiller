Gold edged higher as geopolitical optimism lifted risk sentiment, with XAU/USD trading around $4,497, up 1.40% on the day. The US Dollar softened following news of a US-brokered ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon, easing safe-haven demand. Investors are closely watching currency pairs as renewed hopes for a US-Iran deal ripple through markets, influencing trading across major FX pairs including USD and EUR.  

The recent uptick in gold reflects broader shifts in exchange rates, as the weakening USD makes bullion more attractive for international buyers. Traders noted that support levels held firm, reinforcing bullish momentum, while resistance near $4,500 could test buying interest. FX markets responded swiftly, with heightened volatility in currency pairs reflecting cautious positioning ahead of further geopolitical developments.  

Analysts suggest that if tensions continue to ease, gold could maintain gains while the USD faces headwinds, influencing the wider forex landscape. EUR/USD and other major pairs may see increased activity as capital flows adjust, with traders balancing safe-haven strategies against higher-yield opportunities. Overall, the XAU/USD movement underscores the sensitivity of both metals and currencies to geopolitical catalysts and market sentiment shifts.
