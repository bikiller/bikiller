Gold (XAU/USD) slipped lower toward $4,100 during early Asian trading on Wednesday, extending its recent pullback in the precious metals market. The currency pair weakened as spot prices failed to hold gains from prior sessions, with exchange rate momentum turning negative amid cautious trading conditions across USD-linked assets.

Sentiment is being driven by rising inflation concerns and growing bets that the US Federal Reserve will raise interest rates later this year. Higher rate expectations typically support the USD and weigh on non-yielding assets like gold, intensifying selling pressure. Traders are reassessing risk across major forex pairs, including EUR/USD, as bond yields firm and dollar demand strengthens.

From a technical trading perspective, gold hovering near the $4,100 level signals a key short-term area of interest, with further downside possible if Fed rhetoric remains hawkish. However, any shift in inflation data could quickly reverse momentum. Volatility in USD-denominated assets is expected to persist, keeping gold sensitive to macroeconomic releases and central bank guidance.
