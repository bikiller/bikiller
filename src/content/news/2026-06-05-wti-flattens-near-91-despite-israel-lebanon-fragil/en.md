West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude futures held steady near $91.00 in early European trading on Friday, reflecting cautious sentiment despite ongoing geopolitical tensions. The fragile US-brokered ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon has yet to fully stabilize the region, as sporadic attacks continue to threaten supply expectations. Traders are weighing these developments against broader market fundamentals, keeping WTI in a narrow trading range.

The flat oil movement is coinciding with muted shifts in key currency pairs. The USD showed minor strength against the EUR and JPY as investors favored the dollar amid global uncertainty, while oil-linked currencies like the CAD remained range-bound. Market participants are monitoring whether crude’s technical support around $90.50 and resistance near $92.00 will hold, influencing both commodity-linked currencies and broader exchange rate trends.

Analysts note that sustained volatility in the Middle East could eventually spur sharper reactions in both energy and forex markets. A breakdown in the ceasefire might trigger higher WTI prices, prompting risk-on trading that could benefit commodity-linked pairs, while safe-haven flows could strengthen the USD. For now, the market remains in consolidation, signaling cautious positioning ahead of any definitive geopolitical shifts.
