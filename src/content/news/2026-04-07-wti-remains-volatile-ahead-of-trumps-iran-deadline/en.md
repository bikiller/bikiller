West Texas Intermediate (WTI) Crude Oil prices remain volatile as traders navigate a choppy market ahead of a critical deadline set by U.S. President Donald Trump. With Iran facing pressure to finalize a deal, WTI futures are trading within a narrow range. As of Tuesday, WTI stands at $72.50 per barrel, fluctuating between $71.80 and $73.10. The ongoing uncertainty around the Iran situation continues to fuel market caution, impacting global oil sentiment.

The U.S. Dollar (USD) is also reacting to crude price movements, with the EUR/USD exchange rate trading at 1.0950, reflecting the market's risk-off sentiment. Oil price fluctuations often influence the USD, with a stronger greenback generally pressuring commodity prices. Traders are keeping a close eye on any developments around the Iran deadline, as a resolution could significantly shift market dynamics, especially within energy and currency markets.
