The AUD/USD currency pair fell by 0.42% on Monday, closing near 0.6850 and marking its fifth consecutive day of losses. This drop extends the pair's decline from its year-to-date high of 0.7190, recorded in mid-March. Over the past week, the pace of selling has picked up, highlighting growing bearish pressure on the Australian dollar.

As the AUD/USD continues its downtrend, traders are eyeing key economic data releases scheduled for later this week, which could influence the pair's future movement. With over 300 pips lost from its March high, market participants are increasingly focused on the USD's strength and its potential impact on the broader forex market. The coming data could either bolster the USD further or trigger a correction in the current trend.
