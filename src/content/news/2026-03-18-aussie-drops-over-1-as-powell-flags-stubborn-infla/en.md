The Australian Dollar (AUD) dropped by over 1% against the US Dollar (USD) on Wednesday, with AUD/USD slipping roughly 1.15% to settle around 0.7025. After briefly pushing above 0.7120 early in the session, the currency pair faced renewed selling pressure, failing once again to break past its year-to-date high near 0.7190. 

The pullback came as Federal Reserve Chairman Jerome Powell highlighted persistent inflation risks, strengthening the case for continued tightening in US monetary policy. As a result, the USD gained momentum, reinforcing a bearish outlook for the AUD. Traders are now eyeing key support levels, with a potential test of the 0.7000 mark on the horizon.
