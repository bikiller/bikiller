GBP/USD remained steady above 1.32 on Monday, trading near 1.3240 as the market saw thin activity due to the UK’s Easter Monday holiday. The currency pair showed modest resilience, bouncing slightly from last week's low around 1.3180, which marked its weakest point since mid-March. Despite the bounce, the recovery has been limited, reflecting cautious sentiment in the market.

The lack of significant trading volume and market catalysts left GBP/USD in a narrow range. A weaker-than-expected ISM report weighed on the US Dollar, which helped support the pair's slight rebound. However, with little economic data from the UK and ongoing uncertainty in global markets, the outlook for further gains in GBP/USD remains uncertain. Traders are likely to stay cautious until more decisive indicators emerge.
