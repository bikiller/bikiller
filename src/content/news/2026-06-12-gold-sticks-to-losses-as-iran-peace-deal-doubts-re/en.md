Gold (XAU/USD) faced renewed selling pressure near the $4,246-$4,247 level during Thursday's Asian session, retracing gains from its previous bounce off the lowest levels since November 2025. Traders noted that doubts over a potential Iran peace deal bolstered the US dollar, offsetting gold’s recent recovery and influencing broader currency pair movements, including USD/EUR trading.

Market participants continue to weigh hawkish Federal Reserve expectations, which are underpinning demand for USD assets. The stronger dollar has pressured gold, keeping exchange rates under tight scrutiny as investors adjust trading strategies. This dynamic also impacts related markets, with safe-haven flows into USD affecting commodities and forex pairs alike.

Analysts suggest that gold’s near-term trajectory will depend on developments in Middle East diplomacy and Fed policy signals. Technical resistance around $4,246-$4,247 remains critical, while any break below recent lows could trigger further selling. Traders in XAU/USD and other major currency pairs are closely monitoring liquidity and volatility for potential short-term opportunities.
