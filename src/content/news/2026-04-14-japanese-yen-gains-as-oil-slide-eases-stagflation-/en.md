The Japanese Yen (JPY) strengthened against the US Dollar (USD) as falling oil prices eased concerns over stagflation in Japan. The USD/JPY pair saw a notable decline, dropping to 133.45 as oil prices slid, helping to calm fears about rising inflation. This shift in energy costs provided relief to Japan's economy, which had been vulnerable to surging oil prices.

However, the recent increase in energy prices, driven by geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, sparked market speculation about a potential near-term interest rate hike by the Bank of Japan (BoJ). Despite the yen's recent gains, analysts suggest that the BoJ could soon tighten policy to address inflationary pressures, with market participants closely watching the USD/JPY pair for further signs of a policy shift. The exchange rate remains volatile, reflecting ongoing uncertainty in global energy markets.
