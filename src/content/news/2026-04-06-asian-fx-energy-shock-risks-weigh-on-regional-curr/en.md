Asian currencies are facing heightened risks as energy disruptions loom, particularly in the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial shipping route for global oil. The potential for an extended energy shock could drive inflation higher, worsen current account deficits, and slow economic growth across the region. As a result, the South Korean won (KRW), Philippine peso (PHP), and Thai baht (THB) are vulnerable in the forex market.

Energy price surges would place significant pressure on these currencies, which are already under strain from rising global costs. Analysts expect that the KRW/USD, PHP/USD, and THB/USD exchange rates could face continued volatility, as any prolonged disruption to energy flows could exacerbate these economic challenges. Traders are closely monitoring energy markets, with the possibility of a ripple effect on the broader forex landscape, especially for emerging market currencies.
