AUD/USD came under renewed selling pressure during Wednesday’s Asian trading session, reversing much of the previous day’s gains as softer-than-expected Australian GDP data weighed on sentiment toward the Australian dollar. The currency pair retreated toward the 0.7170 area, hovering near the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level, while remaining within the familiar range established over the past week.

Despite the fresh decline, AUD/USD managed to hold above the key 0.7150 support level, suggesting that bearish momentum remains contained for now. The exchange rate continues to reflect a cautious trading environment, with investors reassessing growth prospects in Australia while monitoring broader USD dynamics and global risk appetite.

The latest move highlights the market’s sensitivity to domestic economic indicators, particularly as traders gauge the outlook for monetary policy and economic growth. While the Australian dollar remains under pressure, the currency pair’s ability to stay above 0.7150 could limit deeper losses in the near term. Market participants are also keeping an eye on developments in major currencies, including the USD and EUR, for further direction across forex markets.
