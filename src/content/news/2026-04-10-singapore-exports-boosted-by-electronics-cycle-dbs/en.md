DBS Group Research projects that Singapore’s non-oil domestic exports will see continued growth, rising for the seventh consecutive month in March 2026. Exports are expected to jump by 10.3% year-on-year, a notable acceleration from February’s 4.0%. This surge is attributed to the ongoing strength of the electronics cycle, a key driver in the trade-heavy economy.

As the Singapore dollar (SGD) continues to benefit from these positive export figures, traders will closely monitor the impact on the USD/SGD exchange rate. A stronger export outlook could support further SGD appreciation, as demand for the currency rises in response to the favorable economic data. Forex traders will watch for potential volatility in pairs like EUR/SGD and USD/SGD as market sentiment reacts to this uptick in export performance.
