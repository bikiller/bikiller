West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil prices have risen to near $89.00 during the Asian session on Friday, gaining momentum amid geopolitical tensions. Lebanon's military has accused Israel of breaching the ongoing ceasefire, stoking fears of further instability in the region. As a result, WTI has climbed, reflecting market sensitivity to any disruptions in oil supply.

The price move comes as traders react to the news, with WTI trading slightly higher on the day. The ongoing geopolitical strains add to existing concerns around global energy supply, influencing the broader commodities market. The USD remains stable against major currencies like the EUR, though the rising crude price could eventually have ripple effects on the exchange rates, particularly with inflationary pressures in oil-importing nations.
