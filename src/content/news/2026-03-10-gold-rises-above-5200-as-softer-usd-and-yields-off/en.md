Gold (XAU/USD) continued to climb on Tuesday, surpassing the $5,200 mark as the softer US dollar and lower bond yields supported the precious metal. After bouncing from near the $5,000 level on Monday, the yellow metal extended its gains amid a weaker USD and declining yields in the bond market, which reduced the opportunity cost of holding non-yielding assets like gold.

The movement of gold came in the wake of easing concerns over the US economic outlook, as lower yields have lessened the appeal of the dollar. With XAU/USD trading firmly above $5,200, market participants are closely monitoring potential resistance levels, while any shift in USD dynamics could have a significant impact on the exchange rate, especially if the dollar gains strength in upcoming sessions.
