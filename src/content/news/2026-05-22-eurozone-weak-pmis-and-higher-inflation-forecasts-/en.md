Eurozone growth signals are weakening, with recent PMIs from France and Germany showing contraction, while the German IFO index hovers near a five-year low, according to Rabobank’s Senior Macro Strategist Teeuwe Mevissen. The euro has felt the pressure, with the EUR/USD exchange rate facing downward bias as traders digest these signals amid ongoing inflation concerns across the bloc.

Inflation forecasts have risen, adding complexity for the European Central Bank’s policy outlook. Market participants are weighing the impact of weaker economic activity against the persistence of price pressures. EUR trading has become more volatile, with short-term technical levels under scrutiny as investors reassess positions relative to the USD and other major currencies.

The combination of contracting PMIs and elevated inflation expectations is prompting cautious sentiment in forex markets. Analysts suggest that EUR/USD could remain under pressure unless new data signals a stabilization in activity. Traders are closely monitoring upcoming releases and ECB communications, as the interplay between growth and inflation will likely dictate exchange rate movements in the near term.
