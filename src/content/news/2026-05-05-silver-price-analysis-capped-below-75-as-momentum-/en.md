Silver retreated from recent gains on Tuesday, remaining capped below the $75 mark as bearish momentum persists. The metal pared some of Monday’s losses, climbing 0.69% to trade at $73.22 after hitting a daily low of $72.42. Traders cited a modest improvement in risk appetite, which has provided limited support across the precious metals sector, though the overall tone remains cautious amid USD strength.  

The recovery in silver coincides with fluctuations in major currency pairs, including the EUR/USD and GBP/USD exchange rates, which are influencing investor sentiment. A firmer USD continues to weigh on precious metals, keeping trading ranges constrained. Market participants are watching whether silver can sustain momentum above key support levels, with any breakout above $75 likely to trigger renewed buying from speculative and institutional investors.  

Analysts note that short-term technicals remain tilted toward the downside, suggesting traders may look to capitalize on intraday swings rather than prolonged rallies. Volatility in the forex market, particularly against the USD, remains a critical factor for silver’s exchange rate dynamics, impacting trading decisions across commodities and related currency pairs. Investors are expected to monitor macroeconomic cues and risk-on sentiment to gauge potential shifts in positioning.
