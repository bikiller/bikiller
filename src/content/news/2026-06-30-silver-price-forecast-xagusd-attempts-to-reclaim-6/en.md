Silver (XAG/USD) attempted to recover toward the key $60 level on Tuesday, although the broader bearish trend remained intact. The currency pair benefited as the USD surrendered part of its earlier intraday gains, easing pressure on precious metals despite upbeat US economic data. At the time of writing, Silver traded near $59.70, posting a gain of nearly 2.5% during the session as trading sentiment improved.

The rebound came as the US Dollar weakened after an initial advance, allowing the exchange rate of Silver against the USD to stabilize. Even with stronger-than-expected US economic figures supporting the Greenback, investors continued to adjust positions across forex and commodity markets. Market participants also monitored broader currency movements, including the EUR and other major currency pairs, for fresh direction and signs of changing risk appetite.

While the latest recovery highlights renewed buying interest, XAG/USD still faces resistance around the psychologically important $60 level. Traders remain cautious because the prevailing bearish technical outlook has yet to reverse, leaving further gains uncertain. The next phase of trading is likely to depend on whether the USD resumes its strength or extends its pullback, with upcoming economic data and broader forex market sentiment expected to influence price action.
