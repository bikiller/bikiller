US Dollar surged sharply on Wednesday after the Federal Reserve’s June policy meeting, pushing the US Dollar Index (DXY) toward the 100.40 level. The central bank, under newly appointed Chair Kevin Warsh, left interest rates unchanged at 3.50%-3.75%, matching market expectations. The move reinforced USD strength across major currency pairs as traders adjusted positioning.

Currency markets reacted with renewed USD demand as forex trading sentiment shifted toward a more hawkish interpretation of the Fed’s tone. While EUR/USD and other major currency pairs faced pressure, investors increased bets that the policy stance could still tilt tighter in coming meetings. Treasury yields supported the dollar, reinforcing broad-based USD appreciation.

Looking ahead, traders will focus on whether the DXY can sustain momentum above the 100.40 threshold, a key technical level for dollar index trading. Expectations of a potential rate hike continue to underpin USD strength, keeping volatility elevated across forex markets. The outlook for EUR, GBP, and JPY pairs remains closely tied to evolving Fed guidance.
