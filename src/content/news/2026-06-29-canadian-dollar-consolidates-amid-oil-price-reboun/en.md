USD/CAD trades around 1.4210 on Monday as the Canadian dollar consolidates, supported by a rebound in oil prices. The recovery in crude helps underpin the commodity-linked currency, while broader forex markets remain subdued. The exchange rate reflects a lack of strong directional conviction, with traders weighing energy dynamics against steady USD demand in early-week trading conditions.

Attention turns to US labor market data, with investors positioning cautiously ahead of Thursday’s June Nonfarm Payrolls (NFP) report. The USD remains sensitive to incoming signals on employment strength, which could shift expectations for the currency pair. As a result, USD/CAD trading activity stays muted, with participants reluctant to take large positions.

Overall, the currency pair remains in a consolidation phase around the 1.4210 exchange rate, with oil price movements providing near-term support to the Canadian dollar. However, the US dollar’s direction will largely depend on labor market outcomes, suggesting potential volatility for USD/CAD once the Nonfarm Payrolls data is released. Traders are likely to watch for a breakout from the current range.
