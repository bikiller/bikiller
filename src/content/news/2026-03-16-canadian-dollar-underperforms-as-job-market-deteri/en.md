The Canadian Dollar (CAD) is under pressure early Monday in European trading, hovering near 1.3720 against the US Dollar (USD). Investors are weighing the latest labor market report, which revealed that Canadian employers continued layoffs in February, signaling persistent weakness in the job market. The currency underperforms its major peers, reflecting growing concerns about economic momentum and potential central bank policy implications.  

Traders are closely monitoring upcoming inflation data, as weaker employment figures could influence the Bank of Canada’s rate trajectory. The CAD’s exchange rate versus the USD remains subdued, while movements against the euro (EUR) and other major currencies are similarly muted. Market participants are watching technical levels around 1.3700 for support, with 1.3750 acting as a short-term resistance.  

Analysts suggest that continued labor market softness may keep the CAD trading on the defensive. Any surprise in inflation readings could trigger renewed volatility in the USD/CAD pair, affecting cross-currency flows and sentiment. Forex traders are likely to remain cautious, balancing weaker domestic data against broader global trends, while positioning themselves ahead of key economic releases later this week.
