West Texas Intermediate (WTI) futures on NYMEX recovered early-session losses during Wednesday’s European trading hours, stabilising around $89.60. The rebound reflects a cautious improvement in risk sentiment as traders reassess short-term supply dynamics. Despite intraday volatility, crude oil continues to trade within a tight range, influenced by broader USD movements and shifts in global commodity demand expectations.

Market participants are closely watching WTI’s ability to reclaim levels above its 20-day exponential moving average (EMA), a key short-term technical indicator. Sustained trading above this threshold could signal renewed bullish momentum, while rejection may cap further gains. The broader forex market, including USD currency pairs and EUR/USD exchange rate dynamics, continues to influence commodity pricing through dollar strength.

Looking ahead, oil traders remain sensitive to USD fluctuations, geopolitical supply risks, and broader risk appetite across global markets. A stronger US dollar can weigh on USD-denominated commodities like WTI, while a softer tone may support recovery attempts. Market positioning suggests cautious optimism, with traders balancing macroeconomic signals and technical resistance near key moving averages.
