EUR/JPY extends losses for a second successive session, trading around 184.30 during European hours on Tuesday, as the currency pair comes under pressure following disappointing German data. The HCOB Manufacturing PMI for Germany flatlined in June, signaling stagnation in factory activity and weighing on the euro against the Japanese yen in foreign exchange trading.

Sentiment toward the euro remains fragile as weak German factory data reinforces concerns about the broader Eurozone recovery. The yen benefits from its safe-haven appeal, contributing to downside pressure on the EUR/JPY currency pair. Traders are now closely watching upcoming Eurozone PMI releases for fresh signals on growth momentum and potential volatility in FX markets.

Overall, EUR/JPY remains biased to the downside in near-term forex trading, with 184.30 acting as the current pivot level amid fading euro strength. Market participants continue to monitor both EUR and JPY drivers, including USD movements and risk sentiment, while awaiting Eurozone data that could reshape expectations for the currency pair’s short-term direction.
