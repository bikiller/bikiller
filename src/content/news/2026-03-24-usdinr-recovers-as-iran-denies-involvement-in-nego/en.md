The Indian Rupee (INR) slipped against the US Dollar (USD) on Tuesday, following a strong recovery in the previous session. The USD/INR currency pair traded around 82.10, retreating from the previous day's gains. This pullback comes amid market reactions to global geopolitical developments, with traders eyeing potential shifts in sentiment. 

Meanwhile, Iran’s denial of involvement in any negotiations with the United States contributed to added volatility in global markets. Traders are keeping a close watch on political tensions, as any further developments could have significant effects on the USD/INR exchange rate. The pair’s movement remains sensitive to both global and domestic factors, with analysts closely monitoring key support and resistance levels in the coming days.
