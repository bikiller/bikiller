The AUD/USD currency pair held steady near recent highs on Friday, trading around 0.7200 at the time of writing. The exchange rate showed little movement on the day as traders adopted a cautious stance ahead of the Reserve Bank of Australia’s (RBA) upcoming policy decision scheduled for Tuesday. Market participants appear hesitant to take aggressive positions ahead of a widely anticipated rate hike.  

Analysts note that technical support near 0.7180 has helped stabilize the pair, while resistance around 0.7225 could cap further gains in the short term. The Australian dollar’s resilience reflects broader risk sentiment, with investors balancing USD strength against global economic data and commodity-driven flows. Trading volumes remain moderate as the market awaits fresh cues from the RBA on interest rates and monetary policy direction.  

A potential RBA rate hike could reinforce AUD gains versus the USD, though the extent may hinge on accompanying forward guidance. Traders are also watching correlated currency pairs, including EUR/USD, for broader risk appetite signals. For now, AUD/USD remains range-bound near 0.7200, with market focus firmly on next week’s central bank decision and its implications for short- and medium-term trading strategies.
