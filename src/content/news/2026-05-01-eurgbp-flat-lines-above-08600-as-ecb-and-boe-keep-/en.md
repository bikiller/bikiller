The EUR/GBP currency pair remained largely unchanged above the 0.8600 mark during early Friday trading, with the exchange rate hovering near 0.8625. Market participants appeared cautious following the European Central Bank’s (ECB) decision to leave interest rates unchanged, a move mirrored by the Bank of England. The lack of monetary policy surprises kept the trading range narrow, with investors weighing broader macroeconomic cues across the eurozone and the UK.  

Traders noted that EUR/USD and GBP/USD pairs also saw muted volatility as the respective central banks maintained steady policy stances. The flat EUR/GBP trading suggests limited directional momentum, with the market digesting existing economic data and preparing for upcoming releases. Analysts highlight that without fresh stimulus or tightening signals, the cross may continue to oscillate around key technical levels, including the 0.8600 support zone.  

Looking ahead, forex traders are closely monitoring any shifts in economic sentiment, including inflation readings and growth indicators, which could influence EUR and GBP valuations against the USD and other majors. While the immediate outlook appears range-bound, a break above 0.8650 or below 0.8600 could trigger more active trading, providing potential opportunities for both short-term and longer-term strategies in the European session.
