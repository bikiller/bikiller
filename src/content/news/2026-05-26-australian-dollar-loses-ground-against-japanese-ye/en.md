The AUD/JPY currency pair edged lower in early European trading on Tuesday, slipping to around 113.85 as the exchange rate came under renewed selling pressure. Trading activity reflected a softer risk tone across global forex markets, with investors also monitoring USD and EUR moves for broader directional cues in sentiment.

Australia's labour market data surprised to the downside, weighing on the Australian Dollar and prompting renewed selling against the Japanese Yen, a traditional safe-haven currency. The weaker employment signals intensified concerns over domestic growth outlook, while escalating Middle East uncertainty reinforced risk-off flows across forex trading, supporting JPY demand and pressuring the AUD.

The pair now hovers near the 113.85 level, which traders are watching as a short-term pivot amid heightened volatility. In broader FX markets, movements in USD and EUR pairs continue to shape risk appetite, while sentiment-driven flows may keep AUD/JPY sensitive to geopolitical headlines and incoming economic data from Australia and abroad.
