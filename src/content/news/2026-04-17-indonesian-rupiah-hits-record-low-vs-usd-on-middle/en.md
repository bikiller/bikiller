The Indonesian Rupiah (IDR) hit a record low against the US Dollar (USD) during the Asian session on Friday, as the USD/IDR pair surged to an all-time high of 17,188-17,190. The move came amid rising risks related to the Middle East, which pushed traders to seek safe-haven assets like the US Dollar. The currency pair’s sharp rise indicates continued pressure on the Rupiah.

With spot prices on track for strong weekly gains, USD/IDR seems poised to extend its upward momentum. The exchange rate reflects ongoing market instability, and the Rupiah’s vulnerability to global geopolitical developments has investors cautious. As USD/IDR approaches the 17,200 mark, the currency pair’s technical outlook suggests further appreciation in the near term, unless significant market shifts occur.
