Brent crude has experienced sharp swings as traders react to mixed signals on potential US–Iran talks. After falling below $100 a barrel, easing inflation concerns, the benchmark rebounded above $103 following Iran’s denial of negotiations and renewed regional tensions. Deutsche Bank strategists note that these rapid two-way moves reflect heightened uncertainty in energy markets, with traders closely monitoring geopolitical headlines.  

The volatility in oil prices has rippled into forex markets, affecting USD exchange rates and commodity-linked currencies. The euro-dollar pair (EUR/USD) saw cautious trading, with investors weighing inflation risks against safe-haven flows. Oil exporters’ currencies, including the Norwegian krone and Canadian dollar, experienced elevated trading volumes, reflecting Brent’s swings and market sensitivity to geopolitical developments.  

Analysts suggest that Brent’s movements could influence broader market sentiment, pushing USD strength during risk-off phases and boosting commodity-linked currencies when crude rallies. Traders are eyeing technical levels around $100 and $103 for short-term guidance, while the currency pair dynamics underscore how energy markets increasingly drive exchange rate fluctuations amid geopolitical uncertainty.
