West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude edged higher toward the $105 mark on Thursday, with early Asian trading seeing the US benchmark around $104.90. The rise comes amid heightened Middle East tensions as former President Donald Trump maintains a naval blockade on Iran, fueling concerns over supply disruptions. Traders are watching oil closely, as higher energy costs could influence USD currency pairs, particularly against the euro and yen, with potential ripple effects across global exchange rates.  

The energy-driven uptick in WTI has pushed commodity-linked currencies such as the Canadian dollar and Norwegian krone higher, while risk-sensitive pairs like EUR/USD and AUD/USD face pressure. Analysts note that the $105 level acts as a psychological barrier, with technical indicators showing short-term bullish momentum. Forex participants are adjusting positions in anticipation of volatility, factoring in the geopolitical backdrop and its potential impact on USD liquidity and central bank policy outlooks.  

Market watchers emphasize that sustained gains above $105 could signal further upward pressure on oil-related currencies, potentially boosting USD/commodity pair movements. Investors remain alert to geopolitical developments in the Strait of Hormuz and their influence on global crude flows, as any escalation could trigger wider swings in forex markets. The combination of technical levels, trading volumes, and geopolitical risks is keeping WTI in focus for both energy traders and forex speculators.
