The Japanese yen showed tentative gains against the US dollar, with the USD/JPY currency pair oscillating amid a volatile trading session. According to UOB strategists Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann, the exchange rate spiked to 157.76, dipped briefly to 157.04, and ultimately closed at 157.61. This movement highlights the underlying strength of the USD, even as the yen attempts to recover.

Intraday, the yen could edge slightly higher, but resistance appears near the 158.30 level. Analysts suggest that any upward movement is likely to remain capped below 157.95, indicating limited scope for a sustained rally. Traders will be watching these key levels closely as short-term market momentum shifts between buying and selling pressure.

The broader forex market remains sensitive to global cues, including US economic data and risk sentiment, which continue to influence USD/JPY trading. While the yen’s rebound offers some relief to investors, the prevailing trend favors the dollar, underscoring cautious positioning for currency traders seeking to capitalize on minor corrections without overexposing to volatility. Monitoring technical levels like 157.95 and 158.30 is crucial for navigating potential short-term fluctuations.
