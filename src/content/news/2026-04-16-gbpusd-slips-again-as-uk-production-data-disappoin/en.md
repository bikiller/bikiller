GBP/USD reversed recent gains on Thursday, dropping about 0.25% to close near 1.3525 after slipping below the 1.3550 level. The pair struggled through the European and North American sessions, unable to sustain an upward momentum as sellers capitalized on any intraday rallies, pushing the exchange rate lower.

The drop in the British pound came amid disappointing UK production data, which dampened investor sentiment and fueled concerns over economic growth. As a result, traders adjusted their positions, leading to a steady decline in GBP/USD. With the pair currently hovering near 1.3525, further weakness may be seen unless there is a rebound in market sentiment or positive economic data from the UK.
