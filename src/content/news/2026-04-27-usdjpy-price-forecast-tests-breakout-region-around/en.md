The USD/JPY currency pair retraced early gains to trade slightly lower around 159.20 during late Asian session on Monday, testing a key breakout region. Traders reacted to a mixed US Dollar performance, which softened despite ongoing US-Iran diplomatic deadlock. The pair’s movement highlights the market’s cautious tone ahead of the week’s central bank policy announcements from the Bank of Japan (BoJ) and the Federal Reserve.  

Technical analysts note that the 159.20 level serves as a near-term pivot, with upside pressure limited by profit-taking and USD volatility. The exchange rate’s slight retreat reflects market participants weighing safe-haven flows against a resilient yen, while broader currency trading, including EUR and other major pairs, reacts to global geopolitical tensions and mixed US economic signals.  

Investors are positioning cautiously, monitoring potential BoJ-Fed divergence that could influence the USD/JPY trajectory. Sustained trading above 159.20 could open doors for further upside, while a break below might trigger short-term corrections. The market remains attentive to central bank cues, USD sentiment, and geopolitical developments, which together shape intraday and medium-term trends in major currency pairs.
