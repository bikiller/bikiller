Silver (XAG/USD) extended gains of over 1.50% on Tuesday, trading at $58.73, remaining above its opening level despite headwinds from rising US Treasury yields and a firm US Dollar. In the broader forex market, the currency pair shows resilience as traders weigh macro signals and shifting risk sentiment across USD-linked exchange rates.

Sentiment support stems from lingering concerns over a fragile US–Iran truce agreement, which has limited downside pressure on the currency pair. As a result, XAG/USD holds above its opening level while struggling to break the key $60 resistance zone, signaling a consolidation phase despite short-term bullish momentum in silver trading activity.

On the downside, bears are eyeing a potential move toward the $55 support level if selling pressure intensifies and USD strength persists. Market participants in the forex trading space remain cautious, with silver’s exchange rate outlook heavily dependent on US data, Treasury yields, and broader risk appetite across major currency pairs.
