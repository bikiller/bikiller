Platinum prices surged to $1,800 this week, marking the highest level since 2011. Analysts, including Commerzbank's Carsten Fritsch, attribute this uptick to potential changes in the 2035 combustion engine ban, which could bolster the use of platinum in catalytic converters.

This upward momentum in precious metals indicates a shift in market sentiment, affecting related currency pairs. As traders monitor the implications of rising platinum and palladium prices, the exchange rates involving USD and EUR could experience fluctuations, influenced by investor confidence in commodities linked to industrial demand.
