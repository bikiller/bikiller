The upcoming European Central Bank (ECB) meeting is expected to focus heavily on geopolitical risks, particularly the ongoing tensions in the Middle East and their economic impact. Rabobank’s Bas van Geffen highlighted that the war in Iran, combined with rising energy prices, could result in stagflation for the Eurozone. This scenario is likely to affect the EUR/USD exchange rate, with traders keeping a close eye on any ECB signals regarding future policy adjustments.

As energy prices climb, inflationary pressures on the Eurozone are expected to intensify, potentially dampening growth prospects. This shift may lead to market volatility in currency pairs like EUR/USD, with investors reassessing their positions. The ECB's response could dictate further fluctuations in the euro, especially if the central bank signals more cautious or dovish measures in the face of rising risks. The USD remains strong amid these uncertainties, reinforcing the divergence between the two currencies.
