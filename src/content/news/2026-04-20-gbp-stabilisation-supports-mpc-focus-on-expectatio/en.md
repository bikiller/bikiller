The British pound (GBP) is showing signs of stabilisation ahead of the UK's labour market data for February. TD Securities forecasts a slight dip in the unemployment rate and modest job gains, supporting the view that the economy is maintaining a steady pace. This comes as wage growth is expected to moderate across key measures, in line with market expectations.

The stabilisation of the UK labour market may influence the Bank of England's (BoE) future policy decisions. The moderate job gains and slower wage growth could ease inflation concerns, providing the BoE with more flexibility. Traders will be watching closely for any changes in the GBP/USD and EUR/GBP exchange rates, as any deviation from these expectations could trigger movement in the forex market.
