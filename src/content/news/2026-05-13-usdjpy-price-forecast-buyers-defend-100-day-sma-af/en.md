The USD/JPY currency pair shows a mild upside bias on Wednesday, with traders keeping a close eye on the 100-day simple moving average (SMA), which has held firm despite recent intervention-driven volatility. The Japanese Yen remains under pressure against a stronger US Dollar, reflecting market sensitivity to macroeconomic shifts and geopolitical developments, including tensions in the Middle East.

Rising oil prices are adding to the yen’s weakness, highlighting Japan’s dependence on energy imports. The USD/JPY exchange rate has been influenced by a combination of domestic and international factors, with market participants reacting to both technical signals and fundamental drivers. This has resulted in cautious trading around key support and resistance levels, as buyers defend the 100-day SMA to maintain momentum.

Analysts note that continued USD strength, coupled with persistent energy price volatility, could keep the yen on the defensive. Investors are also monitoring other major currencies, such as the EUR, for potential cross-market correlations that may impact USD/JPY trading. For now, the interplay between intervention measures, commodity price swings, and technical levels shapes the outlook for the currency pair in the near term.
