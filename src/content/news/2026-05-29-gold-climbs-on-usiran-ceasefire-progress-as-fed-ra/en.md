Gold surged above $1,563 on Friday, rising more than 1.50% as markets reacted to reports that the US and Iran are close to finalizing a deal to extend the ceasefire for 60 days. The move in XAU/USD follows a rebound from daily lows of $1,489, reflecting renewed safe-haven demand amid easing geopolitical tensions and shifting investor expectations on the US Federal Reserve’s next rate moves.

Currency traders noted that the easing of hawkish bets on the USD contributed to the precious metal’s strength. With the dollar softening against major currencies like the EUR and JPY, gold’s exchange rate gains highlighted the sensitivity of commodities to broader FX market dynamics. Traders remain focused on geopolitical developments, using the currency pair movements to gauge risk sentiment across global markets.

Technical analysts point to XAU/USD holding above key support levels near $1,490, suggesting momentum could extend toward the $1,570 region if ceasefire progress continues. The trading environment remains sensitive to both US monetary policy signals and Middle East developments, making gold a closely watched barometer for cross-market risk, currency flows, and investor positioning in USD-linked assets.
