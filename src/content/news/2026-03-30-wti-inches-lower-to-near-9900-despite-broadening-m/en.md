West Texas Intermediate (WTI) oil prices slipped slightly to $98.90 per barrel during Monday’s Asian session, following three consecutive days of gains. The minor dip comes as traders remain cautious amid the ongoing Middle East conflict, which has failed to provide significant volatility in the market. The oil price correction highlights the balancing act between geopolitical tensions and investor sentiment.

While the WTI outlook shows signs of stability, concerns over global supply disruptions continue to offer support. The fading optimism surrounding a swift resolution to the Iran crisis suggests that market participants may focus more on potential supply shortages. In this environment, crude oil’s price fluctuations are likely to remain tied to shifts in geopolitical risks, influencing both the USD and broader commodity markets.
