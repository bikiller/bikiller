The GBP has gained momentum recently as a result of a hawkish shift in UK front-end rates. According to Deutsche Bank strategists Shreyas Gopal and Sanjay Raja, this repricing is the largest among G10 central banks, effectively erasing previous dovish expectations for the Bank of England (BoE). The shift has provided support for the GBP against major currencies, particularly the USD and EUR, as traders adjust to the changing market outlook.

This shift in BoE expectations has led to a stronger pound, with the GBP/USD exchange rate showing notable strength in recent sessions. As the market reacts to the BoE's stance, the pound is likely to remain supported in the near term. The repricing of UK rates is seen as a key driver for GBP's performance, especially as other G10 currencies maintain a more cautious approach to tightening. Traders will continue to monitor BoE signals for further clues on future monetary policy.
