ING analysts believe that the market has become overly optimistic about the Bank of England's future path, with expectations of rate cuts now effectively priced out following the escalation of the Iran conflict. The shift in sentiment has altered the outlook for EUR/GBP, which has seen a significant negative correlation with oil prices. As geopolitical risks remain heightened, the exchange rate has been pressured, with no further rate hikes expected by year-end.

Despite the volatility, the EUR/GBP pair remains largely stagnant in the short term, with trading expectations now centered on the broader economic developments rather than imminent central bank actions. Analysts caution that the focus has shifted away from anticipated rate changes, leaving the currency pair vulnerable to market shifts and geopolitical factors. The lack of movement in the exchange rate reflects broader market indecision as traders digest the implications of the ongoing crisis.
