Indonesia faces rising fiscal pressure as the government maintains capped fuel prices and boosts subsidies, according to BNP Paribas. Analysts warn that if Brent crude averages USD 92–100 per barrel in 2026, the strain on public finances could intensify, potentially affecting the rupiah’s performance against the USD and EUR. Traders are closely watching USD/IDR exchange rate movements, as higher global yields may attract capital outflows.  

The combination of higher U.S. Treasury yields and Indonesia’s energy policy exposes debt markets to volatility. The rupiah could come under selling pressure if fiscal deficits widen, prompting portfolio investors to reassess positions in local bonds. Market participants are eyeing key technical levels in USD/IDR trading, with sentiment likely sensitive to both oil price trends and global interest rate shifts.  

Currency markets are pricing in these risks, with IDR trading more cautiously versus major currencies. Short-term traders are monitoring intraday swings and potential breakout points, while longer-term strategies may factor in sustained Brent levels and fiscal sustainability. BNP Paribas highlights that Indonesia’s policy path will be a major determinant of exchange rate stability and investor confidence throughout 2026.
