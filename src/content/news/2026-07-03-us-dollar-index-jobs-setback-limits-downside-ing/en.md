US Dollar Index edged lower after softer US jobs data triggered a modest pullback in USD trading, with major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD benefiting from improved risk sentiment. The weaker labour report eased near-term demand for the dollar, though moves remained contained across the foreign exchange market as traders reassessed interest rate expectations.

ING strategist Francesco Pesole noted that while the data setback has weighed on the USD exchange rate, it is unlikely to drive sustained dollar weakness. He said the report reduces expectations for two additional Federal Reserve hikes, but it is not weak enough to trigger a broader dovish repricing in FX markets or shift trading momentum decisively.

Overall, the outlook for the dollar remains mixed, with forex traders focusing on upcoming US macro releases and Fed commentary. While EUR/USD may see short-term support, USD downside is expected to be limited unless data deteriorates further. Market participants continue to balance growth concerns against policy signals, keeping exchange rate volatility contained in the near term.
