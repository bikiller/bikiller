The US Dollar Index (DXY) climbed above the 99.00 level early Friday, trading near 99.25 in Asian markets. The move reflects robust US labor data, which bolstered the USD against a basket of six major currencies, including the EUR, JPY, and GBP. Traders noted the DXY’s upward momentum as risk sentiment remained cautious ahead of potential developments in US-Iran negotiations.

In forex markets, the stronger dollar pressured key currency pairs, with EUR/USD edging lower and USD/JPY testing recent highs. Analysts highlighted that the USD’s resilience is underpinned by solid employment figures, suggesting that US monetary policy may remain supportive. Exchange rates showed heightened activity, with trading volumes picking up around technical levels that previously acted as support and resistance.

Looking ahead, the market is weighing the implications of both domestic economic data and geopolitical developments. Any progress or setbacks in a possible US-Iran deal could prompt swift moves in currency pairs, influencing trading strategies across the board. Investors remain focused on the DXY as a benchmark for USD strength, while closely monitoring volatility in major pairs like GBP/USD and AUD/USD for broader forex sentiment.
