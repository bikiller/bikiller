The US Dollar Index (DXY) lost momentum on Thursday, sliding back toward the 101.40 area after fresh US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) data reinforced a mixed macro backdrop. Inflation remains sticky, while growth indicators and the labor market continue to show resilience, keeping USD currency trading range-bound across major currency pairs.

US dollar exchange rates reacted unevenly across majors, with trading activity in EUR/USD and other currency pairs reflecting shifting rate expectations. While the data supported the broader USD outlook, it also tempered immediate bullish momentum, leading to consolidation rather than breakout moves. Traders remain focused on incoming inflation signals and Fed policy cues to gauge direction in forex markets.

Meanwhile, the Japanese yen continued to hover near perceived intervention levels, keeping USD/JPY trading cautious as market participants watch for potential action from authorities. The safe-haven currency’s sensitivity to yield differentials and risk sentiment added volatility to yen crosses. Overall, forex markets remain driven by macro data, central bank expectations, and shifting risk appetite.
