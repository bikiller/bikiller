The Reserve Bank of Australia (RBA) raised interest rates by 25 basis points to 4.10%, marking a split decision. Initially, the move was interpreted as dovish, prompting a sharp sell-off in AUD/USD. The currency pair fell as traders reacted to the outcome, testing lower levels. However, the decline was short-lived, as Governor Michele Bullock’s comments hinted at a more cautious stance, sparking a rebound in the Aussie dollar.

AUD/USD quickly recovered from the dip, stabilizing as market participants reassessed the RBA's outlook. Despite the hike, positioning in the Australian dollar remains stretched, which could limit further upside in the near term. The exchange rate has found support above 0.6400, with the rebound suggesting a more balanced view of the RBA's approach. As traders adjust to the central bank's signals, the Aussie remains sensitive to shifts in global risk sentiment and USD dynamics.
