US dollar extends gains as a bond market sell-off pushes US real yields higher, reinforcing USD strength across major currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD.  strategists Francesco Pesole, Frantisek Taborsky and Chris Turner note that the move reflects shifting rate expectations and renewed demand for higher-yielding US assets.

Inflation concerns remain the key driver behind the bond market sell-off, with investors demanding higher compensation for holding US debt. Rising real yields are providing a firm tailwind for the USD, while adding downside pressure to the EUR/USD exchange rate and other major forex trading pairs as volatility increases across interest rate and currency markets in the short term.

From a trading perspective, the stronger dollar environment suggests continued support for USD-denominated assets, with traders closely monitoring central bank signals and inflation data. The outlook implies sustained pressure on the euro and other G10 currencies as interest rate differentials continue to favour the US dollar in global exchange rate dynamics, which may keep USD bids firm in near-term forex trading sessions.
