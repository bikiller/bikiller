The euro gained ground against the Japanese yen on Wednesday, with the EUR/JPY currency pair extending its winning streak for the third consecutive day. Early European trading saw the exchange rate hover around 185.30, reflecting growing market expectations that the European Central Bank could signal further interest rate hikes. Traders are weighing the impact of tighter monetary policy on cross-currency flows, supporting the euro’s resilience against the yen.  

Market participants cited rising ECB rate hike odds as a key driver behind the euro’s strength. As investors anticipate higher yields in the eurozone, demand for EUR-denominated assets has increased, pushing EUR/JPY higher. Meanwhile, the Japanese yen remains under pressure amid continued USD strength and a lack of fresh domestic catalysts, leaving the yen vulnerable to gains in other major currencies like the euro and dollar.  

Technically, EUR/JPY appears poised for further upside if it maintains support above 184.80, with momentum favoring buyers. Analysts note that any reversal in ECB guidance or shifts in global risk sentiment could trigger short-term corrections, but current trading reflects robust euro demand. For currency traders, monitoring central bank cues alongside USD movements remains critical to navigating the pair’s near-term trajectory.
