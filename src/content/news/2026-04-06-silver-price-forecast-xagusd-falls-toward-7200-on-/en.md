Silver extended its retreat for a third consecutive session on Monday, with XAG/USD hovering around $72.20 per troy ounce during Asian trading. The precious metal faced renewed pressure as a hawkish Federal Reserve outlook strengthened the US dollar, prompting traders to adjust positions across key currency pairs. The exchange rate momentum in USD-heavy assets weighed on silver, curbing buying interest despite lingering inflation concerns.  

Market participants cited the Fed’s signals of potential rate hikes as the primary driver behind silver’s weakness. Technical levels near $72.00 are now under scrutiny, as a breach could accelerate downside momentum. In forex trading, stronger USD dynamics often shift flows from commodities like silver toward dollar-denominated assets, influencing broader cross-currency movements, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, as investors reassess risk exposure.  

Analysts suggest that while short-term pressures persist, silver could find support if the USD stabilizes or risk appetite improves in global markets. Traders remain watchful of both economic indicators and central bank commentary, recognizing that XAG/USD’s near-term trajectory hinges on the interplay between dollar strength, interest rate expectations, and safe-haven demand in the currency and commodity markets.
