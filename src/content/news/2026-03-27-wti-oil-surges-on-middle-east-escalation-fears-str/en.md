WTI oil prices surged on Friday, rising by 3.55% to trade near $96.00, driven by heightened concerns over escalating tensions in the Middle East. Fears of potential disruptions in the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial shipping route for global oil supplies, have added significant volatility to the energy market. As a result, oil's price spike has reverberated through currency markets, impacting USD-based trades.

The rise in WTI prices has also led to a shift in market sentiment, with traders closely monitoring developments in the region. As oil prices climb, the impact on exchange rates, particularly for USD-related pairs, is becoming more evident. The increasing risk of supply disruptions is expected to keep energy prices volatile, influencing not only crude oil but also broader market movements, with potential implications for EUR/USD and other major forex pairs.
