Silver (XAG/USD) edged higher on Friday, trading near $67.50 at the time of writing and posting a daily gain of 0.21% as broad USD weakness supported precious metals demand. The white metal held a firm tone even as risk appetite improved following signs of diplomatic progress between the United States and Iran, which reduced safe-haven demand across commodity markets and tempered volatility in major currency pair trading.

Despite easing geopolitical concerns, traders continued to favor Silver as the US Dollar softened against major peers including the EUR. The weaker USD exchange rate improved the appeal of dollar-denominated assets, helping XAG/USD maintain its upward bias. Market participants also monitored Treasury yields and expectations surrounding future Federal Reserve policy, with lower yields providing additional support for non-yielding metals during active forex trading sessions.

From a technical perspective, Silver remains resilient above the $67.00 psychological level, while buyers continue targeting resistance around recent highs near $68.00. A sustained move above that region could reinforce bullish momentum in XAG/USD trading. On the downside, immediate support is seen near $66.50, where renewed buying interest may emerge if broader USD sentiment remains fragile against major currencies such as the EUR and JPY.
