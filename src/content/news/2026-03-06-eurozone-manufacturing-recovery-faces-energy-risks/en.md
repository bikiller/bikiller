ABN AMRO sees a rebound in Eurozone industrial production, driven by a recovery in domestic demand and robust defence-related activity in Germany. The bank highlights that stronger manufacturing momentum should support the euro against the USD, potentially influencing EUR/USD trading as investors weigh renewed growth against lingering energy uncertainties. Eurozone factory output could benefit from higher consumption and investment flows in the coming quarters.  

Despite the near-term uplift, ABN AMRO anticipates a pullback in German factory orders after late-2025 strength. This could temper euro gains, with EUR/USD exchange rate volatility reflecting investor caution over cyclical demand. Traders may monitor technical levels closely, as any slowdown in German production could trigger short-term corrections in the currency pair.  

Energy risks remain a key concern for the region, with potential disruptions affecting industrial activity and cost pressures. Market participants are likely to assess the interplay between economic recovery and energy constraints when positioning in EUR trading versus the USD. Overall, a cautious optimism underpins Eurozone industrial prospects, suggesting selective trading opportunities in both spot and derivative markets.
