The US Dollar Index (DXY), which tracks the USD against six major currencies including the EUR, JPY, GBP, CAD, SEK and CHF, is trading near the 101.00 level, hovering around 100.83 during Friday’s Asian session. After two consecutive days of gains, the currency pair basket shows a pause in momentum as trading activity turns cautious across forex markets.

Sentiment around the US dollar remains supported by a hawkish Federal Reserve outlook, which continues to influence forex trading decisions and keeps the USD resilient against major currency pairs. Market participants are closely monitoring interest rate expectations, as higher-for-longer policy signals provide underlying support to the dollar index despite short-term consolidation near the 101.00 threshold.

As a result, the EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and other major currency pairs may continue to trade within tight ranges, reflecting subdued volatility in the forex market. Traders are likely to await fresh economic data and Fed commentary before positioning for a clearer directional move, with the US Dollar Index likely to remain anchored around the 100.80–101.20 zone in the near term.
