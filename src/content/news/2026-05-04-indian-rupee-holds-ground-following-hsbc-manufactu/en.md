The Indian Rupee (INR) held its ground against the US Dollar (USD) during Monday’s Asian trading session, with the USD/INR currency pair hovering around 94.90. This follows modest gains from the previous trading day, as traders assessed India’s HSBC Manufacturing Purchasing Managers' Index (PMI) data. The PMI reading provided some support for the Rupee, but broader market trends kept the exchange rate in a tight range.

The USD/INR saw limited movement after the release of the PMI, which pointed to a stable economic outlook for India. Despite recent gains, the Rupee remains under pressure due to global risk sentiment and fluctuating USD strength. Market participants will closely monitor further economic data and global factors, particularly the movements of the EUR/USD and other major pairs, which could influence the INR’s outlook in the coming days.
