Asian currencies have shown resilience in recent trading sessions, supported by a weaker US Dollar (USD) following tensions surrounding the Iran conflict. The weakening of the USD has boosted various regional currencies, although performance has varied. Notably, the Japanese yen (JPY) has outperformed, strengthening to ¥128.75 against the USD, while the South Korean won (KRW) has seen moderate gains, climbing to 1,265.30 per USD.

Despite the overall positive trend for Asian currencies, MUFG’s Senior Currency Analyst, Michael Wan, points out increasing dispersion in the region. Currencies like the Chinese yuan (CNY) have struggled to make significant gains, with the USD/CNY exchange rate hovering near 6.90. This divergence suggests that while some currencies benefit from the weaker USD, others are facing challenges due to local economic factors, leaving traders with a more selective approach.
