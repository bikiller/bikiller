The Australian Dollar surged past the 0.7200 mark on Thursday, gaining more than 1% as the US Dollar slumped to a seven-day low. The AUD/USD exchange rate saw a notable rebound from a daily low of 0.7110, bolstered by Japan’s intervention in the forex market. This move overshadowed stronger-than-expected US economic data, which failed to prevent the Greenback’s decline.

The Japanese authorities' action has introduced volatility into the currency markets, particularly affecting the USD. The intervention, aimed at curbing excessive yen strength, sent the Greenback tumbling, providing a boost to the AUD/USD pair. Traders are closely monitoring this development, as further moves in the currency markets could shift the balance between major currencies like the USD and EUR in the short term.
