WTI crude oil prices dipped to approximately $63.50 during Asian trading on Tuesday, following a strong 1.5% gain the day before. The pullback comes amid growing concerns over oversupply in the market, which continues to weigh on sentiment. As traders digest the latest news, the potential for a production hike from OPEC+ remains a key focal point for market participants.

The looming US-Iran talks add another layer of uncertainty, potentially affecting oil supply dynamics. This geopolitical development, alongside market expectations of further output adjustments, could influence the USD's performance against major currencies. The ongoing volatility in oil prices has also been a contributing factor to recent fluctuations in the EUR/USD and other key currency pairs, reflecting the broader market risk-off sentiment.
