Gold (XAU/USD) eased slightly from a near four-week peak in early Asian trading on Wednesday, hovering just below the $4,850 mark. Despite reaching this high, the precious metal struggled to attract follow-through buying, leaving its daily movement largely flat. Investors remain cautious as the market weighs ongoing geopolitical tensions around the Strait of Hormuz, which have been driving intermittent safe-haven demand.  

The USD has shown signs of mild weakness, providing support to gold, while major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD remain sensitive to broader risk sentiment. Traders are closely monitoring the exchange rate dynamics, as any shifts in USD strength could influence gold’s short-term trajectory. Technical charts suggest resistance around $4,860, with support near $4,830, highlighting a narrow trading range amid subdued momentum.  

Market participants are weighing geopolitical risk against the backdrop of central bank signals and inflation data. Any escalation in Hormuz tensions could reignite a stronger bullish push for XAU/USD, while stabilization may see the metal consolidate near current levels. Forex traders are keeping an eye on USD liquidity and cross-currency flows, assessing how shifts in the greenback could ripple across commodities and major currency pairs in the coming sessions.
