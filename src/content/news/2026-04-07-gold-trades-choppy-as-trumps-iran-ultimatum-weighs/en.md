Gold (XAU/USD) is navigating a choppy trading range on Tuesday as investors remain cautious ahead of a looming deadline for a US-Iran agreement. The yellow metal is struggling to find direction amid heightened geopolitical tension, with safe-haven demand supporting modest gains. The USD has shown mixed strength against major currencies, including EUR and JPY, reflecting uncertainty in broader risk sentiment.  

Market participants are watching technical levels closely, with gold hovering near $1,950 per ounce, unable to sustain a decisive break above resistance at $1,960. The choppy price action underscores hesitancy among traders, as the potential fallout from the Iran ultimatum could trigger sudden swings in exchange rates across commodities and currency pairs.  

Analysts note that continued volatility in gold may impact correlated currencies and commodities, with traders likely adjusting positions in USD, EUR, and other major pairs. Any escalation in geopolitical tensions could drive further safe-haven flows, while a diplomatic breakthrough might ease pressure on the greenback and prompt renewed trading momentum across forex markets. Investors are advised to monitor developments closely, as headlines remain the primary driver of near-term market behavior.
