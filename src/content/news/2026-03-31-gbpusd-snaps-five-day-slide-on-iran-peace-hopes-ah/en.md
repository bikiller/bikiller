GBP/USD reversed a five-day losing streak, gaining 0.32% on Tuesday. The currency pair closed at 1.3230 after fluctuating between a session low of 1.3160 and a high near 1.3260. Despite the intraday recovery, the move lacked momentum, and the exchange rate remained well below its key moving averages, signaling continued pressure for the British pound.

The market's reaction was driven by growing optimism over peace prospects in Iran, which weighed on the dollar. With crucial U.S. economic data scheduled for release, traders were cautious, as the upcoming figures could influence market sentiment and lead to more volatile trading conditions.
