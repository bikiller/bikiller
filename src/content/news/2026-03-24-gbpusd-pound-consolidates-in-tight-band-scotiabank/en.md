The GBP/USD currency pair is trading in a narrow range, with the Pound slightly softer against the USD as it consolidates near the 1.34 level. According to Scotiabank analysts, this follows a modest rebound from mid-March lows, suggesting that recent upward momentum in the exchange rate is losing traction. Market participants appear cautious, keeping trading activity contained within a tight band.

The subdued movement reflects a balance between lingering support for the Pound and steady demand for the US Dollar. While the earlier recovery hinted at improving sentiment toward the UK currency, the lack of follow-through buying indicates hesitation among traders. Broader forex market dynamics, including USD stability and limited fresh catalysts, are contributing to the current consolidation phase.

From a technical perspective, the 1.34 zone is emerging as a near-term pivot for the GBP/USD pair, with price action signaling indecision rather than a clear directional trend. Traders are likely watching for a breakout or breakdown from this range to guide positioning. In the absence of stronger macro drivers, the currency pair may continue to trade sideways, with short-term fluctuations driven by USD flows and shifting risk sentiment.
