The US Dollar Index (DXY) is holding near 98.20, showing resilience as markets navigate lingering geopolitical tensions. The Strait of Hormuz remains partially obstructed, with reports of a “double blockage” impacting oil flows, though some tankers continue to pass. Investors are weighing these developments against fragile ceasefire headlines, keeping USD trading relatively firm against major currencies like the EUR and JPY.  

EUR/USD has seen muted swings, hovering just below key technical resistance at 1.0900, while USD/JPY remains supported near 146.50. Market participants are reacting to a mix of safe-haven demand and risk-off sentiment, with the greenback benefiting from uncertainty in energy supply routes. Traders are closely watching both geopolitical updates and economic data for directional cues.  

Technical analysts note that if the DXY sustains above 98.20, further gains could pressure EUR and commodity-linked currencies such as AUD and CAD. Conversely, any escalation in Strait of Hormuz disruptions could trigger sharper USD appreciation. The combination of geopolitical risk, tight technical levels, and ongoing trading volatility is shaping near-term exchange rate dynamics across major currency pairs.
