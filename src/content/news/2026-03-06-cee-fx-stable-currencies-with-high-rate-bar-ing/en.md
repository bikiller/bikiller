Central and Eastern European currencies are holding relatively steady in recent trading sessions, even as rate markets in Poland and the Czech Republic face renewed selling pressure. According to ING’s Frantisek Taborsky, the resilience in regional foreign exchange markets reflects the still-high interest rate environment, which continues to provide a supportive backdrop for CEE currency pairs against both the USD and the EUR.

Despite volatility in local bond markets, the Polish zloty and Czech koruna have shown limited reaction in their respective exchange rates. Investors appear cautious about aggressively selling these currencies while regional central banks maintain relatively elevated policy rates. This high-yield profile continues to attract carry-focused trading strategies, helping stabilize key currency pairs such as EUR/PLN and EUR/CZK in the near term.

Market participants are also weighing global FX dynamics, particularly movements in the USD and broader EUR sentiment, when positioning in CEE currencies. While renewed rate market pressure could eventually translate into currency weakness, the current rate differential still acts as a barrier against sharp depreciation. As long as interest rates remain comparatively high, analysts expect the region’s FX market to remain broadly stable despite external volatility.
