Silver (XAG/USD) surged back to approximately $68.20 during the European trading session on Monday, reflecting a notable recovery after recent declines. The rebound came as U.S. President Donald Trump announced a pause on strikes against Iran’s power plants, easing geopolitical tensions. This development prompted a shift in market sentiment, with investors seeking safe-haven assets like silver.

The price action saw silver breaking above the $67.00 level and pushing toward $68.20 as traders adjusted positions. The pause in military escalation provided temporary relief, boosting silver's appeal amid uncertainties in global markets. With geopolitical risks still present, silver's performance could remain sensitive to further developments in U.S.-Iran relations, as well as broader market sentiment regarding the USD and other key assets.
