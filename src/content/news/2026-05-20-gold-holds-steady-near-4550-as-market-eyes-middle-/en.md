Gold hovered near $4,550 in early Thursday trading, with XAU/USD holding steady around $4,540 during the Asian session. Market participants remain cautious as stalled US-Iran peace talks and heightened tensions around the Strait of Hormuz continue to influence risk sentiment. The safe-haven metal is drawing attention from forex traders weighing USD and EUR movements amid geopolitical uncertainty.

Investors are monitoring technical levels closely, noting support near $4,500 and resistance around $4,570. The flat trading reflects a wait-and-see approach, with gold’s exchange rate reacting subtly to news on the Middle East. Analysts suggest that any escalation in regional tensions could push gold higher, impacting currency pairs like USD/JPY and EUR/USD as traders adjust positions.

Currency markets show mixed reactions as the greenback remains broadly supported, while European currencies track risk appetite. Traders are balancing exposure between safe-haven assets and risk-sensitive instruments, keeping a close eye on geopolitical developments. Continued uncertainty in the Middle East could maintain gold’s trading range, shaping USD liquidity flows and influencing forex market strategies in the near term.
