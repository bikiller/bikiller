EUR/USD has recovered sharply, completing its retracement of March losses, according to ING’s Chris Turner. The currency pair’s rebound is underpinned by diminishing expectations of an ECB rate hike in April, even as markets still price in a potential move in June. Traders are weighing the euro’s gains against a broadly firm USD, adjusting positions as central bank signals shift.  

The rally highlights the delicate balance in euro-dollar trading, with the ECB maintaining flexibility in its policy outlook. Analysts suggest that while short-term momentum favors EUR strength, the exchange rate could face corrective pressures if upcoming data or policy commentary reasserts hawkish guidance. Market participants are closely monitoring liquidity and technical levels for potential pullbacks.  

Strategically, EUR/USD’s trajectory reflects broader macro sentiment, including the USD’s resilience and shifting interest rate expectations. Traders navigating this currency pair should consider both the fading April hike and the June option as key drivers, while remaining alert to volatility spikes. The interplay of central bank communication and market positioning continues to shape trading dynamics in the euro-dollar market.
