Silver (XAG/USD) remains largely flat on Friday, hovering around $75.60 as traders struggle to find a clear direction. Despite hopes for a potential US-Iran peace deal improving overall market sentiment, the precious metal has yet to capitalize on these geopolitical developments. The XAG/USD exchange rate is showing minimal movement, signaling caution among investors as the week comes to a close with little change.

The US Dollar (USD) has slipped to a two-week low, providing some support for alternative assets like silver. Currency pair dynamics, particularly the softer USD against major rivals like the EUR, are influencing trading flows, but market participants appear hesitant to push XAG/USD beyond key technical levels. Volatility remains subdued, leaving silver trading in a narrow range despite the backdrop of macroeconomic optimism.

Analysts suggest that without a decisive catalyst, silver’s trading may remain muted. Any shift in USD strength or fresh developments on the US-Iran front could quickly sway the exchange rate. For now, XAG/USD is poised to finish the week near its current price, reflecting a market in balance as traders weigh geopolitical news against ongoing currency trends.
