Gold extended losses on Tuesday as investors reacted to stronger-than-expected US inflation, pushing the XAU/USD currency pair down over 1%. The precious metal hit a daily high of $4,773 before retreating to $4,678, reflecting waning expectations for Federal Reserve rate cuts in 2026. The surge in the USD added pressure on gold, dampening its appeal as a hedge against inflation.  

Crude oil’s rally further weighed on the bullion market, reinforcing the USD’s strength across major currency pairs, including EUR/USD and GBP/USD. Traders noted that hotter CPI readings have reinforced the Fed’s hawkish stance, prompting a reassessment of market positioning in forex and commodities. Gold’s retreat coincided with broader risk-on flows, as investors favored energy and USD-denominated assets over safe-haven metals.  

Technical analysts highlight that XAU/USD may face resistance near $4,773, while immediate support holds at the current $4,678 level. With inflation expectations driving trading sentiment, currency pairs remain sensitive to macroeconomic data, and gold prices are likely to track shifts in Fed policy projections and USD strength in the coming sessions.
