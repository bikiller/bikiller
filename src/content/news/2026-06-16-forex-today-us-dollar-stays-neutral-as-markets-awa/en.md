The US Dollar Index (DXY) trades with a softer bias around the 99.50 level as forex markets remain cautious ahead of the Federal Reserve’s upcoming policy decision, the first chaired by Kevin Warsh. The USD is holding a neutral tone, with traders refraining from strong positioning in major currency pairs amid uncertainty over the central bank’s next guidance.

Trading activity remains subdued across FX markets, with the DXY consolidating near recent levels as investors await clearer signals on interest rate direction. The cautious stance reflects broader uncertainty in USD pricing, while liquidity is thinner ahead of the announcement. Volatility in key currency pairs such as EUR/USD and GBP/USD has been limited, with traders avoiding aggressive exposure.

Looking ahead, markets will closely assess the Fed statement for policy cues that could reshape USD demand and exchange rate trends. Any hawkish surprise could strengthen the dollar, while a dovish tone may pressure the greenback further below the 99.50 threshold on the DXY. Forex traders remain positioned defensively across major currency pairs.
