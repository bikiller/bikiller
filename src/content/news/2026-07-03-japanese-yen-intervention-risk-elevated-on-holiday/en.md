ING analyst Francesco Pesole highlights heightened volatility in the Japanese yen, particularly in the USD/JPY currency pair, noting that recent downward moves in the exchange rate may already have been influenced by foreign exchange intervention. He points to the sensitivity of trading conditions in the forex market as the yen reacts sharply to shifts in USD demand.

US holidays are thinning liquidity across forex markets, amplifying intraday swings in USD/JPY trading and raising the likelihood of additional Japanese authorities' intervention. According to ING, such conditions have historically prompted more active FX policy responses, particularly when volatility in the currency pair becomes disorderly and threatens broader exchange rate stability.

For forex traders, the combination of thin liquidity and heightened intervention risk underscores the importance of cautious positioning in USD/JPY. Sudden moves in the currency pair can be exaggerated in low-volume sessions, while USD and JPY flows remain highly sensitive to official signals. Market participants will closely monitor any further signs of policy action from Tokyo.
