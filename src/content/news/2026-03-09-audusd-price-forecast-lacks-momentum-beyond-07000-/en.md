The AUD/USD currency pair shows limited upside as it trades near the mid-0.6900s, recovering slightly from a four-day low reached earlier on Monday. The exchange rate is attempting to fill the weekly bearish gap, but buying momentum remains muted, reflecting cautious investor sentiment amid persistent global uncertainties. Traders are closely monitoring whether the pair can sustain gains above the 0.7000 level.  

Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East continue to underpin the USD, keeping the Australian dollar under pressure. Market participants are weighing safe-haven flows into the US dollar against the broader risk appetite, which has restrained AUD/USD trading. Technical resistance around 0.7000 suggests that any breakout would require stronger catalysts, while immediate support holds near current levels.  

Overall, the currency pair faces a delicate balance between recovering demand for the AUD and persistent USD strength. Short-term trading is likely to remain range-bound unless geopolitical developments or economic data sharply shift sentiment. Investors are watching both regional events and broader market indicators, including the EUR and other major currencies, for cues on the AUD/USD trajectory in the days ahead.
