USD/JPY has remained stable for the second consecutive week, hovering just below the key 160 level. Societe Generale analysts note that the exchange rate is holding steady amid persistent concerns over potential verbal intervention from Japanese authorities. Despite this, the currency pair shows little sign of significant movement, suggesting that market participants are cautious and awaiting further signals from policymakers.

The USD/JPY remains in a tight range, with traders watching for any signs of government action. If the pair breaches the 160 mark, intervention risks could increase, putting pressure on the Japanese yen. As the US dollar remains relatively strong, the balance between these two currencies will continue to shape market sentiment, keeping the trading environment volatile in the near term.
