The Canadian Dollar (CAD) has shown resilience against the US Dollar (USD), with the USD/CAD currency pair remaining subdued around the 1.3640 mark during the Asian trading session on Tuesday. This stability marks the seventh consecutive day of limited movement for the exchange rate, as the CAD benefits from a surge in oil prices, a key driver for the Canadian economy.

Despite the overall strength of the US Dollar in the forex market, the upward momentum in crude oil prices is providing crucial support for the Canadian currency. Traders are closely monitoring these developments, as fluctuations in oil prices could significantly impact the USD/CAD exchange rate moving forward. The interplay between oil prices and the CAD's performance highlights the ongoing volatility in currency trading, emphasizing the importance of economic indicators in determining market direction.
