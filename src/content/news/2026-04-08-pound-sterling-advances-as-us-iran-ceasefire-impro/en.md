The Pound Sterling extended its gains for the third consecutive day, with GBP/USD rising to around 1.3400 during Asian trading hours on Wednesday. The currency pair strengthened as the US Dollar faced downward pressure, driven by reduced demand for safe-haven assets. Investors reacted positively to the news of a two-week ceasefire agreement between the United States and Iran, boosting market sentiment.

The ceasefire development caused a drop in the USD’s appeal, as traders shifted focus from geopolitical tensions to riskier assets. As the global market sentiment improved, GBP/USD continued its upward momentum, with the exchange rate holding steady near key resistance levels. This shift in sentiment highlights how geopolitical events can directly impact currency trading, particularly in the case of safe-haven currencies like the USD.
