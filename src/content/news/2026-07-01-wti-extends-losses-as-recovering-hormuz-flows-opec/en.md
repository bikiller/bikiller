West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil extended losses on Wednesday, remaining under pressure as recovering crude flows through the Strait of Hormuz eased earlier supply concerns and pushed prices back toward levels seen before the US-Iran conflict. The market reaction came despite the latest US inventory data, which was largely overshadowed by shifting global supply dynamics. In forex-linked markets, the decline in oil supported cautious USD positioning and influenced broader sentiment across major currency pairs.

OPEC+ supply outlook added further weight to crude, with expectations of sustained output keeping traders defensive on risk exposure. Improving shipping conditions in the Middle East combined with potential production increases reinforced a bearish tone in energy markets. This spillover extended into forex trading, with EUR/USD and other key currency pairs adjusting to revised inflation and growth expectations driven by lower energy input costs.

Looking ahead, WTI remains sensitive to headline-driven volatility as traders reassess global supply stability and demand signals. Market participants continue to track USD exchange rate movements alongside crude benchmarks, with oil trading ranges acting as a guide for broader macro positioning. Any renewed disruption in shipping lanes or shifts in OPEC+ policy could quickly reverse the current downward pressure.
