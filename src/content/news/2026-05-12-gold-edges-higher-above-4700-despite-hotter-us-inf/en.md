Gold (XAU/USD) edged higher in early Asian trading, hovering around $4,720, as investors weighed a mix of economic and geopolitical factors. The recent hotter-than-expected US inflation data pushed the USD slightly stronger against major currency pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD, limiting the upside for precious metals. Traders remain cautious, eyeing inflation-driven moves that could influence central bank policies and the broader forex market.

Market focus also centers on the upcoming Trump–Xi summit, with expectations that any breakthroughs or tensions could sway risk sentiment and trading flows. Safe-haven demand for gold persists, supporting its price, while currency exchange rates reflect shifting global risk perceptions. Technical levels around $4,700 continue to act as near-term support, with momentum challenged by stronger USD trading across the board.

For forex traders, the interplay between US inflation prints and geopolitical developments remains critical. The gold market’s mild gains illustrate how XAU/USD is reacting to both macroeconomic indicators and global headlines, highlighting the sensitivity of commodity-linked currency pairs. Investors are balancing short-term technical resistance with broader market fundamentals, positioning carefully amid volatile USD movements.
