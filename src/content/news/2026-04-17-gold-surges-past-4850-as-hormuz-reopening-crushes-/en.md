Gold (XAU/USD) surged on Friday, climbing past the $4,850 mark in a dramatic move that sent ripples through global currency markets. The precious metal gained over 1.50% as traders reacted to Iran’s decision to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, a critical oil shipping lane, easing geopolitical tensions that had weighed on risk sentiment. The rally pressured the US Dollar, prompting shifts in major currency pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD.  

Market participants highlighted that the reopening reduces immediate inflationary pressures from higher energy costs, supporting a stronger appetite for safe-haven assets like gold. Trading volumes in XAU/USD spiked as investors rebalanced positions, while the USD slipped against a basket of currencies, reflecting diminished demand amid calmer global uncertainty. Analysts noted that technical levels around $4,850 now serve as a psychological pivot for the yellow metal.  

The ripple effects extended across forex markets, with traders adjusting exposure in response to shifting risk dynamics. EUR/USD and USD/JPY saw modest gains for the euro and yen, respectively, as the greenback softened. With gold demonstrating strong momentum, strategists suggest that XAU/USD may continue testing resistance levels above $4,850, while currencies sensitive to oil prices and geopolitical risk remain under close watch.
