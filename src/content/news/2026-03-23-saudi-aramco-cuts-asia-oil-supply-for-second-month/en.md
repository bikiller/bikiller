Saudi Aramco has reduced its crude oil shipments to Asia for a second consecutive month in April, according to Reuters. The move follows the escalation of the US-Israeli conflict with Iran, which has disrupted flows through the strategically critical Strait of Hormuz. Traders are now factoring in tighter supply conditions, with Brent crude hovering near $86 per barrel and WTI around $82, prompting increased demand for USD as a safe-haven currency.  

The supply cut has triggered volatility in global energy markets, with oil-linked currencies like the Norwegian krone and Canadian dollar experiencing fluctuations against the USD. The EUR/USD pair slid toward 1.0970 amid rising oil-driven inflation concerns, while the USD/JPY strengthened to 134.50, reflecting heightened risk aversion. Analysts note that continued tension in the Middle East could sustain this dynamic, reinforcing the dollar’s appeal in trading and hedging strategies.  

Market participants are closely watching how the supply tightening will influence broader commodity-linked forex positions. Crude futures and currency pairs remain sensitive to geopolitical developments, with stop-loss levels and short-term technical support for major pairs being tested. Investors are weighing potential interest rate impacts alongside energy-driven cost pressures, keeping both oil and forex trading strategies highly reactive in the near term.
