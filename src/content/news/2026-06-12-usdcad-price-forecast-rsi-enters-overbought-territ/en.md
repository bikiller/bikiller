The USD/CAD currency pair opened Friday on a firm footing, supported by a softening Canadian Dollar amid declining crude oil prices. Traders watched the exchange rate hover near key levels as market sentiment remained sensitive to global supply concerns. The RSI indicator has entered overbought territory, signaling potential resistance around the psychologically significant 1.4000 mark, which bears are actively defending.

Market participants are also digesting developments in the geopolitical arena. Optimism persists that the United States and Iran might reach an agreement to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial oil transit route. Such progress could influence commodity-linked currencies, including CAD, as energy markets react to potential supply stability. USD strength has been underpinned by safe-haven demand, maintaining upward momentum against the loonie.

Technically, traders are keeping an eye on intraday support levels and potential retracements, with short-term trading opportunities emerging around 1.4000. While the USD/CAD shows bullish tendencies, the overbought RSI warns of a possible corrective pullback. Broader forex market activity, including EUR and JPY crosses, may also be affected as global liquidity and risk sentiment respond to oil price swings and geopolitical updates.
