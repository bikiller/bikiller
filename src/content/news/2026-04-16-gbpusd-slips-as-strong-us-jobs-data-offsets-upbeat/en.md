GBP/USD slipped 0.17% on Thursday, falling to 1.3534 after briefly touching near 1.36. The decline came as stronger-than-expected US jobs data overshadowed the UK’s GDP figures, dampening the appeal of the British pound. While the GDP report showed moderate growth, it couldn't match the positive US employment numbers, which strengthened the US dollar.

Market sentiment remained generally upbeat, supported by expectations of a potential peace deal between the US and Iran, which kept risk assets buoyant. However, the strong US labor data continued to favor USD in currency trading, leaving the GBP/USD pair under pressure. This shift in market focus left the pair hovering near critical support levels, signaling cautious trading ahead.
