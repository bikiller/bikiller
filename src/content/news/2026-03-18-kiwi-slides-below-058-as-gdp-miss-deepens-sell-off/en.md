The NZD/USD currency pair experienced a significant drop of approximately 1.25% on Wednesday, extending its losses after a weaker-than-expected GDP report from New Zealand. The exchange rate initially tested the 200-day Exponential Moving Average (EMA), but sellers quickly took control, pushing the pair lower. The pair eventually found support near 0.5790, signaling increased bearish momentum in the market.

The disappointing economic data fueled concerns over the outlook for the New Zealand economy, driving traders to cut positions in the Kiwi. The US dollar benefited from this risk-off sentiment, adding to the downward pressure on NZD/USD. As the pair hovers near 0.5790, further declines could be in store, with key support levels closely watched by market participants.
