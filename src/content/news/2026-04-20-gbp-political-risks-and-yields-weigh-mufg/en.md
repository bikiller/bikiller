The Pound (GBP) has shown weak performance, lagging behind both the US Dollar (USD) and the Euro (EUR), according to MUFG’s Lee Hardman. This has resulted in relatively stable trading for GBP/USD and EUR/GBP, which are holding around key levels of 1.3500 and 0.8700, respectively. Market participants are closely watching these levels for signs of further movement.

The pressure on GBP is driven by ongoing political risks and diverging yields. As investors remain cautious amid domestic uncertainties, the exchange rate for GBP/USD has struggled to make gains, with the pair maintaining a narrow range near 1.3500. Similarly, EUR/GBP remains stable as both currencies are influenced by broader market dynamics, keeping the pair near the 0.8700 mark.
