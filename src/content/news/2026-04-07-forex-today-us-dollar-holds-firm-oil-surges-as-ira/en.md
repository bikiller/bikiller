The US Dollar remained firm on Monday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering near 99.80, just below last week’s peak of 100. Traders are closely monitoring geopolitical tensions, particularly around Iran, as US President Donald Trump set an 8:00pm EST deadline regarding the Strait of Hormuz. The heightened uncertainty is driving safe-haven demand for the USD, keeping major currency pairs like EUR/USD under pressure.  

Oil prices surged amid the same geopolitical concerns, reflecting market anxiety over potential disruptions in the strategic shipping lane. Brent crude climbed sharply, influencing energy-sensitive currencies and supporting the US Dollar further. Traders are adjusting positions in response to potential volatility, with exchange rates reacting to the risk-off sentiment dominating global trading.  

Technical analysts note that key support levels for EUR/USD and other major pairs could be tested if tensions escalate further. Market participants are weighing both the political developments and broader macroeconomic indicators, which may shape short-term trading strategies. Investors are advised to watch liquidity conditions and market reactions around the DXY’s current levels, as sudden shifts could trigger rapid movements across forex markets.
