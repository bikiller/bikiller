The Australian Dollar (AUD) has dropped 0.65% against the US Dollar (USD), trading near 0.6985 during the Asian session on Monday. The decline comes as growing tensions in Iran have led to a spike in oil prices, affecting global markets. Traders are closely monitoring developments in the Middle East, which are putting pressure on risk-sensitive currencies like the AUD.

The surge in oil prices is a direct consequence of geopolitical uncertainties, pushing investors toward safe-haven assets. As a result, the AUD has seen a sharp decline in the currency pair's exchange rate against the USD. The 0.6985 level marks a critical support point, with further weakness likely if global tensions continue to escalate.
