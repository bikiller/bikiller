The ongoing conflict between the U.S., Israel, and Iran has caused significant disruptions in global oil and gas markets, with the key shipping route through the Strait of Hormuz effectively shut down. This has led to a sharp increase in freight rates and is now a growing risk for a supply shock. The situation has sparked volatility in oil prices, putting further pressure on USD and EUR pairs as energy concerns escalate.

The halted shipments through Hormuz have stranded large volumes of oil, intensifying concerns about future supply shortages. As the situation develops, oil prices are likely to remain volatile, with potential impacts on the broader financial markets. Traders are closely watching the USD/EUR exchange rate as energy price fluctuations continue to affect global trade and inflation forecasts.
