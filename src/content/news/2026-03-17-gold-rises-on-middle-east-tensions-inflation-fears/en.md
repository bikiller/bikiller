Gold (XAU/USD) inched higher during Tuesday’s Asian session, buoyed by escalating tensions in the Middle East, though the metal remains near a three-week low hit Monday. Traders are weighing geopolitical risk against persistent inflation concerns, which have tempered expectations for central bank rate cuts, keeping gold’s upside limited despite its safe-haven appeal. The exchange rate hovered just above key support levels, reflecting cautious trading sentiment.  

The precious metal’s modest gains come as the US dollar steadies against major currencies, including the EUR/USD pair, anchoring broader forex markets. Investors remain sensitive to shifts in interest rate expectations, as hawkish signals from the Fed continue to support USD strength, indirectly capping gold prices. Volatility in energy markets and regional geopolitical developments add further complexity to currency and commodity flows.  

Market participants are closely watching whether gold can sustain momentum above technical thresholds or retreat toward $1,950 an ounce. Exchange rate movements in pairs like USD/JPY and GBP/USD also influence gold trading, as shifts in the dollar’s value affect dollar-denominated commodities. Traders are balancing risk sentiment with hedging demand, keeping XAU/USD range-bound despite headline-driven spikes.
