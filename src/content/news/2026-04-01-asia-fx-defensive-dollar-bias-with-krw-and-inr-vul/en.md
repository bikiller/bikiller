Asia FX trading shows a defensive dollar bias as markets weigh the impact of surging oil prices and tightening inventories. TD Securities’ Alex Loo and Jayati Bharadwaj highlight that this dual shock is straining growth while driving inflation higher across the region. As a result, USD/KRW and USD/INR are under pressure, with the Korean won and Indian rupee particularly vulnerable in current trading conditions.  

The firm notes that Asian central banks are likely to prioritize growth over aggressive tightening. Limited rate hikes are expected, which could support regional currencies modestly against the dollar but may not fully offset inflationary pressures. This dynamic leaves USD trading resilient, while EUR/USD and other major pairs navigate cautious sentiment amid broader macro risks.  

Investors are watching exchange rates closely, as commodity-driven inflation could shift market positioning. Technical levels suggest key support zones for USD/INR near 83.50 and USD/KRW around 1,340, while breaches could signal further dollar strength. Traders may see continued demand for USD as a safe-haven, even as selective Asian currencies attempt to stabilize on growth-focused policy signals.
