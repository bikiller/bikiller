Gold (XAU/USD) extended its rebound from Thursday, climbing toward $4,220 in early European trading on Friday. The precious metal’s exchange rate reflects a notable recovery, suggesting a potential mean-reversion move back to the 20-day EMA. Traders are reacting to heightened optimism over a possible US-Iran Memorandum of Understanding, which continues to underpin demand for safe-haven assets amid volatile currency pair movements.

The USD remains under pressure against major currencies such as the EUR, as investors weigh geopolitical developments against macroeconomic fundamentals. Gold’s strength highlights its role as a hedge, with market participants tracking technical levels closely. A sustained move above the $4,220 mark could encourage further buying, while testing of support near the 20-day EMA may attract short-term trading opportunities for forex and commodities traders alike.

Analysts note that the market’s reaction is partially driven by expectations that a US-Iran deal could ease tensions and influence USD liquidity. Currency pairs tied to emerging markets and safe-haven flows may see correlated shifts, emphasizing the interplay between geopolitical news and forex trading. With gold poised near key technical benchmarks, traders are eyeing both momentum and risk sentiment to guide positioning in XAU/USD and related exchange rates.
