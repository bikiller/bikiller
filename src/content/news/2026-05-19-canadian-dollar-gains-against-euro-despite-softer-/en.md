The Canadian Dollar (CAD) edged higher against the Euro (EUR) on Tuesday, driven by firm oil prices amid escalating US-Iran tensions. The EUR/CAD currency pair slipped as traders reacted to the commodity-linked strength of the Loonie, even after Canadian domestic inflation data came in softer than anticipated. Market participants noted that the CAD’s resilience reflects broader demand for energy-linked currencies in global trading.  

USD/CAD trading remained supported by the ongoing rally in crude oil, which has bolstered Canada’s export-driven economy. Analysts highlighted that weaker-than-expected inflation, while easing pressure on the Bank of Canada, did little to offset the positive momentum from commodities. The exchange rate dynamics suggest traders are weighing geopolitical risks and oil market developments more heavily than headline CPI figures.  

Technically, EUR/CAD found resistance near 1.4600, with the CAD gaining ground toward short-term support levels around 1.4500. Forex traders are monitoring the pair closely for further movements, noting that elevated oil prices and US-Iran geopolitical developments could continue to underpin the Loonie. Currency pair strategies may remain tilted toward the CAD in the near term, despite soft inflation data.
