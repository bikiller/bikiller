Japanese yen bears are keeping focus on a potential move toward the 162.00 level against the US dollar, as USD/JPY continues to trade in a tight intraday range. According to United Overseas Bank (UOB) analysts Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann, the currency pair has been oscillating between 161.20 and 161.80 following a recent session range of 161.25–161.73, highlighting persistent consolidation in the exchange rate.

Market participants are seeing the USD/JPY pair remain range-bound as traders weigh US dollar resilience against ongoing yen weakness, with the exchange rate lacking a clear directional catalyst. The consolidation between 161.20 and 161.80 reflects cautious positioning in forex trading, while broader USD strength and interest rate differentials continue to influence sentiment across major currency pairs.

Traders are closely watching whether USD/JPY can break above the 162.00 threshold, which could signal renewed bullish momentum for the currency pair, while support remains near 161.20 in the near term. For forex market participants, the narrow trading band suggests limited volatility, though any breakout could quickly reshape short-term positioning across USD and related currency crosses such as EUR/USD.
