Markets are recalibrating as traders weigh the potential impact of a proposed IEA-coordinated oil reserve release against growing concerns over US inflation. MUFG’s Head of Research, Derek Halpenny, highlighted that the release could temporarily ease supply disruptions stemming from tensions in the Strait of Hormuz. Oil prices responded modestly, but the focus in currency markets is shifting to the outlook for the USD.  

The US dollar has seen mixed trading, with the USD/EUR pair hovering near key technical levels as investors digest inflation signals. Rising inflation expectations are underpinning the greenback, while European traders remain cautious amid energy market volatility. Halpenny suggests that any sustained USD strength could influence cross-currency flows, pushing the USD/JPY and USD/CHF pairs higher in the short term.  

Forex markets are bracing for potential volatility, with energy shocks and inflation data acting as dual catalysts. Traders are closely monitoring exchange rates, liquidity, and positioning, seeking to anticipate swings in USD-linked pairs. While oil interventions may offer temporary relief, the underlying macroeconomic picture suggests that the greenback’s trajectory will remain sensitive to inflation surprises and policy signals from the Fed.
