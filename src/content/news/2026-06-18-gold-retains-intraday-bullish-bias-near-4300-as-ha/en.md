Gold (XAU/USD) eases slightly from its daily peak but maintains an intraday bullish bias, holding near the $4,300 level as trading heads into the European session on Thursday. The currency pair continues to attract buyers on dips, reflecting steady demand despite mild profit-taking after the earlier upside move in the exchange rate.

Hawkish Federal Reserve expectations continue to limit further gains, as stronger USD dynamics and elevated US Treasury yields keep upside momentum in check. However, underlying demand for the safe-haven metal remains supportive, preventing a deeper pullback in the XAU/USD exchange rate. Traders remain cautious ahead of fresh US economic signals that could reshape interest rate outlooks.

Overall, Gold’s trading profile suggests a consolidation phase with a mildly positive intraday tone, as the currency pair balances safe-haven inflows against a firm USD backdrop. In the near term, XAU/USD may remain range-bound around the $4,300 mark until either Fed repricing or softer US data triggers a clearer directional breakout.
