Gold prices fell below the $4,700 mark, settling around $4,690 during the early Asian trading session on Friday. This decline in the XAU/USD currency pair can be attributed to a stronger US Dollar, which has been gaining traction amid rising geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Elevated oil prices further exacerbate inflation concerns, adding pressure to the precious metal.

As the USD strengthens, investors are likely shifting their focus to safer assets, causing a shift in trading dynamics. The current exchange rate reflects market sentiment, which remains cautious amid escalating global uncertainties. Traders will be closely monitoring these developments, as fluctuations in gold prices could signal broader economic implications, particularly concerning inflation and currency stability.
