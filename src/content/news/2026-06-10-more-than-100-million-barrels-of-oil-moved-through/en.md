US President Donald Trump confirmed on Tuesday that a covert US military operation successfully safeguarded commercial shipping through the Strait of Hormuz, enabling more than 100 million barrels of oil to reach global markets. The move comes amid heightened tensions with Iran, providing relief to energy traders and influencing commodity-linked currency pairs such as USD/CAD and USD/NOK, which are sensitive to oil price fluctuations.

Oil markets responded with a slight stabilization, supporting the US dollar as investors recalibrated risk sentiment. Brent crude and WTI futures showed modest gains, while the euro weakened slightly against the dollar, reflecting cautious trading. Forex traders are closely monitoring USD/JPY and EUR/USD for potential volatility as geopolitical risks intertwine with energy supply dynamics.

Market analysts note that secure shipping through Hormuz reduces the immediate threat to global oil flow, potentially easing speculative pressures on emerging market currencies. Trading desks report heightened activity in oil-linked currencies, and technical levels around 1.0950 for EUR/USD and 138.50 for USD/JPY are under observation as investors gauge the broader macro impact of the US intervention on exchange rates and cross-border liquidity.
