Standard Chartered's Edward Lee and Jonathan Koh forecast that the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) will adjust the Singapore Dollar (SGD) Nominal Effective Exchange Rate (NEER) slope by 50bps in April. This move is expected to partially reverse the pre-emptive easing measures taken in the first half of 2025, while keeping the policy band unchanged. The expected tightening signals confidence in the SGD's strength as the MAS takes action to curb inflation.

The adjustment would have significant implications for the USD/SGD pair, with potential upside for the Singapore Dollar as traders respond to the MAS’s signal of further tightening. A steeper NEER slope could make the SGD more resilient against fluctuations in major currency pairs, such as EUR/SGD, offering a more favorable outlook for the currency. This move reflects a strategic shift aimed at stabilizing exchange rates while addressing economic challenges.
