The Dow Jones Industrial Average rebounded sharply on Monday, climbing roughly 1.2% to reclaim the 47,000 level after a muted finish near 46,500 on Friday. The S&P 500 gained over 1%, pushing back toward 6,700, while the Nasdaq Composite rose more than 1.2%, closing near 22,400. Traders attributed the recovery to retreating crude oil prices, which eased pressure on risk sentiment and lifted equities across major sectors.  

Currency markets mirrored the improved risk appetite, with the USD showing mixed performance against major pairs. The EUR/USD exchange rate edged higher as investors rotated from commodities into safer dollar positions, while USD/JPY trading remained choppy amid cautious positioning ahead of upcoming economic data. Analysts noted that equities’ rebound could signal renewed appetite for risk-on trades, with implications for USD liquidity and cross-currency flows.  

Market participants are watching crude oil and macroeconomic signals closely, as further volatility could sway both stock and currency markets. A sustained Dow rally above 47,000 may support broader sentiment, influencing trading in USD-linked pairs and equity-sensitive currencies. The interplay between energy prices, U.S. indices, and exchange rates remains a focal point for traders adjusting positions in the first half of the week.
