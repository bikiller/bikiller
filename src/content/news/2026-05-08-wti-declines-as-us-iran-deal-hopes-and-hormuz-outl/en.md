WTI crude oil slipped 2.49% on Thursday, extending its weekly decline to over 7.39%, as optimism grew over a potential US-Iran deal aimed at easing tensions in the Middle East. The improving outlook for the Strait of Hormuz, a key oil transit route, added pressure on prices, prompting traders to adjust positions in both oil and related currency pairs, including USD and EUR.  

The stronger risk sentiment lifted the US dollar slightly, with the USD/EUR exchange rate edging higher amid reduced geopolitical risk. Forex traders monitored energy-linked flows closely, noting that lower oil prices often weigh on commodity-linked currencies, while supporting safe-haven demand for the greenback. Technical charts showed WTI struggling to hold the $83.50 support, signaling potential further downside if deal optimism persists.  

Market participants are now weighing the broader implications for trading strategies. A sustained drop in WTI could influence USD/JPY and AUD/USD movements, as energy-linked currencies adjust to softer crude prices. Investors remain attentive to headlines on the US-Iran talks, which could reshape both oil markets and cross-border currency flows in the coming sessions.
