The National Bureau of Statistics of China (NBS) is set to release February’s CPI and PPI data at 01:30 GMT, drawing close attention from forex traders. Analysts anticipate the Consumer Price Index (CPI) to rise 0.8% year-on-year, up from January’s 0.2%, signaling potential inflationary pressure. This increase could influence global market sentiment, especially on commodity-linked currencies like the AUD/USD, which often reacts to Chinese economic momentum.  

Investors will watch the Producer Price Index (PPI) for additional clues on inflation trends and manufacturing costs. A stronger-than-expected reading may support the Australian dollar against the US dollar, lifting the AUD/USD exchange rate. Conversely, softer figures could weigh on risk sentiment, favoring safe-haven currencies like the USD and EUR. Traders are likely to adjust positions ahead of the data release, reflecting broader market sensitivity to China’s growth dynamics.  

Technical levels for AUD/USD are closely monitored in this context, with resistance near 0.6800 and support around 0.6700. A breakout in either direction could spark increased volatility, as market participants react to the interplay between Chinese inflation data and global risk appetite. Currency pair movements may also be influenced by corresponding shifts in USD strength, EUR performance, and commodity price trends, emphasizing the interconnected nature of forex trading in the current environment.
