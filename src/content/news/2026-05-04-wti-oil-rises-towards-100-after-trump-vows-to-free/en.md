Crude oil prices surged on Monday, with the US benchmark, West Texas Intermediate (WTI), rising to $99.40 per barrel. This marks a $3 increase from the daily opening of $96.46, driven by geopolitical concerns. The price hike follows US President Donald Trump’s pledge to secure ships in the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial oil shipping route.

The move highlights the ongoing tension in the Middle East and its potential impact on global oil supply. The surge in oil prices is also likely to affect the broader forex market, particularly the USD/EUR pair, as fluctuations in commodity prices can influence investor sentiment toward the US dollar. Traders will closely monitor any further developments in the region for signs of sustained volatility.
