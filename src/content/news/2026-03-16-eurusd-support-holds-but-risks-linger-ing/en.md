EUR/USD has stabilized near the 1.1390/1.1400 zone following a sharp 3% decline, providing a key technical support for traders. The currency pair’s recent pullback reflects heightened market caution, as investors weigh broader USD strength against the Euro’s underlying pressures. Despite the pause, the exchange rate remains vulnerable to renewed selling if risk sentiment shifts.  

Analysts at ING, led by Chris Turner, point to persistent downside risks. Elevated Oil prices continue to pressure the Euro, as energy-driven inflation concerns weigh on the single currency. At the same time, heavy net long positions in EUR leave the market exposed, suggesting that a reversal could accelerate if the support zone fails.  

For forex traders, this combination signals a delicate balancing act. EUR/USD trading around 1.14 requires careful monitoring of both technical and macro factors, including commodity movements and positioning flows. A break below the current support could open the door for further losses, while stabilization might encourage tactical buying, keeping the exchange rate range-bound in the near term.
