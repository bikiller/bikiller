The GBP/JPY currency pair extended gains on Tuesday, climbing amid a softer Japanese Yen. Investors weighed the ongoing tensions between the US and Iran, with fears that any disruption in the Strait of Hormuz could threaten Japan’s energy imports. The Yen’s vulnerability under geopolitical pressure pushed the exchange rate higher, supporting sterling’s advance against Tokyo’s currency. Traders noted the pair’s move above key technical resistance, signaling renewed bullish momentum.  

Market participants highlighted that risk sentiment favored higher-yielding currencies, with the US dollar and euro also maintaining strength versus the Yen. Analysts pointed out that Japan’s heavy reliance on imported oil leaves the Yen sensitive to Middle East energy supply risks. As a result, the GBP/JPY surged, reflecting both safe-haven outflows from JPY and ongoing demand for the British pound amid broader forex trading trends.  

Technical traders noted that GBP/JPY breached the 200-day moving average, with immediate support around 175.50 and resistance near 178.00. Analysts caution that continued escalation in the Middle East could sustain Yen weakness, potentially amplifying currency pair volatility. Forex investors remain watchful, balancing geopolitical concerns with global liquidity conditions, as movements in USD and EUR also influence cross-currency trading dynamics.
