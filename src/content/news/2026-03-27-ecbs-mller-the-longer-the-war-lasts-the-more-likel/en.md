European Central Bank (ECB) Governing Council member Madis Müller stated that the ECB may need to wait for second-round inflation effects before adjusting its monetary policy. In an interview with Econostream, Müller highlighted that the central bank’s response would depend on how the ongoing war impacts inflation, particularly in terms of its extended effects on prices. This cautious stance has kept EUR/USD trading under pressure, with the exchange rate fluctuating between 1.0500 and 1.0600.

Müller’s comments suggest that the ECB remains focused on inflationary risks, with market participants closely watching any signals for future rate hikes. The euro remains sensitive to geopolitical developments, and any escalation in the conflict could push the EUR further lower against the USD. In light of these uncertainties, traders are keeping an eye on the ECB's next moves while watching for any shifts in the broader inflation outlook.
