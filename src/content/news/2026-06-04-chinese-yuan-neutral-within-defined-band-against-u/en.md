The USD/CNH currency pair rebounded sharply above 6.78, reversing the previous bearish outlook, according to UOB analysts Quek Ser Leang and Lee Sue Ann. The unexpected climb suggests a neutral stance in the near term, with the exchange rate likely to trade within a defined band. Market participants are now eyeing consolidation between 6.7620 and 6.7980, reflecting caution amid mixed trading signals.  

Key resistance levels are pegged at 6.7850 and 6.7980, where the pair could face selling pressure. Traders are closely monitoring USD strength against other major currencies like the EUR, as broad market dynamics and liquidity flows influence the yuan’s trajectory. The rebound indicates that prior expectations of yuan weakness may need recalibration, especially given ongoing US-China economic interactions and monetary policy considerations.  

Analysts suggest that short-term trading could remain range-bound, with the 6.7620 floor providing support. The neutral outlook implies limited directional bias, prompting cautious positioning from forex traders. Investors may adjust strategies around the highlighted technical levels, balancing exposure to USD volatility while watching for any breakout that could redefine near-term momentum in the yuan-dollar exchange rate.
