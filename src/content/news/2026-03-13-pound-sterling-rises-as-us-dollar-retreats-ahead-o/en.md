Pound Sterling edged higher against the US Dollar on Friday, reclaiming some ground lost in the previous session. The GBP/USD currency pair traded around 1.3370 during Asian hours, reflecting a mild recovery as the US Dollar retreated. Investors appeared cautious ahead of the US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) inflation data, which could influence the Federal Reserve’s policy outlook and broader market sentiment.  

The US Dollar Index (DXY) pulled back after gaining nearly 0.5% on Thursday, easing pressure on major currency pairs. The retreat in the USD allowed the pound to stabilize, while other currencies such as the euro also found support amid softer greenback demand. Traders are watching key technical levels closely, with 1.3370 serving as a near-term reference point for GBP/USD movements.  

Market participants remain alert to upcoming US economic indicators, particularly the PCE inflation report, which may drive volatility across forex markets. Any surprises in inflation data could reshape expectations for interest rates and impact currency pair trading strategies. For now, the pound’s modest gains against the USD highlight a cautious yet opportunistic approach as traders navigate pre-data positioning.
