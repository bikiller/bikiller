BNY’s Geoff Yu notes clients have largely capitulated on Japanese yen (JPY) and South Korean won (KRW) long positions as both currencies continue to trade on the weaker side against the US dollar (USD) and Chinese yuan (CNY). Persistent downside pressure in these currency pairs has reinforced bearish sentiment across Asian FX trading desks, with spot exchange rates drifting in favor of USD-led momentum.

Market dynamics reflect sustained USD strength and relative resilience in the CNY, which has kept broader Asian FX under pressure. The unwind of long positioning in JPY and KRW suggests momentum-driven trading flows are dominating currency pair movements, with investors reducing exposure amid fading conviction in a near-term reversal. In FX trading, positioning adjustments are amplifying intraday volatility.

However, BNY sees scope for a sharp rebound if US-related pressure on global FX eases or positioning becomes excessively stretched. In such a scenario, Asian currencies could recover rapidly, driven by short-covering and valuation resets across major currency pairs. Traders remain focused on USD direction as the key driver of potential trend shifts and renewed volatility in Asian FX markets.
