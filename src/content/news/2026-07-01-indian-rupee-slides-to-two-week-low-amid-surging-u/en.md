Indian Rupee extended its weakness against the US Dollar on Wednesday, sliding to a two-week low as the USD/INR currency pair climbed toward the 95.25 level, its highest in two weeks. The exchange rate reflected renewed USD demand in forex trading, with the dollar strengthening across majors as traders reassessed global rate expectations.

Pressure on the rupee intensified as surging US Treasury yields boosted the US Dollar, widening interest rate differentials and encouraging capital outflows from emerging markets like India. Higher yields have strengthened USD positioning in the forex market, keeping USD/INR bids firm and limiting any corrective pullbacks in the currency pair during intraday trading.

From a technical perspective, USD/INR holding near 95.25 signals strong bullish momentum for the greenback, with traders eyeing whether the pair can sustain levels above this resistance zone. A break higher could extend gains, while rupee recovery attempts remain vulnerable unless US yields stabilize and risk sentiment improves in broader forex markets.
