The USD/CAD currency pair rose 0.2% to hover near 1.3772 during Wednesday’s European trading session, extending its recent gains. Market sentiment is influenced by a softer Canadian Dollar, which underperforms against major currencies including the USD and EUR. Traders are closely watching whether the pair can sustain momentum above the 50% Fibonacci retracement level, a key technical threshold for further upside.

The Loonie’s weakness reflects diminishing expectations that the Bank of Canada will hike interest rates in its upcoming July policy meeting. This shift has prompted investors to favor the U.S. Dollar, boosting the USD/CAD exchange rate and encouraging speculative trading in the forex market. Analysts note that sustaining above key technical levels could pave the way for additional gains in the pair.

Looking ahead, the USD/CAD’s trajectory will depend on central bank signals and economic data from both the U.S. and Canada. Should the pair maintain above the 50% Fibonacci retracement, it could extend its rally, attracting short-term traders and institutional flows. Conversely, any recovery in CAD or dovish U.S. data may cap gains and increase volatility in the currency pair.
