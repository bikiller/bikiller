NZD/USD currency pair steadied on Thursday, pausing a six-day losing streak as trading conditions stabilized in the forex market. The exchange rate hovered around 0.5650, remaining near seven-month lows after sustained USD strength weighed on the New Zealand dollar. Price action reflected cautious sentiment as traders assessed whether downside momentum would extend further or consolidate.

The US Dollar softened modestly after the latest US Personal Consumption Expenditures (PCE) data aligned broadly with expectations, reducing immediate policy surprises from the Federal Reserve outlook. This USD easing provided limited relief to risk-sensitive currencies, including the New Zealand dollar, though broader forex trading conditions remained tilted in favor of the greenback.

Technical indicators signaled caution as the Relative Strength Index (RSI) flashed oversold conditions for NZD/USD, suggesting potential for short-term consolidation. Despite this, the currency pair remains pinned near seven-month lows around 0.5650, leaving traders watching whether momentum stabilizes or further USD-driven pressure extends the downtrend in forex trading sessions ahead.
