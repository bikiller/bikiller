The Japanese Yen edged higher on Friday as the USD/JPY currency pair extended its losses for a second consecutive session, trading around 159.90 during Asian hours. Market participants attributed the decline to mounting concerns over potential government intervention, which provided support to the Yen amid a broadly volatile forex landscape.

Traders noted that the exchange rate’s retreat comes as the Bank of Japan continues to maintain ultra-loose monetary policies, while the broader USD remains under pressure. Technical analysts highlighted that the 159.90 level could act as a short-term pivot, with the Yen benefiting from safe-haven flows as intervention fears gain traction.

The move also reverberated across related currency pairs, with the EUR/JPY and other Yen crosses showing similar stabilization. Market watchers remain attentive to any official comments or actions from Japanese authorities, as potential interventions could sharply influence trading dynamics and risk sentiment in the USD, JPY, and broader forex markets.
