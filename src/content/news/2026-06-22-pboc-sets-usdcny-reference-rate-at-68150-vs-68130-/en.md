On Monday, the People’s Bank of China (PBOC) set the USD/CNY central parity rate at 6.8150 for the trading session, slightly higher than the previous fix of 6.8130 last Thursday and above the Reuters estimate of 6.7733. The adjustment signals a modestly softer yuan bias against the US dollar, while maintaining tight control over onshore FX stability within the trading framework.

Market participants noted the fix continues to guide onshore USD/CNY trading, with the yuan remaining closely managed within its daily band. The slightly stronger midpoint compared with market expectations helped anchor sentiment in early Asian trading, even as broader USD strength persisted across global forex markets, including EUR/USD softness. Liquidity conditions and state guidance continue to play a key role in price action dynamics.

Looking ahead, the PBOC’s steady fixing pattern suggests a continued preference for currency stability amid external volatility and shifting US dollar expectations. Traders will monitor whether the USD/CNY exchange rate maintains a controlled range or tests the upper end of its trading corridor. Broader forex sentiment, including EUR and USD index movements, will remain influential in shaping near-term yuan direction.
