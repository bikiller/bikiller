The EUR/GBP currency pair is showing signs of gradual upward momentum, as traders reassess expectations around Bank of England policy. According to Rabobank’s FX Strategy team, the market may be overpricing the likelihood of up to three rate hikes over the next year. The forecast comes amid a UK labour market that is beginning to loosen and economic growth that has remained underwhelming, weighing on sterling sentiment.  

Trading activity in EUR/GBP has picked up as investors recalibrate positions against the euro. With the pound facing headwinds from slower employment gains and subdued domestic output, the euro is finding support, nudging the exchange rate higher. Market participants are also weighing the relative strength of the USD in global trading, adding further context to cross-currency dynamics involving EUR and GBP.  

For traders, the current pricing signals a potential opportunity to exploit shifts in the cross. Rabobank highlights that while central bank rhetoric has kept expectations elevated, underlying economic indicators suggest limited room for aggressive tightening. This dynamic may drive continued modest gains in EUR/GBP, influencing broader forex portfolios and positioning decisions as market participants track both UK fundamentals and euro-area trends.
