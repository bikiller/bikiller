Gold prices have shown a slight upward trend amid turbulent trading, supported by a fragile ceasefire between the US and Iran, as well as ongoing negotiations between Israel and Lebanon. According to OCBC strategists Sim Moh Siong and Christopher Wong, the recent decline in US nominal and real yields has contributed to improved risk sentiment, creating a favorable environment for gold trading. 

As traders navigate these geopolitical developments, the bank suggests that the risks for gold prices are now skewed towards the upside. This shift in market sentiment may lead to increased demand for the yellow metal, potentially impacting currency pairs, particularly the USD. With gold's appeal as a safe haven in uncertain times, its movement could also influence broader market dynamics in the coming weeks.
