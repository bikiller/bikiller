West Texas Intermediate (WTI) slipped to around $101.10 on Tuesday, down 1.26% from its previous close, easing after a surge triggered by escalating Middle East tensions. The pullback comes as concerns over disruptions to oil flows through the Strait of Hormuz show signs of moderation, though geopolitical risk remains elevated, keeping volatility in energy markets heightened. Traders remain watchful of supply developments and their knock-on effects for USD liquidity.  

The recent WTI movements have influenced broader forex trading, with the USD showing modest gains against a basket of currencies amid safe-haven demand. The EUR/USD pair hovered near 1.1005, reflecting cautious investor sentiment as market participants assess the impact of Middle East risks on global trade and commodity-linked currencies. Oil-linked currencies like the CAD and NOK remain sensitive to crude swings, prompting active exchange rate monitoring.  

Analysts suggest that while short-term trading may see corrections, underlying tension between Iran and the US continues to support elevated risk premiums in both oil and forex markets. Market watchers emphasize that shifts in crude prices could drive USD-based currency pairs and influence central bank considerations, reinforcing the importance of technical levels in guiding intraday trading strategies. Hedging and position adjustments remain prevalent among institutional traders navigating this uncertain backdrop.
