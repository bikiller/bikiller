West Texas Intermediate (WTI) crude oil extended a sharp sell-off as traders reacted to speculation around a potential Iran peace deal, triggering expectations of eased supply risks. Prices slid through key support levels, pushing the benchmark to a 15-week low as market participants reacted before any official confirmation. The move highlights how sensitive energy trading has become to geopolitical headlines and shifting risk premiums.

Broader market sentiment turned risk-off, with traders pricing in improved global supply prospects and weaker risk premiums in crude markets. The sell-off was amplified by algorithmic trading flows and repositioning across commodity desks. Correlation with USD strength and a firmer dollar index added pressure, while forex markets saw renewed attention to USD pairs as energy volatility increased.

Looking ahead, traders will monitor whether the geopolitical narrative continues to dominate crude pricing or if fundamentals reassert control. A sustained break to new lows could weigh on inflation expectations and influence central bank outlooks, indirectly affecting EUR/USD and broader currency pair volatility as capital shifts between energy and FX markets.
