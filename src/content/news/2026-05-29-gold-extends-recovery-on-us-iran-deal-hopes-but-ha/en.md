Gold (XAU/USD) extended its recovery on Friday as investors weighed the possibility of a US-Iran agreement that could ease geopolitical tensions and influence broader market sentiment. The precious metal climbed to around $4,530 during early trading, rebounding from a two-month low of $4,366 recorded on Thursday. The move reflects renewed buying interest after recent losses, while traders continue to monitor developments affecting the USD and global risk appetite.

Despite the rebound, gains in the yellow metal remain constrained by the Federal Reserve’s hawkish stance. Expectations that US interest rates could stay elevated for longer have supported the US Dollar, limiting upside potential for non-yielding assets such as gold. As a result, the XAU/USD currency pair remains caught between safe-haven demand and persistent pressure from higher Treasury yields and a firmer USD exchange rate.

Market participants are also assessing the broader implications for major currency pairs, including EUR/USD, as shifting expectations around US monetary policy continue to drive trading activity. While gold’s recovery from $4,366 to $4,530 signals improving sentiment, traders remain cautious. A sustained move higher may require clearer signs of easing geopolitical risks or a softer Fed outlook, while renewed USD strength could cap further advances in the near term.
