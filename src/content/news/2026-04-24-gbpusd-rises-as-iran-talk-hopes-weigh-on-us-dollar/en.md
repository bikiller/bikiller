GBP/USD rose on Friday as the currency pair advanced in trading, with improved risk appetite across global markets undermining demand for the US Dollar (USD). The exchange rate benefited from a shift away from safe-haven positioning, as investors rotated into risk assets during the session. Broader forex trading activity reflected a softer USD tone against major peers.

Sentiment was further supported by speculation that a second round of US-Iran talks may be approaching, easing geopolitical concerns and pressuring the US Dollar’s safe-haven appeal. At the same time, a three-week extension of the Israel–Lebanon ceasefire added to market optimism, reinforcing risk-on flows across the forex market and keeping USD demand subdued against the pound.

Traders now focus on whether improved risk sentiment can sustain further GBP/USD upside, with volatility in the currency pair likely driven by developments in Middle East diplomacy and broader USD movement across major forex markets. Participants will also monitor incoming economic data and central bank expectations for cues on the USD outlook, which remains highly sensitive to shifts in global risk appetite and geopolitical headlines.
