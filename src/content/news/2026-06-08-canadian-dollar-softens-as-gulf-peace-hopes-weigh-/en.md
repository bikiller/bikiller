The Canadian Dollar eased on Monday as optimism over peace in the Gulf put downward pressure on crude oil, tempering the commodity-linked Loonie’s gains. USD/CAD remained in a tight range, trading around 1.3950, near two-month highs, as the softer US Dollar provided only limited support. Traders noted that the exchange rate’s resilience reflects a cautious market amid shifting geopolitical sentiment.  

Oil’s mild pullback weighed on the Canadian currency, highlighting the Loonie’s sensitivity to energy prices. Despite a broadly weaker USD, CAD struggled to extend its advance, leaving the currency pair oscillating within familiar technical bounds. Market participants pointed to ongoing geopolitical developments and global risk appetite as key drivers influencing intraday trading.  

Analysts suggest that USD/CAD may continue to hover near 1.3950 unless fresh catalysts emerge. The exchange rate’s narrow range indicates market indecision, with investors balancing gains in the US Dollar against potential rebounds in oil. A sustained move above the recent highs could signal further momentum for the pair, while any renewed drop in crude may cap the Loonie’s upside.
