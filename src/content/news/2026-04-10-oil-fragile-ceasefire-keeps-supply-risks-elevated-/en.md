Oil prices remain volatile as a fragile ceasefire in the Middle East keeps supply risks high. Wells Fargo’s international economics team highlighted that geopolitical tensions continue to elevate the uncertainty surrounding global oil markets, with the outlook remaining unclear. This geopolitical instability has prompted cautious trading, contributing to fluctuations in the USD and EUR as investors seek safer assets.  

In the forex market, concerns over oil supply disruptions are impacting major currency pairs. The USD has seen fluctuations against the EUR and JPY as traders adjust their positions in response to the ongoing risk. The price of crude oil continues to hover above $80 per barrel, further weighing on market sentiment. The fragile situation in the Middle East complicates oil price predictions, leaving traders wary of potential spikes in volatility across both commodities and currencies.
