USD/JPY currency pair extends losses for a second consecutive session on Friday, reversing an intraday uptick toward the mid-161.00s before sliding during the first half of the European trading session. The exchange rate touched a more than two-week low, reflecting renewed selling pressure as traders reassess short-term momentum in the forex market.

Technically, the USD/JPY exchange rate has slipped below the 23.6% Fibonacci retracement level at 161.00, a key threshold watched by forex traders. This breakdown signals increasing vulnerability in the currency pair, with intraday sellers gaining control after the failed recovery toward mid-161.00s. Momentum indicators suggest fading bullish strength in the short-term trading structure.

Looking ahead, forex traders will monitor whether USD/JPY can stabilize above near-term support or extend its decline further, as broader USD dynamics and risk sentiment influence the trading outlook. A sustained break below 161.00 could open room for additional downside volatility, while any rebound may face resistance around the mid-161.00 area.
