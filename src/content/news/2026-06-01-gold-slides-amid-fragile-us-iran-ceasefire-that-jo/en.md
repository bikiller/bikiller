Gold retreated sharply on Monday as uncertainty over the US-Iran ceasefire rattled markets, pushing traders to reassess risk sentiment. The XAU/USD pair fell more than 1%, retreating to $4,490 after hitting a daily high of $4,546. Investors moved to neutral positions amid geopolitical concerns, with the US Dollar gaining modest strength in early trading, reflecting caution across major currency pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD.

The fragile ceasefire developments in the Middle East have injected volatility into commodity and forex markets alike. Oil prices spiked in response to potential supply disruptions, indirectly pressuring gold as a safe-haven asset. Traders monitoring the XAU/USD exchange rate noted that the pullback aligns with technical resistance near $4,550, suggesting a potential consolidation phase before further directional moves.

Market participants remain alert to headlines, as any escalation between the US and Iran could trigger renewed USD volatility and affect other key trading instruments. Forex traders are watching USD pairs closely, with heightened sensitivity to risk-off sentiment that may benefit safe-haven currencies. Analysts suggest that gold could test lower support levels if uncertainty persists, while the broader forex market braces for reactive swings in response to geopolitical shifts.
