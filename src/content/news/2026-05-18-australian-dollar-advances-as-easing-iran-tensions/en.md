The Australian Dollar edged higher on Monday, with the AUD/USD currency pair trading around 0.7160, up 0.15% as the US Dollar softened after a recent rebound. Traders are reacting to easing tensions in Iran, which has reduced safe-haven demand for the USD. The exchange rate movement comes amid broad currency market adjustments, with investors weighing geopolitical developments alongside macroeconomic signals from major central banks.

Market participants are also eyeing the upcoming Reserve Bank of Australia minutes, which could offer fresh insights into future monetary policy. Analysts note that a dovish tilt in the RBA’s commentary might further support the Australian Dollar, potentially boosting its appeal against the greenback. Technical levels near 0.7150-0.7175 are being closely watched by forex traders for short-term trading opportunities.

Meanwhile, broader sentiment in global forex markets remains sensitive to US Dollar fluctuations. The EUR/USD and other major pairs are experiencing modest volatility as investors reassess risk appetite in light of geopolitical developments and central bank communications. With the AUD showing resilience, trading strategies are increasingly focused on potential cross-currency impacts and near-term exchange rate trends.
