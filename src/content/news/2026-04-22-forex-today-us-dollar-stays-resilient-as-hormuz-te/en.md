The US Dollar maintained its resilience on Thursday, with the US Dollar Index (DXY) hovering around the 98.60 mark. Investors have favored the USD amid heightened geopolitical tension in the Strait of Hormuz, as uncertainty over the US-Iran situation keeps markets cautious. Safe-haven demand supported the Greenback, while risk-sensitive currencies, including the euro and commodity-linked pairs, faced pressure in early trading.  

EUR/USD remained under pressure, dipping toward the 1.0800 area, reflecting broad USD strength. Meanwhile, USD/JPY traded near 148.50, with traders monitoring technical levels closely for potential breakouts. Analysts noted that the current trading environment is being shaped by both geopolitical concerns and expectations for US economic data, which may influence interest rate projections and FX positioning in the coming sessions.  

Traders are watching how this tension might impact liquidity and volatility across major currency pairs. The USD’s resilience suggests that investors are pricing in continued uncertainty, affecting exchange rates against the euro, yen, and other major currencies. Market participants are positioning for potential shifts, emphasizing the importance of technical support and resistance levels in guiding short-term forex trading strategies.
