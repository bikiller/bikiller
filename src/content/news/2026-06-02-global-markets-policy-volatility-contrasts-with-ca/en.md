Global markets have experienced pronounced volatility in 2026, with investors adjusting rapidly to changing central bank signals. HSBC Asset Management notes that expectations for the Bank of England and European Central Bank have swung from rate cuts to potential hikes, fueled by rising oil-linked inflation risks. This uncertainty has rippled through forex markets, prompting fluctuations in major currency pairs including EUR/USD and GBP/USD, as traders recalibrate exchange rates in response to shifting policy expectations.  

The USD has seen periods of strength as safe-haven flows increase amid rate speculation, while the EUR has struggled against the greenback, reflecting market concerns over the ECB’s policy trajectory. Analysts point to technical levels being tested, with EUR/USD trading near critical support zones and GBP/USD reacting sharply to statements from the BoE. Oil-linked inflation pressures are expected to continue influencing both central bank moves and currency volatility, affecting trading strategies across global markets.  

Investors and forex traders are navigating a landscape of policy-driven swings, where calm assets contrast sharply with volatile currency pairs. The focus remains on upcoming central bank decisions and inflation data, which could redefine exchange rates and trading ranges in the near term. Market participants are watching for signals that may either stabilize or exacerbate the current rate-driven oscillations in currencies like the USD, EUR, and GBP, impacting global liquidity and trading volumes.
