GBP/USD traded in a narrow range on Wednesday, with the currency pair holding near recent levels as persistent tensions between Washington and Tehran kept the USD supported but prevented a stronger advance. The lack of progress toward restarting US-Iran negotiations maintained a cautious tone across financial markets, limiting risk appetite and leaving the Dollar bid against most major currencies, including the EUR and GBP.

With no meaningful entries on the US economic calendar, trading activity remained driven largely by external themes and by the latest UK inflation data. Investors focused on signs that Britain is still feeling the impact of the energy shock, reinforcing expectations that price pressures may remain elevated for longer. That helped Sterling avoid heavier losses despite the broader strength in the US Dollar.

The relatively stable GBP/USD exchange rate reflects a market caught between competing forces. On one side, geopolitical uncertainty continues to underpin demand for the USD as a safe-haven currency. On the other, persistent inflation in the UK offers support to the Pound, suggesting the currency pair could remain range-bound until either fresh US data or a breakthrough in the Iran talks shifts sentiment.
